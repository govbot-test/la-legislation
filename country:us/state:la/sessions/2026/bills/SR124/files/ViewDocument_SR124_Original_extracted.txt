SLS 26RS-2975 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 124
BY SENATOR FOIL
CONDOLENCES. Expresses sincere and heartfelt condolences to the family of Carolyn Ann
Cherry Moore on the occasion of her passing.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the sincere condolences of the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana upon the
3 occasion of the passing of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore.
4 WHEREAS, Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore was born on July 26, 1941, in Tyler, Texas,
5 and was reared in Franklin, Louisiana; and
6 WHEREAS, she was a longtime resident of Potomac, Maryland, and Baton Rouge,
7 Louisiana, the latter of which she affectionately considered her home; and
8 WHEREAS, Carolyn departed this life peacefully on April 16, 2026, at her residence
9 in Baton Rouge, surrounded by her loving husband and children; and
10 WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, Christopher Columbus Cherry
11 Jr. and Velda Ann McGarrahan Cherry; and
12 WHEREAS, Carolyn leaves to cherish her memory her devoted husband of over
13 sixty-three years, William Henson Moore III; her children, William Henson Moore IV and
14 his wife, Mary, Jennifer Lee Moore McGehee and her husband, Arthur, and Cherry Ann
15 Moore Duckworth and her husband, Theodore; her grandchildren, her step-grandchildren,
16 and her great-grandchildren; and
17 WHEREAS, she excelled academically and socially, graduating in 1959 as Franklin
18 High School's salutatorian, also serving as yearbook editor, a Homecoming Court member,

1 Drum Majorette, Most Beautiful Girl, and Miss Franklin High School; and
2 WHEREAS, Carolyn was selected as a delegate to Pelican Girls State, where she was
3 elected Commissioner of Agriculture, and in later years was invited to return as a counselor,
4 an especially momentous chapter in her life, as it was during this time that she met her future
5 husband, William Henson Moore III, who was also a counselor; and
6 WHEREAS, her accomplishments extended beyond school as she was crowned Miss
7 St. Mary Parish in 1958, Louisiana's Junior Miss in 1958, International Rice Queen for
8 1958–1959, and also earning runner-up in the 1960 Miss Baton Rouge Beauty Pageant and
9 the titles of Miss Capital City and Miss Holiday Queen; and
10 WHEREAS, Carolyn attended Louisiana State University, where she was elected to
11 represent the Junior Division as a freshman and went on to graduate in 1963 with a Bachelor
12 of Science degree in Education; and
13 WHEREAS, during her years at Louisiana State University, she demonstrated
14 outstanding school spirit and leadership, serving as a cheerleader for four consecutive years,
15 participating in several bowl games, and actively involved in campus life as a member of the
16 National L Club and Pi Beta Phi Sorority; and
17 WHEREAS, her many honors at LSU also included Darling of the LSU Court,
18 Gumbo Favorite, Rodeo Queen's Court, Sigma Chi Fraternity Court, LSU Centennial Court,
19 Bengal Belle of the Week, and serving as ROTC Sponsor for both the Air Force Squadron
20 and the Army ROTC Pershing Rifles Regiment; and
21 WHEREAS, Carolyn dedicated herself to church and community service, teaching
22 Sunday School at Trinity Episcopal Church, participating in the Junior League and the
23 Congressional Wives Club, and working as a part-time real estate agent in Maryland and
24 Baton Rouge, earning multiple recognitions as an Outstanding Sales Agent from the
25 Montgomery County, Maryland Board of Realtors; and
26 WHEREAS, Carolyn lovingly supported her husband's public service career while
27 raising her family and maintaining a warm, loving home; and
28 WHEREAS, her own commitment to public service was demonstrated through her
29 appointment by President George H. W. Bush as Director of the Presidential Scholars
30 Program in the United States Department of Education, where she served for four years and

1 was awarded a medal for exemplary service; and
2 WHEREAS, she continued her extensive civic and community involvement in Baton
3 Rouge through her involvement on the Louisiana Governor's Mansion Foundation Board,
4 membership in the Bells Jubilate and Trinity Episcopal Church Bell Ringer Choirs, and
5 numerous social organizations including the Bal Societe, Revelers, Morning Callers, serving
6 as President, Les Saison, and La Fiesta, and also as an active member of the Louisiana
7 Multiple Sclerosis Society, serving on its board; and
8 WHEREAS, throughout her life she excelled in all of her endeavors and had an
9 extraordinary gift for hospitality and service; and
10 WHEREAS, Carolyn was known for her servant's heart and unwavering devotion to
11 her family, whom she loved deeply and supported faithfully; and
12 WHEREAS, Carolyn's life was a testament to faith, service, and love, exemplified
13 by the Scripture Proverbs 31:28-29: "Her children rise up and call her blessed; her husband
14 also praises her. Many women have done excellently, but you surpass them all".
15 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
16 does hereby honor and celebrate the life and legacy of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore, giving
17 thanks for her many contributions to her family, church, and community.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that we extend our deepest sympathy and heartfelt
19 condolences to her husband, children, grandchildren, step-grandchildren,
20 great-grandchildren, and all who knew and loved her.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
22 family of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore.
