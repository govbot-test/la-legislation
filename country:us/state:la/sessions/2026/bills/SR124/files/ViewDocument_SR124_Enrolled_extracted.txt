SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 124
BY SENATOR FOIL
A RESOLUTION
To express the sincere condolences of the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana upon the
occasion of the passing of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore.
WHEREAS, Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore was born on July 26, 1941, in Tyler, Texas,
and was reared in Franklin, Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, she was a longtime resident of Potomac, Maryland, and Baton Rouge,
Louisiana, the latter of which she affectionately considered her home; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn departed this life peacefully on April 16, 2026, at her residence
in Baton Rouge, surrounded by her loving husband and children; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, Christopher Columbus Cherry
Jr. and Velda Ann McGarrahan Cherry; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn leaves to cherish her memory her devoted husband of over
sixty-three years, William Henson Moore III; her children, William Henson Moore IV and
his wife, Mary, Jennifer Lee Moore McGehee and her husband, Arthur, and Cherry Ann
Moore Duckworth and her husband, Theodore; her grandchildren, her step-grandchildren,
and her great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, she excelled academically and socially, graduating in 1959 as Franklin
High School's salutatorian, also serving as yearbook editor, a Homecoming Court member,
Drum Majorette, Most Beautiful Girl, and Miss Franklin High School; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn was selected as a delegate to Pelican Girls State, where she was
elected Commissioner of Agriculture, and in later years was invited to return as a counselor,
an especially momentous chapter in her life, as it was during this time that she met her future
husband, William Henson Moore III, who was also a counselor; and
WHEREAS, her accomplishments extended beyond school as she was crowned Miss
St. Mary Parish in 1958, Louisiana's Junior Miss in 1958, International Rice Queen for 1958-
1959, and also earning runner-up in the 1960 Miss Baton Rouge Beauty Pageant and the
titles of Miss Capital City and Miss Holiday Queen; and

WHEREAS, Carolyn attended Louisiana State University, where she was elected to
represent the Junior Division as a freshman and went on to graduate in 1963 with a Bachelor
of Science degree in Education; and
WHEREAS, during her years at Louisiana State University, she demonstrated
outstanding school spirit and leadership, serving as a cheerleader for four consecutive years,
participating in several bowl games, and actively involved in campus life as a member of the
National L Club and Pi Beta Phi Sorority; and
WHEREAS, her many honors at LSU also included Darling of the LSU Court,
Gumbo Favorite, Rodeo Queen's Court, Sigma Chi Fraternity Court, LSU Centennial Court,
Bengal Belle of the Week, and serving as ROTC Sponsor for both the Air Force Squadron
and the Army ROTC Pershing Rifles Regiment; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn dedicated herself to church and community service, teaching
Sunday School at Trinity Episcopal Church, participating in the Junior League and the
Congressional Wives Club, and working as a part-time real estate agent in Maryland and
Baton Rouge, earning multiple recognitions as an Outstanding Sales Agent from the
Montgomery County, Maryland Board of Realtors; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn lovingly supported her husband's public service career while
raising her family and maintaining a warm, loving home; and
WHEREAS, her own commitment to public service was demonstrated through her
appointment by President George H. W. Bush as Director of the Presidential Scholars
Program in the United States Department of Education, where she served for four years and
was awarded a medal for exemplary service; and
WHEREAS, she continued her extensive civic and community involvement in Baton
Rouge through her involvement on the Louisiana Governor's Mansion Foundation Board,
membership in the Bells Jubilate and Trinity Episcopal Church Bell Ringer Choirs, and
numerous social organizations including the Bal Societe, Revelers, Morning Callers, serving
as President, Les Saison, and La Fiesta, and also as an active member of the Louisiana
Multiple Sclerosis Society, serving on its board; and
WHEREAS, throughout her life she excelled in all of her endeavors and had an
extraordinary gift for hospitality and service; and

WHEREAS, Carolyn was known for her servant's heart and unwavering devotion to
her family, whom she loved deeply and supported faithfully; and
WHEREAS, Carolyn's life was a testament to faith, service, and love, exemplified
in Proverbs 31:28-29, "Her children rise up and call her blessed; her husband also praises
her. Many women have done excellently, but you surpass them all".
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby honor and celebrate the life and legacy of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore, giving
thanks for her many contributions to her family, church, and community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that we extend our deepest sympathy and heartfelt
condolences to her husband, children, grandchildren, step-grandchildren,
great-grandchildren, and all who knew and loved her.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
family of Carolyn Ann Cherry Moore.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
