HLS 26RS-982 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 251
BY REPRESENTATIVE CARVER
SCHOOLS: Requests the state Department of Education to study the feasibility of
incorporating recess into schedules for elementary school students in grades
kindergarten through three
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the state Department of Education to study the feasibility of
3 incorporating recess into schedules for elementary school students in grades
4 kindergarten through three.
5 WHEREAS, evidence from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and
6 other researchers shows multiple benefits that recess can provide to students from
7 elementary through high school: improved academic outcomes, social and emotional
8 learning, and boosts in overall well-being; and
9 WHEREAS, Louisiana does not require daily recess; R.S. 17:154.1 provides that the
10 minimum school day for grades one through twelve in each public school is required to
11 consist of three hundred sixty minutes of instructional time, exclusive of recess; and
12 WHEREAS, recess can be an essential component of the total educational experience
13 for children, providing unstructured play opportunities that allow students to engage in
14 physical activity and camaraderie with their peers.
15 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
16 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the state Department of Education to
17 study the feasibility of incorporating recess into schedules for elementary school students
18 in grades kindergarten through three.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in conducting the study, the department shall not
2 prioritize the potential for required recess at the expense of the importance of educating the

5 recommendations to the House Committee on Education no later than March 12, 2027.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
7 state superintendent of education.
