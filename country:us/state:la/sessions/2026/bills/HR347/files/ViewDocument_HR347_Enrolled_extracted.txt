HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 347
BY REPRESENTATIVE BRAUD
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to study the
feasibility of establishing uniform toll signage, toll dispute procedures, and toll
customer service centers at all toll facilities within a certain radius.
WHEREAS, there are three bridges with tolls in Louisiana, namely: the Belle Chasse
Bridge, the Louisiana 1 Highway Bridge, and the Lake Pontchartrain Causeway Bridge; and
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is under contract with the Plenary Group for the
construction and management of the Belle Chasse Bridge, utilizing tolls to assist in financing
the project; and
WHEREAS, multiple lawsuits, including but not limited to contractual and
constitutional claims, involving the parish of Plaquemines government, residents of
Plaquemines, the Department of Transportation and Development, and the Plenary Group
involve a multitude of issues with toll collections and operations; and
WHEREAS, residents were charged expensive and inconsistent administrative fees,
not advertised throughout the bridge's development, with no explanation for the varying
costs; and
WHEREAS, residents were not being given a fair chance to challenge these fees with
any sort of appeal process; and
WHEREAS, one resident and plaintiff in the suit stated that he had to pay $1,600 in
administrative fees and at one point, his account was billed a $25 administrative fee eighteen
times in one day; and
WHEREAS, in the period of tolling from May 2025 to October 2, 2025, $6,779,337
in fees and penalties were charged compared to $5,178,012.67 billed in actual toll charges;
and
WHEREAS, there are no toll fee signs to indicate actual toll rates and the cost of tolls
for those crossing the Belle Chasse Bridge; and

WHEREAS, there is only one customer service center in Golden Meadow for the
Belle Chasse toll bridge, which is over ninety miles away and a four hour round trip; and
WHEREAS, toll fees are impeding the ability of local businesses to earn a living; and
WHEREAS, toll and administrative fees are excessive and lack uniformity; and
WHEREAS, multiple small businesses, including but not limited to Salvo's Seafood,
have provided testimony at multiple Joint Transportation, Highways and Public Works
hearings; and
WHEREAS, testimony has included numerical and graphical proof that the toll rates,
fees, and penalties caused a significant decline in their sales; and
WHEREAS, the appeals process for challenging toll fees is inconsistent and difficult
for residents to navigate; and
WHEREAS, the lack of uniform toll signage, toll dispute procedures, and toll
customer service centers within a certain radius have all served to complicate the lives of
residents, business owners, and customers alike.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request that the Department of Transportation
and Development study the feasibility of establishing uniform toll signage, toll dispute
procedures, and toll customer service centers at all toll facilities within a certain radius.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Transportation and
Development shall submit a written report to the House Committee on Transportation,
Highways and Public Works, by February 1, 2027, detailing its findings on ways to establish
uniform toll signage, the most efficient procedures for toll disputes, and the benefits of
establishing toll customer service centers at all toll facilities within a close radius of the toll
bridge.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.
