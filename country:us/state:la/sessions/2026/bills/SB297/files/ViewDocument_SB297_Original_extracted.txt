SLS 26RS-484 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR DUPLESSIS
INSURANCE RATES. Provides a process for insurers to assert the right to prevent public
inspection of certain filing information based on a claim of trade secret. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 22:1464(D), relative to insurance rate filings; to provide for
3 transparency in rate filings; to provide for trade secret protection for insurance rate
4 filings; to provide for definitions; to provide for penalties; and to provide for related
5 matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 22:1464(D) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
8 §1464. Rate filing
9 * * *
10 D.(1) All rates, supplementary rate information, and any supporting
11 information filed pursuant to this Subpart shall be open to public inspection upon
12 expiration of the notification period as applicable pursuant to R.S. 22:1451, or upon
13 disapproval, except for information that is confidential, trade secret, or proprietary.
14 The determination of whether such information is in fact confidential, trade secret,
15 or proprietary shall be made by the commissioner in accordance with the
16 provisions of this Subsection.
17 (2)(a) If the commissioner receives a request for any information which has

3 request and the commissioner's determination of whether or not the information so
4 requested is subject to disclosure.
5 (b) Within ten days of receipt of the notification, the insurer or filer may

1 to be a trade secret shall be clearly marked as "trade secret".
2 (c) All material marked as a trade secret shall be separated from all non-
3 trade secret material and submitted in a separate envelope or document clearly
4 marked as "trade secret".
5 (d) In submitting a notice of trade secret to the commissioner, the
6 submitting party shall include a sworn affidavit that states the basis for the
7 claim that the submission qualifies as a trade secret and attests to the truth of
8 all of the following:

10 value and provides an advantage or an opportunity to obtain an advantage over
11 those who do not know or use it.
12 (ii) The affiant has taken measures to prevent the disclosure of the
13 information to anyone other than those who have been selected to have access
14 for limited purposes and the affiant continues to take those measures.
15 (iii) The information is and has not been reasonably obtainable by other
16 persons by use of legitimate means without the affiant's consent.
17 (iv) The information is not publicly available elsewhere, and the public
18 release of the information would cause actual, demonstrable harm to affiant.
19 (e) The submission shall include a summary prepared for the public
20 describing the nature of the submission claimed to be a trade secret without
21 disclosing the protected details of the submission.
22 (4) The following items shall not be considered a trade secret and shall
23 be subject to public disclosure:
24 (a) Any financial information, including data, models, or assumptions,
25 used in the calculation or justification for insurance rates.
26 (b) All transactions between an insurer and any affiliate including but
27 not limited to fees, commissions, payments, or profit-sharing agreements with
28 managing general agents, claims adjusters, reinsurers, or third-party
29 administrators.

1 (c) Office and employee compensation.
2 (d) Dividends paid to shareholders.
3 (e) Any information contained in rate filings.
4 (f) Any information used to support or oppose proposed legislation.
5 (g) The summary prepared in accordance with Subparagraph (3)(e) of
6 this Subsection.
7 (5) The commissioner shall review all claims of trade secret protection
8 submitted pursuant to this Section. If the commissioner determines that the
9 information does not meet the definition of a trade secret pursuant to this
10 Section or falls within the categories provided for in Paragraph (4) of this
11 Subsection, the claim shall be denied, and the commissioner shall issue a written
12 notice of denial to the submitting party.
13 (6) The commissioner shall, at least once every other year, contract with
14 an independent third-party entity with expertise in insurance regulation to
15 conduct an independent review of trade secret claims submitted pursuant to this
16 Subsection. The contract shall require submission of a final report to the
17 commissioner, the president of the Senate, and the speaker of the House of
18 Representatives no later than February 1 of the year following the year in which
19 a review is conducted identifying any trade secret claims the third-party entity
20 recommends be rejected by the commissioner.
21 (7) Any person who knowingly asserts a false claim of trade secret
22 protection in order to conceal unlawful financial practices shall be subject to a
23 fine imposed by the commissioner in an amount not to exceed twenty-five
24 thousand dollars per violation and suspension or revocation of any certificate
25 of authority or insurance license held by that person.

DIGEST
SB 297 Original 2026 Regular Session Duplessis
Present law provides that all rates, supplementary rate information, and any supporting
information filed with commissioner of insurance in support of a rate filing shall be open to
public inspection, except for information that is confidential, trade secret, or proprietary.
Further authorizes the commissioner to determine if the information claimed to be
confidential, trade secret, or proprietary is in fact confidential, trade secret, or proprietary.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires that the insurer claiming documents or information contain trade
secrets file with the commissioner a notice of request for nondisclosure on the basis of trade
secret protection. Further provides that failure to file the notice constitutes a waiver of a
claim of trade secrets.
Proposed law provides criteria to determine if a claim of nondisclosure is correct.
Proposed law requires the party submitting a notice of trade secret to the commissioner to
include a sworn affidavit that states the basis for the claim of trade secret.
Proposed law provides that certain information shall not be considered a trade secret.
Proposed law requires the commissioner to employ an independent third-party entity to
conduct an independent review of trade secret claims submitted at least once every other
year, and to submit a report to the commissioner, president of the Senate, and speaker of the
House of Representatives.
Proposed law provides penalties for knowingly asserting a false claim of trade secret
protection to conceal unlawful financial practices.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 22:1464(D))
