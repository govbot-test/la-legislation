SLS 26RS-1049 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 19
BY SENATOR MILLER
TRUSTS. Requests the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use of trusts with extended
durations and report its findings and any recommendations for revisions to the Louisiana
Trust Code and applicable Louisiana laws to the legislature.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use of trusts with
3 extended durations and to report its findings and any recommendations for revisions
4 to the Louisiana Trust Code and applicable Louisiana laws to the Legislature of
5 Louisiana.
6 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Trust Code establishes rules regarding the creation,
7 administration, and duration of trusts consistent with Louisiana's civil law tradition; and
8 WHEREAS, under existing Louisiana law, trusts are subject to limitations on
9 duration that have historically reflected the public policy of preventing the long-term
10 restriction of property and ensuring that assets remain capable of transfer and productive
11 economic use; and
12 WHEREAS, the administration of trusts and related fiduciary services has become
13 increasingly competitive, with many states adopting trust laws that allow extended durations
14 to promote the in-state management of trust assets; and
15 WHEREAS, as a result, Louisiana residents may elect to establish trusts under the
16 laws of other states to take advantage of such provisions, potentially leading to a loss of
17 trust-related business activity and associated assets within Louisiana; and
18 WHEREAS, there is a need to review the Louisiana Trust Code to ensure that its

1 provisions reflect current industry practices and provide sufficient flexibility to address the
2 increasingly complex estate planning needs of Louisiana individuals and families who utilize
3 trusts; and
4 WHEREAS, potential revisions may also raise important policy considerations,
5 including the long-term concentration of wealth, reduced circulation of property interests,
6 impacts on taxation and creditor rights, increased litigation, administrative complexity, and
7 questions concerning compatibility with Louisiana's civil law tradition; and
8 WHEREAS, a comprehensive and balanced study would assist the legislature in
9 evaluating whether modifications to Louisiana laws governing trust duration and vesting
10 requirements may be appropriate and in understanding both the opportunities and policy
11 implications of such changes.
12 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
13 urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use of trusts with extended
14 durations and to report its findings and any recommendations for revisions to the Louisiana
15 Trust Code and applicable Louisiana laws to the legislature.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute report its
17 findings and recommendations to the legislature on or before March 1, 2027.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
19 director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
