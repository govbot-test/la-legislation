HB 330 Original 2026 Regular Session Bayham
Abstract: Increases penalties and adds a restitution clause to the crimes of desecration of
graves (R.S. 14:101) and institutional vandalism (R.S. 14:225).
Present law provides a penalty of not more than a $500 fine and up to six months
imprisonment for desecration of graves.
Proposed law increases the fine for desecration of graves from not more than $500 to not
more than $1,000 and requires the offender to make restitution for damages.
Present law provides the following penalties for institutional vandalism:
(1) When damage is less than $500, a fine of up to $500 or up to six months
imprisonment, or both.
(2) When damage is more than $500 but less than $50,000, a fine of not more than
$1,000 or imprisonment with or without hard labor not more than two years.
Proposed law increases the fine for damage less than $500 from $500 to $1,000.
Proposed law increases the fine for damage between $500 and $50,000 from not more than
$1,000 to not less than $1,000 nor more than $5,000.
Proposed law requires the offender to make restitution when institutional vandalism is
committed against a religious building.
(Amends R.S. 14:101(B) and 225(B)(1) and (2); Adds R.S. 14:225(D))
