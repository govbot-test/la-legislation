HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 116
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
COMMENDATIONS: Commends Lane "Frenchie" Boudreaux for extraordinary heroism
and self-sacrifice during the Vietnam War
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend Lane "Frenchie" Boudreaux for his extraordinary heroism and self-sacrifice
3 during the Vietnam War and to honor his military service in the spirit of Memorial
4 Day.
5 WHEREAS, on March 19, 1969, United States Army Sergeant First Class James H.
6 Moore, who served as Heavy Weapons Infantry Advisor with Mobile Advisory Team I-12,
7 was killed in action near the District Headquarters in Dien Ban District while engaged in
8 combat against a well-fortified North Vietnamese Army force; and
9 WHEREAS, after learning that Sergeant Moore's body had been left behind, Marine
10 Sergeant David Carpenter, Corporal Lane "Frenchie" Boudreaux, Corporal H.T. Berryhill,
11 Corporal James T. Polich, and other members of Mobile Training Team II made the
12 voluntary decision to re-enter the village while under heavy fire to recover their fallen
13 comrade; and
14 WHEREAS, Corporal Boudreaux, who was described by his teammates as the
15 strongest member of the unit, carried the body of the much larger Sergeant Moore in a
16 fireman's carry while under continuous enemy fire and refused to leave him behind; and
17 WHEREAS, this extraordinary act of bravery and devotion to the sacred principle
18 that no comrade in arms is ever abandoned reflects the highest traditions of the United States
19 Marine Corps and the United States military and is emblematic of the Marine Corps ethos
20 that "no one is left behind"; and

1 WHEREAS, Sergeant First Class Moore was posthumously awarded the Silver Star
2 for his gallantry that day, and the Marines involved, including Corporal Boudreaux, were
3 recognized by their chain of command as having performed actions worthy of the highest
4 decorations; and
5 WHEREAS, Memorial Day is a solemn time to remember the fallen, honor those
6 who served, and recommit ourselves to the values of courage, sacrifice, and brotherhood that
7 define our nation's defenders; and
8 WHEREAS, Lane Boudreaux is a living hero of that day and continues to embody
9 those values more than fifty-seven years later.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
11 commend Lane "Frenchie" Boudreaux for his heroism and self-sacrifice on March 19, 1969,
12 in recovering the body of Sergeant First Class James H. Moore under enemy fire, and does
13 hereby express enduring appreciation for his dedicated service in defense of American
14 liberties.
