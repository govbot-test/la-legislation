HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 115
BY REPRESENTATIVE COATES
Day at the state capitol
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tangipahoa Parish Fair Day at the state capitol,
3 to recognize Tangipahoa Parish for its enduring legacy since 1888, and to commend
4 the Tangipahoa Parish Fair for its historical, agricultural, and economic significance
5 to the city of Amite.
6 WHEREAS, the Tangipahoa Parish Fair was first held one hundred thirty-eight years
7 ago on seventy-five acres in Amite; the fair is the oldest such event in Louisiana and was
8 created to celebrate local agriculture, promote student involvement, and preserve parish
9 culture; and
10 WHEREAS, the growth of the Tangipahoa Parish Fair is largely attributed to local
11 families who pass traditions down to younger generations and preserve the importance of
12 agriculture; and
13 WHEREAS, the Tangipahoa Parish Fair has evolved into a five-day celebration and
14 fall tradition that attracts residents and visitors alike; it features a wide array of activities,
15 including the display of hundreds of student 4-H entries, a children's livestock show, parish
16 school booths, live musical entertainment, a cheerleading and dance competition showcasing
17 local students, a children's school field trip day, a pioneer town, a carnival, a parade, vendor
18 exhibits, and community gatherings; and
19 WHEREAS, a hallmark of the fair is its annual pageant, which has crowned a fair
20 queen each year since 1951, and many local families proudly pass down participation in the
21 royal court through multiple generations; and

1 WHEREAS, the 2026 Miss Tangipahoa Parish Fair Queen, Maree Mackynzie
2 Mannino, is honored to be this year's ambassador of the fair; and
3 WHEREAS, the 2026 Grand Marshall, Kelly Wells, was selected for his love and
4 dedication to the fair and to Tangipahoa Parish; and
5 WHEREAS, the 2026 royalty is made up of parish students Harper Thornton, Joleigh
6 Thibodeaux, Karley Cavalier, Hayden Williams, and Molly Lott; they are honored to be
7 dedicated representatives for the fair; and
8 WHEREAS, the 2026 President, Wendy Carrier, has demonstrated leadership and
9 direction for a successful fair; and
10 WHEREAS, the Tangipahoa Parish Fair serves as a celebration of the region's local
11 culture and heritage, while honoring the families and traditions that helped shape the
12 community; and
13 WHEREAS, the fair continues to play a significant role in fostering community
14 pride, preserving local history, and promoting student involvement and economic activity
15 in Tangipahoa Parish; and
16 WHEREAS, the one hundred thirty-eighth Tangipahoa Parish Fair will be celebrated
17 September 30 through October 4, 2026, in Amite, Louisiana.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
19 designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tangipahoa Parish Fair Day at the state capitol;
20 does hereby commend the Tangipahoa Parish Fair for its historical, agricultural, and
21 economic significance to the city of Amite; does hereby commemorate the one hundred
22 thirty-eighth anniversary of the Tangipahoa Parish Fair; and does hereby recognize its
23 distinction as the oldest fair in the state.
