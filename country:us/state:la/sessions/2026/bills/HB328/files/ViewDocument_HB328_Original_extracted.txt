HB 328 Original 2026 Regular Session Marcelle
Abstract: Prohibits the carrying of firearms in college or university dormitories.
Present law provides for the crime of carrying a firearm or dangerous weapon by a student
or nonstudent on school property, at school-sponsored functions, or in a firearm-free zone
and provides for penalties.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law lists exceptions to this criminal offense and provides that three of these
exceptions are as follows:
(1) Any student carrying a firearm to or from a class, in which he is duly enrolled, that
requires the use of the firearm in the class.
(2) A student enrolled or participating in an activity requiring the use of a firearm
including but not limited to any ROTC function under the authorization of a
university.
(3) A student who possesses a firearm in his dormitory room or while going to or from
his vehicle or any other person with permission of the administration.
Relative to the present law exceptions of carrying a firearm to or from a class that requires
the use of the firearm in that class and activities that require the use of a firearm under the
authorization of a university, proposed law prohibits the possession of such firearm in the
dormitory room of that student.
Proposed law further amends present law to remove the dormitory room of a student as a
location where possession of a firearm is permitted as an exception to the present law crime.
(Amends R.S. 14:95.2(C)(intro. para.) and (6)-(8))
