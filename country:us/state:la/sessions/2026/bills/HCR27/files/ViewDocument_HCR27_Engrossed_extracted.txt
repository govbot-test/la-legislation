HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 27
BY REPRESENTATIVE SPELL
Department of Education to analyze the adequacy of autism support services in the
state
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health and the state Department of
3 Education, in collaboration with the Louisiana Chapter of the American Academy
4 of Pediatrics, to develop and complete a comprehensive report analyzing the current
5 breadth, quality, and adequacy of autism support services in Louisiana.
6 WHEREAS, autism spectrum disorder affects a significant number of individuals and
7 families in Louisiana and requires a range of specialized support services across health,
8 education, and community sectors; and
9 WHEREAS, accurate, timely, and consistent diagnosis of autism spectrum disorder
10 is the essential gateway to early intervention and appropriate support services; and
11 WHEREAS, clinical assessments and diagnostic evaluations for autism spectrum
12 disorder are performed by multiple provider types, including pediatric primary care
13 providers, developmental-behavioral pediatricians, child psychologists, child psychiatrists,
14 pediatric neurologists, and school psychologists; and
15 WHEREAS, variations in tools, protocols, training, and standards across such fields
16 may impact diagnostic accuracy, consistency, timeliness, and equity of access to services;
17 and
18 WHEREAS, the Trump administration's Make America Healthy Again Commission
19 issued the Make Our Children Healthy Again Assessment; it brings critical attention to the
20 rising prevalence of autism spectrum disorder in the United States, highlighting it as part of
21 an unprecedented childhood chronic health crisis and calling for urgent action to address root
22 causes and improve outcomes for affected children; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-651 ENGROSSED
HCR NO. 27
1 WHEREAS, following the federal government's leadership in prioritizing the
2 assessment and reversal of chronic childhood conditions, including autism spectrum
3 disorder, it is necessary to undergo a thorough evaluation of Louisiana's autism support
4 services to ensure alignment with best practices and to address any deficiencies; and
5 WHEREAS, collaboration between the Louisiana Department of Health, the state
6 Department of Education, and the Louisiana Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics
7 is critical to address the multifaceted needs of children with autism.
8 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
9 urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health and the state Department of Education,
10 in collaboration with the Louisiana Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics, to
11 develop and complete a comprehensive report analyzing the current breadth, quality, and
12 adequacy of autism support services in Louisiana.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall include a comprehensive review
14 and evaluation of existing clinical assessment tools, diagnostic protocols, and evaluation
15 processes for autism spectrum disorder across all provider types including but not limited
16 to pediatricians, developmental-behavioral pediatricians, child psychologists, child
17 psychiatrists, pediatric neurologists, and school psychologists.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall include an assessment of
19 standardization; training requirements; inter-rater reliability; adherence to current
20 evidence-based guidelines, such as those from the American Academy of Pediatrics and
21 DSM-5-TR; wait times; cultural and linguistic competency; access barriers; consistency of
22 application; and overall diagnostic quality and equity.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall include a comparison of
24 Louisiana's autism support services and diagnostic practices to those in other states.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall identify system and service gaps;
26 service utilization trends; reimbursement inadequacies; workforce gaps; clinical quality
27 concerns, including diagnostic practices; fraud concerns; and any other factors deemed
28 relevant by the Louisiana Department of Health, the state Department of Education, and the
29 Louisiana Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, in recognition of the Make Our Children
2 Healthy Again Assessment's critical spotlight on autism spectrum disorder, and following
3 the federal government's leadership on this issue, the report shall inform Louisiana's efforts
4 to improve autism support services and outcomes for children and families in the state.
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall be submitted to the legislature
6 no later than January 1, 2027.
DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part

HCR 27 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Spell
Requests the La. Dept. of Health and the state Dept. of Education, in collaboration with the
La. Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics, to develop and complete a
comprehensive report analyzing the current breadth, quality, and adequacy of autism support
services in La., and to submit the report to the legislature by Jan. 1, 2027.
