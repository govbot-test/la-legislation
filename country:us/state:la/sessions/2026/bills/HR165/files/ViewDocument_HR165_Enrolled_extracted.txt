2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 165
BY REPRESENTATIVE JORDAN
A RESOLUTION
Lawrence.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on April 13,
2026, of Ray Helen Lawrence; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence was the first African-American woman appointed and
then elected to the Port Allen City Council; in 2013, she served as the city's mayor pro
tempore; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence served Port Allen for twenty years, and her service was
a testament to the trust placed in her by the community; one of her proudest achievements
was the establishment of the city's annual Juneteenth celebration; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence extended her service beyond the council chambers; she
led volunteers at Port Allen Cemetery, where residents mowed, picked up litter, pressure
washed, and painted abandoned tombs; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her time in public office, Ms. Lawrence dedicated more
than thirty years of service educating students across West Baton Rouge Parish; she was a
science teacher who brought the curriculum to life and arranged field trips to the John C.
Stennis Space Center in Mississippi; and
WHEREAS, Ray Helen Lawrence made an indelible impact on the Port Allen
community, and though she will be deeply missed, her legacy of love and service to the city,
its residents, and her students shall live on in perpetuity in the hearts and minds of those who
were privileged to have known and loved her.

HR NO. 165 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Ray
Helen Lawrence; does hereby extend enduring appreciation for her outstanding contributions
to the city of Port Allen as public office holder, educator, and community servant; and does
hereby extend sincere wishes that her family, friends, and loved ones find comfort in their
shared memories of her beautiful life.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
