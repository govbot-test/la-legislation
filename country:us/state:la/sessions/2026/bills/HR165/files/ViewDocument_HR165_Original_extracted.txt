HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 165
BY REPRESENTATIVE JORDAN
CONDOLENCES: Expresses the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death
of Ray Helen Lawrence
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Ray Helen
3 Lawrence.
4 WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
5 Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on April 13,
6 2026, of Ray Helen Lawrence; and
7 WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence was the first African-American woman appointed and
8 then elected to the Port Allen City Council; in 2013, she served as the city's mayor pro
9 tempore; and
10 WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence served Port Allen for twenty years, and her service was
11 a testament to the trust placed in her by the community; one of her proudest achievements
12 was the establishment of the city's annual Juneteenth celebration; and
13 WHEREAS, Ms. Lawrence extended her service beyond the council chambers; she
14 led volunteers at Port Allen Cemetery, where residents mowed, picked up litter, pressure
15 washed, and painted abandoned tombs; and
16 WHEREAS, in addition to her time in public office, Ms. Lawrence dedicated more
17 than thirty years of service educating students across West Baton Rouge Parish; she was a
18 science teacher who brought the curriculum to life and arranged field trips to the John C.
19 Stennis Space Center in Mississippi; and
20 WHEREAS, Ray Helen Lawrence made an indelible impact on the Port Allen
21 community, and though she will be deeply missed, her legacy of love and service to the city,
