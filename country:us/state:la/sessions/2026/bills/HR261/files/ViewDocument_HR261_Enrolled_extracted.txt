2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 261
BY REPRESENTATIVE DANA HENRY
A RESOLUTION
To designate Tuesday, May 12, 2026, as A Day Without Child Care at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, early childhood education (ECE) represents education from birth to
kindergarten; it is the workforce behind the workforce and supports the employment of tens
of thousands of working Louisiana parents; and
WHEREAS, ECE is significantly underfunded, which leaves many families unable
to access necessary care and causes some early childhood educators to leave the field
because they cannot earn a livable wage; and
WHEREAS, the lack of ECE leaves families, providers, and businesses vulnerable;
childcare disruptions cost Louisiana's economy $1.3 billion each year; and
WHEREAS, For Providers By Providers (4PXP) is Louisiana's only provider-led
ECE organization and leads the statewide coordination of the National Day Without Child
Care to elevate the voices of educators, families, and providers; and
WHEREAS, early childhood educators, parents, families, and caregivers have been
fighting for generations to achieve equitable access to affordable child care, better pay, and
working conditions for providers; and
WHEREAS, fewer than half of child care providers offer paid sick leave to staff, and
fewer than one-third provide health insurance or retirement benefits, which highlights the
ongoing challenges in ensuring a stable and supported early childhood workforce; and
WHEREAS, nearly half of the childcare providers who lost staff in 2024 reported
that departing employees left for jobs offering higher wages, and lower-paying providers
experienced staff turnover at higher rates than those able to offer more competitive
compensation; and

WHEREAS, most child care providers report increased staffing costs or challenges,
including the need to pay higher wages and hire less experienced staff; these providers also
experience significant turnover because new staff leave within six months of starting; and
WHEREAS, early childhood educators earn an average of $10.63 per hour in
Louisiana, which makes them among the lowest-paid professionals in the education sector,
despite the critical role they play in supporting children's development and learning during
the most formative years; and
WHEREAS, many early childhood teachers qualify for public assistance programs,
which highlights the disconnect between the value of their work and the compensation they
receive; and
WHEREAS, inadequate pay contributes to high turnover rates among early childhood
educators and disrupts the stability and quality of care that young children need to thrive;
and
WHEREAS, low wages make it difficult to recruit and retain skilled and passionate
professionals in the early childhood education workforce, which places an additional strain
on child care providers and families; and
WHEREAS, the lack of accessible high-quality early childhood education hinders
Louisiana's capacity to attract and keep young professionals, which results in a loss of talent
in the workforce, particularly in vital sectors like healthcare and education; and
WHEREAS, on May 12, 2026, child care providers, teachers, families, and
community members across Louisiana are joining the National Day Without Child Care to
raise awareness about the urgent need for investment in early childhood education and to
advocate for a fully funded child care system that powers the economy and supports working
families; and
WHEREAS, investment in early childhood education plays a critical role in
supporting Louisiana's working families, strengthening businesses, growing the state's
economy, building a more prosperous future for all communities, and ensuring every child
has a strong start in life; and
WHEREAS, early childhood education is a foundational part of Louisiana's
economic infrastructure and is as essential as roads, electricity, water, and broadband; it
must be prioritized and funded accordingly to maximize the state's potential for growth.

THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Tuesday, May 12, 2026, as A Day Without
Child Care Day at the state capitol and does hereby express enduring appreciation for the
outstanding contributions of the state's early childhood education providers.
