HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 53
BY REPRESENTATIVE BAYHAM
COMMENDATIONS: Commends decorated veteran and centenarian James "Jim" Bollich
for his military service in the United States Army Air Corps on the occasion of
Louisiana America 250
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend decorated veteran and centenarian James "Jim" Bollich for his military service
3 in the United States Army Air Corps on the occasion of Louisiana America 250.
4 WHEREAS, James "Jim" Bollich was born on August 15, 1921, near Eunice,
5 Louisiana; he enlisted in the U.S. Army Air Corps in 1940 and was stationed in the
6 Philippines three weeks before the attack on Pearl Harbor; and
7 WHEREAS, Mr. Bollich fought in the Battle of Bataan on the Bataan Peninsula in
8 Central Luzon, Philippines, in 1942; following the fall of Bataan, he and his fellow troops
9 endured the infamous Bataan Death March, a sixty-five mile forced march in brutal
10 conditions that resulted in the deaths of roughly twelve thousand American and Filipino
11 soldiers; and
12 WHEREAS, after the march, Mr. Bollich was held for nearly four and a half years
13 as a prisoner of war in Manchuria; he was released after the camp was liberated on his
14 twenty-fourth birthday on August 15, 1945; and
15 WHEREAS, Mr. Bollich is a recipient of the Purple Heart, the Bronze Star, the
16 Congressional Gold Medal, and numerous other military honors; he is a Fulbright Scholar
17 and the author of twelve books, including Bataan Death March: A Soldier's Story; and
18 WHEREAS, Mr. Bollich is currently one hundred four years old and resides in
19 Lafayette, Louisiana; he is believed to be one of the last surviving United States veterans
20 who endured the Bataan Death March; and

1 WHEREAS, James "Jim" Bollich is a true American hero who is most deserving of
2 the highest recognition for the inspiring degree of perseverance he displayed while defending

4 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
5 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend decorated veteran and centenarian James
6 "Jim" Bollich for his military service in the United States Army Air Corps on the occasion
7 of Louisiana America 250 and does hereby express enduring appreciation to Mr. Bollich and
8 all American veterans for their unrelenting dedication to the preservation of liberty.
