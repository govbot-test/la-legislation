4 §82.2. Transfer
5 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a manufacturing
6 distillery that operates a distilling facility located entirely within this state and that

23 municipal sales and excise taxes to the proper tax collecting authority for all products

HB 1069 Original 2026 Regular Session Berault
Abstract: Provides relative to shipping to consumers from local distillers.
Proposed law defines "distilling facility" as a licensed premises of a manufacturing distillery
where liquor is manufactured for the primary purpose of selling and that is authorized to sell
the finished product.
Proposed law defines "premises", "licensed premises" and "premises to be licensed" as the
building or that part of the building where alcoholic beverages having an alcoholic content
of more than 6% by volume are sold. Includes the outside area of a building if alcoholic
beverages are regularly sold outside of the building.
Proposed law authorizes a manufacturer to use a wholesaler to transfer liquor manufactured
at a distilling facility to another distilling facility in the state of La. to sell or serve to the
public for consumption on or off the licensed premises if the manufacturer manufactures no
less than 4,000 gallons of liquor annually; the manufacturer limits the amount of products
sold to the public for on or off the licensed premises consumption; the quantity of liquor
transferred does not exceed an amount greater than 50% of the distilling facility's production
of liquor for the previous month; and all state and parish or municipal sales and excise taxes
are paid to the proper tax collecting authority.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 26:2(38) and (39) and 82.2)
