SLS 26RS-3353 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 78
BY SENATOR OWEN
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH. Recognizes Friday, May 29, 2026, as Bonfouca Museum
and Research Center Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate Friday, May 29, 2026, as Bonfouca Museum and Research Center Day at the
3 Louisiana State Capitol, to recognize the dedication of the Bonfouca Museum and
4 Research Center by the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe, and to express sincere
5 appreciation to the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe for the preservation of the
6 indigenous history of the tribal nation in historic Bonfouca for the cultural
7 enrichment and civic pride of Louisiana citizens.
8 WHEREAS, the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe will dedicate the Bonfouca
9 Museum and Research Center at the historic train depot located in Slidell, St. Tammany
10 Parish, Louisiana on Friday, May 29, 2026; and
11 WHEREAS, the museum, one of the oldest documented locations in Louisiana and
12 West Florida, will preserve and promote the artifacts and history of Bonfouca; and
13 WHEREAS, the museum will provide a dedicated space to showcase the history and
14 related American Indian artifacts for the purposes of education, cultural enrichment, tourism,
15 economic development, and community representation; and
16 WHEREAS, the Bonfouca Museum and Research Center will be beneficial to
17 Louisiana residents, newcomers, students, and tourist to the area; and
18 WHEREAS, the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe are descendants of the mound

1 builders; and
2 WHEREAS, the ancestors of the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe are buried in
3 traditional cemeteries throughout the parish of St. Tammany; and
4 WHEREAS, the Chahta Tribe fought for the United States of America under
5 President Andrew Jackson, including in the Battle of New Orleans and throughout West
6 Florida; and
7 WHEREAS, in 1830, the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe were the earliest Native
8 Americans to become United States citizens, with dual citizenship, ratified under Article 14
9 of the Treaty of Dancing Rabbit Creek; and
10 WHEREAS, President Andrew Jackson gave special provisions to the hereditary
11 chief of the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe, his heirs, and assigns who did not have to
12 relocate to Indian territory in Oklahoma.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 recognize Friday, May 29, 2026, as Bonfouca Museum and Research Center Day at the
15 Louisiana State Capitol, recognize the dedication of the Bonfouca Museum and Research
16 Center by the Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe, and does express sincere appreciation to the
17 Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe for the preservation of the indigenous history of the tribal
18 nation in historic Bonfouca for the cultural enrichment and civic pride of Louisiana citizens.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
20 Tchefuncta Nation, Chahta Tribe.
