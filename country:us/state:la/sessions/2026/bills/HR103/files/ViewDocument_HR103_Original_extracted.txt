2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 103
BY REPRESENTATIVES WALTERS AND MARCELLE
Incorporated, Day at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, April 8, 2026, as Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, Day
3 at the state capitol.
4 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, was founded on January 13,
5 1913, by twenty-two collegiate women at Howard University who sought to use their
6 collective strength to promote academic excellence, provide scholarships and support to
7 those in need, educate and stimulate participation in the establishment of positive public
8 policy, and address issues affecting their communities; and
9 WHEREAS, since its founding, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, has been
10 committed to civic engagement and public service, including historic efforts to promote
11 voter education, civil rights, and participation in the democratic process, while encouraging
12 leadership and advocacy in communities across the nation; and
13 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, was incorporated in 1930 and
14 is a private, nonprofit organization that provides assistance and support through established
15 programs in local communities and throughout the world; and
16 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, is the largest African
17 American Greek-letter sorority, a sisterhood of more than three hundred fifty thousand
18 predominantly African American college-educated women, comprised of more than one
19 thousand fifty chapters worldwide; and
20 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, maintains a strong presence
21 throughout the state of Louisiana, with numerous alumnae and collegiate chapters actively

1 serving their communities through programs focused on education, health, economic
2 empowerment, and civic engagement; and
3 WHEREAS, the members of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, stand on the
4 shoulders of giants whose leadership has left an enduring imprint on the history of this state;
5 these members include former state representatives Renee Gill Pratt, Rosalind Peychaud,
6 Jalila Jefferson Bullock, and the late Pinkie Carolyn Wilkerson; former state senators Diana
7 E. Bajoie, Karen Carter Peterson, and Cheryl Gray Evans; and Louisiana Senate Secretary
8 Yolanda J. Dixon; and
9 WHEREAS, to uplift, encourage, and develop the global community, Delta Sigma
10 Theta Sorority, Incorporated, focuses its efforts on the Five-Point Programmatic Thrust of
11 economic development, educational development, international awareness and involvement,
12 physical and mental health, and political awareness and involvement; and
13 WHEREAS, the organizational structure and governance of Delta Sigma Theta
14 Sorority, Incorporated, are vested in the Grand Chapter, which meets at a biennial national
15 convention and regional conferences held in the organization's seven geographic regions
16 during nonconvention years; and
17 WHEREAS, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, emphasizes its commitment
18 to community service and academic achievement, as well as its quest for excellence in all
19 endeavors, through the success of its public service programs; its members are truly
20 deserving of the highest recognition for their outstanding contributions to Louisiana and
21 beyond.
22 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
23 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 8, 2026, as Delta Sigma
24 Theta Sorority, Incorporated, Day at the state capitol; does hereby commend and recognize
25 the women of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Incorporated, for their exceptional achievements
26 and manifold contributions; and does hereby extend enduring appreciation to the
27 organization and its members for the tremendous pride and honor they bring to their local
28 communities, the state of Louisiana, and the United States of America.
