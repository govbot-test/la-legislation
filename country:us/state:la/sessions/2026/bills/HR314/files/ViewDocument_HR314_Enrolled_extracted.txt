Environment prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session of the Legislature.
WHEREAS, Louisiana has enacted a comprehensive policy for the reclamation and
conservation of areas impacted by the mining of lignite and other forms of coal; and
WHEREAS, proper reclamation of surface mined lands is necessary to prevent
unreasonable degradation of land and water resources that would be detrimental to the
general welfare, health, safety, and property rights of the citizens of this state; and
WHEREAS, regulation of the surface mining of gravel is in the public interest and
will benefit the public health, safety, welfare, and economy of the state of Louisiana,
including preventing the destruction or diminishment of the utility of land for commercial,
industrial, residential, recreational, agricultural, and forestry purposes by preventing
unreasonable erosion, landslides, floods, water pollution, destruction of fish and wildlife
habitat and impairment of natural beauty, and will benefit governmental programs and
efforts to conserve soil, water, and other natural resources.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Conservation and
Energy to study the enactment of gravel surface mining reclamation and conservation laws
and to submit a written report of its findings to the House Committee on Natural Resources
and Environment prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session of the Legislature.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the study should consider the need for a statutory
process for identifying planned post-mining land use, removal of equipment and facilities,
grading or filling the site, and revegetation of the area as well as the need for setbacks from
residential areas, roads, and rivers.
