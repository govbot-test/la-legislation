HLS 26RS-4718 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 314
BY REPRESENTATIVE ROBBY CARTER
NATURAL RESOURCES DEPT: Urges and requests the Department of Conservation and
Energy to study the implementation of gravel surface mining reclamation and
conservation
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Conservation and Energy to study the need for
3 enactment of gravel surface mining reclamation and conservation laws and to submit
4 a written report of its findings to the House Committee on Natural Resources and
5 Environment prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session of the Legislature.
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana has enacted a comprehensive policy for the reclamation and
7 conservation of areas impacted by the mining of lignite and other forms of coal; and
8 WHEREAS, proper reclamation of surface mined lands is necessary to prevent
9 unreasonable degradation of land and water resources that would be detrimental to the
10 general welfare, health, safety, and property rights of the citizens of this state; and
11 WHEREAS, regulation of the surface mining of gravel is in the public interest and
12 will benefit the public health, safety, welfare, and economy of the state of Louisiana,
13 including preventing the destruction or diminishment of the utility of land for commercial,
14 industrial, residential, recreational, agricultural, and forestry purposes by preventing
15 unreasonable erosion, landslides, floods, water pollution, destruction of fish and wildlife
16 habitat and impairment of natural beauty, and will benefit governmental programs and
17 efforts to conserve soil, water, and other natural resources.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
19 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Conservation and
20 Energy to study the enactment of gravel surface mining reclamation and conservation laws
