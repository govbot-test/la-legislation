HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 61
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
constitutionality of expropriation as it pertains to carbon capture and sequestration
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To authorize and direct the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the Louisiana Supreme
3 Court decision in Plaquemines Port Harbor and Terminal Dist. v. Nguyen, No.
4 2025-C-00827 (La. 3/6/26), and its implications for the Louisiana Geologic
5 Sequestration of Carbon Dioxide Act, as enacted by Act No. 517 of the 2009 Regular
6 Session and amended by Act No. 61 of the 2020 Regular Session.
7 WHEREAS, on March 6, 2026, the Louisiana Supreme Court issued its opinion in
8 Plaquemines Port Harbor and Terminal Dist. v. Nguyen, No. 2025-C-00827 (La. 3/6/26),
9 holding that a public port authority's expropriation of private property for the purpose of
10 leasing that property exclusively for a private company's predominant use is unconstitutional
11 because the taking does not satisfy the "public purpose" requirement of Article I, Section 4
12 of the Constitution of Louisiana; and
13 WHEREAS, the court emphasized that the 2006 amendment to Article I, Section 4
14 strictly limits the definition of "public purpose" for eminent domain and prohibits takings
15 that result in predominant private use or benefit even when a public entity retains nominal
16 ownership; and
17 WHEREAS, in 2009, the legislature enacted Act No. 517, the Louisiana Geologic
18 Sequestration of Carbon Dioxide Act, which added R.S. 19:2(12) to grant private
19 corporations and partnerships the power of expropriation for the underground storage of
20 carbon dioxide and created a comprehensive statutory framework authorizing private entities
21 to exercise eminent domain for commercial carbon sequestration projects; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-2511 ORIGINAL
HCR NO. 61
1 WHEREAS, in 2020, the legislature enacted Act No. 61, which further amended the
2 2009 Act by refining definitions, procedures, and the exercise of eminent domain for carbon
3 dioxide storage facilities; and
4 WHEREAS, in 2024, the legislature enacted Act No. 620 which changed R.S.
5 19:2(12) to 19:2(11).
6 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Supreme Court's ruling in Nguyen raises serious questions
7 about whether the eminent domain provisions contained in the 2009 and 2020 Acts remain
8 constitutionally valid in light of the strict "public purpose" limitations imposed by Article
9 I, Section 4; and
10 WHEREAS, continued uncertainty surrounding these provisions may expose the
11 state, political subdivisions, and private parties to protracted litigation, increased costs,
12 regulatory instability, and potential societal disruption in the rapidly evolving carbon
13 capture, utilization, and storage industry.
14 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
15 Legislature of Louisiana, the Senate concurring, does hereby direct the Louisiana State Law
16 Institute to conduct a comprehensive study of the Plaquemines Port Harbor and Terminal
17 Dist. v. Nguyen decision and its impact on the Louisiana Geologic Sequestration of Carbon
18 Dioxide Act (R.S. 30:1101 et seq.) as enacted in 2009 and amended in 2020.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute shall
20 specifically examine whether the eminent domain and expropriation authority granted to
21 private entities under those Acts is consistent with the current requirements of Article I,
22 Section 4 of the Constitution of Louisiana, and shall evaluate the advisability of repealing,
23 amending, or replacing those provisions to eliminate constitutional vulnerabilities, prevent
24 future litigation, and protect public safety, property rights, and economic development.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute shall submit
26 a written report of its findings, conclusions, and any recommended legislation to the House
27 Committee on Civil Law and Procedure and the Senate Committee on Judiciary A no later
28 than February 1, 2027.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
30 director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
