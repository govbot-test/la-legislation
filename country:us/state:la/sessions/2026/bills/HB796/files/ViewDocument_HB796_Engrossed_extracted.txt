HB 796 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Stagni
Abstract: Provides relative to the Louisiana Board of Chiropractic Examiners definitions
for preceptor and preceptee and their duties and responsibilities.
Present law defines board, chiropractor duties, and the practice of chiropractic.
Proposed law adds definitions for preceptor and preceptee.
Proposed law provides for preceptors to delegate responsibilities and tasks to a preceptee,
requires a preceptor to be licensed and in good standing in this state or another jurisdiction
for five years, and be licensed by the Louisiana Board of Chiropractic Examiners for at least
one year.
Proposed law prohibits preceptors from delegating responsibility to render a diagnosis or
prescribe a treatment plan to a preceptee and prohibits preceptees performing adjustments
or manipulation in accordance with the proposed law from presenting to the public that the
preceptee is a licensed chiropractor.
Proposed law provides for preceptees to perform adjustments or manipulation while a
preceptor is physically onsite and subjects preceptors and preceptees to disciplinary actions
for any violations of the proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 37:2801(4) and (5) and 2807)
