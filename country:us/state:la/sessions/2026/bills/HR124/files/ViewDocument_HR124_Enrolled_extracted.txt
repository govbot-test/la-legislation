HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 124
BY REPRESENTATIVE SPELL
A RESOLUTION
To designate Monday, April 13, 2026, as Social Work Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, social workers positively impact the lives of millions of Americans on
a daily basis in hospitals, schools, child welfare agencies, community centers, and within
federal, state, and local governments; and
WHEREAS, for more than a century, social workers have worked to improve human
well-being, advocate for their communities, and support basic needs; and
WHEREAS, social workers select this profession out of a deep commitment to help
uplift, defend, and transform the lives of people, their families, and their communities; and
WHEREAS, the theme of social work month in 2026 is "Social Workers: Uplift.
Defend. Transform.", which shines a light on the resolute spirit of social workers who
transform lives and bring hope and healing to the communities they serve; and
WHEREAS, social workers are guided by a professional code of ethics that
emphasizes service, integrity, the dignity and worth of every person, the importance of
human relationships, competence, and a commitment to fairness and opportunity in all
communities; and
WHEREAS, social workers serve individuals, families, and communities across
diverse backgrounds, including those experiencing economic hardship, discrimination,
displacement, or other life challenges, and help connect people to essential supports and
community resources; and
WHEREAS, social workers assist individuals navigating trauma, grief, health
concerns, natural disasters, and public health emergencies and help strengthen resilience and
long-term stability; and

WHEREAS, social workers work to reduce barriers that limit opportunity, promote
respectful dialogue, advocate for policies and practices that empower clients, and support
systems that improve social mobility; and
WHEREAS, social workers promote safety and positive outcomes by providing care
and coordination for individuals experiencing mental health conditions, substance use
challenges, family instability, and child welfare concerns; and
WHEREAS, the profession continues to grow, with more than eight hundred ten
thousand social workers in the United States contributing through direct practice, education,
research, leadership, and community engagement to support a fair, responsive, and
compassionate society.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express enduring appreciation for the vital
contributions of social workers across the nation; does hereby designate Monday, April 13,
2026, as Social Work Day at the state capitol; and does hereby urge Louisianians to join the
National Association of Social Workers in celebrating and supporting the work of social
workers who uplift, defend, and positively transform communities in the state and across
America.
