BY REPRESENTATIVE MIKE JOHNSON
COURTS: Provides relative to the appointment of a special master by a trial court
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 13:4165(G), relative to the appointment of a special master; to provide for the
3 application of the code of judicial conduct to special masters; and to provide for
4 related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 13:4165(G) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §4165. Special masters; appointment; duties and powers; compensation; code of
8 judicial conduct
9 * * *
10 G. For complex causes of action with multiple cases sharing common
11 questions of fact, when consolidated pursuant to an order or rules adopted by the
12 supreme court, any appointment of a special master by a trial court pursuant to this
13 Section shall comply with the ethical requirements of the Code of Judicial Conduct
14 as applicable and the requirements of Fed. Civ. R. 53 as to the appointment and
15 disqualification of a master, except to the extent those requirements are specifically
16 inconsistent with state law. Any appointment as described in this Subsection shall
17 also be subject to the provisions of Paragraphs (2) and (6) of Subsection F of this
18 Section. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the provisions of this
19 Subsection apply to those complex cases designated or approved under rules adopted
20 for this purpose by the Louisiana Supreme Court with all appointments under this
21 Subsection complying with those rules and the ethical requirements of this
22 Subsection.

HB 519 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Mike Johnson
Abstract: Requires a special master in complex civil litigation to comply with ethical
requirements of the Code of Judicial Conduct.
Present law provides for the appointment of a special master in any civil action where
complicated legal or factual issues are presented or exceptional circumstances of the case
warrant such appointment upon consent of all parties.
Proposed law retains present law and requires a special master appointed by a trial court to
comply with ethical requirements of the Code of Judicial Conduct. Provides for exceptions.
Proposed law provides that any cases designated as complex pursuant to rules adopted by
the La. Supreme Court are subject to the supreme court's rules regarding complex cases and
the provisions of proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 13:4165(G))
