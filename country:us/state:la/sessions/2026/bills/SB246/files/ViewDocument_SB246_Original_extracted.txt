HEALTH/ACC INSURANCE. Establishes requirements for insurers using artificial
intelligence. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 22:1260.44(E)(2) and to enact R.S. 22:1260.49 and 2401(4),
3 relative to the use of artificial intelligence by insurers; to provide for definitions; to
4 provide for notice; to provide for utilization review and determination by a human;
5 to provide for appeals; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 22:1260.44(E)(2) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
8 22:1260.49 and 2401(4) are hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §1260.44. Timeframes for determinations; concurrent review; retrospective review;
10 adverse determination
11 * * *
12 E.(1) * * *
13 (2) A health insurance issuer shall include in its written or electronic
14 notification of an adverse determination all of the reasons for the determination,
15 including the clinical rationale, and the instructions for initiating an appeal or
16 reconsideration of the determination, and whether artificial intelligence, as defined
17 in R.S. 22:1260.49, was used in the determination process.

2 §1260.49. Right to human review of health insurance claims
3 A. "Artificial intelligence" as used in this Section means an engineered
4 or machine-based system that varies in its level of autonomy, can receive input,
5 and can, for explicit or implicit objectives, generate or infer how to generate
6 outputs, including but not limited to predictions, simulations, and
7 recommendations that can influence physical or virtual environments.
8 B. The provisions of this Section are applicable to insurers, pharmacy
9 benefit managers, or an independent review agent or organization that does
10 either of the following:
11 (1) Uses artificial intelligence for utilization review.
12 (2) Contracts with a carrier that uses artificial intelligence for the
13 purpose of utilization review.
14 C.(1) No entity subject to this Section shall utilize an artificial
15 intelligence that does any of the following:
16 (a) Engages in discrimination that is prohibited by federal or state law.
17 (b) Violates regulations or guidance disseminated by the United States
18 Department of Health and Human Services.
19 (c) Delays, denies, or modifies healthcare services.
20 (2) Artificial intelligence used in the determination process shall not base
21 its determination or determination recommendation solely on a group data set.
22 (3) Artificial intelligence shall base its determination or determination
23 recommendation on any the following:
24 (a) The insured's medical or other clinical history.
25 (b) Individual clinical circumstances as presented by a requesting
26 provider.
27 (c) Other relevant clinical information contained in the insured's medical
28 or other clinical history.
29 D.(1) An entity subject to this Section shall not replace the role of a

1 healthcare provider in the determination process with artificial intelligence.
2 (2) An entity subject to this Section shall do all of the following:
3 (a) Require independent judgment from human utilization review
4 personnel in the utilization review process before making an adverse
5 determination for either of the following:
6 (i) Any claim submitted by a provider based on medical necessity.
7 (ii) Any claim submitted by a provider for a procedure requiring prior
8 authorization.
9 (b) Comply with applicable regulations and guidance for artificial
10 intelligence use issued by the United States Department of Health and Human
11 Services.
12 (c) Review the performance, use, and outcomes of an artificial
13 intelligence at a minimum of once per quarter, and revise the policies and
14 procedures as needed to ensure compliance with this Section.
15 (d) Allow the commissioner to inspect and audit the artificial intelligence
16 for compliance with this Section and review policies and procedures for how the
17 artificial intelligence is used in the determination process.
18 (e) Use patient data within its intended and stated purpose consistent
19 with the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996,
20 as applicable.
21 E.(1) Any insured has the right to appeal a determination that he has
22 learned was made with a recommendation from an artificial intelligence.
23 (2) If an adverse determination is appealed on the basis of the use of an
24 artificial intelligence, the insurer shall not use an artificial intelligence in any
25 subsequent review of the claim.

DIGEST
SB 246 Original 2026 Regular Session Luneau
Present law provides procedures for utilization review determinations by healthcare insurers.
Further provides for appeals from the utilization review decision. Proposed law retains
present law.
Present law requires a healthcare insurer to provide written or electronic notice to an insured
of an adverse utilization review determination and the reasons for the adverse determination.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires a healthcare insurer to inform an insured if artificial intelligence was
used in the determination process.
Proposed law defines artificial intelligence.
Proposed law provides requirements for the use of artificial intelligence in utilization review.
Proposed law provides that artificial intelligence shall not replace the role of a healthcare
provider in the utilization review determination.
Proposed law requires independent judgment from human utilization review personnel
before making an adverse determination for a claim based on medical necessity or a
procedure requiring prior authorization.
Proposed law requires compliance with applicable regulations and guidance from the United
States Department of Health and Human Services.
Proposed law requires compliance with the Health Insurance and Portability Act of 1996.
Proposed law provides that an insured has a right to appeal an adverse determination made
with a recommendation from artificial intelligence.
Proposed law forbids the use of artificial intelligence in any subsequent review of an adverse
determination that used artificial intelligence.
Proposed law allows an insured as part of the appeals process to review and have copies of
all documents relevant to any artificial intelligence used in the utilization review or
determination process.
