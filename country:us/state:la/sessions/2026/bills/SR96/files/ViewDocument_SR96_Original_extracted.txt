SLS 26RS-2369 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 96
BY SENATOR JENKINS
CONDOLENCES. Extends sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Rubye
Helen Jackson.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To extend sincere and heartfelt condolences of the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
3 upon the passing of Rubye Helen Jackson.
4 WHEREAS, it is with deep respect that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
5 recognizes the remarkable life and enduring legacy of Mrs. Rubye Helen Jackson, who
6 departed this life on April 13, 2026, at the age of ninety-four, leaving behind an indelible
7 record of faith, service, and dedication to her family, her community, and the state of
8 Louisiana; and
9 WHEREAS, Mrs. Jackson was born on August 22, 1931, in Keatchie, Louisiana, to
10 Theodore and Mary B. McClure, who instilled in her the foundational values of faith,
11 education, and selfless service that would define her life; and
12 WHEREAS, she accepted Jesus Christ as her savior at an early age and was a
13 devoted member of Avenue Baptist Church in Shreveport, Louisiana, where her spiritual
14 leadership and unwavering commitment to the ministry served as a beacon of light for the
15 congregation; and
16 WHEREAS, a proud graduate of the final class of Central Colored High School in
17 Shreveport, she continued her academic pursuits at Southern University in Baton Rouge,
18 Louisiana, earning a Bachelor of Arts degree in education in 1953; and

1 WHEREAS, that same year, she married Alphonse Jackson Jr., and together they
2 raised their beloved family, including their two cherished daughters, Lydia Patrice and
3 Angela Eileen, while remaining steadfast in their commitment to public service; and
4 WHEREAS, Mrs. Jackson was a dedicated educator for more than thirty years,
5 serving students in Webster Parish and Caddo Parish first as a teacher and later, after earning
6 a Master's degree in counseling from New York University, as a guidance counselor; and
7 WHEREAS, her career was defined by a tireless advocacy for the youth of Northwest
8 Louisiana, leaving a lasting imprint on generations of students whose lives were transformed
9 by her mentorship, compassion, and academic guidance; and
10 WHEREAS, following her retirement in 1986, she continued her mission of service
11 as a board member of Volunteers of America of North Louisiana and the Regional Mental
12 Health Association, earning the distinguished title of Louisiana State Volunteer of the Year
13 in 1987 for her meaningful work with the "I Am Thumbody" program; and
14 WHEREAS, Mrs. Jackson was a distinguished member of Alpha Kappa Alpha
15 Sorority, Incorporated, and was honored on January 31, 2026, by the Delta Lambda Omega
16 Chapter as a "Diamond Soror", commemorating seventy-five years of exemplary sisterhood
17 and service to all mankind; and
18 WHEREAS, she is survived by many who will honor her legacy and memory,
19 including her loving daughters, Lydia Patrice and Angela Eileen; her devoted friend of more
20 than sixty years, Evelyn G. Valore; and a vast network of family, friends, and "adopted"
21 loved ones who knew her affectionately as "Aunt Helen"; and
22 WHEREAS, the widespread outpouring of tributes from throughout the state stands
23 as a testament to the profound respect and admiration she earned as a pillar of grace,
24 intelligence, and community strength.
25 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
26 does hereby extend sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Rubye Helen
27 Jackson and does hereby honor her legacy and outstanding contributions to education and
28 community service.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
30 family of Mrs. Rubye Helen Jackson.
