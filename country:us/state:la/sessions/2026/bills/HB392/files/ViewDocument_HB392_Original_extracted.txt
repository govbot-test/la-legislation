HLS 26RS-756 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE BOYD
SCHOOLS: Provides relative to requirements for posting suicide hotline information on
school websites and student ID cards
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:282.4(F), relative to suicide prevention programs for students;
3 to provide relative to information required to be shared on school websites and
4 student identification cards; to revise the content of the information; to broaden
5 applicability of requirements; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 17:282.4(F) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
8 §282.4. Youth suicide prevention programs; student safety and violence and social
9 isolation prevention training; policies; intent; rules; services; funding
10 * * *
11 F.(1) Each public and approved nonpublic secondary middle school and high
12 school shall post on its website the following information:
13 (a) The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline hotline number, "988", with an
14 explanation that this number may be called or texted.
15 (b) A local suicide prevention hotline number, if available, and a state
16 suicide prevention hotline number, if available.
17 (2) The governing authority of each public and approved nonpublic
18 secondary middle school and high school that issues student identification cards shall

DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HB 392 Original 2026 Regular Session Boyd
Abstract: Provides relative to requirements for posting suicide prevention hotline
information on school websites and student ID cards.
Present law requires each secondary school to post a national suicide prevention hotline
number, 988, and a local suicide prevention hotline number, if available, on its website.
Proposed law broadens the application of this requirement to both middle schools and high
schools and additionally requires an explanation that 988 may be called or texted and the
inclusion of a state suicide prevention hotline number, if available.
Present law requires each secondary school that issues ID cards to include 988 and a local
hotline number, if available, on the back of the cards. Proposed law broadens the application
of this requirement to both middle schools and high schools and additionally requires an
explanation that 988 may be called or texted and the inclusion of a state hotline number, if
available.
(Amends R.S. 17:282.4(F))
