HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 242
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREIBERG
A RESOLUTION
To commend the YMCA on the occasion of its one hundred seventy-fifth anniversary and
to express appreciation to its staff and volunteers for their efforts to strengthen
communities and promote well-being and connection.
WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association (YMCA) marks its one hundred
seventy-fifth anniversary in 2026; it was founded in the United States by Thomas Valentine
Sullivan in Boston on December 29, 1851, with a mission to put Christian principles into
practice by building a healthy spirit, mind, and body for all; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA continually adapts to meet the evolving needs of American
communities, which strengthens society by bringing people together, fostering relationships,
and creating a sense of belonging; and
WHEREAS, the legacy of YMCA includes providing safe housing during the
Industrial Revolution, pioneering the first night school and English as a Second Language
classes, inventing basketball, and delivering the first group swim lessons; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA established the Youth and Government Program, which is
distinguished as the birthplace of Father's Day, and has grown into the nation's leading
nonprofit provider of child care and after-school programs to support working families; and
WHEREAS, today, nearly two thousand six hundred YMCAs serve more than ten
thousand communities and have more than three hundred thousand staff and three hundred
fifty thousand volunteers who deliver essential programs to over seventeen million people
annually; and
WHEREAS, the YMCAs of Louisiana foster a commitment to strengthening
communities by connecting people to their potential, purpose, and each other; and

WHEREAS, the YMCA of the Capital Area was founded in 1917 in Baton Rouge,
Louisiana, and has served as a cornerstone of the community for more than a century by
advancing youth development, healthy living, and social responsibility; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA of the Capital Area engages dedicated staff and volunteers
across seven branches and delivers essential programs and services that strengthen
individuals, families, and communities throughout the Capital Area; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA is a leader in water safety and swim lessons, child care,
youth sports, meal programs, and health and wellness initiatives, and is the largest provider
of fitness programs for older adults; and
WHEREAS, the YMCA creates welcoming spaces for people of all ages,
backgrounds, and abilities, and the organization is deserving of recognition for its
outstanding contributions to the state.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the YMCA on the occasion of its one
hundred seventy-fifth anniversary; does hereby recognize the role of the YMCA in
responding to community needs, fostering relationships, and building a sense of belonging;
does hereby commend the staff and volunteers who create connected communities and
advance the YMCA's mission; and does hereby encourage continued support for the efforts
of the YMCA to promote health, wellness, nutrition, and social interaction and to create
spaces that help people build relationships and thrive together.
