HB 1108 Original 2026 Regular Session Amedée
Abstract: Creates the "Homeschool Proud" special prestige license plate and provides for
the issuance, design, implementation, fees, distributions, and promulgation of rules
applicable to these license plates.
Proposed law requires that the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections (DPS&C), office of
motor vehicles (OMV), issue a special prestige license plate to be known as the
"Homeschool Proud" license plate, provided that there is a minimum of 1,000 applicants for
such plates.
Proposed law specifies that the plate is restricted to use on passenger cars, pickup trucks,
recreational vehicles, motorcycles, and vans. Further directs the DPS&C, OMV, to create
this special prestige license plate when the applicable statutory provisions are met and the
department's electronic vehicle and registration system is updated to accommodate the
creation of the new plates.

Proposed law requires that the secretary work in conjunction with the artist selected by
Homeschool La. to design the plate. Requires that the bottom of the plate bear the words
"Homeschool Proud". Requires a symbol representing Homeschool La. appear on the left
portion of the plate. Requires the center portion of the plate have five alpha-numeric digits
for registration purposes.
Proposed law specifies that the plate will be issued, upon application, to any citizen of the
state in the same manner as any other motor vehicle license plate.
Proposed law requires that the dept. collect an annual fee of $25 in addition to the standard
motor vehicle license tax imposed in present law and a handling fee of $3.50 for each plate
to be retained by the dept. to offset a portion of administrative costs.
Proposed law requires the annual royalty fee collected by the dept. be forwarded to
Homeschool La. and all monies be used solely for the activities of Homeschool La.
Proposed law requires the secretary promulgate and adopt rules and regulations as are
necessary to implement the provisions of proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 47:463.243)
