2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR CLOUD
MOTOR VEHICLES. Provides relative to Department of Public Safety and Corrections
oversight of commercial vehicle parking. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact the introductory paragraph of R.S. 32:296(A), 400.2(B), 400.3(C) and
3 400.7(B), relative to the Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of state
4 police; to provide for vehicles on state highway shoulders; to provide for proper
5 agency oversight; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:

8 400.7(B) are hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:

13 made necessary by an emergency, except:

20 Public Safety and Corrections, office of state police certifying that the vehicle
21 meets the requirements of this Section.

DIGEST
SB 133 Original 2026 Regular Session Cloud
Present law provides for vehicles left unattended on state highway shoulders. Proposed law
adds attended vehicles in addition to unattended vehicles and changes the classification from
"state highway shoulders" to "highway shoulders".
Present law provides the Department of Transportation and Development (DOTD) is the
only agency with jurisdiction over autonomous commercial motor vehicles and automated
driving systems. Proposed law changes the agency jurisdiction from DOTD to Department

Present law provides a written statement must be presented before using a driverless,
autonomous, commercial vehicle certifying the vehicle meets DOTD requirements. Proposed
law retains present law and changes the agency from DOTD to Department of Public Safety
and Corrections, office of state police.
Present law provides before operating a driverless commercial motor vehicle with a
teleoperation system, a written statement must be submitted by the vehicle's owner, a remote
driver or the remote drivers employer certifying the vehicle meets certain requirements.
Proposed law retains present law and changes the agency from DOTD to Department of
Public Safety and Corrections, office of state police.
Effective August 1, 2026.
