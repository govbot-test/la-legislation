DIGEST
SB 319 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Pressly
Present law provides instruction for a commissioner to offer any voter that does not have
picture identification an affidavit to sign.
Proposed law removes the affidavit option and provides new identification requirements.
Present law provides the information for a clerk to provide the course of instruction to the
commissioners during the week prior to an election. This information includes that the
commissioners are to offer any voter who does not have picture identification an affidavit
to sign to that effect.
Proposed law retains present law but removes language related to affidavits and provides
alternative identification options.
Present law provides a list of what qualifies for identification in order to be eligible to vote.
Proposed law retains present law but adds additional methods that qualify as identification
in order to be eligible to vote.
Present law provides the procedure to fill out an affidavit for an applicant that does not have
one of the forms of identification allowed by law.
Proposed law removes the affidavit process and provides a new procedure for an applicant
that does not have one of the forms of identification allowed by law.
Present law provides procedures for commissioners after termination of voting which
includes including all voter identification affidavits in the "Registrar of Voters" envelope.
Proposed law removes the voter identification affidavits from the documents that are
required to go in the "Registrar of Voters" envelope.

Present law provides the information required as evidence of election results.
Proposed law retains present law but removes voter identification affidavits from the
information required as evidence.
Present law provides early voting verification requirements which include what may be
submitted to establish the voter's identity.
Proposed law removes the requirement of an identification that is an other generally
recognized picture identification card that contains the name and signature of the voter.
Proposed law adds additional methods that may be used to establish the voter's identity.
Present law provides a process for a voter that does not have one of the forms of
identification provided in order to prove their identity which includes an affidavit.
Proposed law provides instructions to the Parish Board of Election Supervisors on the
handling of conditional paper ballots if an applicant has presented proper voting
identification.
Proposed law provides a directive to the secretary of state to promulgate administrative rules
pursuant to the Administrative Procedure Act to implement this law.
Proposed law removes the affidavit from the process to prove identity but replaces it with
additional documents that will be accepted.

DIGEST
SB 319 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Pressly
Present law provides instruction for a commissioner to offer any voter that does not have
picture identification an affidavit to sign.
Proposed law removes the affidavit option and provides new identification requirements.
Present law provides the information for a clerk to provide the course of instruction to the
commissioners during the week prior to an election. This information includes that the
commissioners are to offer any voter who does not have picture identification an affidavit
to sign to that effect.
Proposed law retains present law but removes language related to affidavits and provides
alternative identification options.
Present law provides a list of what qualifies for identification in order to be eligible to vote.
Proposed law retains present law but adds additional methods that qualify as identification
in order to be eligible to vote.
Present law provides the procedure to fill out an affidavit for an applicant that does not have
one of the forms of identification allowed by law.
Proposed law removes the affidavit process and provides a new procedure for an applicant
that does not have one of the forms of identification allowed by law.
Present law provides procedures for commissioners after termination of voting which
includes including all voter identification affidavits in the "Registrar of Voters" envelope.
Proposed law removes the voter identification affidavits from the documents that are
required to go in the "Registrar of Voters" envelope.

Present law provides the information required as evidence of election results.
Proposed law retains present law but removes voter identification affidavits from the

Present law provides early voting verification requirements which include what may be
submitted to establish the voter's identity.
Proposed law removes the requirement of an identification that is an other generally
recognized picture identification card that contains the name and signature of the voter.
Proposed law adds additional methods that may be used to establish the voter's identity.
Present law provides a process for a voter that does not have one of the forms of

Proposed law provides instructions to the Parish Board of Election Supervisors on the
handling of conditional paper ballots if an applicant has presented proper voting
identification.
Proposed law provides a directive to the secretary of state to promulgate administrative rules
pursuant to the Administrative Procedure Act to implement this law.
Proposed law removes the affidavit from the process to prove identity but replaces it with
additional documents that will be accepted.
