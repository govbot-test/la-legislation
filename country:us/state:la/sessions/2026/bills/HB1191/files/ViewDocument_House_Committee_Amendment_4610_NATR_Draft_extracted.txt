5 "(7) "Operator of interest" means any current operator of record for the site
6 as reflected in department records at the time the notice is filed, or the most recent
7 solvent prior operator of record if the current operator of record is bankrupt or
8 insolvent, or any registered operator in good standing with the department that has
9 acquired either a lease or other right to discover and produce minerals from the site
10 or a bona fide option to acquire such a lease.
11 (a) No person or entity shall initiate or participate in the certificate of
12 compliance process for the purpose of generating, obtaining, or preserving
13 environmental data or sampling results for use as evidence in any pending or
14 anticipated litigation or administrative proceeding to which that person is a party and
15 in which conditions at the site are at issue, other than in a proceeding in which a
16 certificate of compliance itself is asserted as an affirmative defense following its
17 issuance.
18 (b) For purposes of this Section, "operator of interest" does not include a
19 royalty owner, overriding royalty interest owner, or any other person whose interest
20 in the site is limited to a financial or production interest and who does not bear direct
21 operational responsibility for the site as operator of record or as a lessee under
22 applicable law. The exclusion of the owner of an interest, including any working
23 interest owners, from this definition does not affect any rights or obligations such
24 owners may have under applicable law."

33 (i) A full or partial certificate of compliance, where the department has

42 environmental noncompliance, the corrective action period shall commence in
43 accordance with Subsection D of this Section.

1 (iii) A notice of administrative deficiency, where the department determines
2 that the site does not present contamination of environmental media above applicable
3 regulatory standards, but that one or more administrative, operational, or
4 recordkeeping requirements under applicable law have not been satisfied. A notice
5 of administrative deficiency shall not constitute a report of noncompliance for
6 purposes of this Section, shall not trigger the corrective action period under
7 Subsection D of this Section, and shall not give rise to any inference or presumption
8 of environmental liability with respect to the site. Administrative deficiencies
9 identified in a notice pursuant to this Item shall be addressed through the
10 department's regulatory and enforcement authority pursuant to this Subtitle and shall
11 not affect the admissibility or preclusive effect of any certificate of compliance
12 issued as to environmental conditions at the site.
13 (b) Notwithstanding any outstanding administrative deficiencies that may be
14 addressed pursuant to Item (B)(8)(a)(iii) of this Section, where environmental
15 sampling and testing conducted during the review period demonstrates that all
16 sampled environmental media at the site meet the requirements of applicable law, the
17 department shall at minimum issue a partial certificate of compliance as to the
18 environmental conditions of those media. The existence of an outstanding notice of
19 administrative deficiency shall not delay, condition, or preclude the issuance of such
20 a certificate and the certificate shall carry the full preclusive effect described in
21 Subsection C of this Section as to environmental damage and property damage
22 claims relating to pre-certification conditions of those media.
23 (9) All data, information, communications, analyses, and documentation
24 generated or exchanged between the requesting parties and the department during the
25 evaluation process established by this Subsection, other than a certificate, partial
26 certificate, and a final report of environmental noncompliance, shall be confidential,
27 shall not be subject to the Public Records Law, R.S. 44:1 et seq., and shall not be
28 disclosed, used as evidence, or otherwise relied upon in any judicial or administrative
29 proceeding by a person other than a requesting party, the department, or any of their
30 respective successors or assigns. The requesting parties may, by mutual written
31 agreement, waive confidentiality as to any specific document or communication."

20 On page 10, between lines 4 and 5, insert the following:
21 "H. Effect on operator succession and post-certification acquisition.
22 (1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, a person who
23 acquires a mineral lease, operating interest, or other right to conduct exploration and
24 production activities at a site for which a certificate of compliance has been issued
25 pursuant to this Section shall not, by virtue of that acquisition alone, become
26 responsible for or assume any obligation with respect to environmental damage or
27 property damage arising from pre-certification site conditions. Such acquisition shall
28 not subject the acquiring party to liability in solido with any prior operator, lessee,
29 or working interest owner for pre-certification conditions, nor shall it constitute a
30 basis for proceeding against the acquiring party as the operator of record for
31 pre-certification conditions. The acquiring party's regulatory and civil liability shall
32 be limited to site conditions arising from its own operations conducted after the date
33 of the certificate of compliance.
34 (2) A certificate of compliance issued pursuant to this Section constitutes
35 conclusive documentation of the baseline environmental conditions at the site as of
36 the date of certification. In any subsequent judicial or administrative proceeding,
37 contamination or damage identified after the date of the certificate shall be presumed
38 to constitute post-certification conditions and shall not be deemed indistinguishable
39 from pre-certification conditions absent clear and convincing evidence to the
40 contrary. This presumption shall apply in favor of any operator who acquires an
41 interest in the site on or after the date of the certificate and against any claim seeking
42 to hold that operator responsible for pre-certification conditions on the basis that
43 post-certification and pre-certification damage cannot be distinguished.
44 (3) Nothing in this Subsection shall limit the liability of any person for
45 environmental damage or property damage caused by that person's own
46 post-certification operations, nor shall it limit the enforcement authority of the
47 department or any other governmental entity with respect to post-certification site
48 conditions. Nothing in this Subsection shall be construed to release any prior
49 operator from liability to a subsequent operator or to the surface owner for
50 pre-certification conditions."
