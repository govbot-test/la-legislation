BY SENATOR ALLAIN

WHEREAS, Louisiana has become a critical hub for the development of the U.S.

Louisiana's shipbuilding industrial base at a disadvantage; and
WHEREAS, the workforce at Conrad Shipyard is currently building these vessels "at
risk" to maintain the delivery schedule, supporting an estimated one hundred eight direct
jobs and nearly four hundred total jobs across the Louisiana supply chain this year; and
WHEREAS, currently four hundred direct and indirect jobs are at work, with a full
production rate of twenty vessels per year, the program is projected to sustain approximately
one thousand direct jobs and four thousand indirect jobs, contributing up to $700 million in
direct annual shipbuilding work to the Louisiana economy; and

WHEREAS, these high-tech jobs bring essential expertise in robotics, software
development, and advanced engineering to the region, creating a human capital ecosystem
that will complement and benefit Louisiana's maritime industry for decades.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the United States Navy expeditiously execute a transparent and
competitive MUSV acquisition strategy to provide a stable production forecast to prevent
the loss of high-tech talent and industrial capacity.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the United States Navy is requested to minimize
further delays and requirement shifts that leave the Louisiana shipbuilding workforce and
private research and development investments at unnecessary risk.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
United States Secretary of the Navy, the Chief of Naval Operations, and the members of the
Louisiana congressional delegation.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE

competitive Medium Unmanned Surface Vessel (MUSV) acquisition strategy to
protect the maritime workforce at Conrad Shipyard in Morgan City, Louisiana, to

advanced vessels in Morgan City; and

production; and
