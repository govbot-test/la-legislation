ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 378
BY REPRESENTATIVES PHELPS AND TURNER
A RESOLUTION
Kaejha Mary-Anne Christine Stewart.
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Department of Education (LDOE) honored twenty-four
regional finalists and named three Louisiana Students of the Year during a ceremony at The
Cook Hotel and Conference Center in Baton Rouge; and
WHEREAS, each year, the LDOE and the Louisiana Board of Elementary and
Secondary Education (BESE) recognize students who exemplify academic excellence,
leadership, and service; finalists include one fifth, eighth, and twelfth grader from each of
the state's eight BESE regions; and
WHEREAS, the 2026 overall honorees are Sanil Savita Thorat, Kaejha Mary-Anne
Christine Stewart, and Melissa An Le; and
WHEREAS, Kaejha Mary-Anne Christine Stewart is the Louisiana Middle School
Student of the Year; she attends A.E. Phillips Laboratory School in Lincoln Parish, served
as student council president for the 2025-2026 school year, and represented her school as an
eighth-grade student ambassador during its back to school night; she is a leader in the
Fellowship of Christian Athletes, a member of the National Junior Honor Society, and
participates on the Science Olympiad and MathCounts teams; she is also active in choir,
music, and theater and has performed as a national anthem soloist, Patriots Day Program
soloist and speaker, and pianist for her eighth-grade graduation; and
WHEREAS, Kaejha Mary-Anne Christine Stewart is most deserving of recognition
for bringing great pride to Lincoln Parish and the state of Louisiana through her outstanding
achievements.
Page 1 of 2
HR NO. 378 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Louisiana Department of Education 2026
Middle School Student of the Year Kaejha Mary-Anne Christine Stewart; does hereby
recognize her for building a strong foundation for the future success of the state; and does
hereby encourage her to continue her excellent work and always strive for greatness.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
