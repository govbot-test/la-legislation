2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 374
BY REPRESENTATIVE FIRMENT
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Department of Wildlife and Fisheries and the Department of
Conservation and Energy to study cooperative endeavor agreements for the
withdrawal of surface water and provide a written summary of their findings to the
House Committee on Natural Resources and Environment prior to the convening of
the 2027 Regular Session of the Legislature.
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana finds and
declares that the surface waters of the state of Louisiana are among its most valuable natural
resources and are essential to the state's economy, environment, public safety, and quality
of life; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana further
finds that the responsible management of surface water resources requires a balanced
approach that supports economic development while ensuring the long-term sustainability
and usability of the state's waterways; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana recognizes
that invasive aquatic vegetation and species including but not limited to giant salvinia, water
hyacinth, and hydrilla pose a significant and growing threat to Louisiana's lakes, rivers, and
other water bodies by impairing navigation, increasing flood risk, degrading wildlife habitat,
and limiting public access; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana further
finds that aquatic vegetation overgrowth and invasive species infestations can render water
bodies unusable for commerce, recreation, and resource management if not effectively
controlled; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana intends
to:
(1) Ensure that the use and withdrawal of surface water resources contributes to the
preservation and maintenance of those resources.

(2) Support a statewide, coordinated approach to aquatic vegetation management and
invasive species control.
(3) Provide for research, monitoring, and long-term management strategies to
address invasive aquatic species.
(4) Establish sustainable funding mechanisms to assist in the protection and
restoration of Louisiana's waterways for the benefit of present and future generations.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Wildlife and
Fisheries and the Department of Conservation and Energy to study cooperative endeavor
agreements for the withdrawal of surface water including consideration of the four
enumerated intentions of the House of Representatives as expressed in this Resolution and
provide a written summary of their findings to the House Committee on Natural Resources
and Environment at least thirty days prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session of
the Legislature.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Wildlife and Fisheries and the secretary of the Department
of Conservation and Energy.
