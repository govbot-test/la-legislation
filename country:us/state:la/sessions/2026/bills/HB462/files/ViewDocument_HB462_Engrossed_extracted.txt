HB 462 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session McMakin
Abstract: Adds appointed members to the board of commissioners of the Capital Area
Road and Bridge District.
Present law (R.S. 48:771) creates the Capital Area Road and Bridge District as a political
subdivision of the state and for the purpose of raising revenue to finance road and bridge
projects to alleviate traffic congestion. Provides that the district shall be comprised of all
territories within the geographical boundaries of the parishes of East Baton Rouge,
Ascension, Livingston, Iberville, and West Baton Rouge.
Present law (R.S. 48:773) provides that the management and control of the district shall be
vested in a board of commissioners composed of seven members, including the secretary of
the Department of Transportation and Development or his designee, the president of each
of the parishes in the district or their designees, and one gubernatorial appointee who shall
be a resident of the district and serve at the pleasure of the governor.
Proposed law adds members to the board of commissioners as follows:
(1) The speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint at least one and not more
than five representatives whose representative district includes a portion of at least
one of the five parishes comprising the district. The speaker shall appoint, as
practicable, one representative from each of the parishes comprising the district.
(2) The president of the Senate shall appoint at least one and not more than five senators
whose senate district includes a portion of at least one of the five parishes
comprising the district. The president shall appoint, as practicable, one senator from
each of the parishes comprising the district.
(Amends R.S. 48:773)
