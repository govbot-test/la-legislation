9 "Section 2. R.S. 40:1087.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1087.2. Youth injury mitigation courses; approval process
11 A.(1) The Louisiana Department of Health shall establish and administer
12 a process for approving courses that meet the requirements of a youth injury
13 mitigation course.
14 B. Completion of an approved youth injury mitigation course may be
15 used to satisfy the requirements of a safety orientation and training program in
16 R.S. 9:2798. Each youth injury mitigation course shall include instruction on
17 the following:
18 (1) Emergency preparedness and planning and rehearsal for responding
19 to traumatic injuries sustained during youth athletic activities.
20 (2) Heat and extreme weather-related injuries.
21 (3) Musculoskeletal overuse, physical conditioning, recovery, and
22 training equipment usage.
23 (4) Heart defects and abnormalities leading to sudden cardiac death.
24 C. Not later than one hundred eighty days after the effective date of this
25 Section, the department shall publish and maintain on its official website a list
26 of approved courses. The department shall annually review and update the list
27 of approved injury mitigation courses.
28 D. The department may adopt rules, in accordance with the
29 Administrative Procedure Act, as necessary to carry out the provisions of this
30 Section.
31 E. This Section shall not require the department to spend its funds to
32 pay for the development of the training courses provided in this Section. The
33 department may spend donated funds to purchase the courses or may accept
34 donated online courses, provided that the donated courses are approved by the
35 department.
36 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
37 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
38 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
39 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
40 effective on the day following such approval."
