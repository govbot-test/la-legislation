Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ACT No. 377 ENROLLED
BY SENATORS EDMONDS, CARTER, JENKINS, LUNEAU, MILLER AND MIZELL
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 9:2798(B)(1) and to enact R.S. 9:2798(D)(4) and R.S. 40:1087.2,
3 relative to youth athletics; to provide for the Coach Safely Act; to provide for
4 limitation of liability; to provide for definitions; to provide for an injury mitigation
5 course; to provide for content of youth injury mitigation courses; to provide for
6 posting of certain information on the internet; to provide for an effective date; and
7 to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 9:2798(B)(1) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 9:2798(D)(4)
10 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §2798. Limitation of liability of a volunteer athletic coach, manager, team volunteer
12 health care healthcare provider, or official; definitions
13 * * *
14 B.(1) The provisions of this Section shall not be applicable unless the
15 volunteer athletic coach, manager, athletic trainer, team volunteer health care
16 healthcare provider, or sports team official has participated in a safety orientation
17 and training program established by the league or team with which he is affiliated
18 or a youth injury mitigation course. Any safety orientation and training program
19 established in compliance with this Subsection may include, as a condition of
20 satisfactory completion, a course in child cardiopulmonary resuscitation.
21 Participation in a safety orientation and training program by a coach, manager,
22 athletic trainer, team volunteer health care healthcare provider, or sports team
23 official may be waived by the league prior to the individual's or person's participation
24 in the sporting activities or in the practice thereof upon submission of appropriate
25 documented evidence as to that individual's or person's proficiency in first aid and

1 safety, which may include a current certification in child cardiopulmonary
2 resuscitation.
3 * * *
4 D. For the purposes of this Section, the following phrases shall have the
5 meanings hereafter ascribed to them:
6 * * *
7 (4) "Youth injury mitigation course" means a course approved by the
8 Louisiana Department of Health that includes instruction in all of the following:
9 (a) Emergency preparedness and planning and rehearsal for responding
10 to traumatic injuries sustained during youth athletic activities.
11 (b) Concussions and head trauma.
12 (c) Heat and extreme weather-related injuries.
13 (d) Musculoskeletal overuse, physical conditioning, recovery, and
14 training equipment usage.
15 (e) Heart defects and abnormalities leading to sudden cardiac death.
16 Section 2. R.S. 40:1087.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
17 §1087.2. Youth injury mitigation courses; approval process
18 A. The Louisiana Department of Health shall establish and administer
19 a process for approving courses that meet the requirements of a youth injury
20 mitigation course.
21 B. Completion of an approved youth injury mitigation course may be
22 used to satisfy the requirements of a safety orientation and training program in
23 R.S. 9:2798. Each youth injury mitigation course shall include instruction on the
24 following:
25 (1) Emergency preparedness and planning and rehearsal for responding
26 to traumatic injuries sustained during youth athletic activities.
27 (2) Heat and extreme weather-related injuries.
28 (3) Musculoskeletal overuse, physical conditioning, recovery, and
29 training equipment usage.
30 (4) Heart defects and abnormalities leading to sudden cardiac death.

1 C. Not later than one hundred eighty days after the effective date of this
2 Section, the department shall publish and maintain on its official website a list
3 of approved courses. The department shall annually review and update the list
4 of approved injury mitigation courses.
5 D. The department may adopt rules, in accordance with the
6 Administrative Procedure Act, as necessary to carry out the provisions of this
7 Section.
8 E. This Section shall not require the department to spend its funds to pay
9 for the development of the training courses provided in this Section. The
10 department may accept donated online courses, provided that the donated
11 courses are approved by the department.
12 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
13 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
14 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
15 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
16 effective on the day following such approval.
17 Section 4. This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Coach Safely Act".
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
GOVERNOR OF THE STATE OF LOUISIANA
