HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 176
BY REPRESENTATIVE NEWELL
A RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize Mable Harris Thomas and to commend
her as she approaches the incredible milestone of one hundred years of life; and
WHEREAS, Mable was born on May 14, 1926, in Napoleonville, Louisiana, the
youngest of four children born to Andrew and Florestine Harris; and
WHEREAS, Mable was baptized by her uncle, Reverend Freddie Southall, at Beulah
Baptist Church in Bertrandville, Louisiana; she has been a member of First Zion Baptist
Church, Phillip's Memorial United Methodist Church, Napoleon Ave United Methodist
Church, and Rayne Memorial United Methodist Church, and her service is known
throughout the United Methodist community; and
WHEREAS, Mable has a passion for education, which she discovered at an early
age, and she walked nearly four miles to attend a one-room school in Napoleonville; she
graduated from Lowery Training School in Donaldsonville, Louisiana, and went on to attend
Grambling College; and
WHEREAS, while at Grambling, Mable met her husband, Joseph "Brushback"
Thomas, who was a standout member of legendary Coach Eddie Robinson's Grambling
football team; she later transferred to Leland College in Baker, Louisiana, and earned a
bachelor's degree in education; and
WHEREAS, Mable enjoyed a thirty-one year career as an educator in Assumption
and Jefferson Parishes; she consistently pursued excellence in her field and obtained a
certification to teach special education from Dominican College; after retiring, she served
as a minute clerk in New Orleans Traffic Court under Judge Louis Trent; and

WHEREAS, after dedicating many years of service as a minute clerk, Mable
embarked on a life of travel and volunteer work with numerous charitable organizations; she
became the first lieutenant for the neighborhood watch program in Hollygrove, where she
has resided since 1956; she is a familiar face at meetings of the 2nd District Police Station
and a friend of each councilman who represented her in District "A"; and
WHEREAS, Mable has been a member of Delta Sigma Theta for forty-five-years,
and she is a devoted supporter of the New Orleans Saints and Pelicans; and
WHEREAS, Mable is the mother of Cardell A. Thomas; grandmother of Jeandele
Frank, Austin Thomas, and Jordan Thomas; great-grandmother of Charlie J. Thomas; and
godmother of Arnell Chatman, Spergeon Holly, and Corey Peterson; she also has numerous
nieces, nephews, and cousins who cherish her; and
WHEREAS, Mable Harris Thomas has lived a life defined by unwavering hospitality
and love, and she is most deserving of the highest recognition as she approaches this
incredible milestone.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Mable Harris Thomas as she approaches her
one hundredth birthday and does hereby extend sincere wishes that she remain prosperous
as she joins the exclusive club of Louisiana centenarians.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
