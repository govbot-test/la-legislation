CHAPTER 18. DATA CENTERS
§1601. Definitions
As used in this Chapter the following term shall have the following meaning
unless context clearly requires otherwise:
"Data center" means a facility that is designed to have a load of one hundred
megawatts or more and whose primary purpose is the storage, management, and
processing of digital data via the interconnection and operation of information
technology and network telecommunications equipment, including all related
facilities and infrastructure for backup electricity generation, power distribution,
environmental control, cooling, and security.

§1602. Permitting
A. No data center shall commence operations without first obtaining a water
use permit from the Department of Conservation and Energy.
B. The secretary shall promulgate and adopt such rules and regulations as are
necessary to implement this Section by August 31, 2027.
§1603. Agricultural use exemption
Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, nothing in this
Chapter shall be construed to impact, burden, or impose regulatory requirements or
fees upon agricultural water users. Agricultural water use, including but not limited
to irrigation, livestock watering, and other customary agricultural and horticultural
purposes, shall be explicitly exempt from any regulatory or administrative oversight
established pursuant to this Chapter. Participation in programs or initiatives
established in this Chapter by agricultural users shall be strictly voluntary and
without a fee.

CHAPTER 24. DATA CENTERS
§2591. Data center permitting; hearings
A Permits issued by the department for data centers, as defined in R.S.
30:1601, shall be subject to the requirements of R.S. 30:2017 and 2018 and the
provisions of LAC 33:IX.3113, 3115, and 3117.
§2592. Permit transparency
The department shall assign a unique identifier to each data center project.
Any permit associated with the data center project, regardless of the named applicant
or entity seeking the permit, shall include the unique identifier.
Section 2. The Department of Environmental Quality shall implement the provisions
of R.S. 30:2592 as enacted by this Act within one year of the effective date of this Act.

HB Draft 2026 Regular Session
Abstract: Provides authority for the secretary of the Dept. of Energy and Conservation to
monitor and regulate the withdrawal of groundwater and surface waters and provides
for water use permitting.
Proposed law authorizes the secretary of the Dept. of Conservation and Energy to monitor
and regulate the withdrawal of groundwater and surface waters of the state, subject to the
provisions of present law and proposed law.
Proposed law provides a definition for "data center".
Proposed law provides for permitting of data centers by the Dept. of Conservation and
Energy and for the secretary to promulgate rules and regulation necessary for permitting by
Aug. 31, 2027.
Proposed law provides an agricultural use exemption from the requirements applicable to
data centers.
Proposed law provides that permits issued by the Dept. of Environmental Quality for data
centers are subject to the provisions of present law related to public hearings.
Proposed law requires the Dept. of Environmental Quality to assign a unique identifier to
data center projects and to include the identifier on all permits associated with a data center
project and to have a protocol in place to implement the requirement for a unique identifier
within one year of the effective date of proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 30:4(U), 1601-1603, and 2591-2592)
