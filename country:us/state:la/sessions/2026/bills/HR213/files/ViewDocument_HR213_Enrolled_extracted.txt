occasion of his designation as pastor emeritus of First Presbyterian Church of Baton

WHEREAS, Pastor Gerrit Scott Dawson joined the First Presbyterian family as
senior pastor in 2004; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson and his wife Rhonda have four children and seven
grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson earned an undergraduate degree at Vanderbilt
University, a master of divinity at Princeton Theological Seminary, and a doctorate of
ministry at the Reformed Theological Seminary in Charlotte, North Carolina; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson is the author of eight books, including The Blessing Life
and Raising Adam: Why Jesus Descended into Hell; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson's interests include J.R.R. Tolkien, movies from the
1940s, poet and minister George Herbert, the Miami Hurricanes, and Harry Potter; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson has tirelessly provided scriptural preaching, counseling,
visitation and mentoring, and administrative guidance for the past twenty-two years; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson guided First Presbyterian Church into its Evangelical
Presbyterian Church (EPC) denominational home; and
WHEREAS, Pastor Dawson has provided rich and meaningful Lenten devotional
guides, national leadership through timely and challenging matters for the EPC, and writings
on spiritual formation that have guided and blessed the church across denominational lines;
and

WHEREAS, First Presbyterian Church has experienced significant spiritual and
numerical growth of all generations and has become increasingly generous and creative in
its local and global mission endeavors; and
WHEREAS, the congregation of First Presbyterian Church of Baton Rouge gratefully
bestowed the title of pastor emeritus on the Reverend Doctor Gerrit Scott Dawson on
April 26, 2026.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Reverend Doctor Gerrit Scott Dawson
of First Presbyterian Church of Baton Rouge on the occasion of his designation as pastor
emeritus of the church and does hereby extend enduring appreciation for his spiritual
leadership throughout his career in ministry.
