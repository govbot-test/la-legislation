HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 69
CARVER, CHASSION, COATES, DICKERSON, EGAN, GLORIOSO, TERRY
LANDRY, MACK, MURRAY, SAWYER, SCHAMERHORN, TAYLOR,
WALTERS, WRIGHT, AND WYBLE AND SENATOR WHEAT
TRANSPORTATION: Urges and requests the Department of Transportation and
Development make certain improvements to Interstate 12 in certain parishes
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to implement
3 improvements to Interstate 12 (I-12) in Livingston Parish, East Baton Rouge Parish,
4 Tangipahoa Parish, and St. Tammany Parish.
5 WHEREAS, I-12 spans approximately eighty-six miles in the state of Louisiana; and
6 WHEREAS, approximately seventy thousand vehicles per day travel on I-12 in the
7 state making it a vital corridor in Louisiana; and
8 WHEREAS, I-12 serves as a major hurricane evacuation route, economic corridor,
9 and primary interstate for the state; and
10 WHEREAS, there is constant and significant congestion and elevated crash rates on
11 I-12; and
12 WHEREAS, several serious and fatal crashes have occurred on I-12 over the past
13 year; and
14 WHEREAS, on September 8, 2020, a collision claimed the life of a teenager when
15 she lost control of her vehicle and collided with an eighteen-wheeler in St. Tammany Parish;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, a lawsuit was filed in 2021 by her family against the State of Louisiana
18 and the Department of Transportation and Development alleging the cable barrier was
19 defective; and
Page 1 of 4
HLS 26RS-1091 REENGROSSED
HCR NO. 69
1 WHEREAS, on October 10, 2025, a single-vehicle, fatal crash occurred on I-12
2 eastbound near Essen Lane and Airline Highway in Baton Rouge when a vehicle struck a
3 concrete median resulting in a highway closure for over three hours; and
4 WHEREAS, on February 13, 2026, a fatal, three-vehicle collision occurred on I-12
5 westbound near the Albany/Springfield exit in Livingston Parish; and
6 WHEREAS, on December 19, 2025, a fatal, two-vehicle collision occurred on I-12
7 in St. Tammany Parish; and
8 WHEREAS, on October 25, 2025, a single-vehicle collision shut down parts of I-12
9 in Livingston Parish for hours; and
10 WHEREAS, on April 25, 2025, a collision claimed the life of a seventy year old
11 resident on I-12, west of the Louisiana Highway 441 exit in Livingston Parish; and
12 WHEREAS, the I-12 and I-55 interchange in Tangipahoa Parish presents ongoing
13 safety concerns due to heavy commercial freight traffic, complex merging patterns, and
14 recurring congestion, which have resulted in serious collisions, overturned freight trucks, and
15 traffic shutdowns; and
16 WHEREAS, recent litigation concerning I-12 has demonstrated that certain median
17 barrier and roadway designs may have impeded natural drainage patterns and contributed
18 to increased flood risk and damages to adjacent properties during major storm events; and
19 WHEREAS, I-12 serves as a critical evacuation and emergency response corridor,
20 and improving its drainage performance and flood resilience presents an opportunity to
21 enhance both public safety and infrastructure reliability during extreme weather events; and
22 WHEREAS, common improvements to interstates typically involve widening
23 projects, interchange reconstruction, asphalt overlays, concrete median protection, and
24 bridge replacements.
25 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
26 urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to implement
27 improvements to I-12 in the parishes of Livingston, East Baton Rouge, Tangipahoa, and St.
28 Tammany.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Transportation and
30 Development is urged to incorporate drainage capacity, flood risk reduction, and hydrologic

1 connectivity considerations into any evaluation, design, or improvement of I-12, including
2 the assessment of median barrier permeability, cross-drainage structures, and other measures
3 necessary to ensure that roadway improvements do not exacerbate flooding impacts on
4 surrounding communities.
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
6 secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.

HCR 69 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Wilder
Urges and requests that the Department of Transportation and Development implement
improvements to Interstate 12 within certain parishes in Louisiana.
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Transportation,
Highways and Public Works to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Add a clause that specifies that recent litigation concerning Interstate 12 has
demonstrated that certain median barrier and roadway designs may have
impeded natural drainage patterns and contributed to increased flood risk and
damages to adjacent properties during major storm events.

Section 3:
2. Add a clause that specifies that Interstate 12 serves as a critical evacuation
and emergency response corridor, and improving its drainage performance
and flood resilience presents an opportunity to enhance both public safety
and infrastructure reliability during extreme weather events.

Section 4:
3. Add a clause that specifies that the Department of Transportation and
Development is urged to incorporate drainage capacity, flood risk reduction,
and hydrologic connectivity considerations into any evaluation, design, or
improvement of Interstate 12, including the assessment of median barrier
permeability, cross-drainage structures, and other measures necessary to
ensure that roadway improvements do not exacerbate flooding impacts on
surrounding communities.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:

Section 5:
1. Add the parishes of East Baton Rouge, Tangipahoa, and St. Tammany to the
areas requesting improvements to Interstate 12.

Section 6:
2. Add clauses that detail a fatal vehicle collision in St. Tammany Parish on
Interstate 12 and an associated lawsuit against the state of Louisiana and the
Department of Transportation and Development alleging the cable barrier
was defective.

Section 7:
3. Add a clause that details a fatal vehicle collision in East Baton Rouge Parish
on Interstate 12 that caused a highway closure for over three hours.
