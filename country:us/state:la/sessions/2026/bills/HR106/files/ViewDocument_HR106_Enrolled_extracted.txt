HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 106
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREEMAN
A RESOLUTION
of New Orleans.
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was born on April 21, 1966, in Camden, New
Jersey, and is one of four children born to James and Helen Checchio; he was raised in
Collingswood, where he attended St. John School, and he attended Pope Paul VI High
School in Haddon Township; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio earned a bachelor of arts degree in philosophy
from the University of Scranton and studied at North American College in Rome; he holds
a master of business administration degree from LaSalle University and a doctorate of canon
law and bachelor of sacred theology degree from the University of Saint Thomas Aquinas
in Rome; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was ordained a priest for the Diocese of Camden
at the Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception on June 20, 1992; he held various roles
within the diocese, including secretary to Bishop James T. McHugh, vice chancellor, director
of communications, moderator of the curia, and episcopal vicar for administration of the
diocese; he also served in the tribunal and as a member of the College of Consultors,
Presbyteral Council, Investment Committee, and Diocesan Finance Council; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio served as parochial vicar at St. Agnes Parish and
assisted at St. Jude Parish, both in Blackwood County, New Jersey; he also served as a
summer parochial vicar at St. Peter Parish in Merchantville and St. Peter Celestine Parish
in Cherry Hill; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was appointed a Chaplain to His Holiness by
Pope John Paul II and granted the title of monsignor in 2000; he served as rector of the
Pontifical North American College in Rome from January 2006 to January 2016, during
which the college enjoyed its highest enrollment of American seminarians since the 1960s;
and

WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was appointed a Prelate of Honor, a papal honor
bestowed upon diocesan priests, by Pope Benedict XVI in 2011; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was named the Bishop of the Diocese of
Metuchen by Pope Francis on March 8, 2016, and was ordained and installed as the Fifth
Bishop of Metuchen on May 3, 2016; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio was appointed Coadjutor Archbishop of New
Orleans by Pope Leo XIV on September 24, 2025, and began his ministry with a mass of
welcome at St. Louis Cathedral on November 18, 2025; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio formally became the Fifteenth Archbishop of
New Orleans on February 11, 2026, after Pope Leo XIV accepted the resignation of Most
Reverend Gregory M. Aymond; and
WHEREAS, outside of his role with the Archdiocese of New Orleans, Archbishop
Checchio serves the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops as conference treasurer,
chairman of the Committee on Budget and Finance, vice chairman of the Committee on
Priorities and Plans, and member of the Executive Committee and Member Committee; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop Checchio is a member of the Papal Foundation board of
trustees, an appointed member of the board of trustees for the Basilica of the National Shrine
of the Immaculate Conception, a member of the Seton Hall University board of regents; a
member of the Seton Hall University board of overseers for Immaculate Conception
Seminary, a member of the board of directors for the National Catholic Bioethics Center, and
a member of the board of visitors for the Catholic University of America Busch School of
Business; and
WHEREAS, Archbishop James F. Checchio merits recognition for his service to the
Archdiocese of New Orleans.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Most Reverend James F. Checchio on
becoming the Fifteenth Archbishop of New Orleans.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
