Abstract: Creates the Allen Parish Economic Development District and abolishes the Allen
Parish Tourist Commission.
Proposed law creates the Allen Parish Economic Development District as a political
subdivision to provide for cooperative economic and community development among the
district, the parish, the state, and the owners of property in the district; to provide for
tourism-related activities to enhance the development of and improvement to the property
within the area of the district; to develop stable and more extensive employment

opportunities, to improve infrastructure; and to promote the overall welfare of the citizens
of the district. Provides for district boundaries.
Proposed law provides that the district shall be governed by a five-member board of
commissioners composed as follows:
(1) The president of the Allen Parish Industrial Development Board.
(2) The president of the Oakdale Chamber of Commerce
(3) The president of the Kinder Chamber of Commerce.
(4) The president of the governing authority of Allen Parish.
(5) The member of the La. House of Representatives whose district encompasses all or
the greater portion of the area of the district, or his designee.
Proposed law provides for the powers and duties of the district, including but not limited to
the following:
(1) To sue and to be sued.
(2) To adopt, use, and alter at will a corporate seal.
(3) To adopt bylaws and rules and regulations.
(4) To receive by gift, grant, or donation any sum of money, property, aid, or assistance
from the U.S. the state of La., or any political subdivision thereof, or any person,
firm, or corporation.
(5) To enter into contracts, agreements, or cooperative endeavors with the state and its
political subdivisions or political corporations and with any public or private
association, corporation, business entity, or individual.
(6) To appoint officers, agents, and employees; prescribe their duties; and fix their
compensation.
Proposed law authorizes the district to levy and collect a tax upon the paid occupancy of
hotel rooms and overnight camping facilities. Provides that such tax shall not exceed 3%
of the rent or fee charged for such occupancy.
Proposed law requires that the tax be paid by the person who exercises or is entitled to
occupancy of the hotel room or overnight camping facility at the time the rent or fee for
occupancy is paid. Authorizes the governing authority to contract with any public entity
authorized to collect sales or use taxes for the collection of the occupancy tax. Requires that
all monies be used exclusively for the benefit of the district.
Proposed law requires the board to prepare a plan(s) specifying services, public
improvements, facilities, and services proposed to be furnished, constructed, or acquired
which may be improvements, facilities, and services the parish is authorized to provide
pursuant to the constitution and laws of the state.
Proposed law requires the board to conduct hearings and disseminate information as it deems
appropriate or advisable. Requires that any plan developed by the board include an estimate
of the annual and total cost of acquiring, constructing, or providing the services,
improvements, or facilities.

Proposed law authorizes all state officers and agencies to render services to the district
within their respective functions as may be requested by the district. Additionally authorizes
the district and the agencies to enter into contracts, cooperative endeavor agreements, or
other agreements necessary or convenient to carry out the purposes of proposed law.
Present law creates the Allen Parish Tourist Commission as a political subdivision of the
state composed of all of the territory of Allen Parish. Provides that the commission is
governed by a seven-member board of directors. Provides that each member of the board
shall receive a per diem not to exceed $50 for each meeting they attend. Additionally
provides that the board members shall not be paid for more than 12 meetings in each year.
Present law authorizes the commission to levy and collect a tax upon the occupancy of hotel
rooms, motel rooms, and overnight camping facilities within the jurisdiction of the
commission, not to exceed 3%. Provides that the proceeds of the tax shall be used by the
commission for the operation of the commission and for the purpose of attracting
conventions and tourists into the area and jurisdiction of the commission, including but not
limited to the authority to spend money for advertising, promotion, and publication of
information, or for any other purposes authorized by law.
Proposed law repeals present law and provides that all remaining funds and property of the
commission shall be transferred to the Allen Parish Economic Development District.
Requires the district to hold the funds in a separate account to be used for the purpose of
attracting conventions and tourist into the area and former jurisdiction of the commission.
(Adds R.S. 33:2740.70.11; Repeals R.S. 33:4574(B)(3), 4574.1.1(A)(3), and 4574.14)
