10 §271. Short title
11 This Part shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Behind the
12 Counter Protection Act".
13 §272. Definitions
14 As used in this Part, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:
15 (1) "Department" means Louisiana Works.
16 (2) "Employee" means an employee who engages in duties at or behind a
17 checkout station, service counter, drive-through window, customer service desk, or
18 similar place or structure utilized as the point of transaction for an employee and a
19 customer.
20 (3) "Workplace violence" means any act of violence or credible threat of
21 violence, including but not limited to assault, battery, robbery, intimidation, verbal
22 abuse, threats with a weapon, or any conduct that places an employee in reasonable
23 fear of physical harm or is directed at an employee while he is performing his duties
24 at a regulated establishment.

26 No person shall commit the offense of workplace violence against an
27 employee of a regulated establishment. Whoever commits the offense of workplace
28 violence against an employee of a regulated establishment shall be charged with any
29 of the following crimes as appropriate:

HB 1238 Original 2026 Regular Session McMakin
Abstract: Enacts the La. Behind the Counter Protection Act, which prohibits workplace
violence against an employee of a regulated establishment.
Present law defines and provides penalties for the crime of simple battery.
Proposed law adds an enhanced penalty, which includes enforcing a fine of not more than
$2,000 or imprisonment, with or without hard labor, for not more than two years, or both
against a person who commits such crime against an employee of a regulated establishment.
Proposed law otherwise retains present law.
Present law defines and provides penalties for the crime of simple assault.
Proposed law adds an enhanced penalty, which includes enforcing a fine of not more than
$1,000 or imprisonment for not more than six months, or both, against a person who
commits such crime against an employee of a regulated establishment. Proposed law
otherwise retains present law.
Proposed law states that proposed law shall be known and may be cited as the "La. Behind
the Counter Protection Act".
Proposed law defines "department", "employee", and "workplace violence".
Proposed law prohibits workplace violence against an employee of a regulated establishment
and provides that whoever commits such an offense shall be charged with any of the
appropriate crimes pursuant to proposed law (R.S. 14:35(B)(2) and 14:38(B)(2)).
