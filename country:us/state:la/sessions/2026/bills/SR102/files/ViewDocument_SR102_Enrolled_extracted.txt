2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 102
BY SENATOR BASS
A RESOLUTION
To designate Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as Literacy Day at the Louisiana State Capitol and to
recognize The Center for Literacy & Learning for its leadership in advancing literacy
statewide.
WHEREAS, studies have shown that students who do not read proficiently by the
end of third grade are four times more likely to drop out of school and those students who
lack basic reading proficiency are nearly six times more likely to leave school without
earning a diploma; and
WHEREAS, dyslexia, a specific learning disability that affects as many as one in five
individuals, presents a significant barrier to reading success without proper support; and
WHEREAS, literacy is foundational to a student's academic success, long-term
opportunity, and personal empowerment; and
WHEREAS, a study, in 2022, found that seventy-two percent of Louisiana's fourth
grade students did not read proficiently, placing the state forty-second in the nation for
fourth grade reading achievement; and
WHEREAS, in 2024, Louisiana made historic progress by climbing twenty-six
places in national ranking and now ranks sixteenth in the nation for fourth grade reading,
according to the National Assessment of Educational Progress; and
WHEREAS, The Center for Literacy & Learning, founded in 1992, has for the past
thirty-three years implemented evidence-based instructional and coaching practices that
improve teaching and increase student learning outcomes from birth to twelfth grade; and
WHEREAS, the center draws on over three hundred fifty combined years of
professional education experience, focusing on closing the achievement gap through
targeted, high-quality professional development for educators of at-risk students; and
WHEREAS, The Center for Literacy & Learning employs a comprehensive literacy
wraparound approach, providing services to educators, families, and communities that
support learning both inside and outside the classroom; and

WHEREAS, over the past seven years the center has successfully advocated for the
creation of instrumental commissions that have shaped the state's literacy policies, including
the Early Literacy Commission and the Louisiana Literacy Advisory Commission; and
WHEREAS, in partnership with the Louisiana Department of Education, the center
launched the Literacy Coaching Program in 2020; and
WHEREAS, improving literacy across Louisiana is vital to building a strong
workforce, creating high-quality jobs, and sustaining long-term economic growth.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby designate Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as Literacy Day at the Louisiana State Capitol,
does hereby commend the dedicated work of The Center for Literacy & Learning, and does
hereby celebrate the notable progress in fourth grade literacy scores in Louisiana.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to The
Center for Literacy & Learning.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
