1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To request the legislative auditor to submit a report to the Senate and House health and
3 welfare committees regarding opioid settlement fund expenditures.
4 WHEREAS, as a result of the nationwide opioid epidemic, multiple states filed
5 lawsuits against companies that manufacture, market, promote, distribute, or dispense
6 opioids; and
7 WHEREAS, Louisiana participated in the nationwide settlement, which will result
8 in Louisiana receiving approximately six hundred million dollars in opioid settlement funds
9 during calendar years 2022 through 2038, to be used for opioid abatement strategy; and
10 WHEREAS, Louisiana developed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) between
11 the state and the parishes and sheriffs which serves as the governing document for all opioid
12 settlements and details the allocation and disbursement of funds; and
13 WHEREAS, the MOU establishes the Louisiana Opioid Abatement Task Force as
14 the advisory body over all opioid settlement funds for the state; and
15 WHEREAS, the Task Force contracted with the Louisiana Opioid Abatement
16 Administration Corporation to carry out the administrative responsibilities of receiving and
17 dispersing funds and receiving reports from recipient entities; and
18 WHEREAS, while the Task Force and Corporation have been successful in

1 establishing a framework for the administration of funds, barriers to local governments
2 spending the funds still exist; and
3 WHEREAS, a May 2025 report from the Louisiana Legislative Auditor showed that
4 parishes and sheriff's offices indicated that barriers to spending settlement funds include a
5 lack of knowledge of approved purposes, understanding of community needs, staffing,
6 expertise in substance use or opioid use disorders, and identification of programs and
7 services in the area that are eligible for settlement funds; and
8 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Department of Health, through the office of behavioral
9 health and the human service districts and authorities, has resources available to assist local
10 governments in identifying appropriate uses of the funds; and
11 WHEREAS, the legislature could also assist in developing programs to help meet
12 local needs; and
13 WHEREAS, in order to better help local governments and sheriff's offices identify
14 how to connect residents with treatment and help they need, the legislature needs more
15 information on current spending of the funds.
16 THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
17 request the legislative auditor to prepare a written report on opioid settlement fund
18 expenditures, in coordination with the Louisiana Opioid Abatement Administration
19 Corporation and the Opioid Abatement Task Force, and present the report to the Senate and
20 House health and welfare committees no later than December 31, 2026.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the legislature requests that the report includes
22 the following information:
23 (1) The total amount of opioid settlement funds received to date.
24 (2) The total amount of funds distributed to each parish and sheriffs' office.
25 (3) An itemized account of expenditures by each parish and sheriff's office.
26 (4) The purpose and classification of expenditures.
27 (5) The legal basis used to classify spending as "treatment" or "abatement".
28 (6) The outcome or performance metrics tied to each expenditure.
29 (7) The reporting compliance status of each recipient.
30 (8) Unspent balances and anticipated future allocations.
