SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 113
BY SENATOR BOUDREAUX
A RESOLUTION
Louisiana upon the passing of Martha Elizabeth Odom.
WHEREAS, it is with sincere regret and immense sorrow that members of the Senate
of the Legislature of Louisiana have learned of the passing of Martha Elizabeth Odom, on
April 23, 2026, at the age of seventeen; and
WHEREAS, Martha was welcomed into this world on May 23, 2008, in Biloxi,
Mississippi, to her loving parents, Michael and Brooke Bingham Odom, and later made her
home in Lafayette, Louisiana, where she flourished into a multitalented, compassionate,
intelligent, inspiring, and remarkable young woman; and
WHEREAS, Martha was thoughtful, intentional, and lived each and every moment
with purpose; and
WHEREAS, she studied and trained in classical ballet her entire life, maturing into
a talented and beautiful ballerina, and shared her talents on stage in ten performances of the
Lafayette Ballet Theatre's production of The Nutcracker; and
WHEREAS, the Ballet Studio was a formative place where she found joy, strength,
and peace, and through her dance training, she developed a love for teaching young dancers
and gained the discipline and inner confidence to explore and embrace other art forms; and
WHEREAS, throughout her high school career at Ascension Episcopal School,
Martha pursued theater, writing, and music with the same steadfast dedication she applied
to all her endeavors, appearing in five school musicals and three plays, earning recognition
in the Writes of Spring Regional Writing Contest for her undeniable gift as a storyteller, and
receiving multiple short fiction awards for three consecutive years; and
WHEREAS, in addition to her artistic accomplishments, Martha was a standout
softball player, playing on the junior varsity and varsity teams and earning freshman all-

district honorable mention; she was a leader on the varsity soccer team for four years, being
voted the happiest team player and team captain during her senior season; and during her
senior year she was selected for the varsity tennis team; and
WHEREAS, to further broaden her horizons, Martha was an active member in
several school clubs including the Creative Writing Club, Robotics Club, Beta Club, Mu
Alpha Theta, National English Honor Society, and Rho Kappa National Social Studies
Honor Society; and
WHEREAS, through her experiences, Martha emerged into a strong, thoughtful,
natural leader; she was selected to attend Louisiana Girls State and she served as Editor-in-
Chief of The Authored Ascension, her school's paper, as an Ascension Ambassador, and as
the communications officer for Rho Kappa National Social Studies Honor Society; and
WHEREAS, Martha's presence and abilities were noticed by many with school
counselors recounting how "Martha is undaunted by challenges, and even with her quiet
nature, she does not shy away from approaching a microphone, stepping into the spotlight,
or speaking her mind"; and
WHEREAS, in her free time, Martha could often be found curled up with a book and
a matcha beverage, or playing cards and board games with friends; she cherished car rides
with her sister, listening to their favorite songs on the way to school, and she was honored
to be presented at the Krewe of Gabriel 2025 Fall Ball, where she was escorted by and
danced the night away with her father; and
WHEREAS, upon graduation, Martha was accepted to attend the University of the
South in Sewanee, Tennessee, as a member of the class of 2030, where she planned to major
in English and Creative Writing and Rhetoric, and minor in Dance; and
WHEREAS, Martha was preceded in death by her paternal grandparents, Dr. James
C. and Martha Tolley Davis Odom and her uncle, James Calhoun "Cal" Odom, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Martha leaves behind family and friends who will cherish her memory,
including her parents; her sister, Margaret Odom; maternal grandparents, Kenneth and Joan
Bingham; godmother, Allison Clarke; her aunts and uncles, Brent and Gina Bingham,
Suzanne Odom, Cade and Dave Cheshire, Catherine and Tom Pankey; and a host of cousins
who loved and adored her; and

WHEREAS, Martha Elizabeth Odom was a light to everyone around her and
recognized and nurtured that same light in others; she carried herself with quiet strength and
grace and will be remembered by all those who knew her for her unwavering compassion,
wit, wisdom, sense of humor, and her breathtaking smile.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby honor the life of Martha Elizabeth Odom and her numerous accomplishments
during her short life and does hereby express the sincere and heartfelt condolences to the
family of Martha Elizabeth Odom.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
Martha's parents, Michael and Brooke Bingham Odom.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
