14 appointment; removal; terms

17 governor and subject to Senate confirmation as follows:
18 (1) Two members who are physicians from a list of names submitted by the
19 Louisiana State Medical Society. One of the members so appointed shall practice
20 in a parish or municipality with a population of less than twenty thousand people.

1 (b) The consumer member shall be a full voting member of the board with
2 all rights and privileges conferred on board members, except that the consumer
3 member shall not participate in the grading of individual examinations. The
4 consumer member shall serve at the pleasure of the governor.

6 governing body of a nominating organization provided for in Subsection B of this
7 Section.
8 D.(1) The governor shall appoint the members of the board in accordance
9 with other the provisions of this Section and the state constitution. The governor
10 shall ensure that his appointments demonstrate race, gender, ethnic, and geographical
11 diversity and shall consider an allocation of medical specialties that broaden the
12 expertise of the physician members of the board.
13 * * *
14 (4) The governor shall request a list of nominees from the nominating
15 organization when he intends to fill a vacancy on the board. The nominating
16 organization shall respond to the governor's request within thirty days and provide
17 a list of nominees within ninety days of the request. If the nominating organization
18 fails to meet either deadline provided for in this Paragraph, the governor shall
19 appoint a physician of his choosing.
20 (5) The governor may select the president of the board from the members of
21 the board in accordance with R.S. 42:4(B)(1). If the governor does not select a
22 president, the members of board shall select a president by majority vote.
23 * * *
24 §1266. Meetings of board; domicile
25 A. The board shall hold at least one five regular meeting meetings each year
26 and special meetings as are necessary. A special meeting of the board may be called
27 by the president ,or at the written request of a majority of the board members, at any
28 place time when a majority of the board, or its president, deems it expedient.

5 §1270. Duties and powers of the board
6 A. The board shall do all of the following:
7 * * *
8 (9) Appoint executive staff as appropriate to fulfill the mission of the board.
9 The board shall, at a minimum, appoint the following staff:
10 (a) An executive director who is the administrative head of the board, has
11 operational oversight over all departments, and perform other such duties as may be
12 prescribed by the board. The executive director is not required to be a physician and
13 shall not participate in discussions related to investigations and discipline of
14 licensees.
15 (b) A a director of investigations to who shall act as the lead investigator for
16 any complaint regarding a physician received by the board or any investigation
17 regarding a physician initiated by the board upon its own motion in accordance with
18 R.S. 37:1285.2(A). The director of investigations shall serve at the pleasure of the
19 board and be answerable directly to the board. The director of investigations shall
20 be prohibited from concurrently serving as the executive director of the board. Any
21 person appointed by the board to serve as director of investigations shall be a
22 Louisiana-licensed physician who maintains board certification and has engaged in
23 the active practice of medicine in this state for at least five years.

HB 1220 Original 2026 Regular Session LaCombe
Abstract: Provides relative the La. St. Board of Medical Examiners (board).
Present law provides that the board shall have 10 voting members who are all appointed by
the governor and subject to Senate confirmation.
Proposed law requires that all members of the board, except the consumer member, be
physicians.
Present law provides that two members of the board shall be appointed by the governor from
a list of names provided by the La. Medical Association in this state.
Proposed law modifies present law by removing the La. Medical Association as the
nominating authority.
Proposed law provides that two members of the board shall be appointed by the governor
from a list of names provided by Xavier Ochsner College.
Present law provides that a physician member of the board shall, at the time of appointment,
be a resident of this state for six months.
Proposed law modifies present law changing the resident requirement from six months to
five years.
Present law provides for one consumer members of the board.
Proposed law provides that the consumer member shall serve at the pleasure of the governor.
Proposed law provides that no member shall serve on the board if he is also a member of the
governing body of a nominating organization provided by present law.
Proposed law requires the governor to ensure that his appointments to the board demonstrate
diversity.
Proposed law provides that the governor shall provide a nominating organization notice of
his intent to fill a vacancy on the board. Proposed law further provide that the nominating
organization must respond to the notice within 30 days and provide a list of nominees within
90 days, if the nominating agency fails to meet either deadline, the governor shall appoint
a physician of his choosing.
Present law provides that the board shall hold at least one regular meeting each year.
Proposed law requires the board to hold at least five meetings each year.
Proposed law provides that the governor may select the president of the board from the
members of the board, but if he does not select a president, the board shall select a president.
Proposed law requires the board to hire an executive director of the board that has
administrative oversight of board and its subdivisions. Proposed law further provides that
