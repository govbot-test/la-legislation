HB 846 Original 2026 Regular Session Bayham
Abstract: Prohibits drivers from obscuring license plates with films, coatings, spray, frame,
bracket, or devices placed over the license plate. Additionally, defines "obscure" and
establishes penalties for violations.
Present law (R.S. 32:53(A)(3)) requires that a permanent registration license plate be
securely fastened, clearly visible, and free from foreign materials. Prohibits application of
any covering or substance to the license plate or using an electronic or electrochromatic film
that obscures the numbers, characters, registration sticker, or name of the issuing
jurisdiction.
Proposed law modifies present law by clarifying that the prohibition applies to any covering,
film, including electrochromatic film, coating, spray, frame, bracket, substance, electronic
and nonelectronic device, or any material over or on the license plate. Defines "obscure" as
impairing the visibility, legibility, or reflectivity of any feature of the license plate, including
the numbers, letters, symbols, registration stickers, or security features, when viewed from
any angle. Establishes a tiered penalty structure, different from present law (R.S. 32:57),
under which a first offense results in a fine not to exceed $75 and a fine of $250 for a second
and any subsequent offense.
(Amends R.S. 32:53(A)(3))
