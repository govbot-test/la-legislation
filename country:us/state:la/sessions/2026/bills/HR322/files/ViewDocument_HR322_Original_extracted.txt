HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 322
BY REPRESENTATIVE BRYANT
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To create the Task Force on Sentencing for Second Degree Murder to determine the best
3 changes to sentencing for second degree murder.
4 WHEREAS, second degree murder in Louisiana under R.S. 14:30.1 includes a
5 homicide committed with the specific intent to kill or inflict great bodily harm and a
6 homicide committed without intent during the perpetration or attempted perpetration of
7 certain enumerated felonies, commonly referred to as the "felony murder" doctrine; and
8 WHEREAS, pursuant to R.S. 14:30.1(B), a conviction for second degree murder
9 carries a mandatory, non-discretionary penalty of life imprisonment at hard labor without
10 benefit of parole, probation, or suspension of sentence; and
11 WHEREAS, in many other jurisdictions across the United States, a homicide
12 committed with specific intent or under the felony murder doctrine is classified as first
13 degree murder, whereas second degree murder frequently encompasses lower grades of
14 intent or lacks a mandatory sentence of life without parole eligibility; and
15 WHEREAS, a comprehensive evaluation is necessary to determine whether
16 Louisiana's second degree murder framework conceptually aligns with the legal definitions
17 utilized nationally, and to understand the specific historical and legal justifications for
18 applying a mandatory life sentence without parole eligibility to this category of offense; and
19 WHEREAS, there is currently a lack of centralized, empirical data indicating how
20 many second degree murder indictments and convictions within this state proceed under a
21 specific intent theory versus a felony murder theory; and
Page 1 of 3

1 WHEREAS, analyzing the exact volume and proportion of cases prosecuted under
2 the felony murder doctrine is vital for the legislature to make informed, data-driven policy
3 decisions regarding sentencing that is proportional to the offense.
4 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
5 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby create the Task Force on Second Degree Murder to
6 study, analyze, and evaluate the definition, application, and penalty structure of second
7 degree murder in Louisiana.
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall be composed of the
9 following members:
10 (1) The chairwoman of the House Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice
11 or her designee.
12 (2) One member of the Louisiana House of Representatives appointed by the
13 speaker.
14 (3) The secretary of the Department of Public Safety and Corrections or his
15 designee.
16 (4) The chairwoman of the Board of Pardons or her designee.
17 (5) The executive director of the committee on parole or his designee.
18 (6) The executive director of the Louisiana Sheriffs’ Association or his designee.
19 (7) The executive director of the Louisiana District Attorneys Association or his
20 designee.
21 (8) The state public defender or his designee.
22 (9) The executive director of the Louisiana Parole Project or his designee.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the chairman and vice chairman of the task force
24 shall be elected by a majority of the task force members at its first meeting, which shall be
25 called no later than September 1, 2026, and the members may select other officers as the task
26 force deems appropriate.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall meet at such times and
28 places as determined by its chair.

HLS 26RS-4340 ORIGINAL

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force constitutes a quorum
2 for the transaction of business. All official actions of the task force require the affirmative
3 vote of a majority of the members.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
5 without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be
6 individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Rules of Order of the House of
8 Representatives or applicable Joint Rules shall apply to all meetings of the task force.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that meetings of the task force are subject to the
10 Open Meetings Law, R.S. 42:11 et seq., and records of the task force are subject to the
11 Public Records Law, R.S. 44:1 et seq.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate following the
13 conclusion of its business or February 1, 2027, whichever occurs first.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall be staffed by the Department
15 of Public Safety and Corrections, which shall provide the necessary support and facilities
16 for the task force to perform its duties.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall submit a written report of
18 its findings and recommendations to the legislature no later than February 1, 2027.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to each
20 of the task force members provided in this Resolution.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall submit one print copy and
22 one electronic copy of any report produced pursuant to this Resolution to the David R.
23 Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772.
