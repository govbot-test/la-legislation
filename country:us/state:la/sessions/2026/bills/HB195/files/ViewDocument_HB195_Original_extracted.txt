HLS 26RS-858 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE BAYHAM
COLLEGES/UNIVERSITIES: Prohibits, with exceptions, a public postsecondary education
institution from prohibiting a student from possessing self-defense chemical spray
on campus
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 17:3399.20, relative to postsecondary education; to prohibit public
3 postsecondary education institutions from prohibiting the possession of self-defense
4 chemical spray on campus; to provide for definitions; to provide for exceptions; and
5 to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 17:3399.20 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §3399.20. Self-defense chemical spray; possession on campus; exceptions
9 A. Except as provided in Subsection B of this Section, no public
10 postsecondary education institution shall prohibit students from possessing
11 self-defense chemical spray on campus. For purposes of this Section, "self-defense
12 chemical spray" means a device carried solely for purposes of lawful self-defense
13 that is compact in size, designed to be carried on or about the person, and contains
14 not more than two ounces of chemical.
15 B. A public postsecondary education institution may prohibit students from
16 possessing self-defense chemical spray on campus in any building or at any event
17 where armed security is provided or where such possession is prohibited by contract.

HB 195 Original 2026 Regular Session Bayham
Abstract: Prohibits, with exceptions, a public postsecondary education institution from
prohibiting students from possessing self-defense chemical spray on campus.
Proposed law prohibits a public postsecondary education institution from prohibiting
students from possessing self-defense chemical spray on campus. Defines "self-defense
chemical spray" as a device carried solely for purposes of lawful self-defense that is compact
in size, designed to be carried on or about the person, and contains not more than two ounces
of chemical. Provides an exception to proposed law to allow an institution to prohibit
students from possessing self-defense chemical spray on campus in any building or at any
event where armed security is provided or where such possession is prohibited by contract.
(Adds R.S. 17:3399.20)
