LAW ENFORCE/OFFICERS: Requires peace officers to be citizens of the United States
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:2402(5) and 2404.4, relative to the qualification of peace officers in
3 Louisiana; to provide for definitions; to provide that peace officers shall be United
4 States citizens; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 40:2402(5) and 2404.4 are hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §2402. Definitions
8 As used in this Chapter:
9 * * *
10 (5) "United States citizen" shall have the same meaning as "United States
11 citizen" in 42 U.S.C. 9102(18).
12 * * *
13 §2404.4. Peace officers; United States citizens
14 In addition to all training requirements and qualifications for employment
15 provided in this Chapter and any other provision of law, in order to be certified as
16 a law enforcement peace officer in Louisiana, the person shall be a United States
17 citizen.
18 Section 2. The provisions of this Act do not apply to a peace officer who is currently
19 employed and who is lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States as
20 described in 8 U.S.C. 1101(a), provided that the peace officer becomes a United States
21 citizen no later than August 1, 2031.

HB 39 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Firment
Abstract: Requires peace officers to be U.S. citizens.
Present law provides for qualifications, requirements, and training requirements for all peace
officers in the state of La.
Present federal law defines "United States citizen" as any individual who is a citizen of the
U.S. by law, birth, or naturalization.
Proposed law requires any person seeking to be a peace officer in the state of La. to be a U.S.
citizen as defined in federal law.
Proposed law excludes current peace officers who are lawfully admitted for permanent
residence in the U.S. from proposed law if such peace officers become citizens no later than
Aug. 1, 2031.
(Adds R.S. 40:2402(5) and 2404.4)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Judiciary to the original
bill:
1. Exclude current peace officers from the requirement to be current U.S. citizens
if they are lawful permanent residents of the U.S. who become citizens no later
than Aug. 1, 2031.
