DIGEST
SB 477 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Price
Present law designates certain elected or appointed officials as part-time public servants who
are not eligible to participate in any public retirement system, fund, or plan sponsored by the
state of La. or any instrumentality or political subdivision.
Proposed law creates an exception allowing certain members of a board or commission to
become eligible to participate in a public retirement system, fund, or plan subject to a two-
thirds vote of each house.
Proposed law provides for the membership of the chairman of the La. Gaming Control Board
into the La. State Employees' Retirement System (LASERS), if appointed and employed on
or after July 1, 2024.
Present law provides that the governor shall fix the salary of the members of the La. Gaming
Control Board, not to exceed $15,000 per year, and that a member is entitled to a per diem.
Present law restricts the chairman of the La. Gaming Control Board from engaging in any
other business or profession or earning compensation other than his salary. The chairman
salary shall be that of a court of appeal judge. Proposed law allows the chairman to also earn
compensation as an adjunct professor.
Proposed law provides that the chairman be considered a full-time employee, eligible for
LASERS membership.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends 11:164(A)(3) and R.S. 27:14(B)(2); adds R.S. 11:411(16))
