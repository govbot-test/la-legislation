11 On page 3, between lines 5 and 6, insert the following:
12 "(l)(i) Within twenty-one days after a parent or guardian submits a
13 behavioral health treatment plan to a school administrator and no later than fourteen
14 school days after behavioral health services begin, the school administrator or his
15 designee shall ensure that a school-based service delivery review meeting is held in
16 person or virtually. At a minimum, the meeting shall include the school
17 administrator or his designee, the student's classroom teacher or teachers, and the
18 behavioral health provider. The meeting may also include the student's special
19 education teacher, case manager, or other school employee responsible for
20 implementation of the student's Individualized Education Plan, Individualized
21 Accommodation Plan, Section 504 Plan, Behavior Intervention Plan, or
22 Individualized Health Plan.
23 (ii) Behavioral health services may begin prior to the school-based service
24 delivery review meeting, if reasonable efforts are made to coordinate with school
25 personnel. A behavioral health provider may initiate and continue medically
26 necessary services, and the school shall provide reasonable access to the student,
27 pending completion of the meeting. Reasonable access shall include the ability to
28 enter the school, observe the student, and deliver authorized services during the
29 school day in coordination with instructional activities.
30 (iii) The school-based service delivery review meeting shall address, at a
31 minimum, the following:
32 (aa) Review of the provider's written treatment plan and any applicable
33 Individualized Education Plan, Individualized Accommodation Plan, Section 504
34 Plan, Behavior Intervention Plan, or Individualized Health Plan.
35 (bb) The role and responsibilities of the behavioral health provider while on
36 the school campus, including activities to be conducted in the classroom during
37 instructional time.
38 (cc) The classroom routines, school policies, and behavioral expectations
39 applicable to the student.

1 (dd) The method and frequency of ongoing communication between the
2 behavioral health provider and school personnel and the chain of communication for
3 questions or concerns.
4 (ee) The schedule for behavioral health services during the school day,
5 including how services will be coordinated with instructional activities, assessments,
6 and school-wide events.
7 (ff) Procedures for requesting a follow-up school-based service delivery
8 review meeting when the behavioral health provider changes or there is a material
9 change in the student's classroom or educational placement or in the behavioral
10 health provider's written treatment plan.
11 (iv) A behavioral health provider delivering services pursuant to this Section
12 shall use reasonable efforts to minimize disruption to instruction and classroom
13 management.
14 (v) Recommendations, concerns, or requested changes regarding classroom
15 strategies, interventions, accommodations, modifications, supports, or safety
16 procedures shall be communicated to the teacher and the school administrator or his
17 designee outside the presence of other students, except when immediate action is
18 necessary to prevent imminent risk of harm to the student or another person.
19 (vi) A behavioral health provider shall not direct, supervise, or evaluate the
20 job performance of a teacher or other school employee.
21 (vii) A behavioral health provider's role in the classroom shall be limited to
22 delivery of services to the designated student, which may include collaboration with
23 school personnel to support implementation of the student's treatment plan.
24 (viii) A behavioral health provider shall not implement or direct the
25 implementation of medically necessary services that knowingly interfere, impede,
26 or conflict with other mandated services without collaboration and agreement
27 between service providers.
28 * * *
29 B. For purposes of this Section, the following terms shall have the following
30 meanings:
31 * * *
32 (9) "School-based service delivery review meeting" means a meeting held,
33 either in person or virtually, for the purpose of reviewing the delivery of behavioral
34 health services in the school setting, including the behavioral health provider's role,
35 applicable student plans, classroom expectations, communication, scheduling, and
36 other procedures necessary to support uninterrupted instruction and appropriate
37 coordination with school personnel and the behavioral health provider.
38 * * *"
