HLS 26RS-1408 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE MILLER
NURSES: Provides relative to reconstitution of medications for intravenous therapy
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 37:933, relative to the administration of medication by a nurse;
3 to provide for qualifications to administer reconstituted medication; to provide for
4 the administration of certain medications; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 37:933 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §933. Distributing medications in public health clinics; administering reconstituted
8 medication
9 A. Nothing in this Part shall prohibit a registered nurse who is properly
10 licensed and recognized by the board as a nurse practitioner, nurse midwife, or
11 clinical nurse specialist, and who is employed by a municipal, parochial, or state-
12 operated or contracted public health clinic from inserting into the subcutaneous space
13 a medication implant or distributing a therapeutic regime of medication, to be
14 consumed by a patient off premises, to treat sexually transmitted diseases, or to
15 prevent pregnancy, provided such insertion or distribution is performed only under
16 a protocol approved by a physician licensed to practice medicine by the Louisiana
17 State Board of Medical Examiners. The distribution of the medication shall be
18 accomplished under procedures developed by the director of pharmacy of the office
19 of public health and approved by the Louisiana Board of Pharmacy.

1 B. Nothing in this Part shall prohibit a registered nurse who is licensed and
2 recognized by the board as a nurse practitioner or advanced practice registered nurse
3 from administering reconstituted medications for intravenous therapy if the nurse is
4 operating within his scope of practice and the medication is used in accordance with
5 the medication's labeling. The process described in this Subsection is not considered
6 compounding.
DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HB 962 Original 2026 Regular Session Miller
Abstract: Clarifies that a nurse practitioner or advanced practice nurse may administer a
reconstituted medication.
Present law establishes guidelines and procedures relative to licensing and governing the
nursing profession.
Proposed law provides that a nurse practitioner or advanced practice nurse working within
his scope of practice may administer a reconstituted medication for intravenous therapy in
accordance with product packaging.
Proposed law further provides that the process described by proposed law is not considered
compounding.
(Amends R.S. 37:933)
