HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 367
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
To continue the Clean Water Task Force to study the public water system in Shreveport and
report its findings no later than November 18, 2026.
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Department of Health implemented the Safe Drinking
Water Program that provides comprehensive drinking water protection to the citizens and
visitors of Louisiana, supervising over a thousand public water systems for compliance with
state and federal drinking water regulations; and
WHEREAS, the goal of the Safe Water Drinking Program is to prevent illness and
death that can occur from waterborne disease outbreaks or chemical exposure associated
with contaminated drinking water; and
WHEREAS, the Safe Drinking Water Program monitors collection, analysis, and
review of bacteriological and chemical samples to ensure compliance with maximum
contaminant levels, treatment technique requirements, monitoring and analytical
requirements; and
WHEREAS, the Safe Drinking Water Program conducts sanitary surveys and site
inspections to evaluate the capability of a drinking water system to consistently and reliably
deliver an adequate quality and quantity of safe drinking water to the consumer and to ensure
compliance with state and federal drinking water regulations; and
WHEREAS, Shreveport's water system has been facing a series of issues, including
a failing grade for the third consecutive year due to aging infrastructure, manganese levels,
and algae blooms; and

WHEREAS, these issues have led to taste and odor problems, as well as water
discoloration; and
WHEREAS, the city of Shreveport has experienced boil advisories in the past due
to water quality issues; and
WHEREAS, through state legislation that creates a political subdivision or through
cooperative endeavor agreements establishing an intergovernmental compact among the
stakeholders; and
WHEREAS, a collaborative effort is essential in order to develop responsible
practices to improve and maintain the quality of drinking water in the region.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Louisiana House of Representatives of
the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby continue the Clean Water Task Force, with the
following changes, to study the public water system in Shreveport, Louisiana.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall be composed of the
following members:
(1) The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health or his designee.
(2) The chief executive officer of Axom Development Company or his designee.
(3) A representative of the city of Shreveport appointed by the governor.
(4) A representative of the city of Shreveport appointed by the speaker of the House.
(5) One appointee from the city of Shreveport Water and Sewage Department
appointed by the mayor of Shreveport.
(6) The dean of the school of Allied Health from Oschner LSUS Health System or
his designee.
(7) The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Environmental Quality or his
designee.
(8) One representative of the city of Shreveport appointed by the mayor of
Shreveport.
(9) The president of Louisiana State University in Shreveport or his designee
(10) The president, chancellor, or his designee of Southern University at Shreveport.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall evaluate the current
condition, infrastructure, management, and sustainability of Shreveport’s public water
systems, and to provide recommendations to improve reliability, water quality, affordability,
and long-term resilience.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall do all of the following:
(1) Assess the condition of existing water infrastructure, including treatment plans,
pipelines, and pumping stations.
(2) Review water quality reports, boil advisory history, and public health concerns.
(3) Analyze water utility management practices, rates, and customer service
responsiveness.
(4) Identify funding gaps and potential state and federal support options.
(5) Recommend improvements to infrastructure, management, and policy.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall submit a written report of
its findings and recommendations to the House Committee on Health and Welfare no later
than November 18, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the member appointed by the governor shall
serve as the chair of the task force, and the member appointed by the speaker of the House
shall serve as the vice chair.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall be appointed
no later than July 1, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the chair shall call the first meeting of the task
force, and the meeting shall be held no later than August 1, 2026, and the first public hearing
by September 2, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall conduct public monthly
hearings at a public facility located in Shreveport until November 1, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the Shreveport City Council
staff shall provide staff support for the task force.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force may cooperate with all agencies,
departments, and political subdivisions of the state of Louisiana in all undertakings the
purpose of which shall be the furtherance of the objects and purposes of the task force.
