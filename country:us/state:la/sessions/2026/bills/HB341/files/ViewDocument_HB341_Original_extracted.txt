LAW ENFORCEMENT: Provides for rights of law enforcement officers while under

1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 40:2531(A) and (B), relative to the rights of law enforcement
3 officers under investigation; to require the appointing authority to provide an
4 employee or officer under investigation with certain information; to provide for
5 notice; to provide for definitions; to provide for technical changes; and to provide for
6 related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 40:2531(A) and (B) are hereby amended and reenacted to read as
9 follows:
10 §2531. Applicability; minimum standards during investigation; penalties for failure
11 to comply
12 A.(1) The provisions of this Chapter shall apply only to police employees as
13 defined by R.S. 40:1372(5), Louisiana P.O.S.T. certified probation and parole
14 officers employed by the Louisiana Department of Public Safety and Corrections,
15 division of probation and parole, and to those law enforcement officers employed by
16 any municipality and campus police employed at any state-supported college or
17 university who are under investigation with a view to possible disciplinary action,
18 demotion, or dismissal.
19 (2) Unless context otherwise requires, the following terms when used in this
20 Section have the following meanings:

1 (a) "Interrogation" includes but is not limited to any formal interview,
2 inquiry, or questioning of any police employee by the appointing authority or the
3 appointing authority's designee regarding misconduct, allegations of misconduct, or
4 policy violations. An initial inquiry conducted by the police employee's immediate
5 supervisors shall not be considered an interrogation.
6 B. Whenever a police employee or law enforcement officer is under
7 investigation, the following minimum standards shall apply:
8 (1) Prior to commencing a formal investigation of a police employee or law
9 enforcement officer, the appointing authority shall notify the employee in writing of
10 the nature of the investigation, of the identity and authority of the person conducting
11 such investigation, and of the specific charges or violations being investigated.
12 (1)(2) The police employee or law enforcement officer being investigated
13 shall be informed, at the commencement of interrogation, of the nature of the
14 investigation and the identity and authority of the person conducting such
15 investigation, and at the commencement of any interrogation, such officer shall be
16 informed as to the identity of all persons present during such interrogation. The
17 police employee or law enforcement officer shall be allowed to make notes.
18 (2)(3) Any interrogation of a police employee or law enforcement officer in
19 connection with an investigation shall be for a reasonable period of time and shall
20 allow for reasonable periods for the rest and personal necessities of such police
21 employee or law enforcement officer.
22 (3)(4) All interrogations of any police employee or law enforcement officer
23 in connection with the investigation shall be recorded in full. The police employee
24 or law enforcement officer shall not be prohibited from obtaining a copy of the
25 recording or transcript of the recording of his statements upon his written request.
26 (4)(a)(5)(a) The police employee or law enforcement officer being
27 questioned, whether as a target or as a witness in an administrative investigation,
28 shall have the right to may be represented by counsel, other representative, or both,
29 of the police employee or law enforcement officer's choice.

Present law provides certain standards of investigating law enforcement officers and
provides relative to the rights of such officers.
Proposed law requires the agency conducting the investigation to notify the officer under
investigation and provide him with the identity of the person conducting an interrogation,
as well as the charges or violations being investigated.
Proposed law provides a definition for "interrogation".
Proposed law makes technical changes.
(Amends R.S. 40:2531(A) and (B))
