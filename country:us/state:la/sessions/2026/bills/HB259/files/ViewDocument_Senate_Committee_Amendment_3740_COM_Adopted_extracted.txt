16 §1749.12. Definitions
17 As used in this Part, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them
18 in this Section:
19 (1) "Agricultural excavator" means a person who owns or operates a farm and
20 is directly involved in the cultivation of land or crops or who raises livestock.
21 (2) "Clear or no conflict" means a response provided by an owner or operator
22 of underground utilities or facilities when the owner or operator determines that its
23 facilities or utilities are not in conflict with the proposed area of excavation.
24 (2)(3) "Commissioner" means the commissioner of conservation.
25 (3)(4) "Damage" means any defacing, scraping, gouging, breaking, cutting,
26 or displacement of, impact upon or removal of an underground facility or utility or
27 its means of primary support.
28 (4)(5) "Demolisher" means any person engaged in the act of demolishing as
29 defined in Paragraph (2)(6) of this Section.
30 (5)(6) "Demolition" means the total or partial wrecking, razing, rendering,
31 moving, or removing of any building or structure, movable or immovable.
32 (6)(7) "Emergency" means any crisis situation which poses an imminent
33 threat or danger to life, health, or property, which requires immediate action, if such
34 action is taken. The term also includes an unplanned utility outage, which requires
35 immediate action, if such action is taken.
36 (7)(8) "Excavation" or "excavate" means any operation causing movement
37 or removal of earth, rock, or other materials in or on the ground or submerged in a
38 marine environment that could reasonably result in damage to underground or
39 submerged utilities or facilities by the use of powered or mechanical or manual
40 means, including but not limited to pile driving, digging, blasting, augering, boring,
41 back filling, dredging, compaction, plowing-in, trenching, ditching, tunneling,
42 land-leveling, grading, and mechanical probing. "Excavation" or "excavate" shall not
43 include manual probing, normal commercial farming operations, or any activity
44 resulting from force majeure related occurrences, including but not limited to an act
45 of God or an act of nature.
46 (8)(9) "Excavator" means any person who engages in excavation operations.
47 (9)(10) "Forestry excavator" means an excavator who is a logger, prescribed
48 burner, site preparation operator, or tree planter for commercial forestry operations.

19 facility as provided for in R.S. 40:1749.14(D). The mark-by time may be extended
20 if mutually agreed upon and documented between the excavator and operator.

29 private underground facility or utility which furnishes a service or material or stores,

36 liability company, corporation, joint venture, municipality, governmental agency,
37 political subdivision, or agent of the state or any legal representative thereof.
38 (17)(19) "Pipeline" means all intrastate and interstate pipeline facilities
39 defined by 49 CFR 192.3 and 49 CFR 195.2.
40 (20) "Positive response" means once a facility owner or facility operator
41 marks the location of existing facilities in the proposed excavation area or
42 determines that excavation or demolition is not in conflict with any existing
43 underground facilities, the facility owner or facility operator then notifies the
44 excavator of the status of the location request ticket by appropriate response through
45 the regional notification center's positive response system.
46 (18)(21) "Regional notification center" means any one of the following:
47 (a) An entity designated as nonprofit by the Internal Revenue Service under
48 Section 501(c) of the Internal Revenue Code and which is organized to protect its
49 members from damage and is certified by the Department of Public Safety and
50 Corrections in accordance with this Part.
51 (b) An organization of operators, consisting of two or more separate operators
52 who jointly have underground utilities or facilities in three or more parishes in
53 Louisiana, which is organized to protect its own installation from damage and has
54 been certified by the Department of Public Safety and Corrections in accordance
55 with this Part.
56 (c) An operator who has underground utilities or facilities in a majority of
57 parishes in Louisiana and is organized to protect its own installation from damage,

47 basis alone. The operator may comply with either of the following:
48 (a) The standard marking requirements and timelines set forth in this Chapter.

50 agreement already agreed to by other operators for the same excavation project.
51 Nothing in this Section shall relieve any party of its obligations under this Chapter.

53 excavator or demolisher to the regional notification center and attached to the
54 applicable large project excavation or demolition ticket. The written agreement shall
55 be available at the excavation site for inspection.

60 underground utility or facility. The operator shall have three hours from the time
61 documented notice is made to either mark the facility or provide a positive response
