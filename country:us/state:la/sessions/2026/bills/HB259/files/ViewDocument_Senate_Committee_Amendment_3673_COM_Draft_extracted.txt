18 §1749.12. Definitions
19 As used in this Part, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them
20 in this Section:
21 (1) "Agricultural excavator" means a person who owns or operates a farm and
22 is directly involved in the cultivation of land or crops or who raises livestock.
23 (2) "Clear or no conflict" means a response provided by an owner or operator
24 of underground utilities or facilities when the owner or operator determines that its
25 facilities or utilities are not in conflict with the proposed area of excavation.
26 (2)(3) "Commissioner" means the commissioner of conservation.
27 (3)(4) "Damage" means any defacing, scraping, gouging, breaking, cutting,
28 or displacement of, impact upon or removal of an underground facility or utility or
29 its means of primary support.
30 (4)(5) "Demolisher" means any person engaged in the act of demolishing as
31 defined in Paragraph (2)(6) of this Section.
32 (5)(6) "Demolition" means the total or partial wrecking, razing, rendering,
33 moving, or removing of any building or structure, movable or immovable.
34 (6)(7) "Emergency" means any crisis situation which poses an imminent
35 threat or danger to life, health, or property, which requires immediate action, if such
36 action is taken. The term also includes an unplanned utility outage, which requires
37 immediate action, if such action is taken.
38 (7)(8) "Excavation" or "excavate" means any operation causing movement
39 or removal of earth, rock, or other materials in or on the ground or submerged in a
40 marine environment that could reasonably result in damage to underground or
41 submerged utilities or facilities by the use of powered or mechanical or manual
42 means, including but not limited to pile driving, digging, blasting, augering, boring,
43 back filling, dredging, compaction, plowing-in, trenching, ditching, tunneling,
44 land-leveling, grading, and mechanical probing. "Excavation" or "excavate" shall not
45 include manual probing, normal commercial farming operations, or any activity

1 resulting from force majeure related occurrences, including but not limited to an act
2 of God or an act of nature.
3 (8)(9) "Excavator" means any person who engages in excavation operations.
4 (9)(10) "Forestry excavator" means an excavator who is a logger, prescribed
5 burner, site preparation operator, or tree planter for commercial forestry operations.
6 (10)(11) "Inclement weather" means weather that prohibits or impedes a
7 worker's use of his locating equipment or causes undue risk to himself or his
8 equipment such as lightning, heavy rain, tornadoes, hurricanes, floods, sleet, snow,
9 or flooding conditions.
10 (11)(12) "Large project excavation or demolition" means excavation or
11 demolition activity within a contiguous area that cannot reasonably be completed
12 within twenty calendar days for routine demolition or excavation or within thirty
13 calendar days for agricultural, forestry, or marine excavation or demolition activity.
14 (13) "Locate request" means a communication between an excavator or
15 demolisher and the regional notification center in which a request for locating
16 underground utilities or facilities is processed.
17 (12)(14) "Marine excavator" means an excavator or demolisher who is
18 performing excavation or demolition in areas such as swamps, wetlands, shallow
19 water, waterways, rivers, bayous, bays, lakes, the sea, and arms of the sea.
20 (13)(15) "Mark-by time" is the date and time provided by the regional
21 notification center by which the utility or facility operator is required to mark the
22 location or provide information to enable an excavator or demolisher, using
23 reasonable and prudent means, to determine the specific location of the utility or
24 facility as provided for in R.S. 40:1749.14(D). The mark-by time may be extended
25 if mutually agreed upon and documented between the excavator and operator.
26 (14)(16) "Normal commercial farming operations" means the following
27 operations or activities for agriculture cultivation purposes:
28 (a) Operations or activities that do not encroach upon a private utility or
29 pipeline servitude, public right-of-way, or a public franchise area.
30 (b) Operations or activities that do encroach upon a private utility or pipeline
31 servitude and the depth of the excavation is less than twelve inches in the soil below
32 the existing surface grade.
33 (15)(17) "Operator" means any person who owns or operates a public or
34 private underground facility or utility which furnishes a service or material or stores,
35 transports, or transmits electric energy, steam, oil, gases, natural gas, gas, mixture
36 of gases, petroleum, petroleum products, hazardous or flammable fluids, toxic or
37 corrosive fluids/gases, including telephone or telegraph system, fiber optic electronic
38 communication systems, or water or water systems, or drainage, sewer systems, or
39 traffic control systems or other items of like nature.
40 (16)(18) "Person" means an individual, firm, partnership, association, limited
41 liability company, corporation, joint venture, municipality, governmental agency,
42 political subdivision, or agent of the state or any legal representative thereof.
43 (17)(19) "Pipeline" means all intrastate and interstate pipeline facilities
44 defined by 49 CFR 192.3 and 49 CFR 195.2.
45 (20) "Positive response" means once a facility owner or facility operator
46 marks the location of existing facilities in the proposed excavation area or
47 determines that excavation or demolition is not in conflict with any existing
48 underground facilities, the facility owner or facility operator then notifies the
49 excavator of the status of the location request ticket by appropriate response through
50 the regional notification center's positive response system.
51 (18)(21) "Regional notification center" means any one of the following:
52 (a) An entity designated as nonprofit by the Internal Revenue Service under
53 Section 501(c) of the Internal Revenue Code and which is organized to protect its
54 members from damage and is certified by the Department of Public Safety and
55 Corrections in accordance with this Part.
56 (b) An organization of operators, consisting of two or more separate operators
57 who jointly have underground utilities or facilities in three or more parishes in
58 Louisiana, which is organized to protect its own installation from damage and has
59 been certified by the Department of Public Safety and Corrections in accordance
60 with this Part.
61 (c) An operator who has underground utilities or facilities in a majority of
62 parishes in Louisiana and is organized to protect its own installation from damage,

2 accordance with this Part.
3 (19)(22) "Routine excavation or demolition" means excavation or demolition
4 activity that requires no more than twenty calendar days to be completed, and no
5 more than thirty calendar days for agricultural, forestry, or marine excavation or
6 demolition activity.
7 (20)(23) "Service line or lines" means underground facilities or utilities
8 which provide power, gas, natural gas, communication, or water capabilities to a
9 building or structure or buildings or group of structures.
10 (24) "Ticket" means the notification sent by the regional notification center
11 to any owner or operator who has a facility located within the proposed area of
12 excavation.
13 (25) "Ticket number" means a unique identification number assigned by the
14 regional notification center to each locate request.
15 (21)(26) "Underground facility or utility" means any pipe, conduit, duct,
16 wire, cable, valve, line, fiber optic equipment, or other structure which is buried or
17 placed below ground or submerged for use in connection with storage, conveyance,
18 transmission, or protection of electronics communication system, telephone or
19 telegraph system, or fiber optic, electric energy, oil, natural gas, gas, gases, steam,
20 mixture of gases, petroleum, petroleum products, hazardous or flammable
21 fluids/gases, toxic or corrosive fluids/gases, hazardous fluids/gases, or other
22 substances of like nature or water or water systems, sewer systems or traffic,
23 drainage control systems, or other items of like nature.
24 (22)(27) "Wildfire" means an uncontrolled combustion of natural vegetation.
