2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 106
BY SENATOR BASS
A RESOLUTION
To express sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Vickey Thrash and to
recognize and commend her life of service, faith, and devotion to her family and
community.
WHEREAS, it is with deep sadness and profound sorrow that the members of the
Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana have learned of the passing of Vickey Thrash, at the
age of sixty-nine on Thursday, April 16, 2026, in Bossier City, Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Vickey Thrash was born on October 14, 1956, in Bossier City,
Louisiana, to Julius Shepherd Green Sr. and Mildred Bernice Tillery Green and was raised
with values of faith, perseverance, and compassion that would guide her throughout her life;
and
WHEREAS, Vickey dedicated the majority of her professional life to the field of
education, serving with distinction for nineteen years as a teacher at Airline High School,
where she positively impacted countless students through her passion for learning and
commitment to excellence; and
WHEREAS, following her time in the classroom, Vickey continued her service in
education as an assistant principal for seven years before assuming the role of principal at
Benton High School, where she led with integrity, strength, and a steadfast commitment to
student success for three years; and
WHEREAS, during her distinguished career, Vickey was the recipient of numerous
honors, including Teacher of the Year and Principal of the Year, reflecting her exceptional
leadership and unwavering dedication to the advancement of education in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, known affectionately for her warmth and kindness, Vickey will be
remembered for her radiant smile, generous spirit, and her ability to uplift those around her;
and
WHEREAS, beyond her professional accomplishments, she found great joy in
reading, traveling, especially to the beach, and cooking for her loved ones, always placing
her family at the center of her life; and

WHEREAS, Vickey was a devoted mother and grandmother, lovingly known as
"Sassy" by her grandchildren, and served as a constant source of encouragement and support
to her children and family; and
WHEREAS, Vickey demonstrated remarkable courage and faith as she battled cancer
for over a year, remaining steadfast and inspiring others through her strength and her deep
commitment to her Christian faith; and
WHEREAS, Vickey is survived by her son, Joshua Forrest, and his children,
Schuyler Heflin and Caid Heflin; her daughter, Whitney Clark, her husband, Jarrod, and
their children, Olivia Clark and Audrey Clark; her brother, Ronnie Green; her sister, Betty
Collins; her sister-in-law, Diane Green; and a host of nieces, nephews, cousins, and extended
family members; and
WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, Mildred "Mickey" Green and
Julius Shepherd Green Sr., and her brother, Julius "Butch" Green Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Vickey's life was a testament to grace, perseverance, and devotion and
her greatest desire was that others would see Christ in her life, a reflection of the love and
grace she carried each day, and her legacy will live on in the lives she touched, the students
she inspired, and the family she cherished.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby express sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Vickey Thrash
and does hereby recognize and commend her life of service, faith, and devotion to her family
and community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
family of Vickey Thrash.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
