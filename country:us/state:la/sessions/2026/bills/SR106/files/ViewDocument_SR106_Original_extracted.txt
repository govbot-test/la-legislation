SLS 26RS-2496 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 106
BY SENATOR BASS
CONDOLENCES. Expresses sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Vickey
Thrash.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Vickey Thrash and to
3 recognize and commend her life of service, faith, and devotion to her family and
4 community.
5 WHEREAS, it is with deep sadness and profound sorrow that the members of the
6 Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana have learned of the passing of Vickey Thrash, at the
7 age of sixty-nine on Thursday, April 16, 2026, in Bossier City, Louisiana; and
8 WHEREAS, Vickey Thrash was born on October 14, 1956, in Bossier City,
9 Louisiana, to Julius Shepherd Green Sr. and Mildred Bernice Tillery Green and was raised
10 with values of faith, perseverance, and compassion that would guide her throughout her life;
11 and
12 WHEREAS, Vickey dedicated the majority of her professional life to the field of
13 education, serving with distinction for nineteen years as a teacher at Airline High School,
14 where she positively impacted countless students through her passion for learning and
15 commitment to excellence; and
16 WHEREAS, following her time in the classroom, Vickey continued her service in
17 education as an assistant principal for seven years before assuming the role of principal at
18 Benton High School, where she led with integrity, strength, and a steadfast commitment to

1 student success for three years; and
2 WHEREAS, during her distinguished career, Vickey was the recipient of numerous
3 honors, including Teacher of the Year and Principal of the Year, reflecting her exceptional
4 leadership and unwavering dedication to the advancement of education in Louisiana; and
5 WHEREAS, known affectionately for her warmth and kindness, Vickey will be
6 remembered for her radiant smile, generous spirit, and her ability to uplift those around her;
7 and
8 WHEREAS, beyond her professional accomplishments, she found great joy in
9 reading, traveling, especially to the beach, and cooking for her loved ones, always placing
10 her family at the center of her life; and
11 WHEREAS, Vickey was a devoted mother and grandmother, lovingly known as
12 "Sassy" by her grandchildren, and served as a constant source of encouragement and support
13 to her children and family; and
14 WHEREAS, Vickey demonstrated remarkable courage and faith as she battled cancer
15 for over a year, remaining steadfast and inspiring others through her strength and her deep
16 commitment to her Christian faith; and
17 WHEREAS, Vickey is survived by her son, Joshua Forrest, and his children,
18 Schuyler Heflin and Caid Heflin; her daughter, Whitney Clark, her husband, Jarrod, and
19 their children, Olivia Clark and Audrey Clark; her brother, Ronnie Green; her sister, Betty
20 Collins; her sister-in-law, Diane Green; and a host of nieces, nephews, cousins, and extended
21 family members; and
22 WHEREAS, she was preceded in death by her parents, Mildred "Mickey" Green and
23 Julius Shepherd Green Sr., and her brother, Julius "Butch" Green Jr.; and
24 WHEREAS, Vickey's life was a testament to grace, perseverance, and devotion and
25 her greatest desire was that others would see Christ in her life, a reflection of the love and
26 grace she carried each day, and her legacy will live on in the lives she touched, the students
27 she inspired, and the family she cherished.
28 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
29 does hereby express sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the passing of Vickey Thrash
30 and does hereby recognize and commend her life of service, faith, and devotion to her family
