HB 403 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Coates
Abstract: Increases the annual sales amount to qualify for an exemption to certain food
safety laws for low-risk, homemade foods sold to public.
Present law provides that producers of low-risk, homemade foods may sell such foods to the
public and are exempt from food safety and sanitary requirements if the producer sells less
than $30,000 of low-risk, homemade foods annually.

Proposed law increases the gross annual sales limit for this exemption from $30,000 to
$50,000, after which a producer would no longer qualify for the exemption.
(Amends R.S. 40:4.9(B))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Health and Welfare to
the original bill:
1. Change the gross annual sales limit amount that qualifies a low-risk food
producer for the exemption to $50,000.
