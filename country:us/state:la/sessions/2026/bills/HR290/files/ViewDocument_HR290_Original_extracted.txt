2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION 290
BY REPRESENTATIVE DOMANGUE
affirming therapy on mental health
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to study the potential correlation
3 between medications prescribed for any gender-affirming hormone therapy and the
4 incidence of psychosis and related psychiatric conditions in individuals twenty-six
5 years of age and younger, and to report its findings to the legislature.
6 WHEREAS, the Legislature of Louisiana recognizes the importance of ensuring the
7 health, safety, and well-being of all residents of the state, particularly children, adolescents,
8 and young adults; and
9 WHEREAS, any gender-affirming hormone therapy may involve the administration
10 of medications including but not limited to androgen blockers, estrogen, testosterone,
11 puberty-blocking medications, and related pharmaceutical agents; and
12 WHEREAS, certain medications affecting endocrine and neurological function have
13 been associated in medical literature with possible psychological and psychiatric side effects
14 in some individuals; and
15 WHEREAS, psychosis and related psychiatric symptoms, including hallucinations,
16 severe mood disturbances, and dissociative symptoms, may significantly impair an
17 individual's functioning and quality of life; and
18 WHEREAS, the legislature finds that review and analysis of available scientific and
19 medical evidence concerning any potential relationship between gender-affirming hormone
20 therapy medications and psychiatric outcomes in minors and young adults would be
21 beneficial to healthcare professionals, patients, and families; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-1895 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 290
1 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Department of Health possesses the institutional capacity
2 necessary to conduct or coordinate such a study in consultation with qualified medical
3 professionals, researchers, and mental health experts.
4 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
5 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health
6 to study the potential correlation between medications prescribed for gender-affirming
7 hormone therapy and the incidence of psychosis and related psychiatric conditions in
8 individuals twenty-six years of age and younger.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study should include but not be limited to
10 all of the following:
11 (1) A review of available peer-reviewed medical and scientific literature concerning
12 psychiatric outcomes associated with estrogen and testosterone therapy, androgen blockers,
13 puberty blockers, cross-sex hormones, and related medications.
14 (2) An examination of adverse event data, where available, relating to psychosis,
15 schizophrenia-spectrum disorders, severe mood disorders, dissociative conditions, suicidal
16 ideation, and other significant psychiatric symptoms.
17 (3) Consultation with behavioral health specialists, endocrinologists,
18 obstetrician-gynecologists, psychiatrists and therapists, pediatric specialists,
19 pharmacologists, and other qualified healthcare professionals representing a diversity of
20 medical perspectives.
21 (4) Recommendations, if warranted, regarding informed consent standards, patient
22 screening practices, mental health evaluations, monitoring protocols, and areas requiring
23 additional research.
24 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health submit a
25 written report of its findings, and if needed, recommendations to the House Committee on
26 Health and Welfare no later than February 1, 2027.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
28 secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health.
