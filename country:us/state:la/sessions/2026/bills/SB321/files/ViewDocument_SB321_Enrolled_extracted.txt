SENATE BILL NO. 321
BY SENATOR KLEINPETER
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 328(C) and R.S. 22:822(A), relative
3 to electronic bonds; to provide for certain conditions on the use of electronic bonds;
4 to provide for requirements on electronic bonds; to provide for a parish registry of
5 electronic bond agents; to provide for procedures for the issuance of electronic
6 bonds; to provide for types of payment; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 328(C) is hereby amended and reenacted
9 to read as follows:
10 Art. 328. Bail undertaking
11 * * *
12 C.(1) An electronic bond may shall only be executed in a parish where the
13 sheriff has electronic bond software in place. Agents Only agents may be approved
14 by the sheriff to shall execute an electronic bond. A licensed bail bond producer
15 applying for electronic bond authority may shall make an application to the sheriff
16 and shall be domiciled and maintain a principal place of business in this state and
17 possess a Louisiana bail bond producer license for at least three years preceding the
18 date of application.
19 (2) The approving sheriff authority may require any documents deemed
20 necessary to verify the information contained in the application and may establish
21 additional requirements for approval of electronic bond authority within the
22 parish, including but not limited to geographic limitations, proof of local
23 domicile or principal place of business within the parish, geofencing
24 parameters, or other requirements that the sheriff deems appropriate for the
25 administration of electronic bonds within the parish.
26 (3) Any parish that elects to implement electronic bonds shall allow both
27 in-person and electronic methods of posting bail bonds.

3 Section 2. R.S. 22:822(A) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
4 §822. Criminal bail bond annual license fee
5 A.(1) On premiums for all commercial surety underwriters who write
6 criminal bail bonds in the state of Louisiana, there shall be a fee equal to two dollars
7 for each one hundred dollars worth of liability underwritten by the commercial
8 surety. Except as authorized under the provisions of R.S. 13:718(I)(2), this shall be
9 the exclusive fee or tax on any criminal bail bond premium, including thereto
10 premium taxes owed. In furtherance of the payment of this premium fee all
11 commercial surety underwriters underwriting criminal bail bonds in the state of
12 Louisiana shall, upon submitting the appearance bond and their power of attorney,
13 simultaneously pay to the sheriff of the parish, a fee of two dollars for each one
14 hundred dollars worth of liability on the bail bond being presented for the release of
15 a person on bail. Failure to pay the fee shall prevent the sheriff from accepting the
16 appearance bond and power of attorney. The sheriff may receive the fee by check,
17 or cash, or automated clearing house (ACH) and shall only accept it only from the
18 surety or the agent of the surety.
19 (2)(a) When an electronic bond is posted pursuant to Code of Criminal
20 Procedure Article 328, the sheriff shall accept payment of the fee by ACH
21 electronic funds transfer, and a licensed bail bond producer may collect from
22 the defendant, principal, or indemnitor any fees associated with the posting of
23 an electronic bond that are authorized by law, including but not limited to
24 platform access fees, transaction fees, and ACH processing fees.
25 (b) The fees shall not exceed the actual cost charged to the producer by
26 the electronic bond provider.
27 (c) In the event a surety or agent of the surety presents payment of the fee by
28 an instrument which is returned for insufficient funds, the agent or the agent of the
29 surety shall be prevented from presenting the appearance bonds with their power of
30 attorney attached until the outstanding fees are paid to the sheriff.
