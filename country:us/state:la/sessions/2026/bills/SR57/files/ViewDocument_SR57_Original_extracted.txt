SLS 26RS-1665 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 57
BY SENATOR CARTER
COMMENDATIONS. Recognizes the vital contributions of Tulane University to the state
of Louisiana.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, April 1, 2026, as Tulane Day at the Louisiana State Capitol and
3 to recognize Tulane University for its vital contributions to the state of Louisiana.
4 WHEREAS, Tulane University is one of the country's leading research and
5 educational institutions, home to the nation's oldest school of public health, the ninth-oldest
6 private university in the prestigious Association of American Universities, twelfth-oldest law
7 school, and fifteenth-oldest medical school; and
8 WHEREAS, Tulane University brings together scholars, students, and partners across
9 disciplines to turn discovery into meaningful progress that advances work in public health,
10 medicine, energy, innovation, and other areas critical to the Gulf South; and
11 WHEREAS, Tulane University plays a vital role in the state's economy, supporting
12 more than thirty thousand jobs, generating $88.2 million in annual state tax revenue, and
13 contributing an estimated $5.2 billion in annual economic impact; and
14 WHEREAS, Tulane researchers and physicians are improving outcomes for children
15 and families while leading advances in areas such as infectious disease, cancer, aging, and
16 brain health; and
17 WHEREAS, Tulane University's research enterprise continues to expand, which
18 attracts significant external funding and produces work that has both global reach and direct

1 impact across Louisiana; and
2 WHEREAS, Tulane University is making historic investments in downtown New
3 Orleans, including the revitalization of the iconic former Charity Hospital building, which
4 is helping to create a center of innovation, workforce development, and opportunity in the
5 heart of the city; and
6 WHEREAS, Tulane's efforts are strengthening Louisiana's position in health care and
7 the biosciences while opening new pathways for economic growth and job creation; and
8 WHEREAS, Tulane University is advancing leadership in the future of energy
9 through initiatives such as the Future of Energy Forum and the establishment of the Institute
10 for Advanced Energy Studies, both of which position Louisiana and the Gulf South at the
11 forefront of energy innovation and transition; and
12 WHEREAS, Tulane faculty are engaged in groundbreaking scholarship and
13 lifesaving research that supports national defense and protects the health of military
14 personnel and first responders, including efforts focused on traumatic brain injury,
15 biothreats, and diseases affecting deployed service members; and
16 WHEREAS, Tulane University is expanding access for Louisiana students through
17 initiatives such as the Louisiana Promise No Loan Assistance Scholarship, which ensures
18 that more students from low- and middle-income families can pursue a Tulane education
19 without financial barriers; and
20 WHEREAS, these initiatives complement such efforts as the Legislative Scholars
21 Program and the Mayoral Scholars Program, which provide scholarships for Louisiana
22 students; they also support student success and help retain talent in Louisiana, which
23 contributes to the state's long-term strength and stability; and
24 WHEREAS, Tulane University works closely with communities and partners across
25 the state, advancing shared progress and improving lives, and it is most deserving of the
26 highest recognition for its enduring contributions to the state.
27 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
28 does hereby designate Wednesday, April 1, 2026, as Tulane Day at the Louisiana State
29 Capitol and does hereby recognize Tulane University as an invaluable asset to the state for
30 its commitment to education, research, health care, innovation, and economic growth.
