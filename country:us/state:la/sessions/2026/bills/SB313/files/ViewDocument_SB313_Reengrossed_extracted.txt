SLS 26RS-560 REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR JACKSON-ANDREWS
CLERKS OF COURT. Prohibits clerks of court's vendors from charging additional
electronic filing fees in criminal and traffic actions. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 14.1(D), relative to electronic filings in criminal
3 matters; to prohibit additional fees from being assessed; and to provide for related
4 matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 14.1(D) is hereby enacted to read as
7 follows:
8 Art. 14.1. Electronic filings
9 * * *
10 D. Except as provided in R.S. 13:847, no additional fees shall be charged
11 by the clerk of court's e-filing vendor for electronic filings in criminal and
12 traffic actions, to either the clerk or the filer, including service fees related to
13 electronic filings.

The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 313 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Jackson-Andrews
Present law requires that all filings in criminal matters be filed in person in paper form or
transmitted electronically in accordance with a system established by a clerk of court or by
the Louisiana Clerks' Remote Access Authority.
Proposed law prohibits any additional fees from being charged by the clerk of court's e-filing
vendor for electronic filings in criminal and traffic actions, to either the clerk or the filer,
except for those certain fees authorized in present law.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(D))

original bill
