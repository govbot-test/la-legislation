WHEREAS, George "Buddy" Guy was born on July 30, 1936, in Lettsworth,
Louisiana, and rose from humble beginnings in Pointe Coupee Parish to become one of the
most influential Blues musicians in American history; and
WHEREAS, Buddy's musical career began in Louisiana, where he built his first
two-string guitar at age seven; and
WHEREAS, after working for a few years as a janitor at Louisiana State University,
he moved to Chicago where he recorded for Cobra Records and Chess Records and
collaborated with childhood idols and musical giants such as Muddy Waters and Howlin'
Wolf; and
WHEREAS, Buddy's raw emotion, showmanship, and blistering guitar style reshaped
electric Blues; and
WHEREAS, his unmatched guitar technique, electrifying stage presence, and soulful
artistry have shaped the modern Blues genre and inspired generations of musicians across
the world, including Eric Clapton, Jimi Hendrix, Carlos Santana, Stevie Ray Vaughan, Joe
Bonamassa, and countless others; and
WHEREAS, one of Rock and Roll's most iconic evenings took place at Buddy Guy's
Checkerboard Lounge on the South Side of Chicago on November 22, 1981, when members
of the Rolling Stones stopped by to watch their idol, Muddy Waters, perform, resulting in
one of the greatest jam sessions ever recorded, with Mick Jagger, Keith Richards, and
Ronnie Wood joining Muddy, Buddy, and other members of Chicago Blues royalty on stage;
and
WHEREAS, aware that history was unfolding, Buddy ensured the moment was
preserved for posterity by securing proper audio and video recording equipment; and

WHEREAS, Buddy's career spans more than six decades, during which he has earned
numerous accolades, including multiple Grammy Awards, a Grammy Lifetime Achievement
Award, a Presidential National Medal of Arts, a Kennedy Center Honor, induction into the
Rock & Roll Hall of Fame, induction into the Louisiana Music Hall of Fame, recognition
as a Living Legend of the Blues, and recognition as a "Louisiana Legend" by Louisiana
Public Broadcasting; and
WHEREAS, despite his international acclaim, he has remained deeply connected to
his Louisiana roots, often crediting his upbringing in Lettsworth and the musical traditions
of the state for shaping his sound and identity; and
WHEREAS, Buddy continues to tour the world, play at his Chicago blues club,
Buddy Guy's Legends, and ensures that the Blues endures as a great American art form; and
WHEREAS, he recently appeared in the Oscar-winning film Sinners, introducing
him to a new generation of fans; and
WHEREAS, Buddy has served as a cultural ambassador for Louisiana, preserving
and promoting the Blues as a foundational American art form and ensuring its vitality for
future generations through mentorship, performance, and education; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Senate wishes to honor Buddy Guy not only for his
artistic achievements but also for the pride he brings to the people of Louisiana through his
lifelong dedication to music, creativity, and cultural preservation; and
WHEREAS, July 30, 2026, marks the ninetieth birthday of Buddy Guy, a milestone
deserving of the highest recognition from his home state.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby commend and congratulate Louisiana, Blues, and guitar legend George "Buddy"
Guy on the occasion of his ninetieth birthday and does hereby extend its deepest appreciation
for his immeasurable contributions to the musical heritage of Louisiana and the world.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
Buddy Guy as a symbol of the respect, admiration, and gratitude of the Louisiana Senate.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
