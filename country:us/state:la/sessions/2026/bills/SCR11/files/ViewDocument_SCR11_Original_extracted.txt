SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 11
BY SENATOR FESI
tax credits to incentivize the hiring of resident Louisiana college graduates to live and work

1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To create and provide for the Anchor Home Task Force to study the feasibility of offering
3 tax credits to incentivize the hiring of resident Louisiana college graduates to live
4 and work in Louisiana during the first five years after graduation.
5 WHEREAS, Louisiana has a vested interest in retaining its residents, especially its
6 best and brightest college graduates, to live, work, and prosper in the state; and
7 WHEREAS, Louisiana has invested significant resources in the Taylor Opportunity
8 Program for Students (TOPS) to reward high school graduates meeting specific academic
9 requirements for the purpose of incentivizing academic excellence, encouraging top students
10 to attend in-state colleges or universities, and reducing the financial burden for qualified
11 residents; and
12 WHEREAS, Louisiana leads the nation in population loss for the second consecutive
13 year; and
14 WHEREAS, Louisiana is a consistent net exporter of young, college-educated
15 residents, suffering from significant "brain drain" as graduates leave for better economic
16 opportunities in other surrounding states, particularly Texas; and
17 WHEREAS, it is the hope that an incentive could be created that ultimately results
18 in a higher number of graduates from Louisiana colleges and universities choosing to live
Page 1 of 3
SLS 26RS-324 ORIGINAL
SCR NO. 11
1 and work in Louisiana after graduation.
2 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
3 establish and provide for the Anchor Home Task Force to study the feasibility of offering
4 tax credits to incentivize the hiring of resident Louisiana college graduates to live and work
5 in Louisiana during the first five years after graduation.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall propose recommendations,
7 by written report to the legislature and to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library,
8 as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772, no later than April 1, 2027.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force should include consideration of
10 the following:
11 (1) Type of incentive and financial structure.
12 (2) Strategic alignment with workforce needs.
13 (3) Targeting and eligibility criteria.
14 (4) Implementation and sustainability, including a comparison of average
15 compensation for targeted jobs within Louisiana and within the southern or other regional
16 average, maximum annual state financial obligation for the incentive, agency selection for
17 administration of the incentive program, and metrics by which to assess the success of the
18 incentive program.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
20 members:
21 (1) The senator from Senate District 20 who shall serve as chairman of the task force.
22 (2) Two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate.
23 (3) Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of the
24 House of Representatives.
25 (4) The secretary of the Department of Revenue, or his designee.
26 (5) The secretary of Louisiana Economic Development, or his designee.
27 (6) The secretary of Louisiana Works, or his designee.
28 (7) One member appointed by the Louisiana Association of Business and Industry.
29 (8) The commissioner of higher education, or his designee.
30 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each designating authority shall submit the
Page 2 of 3
SLS 26RS-324 ORIGINAL
SCR NO. 11
1 names of designees to the task force and the chairman no later than August 1, 2026.
2 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall convene for its first meeting
3 no later than September 30, 2026, at the call of the chairman, and at the first meeting, the
4 members shall elect officers as the task force may deem appropriate.
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force shall constitute a
6 quorum for the transaction of business. All official actions of the task force shall require the
7 affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
9 without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be
10 individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force, for purposes of its meetings and
12 accomplishing its goals, shall be staffed by the staff of the Senate Committee on Revenue
13 and Fiscal Affairs.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate on the date of the
15 submission of its report.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
17 secretary of the Department of Revenue, the secretary of Louisiana Economic Development,
18 the secretary of Louisiana Works, the president of the Louisiana Association of Business and
19 Industry, and the commissioner of higher education.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
