1 A RESOLUTION
2 To request the Louisiana Department of Health to study the feasibility of providing
3 suboxone to first responders in Louisiana.
4 WHEREAS, every year over 2,000 people in Louisiana die from a drug overdose,
5 accounting for over 2% of nationwide overdose deaths; and
6 WHEREAS, death from drug overdose may be preventable with attentive care from
7 first responders if they have life-saving medication available to them; and
8 WHEREAS, in 2014, Louisiana enacted a law to allow first responders to carry and
9 administer naloxone to individuals experiencing an opioid-related drug overdose; and
10 WHEREAS, up to 47% of patients refuse transport to the hospital after a naloxone
11 reversal, leaving them at high risk for a fatal, repeat overdose; and
12 WHEREAS, suboxone is a prescription medication that contains buprenorphine and
13 naloxone and helps people with opioid use disorder manage opioid cravings and reduce
14 withdrawal symptoms; and
15 WHEREAS, patients who encountered a suboxone-equipped ambulance were over
16 five times more likely to engage in addiction treatment within thirty days; and
17 WHEREAS, several states, including New Jersey and Delaware, and municipalities
18 have authorized certain first responders to administer suboxone; and
Page 1 of 2

1 WHEREAS, programs in these states aim to bridge the gap between emergency care
2 and addiction recovery.
3 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
4 does hereby request that the Louisiana Department of Health study the feasibility of
5 providing suboxone to first responders in Louisiana, including information on efforts in
6 other states and municipalities, any potential logistical and legal challenges, and
7 recommended protocols.
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health shall submit
9 its findings and recommendations to the Senate Committee on Health and Welfare and to
10 the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library, as required by R.S. 24:771, no later than
11 February 1, 2027.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
13 secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health.
