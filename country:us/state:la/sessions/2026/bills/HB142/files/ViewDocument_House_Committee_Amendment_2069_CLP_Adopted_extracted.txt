29 Comments – 2026
30 (a) The amendments to Subparagraph (A)(2) of this Article retain the rule
31 that abandonment is operative without formal order. The amendments also adopt
32 current jurisprudential rules providing that a step in the prosecution or defense of an
33 action interrupts the three-year abandonment period and that, after the three-year
34 abandonment period has expired, the defendant may renounce the defense of
35 abandonment. See Foundation Elevation & Repair, LLC v. Miller, 408 So. 3d 893
36 (La. 2025).
37 (b) Subparagraph (B)(1) of this Article is new and requires that a more
38 detailed affidavit be included with the ex parte motion to dismiss based on
39 abandonment.
40 (c) Paragraph C of this Article now requires the court to sign an ex parte
41 judgment of dismissal without prejudice if the mover’s affidavit attests to all of the
42 following: the abandonment period has expired; no timely step in the prosecution or
43 defense of the action has occurred; the defendant has not renounced the defense of
44 abandonment; no pleadings, documents, or judgments have been filed in the record
45 during the three years immediately preceding the expiration of the abandonment
46 period; and there has been no written communication pertaining to the action
47 between the mover and any adverse party or their attorneys of record during the year
48 preceding the expiration of the abandonment period.
49 (d) Paragraph D of this Article is new and requires the court to either sign
50 a judgment of dismissal ex parte or set the motion to dismiss for a contradictory
51 hearing if the mover’s affidavit indicates that pleadings, documents, or judgments
52 have been filed in the record during the three-year abandonment period or that there
53 has been written communication pertaining to the action between the mover and any
54 adverse party or their attorneys of record during the year immediately preceding the
55 expiration of the abandonment period.
