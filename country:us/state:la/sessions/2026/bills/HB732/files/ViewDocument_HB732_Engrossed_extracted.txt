BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
MTR VEHICLE/OFFICE: Provides for a temporary waiver of fees for final delinquent debt
based on special circumstances
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 32:8(E), relative to delinquent debt owed to the office of motor vehicles; to
3 require the commissioner to temporarily waive or suspend fines, fees, penalties, or
4 license suspensions for emergency or humanitarian reasons; to provide for the
5 verification of a life-threatening condition; to provide for temporary suspension of
6 obligations; to require a written decision concerning the temporary waiver of
7 penalties; to provide for the authority of the commissioner; to provide for an
8 effective date; and to provide for related matters.
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. R.S. 32:8(E) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §8. Final delinquent debt; office of motor vehicles
12 * * *
13 E.(1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
14 commissioner of the office of motor vehicles, or his designee, shall have emergency
15 authority to temporarily waive or place on hold, for a period not to exceed thirty
16 days, any fine, fee, penalty, or license suspension appearing on a person’s driving
17 record when the commissioner determines that such action is necessary, for
18 humanitarian reasons, to allow the person immediate access to life-sustaining
19 medicine or medical treatment.
20 (2) The person or their representative shall provide documentation from a
21 licensed physician, hospital, hospice, or other home health provider verifying the
22 life-threatening nature of the condition.

HB 732 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Owen
Abstract: Grants the commissioner of the office of motor vehicles, or his designee, the
authority to temporarily waive or suspend certain fines, fees, penalties, or license
suspensions for up to 30 days under certain circumstances and to reduce or eliminate
any obligation.
Present law (R.S. 32:8) allows the office of motor vehicles (OMV) to refer debts that are past
due 180 days and final for collection through the Office of Debt Recovery, while notifying
debtors of payment options and possible fees. Authorizes the OMV to adopt rules for
referrals and, through the commissioner, compromise or settle debts to reduce collection
costs.
Proposed law retains present law but grants the commissioner of the OMV, or his designee,
emergency authority to temporarily waive or suspend fines, fees, penalties, or license
suspensions for up to 30 days to allow access to life-sustaining medical treatment, with
documentation from a healthcare provider. Clarifies that the underlying obligation is not
forgiven, and the decision must be documented in writing by the OMV. Additionally, grants
the commissioner full authority to reduce or eliminate any obligation.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 32:8(E))
