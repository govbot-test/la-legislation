ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE BILL NO. 732
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 32:8(E) and (F), relative to delinquent debt owed to the office of motor
3 vehicles; to require the commissioner to temporarily waive or suspend fines, fees,
4 penalties, or license suspensions for emergency reasons; to provide for the
5 verification of a life-threatening condition; to provide for the suspension of certain
6 fees; to provide for the authority of the commissioner; to provide for an effective
7 date; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 32:8(E) and (F) are hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §8. Final delinquent debt; office of motor vehicles; temporary waivers and holds
11 * * *
12 E.(1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
13 commissioner, or his designee, shall have emergency authority to temporarily
14 suspend or place on hold, for a period not to exceed thirty days, any fine, fee,
15 penalty, or license suspension appearing on a person’s driving record when the
16 commissioner determines that such action is necessary to allow the person immediate
17 access to life-sustaining medicine or medical treatment.
18 (2) The person or their representative shall provide documentation from a
19 licensed physician, hospital, hospice, or other home health provider verifying the
20 life-threatening nature of the condition.
21 (3) For the purposes of this Subsection, "commissioner" means the
22 commissioner of the office of motor vehicles.

HB NO. 732 ENROLLED
1 F. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the hybrid
2 vehicle road usage fee provided for in R.S. 32:461(A)(2) is hereby suspended for
3 non-plugin hybrids until August 1, 2027. Failure by any person to pay such fee shall
4 not be considered debt that may be collected pursuant to this Section or any other
5 applicable collection authority.
6 Section 2. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
7 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
8 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
9 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
10 effective on the day following such approval.
