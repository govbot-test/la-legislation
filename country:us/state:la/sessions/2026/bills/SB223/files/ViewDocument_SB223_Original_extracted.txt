CRIME/PUNISHMENT. Creates the crime of failure to test for alcohol and controlled

1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 14:39.3, relative to assault and battery with related offenses; to create the
3 crime of failure to test for alcohol and controlled substances resulting in serious
4 bodily injury or death; to provide for elements of the offense; to provide definitions;
5 to provide for penalties; and to provide for related matters.

7 Section 1. R.S. 14:39.3 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §39.3. Failure to test for alcohol and controlled substances resulting in serious
9 bodily injury or death
10 A. A violation of this Section is committed by a motor carrier when all
11 of the following conditions exist:
12 (1) The motor carrier is required by 49 CFR Part 382 to test an operator
13 of a commercial motor vehicle for alcohol and controlled substance use.
14 (2) The motor carrier fails to conduct the testing required by 49 CFR
15 Part 382.
16 (3) The operator of a commercial motor vehicle who is required to be
17 tested by the motor carrier pursuant to Paragraph (1) of this Subsection, but

DIGEST
SB 223 Original 2026 Regular Session Luneau
Proposed law creates the crime of failure to test for alcohol and controlled substances
resulting in serious bodily injury or death. Proposed law is violated by a motor carrier when
all of the following conditions exist:
(1) The motor carrier is required by federal law to test an operator of a commercial
motor vehicle for alcohol and controlled substance use.
(2) The motor carrier fails to conduct the testing required by federal law.

(3) The operator of a commercial motor vehicle who is required to be tested by the
motor carrier, but has not been tested, causes an accident while operating the
commercial vehicle under the influence of alcohol or under the influence of a
controlled substance.
(4) The accident results in the serious bodily injury or death of another person.
Proposed law defines "commercial motor vehicle", "motor carrier", "operator", and "under
the influence of alcohol".
Proposed law provides that an offender will be fined not more than $25,000, or imprisoned
for not more than one year, or both.
Effective August 1, 2026.
