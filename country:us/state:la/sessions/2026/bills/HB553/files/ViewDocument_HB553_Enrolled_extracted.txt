ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE BILL NO. 553
BY REPRESENTATIVES CARLSON AND CHASSION
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 47:1907(E), (F)(1)(a) and (b), and (G)(1), relative to assessors;
3 to increase the membership of the assessors' certification program committee; to
4 provide for committee member qualifications; to expand the courses eligible to meet
5 the educational requirements for the assessors' professional certification program;
6 to expand courses eligible to meet educational requirements for the recertification
7 of assessors; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 47:1907(E), (F)(1)(a) and (b), and (G)(1) are hereby amended and
10 reenacted to read as follows:
11 §1907. Salaries
12 * * *
13 E. The assessors' certification program committee, hereinafter referred to as
14 the "certification committee", is hereby created to govern the assessors' professional
15 certification program. The certification committee shall be composed of five eleven
16 members who shall serve one-year terms and who may be reappointed. The
17 members shall be appointed as follows:
18 (1) One member shall be the chairman of the education committee Education
19 Coordinator of the Louisiana Assessors Association who shall serve as chairman of
20 the certification committee.
21 (2) One member Two members shall be appointed by the Louisiana Tax
22 Commission.

1 (3) Two Eight members shall be assessors who have met the requirements
2 for certification as certified Louisiana assessors (CLA's) or deputy assessors who
3 have met the Louisiana Assessors' Association requirements for certification as
4 certified Louisiana deputy assessors who shall be appointed by the president of the
5 Louisiana Assessors Assessors' Association. The president of the Louisiana

7 Directors' districts established by the Louisiana Assessors' Association.
8 (4) One member shall be a deputy assessor who has met the Louisiana
9 Assessors Association requirements for certification as a certified Louisiana deputy
10 assessor (CLDA) who shall be appointed by the president of the Louisiana Assessors
11 Association.
12 F.(1) The educational requirements for certification shall be as follows:
13 (a) A passing grade on the examination for the International Association of
14 Assessing Officers course 1 101-- Fundamentals of Real Property Appraisal.
15 (b) A passing grade on the examination for the International Association of
16 Assessing Officers course 2--Income Approach to Valuation or equivalent courses
17 offered by the Appraisal Institute 400-- Assessment Administration.
18 * * *
19 G.(1) Recertification shall be obtained by completing either of the following
20 requirements:
21 (a) A minimum of fifty-nine hours of continuing education offered by the
22 International Association of Assessing Officers or the Appraisal Institute, which
23 shall include the International Association of Assessing Officers Workshop 151
24 Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) course, the
25 Workshop 191 National USPAP Update, or an approved Appraisal Foundation one
26 or two-day Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) course
27 and at least one course, thirty hours minimum, on appraisal of property completed
28 by passing a written examination. The remaining hours shall be selected from
29 recertification guidelines as adopted by the certification committee. All designees
30 shall obtain recertification within a five-year period.

1 (b) A minimum of seventy-four hours of continuing education offered by the
2 International Association of Assessing Officers or the Appraisal Institute, which
3 shall include the International Association of Assessing Officers Workshop 151
4 Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) course, the
5 Workshop 191 National USPAP Update, or an approved Appraisal Foundation one
6 or two-day Uniform Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice (USPAP) course
7 and at least one course, thirty hours minimum, on appraisal of property, for which
8 no written examination shall be required. The remaining hours shall be selected
9 from recertification guidelines as adopted by the certification committee. All
10 designees shall obtain recertification within a five-year period.
11 * * *
12 Section 2. This Act shall become effective on January 1, 2027.
