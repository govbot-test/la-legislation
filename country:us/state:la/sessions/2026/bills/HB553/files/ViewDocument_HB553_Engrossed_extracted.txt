ASSESSORS: Increases the membership of the assessor's certification program committee

2 To amend and reenact R.S. 47:1907(E), (F)(1)(a) and (b), and (G)(1), relative to assessors;
3 to increase the membership of the assessors' certification program committee; to
4 provide for committee member qualifications; to expand the courses eligible to meet
5 the educational requirements for the assessors' professional certification program;
6 to expand courses eligible to meet educational requirements for the recertification
7 of assessors; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.

16 members who shall serve one-year terms and who may be reappointed. The
17 members shall be appointed as follows:
18 (1) One member shall be the chairman of the education committee Education
19 Coordinator of the Louisiana Assessors Association who shall serve as chairman of
20 the certification committee.

HB 553 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Carlson
Abstract: Increases the membership of the assessors' certification committee from five to
11 members and expands courses eligible to meet educational requirements for
certification and recertification of assessors.
Present law establishes the membership of the assessors' certification committee, totaling
five members, as follows:
(1) The chairman of the education committee of the La. Assessors' Association, who
serves as chairman of the certification committee.
(2) One member appointed by the La. Tax Commission.
(3) Two certified La. assessors appointed by the president of the La. Assessors'
Association.
(4) One deputy assessor appointed by the president of the La. Assessors' Association.

Proposed law increases the membership of the assessors' certification committee from 5
members to 11 members by adding an additional member appointed by the La. Tax
Commission and increasing the number of assessors and deputy assessors appointed by the
president of the La. Assessors' Association from a total of three to eight. Further provides
that the president of the La. Assessors' Association must appoint at least one member from
each of the eight Board of Director districts established by the La. Assessors' Association.
Present law requires an assessor to receive a passing grade on the examination for education
courses, including the Fundamentals of Real Property Appraisal and the Income Approach
to Valuation or equivalent courses offered by the Appraisal Institute in order to be a certified
assessor.
Proposed law retains present law but changes the educational course an assessor must pass
in order to be certified from the Income Approach to Valuation to Assessment
Administration.
Present law requires an assessor to obtain recertification by completing at least 59 hours of
continuing education courses offered by the International Association of Assessing Officers
or the Appraisal Institute and at least one course, 30 hours minimum, on appraisal of
property which requires the passage of a written exam. Alternatively, if the assessor does
not want to achieve recertification by passing a written exam, the assessor may attend at least
74 hours of continuing education courses.
Proposed law retains present law in terms of the hours of continuing education courses
needed to attain recertification but adds the Workshop 191 National USPAP Update course
and gives assessors an option to attend a one-day or two-day USPAP course in order to attain
recertification.
Effective Jan. 1, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 47:1907(E), (F)(1)(a) and (b), and (G)(1))
