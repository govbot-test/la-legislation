HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 212
BY REPRESENTATIVE BAYHAM
the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, April 29, 2026, as St. Bernard Parish Day at the capitol.
3 WHEREAS, St. Bernard Parish lies five miles south of downtown New Orleans,
4 Louisiana, and bestows scenic beauty, cultural treasures, bountiful waterways, and tranquil
5 surroundings to the environs of the southeast region of the state; and
6 WHEREAS, St. Bernard is a community of congenial people who have maintained
7 a unique cultural identity since its founding under Spanish colonial rule; Canary Islanders,
8 who identified themselves as "Isleños", immigrated to the lower edge of the parish to join
9 Creole-Acadian pioneers who settled there in 1720; and
10 WHEREAS, its extraordinary history is commemorated throughout the year as
11 visitors view historical sights such as the Chalmette Battlefield at Jean Lafitte National
12 Historic Park and Preserve where, in 1815, the nascent United States of America maintained
13 control of the Mississippi River trade route by the defeat of British forces during the Battle
14 of New Orleans on the plains of Chalmette; and
15 WHEREAS, the historic Malus-Beauregard House was built in the 1830s on the
16 battlefield grounds and is one of the most significant historic structures in the New Orleans
17 area; and
18 WHEREAS, the Chalmette National Cemetery was established in 1864 and sits
19 adjacent to the battlefield; the cemetery recalls the various military conflicts throughout
20 American history from the War of 1812 and the Civil War to the Vietnam War; and
Page 1 of 3

1 WHEREAS, the San Bernardo Scenic Byway follows an ancient estuary that was
2 once a main channel of the Mississippi River; this meandering roadway is festooned with
3 grand architectural wonders constructed in the nineteenth century; and
4 WHEREAS, an abundance of waterways and wetlands make St. Bernard Parish a
5 "Sportsman's Paradise" for fishing, hunting, trapping, and birdwatching; the parish is well
6 known for its fresh seafood and culinary delights that reflect Creole, African, Filipino, and
7 Spanish influences; and
8 WHEREAS, the impact of St. Bernard Parish's cultural cornucopia is evident in
9 year-round activities and festivals that reflect the heritage of this inviting southern
10 community, including the Battle of New Orleans Commemoration; the Knights of Nemesis
11 Mardi Gras parade; the Irish, Italian, and Isleños parades; the Los Isleños Festival; the
12 Louisiana Crawfish Festival; the Tomato Festival; the Sugar Festival; the St. Bernard Salutes
13 America Celebration; the Blessing of the Fleet; Trick or Trunk; the Oyster Festival; Fiesta
14 de la Concepcion; All Saints Day Mass and Celebration; Isleños Christmas; the Christmas
15 Tour of Homes; the Community Christmas Concert; and Santa on the Bayou; and
16 WHEREAS, ancient oaks, magnolias, and other fragrant flora align with ageless
17 plantation homes and cemeteries where the past and present meet and come alive; and
18 WHEREAS, the Old Arabi Historic District houses several sites that are recognized
19 by the National Register of Historic Places, including St. Bernard Voice, the weekly
20 community newspaper that dates back to 1870; the Old Jail, which was constructed in 1911;
21 the Maumus Center, which is the site of the parish's first high school; the Cavaroc House,
22 which was built in 1839; and the Domino Sugar Refinery; and
23 WHEREAS, the people, businesses, and governmental entities of St. Bernard Parish
24 displayed their resilience in rebuilding their community after its near-total destruction by
25 Hurricane Katrina on August 29, 2005; and
26 WHEREAS, the multicultural oasis that is St. Bernard Parish is a medley of
27 waterways, townships, and historic destinations teeming with a kaleidoscope of attractions
28 and interesting people that lie nestled in the lush natural beauty of Southeastern Louisiana.
29 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
30 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 29, 2026, as St. Bernard
