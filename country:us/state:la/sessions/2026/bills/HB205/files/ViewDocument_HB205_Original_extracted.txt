ELECTIONS/COMMISSIONERS: Provides for compensation for election commissioners
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 18:426.1, relative to compensation for election commissioners;
3 to increase compensation paid to certain election day commissioners; to provide for
4 the scope of compensated services; to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for
5 related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 18:426.1 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
8 §426.1. Election commissioners; compensation
9 A. Each commissioner who serves at the polling place on election day shall
10 be paid according to the following:
11 (1) A commissioner-in-charge shall receive two three hundred fifty dollars.
12 (2) A commissioner-in-charge who serves at more than one precinct shall
13 receive three four hundred fifty dollars.
14 (3) A commissioner who has received a certificate of instruction, as provided
15 in R.S. 18:431(A), shall receive one hundred dollars.
16 (4) A commissioner who has received a certificate of instruction, as provided
17 in R.S. 18:431(B), shall receive two three hundred dollars.
18 (5) An uncertified commissioner shall receive thirty-five dollars.

HB 205 Original 2026 Regular Session Bacala
Abstract: Increases compensation for certain classes of election commissioners.
Present law provides that each commissioner who serves at the polling place on election day
shall be paid according to the following:
(1) A commissioner-in-charge shall receive $250. Proposed law increases such pay to
$350.
(2) A commissioner-in-charge who serves at more than one precinct shall receive $350.
Proposed law increases such pay to $450.
(3) A commissioner who has received a certificate of instruction for commissioners shall
receive $100. Proposed law retains present law.
(4) A commissioner who has received a certificate of instruction for commissioners-in-
charge shall receive $200. Proposed law increases such pay to $300.
(5) An uncertified commissioner shall receive thirty-five dollars. Proposed law retains
present law.
Proposed law provides that the compensation as provided for in proposed law shall serve as
compensation for time worked on election day and for participation in any courses of
instruction provided to election officials.
Present law provides that for an election held within one year following the date of the
issuance of any gubernatorial declaration of an emergency, upon application of a parish
board of election supervisors, the secretary of state may approve payment of up to $100 per
election or each day of assistance, if applicable, as compensation to each commissioner in
addition to compensation provided for by proposed law.
