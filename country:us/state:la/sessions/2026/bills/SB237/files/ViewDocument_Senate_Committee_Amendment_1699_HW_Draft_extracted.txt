18 §52.2. Incidents involving fatality or near fatality; public website
19 The Department of Children and Family Services shall promptly provide
20 information to the public regarding a substantiated case of child abuse or
21 neglect that has resulted in a fatality or near fatality on its public website as
22 follows:
23 (1) The department shall provide the following preliminary information:
24 (a) The age, gender, parish, and general location of the residence of the
25 child who has suffered a fatality or a near fatality.
26 (b) The fact that a child suffered a fatality or near fatality as the result
27 of abuse or neglect.
28 (c) The name, city, town, or general location of the residence of the
29 alleged perpetrator, if available, unless the disclosure would violate any privacy
30 laws of this state.
31 (d) Whether there have been reports, or any current or past cases, of
32 abuse or neglect involving the child or the alleged perpetrator.
33 (e) Actions taken by the department in response to the fatality or near
34 fatality of the child.
35 (f) A detailed synopsis of prior reports or cases of abuse or neglect
36 involving the child or the alleged perpetrator and of the actions taken or
37 determinations made by the department in response to these reports or cases.
38 (2) Each fatality or near fatality shall be reviewed by a critical incident
39 review team in accordance with R.S. 46:52.3.
40 (3) Within one hundred twenty days after the date of the report for a
41 case involving a fatality or a near fatality, the department shall provide to the
42 public a summary report that includes any actions taken by the department in
43 response to the case, any changes in policies or practices that have been made
44 to address any issues raised in the review of the case, and any recommendations
45 made by the critical incident review team for further changes in policies,
46 practices, rules, or statutes to address those issues.
47 §52.3. Critical incident review
48 A. Each case of child abuse or neglect received by the department that
49 has resulted in a fatality or near fatality, whether substantiated or
50 unsubstantiated by the department, shall be reviewed by a multidisciplinary
51 critical incident review team as provided for in this Section.
52 B. The team shall consist of the following members:
53 (1) A representative of the Department of Children and Family Services
54 designated by the secretary of the Department of Children and Family Services.
55 (2) The chief medical director of the Department of Children and Family
56 Services or his designee.

49 (3) In no instance shall the name or identifying information of reporter
50 in a case of abuse or neglect be disclosed to the review team.
51 (4) The furnishing of confidential information, documents, and reports
52 in accordance with this Section by any person, agency, or entity furnishing such
53 information, documents, and reports shall not expose such person, agency, or
