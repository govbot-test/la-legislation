Section 1:
ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE MUSCARELLO
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 13:754(F)(3), relative to recordation of documents with the clerk of court; to
3 provide for a fee for recording documents; to provide for the deposit of the funds; to
4 authorize cooperative agreements; to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for
5 related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 13:754(F)(3) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §754. Louisiana Clerks' Remote Access Authority; membership; board of
9 commission; statewide portal
10 * * *
11 F.
12 * * *
13 (3) An additional fee of five dollars shall apply to each recording subject to
14 the provisions of Paragraph (1) of this Subsection. The member shall retain fifty
15 cents of the fee provided for in this Paragraph. The remainder of the fee shall then
16 be remitted to LCRAA by the tenth of the month. The LCRAA shall retain fifty cents
17 of the fee and remit the remainder to the Court Modernization and Technology Fund,
18 created pursuant to R.S. 39:100.211, to be dedicated for those purposes including
19 cooperation with LCRAA concerning statewide court electronic filing integration.
20 The Louisiana Supreme Court and appellate courts shall enter into cooperative
21 agreements concerning the delivery of a single filing platform for the appellate

1 courts with the goal of full integration between all appellate courts and the statewide
2 LCRAA filing platform.
3 * * *
4 Section 2. The additional fee for recordings required by R.S. 13:754(F)(3) as enacted
5 by Section 1 of this Act shall apply on January 1, 2027, and thereafter.
6 Section 3. The LCRAA shall provide a report to the legislature no later than the start
7 of the 2028 Regular Session of the Legislature of Louisiana detailing the total dollars
8 collected pursuant to R.S. 13:754(F)(3) and the progress made on the delivery of an
9 integrated single filing platform between all appellate courts and the statewide LCRAA
10 filing platform.
11 Section 4. The provisions of R.S. 13:754(F)(3) shall expire on August 1, 2029,
12 unless extended by legislative Act.
