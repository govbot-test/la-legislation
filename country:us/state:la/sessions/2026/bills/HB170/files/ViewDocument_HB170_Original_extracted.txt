HOUSE BILL NO. 170
BY REPRESENTATIVE RISER AND SENATOR CATHEY
CONTRACTS: Provides relative to construction contracts
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 9:2779, relative to construction contracts; to provide for
3 definitions; to provide for technical changes; and to provide for related matters.
4 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
5 Section 1. R.S. 9:2779 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
6 §2779. Construction contracts, subcontracts, and purchase orders; certain provisions
7 invalid
8 A.(1) For purposes of this Chapter, "construction contract" means any
9 agreement for the design, construction, alteration, renovation, repair, or maintenance
10 of a building, structure, highway, road, bridge, water line, sewer line, oil line, gas
11 line, appurtenance, or other improvement to real property, or repair or maintenance
12 of a highway, road, or bridge, including any moving, demolition, or excavation.
13 (2) "Construction contract" does not include any design, construction,
14 alteration, renovation, repair, or maintenance of the following:
15 (a) Any dirt or gravel road used to access oil and gas wells and associated
16 facilities.
17 (b) Oil flow lines or gas gathering lines used in association with the
18 transportation of production from oil and gas wells from the point that oil and gas
19 becomes comingled for transportation to oil storage facilities or gas transmission
20 lines.

1 (c) Any deed, lease, easement, license, or other instrument granting an
2 interest in or the right to possess property even if the instrument includes the right
3 to design, construct, alter, renovate, repair, or maintain improvements on such real
4 property.
5 A. B. The legislature finds that, with respect to construction contracts,
6 subcontracts, and purchase orders for public and private works projects, when one
7 of the parties is domiciled in Louisiana, and the work to be done and the equipment
8 and materials to be supplied involve construction projects in this state, provisions in
9 any provision in such agreements requiring disputes arising thereunder to be resolved
10 in a forum outside of this state or requiring their interpretation to be governed by the
11 laws of another jurisdiction are inequitable and against the public policy of this state.
12 B. C. The legislature hereby declares null, and void, and unenforceable as
13 against public policy any provision in a contract, subcontract, or purchase order, as
14 described in Subsection A B of this Section, which either:
15 (1) Requires a suit or arbitration proceeding to be brought in a forum or
16 jurisdiction outside of this state; rather, such actions or proceedings may be pursued
17 in accordance with the Louisiana Code of Civil Procedure or other laws of this state
18 governing similar actions.
19 (2) Requires interpretation of the agreement according to the laws of another
20 jurisdiction.
21 C. D. The provisions of this Section apply to contracts, subcontracts, and
22 purchase orders, as described in Subsection A, B of this Section entered into on or
23 after September 6, 1991.
24 D. E. Notwithstanding any other provisions of law to the contrary, the
25 provisions of this Section shall do not apply to negotiated labor contracts.

HB 170 Original 2026 Regular Session Riser
Abstract: Provides a definition for "construction contract".
Present law provides relative to conflict of laws and the proper forum for disputes involving
construction contracts, subcontracts, and purchase orders for public and private works
projects.
Proposed law retains present law. Defines "construction contract" and makes technical
changes.
(Amends R.S. 9:2779)
