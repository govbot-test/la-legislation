HLS 26RS-4452 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 383
BY REPRESENTATIVE JORDAN
CIVIL SERVICE/FIRE & POL: Creates a task force to examine the position of fire chief
in the fire and police civil service
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To create the Task Force on Fire Chief Operations to examine the position of fire chief in
3 the fire and police civil service, including providing for a uniform definition in state
4 law for the position of fire chief, and to provide a written report of its findings and
5 recommendations to the members of the House of Representatives no later than thirty
6 days prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
7 WHEREAS, Article X, Section 16 of the Constitution of Louisiana provides for a
8 system of classified fire and police civil service that is applicable to all municipalities having
9 a population exceeding thirteen thousand persons and operating a regularly paid fire and
10 police department and to all parishes and fire protection districts operating a regularly paid
11 fire department; and
12 WHEREAS, R.S. 33:2531 et seq. additionally provides for a system of classified fire
13 and police civil service that is applicable to all municipalities having a population of not less
14 than seven thousand persons and not more than thirteen thousand persons; and
15 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Legislature has enacted a number of bills over the years
16 to change the fire and police civil service laws, and these changes have directly impacted the
17 employment of firemen and policemen throughout the state of Louisiana; and
18 WHEREAS, although there have been many changes to the fire and police civil
19 service laws, these changes have not provided for a uniform definition for the position of fire
20 chief; and

1 WHEREAS, a fire chief plays a crucial role in ensuring the efficient and effective
2 functioning of a fire department; and
3 WHEREAS, the fire chief is the top executive in a fire department, responsible for
4 planning, directing, and coordinating the department's activities to protect life and property;
5 and
6 WHEREAS, the fire chief has ultimate supervisory authority over operations,
7 personnel, and administration of a fire department and is directly responsible to the
8 municipal, parish, or fire protection government or the appointing authority for the
9 management and performance of the department; and
10 WHEREAS, a fire chief's duties may include fire suppression, emergency medical
11 services, hazardous materials response, and disaster management; and
12 WHEREAS, because a fire chief has numerous duties and responsibilities, the
13 position of fire chief should be a full-time position, and no person should serve as fire chief
14 on a part-time, volunteer, or purely contractual basis; and
15 WHEREAS, it is essential to provide for a comprehensive review of the duties and
16 responsibilities of fire chiefs throughout the state and to provide for a uniform definition in
17 state law for the position of fire chief.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
19 establish the Task Force on Fire Chief Operations to examine the position of fire chief in the
20 fire and police civil service, including providing for a uniform definition in state law for the
21 position of fire chief, and to provide a written report of its findings and recommendations
22 to the members of the House of Representatives and to the David R. Poynter Legislative
23 Research Library as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772 no later than thirty days prior to the
24 convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
26 members:
27 (1) The chairman of the House Committee on Municipal, Parochial and Cultural
28 Affairs or his designee.
29 (2) The chairman of the House Committee on Commerce or his designee.
30 (3) The state examiner of municipal fire and police civil service or his designee.

19 the affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
21 without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be

24 Open Meetings Law, R.S. 42:11 et seq., and records of the task force shall be subject to the

26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate following the
27 conclusion of its business or February 15, 2027, whichever occurs first.

Creates the Task Force on Fire Chief Operations to examine the position of fire chief in the
fire and police civil service, including providing for a uniform definition in state law for the
position of fire chief. Requires the task force to provide a written report of its findings and
recommendations to the members of the House of Representatives no later than 30 days prior
