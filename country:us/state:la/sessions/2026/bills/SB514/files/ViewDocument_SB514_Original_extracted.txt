2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR OWEN
TRANSPORTATION/DEV DEPT. Provides relative to Department of Transportation and
Development aeronautic activities. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 2:5 and 6(A) and (B)(1), relative to the Department of
3 Transportation and Development aeronautic activities; to provide for the office of
4 multimodal commerce; to provide for delegation of powers; to provide for
5 aeronautics and aviation; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 2:5 and 6(A) and (B)(1) are hereby amended and reenacted to read
8 as follows:
9 §5. Duties of Department of Transportation and Development; aeronautic activities;
10 exceptions
11 The secretary and employees of the department designated by him
12 commissioner of the office of multimodal commerce or his designee shall
13 supervise and direct all aeronautic activities and facilities, except those activities
14 pertaining to aircraft registration, identification, equipment, and enforcement
15 delegated to the secretary of the Department of Public Safety and Corrections or his
16 designee. Unless specified, all licenses, orders, rules, and regulations pertaining to
17 aeronautics shall bear the secretary's commissioner's signature, or the signature of

1 employees designated by him to sign such licenses, orders, rules, or regulations; and
2 the secretary commissioner shall insure that all rules and regulations of the
3 department pertaining to aeronautics are obeyed. He shall direct investigations with
4 respect to aeronautic activities. He, and any employee designated by him, may
5 administer oaths when necessary in the discharge of official duties and may affix his
6 jurat to any license or document pertaining to the promotion or regulation of
7 aviation, whether state or federal, concerning which oath is to be made.
8 §6. Powers and duties of the department; promulgation of rules and regulations
9 pertaining to aeronautics
10 A. The department shall foster air commerce within the state of Louisiana
11 and the department shall have supervision over the aeronautical activities and
12 facilities, which authority shall include supervision and control over all airports,
13 landing fields, landing strips, air instruction, air marking, air beacons, and all other
14 nonfederal air-navigation facilities.
15 B.(1) Accordingly, the department may prescribe such reasonable rules and
16 regulations as it deems necessary and advisable:
17 (a) For the public safety and for the promotion of aeronautics governing the
18 designing, laying out, location, building, equipping, operation, and use of all airports,
19 landing fields, or landing strips;
20 (b) Governing the curriculum, equipment, personnel, and operation and
21 management of all air instruction;
22 (c) For the purpose of protecting the health and safety of students receiving
23 or to receive such instruction of student aviators;
24 (d)(b) For the public safety and safety of those engaged in aeronautics;
25 (e)(c) For the promotion of aeronautics governing the establishment, location,
26 maintenance, and operation of all air markings, air beacons, and other air-navigation
27 facilities.

DIGEST
SB 514 Original 2026 Regular Session Owen
Present law provides for the secretary and employees of the department designated by him
to supervise and direct all aeronautic activities and facilities. Proposed law changes the
duties of aeronautics from the secretary of DOTD to the DOTD office of multimodal
commerce commissioner.
Present law provides for activities pertaining to aircraft registration, identification,
equipment, and enforcement delegated to the secretary of the Department of Public Safety
and Corrections or his designee. Proposed law moves the provisions relative to aircraft
registration, identification, equipment, and enforcement from the secretary of DPS to the
DOTD, office of multimodal commerce commissioner.
Present law provides the secretary of DOTD may administer oaths when necessary and sign
any license or document relevant to the promotion or regulation of aviation, whether state
or federal. Proposed law changes this responsibility from the secretary of DOTD to the
DOTD, office of multimodal commerce commissioner.
Present law provides for powers and duties of the department and promulgation of rules and
regulations pertaining to aeronautics. Proposed law retains present law except for air
instruction and air marketing and all other nonfederal air navigation facilities.
Present law provides for the department to prescribe reasonable rules and regulations as it
deems necessary and advisable regarding curriculum, equipment, personnel, and operation
and management of all air instruction. Proposed law removes this provision.
Present law provides for protecting the health and safety of students receiving or to receive
instruction of student aviators. Proposed law removes this provision.
Present law provides for the promotion of aeronautics governing the establishment, location,
maintenance, and operation of all air markings, air beacons, and other air-navigation
facilities. Proposed law retains present law and removes the provisions governing the
establishment, location, maintenance and operation of air markings.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 2:5 and 6(A) and (B)(1))
