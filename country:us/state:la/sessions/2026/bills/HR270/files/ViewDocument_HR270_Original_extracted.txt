2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 270
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH: Designates May 13, 2026, as Alpha Phi Alpha,
Incorporated, Day at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, May 13, 2026, as Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated, Day
3 at the state capitol.
4 WHEREAS, it is most fitting to commend Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
5 on the occasion of the organization's one hundred twentieth anniversary and to designate
6 May 13, 2026, as Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated, Day at the state capitol; and
7 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated, was founded on December 4,
8 1906, by seven students at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York, and the organization
9 holds the distinction of being the first intercollegiate Greek letter fraternity established for
10 African American men; and
11 WHEREAS, the mission of Alpha Phi Alpha is to develop leaders and promote
12 brotherhood and academic excellence, while providing service and advocacy for its
13 surrounding communities; and
14 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha initially served as a study and support group for
15 minority students who faced racial prejudice at Cornell University, and throughout its
16 history, members of the fraternity have recognized the need to help correct the educational,
17 economic, political, and social injustices historically faced by African Americans; and
18 WHEREAS, members of Alpha Phi Alpha have stood at the forefront of the civil
19 rights movement and provided a voice and vision for African Americans and people of color
20 around the world; and

1 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha has accepted men of all races since 1945 in accordance
2 with the fraternity motto: "First of All, Servants of All, We Shall Transcend All"; and
3 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha programs and charitable activities include the
4 "Go-To-High-School, Go-To-College" campaign, "A Voiceless People is a Hopeless People"
5 voter registration campaign, Project Alpha, and the Martin Luther King, Jr., National
6 Memorial project; and
7 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha has numerous chapters in the state of Louisiana, and
8 the members of its hundreds of chapters worldwide continue the legacy of their predecessors
9 by helping their fellow man, serving their communities, and acting as mentors to youth;
10 members also carry on the tradition of their fellow member, Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., by
11 actively discussing civil rights; and
12 WHEREAS, other notable members of Alpha Phi Alpha include W.E.B. Dubois,
13 Adam Clayton Powell, Jr., Edward Brooke, Thurgood Marshall, Paul Robeson, William
14 Gray, and Andrew Young; and
15 WHEREAS, Alpha Phi Alpha members from across the state will assemble in Baton
16 Rouge to celebrate the contributions to the legislature of eighteen past Alpha brothers and
17 those currently serving: Representatives Ken Brass, Adrian Fisher, Travis Johnson, and
18 Ed Murray; and
19 WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize Alpha Phi Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated,
20 for its programs and activities, which exemplify the importance of service to all mankind,
21 and to recognize the positive impact the fraternity continues to have on Louisiana.
22 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
23 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, May 13, 2026, as Alpha Phi
24 Alpha Fraternity, Incorporated, Day at the state capitol and does hereby extend sincerest
25 wishes that the organization and its members continue to prosper in all of their endeavors.
