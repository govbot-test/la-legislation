HLS 26RS-495 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 26
EDMONSTON, MARTINEZ, AND OWEN
STUDENT/STANDARDS: Requests that the State Board of Elementary and Secondary
Education incorporate the science of human growth and development into content
standards and to provide curriculum and instructional materials on its website
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education to incorporate
3 the science of human growth and development into the state content standards for the
4 subjects of science or health education, to provide resources on its website for
5 materials and information that may be used in curriculum and instruction related to
6 the standards, and to submit a written report to the House Committee on Education
7 and the Senate Committee on Education not later than March 1, 2027.
8 WHEREAS, current law, R.S. 17:24.4, requires the State Board of Elementary and
9 Secondary Education (BESE) to develop state content standards for required subjects; state
10 content standards are statements that define what a student should know or be able to
11 accomplish at the end of a specific time period or grade level or at the completion of a
12 course; and
13 WHEREAS, there are content standards for the subjects of science and health
14 education, both of which are subjects in which it would be appropriate to incorporate
15 standards on the science of human growth and development; and
16 WHEREAS, current law, R.S. 17:6(A)(15), authorizes BESE to promulgate rules that
17 require the inclusion of certain topics in student instruction; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-495 ENGROSSED
HCR NO. 26
1 WHEREAS, current middle and high school science and health standards provide for
2 topics related to genetic traits, embryo development, sexual reproduction, living things made
3 of cells and distinction between living and non-living things, and cellular contribution to the
4 whole of an organism, but there is no requirement for instruction on human development
5 from conception to birth; and
6 WHEREAS, the use of high definition ultrasound video and computer-generated
7 animation showing the development of vital organs in the early stages of human fetal
8 development is being incorporated into instruction across the country in courses concerning
9 human biology.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
11 urge and request the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education to incorporate the
12 science of human growth and development, including instruction on prenatal growth and in
13 utero development of all vital organs and significant markers and cell growth, into the state
14 content standards for the subjects of science or health education at an age- and grade-
15 appropriate level.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the legislature urges and requests the State
17 Board of Elementary and Secondary Education to provide age- and grade-appropriate
18 materials and information on its website that may be used in curriculum and instruction
19 related to the standards, including high definition ultrasound video or computer-generated
20 animation that depicts prenatal growth and development.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the legislature urges and requests the State
22 Board of Elementary and Secondary Education to submit a written report to the House
23 Committee on Education and the Senate Committee on Education not later than
24 March 1, 2027, relative to any action taken with respect to the content standards and the
25 clearinghouse as requested in this Resolution.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
27 president of the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education and the state
28 superintendent of education.

DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HCR 26 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Chenevert
Requests that the State Bd. of Elementary and Secondary Education:
(1) Incorporate the science of human growth and development, including but not limited
to prenatal development, into the state content standards for science or health
education at an age- and grade-appropriate level.
(2) Provide age- and grade-appropriate materials and information on its website that may
be used in curriculum and instruction related to the standards, including but not
limited to videos showing prenatal development.
(3) Submit a written report to the House and Senate education committees by March 1,
2027, relative to any action taken with respect to the content standards and the
clearinghouse as requested in this Resolution.
