SLS 26RS-1421 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 48
BY SENATOR MIZELL
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH. Designates Monday, March 30, 2026, as Ellevate
Louisiana Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Monday, March 30, 2026, as Ellevate Louisiana Day at the Louisiana State
3 Capitol and to commend its mission to empower women.
4 WHEREAS, Ellevate Louisiana was founded in 2020 by Julie Stokes and has
5 emerged as a statewide policy organization dedicated to empowering women leaders
6 throughout Louisiana; and
7 WHEREAS, in support of its mission, Ellevate Louisiana is committed to educating
8 its members and the communities they represent by offering data-driven, nonpartisan
9 solutions to address the challenges faced in Louisiana, while also providing a wealth of
10 resources; and
11 WHEREAS, videocasts by Ellevate Louisiana offer its membership and interested
12 citizens a platform to establish new goals and formulate effectual plans, covering diverse
13 topics such as health care, environment, infrastructure, workforce, education, taxation, and
14 economic development; and
15 WHEREAS, each year Ellevate Louisiana directs its attention to Louisiana state
16 legislative sessions from February through June, organizing leadership conferences prior to
17 the session, participating in legislative days during the session, and conducting session
18 wrap-ups upon the legislature's adjournment sine die; and

1 WHEREAS, policy symposiums are organized from September through December,
2 which facilitate direct engagement with state and federal decision-makers and share answers
3 to questions regarding topics, such as local impact of the United States census data,
4 remaining in Louisiana for work, healthcare debates, child care, jobs, sports, and more; and
5 WHEREAS, Ellevate Louisiana shares informative and beneficial editorials aimed
6 at addressing problematic living issues that Louisiana families face, including childhood
7 development, while striving to eliminate daily divisiveness that can erode unity for all of
8 Louisiana and our nation, particularly in the face of one-sided solutions to multifaceted
9 problems; and
10 WHEREAS, Ellevate Louisiana actively addresses the current needs of Louisiana's
11 citizenry through focused policy positions in various crucial areas, including early childhood
12 care, education, statewide broadband, long-term care, behavioral health, telehealth, women's
13 health, domestic violence, healthcare workforce shortages, data-sharing for career readiness,
14 occupational licensing reform, centralized sales tax collection, and economic development,
15 all aimed at fostering a better future for the state and promoting collective learning and
16 brighter prospects for the future.
17 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
18 does hereby designate Monday, March 30, 2026, as Ellevate Louisiana Day at the Louisiana
19 State Capitol and does hereby commend its mission to empower women.
