HB 1068 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Villio
Abstract: Authorizes parish and municipal governing authorities to enter into contracts for
the collection, transportation, and disposal of garbage or trash that include an option
to extend the term of the contract and removes certain requirements for granting
exclusive contracts.
Present law grants parish and municipal governing authorities certain powers with respect
to the collection and disposal of garbage and trash, including but not limited to the
following:
(1) To engage in the collection and disposal of garbage and trash within its jurisdiction
to the exclusion of other garbage and trash collectors.

(2) To grant permits, licenses, exclusive or nonexclusive franchises, exclusive contracts,
or any combination thereof to garbage and trash collectors and disposers.
(3) To enter into time contracts for the collection and transportation of garbage or trash
for a term of up to 10 years, and for disposal of garbage or trash for a term of up to
25 years.
(4) To assess a service charge against any person provided any service pursuant to
present law.
(5) To regulate the collection and disposal of garbage and trash.
Proposed law authorizes parish and municipal governing authorities to enter into contracts
for the collection and transportation of garbage or trash and for the disposal of garbage or
trash that include an option to extend the term of the contract for five years. Otherwise
retains present law.
Present law provides that any exclusive contract may be granted only after issuing a request
for proposals, or advertising for bids and receiving bids, and awarding the contract in
accordance with the public bid laws of the state or any other provisions of law.
Proposed law repeals present law.
(Amends R.S. 33:4169.1(A)(1)(b) through (e); Repeals R.S. 33:4169.1(A)(1)(f))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Municipal, Parochial
and Cultural Affairs to the original bill:
1. Remove requirement that parish and municipal governing authorities issue a
request for proposals or advertise for bids before granting exclusive contracts.
2. Make technical changes.
