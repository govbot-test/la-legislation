28 §2355. Functions and responsibilities; Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation,
29 Economy, Navigation, and Transportation Authority Board
30 A. The board shall:
31 (1) Represent the state's position on policy relative to the protection,
32 conservation, enhancement, and management of the upland noncoastal area of the
33 state through oversight of integrated projects and programs and by addressing
34 activities which could significantly affect integrated projects and programs, all
35 consistent with the legislative intent as expressed in this Title.
36 (2) Develop, coordinate, make reports on, and provide oversight for a
37 comprehensive upland noncoastal area master plan and annual plans, working in
38 conjunction with state agencies, political subdivisions, including flood protection
39 authorities, levee districts, and federal agencies. The master plan shall include a
40 comprehensive strategy addressing the protection, conservation, enhancement, and
41 management of the upland area through the construction and management of
42 integrated projects and programs, all consistent with the legislative intent as
43 expressed in this Chapter. The annual plan shall be developed as the annual
44 implementation of the comprehensive master plan and shall be submitted to the
45 legislature for approval as set forth in R.S. 38:2354. The annual plan shall include
46 a description and status of all projects and programs pertaining to the authority's
47 mission, including privately funded projects or plans, and addressing those activities
48 which significantly affect projects set forth in the plan, all consistent with the
49 legislative intent as expressed in this Title.
50 (3) Submit to the House Committee on Natural Resources and Environment
51 and the Senate Committee on Natural Resources and the House Committee on
52 Transportation, Highways and Public Works and the Senate Committee on
53 Transportation, Highways and Public Works meeting jointly, the integrated plans
54 developed pursuant to R.S. 38:2354. Upon approval of the plans by the legislative
55 committees and prior to implementation of the plans, in whole or in part, the plans
56 shall be approved by the legislature as provided in R.S. 38:2354(E).
57 (4) Develop procedures in accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act
58 and take actions against any entity, including political subdivisions, to enforce
59 compliance with the comprehensive master upland noncoastal area plan. Such
60 procedures and actions may include but are not limited to determinations of
61 noncompliance; appeal from such determinations; the taking of administrative
