23 Transportation Authority; purpose and intent
24 A. The Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation, Economy, Navigation,
25 and Transportation Authority, referred to in this Chapter as the "authority", is hereby
26 established as the single coordinating entity responsible for the prioritization,
27 planning, and funding of projects related to flood control, flood risk reduction,
28 navigation, and water resource floodplain and watershed management in all areas
29 of the state not included within the coastal area as defined in R.S. 49:214.2.

DIGEST
SB 171 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Pressly
Present law provides the Department of Public Works, state of Louisiana provides planning
assistance to municipal, parish, regional and other planning agencies of the state and is
authorized to apply for and accept grants of money from the U.S. government or any federal
agencies and may contract with them as necessary to accept the grant and any requirements
for matching the grants. Proposed law retains present law and changes the agency from the
Department of Public Works, state of Louisiana to the Department of Transportation and
Development (DOTD).
Proposed law provides the chief engineer of the office of engineering of the Department of
Transportation and Development will be a member appointed to the Red River Compact
Commission. Proposed law changes the member from the chief engineer of the office of
engineering of the Department of Transportation and Development to the secretary of the
Department of Conservation and Energy.
Present law provides parishes and municipalities authorized to comply with federal flood
insurance may adopt, ordinances, rules, and regulations, including zoning and land use
regulations, necessary to comply with the requirements of the National Flood Insurance Act
of 1968 in coordination with the chief resilience officer of DOTD. Proposed law retains
present law and removes the chief resilience officer designee.
Proposed law provides the chief resilience officer of DOTD will cooperate with the Federal
Insurance Administrator of the Federal Emergency Management Agency in the planning and
carrying out of state participation in the National Flood Insurance Program and will aid,
advise, and cooperate with parishes and municipalities attempting to qualify for participation
in the program. Proposed law retains present law and removes chief resilience office of
DOTD as the designee.
Proposed law changes terminology from "upland" area to "noncoastal" area.
Present law provides the Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation, Economy,
Navigation, and Transportation Authority also known as the "authority", is established as the
single coordinating entity responsible for prioritizing, planning, and funding projects related
to flood control, risk reduction, navigation, and water resource management in all areas of
the state not included within the coastal area. Proposed law retains present law and changes
terminology from "risk reduction" to "flood risk reduction", and changes from "water
resource management" to "floodplain and watershed management".
Present law provides the authority will implement policy addressing issues related to water
quantity, flood risk mitigation, navigation, economic development, and recreation. Proposed
law changes terminology from "water quantity" to "watershed management" and adds
"floodplain management" to the responsibilities of the authority.
Present law provides the definition of "integrated" as the coordinated planning, funding,
construction, and operation of flood control, navigation, recreation, water management, and
economic development projects to ensure efficient use of resources, minimize conflicts, and
promote sustainable development. Proposed law retains present law and adds "floodplain
management" to the definition. Proposed law further changes from "water management" to
"watershed management" in the definition. Proposed law further provides for the definition

Present law provides the board shall have 16 members. Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law further provides certain members of the authority to be subject to Senate

Present law provides the secretary of LED will be a member of the authority and serve as

Present law provides the board will not consist of more than two levee boards. Proposed law

Proposed law provides for technical changes.
Present law provides adoption by the board of the master plan after submission to the House
Committee on Natural Resources and Environment and the Senate Committee on Natural
Resources and the House Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public Works and
the Senate Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public Works for approval. In
addition, the annual plan shall be submitted to the House Committee on Natural Resources
and Environment and the Senate Committee on Natural Resources and the House Committee
on Transportation, Highways and Public Works and the Senate Committee on
Transportation, Highways and Public Works on or before the fifteenth day of the regular
legislative session of each year. Proposed law removes provision for submission to House
Committee on Natural Resources and Environment and the Senate Committee on Natural
Resources and instead receive approval at a joint meeting of the House and the Senate
Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public Works.
Present law provides the board oversee riverine flood management, inland navigation, and
water resource infrastructure within the noncoastal area. Proposed law retains present law
and changes the responsibility from water resource infrastructure to flood control
infrastructure and makes further technical changes from "upland" to "noncoastal".

Present law provides the Department of Public Works, state of Louisiana provides planning
assistance to municipal, parish, regional and other planning agencies of the state and is
authorized to apply for and accept grants of money from the U.S. government or any federal
agencies and may contract with them as necessary to accept the grant and any requirements
for matching the grants. Proposed law retains present law and changes the agency from the
Department of Public Works, state of Louisiana to the Department of Transportation and
Development (DOTD).
Proposed law provides the chief engineer of the office of engineering of the Department of
Transportation and Development will be a member appointed to the Red River Compact
Commission. Proposed law changes the member from the chief engineer of the office of
engineering of the Department of Transportation and Development to the secretary of the
Department of Conservation and Energy.
Present law provides parishes and municipalities authorized to comply with federal flood
insurance may adopt, ordinances, rules, and regulations, including zoning and land use
regulations, necessary to comply with the requirements of the National Flood Insurance Act
of 1968 in coordination with the chief resilience officer of DOTD. Proposed law retains
present law and removes the chief resilience officer designee.
Proposed law provides the chief resilience officer of DOTD will cooperate with the Federal
Insurance Administrator of the Federal Emergency Management Agency in the planning and
carrying out of state participation in the National Flood Insurance Program and will aid,
advise, and cooperate with parishes and municipalities attempting to qualify for participation
in the program. Proposed law retains present law and removes chief resilience office of
DOTD as the designee.
Proposed law changes terminology from "upland" area to "noncoastal" area.
Present law provides the Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation, Economy,
Navigation, and Transportation Authority also known as the "authority", is established as the
single coordinating entity responsible for prioritizing, planning, and funding projects related
to flood control, risk reduction, navigation, and water resource management in all areas of
the state not included within the coastal area. Proposed law retains present law and changes
terminology from "risk reduction" to "flood risk reduction", and changes from "water
resource management" to "floodplain and watershed management".
Present law provides the authority will implement policy addressing issues related to water
quantity, flood risk mitigation, navigation, economic development, and recreation. Proposed
law changes terminology from "water quantity" to "watershed management" and adds
"floodplain management" to the responsibilities of the authority.
Present law provides the definition of "integrated" as the coordinated planning, funding,
construction, and operation of flood control, navigation, recreation, water management, and
economic development projects to ensure efficient use of resources, minimize conflicts, and
promote sustainable development. Proposed law retains present law and adds "floodplain
management" to the definition. Proposed law further changes from "water management" to
"watershed management" in the definition. Proposed law further provides for the definition
