11 §2351. Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation, Economy, Navigation, and
12 Transportation Authority; purpose and intent
13 A. The Coordinated Use of Resources for Recreation, Economy, Navigation,
14 and Transportation Authority, referred to in this Chapter as the "authority", is hereby
15 established as the single coordinating entity responsible for the prioritization,
16 planning, and funding of projects related to flood control, flood risk reduction,
17 navigation, and water resource floodplain and watershed management in all areas
18 of the state not included within the coastal area as defined in R.S. 49:214.2.
19 B. The legislature declares that it is the public policy of the state to develop
20 and implement, on a comprehensive and coordinated basis, an integrated plan that
21 shall support and integrate the development and implementation of policies and
22 initiatives for flood protection, infrastructure planning, and economic development
23 in the upland noncoastal area. Consistent with this goal, it is the policy of this state
24 to achieve a proper balance between development and conservation and encourage
25 the use of state resources in responsible economic development of the area. The
26 authority shall implement the policy within these areas ensuring the efficient and
27 coordinated management of water resources within its jurisdiction, addressing issues
28 related to water quantity watershed management, flood risk mitigation, floodplain
29 management, navigation, economic development, and recreation. It shall be the
30 policy of the state within these areas that through the comprehensive and coordinated

5 §2352. Definitions
6 As used in this Chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed
7 to them:
8 (1) "Integrated" means the coordinated planning, funding, construction, and
9 operation of flood control, floodplain management, navigation, recreation, water
10 watershed management, and economic development projects to ensure efficient use
11 of resources, minimize conflicts, and promote sustainable development.
12 (2) "Upland Noncoastal area" means all land in the state of Louisiana that
13 is not included within the coastal area as defined in R.S. 49:214.2.
14 (3) "Upland Noncoastal parishes" means all parishes of the state, in whole
15 or in part, that are not included within the coastal area as defined in R.S. 49:214.2.
16 These areas are primarily affected by riverine flooding, watershed management,
17 inland navigation, and flood control infrastructure rather than coastal erosion or sea
18 level rise.
