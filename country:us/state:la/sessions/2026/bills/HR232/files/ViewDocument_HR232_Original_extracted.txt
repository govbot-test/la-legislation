HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 232
BY REPRESENTATIVE TAYLOR
COMMENDATIONS: Commends Pastor Neil J. Bernard on his legacy of pastoral
leadership and community service to St. John the Baptist Parish
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend Pastor Neil J. Bernard on his legacy of pastoral leadership and community
3 service to St. John the Baptist Parish.
4 WHEREAS, Pastor Neil J. Bernard is a devoted leader and community advocate who
5 was born in the vibrant city of New Orleans, Louisiana; after graduating from high school,
6 he continued his education at Xavier University of New Orleans and Bethel Bible College,
7 where he developed his skills in ministry and community service; and
8 WHEREAS, Pastor Bernard founded New Wine Christian Church in LaPlace,
9 Louisiana, in 1995, and he has faithfully served as its pastor for over twenty-nine years;
10 under his leadership, the church embraces a mission to renew lives, restore families, and
11 rebuild communities; and
12 WHEREAS, New Wine Christian Church offers a range of programs, including after-
13 school initiatives, summer camps, recreational activities, and financial assistance, which
14 benefit numerous families in the area; and
15 WHEREAS, beyond his clerical role, Pastor Bernard serves as chairman of St. John
16 the Baptist Parish Long-Term Recovery; he has played a critical role in assisting the
17 community's recovery efforts following three major hurricanes and a tornado and
18 demonstrated his unwavering commitment to the well-being of his neighbors; and
19 WHEREAS, Pastor Bernard also serves as chairman of the St. John Pastors Coalition
20 and believes in the power of collaboration and teamwork to address the pressing needs of
21 the community; and

7 WHEREAS, Pastor Neil J. Bernard embodies a commitment to service, faith, and

10 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Pastor Neil J. Bernard on his legacy of
11 pastoral leadership and community service to St. John the Baptist Parish and does hereby
12 extend sincere wishes that he remain prosperous in all of his endeavors.
