HB 160 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Knox
Abstract: Provides relative to the penalties for certain domestic violence offenses that
involve strangulation.
Present law (R.S. 14:34.9) provides for the crime of battery of a dating partner.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that if the battery of a dating partner involves strangulation, the
offender, in addition to any other penalties imposed pursuant to present law, shall be
imprisoned at hard labor for not more than three years.
Proposed law amends present law to change the maximum penalty from three years to 10
years and provides that at least one year of the sentence imposed be served without the
benefit of probation or suspension of sentence. Otherwise retains present law.
Present law (R.S. 14:35.3) provides for the crime of domestic abuse battery.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that if the domestic abuse battery involves strangulation, the offender,
in addition to any other penalties imposed pursuant to present law, shall be imprisoned at
hard labor for not more than three years.
Proposed law amends present law to change the maximum penalty from three years to 10
years and provides that at least one year of the sentence imposed be served without the
benefit of probation or suspension of sentence. Otherwise retains present law.
(Amends R.S. 14:34.9(L)(1) and 35.3(L)(1))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Administration of
Criminal Justice to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Make technical changes.

Section 3:
2. Provide that at least one year of the sentence imposed shall be served without the
benefit of probation or suspension of sentence for an offender who commits
either battery of a dating partner or domestic abuse battery when either offense
involves strangulation.
