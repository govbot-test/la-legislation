HLS 26RS-695 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE BEAULLIEU
SUNSET LAW: Re-creates the Department of State and the statutory entities made a part
of the department by law
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 49:191(4) and to repeal R.S. 49:191(1)(c), relative to the
3 Department of State, including provisions to provide for the re-creation of the
4 Department of State and the statutory entities made a part of the department by law;
5 to provide for the effective termination date for all statutory authority for the
6 existence of such statutory entities; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. Pursuant to R.S. 49:193, the Department of State and the statutory entities
9 made a part of the department by law shall be re-created effective June 30, 2026, and all
10 statutory authority therefor is continued in accordance with the provisions of Part XII of
11 Chapter 1 of Title 49 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950.
12 Section 2. All statutory authority for the existence of the Department of State and
13 the statutory entities made a part of the department as re-created by Section 1 of this Act
14 shall cease as of July 1, 2033, pursuant to R.S. 49:191. However, the Department of State
15 may be re-created prior to such date in accordance with the provisions of Part XII of Chapter
16 1 of Title 49 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950.

HB 117 Original 2026 Regular Session Beaullieu
Abstract: Re-creates the Dept. of State for six years. Changes termination date from July
1, 2027, to July 1, 2033.
Present law (sunset law) provides that the Dept. of State and all the statutory entities made
a part of that department by law shall begin to terminate operations on July 1, 2026, and that
all legislative authority for such entities shall cease as of July 1, 2027, unless the legislature
enacts a bill authorizing the re-creation of the department and its statutory entities prior
thereto.

HLS 26RS-695 ORIGINAL
HB NO. 117
Proposed law provides for a general re-creation of the Dept. of State and its statutory
entities, effective June 30, 2026, in accordance with the sunset law. Supersedes the
provisions of the sunset law which set out the procedure for review and re-creation and
which require a separate bill to re-create each statutory entity within the department along
with additional provisions. July 1, 2033, is the new termination date, and termination would
begin July 1, 2032, unless the department is again re-created.
Effective June 30, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 49:191(4); Repeals R.S. 49:191(1)(c))
