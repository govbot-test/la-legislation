SLS 26RS-849 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 20
BY SENATOR WHEAT
MEDICAID. Memorialize Congress and urges the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid
Services to implement certain eligibility redeterminations.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To memorialize the United States Congress and to urge the Centers for Medicare and
3 Medicaid Services to authorize states to implement a presumption of Medicaid
4 eligibility during redetermination for elderly and disabled beneficiaries and to allow
5 exemption from annual redetermination requirements consistent with reforms
6 enacted by the state of Florida.
7 WHEREAS, the Medicaid program provides critical health coverage to elderly
8 individuals, persons with disabilities, and low-income individuals who rely on consistent
9 access to care; and
10 WHEREAS, under federal Medicaid law, states must periodically redetermine
11 eligibility for beneficiaries, which can create administrative burdens for state agencies and
12 unnecessary disruptions in coverage for vulnerable populations; and
13 WHEREAS, interruptions in Medicaid eligibility often occur due to procedural issues
14 rather than actual ineligibility, resulting in delays in care, increased administrative costs, and
15 adverse health outcomes; and
16 WHEREAS, Florida enacted legislation in 2025 providing that individuals aged
17 sixty-five or older or those determined to be disabled who were initially determined eligible
18 for Medicaid shall be presumed eligible for continued coverage during the redetermination

1 process unless there is a material change in disability or economic status; and
2 WHEREAS, under that policy, beneficiaries or their caregivers are required to notify
3 the appropriate state agency of any material change in circumstances affecting eligibility,
4 and the state may conduct a redetermination when such changes occur; and
5 WHEREAS, the Florida law further directs the state to seek federal authorization to
6 exempt certain Medicaid-eligible disabled individuals from annual eligibility
7 redetermination requirements in order to reduce administrative burdens and ensure
8 continuity of care; and
9 WHEREAS, this approach maintains program integrity while improving
10 administrative efficiency and protecting access to healthcare for vulnerable populations; and
11 WHEREAS, Florida's legislation recognizes that individuals who have already been
12 determined eligible for Medicaid services should maintain coverage during the
13 redetermination process absent a material change in their disability or financial
14 circumstances; and
15 WHEREAS, the legislation also requires the responsible state agency to notify
16 beneficiaries before conducting redeterminations and to provide the results of those
17 redeterminations upon completion; and
18 WHEREAS, these reforms seek federal authorization to exempt Medicaid-eligible
19 disabled individuals from annual redeterminations to simplify eligibility maintenance and
20 improve program administration.
21 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana memorializes
22 the Congress of the United States and urges the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services
23 to authorize states to implement Medicaid eligibility redetermination reforms consistent with
24 those adopted by the state of Florida.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Congress and CMS are urged to authorize states
26 to:
27 (1) Establish a presumption of continued Medicaid eligibility for individuals aged
28 sixty-five or older or determined to be disabled during the redetermination process unless
29 there is a material change in disability or economic status.
30 (2) Require beneficiaries or their caregivers to report material changes in

2 (3) Permit state Medicaid agencies to conduct redeterminations when such material
3 changes occur.
4 (4) Allow states to seek federal authorization to exempt certain Medicaid-eligible
5 disabled individuals from annual redetermination requirements, in order to simplify
6 eligibility maintenance and improve continuity of care.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Legislature supports federal action
8 that provides states greater flexibility to streamline Medicaid eligibility processes, reduce
9 unnecessary administrative burdens, and ensure continuity of care for elderly and disabled
10 beneficiaries.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution shall be transmitted
12 to the secretary of the United States Senate, the clerk of the United States House of
13 Representatives, each member of the Louisiana delegation to the United States Congress, the
14 secretary of the United States Department of Health and Human Services, and the
15 administrator of the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.
