ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 163
BY REPRESENTATIVE ORGERON
A RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, Louisiana boasts impressive supplies of onshore and offshore energy
resources, and the state's natural resources have provided thousands of jobs and billions of
dollars in economic development; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana's rich history and expertise in energy development positions
the state to pave the way for emerging industries; renewable energy is becoming increasingly
more affordable and efficient and has the potential to provide significant economic and
environmental benefits to the state; and
WHEREAS, according to the Solar Energy Industries Association, Louisiana's solar
energy resources are expected to grow more than tenfold over the next few years, and solar
growth in Louisiana could lead to more than sixteen billion dollars in economic development
and over four hundred thirteen thousand jobs over the next decade; and
WHEREAS, wind energy is an abundant and growing source of renewable energy
that can create jobs and economic opportunities in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana's maritime and oil and gas industries played instrumental
roles in the construction of the first offshore wind farm in the United States; and
WHEREAS, battery storage technology is improving rapidly and has the potential
to play a critical role in integrating renewable energy sources into our electric grid and
creating new jobs in the energy sector; and
WHEREAS, hydrogen is a versatile energy carrier that can be produced from
renewable sources and used in a variety of applications, including transportation and
industrial processes, which create new economic opportunities in Louisiana; and
Page 1 of 2
HR NO. 163 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, the vast majority of proposals for power plant facilities in Louisiana are
renewable energy projects across thirty parishes in the state; and
WHEREAS, jobs in fields involving clean and renewable energy pay higher wages
and support local economies in the state, particularly in rural and coastal areas that have been
hard hit by economic challenges; and
WHEREAS, investment in clean and renewable energy will diversify Louisiana's
economy and position the state as a leader in the transition to a clean energy future.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Tuesday, April 21, 2026, as Louisiana
Renewable Energy Day at the state capitol and does hereby express support for the state's
continued investment in clean and renewable energy.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
