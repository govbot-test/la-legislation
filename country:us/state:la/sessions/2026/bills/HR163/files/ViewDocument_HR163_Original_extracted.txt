2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 163
BY REPRESENTATIVE ORGERON
capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Tuesday, April 21, 2026, as Renewable Energy Day at the state capitol.
3 WHEREAS, Louisiana boasts impressive supplies of onshore and offshore energy
4 resources, and the state's natural resources have provided thousands of jobs and billions of
5 dollars in economic development; and
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana's rich history and expertise in energy development positions
7 the state to pave the way for emerging industries; renewable energy is becoming increasingly
8 more affordable and efficient and has the potential to provide significant economic and
9 environmental benefits to the state; and
10 WHEREAS, according to the Solar Energy Industries Association, Louisiana's solar
11 energy resources are expected to grow more than tenfold over the next few years, and solar
12 growth in Louisiana could lead to more than sixteen billion dollars in economic development
13 and over four hundred thirteen thousand jobs over the next decade; and
14 WHEREAS, wind energy is an abundant and growing source of renewable energy
15 that can create jobs and economic opportunities in Louisiana; and
16 WHEREAS, Louisiana's maritime and oil and gas industries played instrumental
17 roles in the construction of the first offshore wind farm in the United States; and
18 WHEREAS, battery storage technology is improving rapidly and has the potential
19 to play a critical role in integrating renewable energy sources into our electric grid and
20 creating new jobs in the energy sector; and
Page 1 of 2
HLS 26RS-3469 ORIGINAL

1 WHEREAS, hydrogen is a versatile energy carrier that can be produced from
2 renewable sources and used in a variety of applications, including transportation and
3 industrial processes, which create new economic opportunities in Louisiana; and
4 WHEREAS, the vast majority of proposals for power plant facilities in Louisiana are
5 renewable energy projects across thirty parishes in the state; and
6 WHEREAS, jobs in fields involving clean and renewable energy pay higher wages
7 and support local economies in the state, particularly in rural and coastal areas that have been
8 hard hit by economic challenges; and
9 WHEREAS, investment in clean and renewable energy will diversify Louisiana's
10 economy and position the state as a leader in the transition to a clean energy future.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
12 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Tuesday, April 21, 2026, as Louisiana
13 Renewable Energy Day at the state capitol and does hereby express support for the state's
14 continued investment in clean and renewable energy.
