HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 351
BY REPRESENTATIVE BAYHAM
A RESOLUTION
To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Russell M.
Nelson, president of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on September 27,
2025, of Russell M. Nelson, president of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints; and
WHEREAS, Russell Marion Nelson, Sr., was born on September 9, 1924, in Salt
Lake City, Utah, to Marion C. Nelson and Edna Anderson Nelson; he was the second of four
children, grew up in a loving home, and was baptized into the Church of Jesus Christ of
Latter-day Saints at the age of sixteen; and
WHEREAS, as a youth, President Nelson had a natural love of science, and he
decided as a teenager that he would study medicine in college; he attended the University
of Utah, where he met Dantzel White; they married on August 31, 1945, in the Salt Lake
Temple and had ten children; and
WHEREAS, because President Nelson attended college during World War II, he
compressed his premedical degree into three years and began medical school at the
University of Utah in 1944; he graduated from medical school at the top of his class; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson served in the United States Naval Reserve from 1945
to 1947; he served his residency in surgery at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston and
at the University of Minnesota and received a Ph.D. in 1954; he received several honorary
degrees, including a doctor of science from Brigham Young University in 1970, a doctor of
medical science from Utah State University in 1989, and a doctor of humane letters from
Snow College in 1994; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson was an exceptional cardiothoracic surgeon, and he and
other medical professionals pioneered the development of the heart-lung machine; in 1955,
he became the first doctor in the state of Utah to perform open-heart surgery; and

WHEREAS, President Nelson authored numerous publications and chapters in
medical textbooks and lectured and visited professionally throughout the United States and
internationally; he received many awards and honors throughout his life, including the Heart
of Gold Award from the American Heart Association, the Golden Plate Award presented by
the American Academy of Achievement, and the Gandhi-King-Mandela Peace Prize; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson served as president of the Society for Vascular
Surgery, a director of the American Board of Thoracic Surgery, chairman of the Council of
Cardiovascular Surgery for the American Heart Association, and president of the Utah State
Medical Association; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson developed close ties with China during his career and
was credited with introducing open-heart surgery to China in 1980; he was awarded
honorary professorships from three universities in the People's Republic of China; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson notably performed open-heart surgery on Spencer W.
Kimball, who went on to serve as the twelfth president of the Church; and
WHEREAS, during his career, President Nelson served in various church callings,
including stake president of the Bonneville Stake, general president of the Sunday School,
regional representative for Brigham Young University, and regional representative for the
Kearns Utah Region; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson accepted the call to serve as a member of the Quorum
of the Twelve Apostles and began serving on April 7, 1984; over nearly thirty-four years of
service in the Quorum, he visited one hundred thirty-four countries to share the gospel; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson served as the president of the Quorum of Twelve
Apostles from July 15, 2015, until he was called to lead the church; he was made the
seventeenth president of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints on January 14, 2018,
shortly after the passing of President Thomas S. Monson; and
WHEREAS, during his presidency, President Nelson announced two hundred
temples, which is the most of any leader of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints;
he also presided over a historic number of large-scale changes in the church and led its
members through the COVID-19 pandemic; and
WHEREAS, President Nelson is survived by his wife, Wendy Watson Nelson; eight
children; and more than two hundred grandchildren and great-grandchildren; he will be
