2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 293
BY REPRESENTATIVE CHANCE HENRY
capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tourism Day at the state capitol.
3 WHEREAS, travel plays an essential role in preserving the culture of Louisiana,
4 propelling its economy, and shaping the success of the state; and
5 WHEREAS, travel creates quality job opportunities; there are more than two hundred
6 twenty-five thousand six hundred people employed in the leisure and hospitality industry,
7 making it the fourth largest employer in the state; and
8 WHEREAS, Louisiana attracted forty-five million domestic and international visitors
9 in 2025; these travelers spent an estimated $19.5 billion while visiting Louisiana in 2025,
10 which represents a 5.2% increase over 2024; and
11 WHEREAS, no state general fund monies are appropriated for tourism; it is funded
12 exclusively by a dedication of three one-hundredths of a cent of sales tax; and
13 WHEREAS, Lieutenant Governor Billy Nungesser directs the funds generated by the
14 dedication to promote tourism, which has an undeniably positive impact on the state; and
15 WHEREAS, on Tourism Day, members of the travel community representing all
16 corners of the state visit the capitol to celebrate the incredible impact of the Louisiana travel
17 industry.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
19 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tourism Day
20 at the state capitol and does hereby express appreciation and support for the outstanding
21 work of the Louisiana travel industry in its mission to attract visitors and provide
22 ambassadorial and financial benefits to the state.
