ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 293
BY REPRESENTATIVE CHANCE HENRY
A RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, travel plays an essential role in preserving the culture of Louisiana,
propelling its economy, and shaping the success of the state; and
WHEREAS, travel creates quality job opportunities; there are more than two hundred
twenty-five thousand six hundred people employed in the leisure and hospitality industry,
making it the fourth largest employer in the state; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana attracted forty-five million domestic and international visitors
in 2025; these travelers spent an estimated $19.5 billion while visiting Louisiana in 2025,
which represents a 5.2% increase over 2024; and
WHEREAS, no state general fund monies are appropriated for tourism; it is funded
exclusively by a dedication of three one-hundredths of a cent of sales tax; and
WHEREAS, Lieutenant Governor Billy Nungesser directs the funds generated by the
dedication to promote tourism, which has an undeniably positive impact on the state; and
WHEREAS, on Tourism Day, members of the travel community representing all
corners of the state visit the capitol to celebrate the incredible impact of the Louisiana travel
industry.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tourism Day
at the state capitol and does hereby express appreciation and support for the outstanding
work of the Louisiana travel industry in its mission to attract visitors and provide
ambassadorial and financial benefits to the state.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
