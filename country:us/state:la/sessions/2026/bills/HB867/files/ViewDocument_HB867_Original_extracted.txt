HB 867 Original 2026 Regular Session LaCombe
Abstract: Authorizes the town of Fordoche to make the office of police chief appointive.
Present law provides that the officers of every municipality governed by the provisions of
the Lawrason Act (mayor-board of aldermen form of government) shall be a mayor,
aldermen, a chief of police, a tax collector, and a clerk. Generally provides for election, at
large, of the police chief. Provides for appointment of a police chief in certain specified
municipalities.
Present law alternatively provides that the office of police chief shall be filled by mayoral
appointment with approval of the board of aldermen if a majority of the municipal electors
approve the change. Provides for an election on the question only upon a petition directed
to the board of aldermen and signed by at least 25% of such electors. Provides that after an
election has been called and held for that purpose, no further election on the same question
may be held for at least four years.
Present law additionally provides a procedure for the election or appointment of a marshal
who is the chief of police in municipalities with a population of 5,000 or less. Provides that
a majority of the electors of such a municipality, at an election held for that purpose, may
authorize the mayor, with the approval of the governing authority to appoint the chief of
police. Further provides that the electors may provide that the chief of police be elected.
The question of the appointment or election of the marshal shall be made only upon the
adoption of an ordinance by a two-thirds vote of the governing authority. Provides that after
an election has been called and held for that purpose, no further election on the same
question may be held for at least four years.
Proposed law provides an exception for the police chief in the town of Fordoche. Provides
that such police chief shall be appointed by the mayor with the approval of the board of
aldermen by majority vote. Provides for initial appointment upon expiration of the current
term if there is an elected chief in office on the effective date of proposed law unless the
office is vacant or filled by an officer temporarily appointed. Provides that the police chief's
term shall be concurrent with that of the mayor and aldermen.
Proposed law requires the board of aldermen, upon recommendation of the mayor, to
establish the salary of the office and to establish the duties, qualifications, and
responsibilities of the office. Provides that the chief shall report directly to the mayor.
(Adds R.S. 33:381(C)(38))
