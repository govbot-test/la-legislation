HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 219
BY REPRESENTATIVE DEVILLIER
A RESOLUTION
Benoit.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on April 23,
2026, of Marvin Dan Benoit; and
WHEREAS, Marvin was born on July 3, 1945, and he lived in Leonville, Louisiana;
his life was defined by devotion, hard work, and deep love for his family; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Benoit served in the United States Army for six years; and
WHEREAS, Marvin was a dedicated residential contractor who took great pride in
his craft and providing for those he loved, and he always put the needs of others before his
own; and
WHEREAS, Marvin shared sixty years of loving marriage with his beloved wife,
Melba, whom he love fiercely and unconditionally; his greatest joy was his role as a father
and grandfather; he was a steady and selfless presence in the lives of those he loved; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Benoit was known for his generosity and had a way of making
others feel valued; his smile lit up a room, and his contagious laughter brought warmth and
comfort to everyone around him; he inspired others by serving as an example of humility,
kindness, and strength; and
WHEREAS, Marvin is survived by his wife, Melba Benoit; sons, Daniel Benoit
(Rhonda) and Elie Benoit (Candis); daughters, Amanda Guidry (Glen Venable), Pamela
Vincent (Willie), Stacey Bonadona (John), and Anita Douget (Joey); grandchildren, Lubert
Guidry, II; Holly Guidry; Hunter Guidry; Brock Benoit; Blake Benoit; Desiree Perry; Braden

Benoit; Maddie Benoit; Natalie Wertz; Willie Vincent, Jr.; Jacob Kelsey; Caleb Kelsey;
Katherine Kelsey; Ashley Douget; Joey Douget, III; Lana Duvall; Cori Campbell; Codi
Campbell; Chandler Douget; Aaron Benoit; and Collin Benoit; twenty-two great-
grandchildren; brothers, Frederick Benoit (Betty Courville) and Paul Benoit (Barbara);
sisters, Patricia Meche (Herman) and Veronica McCartney (Jerry); and numerous
godchildren; and
WHEREAS, Marvin Dan Benoit will be deeply missed, but the legacy of his love,
sacrifice, and unwavering dedication to his family shall live on in the hearts and minds of
those who were privileged to have known and loved him.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Marvin
Dan Benoit; does hereby express enduring appreciation for his military service; and does
hereby extend sincere wishes that his family, friends, and loved ones find comfort in their
shared memories of his beautiful life.
