1 AN ACT
2 To enact Part IV-A of Chapter 2 of Title 49 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to be
3 comprised of R.S. 49:219.1.1 through 219.1.4, relative to the Governor's Task Force
4 on Impaired Driving; to create the Governor's Task Force on Impaired Driving; to

7 agencies relative to the task force; to provide for use of facilities relative to the task

9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. Part IV-A of Chapter 2 of Title 49 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of
11 1950, comprised of R.S. 49:219.1.1 through 219.1.4, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
12 PART IV-A. GOVERNOR'S TASK FORCE ON IMPAIRED DRIVING
13 §219.1.1. Policy and purpose
14 A. It is the policy of this state to take every responsible action, explore all
15 opportunities, invite meaningful contributions, and utilize all available resources to
16 reduce impaired driving involving alcohol, drugs, or a combination of alcohol and
17 drugs, and to mitigate the resulting loss of life, serious injury, and harm to
18 individuals, families, and communities.
19 B. In view of the policy of this state, it is the purpose of this Part to establish
20 the Governor's Task Force on Impaired Driving as a coordinated, multi disciplinary

5 §219.1.2. Governor's Task Force on Impaired Driving; establishment; membership;

20 Department of Health or his designee.

25 designated by the commander of the Louisiana State Police Crime Laboratory.
26 (k) The secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development or

28 (l) The commissioner of the Louisiana Department of Insurance or his

22 §219.1.3. Powers, duties, and functions; staffing; funding
23 A. The task force shall do all of the following:
24 (1) Address the high incidence of driving while impaired by alcohol, drugs,
25 or a combination of alcohol or drugs, including data collection and analysis related
26 to impaired driving arrest, prosecution, and conviction rates, the prevalence of
27 drivers refusing to submit to chemical testing as directed by law enforcement, and
28 strategies to reduce such occurrences.

1 (2) Identify and implement effective impaired driving countermeasures,
2 including substance use assessment and treatment, electronic monitoring, impaired
3 driving offender monitoring, ignition interlock devices, sanctions, and other
4 evidence-based strategies to reduce impaired driving.
5 (3) Analyze data trends related to alcohol-involved, drug-involved, and
6 polysubstance-involved fatal or serious injury motor vehicle crashes.
7 (4) Solicit input and recommendations from agencies, departments,
8 commissions, boards, or offices involved in impaired driving enforcement,
9 prevention, adjudication, and countermeasures, including law enforcement agencies
10 at the state, parish, and local levels to support coordinated implementation efforts.
11 (5) Conduct regular analysis and assessment of Louisiana’s impaired driving
12 statutes and recommend changes as needed to improve effectiveness.
13 (6) Evaluate changes in the methods or priorities of the allocation of funds
14 to state and local agencies.
15 (7) Align priorities to support the Louisiana Statewide Impaired Driving Plan
16 as updated annually by the Louisiana Highway Safety Commission and the Strategic
17 Highway Safety Plan.
18 (8) Act in an advisory capacity to the governor, office of drug policy within
19 the office of the governor, the Louisiana Highway Safety Commission, the office of
20 alcohol and tobacco control, and the office of behavioral health of the Louisiana
21 Department of Health.
22 (9) Assess the roles and interaction of federal, state, and local partners
23 involved in impaired driving enforcement and adjudication, including law
24 enforcement agencies and related operations, and develop recommendations to
25 improve the effectiveness of coordinated and multi-jurisdictional efforts to reduce
26 impaired driving incidents and fatalities throughout the state.
27 (10) Provide an annual report to the governor by February first of each year
28 regarding the task force’s findings, activities, and recommendations and provide
29 additional reports as needed or requested by the governor.

12 §219.1.4. Assistance and cooperation of other agencies; boards; use of facilities
13 All state agencies, boards, commissions, departments, and political
14 subdivisions shall assist the task force in its work by providing requested
15 information, reports, research, aid, services, and other support, without imposing any
16 cost or charge to the office of the governor. However, such assistance shall be
17 subject to the funding constraints of the respective entity. The facilities of the state
18 library and Louisiana State University shall be available for use by the task force.
19 The attorney general shall assist the task force and provide written opinions on any
20 subject requested by the legislative fiscal officer.
