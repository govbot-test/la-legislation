2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 143
BY SENATOR MIZELL
A RESOLUTION
To designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tourism Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
WHEREAS, travel and tourism play an essential role in preserving Louisiana's
culture, propel the economy, and are pivotal in shaping Louisiana's success; and
WHEREAS, travel is vital in creating quality job opportunities with two hundred
twenty-five thousand six hundred people employed in the leisure and hospitality industry,
maintaining its status as the fourth highest employer in the state; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana attracted over forty-five million domestic and international
visitors in 2025; and
WHEREAS, domestic and international travelers spent an estimated nineteen billion
five hundred million dollars while visiting Louisiana in 2025, with a 5.2 percent increase
over 2024; and
WHEREAS, tourism is funded exclusively by a dedication of three one-hundredths
of a cent of sales tax, and the funds are used by the office of tourism, under the direction of
Lieutenant Governor Billy Nungesser, to promote tourism to the state, creating an enormous
economic impact in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, members of the travel and tourism community will gather together at
the Louisiana State Capitol on Wednesday, May 20, 2026, to celebrate tourism in Louisiana.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby designate Wednesday, May 20, 2026, as Tourism Day at the Louisiana State
Capitol and does hereby express appreciation and support for the fine work of the Louisiana
travel industry in its mission to bring visitors to Louisiana and provide ambassadorial and
financial benefits to the state.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
assistant secretary of tourism of the Department of Culture, Recreation and Tourism.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
