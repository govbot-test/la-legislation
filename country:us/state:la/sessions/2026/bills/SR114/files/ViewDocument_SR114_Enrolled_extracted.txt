BY SENATOR MYERS
A RESOLUTION
occasion of its seventy-fifth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) was
founded in 1951 and, for seventy-five years, has advanced the health and well-being of
patients through excellence in clinical practice, education, advocacy, and research; and
WHEREAS, ACOG represents more than sixty-two thousand healthcare
professionals nationwide, including over seven hundred members in Louisiana who provide
essential obstetric and gynecologic care to patients across the state; and
WHEREAS, ACOG and its members in Louisiana have championed initiatives to
improve maternal health, protect the patient–physician relationship, and ensure access to
health care for all individuals; and
WHEREAS, ACOG works to create a stronger, more sustainable practice
environment for obstetricians–gynecologists and their patients; and
WHEREAS, the seventy-fifth anniversary of ACOG provides an opportunity to
recognize its historic contributions and reaffirm a commitment to advancing the health of
patients in Louisiana.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby commemorate and recognize the American College of Obstetricians and
Gynecologists on the occasion of its seventy-fifth anniversary.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this resolution be transmitted to the
American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists as an expression of appreciation for
its service and leadership.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
