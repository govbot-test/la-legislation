2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE BOURRIAQUE
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 36:502(A) and (B), 503, 505(A) and (B), 506(A) through (C),
3 508(A) through (C) and (F), 508.2(A) through (C), (E), and (F), and 508.6(C) and
4 (D) and to enact R.S. 36:505.1, relative to operational reforms within the Department
5 of Transportation and Development; to provide for the creation of the position of
6 chief operating officer within the department; to provide for the duties of the chief
7 operating officer; to provide for the duties of the deputy secretary within the office
8 of transformation; to extend the deadline for the department to implement certain
9 changes and reforms; to extend the deadline for the department to remitting payment
10 to vendors and contractors; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related
11 matters.
12 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
13 Section 1. R.S. 36:502(A) and (B), 503, 505(A) and (B), 506(A) through (C), 508(A)
14 through (C) and (F), 508.2(A) through (C), (E), and (F), and 508.6(C) and (D) are hereby
15 amended and reenacted and R.S. 36:505.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
16 §502. Officers of the department; compensation for one office only
17 A.(1) The officers of the department shall be the secretary, undersecretary,
18 deputy secretary, chief operating officer, assistant secretary of project delivery,
19 commissioner of multimodal commerce, and assistant secretary of operations, each
20 of whom shall be selected and shall perform functions as provided in this Title.
21 (2) The secretary, undersecretary, deputy secretary, chief operating officer,
22 assistant secretary of project delivery, assistant secretary of operations, executive

5 department may be in the classified state service. The secretary shall fill such
6 positions as in his opinion and judgment are necessary to the operation of the
7 department in accordance with applicable rules and regulations of the State Civil
8 Service Commission and applicable law.
9 B. No person serving as a secretary, undersecretary, deputy secretary, chief
10 operating officer, assistant secretary of project delivery, assistant secretary of
11 operations, commissioner of multimodal commerce, or in any other office in the
12 department shall receive any additional salary from the state other than that salary
13 which he receives by virtue of serving in any one of such offices. Any statewide
14 elected official appointed to serve as a secretary or deputy secretary shall not receive
15 any additional salary from the state other than that salary which he receives as a
16 statewide elected official.

18 §503. Secretary of transportation and development
19 There shall be a secretary of transportation and development who shall be
20 appointed by the governor with consent of the Senate. He shall serve at the pleasure
21 of the governor at a salary fixed by the governor, which salary shall not exceed the
22 amount approved for such position by the legislature while in session. He shall be
23 the appointing authority for the department in accordance with civil service rules and
24 regulations. The secretary shall serve as the executive head and chief administrative
25 officer of the Department of Transportation and Development and shall have the
26 responsibility for the policies of the department, except as specifically provided for
27 particular agencies transferred to or placed within the department by other provisions
28 of this Title. He shall also be responsible for the administration, control, and
29 operation of the functions, programs, and affairs of the department; provided that the

4 §505. Deputy secretary; functions; office of transformation
5 A. There shall be a deputy secretary of the department, who shall be
6 appointed by the governor and shall serve as the appointing authority for the office
7 of transformation. He shall serve at the pleasure of the governor at a salary fixed by
8 the governor, which salary shall not exceed the amount approved for such position
9 by the legislature while in session. The duties and functions of the deputy secretary
10 shall be to serve as the chief administrative officer and oversee the office of
11 transformation which shall include all duties, responsibilities, and authorities
12 necessary and lawful to optimize the department and those otherwise assigned by the
13 secretary. He shall serve as acting secretary in the absence of the secretary.
14 B. If the position of deputy secretary is not filled during the absence of the
15 secretary, the secretary may designate either the undersecretary or the assistant
16 secretary for project delivery chief operating officer to serve as acting secretary in
17 his absence. The secretary shall notify the governor in writing of his temporary
18 appointment of an acting secretary in his absence. The governor shall have the full
19 authority to revoke the appointment. In no event shall the temporary appointment
20 exceed thirty days in length, except with the specific written approval of the
21 governor. The governor may appoint a temporary secretary to act in the absence or
22 incapacity of the secretary if an acting secretary has not been appointed.

26 appointed by the secretary. He shall serve at the pleasure of the secretary at a salary
27 fixed by the governor, which shall not exceed the amount approved for the position
28 by the legislature while in session. The duties and functions of the chief operating
29 officer shall be to supervise and direct the assistant secretary of the office of project

3 §506. Undersecretary; functions; office of management and finance
4 A. There shall be an undersecretary of the Department of Transportation and
5 Development who shall be appointed by the governor. He shall serve at the pleasure
6 of the secretary at a salary fixed by the governor, which salary shall not exceed the
7 amount approved for such position by the legislature while in session, and shall
8 receive an annual salary payable monthly out of funds appropriated therefor, and
9 shall be allowed his actual necessary traveling expenses incurred in the discharge of
10 his official duties. The undersecretary shall be directly responsible to and shall
11 perform his functions under the supervision and control of the secretary and the
12 deputy secretary.
13 B. The undersecretary shall direct and be responsible for the functions of the
14 office of management and finance within the Department of Transportation and
15 Development. In such this capacity he shall be responsible for accounting and
16 budget control, procurement, data processing, management and program analysis,
17 personnel management, and grants management for the department and all of its
18 offices, including all agencies transferred to the Department of Transportation and
19 Development, except as otherwise specifically provided in this Title. He shall
20 employ, appoint, remove, assign, and promote such personnel as is necessary for the
21 efficient administration of the office of management and finance and the
22 performance of its powers, duties, functions, and responsibilities, in accordance with
23 applicable civil service laws, rules, and regulations, and with policies and rules of
24 the department, all subject to budgetary control and applicable laws. The
25 undersecretary shall exercise all powers and authority granted to him in this Title
26 subject to the overall direction and control of the secretary and the deputy secretary.
27 C. The duties and functions of the office of management and finance and of
28 the undersecretary shall be as provided in this Section, and these duties and functions
29 shall not be subject to change by the secretary or the deputy secretary, except that the

4 §508. Office of project delivery; functions; assistant secretary; powers and duties
5 A. There is hereby created within the Department of Transportation and
6 Development, the office of project delivery, which shall administer engineering
7 matters related to the state's highway program with respect to project development,
8 planning, design, construction, maintenance, extension, improvement, and regulation
9 of roads, highways, expressways, bridges, and related matters, and any other special
10 highway engineering program as may be directed by the secretary or the chief
11 operating officer. The office shall also administer the public works functions of the
12 state related to flood and drainage control, reclamation, water resources, soil
13 conservation, mapping, disaster relief, and related matters, the state's participation
14 in the National Flood Insurance Program, 42 U.S.C. 4001 et seq., and other special
15 programs as may be directed by the secretary or the chief operating officer.
16 B. The office of project delivery shall be under the immediate supervision
17 of the assistant secretary, who shall be appointed by the secretary. The assistant
18 secretary shall serve at the pleasure of the secretary and under the direct supervision
19 of the chief operating officer. He may be licensed to practice civil engineering in
20 Louisiana. The assistant secretary shall give his whole time to the duties of his
21 office.
22 C. The assistant secretary shall have authority subject to approval of the
23 secretary or chief operating officer and in accordance with applicable rules and
24 regulations of the civil service commission to employ, appoint, transfer, assign, and
25 promote such personnel as is necessary for the efficient administration of the office
26 of project delivery.
27 * * *
28 F. The chief engineer shall approve all plans, specifications, and estimates
29 for the construction of all facilities and projects for which his the office is
30 responsible. He also shall have such other duties as may be assigned to him by the

6 §508.2. Office of operations; functions; assistant secretary; powers and duties
7 A. There is hereby created within the Department of Transportation and
8 Development, the office of operations, which shall administer all matters related to
9 the operations of the department's district offices and other matters as may be
10 directed by the secretary or the chief operating officer.
11 B. The office of operations shall be under the immediate supervision of the
12 assistant secretary, who shall be appointed by the governor and shall serve at the
13 pleasure of the secretary and under the direct supervision of the chief operating
14 officer.
15 C. The assistant secretary shall have authority, subject to approval of the
16 secretary or the chief operating officer and in accordance with applicable rules and
17 regulations of the civil service commission, to employ, appoint, transfer, assign, and
18 promote such personnel as is necessary for the efficient administration of the office
19 of operations.

22 duties for which the office of operations is responsible. He shall also have such other
23 duties as may be assigned to him by the secretary or the chief operating officer, by

25 proceedings of his office annually to the secretary of the department chief operating
