FEES/LICENSES/PERMITS. Provides for the licensed profession of massage therapy.
(8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 37:3556(F), relative to the licensure of massage therapists; to
3 provide for a provisional license for massage therapist graduates; to require
4 notification to a client; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 37:3556(F) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §3556. Licensure; qualifications; provisional licensure
8 * * *
9 F. The board shall make available to any graduate of a state-approved,
10 Louisiana-based school for massage therapy a provisional license for immediate
11 use upon graduation at an established massage therapy business registered with
12 the board. The provisional license shall be active for one three-month term and
13 shall not be renewed. Any facility that employs a massage therapist who is
14 operating under a provisional license shall notify a client of the licensure status
15 of a provisionally licensed massage therapist prior to the client receiving any
16 services from that massage therapist. A provisionally licensed massage therapist

SB NO. 39

The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 39 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Present law (R.S. 37:3556(F)) provides that the board shall make available to any graduate
of a state-approved, La.-based school for massage therapy a provisional license for
immediate use upon graduation at an established massage therapy business registered with
the board.
Present law provides that the provisional license shall be active for one three-month term and
shall not be renewed.
Present law provides that any facility that employs a massage therapist who is operating
under a provisional license shall notify a client of the licensure status of a provisionally
licensed massage therapist prior to the client receiving any services from that massage
therapist. A provisionally licensed massage therapist may only work at a facility that
employs a massage therapist licensed pursuant to present law.
Present law provides that present law shall cease to be effective on December 31, 2025.
Proposed law retains present law but removes the December 31, 2025, sunset provision.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 37:3556(F))
