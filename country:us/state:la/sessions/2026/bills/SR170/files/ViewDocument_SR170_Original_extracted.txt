SLS 26RS-4301 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 170
BY SENATOR TALBOT
GENETICS. Requests the Department of Insurance and the Louisiana Department of Health
to jointly review the implementation and enforcement of Louisiana's biomarker testing
coverage law.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Insurance and the Louisiana Department of Health
3 to conduct a joint comprehensive review and study of the implementation and
4 enforcement of Louisiana's biomarker testing coverage law and to report their
5 findings.
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana enacted legislation requiring health insurance coverage for
7 biomarker testing when supported by medical and scientific evidence and recognized clinical
8 practice guidelines; and
9 WHEREAS, biomarker testing plays a critical role in the diagnosis, treatment, and
10 management of cancer and other serious diseases by enabling personalized and
11 precision-based medical care; and
12 WHEREAS, delays between the initial physician request for biomarker testing and
13 the eventual administration of testing may negatively impact patient outcomes, delay
14 treatment decisions, and increase the risk of disease progression; and
15 WHEREAS, timely access to evidence-based biomarker testing is increasingly
16 recognized as a standard component of precision medicine and modern cancer care; and
17 WHEREAS, stakeholders, including patients, healthcare providers, laboratories, and
18 insurers, have raised concerns regarding access to biomarker testing, claims adjudication and

1 utilization management practices, use of Proprietary Laboratory Analyses (PLA) codes,
2 denial patterns, appeals processes, and delays in testing approval and administration; and
3 WHEREAS, the legislature has an interest in ensuring that the biomarker testing
4 coverage law is being implemented consistently, fairly, and in accordance with legislative
5 intent; and
6 WHEREAS, a comprehensive review of claims data and implementation practices
7 would assist the legislature in evaluating whether additional statutory or regulatory action
8 is necessary to ensure patient access to medically appropriate biomarker testing.
9 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
10 does hereby urge and request the Department of Insurance and the Louisiana Department of
11 Health to conduct a joint comprehensive review and study of the implementation and
12 enforcement of Louisiana's biomarker testing coverage law from January 1, 2024, through
13 the present.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a written report of findings and
15 recommendations shall be submitted to the Senate and House committees on insurance and
16 the Senate and House committees on health and welfare no later than February 1, 2027.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report shall include but not be limited to the
18 following:
19 (1) The number of biomarker testing claims submitted to health insurers and
20 managed care organizations operating in Louisiana.
21 (2) The number of claims approved, denied, partially denied, or otherwise not paid
22 in full.
23 (3) The reasons for denial or adverse benefit determinations, including prior
24 authorization requirements, medical necessity determinations, coding issues, network
25 limitations, or other administrative grounds.
26 (4) The average length of time between the initial request for biomarker testing by
27 a healthcare provider and the administration or completion of the test.
28 (5) The number and outcomes of appeals, reconsiderations, grievances, or external
29 reviews related to biomarker testing claims.
30 (6) A review of all adjudication and utilization management practices associated with

2 (7) The utilization and reimbursement of Proprietary Laboratory Analyses (PLA)

7 including administrative, reimbursement, network-related, or timing-related barriers.
8 (10) Recommendations for legislative, regulatory, or administrative changes
9 necessary to improve compliance, transparency, and patient access.
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Insurance and the Louisiana
11 Department of Health, in conducting the study, may consult with stakeholders and interested
12 parties, including healthcare providers, patient advocacy organizations, laboratories,
13 insurers, and managed care organizations.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that one print copy and one electronic copy of the
