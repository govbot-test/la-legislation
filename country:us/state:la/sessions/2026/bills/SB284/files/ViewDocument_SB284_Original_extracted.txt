Section 1:
SLS 26RS-2 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR EDMONDS
SCHOOLS. Authorizes the Central Community School System to contract with a vendor for
management of the nutrition program and for the training and scheduling of the school
system's cafeteria employees. (gov sig)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:194(B) and to enact R.S. 17:66(H), relative to the Central
3 Community School System and the Central Community School Board; to authorize
4 the Central Community School Board to enter into a contract with respect to the
5 management of the school system's nutrition program; to provide with respect to the
6 training and scheduling of school system cafeteria employees; to provide relative to
7 the use of state and federal funds for the school system's nutrition program; to
8 provide with respect to the operation and upkeep of the school system's cafeterias;
9 to provide for legislative intent; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for
10 related matters.
11 Notice of intention to introduce this Act has been published.
12 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
13 Section 1. R.S. 17:194(B) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 17:66(H) is
14 hereby enacted to read as follows:
15 §66. Central Community School Board and school system; creation; membership;
16 qualifications; apportionment; election; powers, duties, and
17 functions; system operation

1 * * *
2 H.(1) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, the
3 Central Community School Board is hereby authorized to contract with a
4 vendor to provide for the management for the school system's nutrition
5 program or for a particular school's nutrition program. The school board may
6 include provisions in the contract to train and schedule the school system's
7 support employees for school cafeterias, to plan menus, to manage nutrition
8 program budgets, to maintain inventory, to order supplies, or to oversee the
9 daily operations and upkeep of one or more school cafeterias.
10 (2) If the school board enters into a contract with a vendor, as authorized
11 by Paragraph (1) of this Subsection, the contract shall be consistent with
12 requirements to receive federal funding pursuant to the National School Lunch
13 Program or other federal nutrition programs, as determined by the state
14 Department of Education.
15 * * *
16 §194. Administration of nutrition program; general powers of governing authorities
17 of nutrition program providers; funds for privately supplied programs
18 for profit prohibited; exceptions
19 * * *
20 B.(1) The state Department of Education, pursuant to rules and regulations
21 promulgated by the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education, may
22 employ personnel and take other action it may deem necessary to provide for the
23 establishment, maintenance, and expansion of any nutrition program and to direct the
24 disbursement of federal and state funds in accordance with any applicable provisions
25 of federal or state law.
26 (2) However, no state appropriated funds shall be disbursed for the support
27 of any nutrition program which shall be used by any private person, enterprise,
28 concern, or other entity for profit, regardless of any authority in federal or state law
29 for contracting with such a private supplier or provider of nutrition programs, except

1 with regard to the following:
2 (1)(a) The Child and Adult Care Food Program, as provided in regulations
3 issued by the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education.
4 (2)(b) Any elementary or secondary school operated by Louisiana State
5 University and Agricultural and Mechanical College or by Southern University and
6 Agricultural and Mechanical College.
7 (c) Any elementary or secondary school operated by the Central
8 Community School Board.
9 * * *
10 Section 2. It is the intention of the legislature to authorize the Central Community
11 School Board to contract for the management of the school system's nutrition program.
12 Nothing in this Act shall be deemed to require or mandate such contracts in any manner that
13 would violate the provisions of Article III, Section 12 of the Constitution of Louisiana which
14 prohibits the regulating or management of parish or city school systems.
15 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
16 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
17 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
18 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
19 effective on the day following such approval.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 284 Original 2026 Regular Session Edmonds
Present law provides for meals for students in elementary and secondary schools and other
aspects of a school nutrition program. Present law further provides that the Dept. of
Education is to take actions to implement the nutrition programs and to disburse state and
federal funds to the schools to operate the programs.
Present law prohibits disbursement of state appropriated funds for school nutrition programs
to any private person or company. Present law provides exceptions for certain schools and
programs that are allowed to contract with private persons or companies to operate the
nutrition program, as follows:
(1) The Child and Adult Care Food Program.

(2) Any elementary or secondary school operated by Louisiana State University and
Agricultural and Mechanical College.
(3) Any elementary or secondary school operated by Southern University and
Agricultural and Mechanical College.
Proposed law retains present law and provides for an additional exception to allow the
Central Community School Board to contract with a private entity to assist the school board
in training and scheduling the school system's cafeteria employees or to assist the school
board with other functions that are required to operate school cafeterias. Present law further
requires that the contracts with private vendors be consistent with all requirements necessary
to receive federal funding for nutrition programs, as determined by the state Dept. of
Education.
Proposed law provides that the legislature's intention is to authorize the Central Community
School Board to contract with private entities and not to require such contracts in any
manner that would violate the La. Constitution Article III, §12 that prohibits the regulating
or management of parish or city school systems.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 17:194(B); adds R.S. 17:66(H))
