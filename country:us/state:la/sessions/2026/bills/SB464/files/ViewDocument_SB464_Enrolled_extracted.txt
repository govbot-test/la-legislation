Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
BY SENATOR BARROW
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 22:1047(B), relative to coverage for severe obesity treatment;
3 to provide for cost calculations; to provide for duties of the Department of Insurance;
4 to provide for legislative appropriations; to provide for changes to minimum
5 coverage standards; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 22:1047(B) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §1047. Required coverage for severe obesity treatment
10 * * *
11 B.(1) Subsections C, D, E, and F of this Section shall apply only to a health
12 insurance issuer offering health coverage plans in this state to the extent that services
13 are covered by the Essential Health Benefits Benchmark Plan selected by the state
14 in accordance with applicable federal regulations. No later than August 1, 2026,
15 and annually thereafter, each insurer offering a health coverage plan that meets
16 the definition of a "qualified health plan" as defined in 42 U.S.C. 18021 shall
17 submit to the Department of Insurance an estimate, produced by a member of
18 the American Academy of Actuaries and in accordance with generally accepted
19 actuarial principles and methodologies, of the cost to provide the benefits
20 required by this Section for calendar year 2028, and subsequent years
21 thereafter. Unless otherwise modified by the commissioner, such estimate shall
22 include all cost elements including expected volumes of services per
23 member-month, the assumed population, and both an aggregate and
24 per-member per-month estimate.

SB NO. 464 ENROLLED
1 (2) The Louisiana Department of Health shall evaluate bariatric surgery,
2 pre-operative psychological screening and counseling, behavior modification,
3 nutritional counseling, and post-operative follow-up, overview, and counseling of
4 dietary, exercise, and lifestyle changes for coverage by the Essential Health Benefits
5 Benchmark Plan during its next review of the Essential Health Benefits Benchmark
6 Plan. No later than December 1, 2026, and annually thereafter, the Department
7 of Insurance shall publish an estimated cost for the state to defray the cost of
8 services required by this Section as required by 42 U.S.C. 18031.
9 (3) No later than July 1, 2027, and annually thereafter, the Department
10 of Insurance shall notify all health insurance issuers and all health plans of the
11 funding ratio and the minimum coverage standard for this Section. The funding
12 ratio shall be calculated by dividing those sums appropriated by the legislature
13 to be spent to defray the cost of services required by this Section by the
14 estimated cost needed to defray those costs as published pursuant to Paragraph
15 (2) of this Subsection. The minimum coverage standard shall be calculated by
16 multiplying the aggregate expected volumes of services per member-month by
17 the funding ratio.
18 (4) Beginning on January 1, 2028, for any plan year during which the
19 published funding ratio meets or exceeds 1.0, no health insurance issuer subject
20 to this Section shall apply a numerical cap to the services required under this
21 Section. For any plan year during which the published funding ratio is less than
22 1.0, a health insurance issuer subject to this Section may apply a numerical cap
23 to the services required under this Section of no fewer than the product of the
24 minimum coverage standard times the number of member-months from the
25 most recent plan year, rounded up to the nearest whole number.
26 (5) Any numerical cap implemented by the health insurance issuer
27 pursuant to Paragraph (4) of this Subsection shall be clearly stated in the health
28 insurance plan documents and shall be described using the actual numerical cap
29 and not by reference to a formula.
30 * * *

1 Section 2. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
2 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
3 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
4 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
5 effective on the day following such approval.
