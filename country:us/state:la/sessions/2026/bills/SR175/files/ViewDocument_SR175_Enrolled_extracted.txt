WHEREAS, increasing Medicaid coverage for breast pumps from eighty dollars to
one hundred forty dollars may improve patient access, enhance maternal and infant health

WHEREAS, a comprehensive study by the Louisiana Department of Health would
assist the legislature in evaluating the fiscal impact, healthcare benefits, provider
participation, and patient outcomes associated with increasing breast pump reimbursement

SR NO. 175 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to study the impact of
increasing Medicaid reimbursement and coverage for breast pumps from eighty dollars to
one hundred forty dollars in order to improve patient access to medically necessary
breastfeeding equipment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, at a minimum:
(1) Current breast pump utilization and access among Medicaid recipients.
(2) Comparative reimbursement rates in other states.
(3) Supplier participation and network adequacy.
(4) Potential maternal and infant health outcomes associated with increased access.
(5) Fiscal considerations and potential cost savings associated with improved
breastfeeding rates.
(6) Recommendations for legislative or administrative action.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health shall submit
a written report of its findings and recommendations to the Senate Committee on Health and
Welfare no later than February 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE

A RESOLUTION
