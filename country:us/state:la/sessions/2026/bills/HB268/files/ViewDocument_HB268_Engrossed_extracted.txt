HB 268 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Carver
Abstract: Provides relative to career-related activities for public school students.
Present law requires schools to incorporate activities which expose students in grades 6-8
to career and technical and academic fields of study. Requires schools to provide students
with at least six such activities at each grade level during each school year. Proposed law
retains present law and further:
(1) Requires La. Works to provide annually information about workforce priorities to
public school governing authorities, which shall select related activities to the extent
possible.
(2) Requires activities to include at least three activities in certain specified categories
(activities aligned to high-demand sectors in La. as identified by La. Works, direct
interaction with an employer, career pathway instruction linked to high school course
options, work-based learning exposure, and a career interest inventory).
(3) Requires that at least one annual activity be documented in the individualized
graduation plan as provided for in present law.
(4) Requires schools enrolling K-5 students to provide them with at least one of these
activities per school year.
(5) Requires school boards to certify annual compliance to the state Dept. of Education
and maintain auditable records of activities by grade.
(6) Applies present law and proposed law to charter schools.
Present law requires schools, as part of the development of individual graduation plans, to
provide career awareness and exploration activities to students in grades 6-8, including
career interest inventories. Proposed law specifically requires a career interest inventory in
sixth grade and updated inventories in seventh and eighth grades and applies present law and
proposed law to charter schools.
Present law requires BESE to develop an advisement policy for middle and high school
grades that districts can use to equip school counselors with the skills and information
needed to counsel students about career-related issues. Proposed law adds that the policy
equip them with skills and information needed to document at least one middle school
career-related activity in the student's individual graduation plan and ensure that career
interest inventories are updated in seventh grade and eighth grade and applies present law
and proposed law to charter schools.
Present law requires school counselors to provide preventive and developmental counseling
to K-6 students and authorizes them to consult with teachers on certain matters, including
the implementation of a career development program. Proposed law additionally authorizes
them to consult with teachers on providing that K-5 students participate in at least one career
awareness activity during each school year and applies present law and proposed law to
charter schools.

students participate in at least one career awareness activity during each school
year.

Section 8:
7. Apply present law and proposed law to charter schools.
