HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 64
BY REPRESENTATIVE MOORE
COURTS: Requests a study to make recommendations regarding behavioral health courts
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge the Louisiana Supreme Court drug and specialty court program to continue its study
3 of the connection between mental health issues, homelessness, and the criminal
4 justice system and the effectiveness of behavioral health courts as an alternative to
5 the traditional judicial system.
6 WHEREAS, studies have shown that people who suffer from mental illnesses are
7 more likely to enter into the criminal justice system when they do not have access to quality
8 behavioral health services in their communities; and
9 WHEREAS, national studies indicate that the rates of mental illness are consistently
10 higher in the criminal justice system; and
11 WHEREAS, approximately half of the United States jail population and over one-
12 third of the United States prison population were diagnosed with a mental illness in 2020;
13 and
14 WHEREAS, between 2018 and 2019, five hundred twenty-one thousand people in
15 Louisiana received mental health services the previous year; and
16 WHEREAS, over one-third of Louisiana's total incarcerated adult individuals were
17 identified as receiving some level of mental health care in 2019; and
18 WHEREAS, the Legislature of Louisiana enacted the "Mental Health Court
19 Treatment Act" in 2013, which established mental health court treatment programs; and
20 WHEREAS, the program allows people with mental illnesses to serve their sentences
21 on probation if they comply with certain requirements; and

1 WHEREAS, only three behavioral health courts have been launched in the parishes
2 of Calcasieu, Rapides, and St. Tammany; and
3 WHEREAS, homeless individuals may also be more likely to have mental health
4 conditions which may increase their risk of involvement in the criminal justice system; and
5 WHEREAS, people with severe mental illness may be less able to cope with the
6 stresses of homelessness or may perpetrate criminal acts that are manifestations of their
7 illness; and
8 WHEREAS, in 2008, the rate of homelessness among jail inmates who had been
9 homeless in the previous year and homeless at the time of incarceration was approximately
10 seven and a half to eleven and one-third times the annual rate of homelessness in the general
11 population; and
12 WHEREAS, it is important to understand the needs of Louisiana residents who are
13 incarcerated and suffer from mental illness in order to understand the impact of untreated
14 mental illnesses on the criminal justice system and to reduce recidivism; and
15 WHEREAS, a study was conducted and a report was presented to the legislature on
16 February 13, 2026, which recommended that further information should be gathered from
17 the Louisiana Supreme Court Drug and Specialty Office database.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
19 urge the Louisiana Supreme Court Drug and Specialty Court program to study the
20 connection between mental health issues, homelessness, and the criminal justice system.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Supreme Court drug and specialty
22 court program shall continue to survey or invite academic institutions of higher learning or
23 policy institutes to present ideas on how they can assist with the study of the effectiveness,
24 impact, and operational costs of the behavioral health courts in Calcasieu Parish, Rapides
25 Parish, and St. Tammany Parish and report its findings and recommendations to the House
26 and Senate committees on health and welfare and judiciary no later than February 1, 2027.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Supreme Court drug and specialty
28 court program shall expand its study to include a survey of the potential effectiveness,
29 impact, and operational costs to implement behavioral health courts in Ouachita Parish, East
30 Baton Rouge Parish, and Orleans Parish, and report its findings and recommendations to the

HCR 64 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Moore
Requests the La. Supreme Court drug and specialty court program to study and make
recommendations regarding behavioral health courts and report its findings to the House
and Senate committees on health and welfare and judiciary no later than Feb. 1, 2027.
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Judiciary to the
original bill:
1. Expand the study to include the effectiveness, impact, and operational costs to
implement behavioral health courts in Ouachita, East Baton Rouge, and
Orleans Parishes.
2. Require a report to the House and Senate committees on judiciary no later
than Jan. 1, 2027.
3. Make technical changes.
