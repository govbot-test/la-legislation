2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE LARVADAIN
HIGHER EDUCATION: Provides relative to initial eligibility requirements for the M.J.
Foster Promise Program
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:3047.2(A)(1), relative to the M.J. Foster Promise Program;
3 to provide relative to initial eligibility requirements for the program; to revise the
4 initial age requirement; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 17:3047.2(A)(1) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §3047.2. Initial eligibility
8 A. To be initially eligible for the award, an applicant shall meet the
9 following requirements:
10 (1) Be at least twenty-one years old. Meet the following minimum age
11 requirements:
12 (a) Be at least twenty years old in the 2024-2025 award year.
13 (b) Be at least nineteen years old in the 2025-2026 award year.
14 (c) Be at least eighteen years old in the 2026-2027 award year.
15 (d) Be at least seventeen years old in the 2027-2028 award year and
16 thereafter.

HB 407 Original 2026 Regular Session Larvadain
Abstract: Changes certain initial eligibility requirements for the M.J. Foster Promise
Program.
Present law provides for the M.J. Foster Promise Program, a financial assistance program
for students attending a two-year public postsecondary education institution or proprietary
school who are enrolled in certain qualified programs. Provides for specific initial eligibility
requirements, including meeting the following minimum age requirements:
(1) Be at least 20 years old in the 2024-2025 award year.
(2) Be at least 19 years old in the 2025-2026 award year.
(3) Be at least 18 years old in the 2026-2027 award year.
(4) Be at least 17 years old in the 2027-2028 award year and thereafter.
Proposed law removes present law minimum age requirements and requires that an applicant
be at least 21 years old for initial eligibility to receive a program award.
Proposed law otherwise retains present law.
(Amends R.S. 17:3047.2(A)(1))
