HB 407 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Larvadain
Abstract: Changes certain initial eligibility requirements for the M.J. Foster Promise
Program.
Present law provides for the M.J. Foster Promise Program, a financial assistance program
for students attending a two-year public postsecondary education institution or proprietary
school who are enrolled in certain qualified programs. Provides for specific initial eligibility
requirements, including meeting the following minimum age requirements:
(1) Be at least 20 years old in the 2024-2025 award year.
(2) Be at least 19 years old in the 2025-2026 award year.
(3) Be at least 18 years old in the 2026-2027 award year.
(4) Be at least 17 years old in the 2027-2028 award year and thereafter.
Proposed law retains present law minimum age requirements for the 2024-2025 and 2025-
2026 award years, but adjusts minimum age requirements for future years and requires the
following for applicants to be initially eligible:

(1) Be at least 21 years old in the 2026-2027 and 2027-2028 award years.
(2) Be at least 18 years old in the 2028-2029 award year.
(3) Be at least 17 years old in the 2029-2030 award year and thereafter.
Proposed law provides that a recipient who is initially eligible in the 2024-2025 or 2025-

(R.S. 17:3047.3).
Proposed law requires the administering agency to submit a report to the House and Senate
committees on education by Jan. 1, 2028, that includes certain information and data relative
to the program.
Effective on July 1, 2026.
