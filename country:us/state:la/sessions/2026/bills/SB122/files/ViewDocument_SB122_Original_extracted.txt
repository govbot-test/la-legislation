SLS 26RS-75 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR FESI
TRANSPORTATION/DEV DEPT. Provides for bridge maintenance by the Department of
Transportation and Development. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 48:21(A) and to enact R.S. 48:1(25) and 23.1, relative to the
3 preservation of bridges by the Department of Transportation and Development; to
4 provide for terms and definitions; to provide for functions and responsibilities of the
5 department; to provide for reports to the legislature; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 48: 21(A) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 48:1(25) and
9 23.1 are hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1. Terms defined
11 For purposes of this Chapter, the following terms have the meanings ascribed
12 to them by this Section, except where the context clearly indicates otherwise:
13 * * *
14 (25) "Bridges" means bridges, overpasses, culverts, and related
15 structures, together with their structural, load-bearing, safety, and operational
16 components, that are located on or carry roadways designated as part of the
17 state highway system and for which the department has responsibility by

2 * * *
3 §21. Functions
4 A.(1) The functions of the department shall be to study, administer, construct,
5 improve, maintain, repair, and regulate the use of public transportation systems and
6 to perform such other functions with regard to public highways, roads, and other
7 transportation related facilities as may be conferred on the department by applicable
8 law.
9 (2) The Department of Transportation and Development shall be
10 responsible for the following:
11 (a) The inspection, operation, maintenance, repair, rehabilitation,
12 preservation, and replacement of all bridges that are part of the state's bridge
13 infrastructure and state highway system or as applicable pursuant to the terms
14 as defined in R.S. 48:1.
15 (b) Oversight of maintenance projects aimed at restoring structural
16 integrity, improving safety, and extending the lifespan of bridges, including
17 addressing deterioration, implementing rehabilitation strategies, and managing
18 emergency repairs to ensure bridges remain safe and operational for the public.
19 (c) The responsibility of the department under this Subsection shall not
20 apply to bridges owned or operated by a political subdivision, special district,
21 toll authority, or the federal government, unless otherwise expressly provided
22 by law.
23 * * *
24 §23.1. Bridge review and report to the legislature
25 A.(1) Beginning September 1, 2026, the department shall prepare and
26 furnish a proposed plan and review of all bridges maintained, operated or
27 replaced by the department to the Senate Committee on Transportation,
28 Highways and Public Works and the House Committee on Transportation,
29 Highways and Public Works in a joint meeting of the two committees.

1 (2) Upon request of the department, the committees shall hold a meeting
2 for public comment within forty-five days of receiving the request.
3 (3) The report and review shall include at minimum the following:
4 (a) The number of bridges within the department's responsibility as
5 defined in R.S. 48:1.
6 (b) The status of the maintenance including the number of employees
7 necessary to maintain the bridge.
8 (c) The type of bridge and the parish where it is located.
9 (d) The amount of funds required to maintain the bridge.
10 (e) The source of funds to maintain the bridge.
11 (f) The Federal Highway Administration condition rating.
12 B. Beginning January 1, 2027, the department shall present a quarterly
13 review and proposed plan for the preservation of all bridges maintained by the
14 department to the Senate Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public
15 Works and the House Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public
16 Works in a joint meeting of the two committees.
17 Section 2. The Louisiana State Law Institute is hereby authorized and directed to
18 arrange in alphabetical order and renumber the definitions provided in R.S. 48:1.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 122 Original 2026 Regular Session Fesi
Present law provides for terms and definitions. Proposed law retains present law and adds
the term "bridges".
Present law provides for the functions of the department. Proposed law retains present law
and adds the function of inspection, operation, maintenance, repair, rehabilitation,
preservation, and replacement of all bridges that are part of the state's bridge infrastructure
and state highway system and oversight of maintenance projects relative to bridges.
Proposed law provides for the department to provide a report relative to the preservation of
bridges in the state to the legislature beginning September 1, 2026. Proposed law further
provides the report will provide at a minimum the number of bridges under the responsibility
of the department as categorized by Title 48, the status of the maintenance, including the
number of employees necessary to maintain the bridge, the type of bridge, and the parish it
