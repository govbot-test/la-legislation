17 §81. General powers of local public school boards
18 A.(1) Each local public school board shall serve in a policymaking capacity
19 that is in the best interests of all students enrolled in schools under the board's
20 jurisdiction. When establishing board policies, each board shall prioritize student
21 achievement, financial efficiency, and workforce development on a local, regional,
22 and statewide basis. When choosing a local superintendent of schools, each board
23 shall select a leader who shall prioritize student achievement and act in the best
24 interests of all students enrolled in schools under the board's jurisdiction.

18 CHAPTER 47. CAREER PRACTICUMS
19 §4201. Short title
20 This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Learn and Earn
21 Act".
22 §4202. Legislative findings and intent
23 A. The legislature finds that Louisiana's long-term workforce
24 competitiveness depends on the development of a coherent, statewide talent
25 development system.
26 B. Compensated, credit-bearing work-based learning aligned with the
27 State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education (BESE) approved career
28 pathways is a critical component of such a system.
29 C. Reliance solely on external employer work sites creates inequitable
30 access to work-based learning for rural students and students in underserved

1 communities.
2 D. Career practicums, as provided for in this Chapter, shall serve an
3 essential educational purpose by advancing established state objectives related
4 to career and technical education, financial literacy, employability skills, and
5 workforce development.
6 E.(1) A career practicum shall operate pursuant to a cooperative
7 endeavor agreement that complies with Article VII, Section 14 of the
8 Constitution of Louisiana and meets the requirements of the Cabela's test as
9 held in Board of Directors of Indus. Development Bd. of City of Gonzales,
10 Louisiana, Inc. v. All Tax Payers, Property Owners, Citizens of City of Gonzales,
11 et al., 938 So.2d 11 (La. 9/6/06).
12 (2) In order to satisfy the Cabela's test, a career practicum must serve a
13 valid public purpose; be an enforceable, nongratutious obligation; and there
14 must be a reasonable expectation of value equal to or greater than the value of
15 public assets provided.
16 (3) Compliance with the Cabela's test is mandatory and shall be
17 demonstrated through the cooperative endeavor agreement.
18 §4203. Definitions
19 As used in this Chapter, the following terms have the following meanings
20 unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:
21 (1) "Business partner" means a private business entity, financial
22 institution, or nonprofit organization that enters into a cooperative endeavor
23 agreement with a city, parish, or other local school board to operate a career
24 practicum.
25 (2) "Career practicum" means a limited scope educational franchise
26 operation conducted on a public high school campus pursuant to a cooperative
27 endeavor agreement, whose primary purpose is to deliver compensated, credit-
28 bearing work-based learning, aligned with BESE-approved career pathways,
29 and related instruction.
30 (3) "Fair market value" means the value of public assets, services, or

14 §4204. Authorization; limited-scope model
15 A. A city, parish, or other local school board may establish and host one
16 or more career practicums on Louisiana public high school campuses in
17 accordance with this Chapter.
18 B. A career practicum shall operate exclusively within the instructional
19 scope defined in its cooperative endeavor agreement and shall not engage in
20 general commercial activity unrelated to instructional purposes.
21 C. Career practicums shall align with BESE-approved career pathways
22 and integrate financial literacy and employability skill development.
23 D. Any student participating in a career practicum under this Chapter
24 who performs a credit-bearing internship experience aligned to a BESE-
25 approved career pathway shall be deemed to have satisfied the requirements for
26 an internship for purposes of the state school and district accountability system,
27 as defined by rules of the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education.
28 E. A business partner shall provide reciprocity under the cooperative
29 endeavor agreement that meets or exceeds the fair market value of public assets
30 utilized and shall fulfill all educational obligations established in the agreement.

19 §4205. Cooperative endeavor agreements
20 A. Each career practicum shall operate pursuant to a written cooperative
21 endeavor agreement between the city, parish, or other local school board and
22 the business partner.
23 B. The agreement shall include but not be limited to the following:
24 (1) A declaration of the essential educational and public purpose served.
25 (2) Enforceable, nongratuitous obligations of the business partner.
26 (3) An inventory and valuation of public assets utilized, including a fair
27 market value schedule.
28 (4) A reciprocity schedule demonstrating a reasonable expectation that
29 benefits provided by the business partner meet or exceed fair market value.
30 (5) Annual fair market value recertification.

14 §4206. Student labor, safety, and supervision
15 A. Student participants shall:
16 (1) Earn academic credit pursuant to BESE rules.
17 (2) Receive wages at or above the applicable minimum wage.
18 (3) Be employees of the business partner for wage and workers'
19 compensation purposes.
20 (4) Receive required safety training prior to participation.
21 (5) Be prohibited from engaging in hazardous occupations barred for
22 minors.
23 B. Career practicums shall be structured so that student participants are
24 the primary beneficiaries of the educational experience.
25 C. Career practicums shall comply with all applicable school safety,
26 supervision, and visitor access requirements.
27 §4207. Reciprocity and restricted use of funds
28 A. Business partners shall provide reciprocity as established in the
29 cooperative endeavor agreement, meeting or exceeding fair market value
30 requirements.

5 §4208. Rulemaking authority; limitations
6 The State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education shall
7 promulgate rules and regulations necessary to implement this Chapter in
8 accordance with the provisions of the Administrative Procedure Act. Such rules
9 shall implement but not waive, narrow, or redefine the statutory requirements
10 of instructional scope, fair market value valuation, reciprocity, or student
11 protections established in this Chapter.
12 §4209. Trade and commerce limitation
13 A career practicum operating solely within the instructional scope of a
14 compliant cooperative endeavor agreement shall not be deemed to be engaged
15 in trade or commerce for purposes of R.S. 51:1401 et seq.
16 §4210. Proprietary school exemption
17 A career practicum authorized under this Chapter shall be deemed an
18 extension of public educational programming and shall not be classified as a
19 proprietary school pursuant to R.S. 17:3140.1 et seq.
20 §4211. Charter school participation
21 A charter school governing authority may authorize a career practicum
22 on its campus in accordance with applicable charter law and the school's
23 charter contract. Where the career practicum involves the use of facilities or
24 property owned by a city, parish, or other local school board or other public
25 entity, any cooperative endeavor agreement required under this Chapter shall
26 be executed in a manner consistent with applicable law governing such
27 property.
