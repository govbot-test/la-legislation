ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 272
BY REPRESENTATIVE MOORE
A RESOLUTION
Public Safety and Corrections, office of juvenile justice, Louisiana State University,
the Pelican Institute, Casey Family Programs, and the Pelican Center for Children
and Families to study and report their findings on the intersection of parental
discipline, allegations of abuse, and the impact of disciplinary avoidance on juvenile
delinquency since 2015.
WHEREAS, in 2015, the legislature enacted Act No. 260, which added Civil Code
Article 228 to acknowledge that parents have the natural right and the solemn obligation to
correct and discipline their children in a reasonable manner; and
WHEREAS, R.S. 14:18 specifically provides a legal justification for the use of
reasonable discipline of minors by their parents, distinguishing such actions from criminal
conduct; and
WHEREAS, a critical distinction exists between reasonable corporal discipline
intended to instill character and accountability and acts of domestic abuse or cruelty to
juveniles which cause unjustifiable pain or suffering; and
WHEREAS, there is growing concern regarding reports of minors who circumvent
or undermine reasonable parental authority by threatening to initiate contact with law
enforcement or child protective services under the guise of reporting abuse; and
WHEREAS, the subversion of parental authority may contribute to a lack of
behavioral boundaries, potentially leading to an increase in juvenile delinquency and
subsequent adjudication or conviction for serious offenses; and

WHEREAS, the makeup of the Louisiana household, whether led by a single parent
or two parents, serves as the primary environment for child development and the first line
of defense against criminal behavior; and
WHEREAS, the legislature requires comprehensive data to understand how often
parents are facing arrests or criminal consequences for discipline that is ultimately found to
be reasonable, and whether a correlation exists between the breakdown of parental discipline
and the escalation of juvenile criminal conduct.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Children and
Family Services, the Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of juvenile justice,
Louisiana State University, the Pelican Institute, Casey Family Programs, and the Pelican
Center for Children and Families to study and report their findings on the intersection of
parental discipline, allegations of abuse, and the impact of disciplinary avoidance on juvenile
delinquency since 2015.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the entities provided in this Resolution shall
identify and study data regarding the following:
(1) The approximate number of investigations and arrests initiated against parents
for the crime of cruelty to juveniles when the underlying conduct was claimed as parental
discipline.
(2) The frequency of cases in which a minor's threat or successful contact to law
enforcement resulted in the cessation of parental discipline.
(3) To the extent practicable, a review of the disciplinary history of juveniles
currently adjudicated delinquent or prosecuted as adults to determine if prior threats against
parents hindered early behavioral intervention.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the findings of this study be reported to the
Louisiana House of Representatives no later than sixty days prior to the convening of the
2027 Regular Session.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to each
of the entities provided for in this Resolution.
