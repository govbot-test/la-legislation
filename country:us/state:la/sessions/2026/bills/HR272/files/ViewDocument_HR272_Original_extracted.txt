abuse, and the impact of disciplinary avoidance on juvenile delinquency since 2015
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Children and Family Services, the Department of
3 Public Safety and Corrections, office of juvenile justice, Louisiana State University,
4 the Pelican Institute, Casey Family Programs, and the Pelican Center for Children
5 and Families to study and report their findings on the intersection of parental
6 discipline, allegations of abuse, and the impact of disciplinary avoidance on juvenile
7 delinquency since 2015.
8 WHEREAS, in 2015, the legislature enacted Act No. 260, which added Civil Code
9 Article 228 to acknowledge that parents have the natural right and the solemn obligation to
10 correct and discipline their children in a reasonable manner; and
11 WHEREAS, R.S. 14:18 specifically provides a legal justification for the use of
12 reasonable discipline of minors by their parents, distinguishing such actions from criminal
13 conduct; and
14 WHEREAS, a critical distinction exists between reasonable corporal discipline
15 intended to instill character and accountability and acts of domestic abuse or cruelty to
16 juveniles which cause unjustifiable pain or suffering; and
17 WHEREAS, there is growing concern regarding reports of minors who circumvent
18 or undermine reasonable parental authority by threatening to initiate contact with law
19 enforcement or child protective services under the guise of reporting abuse; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-5197 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 272
1 WHEREAS, the subversion of parental authority may contribute to a lack of
2 behavioral boundaries, potentially leading to an increase in juvenile delinquency and
3 subsequent adjudication or conviction for serious offenses; and
4 WHEREAS, the makeup of the Louisiana household, whether led by a single parent
5 or two parents, serves as the primary environment for child development and the first line
6 of defense against criminal behavior; and
7 WHEREAS, the legislature requires comprehensive data to understand how often
8 parents are facing arrests or criminal consequences for discipline that is ultimately found to
9 be reasonable, and whether a correlation exists between the breakdown of parental discipline
10 and the escalation of juvenile criminal conduct.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
12 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Children and
13 Family Services, the Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of juvenile justice,
14 Louisiana State University, the Pelican Institute, Casey Family Programs, and the Pelican
15 Center for Children and Families to study and report their findings on the intersection of
16 parental discipline, allegations of abuse, and the impact of disciplinary avoidance on juvenile
17 delinquency since 2015.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the entities provided in this Resolution shall
19 identify and study data regarding the following:
20 (1) The approximate number of investigations and arrests initiated against parents
21 for the crime of cruelty to juveniles when the underlying conduct was claimed as parental
22 discipline.
23 (2) The frequency of cases in which a minor's threat or successful contact to law
24 enforcement resulted in the cessation of parental discipline.
25 (3) To the extent practicable, a review of the disciplinary history of juveniles
26 currently adjudicated delinquent or prosecuted as adults to determine if prior threats against
27 parents hindered early behavioral intervention.
28 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the findings of this study be reported to the
29 Louisiana House of Representatives no later than sixty days prior to the convening of the
30 2027 Regular Session.
