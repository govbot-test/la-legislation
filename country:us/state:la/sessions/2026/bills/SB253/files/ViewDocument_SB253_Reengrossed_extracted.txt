DIGEST
SB 253 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session McMath
Proposed law provides that no professional or occupational licensing board shall prohibit a
healthcare provider with prescriptive authority from providing patients with peptides shipped
from either of the following:
(1) A FDA-registered 503B outsourcing facility that is in compliance with 21 U.S.C.
353b.
(2) A 503A compounding pharmacy that is in compliance with 21 U.S.C. 353a and
applicable United States Pharmacopeia - National Formulary chapters.
Proposed law requires the prescribing provider to ensure peptides are prescribed pursuant
to proposed law.
Proposed law provides that no professional or occupational licensing board shall prohibit a
La. licensed pharmacist in a state permitted pharmacy from compounding and dispensing
peptides as long as the pharmacist and pharmacy are in compliance with 21 U.S.C. 353a and
applicable United States Pharmacopeia - National Formulary chapters.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 37:23.5)
