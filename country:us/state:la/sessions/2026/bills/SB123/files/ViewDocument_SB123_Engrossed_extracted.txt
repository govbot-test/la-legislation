GOVERNOR. Constitutional amendment to require the governor to certify a legislative
address removing certain judges for cause. (2/3 - CA13s1(A))
1 A JOINT RESOLUTION
2 Proposing to amend Article IV, Section 5(I) of the Constitution of Louisiana, relative to
3 gubernatorial powers and duties; to require gubernatorial certification of the removal
4 by legislative address of certain judges or a Louisiana Supreme Court justice; and to
5 specify an election for submission of the proposition to electors and provide a ballot
6 proposition.
7 Section 1. Be it resolved by the Legislature of Louisiana, two-thirds of the members
8 elected to each house concurring, that there shall be submitted to the electors of the state, for
9 their approval or rejection in the manner provided by law, a proposal to amend Article IV,
10 Section 5(I) of the Constitution of Louisiana, to read as follows:
11 §5. Governor; Powers and Duties
12 Section 5.(A) * * *
13 (I) Removal Power and Duties. The governor may remove from office a:
14 (1) May remove from office a person he appoints, except a person appointed
15 for a term fixed by this constitution or by law.
16 (2) Upon receipt of a joint legislative address, shall certify the removal
17 of an elected or temporarily appointed district, juvenile, family, parish,

DIGEST
SB 123 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Morris
Present constitution allows the governor to remove from office a person he appointed unless
that person is appointed for a term fixed by either present constitution or present law.
Proposed constitutional amendment retains present constitution.
Proposed constitutional amendment requires the governor, on receiving a joint legislative
address, to certify the removal of an elected or temporarily appointed district, juvenile,
family, parish, municipal, or court of appeal judge, or La. Supreme Court justice for
malfeasance, gross misconduct, or incompetence committed while in office if:
(1) The cause for removal was stated and entered in the journal of the house of the
legislature in which the address originated and a copy thereof was served on the
judge.
(2) A trial by the Senate, with senators under oath or affirmation for the trial, is
conducted at which the judge may present a defense to the stated cause for removal.
(3) The address received a favorable vote of a majority of the elected members of the
House of Representatives and a 2/3 majority of the elected members of the Senate.
Proposed constitutional amendment prohibits a judge removed by legislative address from
seeking any judicial office in La.
Proposed constitutional amendment does not preclude either impeachment or disciplinary
action against a judge.
Specifies submission of the amendment to the voters at the statewide election to be held on
April 17, 2027, as authorized by law.
(Amends Const. Art. IV, Sec. 5(I))
