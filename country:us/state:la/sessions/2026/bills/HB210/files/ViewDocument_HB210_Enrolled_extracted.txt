Section 1:
ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVES MELERINE, BOUDREAUX, CREWS, EGAN, JACOB
LANDRY, MCMAKIN, AND WYBLE
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 42:1111(C)(7), relative to payments from nonpublic sources; to provide for
3 the applicability of certain provisions of Act No. 492 of the 2024 Regular Session
4 of the Legislature; to provide for retroactivity; to prohibit a statewide elected official
5 from receiving compensation for lobbying, consulting, or advising; to provide for the
6 outside employment of a member of a school board or municipal governing
7 authority; to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 42:1111(C)(7) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1111. Payment from nonpublic sources
11 * * *
12 C. Payments for nonpublic service.
13 * * *
14 (7) A statewide elected official shall not be compensated for lobbying,
15 consulting, or advising.
16 * * *
17 Section 2. The provisions of R.S. 42:1111(C)(6) as enacted by Act No. 492 of the
18 2024 Regular Session of the Legislature shall be applied prospectively and retroactively.
19 However, the provisions of R.S. 42:1111(C)(6) as enacted by Act No. 492 of the 2024
20 Regular Session of the Legislature shall not apply to any matter subject to a final decision
21 of the Board of Ethics or Ethics Adjudicatory Board rendered on or before the effective date
22 of this Act. Any member of a school board or parish or municipal governing authority who
23 prior to June 5, 2024, was employed with a person that had or was seeking a contractual or
24 other business or financial relationship with the member's governmental entity or an agency
25 under the jurisdiction or supervision of the member's governmental entity shall not be
26 required to comply with the disclosure requirements in R.S. 42:1114 for such employment.
Page 1 of 2
HB NO. 210 ENROLLED
1 However, a person shall be required to file a disclosure for any employment on or after June
2 5, 2024, as required by R.S. 42:1111(C)(6).
3 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
4 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
5 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
6 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
7 effective on the day following such approval.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
GOVERNOR OF THE STATE OF LOUISIANA
