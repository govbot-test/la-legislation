SENATE BILL NO. 162
BY SENATOR SEABAUGH
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 23:1203.1(K), relative to the workers' compensation medical
3 treatment schedule; to provide for appeals; to provide for the admissibility of
4 evidence; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 23:1203.1(K) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §1203.1. Definitions; medical treatment schedule; medical advisory council
8 * * *
9 K. After the issuance of the decision by the medical director or associate
10 medical director of the office, any party who disagrees with the decision may then
11 appeal by filing a "Disputed Claim for Compensation", which is LWC Form 1008,
12 within forty-five days of the date of the issuance of the decision. The decision may
13 be overturned when it is shown, by clear and convincing evidence, the decision of
14 the medical director or associate medical director was not in accordance with the
15 provisions of this Section. The party who filed the appeal shall notify the medical
16 director that he has done so within ten days. The medical director shall compile
17 and forward the entire record used by the medical director in issuing the
18 decision to the district hearing office within fifteen days of receiving the notice.
19 The record shall be made available to the parties upon request. At the hearing
20 on the appeal, if no evidence in addition to the record is introduced, the
21 workers' compensation judge shall either affirm or reverse the decision of the
22 medical director based upon the record that was before the medical director.
23 If additional evidence that was not submitted to the medical director is
24 introduced at the hearing, then the judge shall remand the case to the medical
25 director for consideration of the additional evidence. Following remand, the
26 medical director shall render a new decision after considering the additional
