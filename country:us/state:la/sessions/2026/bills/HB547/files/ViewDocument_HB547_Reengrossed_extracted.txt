HB 547 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Thomas
Abstract: Prohibits a person from photographing, recording, or otherwise reproducing
voter registration information in precinct registers.
Present law (R.S. 18:1461.3(C)) provides for election offenses affecting election officials,
or watchers and further provides for penalties. Provides that no person shall knowingly,
willfully, or intentionally:
(1) Being a registrar, deputy registrar, commissioner-in-charge or commissioner
fail to identify an applicant to vote as required by present law.
(2) Sign another voter's name in the precinct register.
(3) Attempt to influence an election official or watcher in the performance of his
duties on election day.
(4) Disobey any lawful instruction of a registrar, deputy registrar, commissioner-
in-charge or commissioner or a law enforcement officer providing assistance
to maintain order at a polling place.
Proposed law retains present law and provides that no person shall knowingly, willfully, or
intentionally photograph, record, or otherwise reproduce voter registration information in
precinct registers.
Proposed law provides proposed law shall not apply to a person who is performing his duties
as a clerk of court or an employee of a clerk, an election day or early voting commissioner,
a registrar of voters or an employee of a registrar, or the sec. of state or an employee of the
secretary of state.
Present law provides that whoever violates present law shall be fined not more than $500 or
be imprisoned in the parish jail not more than 6 months, or both.
Proposed law retains present law and applies present law penalties to proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 18:1461.3(C)(5))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on House and
Governmental Affairs to the original bill:
1. Provide that proposed law shall not apply to a person who is performing his
duties as a clerk of court or an employee of a clerk, an election day or early
voting commissioner, a registrar of voters or an employee of a registrar, or the
secretary of state or an employee of the secretary of state.
