1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana Gaming Control Board to review the proposed acquisition
3 of Sam's Town Hotel and Casino, Shreveport by Bally's Corporation and to consider
4 economic development in northwest Louisiana.
5 WHEREAS, the gaming market is a major contributor to the economic success of the
6 Shreveport-Bossier area; and
7 WHEREAS, the gaming market is a driver of regional tourism and employment in
8 northwest Louisiana; and
9 WHEREAS, the responsible ownership and continued investment in the gaming
10 market is vital to the economic health of the region; and
11 WHEREAS, the Legislature of Louisiana believes a potential sale could be positive
12 and supports the sale.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 strongly encourage Bally's Corporation and Gaming and Leisure Properties, Inc. to present
15 the Louisiana Gaming Control Board with a redevelopment and capital investment plan for
16 their respective properties in the Shreveport-Bossier area.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge
18 and request the Louisiana Gaming Control Board to conduct stakeholder engagement
19 meetings with local elected leaders to explore land-based redevelopment opportunities and
20 to support local hiring, vendor participation, and workforce development.

HLS 26RS-1472 ORIGINAL
HCR NO. 100
1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge
2 and request the Louisiana Gaming Control Board to protect the jobs of northwest Louisiana
3 and to approve the sale of Sam's Town Hotel and Casino, Shreveport to Bally's Corporation
4 only if there are adequate guarantees and safeguards to preserve, develop, and grow the
5 economic and workforce benefits the development brings to that region.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge
7 and request the Louisiana Gaming Control Board to hold at least two public hearings in
8 Shreveport prior to approval of the sale.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge
10 and request the Louisiana Gaming Control Board to compile a summary report of any
11 meetings held pursuant to this Resolution and to submit the report or updates quarterly to the
12 House Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice, the Senate Committee on Judiciary
13 B, and the officials representing House District 2 and Senate District 39 through December
14 31, 2027.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that one print copy and one electronic copy of any
16 report produced pursuant to this Resolution shall be submitted to the David R. Poynter
17 Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
19 Louisiana Gaming Control Board.
DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HCR 100 Original 2026 Regular Session Jackson
Requests the La. Gaming Control Bd. to meet with local leaders and representatives of the
gaming industry in the Shreveport-Bossier area to discuss development opportunities and
the possibility of a community benefits agreement, and matters regarding the acquisition of
Sam's Town Hotel and Casino, Shreveport by Bally's Corp.
Further requests the La. Gaming Control Bd. to submit a quarterly report of the meetings to
the House Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice, Senate Committee on Judiciary
B, and certain representatives through Dec. 31, 2027.
