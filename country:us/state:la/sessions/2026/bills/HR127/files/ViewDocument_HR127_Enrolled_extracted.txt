WHEREAS, Teach For America also provides a high-impact tutoring program called
the Ignite Fellowship, which recruits and trains college students to provide in-school virtual
high dosage tutoring for students who need additional support in literacy; there are over four
hundred thirty Ignite Fellowship high dosage tutors in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Teach For America and its members are most deserving of the highest
recognition for their dedicated efforts to create educational opportunity for all students,
thereby laying the foundation for a better future for American education.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Teach For America for its efforts to improve
educational outcomes in Louisiana and throughout the country and does hereby extend
sincerest wishes that the organization and its members continue to prosper in their endeavors
to improve American education.

ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 127
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREIBERG
A RESOLUTION
To commend Teach For America for its efforts to improve educational outcomes for
students in Louisiana and throughout the United States.
WHEREAS, Wendy Kopp founded Teach For America to address the national
teacher shortage and to improve academic outcomes for low-income children; the
organization launched in 1990 with three hundred eighty-four corps members in the five
founding regions of New York, Los Angeles, eastern North Carolina, rural Georgia, and
southern Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Teach For America is a leadership development organization for those
who want to help students achieve the most critical educational milestones; today, the
organization celebrates it's thirty-fifth anniversary with a network of more than seventy
thousand leaders and more than one thousand eight hundred fifty leaders in Louisiana; these
individuals start in the classroom and remain in lifelong pursuit of the vision that all children
will be afforded the opportunity to receive a quality education; and
WHEREAS, Teach For America's mission is to find, develop, and support
exceptional leaders in order to expand access and opportunity for all children, and the
organization works to create an environment where educators, policymakers, parents, and
students work together to ensure that children have an adequate foundation; and
WHEREAS, Teach For America recruits outstanding leaders to serve as "corps
members"; these individuals commit to teach for two years in low-income communities,
where they can experience the challenges and rewards of creating and expanding
opportunities for children; afterwards, they become part of the organization's alumni
network, and in Louisiana, there are alumni in more than thirty parishes; and

WHEREAS, Teach For America also provides a high-impact tutoring program called
the Ignite Fellowship, which recruits and trains college students to provide in-school virtual
high dosage tutoring for students who need additional support in literacy; there are over four
hundred thirty Ignite Fellowship high dosage tutors in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Teach For America and its members are most deserving of the highest
recognition for their dedicated efforts to create educational opportunity for all students,
thereby laying the foundation for a better future for American education.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Teach For America for its efforts to improve
educational outcomes in Louisiana and throughout the country and does hereby extend
sincerest wishes that the organization and its members continue to prosper in their endeavors
to improve American education.
