SENATE FLOOR AMENDMENTS
2026 Regular Session
Representative Turner
1 AMENDMENT NO. 1
2 In Amendment No. 2 proposed by the Senate Committee on Insurance and adopted by the
3 Senate on May 7, 2026, on page 1, delete lines 5 and 6 and insert "(5)" Net cost calculation"
4 means the cost to a covered person under the health benefit plan of a brand-name or generic
5 prescription drug or a biological product or biosimilar, net of all applicable rebates and
6 discounts."
7 AMENDMENT NO. 2
8 In Amendment No 10 proposed by the Senate Committee on Insurance and adopted by the
9 Senate on May 7, 2026, on page 1, between lines 31 and 32 insert the following:
10 "(3) If the net cost is based on rebates or discounts including more than one
11 drug product from the brand manufacturers portfolio, then the health insurance issuer
12 shall provide a detailed accounting of the total portfolio rebate or discount, including
13 for each NDC the following:
14 (a) Wholesale acquisitions cost.
15 (b) Net cost."
16 AMENDMENT NO. 3
17 In Amendment No. 10 proposed by the Senate Committee on Insurance and adopted by the
18 Senate on May 7, 2026, on page 1, at the beginning of line 32 change "(3)" to "(4)"
19 AMENDMENT NO. 4
20 In Amendment No. 10 proposed by the Senate Committee on Insurance and adopted by the
21 Senate on May 7, 2026, on page 2, at the beginning of line 1 change "(4)" to "(5)"
22 AMENDMENT NO. 5
23 In Amendment No. 10 proposed by the Senate Committee on Insurance and adopted by the
24 Senate on May 7, 2026, on page 2, after line 7 insert the following:
25 "E. Continuity of therapy for current users.
26 (1) A health insurance issuer shall not, during the plan year, use compliance
27 with this Section as the basis to remove a reference listed drug or reference product
28 from a plan formulary, increase cost sharing, move the drug or product to a less
29 favorable tier, or impose new authorization, step therapy, quantity limits, or other
30 utilization management requirements for an enrollee who is currently receiving the
31 reference listed drug or reference product, unless one of the following applies:
32 (a) The prescribing provider, after consultation with the enrollee, agrees that
33 a switch is medically appropriate.
34 (b) The issuer provides a clear, readily accessible medical-necessity
35 exception process and grants an exception for continued coverage of the reference
36 listed drug or reference listed product.
37 (2) Nothing in this Section shall be construed to require a prescribing
38 provider to prescribe a generic drug or any biological product, or to require an
39 enrollee to switch from a reference listed drug or reference product to a generic drug
40 or any biological product.
41 F. Confidentiality.
42 (1) To ensure transparency regarding formulary decisions, the commissioner
43 shall provide an annual report, providing a summary of the notifications under this
44 Section, including an analysis of the overall impact on patient costs.
