2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 110
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
A RESOLUTION
Conference USA (CUSA) Women's Basketball regular season championship.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize the Louisiana Tech Lady Techsters
basketball team for their outstanding achievements and to commend the team on winning
the 2025-2026 CUSA Women's Basketball regular season championship; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Techsters secured the 2025-2026 CUSA Women's Basketball
regular season title by finishing with an impressive record of seventeen wins and only one
loss in regular season conference games; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Techsters have thirty-eight women's basketball regular season
conference titles in school history; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Techsters finished conference play with a perfect road record
of eight wins and zero losses; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Techsters carried a nineteen-game winning streak into the
2026 Air National Guard CUSA Women's Championship game; the streak was the fourth-
longest winning streak in the country at the time; and
WHEREAS, four Louisiana Tech players averaged double figures in scoring; Paris
Bradley averaged 14.8 points per game, Jianna Morris averaged 11.1 points, Jordan Marshall
averaged 10.7 points, and Alexia Weaver averaged 10.5 points; and
WHEREAS, the Lady Techsters were led by an exceptional coaching staff, including
Head Coach Brooke Stoehr, Assistant Coach Scott Stoehr, Assistant Coach Josh Ashley,
Assistant Coach Olivia Grayson, and Assistant Coach Caleb Livingston; Mikayla Baillie
served as the team's director of basketball operations; and

WHEREAS, the Louisiana Tech University Lady Techsters are most deserving of
the highest recognition for their outstanding achievements during the 2025-2026 season.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Louisiana Tech University Lady
Techsters on winning the 2025-2026 Conference USA Women's Basketball regular season
championship and does hereby encourage the coaches and players to continue their excellent
work and always strive for greatness in competition.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
