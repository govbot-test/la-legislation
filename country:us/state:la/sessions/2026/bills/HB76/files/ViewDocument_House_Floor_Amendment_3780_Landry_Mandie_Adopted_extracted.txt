13 "§549. Notification of release or escape of inmate
14 * * *
15 J.(1) The supervisor, or the person acting on behalf of the supervisor who is
16 physically present at and in charge of the correctional facility at the time it is
17 determined that an inmate has been improperly released from that facility, shall
18 immediately, or as soon as practicable, notify or take necessary steps to ensure that
19 notification of the improper release is provided to all of the following:
20 (a) The appropriate law enforcement agency.
21 (b) The victim of the crime for which the inmate was imprisoned.
22 (c) The district attorney.
23 (d) Local media outlets within the parish, at the discretion of the supervisor
24 or the person acting on behalf of the supervisor.
25 (2) The provisions of this Subsection apply to the improper release of an
26 inmate who has been detained or incarcerated on the basis of an arrest or conviction
27 for a crime of violence as defined in R.S. 14:2(B) that is a felony.
28 (3) As used in this Subsection, the following terms have the following
29 meanings:
30 (a) "Improper release" means the discharge of an inmate from a correctional
31 facility through circumstances that include but are not limited to administrative or
32 clerical error or mistake of staff.
33 (b) "Law enforcement agency" means the Department of Public Safety and
34 Corrections, office of public safety services and office of state police, and all police
35 departments in the parish.
36 (c) "Local media outlet" means a local news service, including but not
37 limited to a print, broadcast, or online platform.
