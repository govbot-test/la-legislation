10 CHAPTER 54. PROXY ADVISOR TRANSPARENCY ACT
11 §3111. Definitions
12 As used in this Chapter, the following terms have the following meanings:
13 (1) "Company" means a publicly traded, for-profit corporation, limited
14 liability company, partnership, or other business entity.
15 (2) "Company proposal" means a proposal made by a company to its
16 shareholders that is included in the company's proxy statement, including but not
17 limited to director nominations or elections, or a proposal relating to director
18 nominations or elections, executive compensation, corporate transactions, corporate
19 structure, auditor selection, or company policy on any subject.

4 §3112. Disclosure of lack of financial analysis to prevent fraud or deceit
5 A. If a proxy advisor makes a recommendation against company
6 management on a company proposal or proxy proposal, or makes a default
7 recommendation or policy involving a vote against company management on a
8 company proposal or a proxy proposal, and the proxy advisor does not do so based
9 on a written financial analysis, the proxy advisor shall do all of the following:
10 (1) Include a clear and conspicuous disclosure to each shareholder, entity,
11 or other person acting on behalf of a shareholder receiving the proxy advisory
12 service, concurrently with providing the proxy advisory service, that does all of the
13 following:
14 (a) Identifies the service being provided.
15 (b) Identifies the recommendation or policy at issue.
16 (c) States that the proxy advisor has made the recommendation or policy
17 without doing so based on a written financial analysis regarding the impact of that
18 recommended action on company investors that does all of the following:
19 (i) Analyzes the expected short-term and long-term financial benefits and
20 costs to the company of implementing the company proposal or proxy proposal.
21 (ii) Concludes what vote or course of action is most likely to positively affect
22 shareholder value.
23 (iii) Explains the methods and processes used to prepare the analysis,
24 including the experience and geographic location of the personnel who formed the
25 recommendation.
26 (2) For a proxy advisory service as defined in Subparagraph (a) or (b) of
27 Paragraph (5) of R.S. 51:3111, concurrently with providing the proxy advisory
28 service, provide the disclosure required by Paragraph (1) of this Subsection to the
29 board of directors of each company that is the subject of the service.

14 §3113. Enforcement
15 A. A violation of this Chapter is an unfair and deceptive trade practice in
16 accordance with the Louisiana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law,
17 R.S. 51;1401, et seq., and is actionable under the enforcement provisions of that law.
18 The attorney general may exercise all investigative powers under the Unfair Trade
19 Practices and Consumer Protection Law if the attorney general has reason to believe
20 a violation has occurred, is occurring, or is about to occur.
21 B. In addition to enforcement pursuant to Subsection A of this Section, any
22 person aggrieved by a violation of this Chapter may bring an action seeking a
23 declaratory judgment or injunctive relief against a proxy advisor who violates this
24 Chapter. Not later than the seventh day after the date on which an action is brought
25 in accordance with this Subsection, the plaintiff shall provide written notice to the
26 attorney general, who has a right to intervene in the action. For purposes of this
27 Subsection, an aggrieved person includes any of the following:
28 (1) A recipient of proxy advisory services provided by the proxy advisor.

HB 830 Original 2026 Regular Session Wright
Abstract: Provides for the regulation of proxy advisors.
Proposed law defines "company", "company proposal", "default recommendation or policy",
"proxy advisor", "proxy advisory service", "proxy proposal", "shareholder", and "written
financial analysis".
Proposed law requires that a proxy advisor making a recommendation or providing a policy
against company management on a decision or vote on a company proposal or proxy
proposal that is not based on a written financial analysis shall do all of the following:
(1) Include a clear and conspicuous disclosure to each person or entity receiving the
proxy advisory service that identifies the service provided, identifies the
recommendation or policy at issue, and states that the proxy advisor's
recommendation or policy is not based on a written financial analysis as defined in
proposed law.
(2) For certain proxy advisory services as defined in proposed law, provide that same
disclosure to the board of directors of the company that is the subject of the service.
(3) For certain proxy advisory services as defined in proposed law, publicly and
conspicuously disclose on the home page of the proxy advisor's website that the
advisor's proxy advisory services include one or more services that include a
recommendation or policy against company management on a company proposal or
a proxy proposal that are not made based on a written financial analysis regarding
the impact of that recommended action on company investors.
Proposed law requires that a proxy advisor making a recommendation or providing a policy
against company management on a decision or vote on a company proposal or proxy
proposal that is based on a written financial analysis shall do all of the following:
(1) Include a clear and conspicuous disclosure to each person or entity receiving the
proxy advisory service that identifies the service provided, identifies the
recommendation or policy at issue, and states that the proxy advisor's
recommendation or policy is based on a written financial analysis as defined in
proposed law, and that the written financial analysis is available upon request.
