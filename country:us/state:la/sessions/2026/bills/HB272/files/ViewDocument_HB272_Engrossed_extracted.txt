1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:4033.1(B)(introductory paragraph) and to enact R.S.
3 17:4033.1(B)(5) and (C)(7), relative to the R.E.A.D. (Reading Enrichment and
4 Academic Deliverables) Program; to expand student eligibility to include all students
5 enrolled in certain schools; to require the state Department of Education to provide
6 for the shipment of books to such schools; to require such schools to distribute books
7 and materials to students; to require the department to make determinations relative
8 to such materials; and to provide for related matters.

11 reenacted and R.S. 17:4033.1(B)(5) and (C)(7) are hereby enacted to read as follows:

13 funding

16 categories is eligible to participate in the program:

HB 272 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Carver
Abstract: Expands student eligibility for the R.E.A.D. program to include those attending
"D" or "F" schools and requires the state Dept. of Education to deliver books to these
schools and the schools to distribute books and related materials.
Present law provides for the R.E.A.D. (Reading Enrichment and Academic Deliverables)
Program, administered by the state Dept. of Education (DOE) and established to provide
books to each student who meets one of the following criteria:
(1) He is in prekindergarten and does not score satisfactorily on the literacy section of
the early childhood assessment.
(2) He is in grades K-3 and reads below grade level.
(3) He is in grades 4-5 and scored below mastery in English language arts (ELA) on the
prior school year's state assessment.
(4) His eligibility based on screeners or assessments cannot be determined but he is
recommended by his ELA teacher.
Proposed law extends eligibility to a student who meets any of the present law criteria or is
enrolled at a school that received a performance letter grade of "D" or "F" the most recent
