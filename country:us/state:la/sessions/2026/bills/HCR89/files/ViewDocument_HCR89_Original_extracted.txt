2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 89
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
Tourism to conduct a study, in consultation with the board of directors of the
Louisiana State Museum and the WWII Museum, of the feasibility of establishing
a museum and trail dedicated to the Louisiana Maneuvers
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To direct the Department of Culture, Recreation and Tourism to conduct a study, in
3 consultation with the board of directors of the Louisiana State Museum and the
4 National WWII Museum, on the feasibility of establishing a museum and trail
5 dedicated to the Louisiana Maneuvers, including potential locations, estimated costs,
6 incorporation of conference space, and affiliation with the National WWII Museum,
7 and to submit a written report of findings and recommendations.
8 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Maneuvers were large-scale military exercises conducted
9 in 1940 and 1941 across western and central Louisiana that involved over four hundred
10 thousand troops and played a pivotal role in preparing the United States Armed Forces for
11 World War II by testing strategies, equipment, and leadership which contributed to the
12 Allied victory; and
13 WHEREAS, these maneuvers highlighted Louisiana's significant contribution to the
14 nation's defense efforts, fostered economic growth in rural areas, influenced military
15 doctrine, and left a lasting legacy on communities such as Alexandria, Leesville, Fort Polk,
16 and the surrounding regions; and
17 WHEREAS, the preservation and commemoration of the history of the Louisiana
18 Maneuvers through a dedicated museum and trail would educate future generations, honor
19 veterans, promote tourism, and stimulate economic development in Louisiana; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-4060 ORIGINAL
HCR NO. 89
1 WHEREAS, a Louisiana Maneuvers museum should incorporate modern facilities,
2 including conference space, to host educational programs, veteran reunions, historical
3 conferences, and community events; and
4 WHEREAS, affiliation with the National WWII Museum in New Orleans would
5 enhance credibility, provide access to national resources, artifacts, and expertise, and elevate
6 the proposed museum to a nationally recognized institution.
7 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
8 direct the Department of Culture, Recreation and Tourism to conduct a comprehensive study,
9 in consultation with the board of directors of the Louisiana State Museum and the National
10 WWII Museum, on the feasibility of establishing a museum and trail dedicated to the
11 Louisiana Maneuvers.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, but not be limited to, the
13 following:
14 (1) Identification and evaluation of potential locations for the museum and trail, with
15 consideration given to sites in central Louisiana near historical maneuver areas, such as
16 Leesville, Alexandria, or Fort Polk, assessing factors including accessibility, land
17 availability, proximity to existing historical sites, and potential for economic impact.
18 (2) Estimation of costs associated with planning, construction, operation, and
19 maintenance of the museum, including initial capital expenditures, ongoing operational
20 budgets, and potential funding sources such as state appropriations, federal grants, private
21 donations, and partnerships.
22 (3) Recommendations for incorporating conference space within the museum design,
23 including specifications for multipurpose venues capable of hosting events, seminars, and
24 exhibitions, with estimates of associated costs and benefits.
25 (4) Analysis of requirements and processes for affiliating the proposed museum with
26 the National WWII Museum, including any necessary amendments to the charter of the
27 National WWII Museum to enable such affiliation, potential governance structures, shared
28 resources, and benefits to historical preservation and public education.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Culture, Recreation and
30 Tourism shall submit a written report of its findings and recommendations, including any
Page 2 of 3

HCR NO. 89
1 proposed legislation, to the House Committee on Municipal, Parochial and Cultural Affairs
2 and the Senate Committee on Local and Municipal Affairs no later than March 1, 2027.
3 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
4 lieutenant governor, the chair of the board of directors of the Louisiana State Museum, and
5 the president and chief executive officer of the National WWII Museum.
