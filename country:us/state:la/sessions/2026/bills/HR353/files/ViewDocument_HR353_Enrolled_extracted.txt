HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 353
BY REPRESENTATIVE SCHLEGEL
A RESOLUTION
Awareness Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, Arthrogryposis multiplex congenita is a condition that causes joints to
be stiff and crooked at birth, and a newborn with AMC lacks the normal range of motion in
one or more joints; the condition develops prenatally and can be caused by an underlying
condition or syndrome; and
WHEREAS, AMC is an umbrella diagnosis with many syndromes and subtypes, and
the condition occurs in one in every three thousand live births; there are more than four
hundred identified types of the condition; and
WHEREAS, medical professionals do not fully understand the cause of AMC, but
it may be due to an unborn baby not moving properly during fetal development; and
WHEREAS, AMC can affect the joints of the hands, feet, hips, knees, elbows,
shoulders, wrists, fingers, toes, jaw, and spine, and the symptoms of the disease vary from
person to person; and
WHEREAS, children across the world battle AMC, and although the condition is not
curable, patients receive treatment, including stretching, physical therapy, occupational
therapy, and speech therapy, which allows them to live with as much independence in their
daily lives as possible; and
WHEREAS, on Tuesday, June 30, families across the world wear the color blue to
honor those affected by Arthrogryposis; increased awareness and expanded knowledge of
AMC will allow the community at large to provide better support for people who face this
condition.

Multiplex Congenita (AMC) Awareness Day at the state capitol and does hereby express
support for individuals facing AMC and their families.
