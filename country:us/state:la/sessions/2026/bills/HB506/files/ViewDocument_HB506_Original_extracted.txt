13 §463.243. Special prestige license plate; blackout plates
14 A. The secretary of the Department of Public Safety and Corrections shall
15 establish a special prestige motor vehicle license plate to be known as the "blackout"
16 plate, provided that there is a minimum of one thousand applicants for such plate.
17 The plate shall be restricted to use on passenger cars, pickup trucks, recreational
18 vehicles, motorcycles, and vans.
19 B. The design shall display white alpha-numeric series on a black
20 background in compliance with R.S. 47:463(A)(3). The design shall include a blue
21 line across the plate to symbolize fallen law enforcement officers.
22 C. The special license plate shall be issued, upon application to any citizen
23 of Louisiana in the same manner as any other motor vehicle license plate.
24 D. The department shall collect an annual royalty fee of twenty-five dollars
25 that shall be disbursed in accordance with Subsection E of this Section. This fee
26 shall be in addition to the standard motor vehicle license tax imposed by Article VII,
27 Section 5 of the Constitution of Louisiana and a handling fee of three dollars and
28 fifty cents for each plate to be retained by the department to offset a portion of
29 administrative costs.

HB 506 Original 2026 Regular Session Chassion
Abstract: Creates the "blackout" and certain high school special prestige license plates, and
provides for the creation, issuance, design, implementation, fees, distributions, and
promulgation of rules applicable to this license plate.
Proposed law (R.S. 47:463.243) requires that the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections
(DPS&C), office of motor vehicles (OMV), issue a special prestige license plate to be known
as the "blackout" license plate, provided that there is a minimum of 1,000 applicants for such
plates.
Proposed law specifies that the plate is restricted to use on passenger cars, pickup trucks,
recreational vehicles, motorcycles, and vans.
Proposed law requires the plate to display white alpha-numeric series on a black background.
The design must include a blue line across the plate to symbolize fallen law enforcement
officers.
Proposed law specifies that the plate will be issued, upon application, to any citizen of the
state in the same manner as any other motor vehicle license plate.
Proposed law requires a one-time fee of $25 in addition to the standard motor vehicle license
tax imposed in present law and a handling fee of $3.50 for each plate to be retained by the
dept. to offset a portion of administrative costs.
Proposed law requires the dept. to retain one dollar from each annual fee to offset
administrative costs. Specifies that the remainder of the fee will be forwarded to the La.
Fraternal Order of Police as a charitable donation to the association by the applicant.
Present law (R.S. 47:463.73) requires the OMV to establish special prestige license plates
for the La. parochial, public, and private high schools, provided there are a minimum of
1,000 applicants for the plates.

Comeaux High School, Early College Academy, and Edward J. Sam Accelerated School of

Proposed law extends existing authority for the secretary to promulgate and adopt rules and
regulations as are necessary to implement the provisions of proposed law.
Directs the DPS&C, OMV, to create these special prestige license plate when the applicable
statutory provisions are met and its system is updated to accommodate the creation of new
plates.
