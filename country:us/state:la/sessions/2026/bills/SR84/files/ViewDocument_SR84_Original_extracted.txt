SLS 26RS-1093 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 84
BY SENATORS STINE, ABRAHAM AND REESE
COMMENDATIONS. Commends the St. Louis Catholic High School boys soccer team
upon winning the Division III state championship.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate the St. Louis Catholic High School boys soccer team upon
3 winning the Division III state championship.
4 WHEREAS, the members of the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana take great
5 pride in recognizing the achievements of student-athletes whose discipline, perseverance,
6 and teamwork bring honor to their schools and communities; and
7 WHEREAS, the boys soccer team of St. Louis Catholic High School in Lake Charles
8 captured the Division III state championship with a hard-fought 1-0 victory over University
9 Laboratory School at Strawberry Stadium; and
10 WHEREAS, the victory marked the Saints' fourteenth state championship in program
11 history and their first since 2017, continuing a long tradition of excellence that has defined
12 the St. Louis Catholic High School's soccer program for decades; and
13 WHEREAS, the Saints compiled an outstanding 20-5 record and demonstrated
14 resilience and determination throughout the season and postseason; and
15 WHEREAS, the team's belief and momentum were forged through several defining
16 moments during the season, including a significant victory at Bossier that broke a
17 longstanding St. Louis Catholic's losing streak at that venue and ignited the team's
18 confidence; and

1 WHEREAS, the Saints also secured a signature victory over perennial powerhouse
2 Catholic High School in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, when Cameron Dufrene scored the
3 decisive goal with ten minutes remaining and goalkeeper Nicholas Jackson delivered a
4 dramatic penalty save in the final minute to preserve the win; and
5 WHEREAS, St. Louis Catholic went on to win eleven of its final thirteen regular
6 season games, including several key victories over local rivals, further establishing the team
7 as a championship contender; and
8 WHEREAS, the Saints displayed remarkable resolve during the postseason, rallying
9 for comeback victories in both the quarterfinal round against E.D. White Catholic High
10 School and the semifinal round against Loyola College Prep in Shreveport; and
11 WHEREAS, in the semifinal match up, Tucker Williams delivered a clutch hat trick
12 and Will Flavin scored two goals as the Saints surged to a 6-2 victory after trailing early; and
13 WHEREAS, the championship match proved to be a defensive battle in which
14 sophomore goalkeeper Nicholas Jackson made key saves to keep the game scoreless in the
15 first half; and
16 WHEREAS, the Saints broke through in the thirty-fourth minute when senior
17 midfielder Drake Hinton lofted a corner kick to junior forward Will Flavin, whose header
18 found the back of the net for the match's only goal, earning Flavin Most Outstanding Player
19 honors; and
20 WHEREAS, Flavin's performance was particularly inspiring as he returned from
21 injury during the playoffs to play a pivotal role in the Saints' championship run; and
22 WHEREAS, throughout the season, Ty Pousson provided consistent offensive
23 leadership, finishing with seventeen goals and twelve assists to lead the team in both
24 categories; and
25 WHEREAS, the Saints' championship season was guided by first-year Head Coach
26 Zach Malik and assistant coaches Alexander Stroh, Tyson Green, Matthew Walsh, and Noah
27 Ellender, whose leadership, preparation, and dedication helped restore the program to
28 championship form; and
29 WHEREAS, Assistant Coach Alexander Stroh devoted countless hours to scouting
30 opponents across the state and conducting detailed film analysis while continuing his
