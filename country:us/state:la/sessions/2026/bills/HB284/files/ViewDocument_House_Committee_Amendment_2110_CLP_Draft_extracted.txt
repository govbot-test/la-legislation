5 AMENDMENT NO. 2
6 On page 2, delete lines 8 through 19 in their entirety and insert the following:
7 "(1)(a) "Abandoned property" means any commercial or residential property
8 that is vacant or not lawfully occupied. The terms "vacant" or "not lawfully
9 occupied" have the same meanings and circumstances described in R.S. 33:1236(49),
10 R.S. 33:4720.59, and R.S. 33:4754 and include any or all of the following property
11 conditions:
12 (i) Not actually occupied by the owner, lessee, or an authorized occupant.
13 (ii) Lacking customary utility service or has been left unsecured or
14 inadequately secured from unauthorized entry such that the premises may be entered
15 and utilized by vagrants or other uninvited persons as a place of harborage.
16 (iii) Deemed detrimental to or endangers the public safety, health, or welfare
17 by reason of dilapidation, deterioration, disrepair, neglect, damage, vacancy,
18 abandonment, or other condition.
19 (iv) Determined to be unfit for human occupancy, unsafe structures, unlawful
20 structures, structures containing unsafe equipment, or housing code violations as
21 those terms may be defined by state law, local ordinances, or applicable building,
22 housing, fire, or health codes.
23 (v) Considered a public nuisance under state law, local ordinance, or
24 applicable administrative or judicial determination.
25 (vi) Deemed uninhabitable or that presents a threat to public health or safety
26 because of the physical condition, use, or disuse.
27 (b) Property that is uninhabitable and for which ad valorem taxes have been
28 delinquent for three or more years may be presumed to be abandoned. However, the
29 presumption shall be rebuttable. Nothing in this definition shall be construed to limit
30 the authority of a governing authority to determine abandonment under other
31 applicable provisions of law.
32 (2)(a) "Blighted property" means any commercial or residential premises
33 including buildings, structures, or vacant lots, which because of physical condition,
34 use, or neglect are detrimental to the public health, safety, morals, or welfare, impair
35 the economic stability of the surrounding area, or constitute a blighting influence on
36 nearby properties. The term includes any or all of the following property conditions:
37 (i) Has been declared vacant, uninhabitable, hazardous, or blighted by a court
38 of competent jurisdiction, an administrative hearing officer acting pursuant to R.S.
39 13:2575 or 2576, or any other applicable law.
40 (ii) Has been determined to be a public nuisance, fire hazard, environmental
41 hazard, or threat to public health or safety pursuant to state law or a local ordinance.
42 (iii) Contains or constitutes an unsafe structure, unlawful structure, structure
43 unfit for human occupancy, or structure containing unsafe equipment as defined by
44 applicable state law, building codes, housing codes, fire codes, or local ordinances.
45 (iv) Is dilapidated, decayed, deteriorated, structurally unsafe, vermin-infested,
46 unsanitary, lacking essential utilities or facilities, or otherwise maintained in a
