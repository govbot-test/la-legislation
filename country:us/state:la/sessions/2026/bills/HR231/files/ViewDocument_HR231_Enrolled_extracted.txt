HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 231
BY REPRESENTATIVE KNOX
A RESOLUTION
To commend the New Orleans Alumni Chapter of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity, Incorporated,
on the occasion of its ninetieth anniversary.
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity,
Incorporated, was chartered in 1936 as the first Louisiana-based alumni chapter of the
fraternity; the chapter was established by a group of accomplished professional men who
were committed to the guiding principle of achievement in every field of human endeavor;
and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter quickly became a cornerstone of
Kappa Alpha Psi in the United States Gulf South and brought together educators, physicians,
ministers, attorneys, and business leaders who sought to extend the fraternity beyond college
campuses and into the broader civic life of New Orleans; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter played a pivotal role in the expansion
of Kappa Alpha Psi throughout Louisiana and the Southwestern Province; chapter members
supported the establishment and development of several undergraduate and alumni chapters,
which strengthened the reach of the fraternity in the New Orleans metropolitan area and
beyond; the chapter also built close relationships with historically Black institutions in the
city, including Dillard University and Xavier University of Louisiana, which allowed it to
mentor generations of young men and support undergraduate chapters at those institutions;
and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter grew in both membership and
influence throughout the mid-twentieth century, and members served in prominent
leadership roles within the Southwestern Province and at the national level of the fraternity;

the chapter hosted major provincial gatherings, including the Southwestern Province Council
meetings in 1950, 1962, 1971, and 1980; the events reinforced the status of New Orleans as
an important center for the fraternity and its activities; and
WHEREAS, one of the most significant milestones in the history of the New Orleans
Alumni Chapter occurred in 1976, when it hosted the Sixtieth Grand Chapter Meeting of
Kappa Alpha Psi; the national conclave brought more than one thousand six hundred
delegates from across the country and set a new attendance record for the fraternity; the
leadership of Polemarch Eddie J. Triggs and Convention Chairman Herbert Jackson allowed
the chapter to organize one of the most memorable national gatherings of the fraternity; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter hosted the Sixty-Fourth Southwestern
Province Council in 2000 and the Seventy-Sixth Southwestern Province Council in 2012;
these events reinforced the organizational strength of the chapter and its ongoing
commitment to supporting the growth and governance of the fraternity; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter has been recognized on numerous
occasions for its outstanding service and leadership; it was named Alumni Chapter of the
Year at the Twenty-Ninth Grand Chapter Meeting in 1939; chapter members have received
numerous provincial and national honors, including lifetime achievement recognitions and
distinguished service awards; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter remains committed to service in the
New Orleans community; members have contributed to educational initiatives, youth
mentorship, civic leadership, and community development efforts across the city; the chapter
has also worked to support the development of future leaders by offering scholarships, youth
programs, and partnerships with local institutions; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter hosted the Eighty-Second Grand
Chapter Meeting of Kappa Alpha Psi in 2015; the chapter was recognized as the
Southwestern Province Chapter of the Year in 2017, and in 2026, it was presented with the
Alumni Social Media Award; and
WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumni Chapter of Kappa Alpha Psi Fraternity,
Incorporated, remains one of the most historic and influential chapters in the Southwestern
Province, and its members are deserving of recognition for upholding their commitment to
achievement, brotherhood, and service.
