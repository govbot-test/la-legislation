HLS 26RS-697 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 72
BY REPRESENTATIVE BEAULLIEU
methods for Acute Myeloid Leukemia
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To memorialize the United States Congress to expedite and prioritize research, clinical
3 trials, and treatment development related to Acute Myeloid Leukemia and to
4 designate such efforts as the "Jonas A. Feeley Act".
5 WHEREAS, Acute Myeloid Leukemia is a rare and aggressive blood cancer
6 characterized by the rapid proliferation of abnormal myeloid cells resulting in
7 life-threatening complications and a high mortality rate; and
8 WHEREAS, Acute Myeloid Leukemia often progresses within days or weeks of
9 diagnosis, leaving patients and families with extremely limited windows for treatment and
10 intervention; and
11 WHEREAS, despite advancements in oncology and hematology, the overall five-year
12 survival rate for Acute Myeloid Leukemia remains approximately thirty percent,
13 underscoring the urgent need for expanded research, improved therapies, and accelerated
14 clinical trials; and
15 WHEREAS, Jonas A. Feeley was unexpectedly diagnosed with Acute Myeloid
16 Leukemia on December 17, 2025, and passed away on December 18, 2025, demonstrating
17 the devastating speed, severity, and unpredictability of this disease; and
18 WHEREAS, Jonas A. Feeley was a devoted husband, family member, and friend
19 whose life, character, and service left a lasting impact on his loved ones and community; and
20 WHEREAS, increased federal investment in early detection, biomedical research,
21 and innovative treatment options for Acute Myeloid Leukemia has the potential to save lives,
Page 1 of 2

1 improve outcomes, and reduce the emotional and financial burden on families nationwide;
2 and
3 WHEREAS, veterans and other populations with environmental and occupational
4 exposures may face elevated risks for hematologic malignancies, including Acute Myeloid
5 Leukemia; and
6 WHEREAS, state legislatures have a long-standing role in urging federal action on
7 matters of national health importance and in advocating for policies that advance medical
8 research and public welfare.
9 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
10 memorialize the Congress of the United States, the National Institutes of Health, the
11 National Cancer Institute, and the Department of Health and Human Services to expedite and
12 prioritize research, clinical trials, and treatment development related to Acute Myeloid
13 Leukemia.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that such accelerated federal efforts be collectively
15 designated and recognized as the "Jonas A. Feeley Act", in honor of his life, and in
16 recognition of all individuals and families affected by Acute Myeloid Leukemia.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
18 presiding officers of the Senate and the House of Representatives of the Congress of the
19 United States of America, the Louisiana congressional delegation, the secretary of the United
20 States Department of Health and Human Services, and the directors of the National Institutes
21 of Health and the National Cancer Institute.
