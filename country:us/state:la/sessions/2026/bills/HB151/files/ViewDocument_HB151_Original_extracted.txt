8 Art. 978.1. Requirements for submitting motion to expunge arrest information
9 A. The following documents, if any exist, shall be submitted to the Bureau
10 of Criminal Identification and Information in addition to the motion to expunge
11 arrest information:
12 (1) Bills of information.

14 (3) In the absence of court minutes, a charge disposition report.
15 (4) A copy of the first offender pardon or governor's "gold seal" pardon.
16 (5) Order for set aside or dismissal pursuant to Article 893 or 894.
17 B. The following documents, if applicable, shall be submitted to the Bureau
18 of Criminal Identification and Information in addition to the motion to expunge
19 arrest information and documents required in Paragraph A of this Article:

HB 151 Original 2026 Regular Session Boyer
Abstract: Requires a person making a motion to expunge arrest information to provide
additional documentation to the Bureau of Criminal Identification and Information.
Present law provides for motions to expunge.
Proposed law retains present law and requires someone making a motion to expunge arrest
information to also provide bills of information, court minutes, a pardon copy, and a motion
to set aside or dismiss, if they exist, to the Bureau of Criminal Identification and Information
in addition to the motion to expunge.
Proposed law requires someone making a motion to expunge arrest information to provide,
if applicable, a criminal background check, a letter from the district attorney for a fee
waiver, certifications from the district attorney regarding criminal charges and pretrial
diversion programs, sex offender notification waiver, and a determination of innocence.
(Adds C.Cr.P. Art. 978.1)
