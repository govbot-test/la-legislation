HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 60
BY REPRESENTATIVE MCMAKIN
Development to study the benefits of implementing the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative
(Department of Transportation and Development's Responsibility for Interstate 12
Vitality and Efficiency) for major improvements to the shoulders and capacity of the
Interstate 12 and Interstate 55 Interchange
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development (DOTD) to study
3 the benefits of implementing the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative (DOTD's Responsibility for
4 Interstate 12 Vitality and Efficiency), including major improvements to the shoulders
5 and capacity of the Interstate 12 (I-12), and to develop a comprehensive,
6 shovel-ready plan that addresses safety, congestion, and regional connectivity.
7 WHEREAS, I-12 serves as a critical corridor for both daily commuters and
8 commercial traffic throughout Louisiana and the southeastern United States; and
9 WHEREAS, as our region continues to grow, so does the pressure on I-12 with
10 traffic volumes increasing steadily and leading to frequent slowdowns, dangerous
11 bottlenecks, and limited access for first responders during emergencies; and
12 WHEREAS, the merging lanes of I-12 present persistent safety risks, increase
13 emergency response times, and hinder the efficiency of traffic flow during peak hours and
14 major incidents; and
15 WHEREAS, strategic improvements to I-12, and its interchanges, are essential to
16 accommodate current and future transportation demands, enhance motorist safety, and foster
17 continued economic growth; and
18 WHEREAS, I-12 traverses through the following House districts: (1) sixty-eight,
19 Representative Don Wallace McMakin, (2) sixty-nine, Representative Paul Sawyer, (3) one-

14 Louisiana's highway network and would benefit from targeted upgrades to improve traffic
15 movement and overall system reliability; and
16 WHEREAS, the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative seeks to address these longstanding
17 transportation challenges through proactive planning and investment in high-impact
18 infrastructure improvements; and
19 WHEREAS, implementing the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative will establish the DOTD's
20 responsibility for I-12's vitality and efficiency through a transformative investment in
21 infrastructure, including the I-12 and I-55 Interchange which is a key junction critical to
22 regional transportation and commerce.
23 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
24 urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to study the benefits
25 of implementing the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative (DOTD's Responsibility for Interstate 12 Vitality
26 and Efficiency) including major improvements to the merging lanes and to develop a
27 comprehensive and shovel-ready plan that addresses long-standing safety concerns, reduces
28 congestion, and supports regional growth, including improvements to the I-12 and I-55
29 Interchange.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that as part of the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative, a
2 comprehensive approach to improving I-12 should include efforts to study the effectiveness
3 and possible removal of ramp metering devices to eliminate unnecessary lane restrictions
4 and evaluate the impact of on-ramp surveillance cameras, which may impede traffic flow
5 and diminish public confidence in transportation infrastructure.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall submit updates via email once
7 a quarter to each representative and senator of the districts encompassing I-12.
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall submit a written report detailing
9 its findings to the House Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public Works and the
10 Senate Committee on Transportation, Highways, and Public Works prior to August 1, 2027.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
12 secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.

HCR 60 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session McMakin
Urges and requests the Dept. of Transportation and Development (DOTD) to study the
benefits of implementing the D.R.I.V.E. Initiative (DOTD's Responsibility for Interstate 12
(I-12) Vitality and Efficiency) including major improvements to the merging lanes and
capacity of I-12 and to develop a comprehensive and shovel-ready plan that addresses
long-standing safety concerns, reduces congestion, and supports regional growth, including
improvements to the I-12 and I-55 Interchange. Requires the DOTD submit an update via
email to legislators representing districts encompassing I-12 once a quarter. Additionally,
requires the DOTD to submit a written report detailing its findings to the House and Senate
transportation committees by Aug. 1, 2027.
