WHEREAS, the Louisiana Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education,

and programs that expand access to care, remove systemic barriers, promote health equity,

HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 123
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
To designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as HIV and Hepatitis C Education, Prevention,
and Treatment Awareness Day at the state capitol and to recognize the Louisiana
Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education, Prevention, and Treatment for its
commitment to improving public health outcomes across the state.
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education,
Prevention, and Treatment was established by the Louisiana Legislature to serve as an
advisory body to the governor, the Louisiana Department of Health, and the legislature on
matters related to HIV and Hepatitis C education, prevention, and treatment; and
WHEREAS, the commission is composed of healthcare providers, public health
professionals, and individuals with lived experience and provides a collaborative
environment with diverse expertise to inform policy and program development; and
WHEREAS, HIV and Hepatitis C are significant public health concerns in Louisiana
that impact thousands of residents and require ongoing, coordinated efforts in education,
prevention, and access to care; and
WHEREAS, effective prevention strategies, early diagnosis, and access to
appropriate treatment are necessary to improve health outcomes, reduce transmission, and
enhance quality of life for individuals living with HIV and Hepatitis C; and
WHEREAS, the commission works to educate people about evidence-based policies
and programs that expand access to care, remove systemic barriers, promote health equity,
and improve coordination across public and private healthcare systems; and
WHEREAS, public awareness and education play pivotal roles to reduce stigma,
encourage testing, and connect individuals to life-saving and life-sustaining treatment; and
