HLS 26RS-2868 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 123
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH: Designates April 15, 2026, as HIV and Hepatitis C
Education, Prevention, and Treatment Awareness Day at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as HIV and Hepatitis C Education, Prevention,
3 and Treatment Awareness Day at the state capitol and to recognize the Louisiana
4 Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education, Prevention, and Treatment for its
5 commitment to improving public health outcomes across the state.
6 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education,
7 Prevention, and Treatment was established by the Louisiana Legislature to serve as an
8 advisory body to the governor, the Louisiana Department of Health, and the legislature on
9 matters related to HIV and Hepatitis C education, prevention, and treatment; and
10 WHEREAS, the commission is composed of healthcare providers, public health
11 professionals, and individuals with lived experience and provides a collaborative
12 environment with diverse expertise to inform policy and program development; and
13 WHEREAS, HIV and Hepatitis C are significant public health concerns in Louisiana
14 that impact thousands of residents and require ongoing, coordinated efforts in education,
15 prevention, and access to care; and
16 WHEREAS, effective prevention strategies, early diagnosis, and access to
17 appropriate treatment are necessary to improve health outcomes, reduce transmission, and
18 enhance quality of life for individuals living with HIV and Hepatitis C; and
19 WHEREAS, the commission works to educate people about evidence-based policies
20 and programs that expand access to care, remove systemic barriers, promote health equity,
21 and improve coordination across public and private healthcare systems; and

1 WHEREAS, public awareness and education play pivotal roles to reduce stigma,
2 encourage testing, and connect individuals to life-saving and life-sustaining treatment; and
3 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana has made innovative and collaborative efforts to
4 address HIV and Hepatitis C and demonstrated the importance of strategic partnerships and
5 sustained public health initiatives; and
6 WHEREAS, it is fitting to recognize the importance of continued education,
7 prevention, and treatment efforts, which help to strengthen the state response to these
8 conditions and support the wellbeing of all residents.
9 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
10 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as HIV and
11 Hepatitis C Education, Prevention, and Treatment Awareness Day at the state capitol and
12 does hereby recognize the Louisiana Commission on HIV and Hepatitis C Education,
13 Prevention, and Treatment for its dedication to advancing education, prevention, and
14 treatment efforts and for its service to the people of Louisiana.
