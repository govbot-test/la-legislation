30 §2150. Purpose; legislative intent
31 The purpose of the legislature in enacting this Chapter is the protection of the
32 health, safety, and general welfare of all those persons dealing with persons engaged
33 in the contracting and plumbing vocation vocations, and the affording of such
34 persons of an effective and practical protection against the incompetent,
35 inexperienced, unlawful, and fraudulent acts of contractors or plumbers with whom
36 they contract. Further, the legislative intent is that the State Licensing Board for
37 Contractors shall monitor construction and plumbing projects to ensure compliance
38 with the licensure requirements of this Chapter.

37 A.(1) There is hereby established within the board the Plumbing Contractors
38 Subcommittee, hereinafter referred to as "plumbing subcommittee", consisting of
39 the following eleven members appointed by the governor, each of whom shall be a

49 Louisiana Associated General Contractors.

51 Associated Builders and Contractors of Louisiana.

53 Louisiana Home Builders Association.
54 (f) Two members, each of whom shall not be associated with the plumbing
55 industry, to serve as members-at-large.
56 (2) Each State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education district shall
57 be represented among the membership provided in this Subsection.

11 PART II. LOUISIANA PLUMBING WORKFORCE ACCESS ACT
12 §2175.1. Short title
13 This Part shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Plumbing
14 Workforce Access Act".
15 §2175.2. Definitions
16 As used in this Part, the following terms have the following meanings:
17 (1) "Approved institution" means a community college, vocational-technical
18 school, or correctional facility training program that has received board approval to
19 offer the Institutional Plumbing Pathway program pursuant to this Part.
20 (2) "Board" means the State Licensing Board for Contractors.
21 (3) "Competency checkpoint" means a standardized practical skills
22 assessment administered or certified by the board at defined stages of the
23 institutional pathway.
24 (4) "Industry rotation" means a structured period of supervised hands-on
25 work experience completed by an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student at a
26 participating licensed plumbing contractor's worksite.
27 (5) "Institutional Plumbing Pathway student" means a person enrolled in an
28 approved institution's program established pursuant to this Part.
29 (6) "LCTCS" means the Louisiana Community and Technical College
30 System.
31 (7) "Participating employer" means a licensed plumbing contractor that has
32 registered with the board to accept Institutional Plumbing Pathway students for
33 industry rotation as provided in this Part.
34 (8) "Returning citizen" means a person who has been released from a state
35 or federal correctional facility within the preceding five years.
36 §2175.3. Institutional Plumbing Pathway established
37 A. The board, in coordination with LCTCS, shall establish the Institutional
38 Plumbing Pathway as an alternative route to qualify for the journeyman plumber
39 examination. The Institutional Plumbing Pathway consists of three components:
40 (1) Classroom and laboratory instruction at an approved institution as
41 provided in R.S. 37:2175.5.
42 (2) Competency checkpoints as provided in R.S. 37:2175.6.
43 (3) Industry rotation as provided in R.S. 37:2175.7.
44 B. Completion of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway satisfies the experience
45 and training requirements for application to sit for the journeyman plumber
46 examination in lieu of the employer-sponsored apprenticeship requirements.
47 C. All other requirements for journeyman licensure, including passage of the
48 journeyman plumber examination administered by the board, remain in full force and
49 effect. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to alter examination content, passing
50 standards, or the board's authority over licensure decisions.
51 D. A person who completes the Institutional Plumbing Pathway is eligible
52 to apply for any license classification established in accordance with this Chapter or
53 administered by the board, including any license classifications established by law
54 or rule on or after the effective date of this Part, if the applicant meets all applicable
55 requirements. The board shall determine eligibility, scope of work, and appropriate
56 level of licensure based on demonstrated competency, training, and experience.
57 E. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to waive or reduce any
58 examination, competency, supervision, or public safety requirements established by
59 either board.

26 §2175.5. Competency checkpoints
27 A. The board shall establish a series of not fewer than three competency
28 checkpoints to be completed by Institutional Plumbing Pathway students at defined
29 stages of the program. Competency checkpoints shall assess practical skills in
30 accordance with standards promulgated by the board.
31 B. Competency checkpoints shall be administered by board-certified
32 assessors and shall be independent of the approved institution. No approved
33 institution employee shall serve as the sole assessor for that institution's own
34 students.
35 C. A student who fails a competency checkpoint may remediate and retake
36 the assessment in accordance with rules promulgated by the board. An approved
37 institution shall provide remediation support for a student who does not pass.
38 D. Successful completion of all required competency checkpoints shall be
39 documented by the board and constitutes official verification of the student's
40 practical competency for purposes of qualification to sit for the journeyman plumber
41 examination.
42 §2175.6. Industry rotation program
43 A. As a component of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway, a student shall
44 complete a minimum of two thousand hours of industry rotation across at least three
45 participating employers, with no single employer providing more than one thousand
46 of the required rotation hours.
47 B. Industry rotation shall expose the student to a range of plumbing
48 applications. An approved institution shall endeavor to ensure that each student
49 completes rotation hours in at least two of the following categories:
50 (1) Residential new construction.
51 (2) Residential service and repair.
52 (3) Commercial construction.
53 (4) Industrial or institutional applications.
54 C. A participating employer shall do all of the following:
55 (1) Register with the board and maintain a current registration in good
56 standing.
57 (2) Assign a licensed journeyman or master plumber to directly supervise
58 each institutional pathway student at all times during industry rotation.

36 or an employer-sponsored apprenticeship.

38 of the following:

40 (2) Administrative licensing requirements and board rules applicable to this
41 state.

43 plumbing practice in this state.
44 C. An applicant who passes the equivalency examination and satisfies all
45 other board requirements, including applicable background checks and fees, shall be

47 applicant's out-of-state license.

50 of the effective date of this Part.

30 §2150. Purpose; legislative intent
31 The purpose of the legislature in enacting this Chapter is the protection of the
32 health, safety, and general welfare of all those persons dealing with persons engaged
33 in the contracting and plumbing vocation vocations, and the affording of such
34 persons of an effective and practical protection against the incompetent,
35 inexperienced, unlawful, and fraudulent acts of contractors or plumbers with whom
36 they contract. Further, the legislative intent is that the State Licensing Board for
37 Contractors shall monitor construction and plumbing projects to ensure compliance
38 with the licensure requirements of this Chapter.

36 §2155.1. Plumbing Contractors Subcommittee; membership; terms; powers; duties
37 A.(1) There is hereby established within the board the Plumbing Contractors
38 Subcommittee, hereinafter referred to as "plumbing subcommittee", consisting of
39 the following eleven members appointed by the governor, each of whom shall be a
40 resident of this state, and each of whom shall have been actively engaged in
41 plumbing or contracting for at least five years prior to his appointment by the
42 governor.
43 (a) Three members, each of whom shall be appointed from the membership
44 of the Plumbing-Heating-Cooling Contractors Association of Louisiana.
45 (b) Three members, each of whom shall be appointed from the membership
46 of The United Association of Journeymen and Apprentices of the Plumbing and
47 Pipefitting Industry of the United States and Canada.
48 (c) One member who shall be appointed from the membership of the
49 Louisiana Associated General Contractors.
50 (d) One member who shall be appointed from the membership of the
51 Associated Builders and Contractors of Louisiana.
52 (e) One member who shall be appointed from the membership of the
53 Louisiana Home Builders Association.
54 (f) Two members, each of whom shall not be associated with the plumbing
55 industry, to serve as members-at-large.
56 (2) Each State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education district shall
57 be represented among the membership provided in this Subsection.
58 B.(1) The terms of office for the initial members appointed to the plumbing
59 subcommittee is three members for a three-year term, four members for a two-year

24 Construction Education or the Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology.

30 contractor an additional fee of one hundred dollars per year to be dedicated and

33 trade. The board shall include on each license renewal form issued to a plumbing
34 contractor an optional election whereby that plumbing contractor may choose to not
35 participate in the allocation of the additional one hundred dollar dedication fee.

37 other monies received by those schools and are intended to supplement and not

11 PART II. LOUISIANA PLUMBING WORKFORCE ACCESS ACT
12 §2175.1. Short title
13 This Part shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Plumbing
14 Workforce Access Act".
15 §2175.2. Definitions
16 As used in this Part, the following terms have the following meanings:
17 (1) "Approved institution" means a community college, vocational-technical
18 school, or correctional facility training program that has received board approval to
19 offer the Institutional Plumbing Pathway program pursuant to this Part.
20 (2) "Board" means the State Licensing Board for Contractors.
21 (3) "Competency checkpoint" means a standardized practical skills
22 assessment administered or certified by the board at defined stages of the
23 institutional pathway.
24 (4) "Industry rotation" means a structured period of supervised hands-on
25 work experience completed by an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student at a
26 participating licensed plumbing contractor's worksite.
27 (5) "Institutional Plumbing Pathway student" means a person enrolled in an
28 approved institution's program established pursuant to this Part.
29 (6) "LCTCS" means the Louisiana Community and Technical College
30 System.
31 (7) "Participating employer" means a licensed plumbing contractor that has
32 registered with the board to accept Institutional Plumbing Pathway students for
33 industry rotation as provided in this Part.
34 (8) "Returning citizen" means a person who has been released from a state
35 or federal correctional facility within the preceding five years.
36 §2175.3. Institutional Plumbing Pathway established
37 A. The board, in coordination with LCTCS, shall establish the Institutional
38 Plumbing Pathway as an alternative route to qualify for the journeyman plumber
39 examination. The Institutional Plumbing Pathway consists of three components:
40 (1) Classroom and laboratory instruction at an approved institution as
41 provided in R.S. 37:2175.5.
42 (2) Competency checkpoints as provided in R.S. 37:2175.6.
43 (3) Industry rotation as provided in R.S. 37:2175.7.
44 B. Completion of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway satisfies the experience
45 and training requirements for application to sit for the journeyman plumber
46 examination in lieu of the employer-sponsored apprenticeship requirements.
47 C. All other requirements for journeyman licensure, including passage of the
48 journeyman plumber examination administered by the board, remain in full force and
49 effect. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to alter examination content, passing
50 standards, or the board's authority over licensure decisions.
51 D. A person who completes the Institutional Plumbing Pathway is eligible
52 to apply for any license classification established in accordance with this Chapter or
53 administered by the board, including any license classifications established by law
54 or rule on or after the effective date of this Part, if the applicant meets all applicable
55 requirements. The board shall determine eligibility, scope of work, and appropriate
56 level of licensure based on demonstrated competency, training, and experience.
57 E. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to waive or reduce any
58 examination, competency, supervision, or public safety requirements established by
59 either board.
