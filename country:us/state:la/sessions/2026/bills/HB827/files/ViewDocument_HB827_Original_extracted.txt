BY REPRESENTATIVE JORDAN
PLUMBERS: Creates the Plumbing Workforce Access Act
1 AN ACT
2 To enact Part II of Chapter 16 of Title 37 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to be
3 comprised of R.S. 37:1381 through 37:1391, relative to plumbing workforce
4 development; to create the Louisiana Plumbing Workforce Access Act; to establish
5 an institution-based pathway to plumbing licensure; to provide for curriculum
6 standards; to authorize competency-based assessment; to create an industry rotation
7 program; to provide for employer incentives; to establish a returning citizen
8 pathway; to provide for reciprocity by examination; to provide for rules and
9 regulations; and to provide for related matters.
10 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
11 Section 1. Part II of Chapter 16 of Title 37 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
12 comprised of R.S. 37:1381 through 37:1391, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
13 PART II. LOUISIANA PLUMBING WORKFORCE ACCESS ACT
14 §1381. Short title
15 This Part shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Plumbing
16 Workforce Access Act".
17 §1382. Legislative findings and purpose
18 A. The legislature finds and declares all of the following:
Page 1 of 11

HB NO. 827
1 (1) A critical shortage of licensed plumbers exists in this state, threatening
2 public health infrastructure, slowing construction and economic development, and
3 increasing costs for families and businesses in this state.
4 (2) The current employer-sponsorship model for plumbing apprenticeship
5 creates systemic inequities, conditioning access to a public credential upon private
6 relationships and disproportionately excluding residents of low-income communities,
7 rural areas, and communities of color.
8 (3) This state's community colleges and vocational-technical schools are
9 well-positioned to provide structured, standardized training that maintains the rigor
10 of plumbing licensure while expanding access to the pathway.
11 (4) This state incarcerates its citizens at one of the highest rates in the nation,
12 and providing returning citizens with viable, licensed trade pathways reduces
13 recidivism and strengthens the workforce.
14 (5) Maintaining rigorous licensing standards while expanding access to the
15 licensing pathway serves the dual public interest of protecting public health and
16 promoting economic mobility.
17 B. The purpose of this Part is to establish an institution-based pathway to
18 plumbing licensure that eliminates the barrier of employer sponsorship, maintains
19 existing examination and competency standards, and expands the licensed plumbing
20 workforce in this state.
21 §1383. Definitions
22 As used in this Part, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
23 (1) "Approved institution" means a community college, vocational-technical
24 school, or correctional facility training program that has received board approval to
25 offer the Institutional Plumbing Pathway program pursuant to this Part.
26 (2) "Board" means the State Plumbing Board established in R.S. 37:1361 et
27 seq.

1 (3) "Competency checkpoint" means a standardized practical skills
2 assessment administered or certified by the board at defined stages of the
3 institutional pathway.
4 (4) "Industry rotation" means a structured period of supervised hands-on
5 work experience completed by an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student at a
6 participating licensed plumbing contractor's worksite.
7 (5) "Institutional Plumbing Pathway student" means a person enrolled in an
8 approved institution's program established pursuant to this Part.
9 (6) "LCTCS" means the Louisiana Community and Technical College
10 System.
11 (7) "Participating employer" means a licensed plumbing contractor
12 that has registered with the Board to accept Institutional Plumbing Pathway students
13 for industry rotation as provided in this Part.
14 (8) "Returning citizen" means a person who has been released from a state
15 or federal correctional facility within the preceding five years.
16 §1384. Institutional Plumbing Pathway established
17 A. The board, in coordination with LCTCS, shall establish the Institutional
18 Plumbing Pathway as an alternative route to qualify for the journeyman plumber
19 examination. The Institutional Plumbing Pathway consists of three components:
20 (1) Classroom and laboratory instruction at an approved institution as
21 provided in R.S. 37:1368.5.
22 (2) Competency checkpoints as provided in R.S. 37:1368.6.
23 (3) Industry rotation as provided in R.S. 37:1368.7.
24 B. Completion of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway satisfies the experience
25 and training requirements for application to sit for the journeyman plumber
26 examination in lieu of the employer-sponsored apprenticeship requirements provided
27 in R.S. 37:1367.

1 C. All other requirements for journeyman licensure, including passage of the
2 journeyman plumber examination administered by the board, remain in full force and
3 effect. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to alter examination content, passing
4 standards, or the board's authority over licensure decisions.
5 §1385. Curriculum and laboratory standards
6 A. The board shall promulgate rules establishing minimum curriculum
7 standards for approved institutions. Such standards shall include, at a minimum, all
8 of the following:
9 (1) The Louisiana Plumbing Code, including all applicable state amendments
10 to the International Plumbing Code.
11 (2) Pipe systems, materials, and installation techniques for residential,
12 commercial, and industrial applications.
13 (3) Blueprint reading and plumbing system design.
14 (4) Workplace safety, including Occupational Safety and Health
15 Administration standards applicable to plumbing work.
16 (5) Applied mathematics for the trades.
17 (6) Water supply, drainage, waste, and venting systems.
18 (7) Medical gas systems and specialty installations, at the board's discretion.
19 B. An approved institution shall maintain laboratory facilities sufficient to
20 allow students to develop hands-on competency in pipe fitting, fixture installation,
21 and system assembly under the supervision of a licensed master plumber or licensed
22 journeyman plumber employed by the institution.
23 C. An institution seeking approval shall submit its proposed curriculum to the
24 board for review. The board shall approve, conditionally approve, or deny the
25 application within ninety days of receipt and shall provide written findings
26 supporting its decision.

1 D. Approved institutions shall be subject to periodic review by the board no
2 less frequently than every three years. The board may revoke or suspend approval
3 for material deficiencies in curriculum, facilities, or instructional quality.
4 §1386. Competency checkpoints
5 A. The board shall establish a series of not fewer than three competency
6 checkpoints to be completed by Institutional Plumbing Pathway students at defined
7 stages of the program. Competency checkpoints shall assess practical skills in
8 accordance with standards promulgated by the board.
9 B. Competency checkpoints shall be administered by board-certified
10 assessors and shall be independent of the approved institution. No approved
11 institution employee shall serve as the sole assessor for its own students.
12 C. A student who fails a competency checkpoint may remediate and retake
13 the assessment in accordance with rules promulgated by the board. An approved
14 institution shall provide remediation support for a student who does not pass.
15 D. Successful completion of all required competency checkpoints shall be
16 documented by the board and constitutes official verification of the student's
17 practical competency for purposes of qualification to sit for the journeyman plumber
18 examination.
19 §1387. Industry rotation program
20 A. As a component of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway, a student shall
21 complete a minimum of two thousand hours of industry rotation across at least three
22 participating employers, with no single employer providing more than one thousand
23 of the required rotation hours.
24 B. Industry rotation shall expose the student to a range of plumbing
25 applications. An approved institution shall endeavor to ensure that each student
26 completes rotation hours in at least two of the following categories:
27 (1) Residential new construction.

1 (2) Residential service and repair.
2 (3) Commercial construction.
3 (4) Industrial or institutional applications.
4 C. A participating employer shall do all of the following:
5 (1) Register with the board and maintain a current registration in good
6 standing.
7 (2) Assign a licensed journeyman or master plumber to directly supervise
8 each institutional pathway student at all times during industry rotation.
9 (3) Submit quarterly hour verification reports to the board in a form
10 prescribed by the board.
11 (4) Certify that the student's work during rotation meets applicable safety and
12 workmanship standards.
13 D. Rotation hours are portable. If a student's rotation with one participating
14 employer is interrupted for any reason, documented hours shall be credited toward
15 the student's total and shall not be forfeited upon transfer to another participating
16 employer.
17 §1388. Returning citizen pathway
18 A. The board shall authorize approved institutions to include correctional
19 facilities operating under the jurisdiction of the Louisiana Department of Public
20 Safety and Corrections as delivery sites for the pre-apprenticeship and classroom
21 components of the Institutional Plumbing Pathway.
22 B. A person incarcerated in a Louisiana correctional facility who completes
23 a board-approved pre-apprenticeship plumbing program while incarcerated shall
24 receive all of the following:
25 (1) Credit toward the classroom and laboratory instruction component of the
26 Institutional Plumbing Pathway upon release.

1 (2) Automatic registration as an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student upon
2 release, subject to any applicable licensing restrictions imposed by the board related
3 to the nature of prior convictions.
4 (3) Eligibility to complete the industry rotation component through
5 participating employers at no additional registration fee for a period of two years
6 following release.
7 C. The board shall not deny licensure to a returning citizen solely on the basis
8 of a prior conviction unless the conviction is directly and substantially related to the
9 duties and responsibilities of a licensed plumber, as determined by the board in
10 accordance with R.S. 37:2950.
11 D. Louisiana Works and the Reentry Advisory Council shall collaborate with
12 the board to develop outreach, support services, and employer recruitment specific
13 to the returning citizen pathway established by this Section.
14 §1389. Louisiana equivalency examination for out-of-state licensees
15 A. A person who holds a current and valid journeyman or master plumber
16 license in good standing in another state may apply for licensure in this state by
17 equivalency examination, in lieu of completing the Institutional Plumbing Pathway
18 or an employer-sponsored apprenticeship.
19 B. The equivalency examination shall test the applicant's knowledge of all
20 of the following:
21 (1) Amendments to the International Plumbing Code specific to this state.
22 (2) Administrative licensing requirements and board rules applicable to this
23 state.
24 (3) Any specific construction or environmental conditions relevant to
25 plumbing practice in this state.
26 C. An applicant who passes the equivalency examination and satisfies all
27 other board requirements, including applicable background checks and fees, shall be

2 applicant's out-of-state license.
3 D. The board shall promulgate rules establishing the content, passing score,
4 and administration of the equivalency examination within one hundred eighty days
5 of the effective date of this Part.
6 §1390. Financial assistance
7 A. Louisiana Works, in coordination with LCTCS and the board, shall
8 develop a financial assistance program for Institutional Plumbing Pathway students
9 who demonstrate financial need. The program may include any of the following:
10 (1) Stipends for students during the industry rotation component.
11 (2) Reimbursement of examination fees for students who pass the
12 journeyman or master plumber examination on the first attempt.
13 (3) Low-interest loans for tools, equipment, and program-related expenses.
14 B. A student who receives financial assistance under this Section and
15 becomes licensed in Louisiana shall commit to working as a licensed plumber in this
16 state for not fewer than two years following licensure as a condition of assistance.
17 Louisiana Works shall promulgate rules governing repayment obligations for a
18 student who does not satisfy this commitment.
19 C. Financial assistance under this Section shall be funded through
20 appropriations made by the legislature. Louisiana Works shall report annually to the
21 legislature on the number of students assisted, pass rates, and licensure outcomes.
22 §1391. Rulemaking; implementation
23 A. The board shall promulgate all rules necessary to implement this Part in
24 accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act, R.S. 49:950 et seq. Initial rules
25 shall be promulgated within one hundred eighty days of the effective date of this
26 Part.

Abstract: Creates the Plumbing Workforce Access Act, providing an alternative
educational pathway to plumbing licensure in partnership with LCTCS.
Proposed law shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Plumbing Workforce
Access Act".
Proposed law provides legislative findings and purpose.
Proposed law defines "approved institution", "board", "competency checkpoint", industry
rotation", "Institutional Plumbing Pathway student", "LCTCS", "participating employer",
and "returning citizen".
Proposed law provides the Institutional Plumbing Pathway as an alternative route to qualify
for the journeyman plumber examination and provides components.
Proposed law provides for rulemaking authority with respect to minimum curriculum
standards and provides for laboratory standards and periodic review by the State Plumbing
Board.
Proposed law provides for required competency checkpoints which shall assess practical
skills in accordance with standards promulgated by the board.
Proposed law requires a student to complete a minimum of 2,000 hours of industry rotation
covering a range of plumbing applications across at least three participating employers, with
no single employer providing more than 1,000 of the required rotation hours.
Proposed law provides that a participating employer shall do all of the following:
(1) Register with the board and maintain a current registration in good standing.
(2) Assign a licensed journeyman or master plumber to directly supervise each
institutional pathway student at all times during industry rotation.
(3) Submit quarterly hour verification reports to the board in a form prescribed by the
board.
(4) Certify that the student's work during rotation meets applicable safety and
workmanship standards.
Proposed law provides for program participation by correctional facilities, and a person
incarcerated in a correctional facility in this state who completes a board-approved
pre-apprenticeship plumbing program while incarcerated shall receive all of the following:
(1) Credit toward the classroom and laboratory instruction component of the
Institutional Plumbing Pathway upon release.
(2) Automatic registration as an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student upon release,
subject to any applicable licensing restrictions imposed by the board related to the
nature of prior convictions.
(3) Eligibility to complete the industry rotation component through participating
employers at no additional registration fee for a period of two years following
release.
Proposed law provides that Louisiana Works and the Reentry Advisory Council shall
collaborate with the board to develop the correctional program in accordance with proposed
law.

DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HB 827 Original 2026 Regular Session Jordan
Abstract: Creates the Plumbing Workforce Access Act, providing an alternative
educational pathway to plumbing licensure in partnership with LCTCS.
Proposed law shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Plumbing Workforce
Access Act".
Proposed law provides legislative findings and purpose.
Proposed law defines "approved institution", "board", "competency checkpoint", industry
rotation", "Institutional Plumbing Pathway student", "LCTCS", "participating employer",
and "returning citizen".
Proposed law provides the Institutional Plumbing Pathway as an alternative route to qualify
for the journeyman plumber examination and provides components.
Proposed law provides for rulemaking authority with respect to minimum curriculum
standards and provides for laboratory standards and periodic review by the State Plumbing
Board.
Proposed law provides for required competency checkpoints which shall assess practical
skills in accordance with standards promulgated by the board.
Proposed law requires a student to complete a minimum of 2,000 hours of industry rotation
covering a range of plumbing applications across at least three participating employers, with
no single employer providing more than 1,000 of the required rotation hours.
Proposed law provides that a participating employer shall do all of the following:
(1) Register with the board and maintain a current registration in good standing.
(2) Assign a licensed journeyman or master plumber to directly supervise each
institutional pathway student at all times during industry rotation.
(3) Submit quarterly hour verification reports to the board in a form prescribed by the
board.
(4) Certify that the student's work during rotation meets applicable safety and
workmanship standards.
Proposed law provides for program participation by correctional facilities, and a person
incarcerated in a correctional facility in this state who completes a board-approved
pre-apprenticeship plumbing program while incarcerated shall receive all of the following:
(1) Credit toward the classroom and laboratory instruction component of the
Institutional Plumbing Pathway upon release.
(2) Automatic registration as an Institutional Plumbing Pathway student upon release,
subject to any applicable licensing restrictions imposed by the board related to the
nature of prior convictions.
(3) Eligibility to complete the industry rotation component through participating
employers at no additional registration fee for a period of two years following
release.
Proposed law provides that Louisiana Works and the Reentry Advisory Council shall
collaborate with the board to develop the correctional program in accordance with proposed
law.
