HLS 26RS-5583 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 302
BY REPRESENTATIVE WYBLE
TECHNOLOGY: Requests the Legislative Youth Advisory Council to discuss artificial
intelligence issues that may affect young people in Louisiana
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Legislative Youth Advisory Council to discuss artificial intelligence
3 issues that may affect young people in Louisiana.
4 WHEREAS, R.S. 24:973.1 establishes the Legislative Youth Advisory Council to
5 examine and facilitate communication between youth and the legislature regarding issues
6 of importance to youth; and
7 WHEREAS, the law provides that the council has thirty-one members who are high
8 school students appointed by the Commission on Civic Education; and
9 WHEREAS, one of the council's duties as provided by law is to identify the concerns
10 and needs of youth and to advise and make recommendations to members of the legislature
11 on proposed or pending legislation and on policy matters related to youth; and
12 WHEREAS, several pieces of legislation were introduced during the 2026 Regular
13 Session of the Legislature that address artificial intelligence, and much of the discussion has
14 centered around the safety of young people; and
15 WHEREAS, it is anticipated that legislation pertaining to artificial intelligence will
16 be introduced in future legislative sessions; and
17 WHEREAS, protecting the safety and well-being of young people in Louisiana is of
18 the utmost importance; and
Page 1 of 2

1 WHEREAS, legislators value the contributions and insights of the members of the
2 Legislative Youth Advisory Council and would benefit from the council's discussion of
3 artificial intelligence issues affecting young people.
4 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
5 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Legislative Youth Advisory
6 Council to discuss artificial intelligence issues that may affect young people in Louisiana.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the discussion may include topics such as the
8 education of students, parents and guardians, and teachers and other youth-serving
9 professionals regarding artificial intelligence as it pertains to matters facing Louisiana's
10 young people.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the council may seek input from individuals with
12 knowledge of artificial intelligence impacting the lives of young people in Louisiana,
13 including but not limited to representatives of the office of the attorney general, law
14 enforcement, the state Department of Education, and the Louisiana Center for Safe Schools.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the council may provide a summary of its
16 discussion of artificial intelligence to the Commission on Civic Education and the
17 legislature.
