2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 302
BY REPRESENTATIVES WYBLE AND CHASSION
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Legislative Youth Advisory Council to discuss artificial intelligence
issues that may affect young people in Louisiana.
WHEREAS, R.S. 24:973.1 establishes the Legislative Youth Advisory Council to
examine and facilitate communication between youth and the legislature regarding issues
of importance to youth; and
WHEREAS, the law provides that the council has thirty-one members who are high
school students appointed by the Commission on Civic Education; and
WHEREAS, one of the council's duties as provided by law is to identify the concerns
and needs of youth and to advise and make recommendations to members of the legislature
on proposed or pending legislation and on policy matters related to youth; and
WHEREAS, several pieces of legislation were introduced during the 2026 Regular
Session of the Legislature that address artificial intelligence, and much of the discussion has
centered around the safety of young people; and
WHEREAS, it is anticipated that legislation pertaining to artificial intelligence will
be introduced in future legislative sessions; and
WHEREAS, protecting the safety and well-being of young people in Louisiana is of
the utmost importance; and
WHEREAS, legislators value the contributions and insights of the members of the
Legislative Youth Advisory Council and would benefit from the council's discussion of
artificial intelligence issues affecting young people.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Legislative Youth Advisory
Council to discuss artificial intelligence issues that may affect young people in Louisiana.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the discussion may include topics such as the
education of students, parents and guardians, and teachers and other youth-serving
professionals regarding artificial intelligence as it pertains to matters facing Louisiana's
young people.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the council may seek input from individuals with
knowledge of artificial intelligence impacting the lives of young people in Louisiana,
including but not limited to representatives of the office of the attorney general, law
enforcement, the state Department of Education, and the Louisiana Center for Safe Schools.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the council may provide a summary of its
discussion of artificial intelligence to the Commission on Civic Education and the
legislature.
