Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ACT No. 746 ENROLLED
BY SENATOR MORRIS
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 13:312.1(D), relative to judges on the court of appeal; to provide
3 for the number of judges on the Fourth Circuit Court of Appeal; to reduce the
4 number of judges serving on the fourth circuit; to provide for implementation of the
5 reduced number of judges; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 13:312.1(D) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §312.1. Circuit court of appeal; domicile; number of judges; election
10 * * *
11 D. The Court of Appeal for the Fourth Circuit, domiciled in the city of New
12 Orleans, shall have twelve ten judges. Eight Seven judges shall be elected from the
13 first district of the fourth circuit by the qualified electors thereof. One judge shall be
14 elected from the second district by the qualified electors thereof. One judge shall be
15 elected from the third district by the qualified electors thereof. Two judges One
16 judge shall be elected from the circuit at large by the qualified electors thereof.
17 * * *
18 Section 2. No provision of this Act shall deprive any judge in office of the ability to
19 serve the entire remainder of his or her term. To effectuate the provisions of this Act, no
20 further elections shall be held for Division G of the first district of the fourth circuit or for
21 the at large seat currently held by Chief Judge Roland Belsome with a term expiring on
22 December 31, 2031, whether at the end of the current term or due to a vacancy caused by the
23 death, resignation, retirement, or incapacity of the judge.

1 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
