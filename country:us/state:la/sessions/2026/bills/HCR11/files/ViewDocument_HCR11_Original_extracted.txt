HLS 26RS-133 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 11
BY REPRESENTATIVE MIKE JOHNSON
MILITARY AFFAIRS: Expresses support for the continued operations of the Louisiana
National Guard at Camp Beauregard in Pineville and commends the
accomplishments of the Louisiana National Guard Youth Challenge Program
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To express support for the continued operations of the Louisiana National Guard at Camp
3 Beauregard and to commend the accomplishments of the Louisiana National Guard
4 Youth Challenge Program.
5 WHEREAS, Camp Beauregard is located in Pineville, Louisiana, and has a long
6 history of supporting the training and mobilization of the United States military that dates
7 back to World War I and continues through present-day; the installation serves as the
8 primary annual training and pre-mobilization training platform for the Louisiana National
9 Guard; and
10 WHEREAS, the mission of Camp Beauregard is to support federal and state military
11 and emergency operations; and
12 WHEREAS, Camp Beauregard provides quality training and training support
13 resources and facilities for pre-mobilization and post-mobilization; the camp also serves as
14 both a staging base and logistical staging area for various emergency response agencies
15 during times of state and federal emergencies; and
16 WHEREAS, Camp Beauregard hosts one of the three Louisiana National Guard
17 Youth Challenge Programs, which works to instill values, life skills, education, and self-
18 discipline in Louisiana's youth; the program is an alternative educational program that offers
19 youth, from ages sixteen to eighteen, the opportunity to change their future, and it is geared

1 towards students seeking a way to succeed outside of a traditional school setting while
2 working to obtain a high school equivalency diploma; and
3 WHEREAS, the Louisiana National Guard Youth Challenge Program encourages
4 teamwork, personal growth, and development through eight core components: life coping
5 skills, academic excellence, job skills, responsible citizenship, leadership/followership,
6 health and hygiene, service to the community, and physical fitness; and
7 WHEREAS, the Youth Challenge Program is a seventeen-month program consisting
8 of two phases: a five-month residential phase and a twelve-month post-residential phase;
9 during the residential phase, students live on-site at one of three locations: Camp Minden
10 near Shreveport, Camp Beauregard Training Center, and The Gillis W. Long Center near
11 Baton Rouge; during phase one, students attend school and receive counseling and twenty-
12 four-hour supervision; and
13 WHEREAS, after graduation, students return home to begin a twelve-month post-
14 residential phase; this phase is designed to help students continue in a positive direction, and
15 graduates are assisted by Youth Challenge Program case managers and community mentors
16 to continue their education, enroll in college, begin job training, find employment, or enlist
17 in the military; and
18 WHEREAS, the Louisiana National Guard at Camp Beauregard is most deserving
19 of the highest recognition for its longstanding efforts to support the United States military
20 and Louisiana's youth.
21 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
22 express support for the continued operations of the Louisiana National Guard at Camp
23 Beauregard in Pineville, Louisiana, and does hereby commend the accomplishments of the
24 Louisiana National Guard Youth Challenge.
