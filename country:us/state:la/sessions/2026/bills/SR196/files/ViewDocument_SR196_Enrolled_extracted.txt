2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 196
BY SENATOR BARROW
A RESOLUTION
To create and provide for the Task Force on Blight to study and make recommendations on
implementing a comprehensive plan to address property blight within the state.
WHEREAS, dilapidated properties and overgrown lots, otherwise known as blight,
have long been among the parishes' and municipalities' most vexing challenges; and
WHEREAS, over the last forty years the legislature has sought ways to address blight
across the state by enacting several statutes; and
WHEREAS, in the 1980s, the legislature sought to address blight by enacting Act
571 of the 1984 Regular Session which is referred to as the Urban Land-Banking Law; and
WHEREAS, under the Urban Land-Banking Law the legislature's findings and
declaration state in part that "[t]here exist in many cities of the state economically and
physically depressed residential neighborhood areas which, among other things, suffer from
high unemployment, substandard and deteriorated structures, vacant and partially used lots,
overcrowded housing, high crime rates, and other blighting influences and undesirable
conditions"; and
WHEREAS, in the 1990s, the legislature enacted the Slum Clearance Law as Act
1188 of the 1997 Regular Session authorizing municipalities to tackle slum conditions in a
just and legal way; and
WHEREAS, a portion of the Slum Clearance Law states "[w]henever any
municipality finds that there exists within it dwellings which are unfit for human habitation
due to dilapidation; defects increasing the hazards of fires, accidents, or other calamities;
lack of ventilation, light, or sanitary facilities, or other conditions rendering the dwellings

unsafe and unsanitary and dangerous or detrimental to health, safety, or morals, or that there
are dwellings therein which are otherwise inimical to the welfare of its residents, it may
exercise its police powers to remedy or eliminate those conditions in the manner provided
in this Part"; and
WHEREAS, in the 2000s, the legislature enacted the Louisiana Housing Preservation
Act as Act 355 of the 2006 Regular Session to address blighted and abandoned residential
housing properties by authorizing the creation of a "blighted property list" which is prepared
and periodically updated by a designated public official that identifies blighted housing
properties in the jurisdiction of the local governmental subdivision; and
WHEREAS, the legislature seeks to address property blight by studying and
reviewing comprehensive plans to eradicate blight in Louisiana.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby establish and provide for the Task Force on Blight to study implementing a
comprehensive plan to address property blight within the state, and to propose
recommendations, together with specific proposals for legislation, by written report to the
legislature and to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S.
24:771 and 772, no later than March 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force should include consideration of
the following:
(1) A strategic plan to abate blight within the state.
(2) Use of data-driven decision-making.
(3) Strategic deployment of resources and place-based revitalization.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
members:
(1) The president of the Senate or his designee, who shall serve as the chairman of
the task force.
(2) The speaker of the House of Representatives, or his designee.
(3) The chairman of the Senate Committee on Local and Municipal Affairs, or his
designee.
(4) The chairman of the House Committee on Municipal, Parochial and Cultural

Affairs, or his designee.
(5) The mayor of the city of New Orleans, or her designee.
(6) The mayor-president of East Baton Rouge Parish, or his designee.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each designating authority shall submit the
names of designees to the task force to the secretary of the Senate no later than August 1,
2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the task force shall convene for its first meeting no
later than September 15, 2026, at the call of the chairman, and at the first meeting, the
members shall elect a vice chairman and other officers as the task force may deem
appropriate.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall be staffed by the Senate
Committee on Local and Municipal Affairs.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force shall constitute a
quorum for the transaction of business. All official actions of the task force shall require the
affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that meetings of the task force shall be subject to the
Open Meetings Law, R.S. 42:11 et seq., and records of the task force shall be subject to the
Public Records Law, R.S. 44:1 et seq.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be
individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate on the date of the
submission of its report or June 1, 2027, whichever occurs first.
