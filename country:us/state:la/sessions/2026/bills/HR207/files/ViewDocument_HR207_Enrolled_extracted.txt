HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 207
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
A RESOLUTION
study to evaluate the benefits and feasibility of adding turn lanes onto Louisiana
Highway 1024.
WHEREAS, according to the Louisiana Highway Safety Commission, turns at
intersections can cause delays and decrease safety; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Highway Safety Commission recommends installation
of dedicated left-turn and right-turn lanes, deceleration and acceleration lanes, and space to
handle turning traffic away from the through traffic will improve delays and increase safety
and efficiency; and
WHEREAS, installing turn lanes can significantly improve intersections; and
WHEREAS, common reasons for installing turn lanes are:
(1) Rates of speeds are too high to turn safely onto or away from a roadway.
(2) Patterns of rear-end, sideswipe, or weaving crashes when through vehicles
conflict with turning vehicles.
(3) Drivers have extended wait times to make a turn.
(4) A higher number of turning vehicles.
WHEREAS, improper turning and a lack of dedicated turn lanes cause a significant
portion of United States traffic accidents, with roughly 1.7 million crashes annually; and
WHEREAS, intersections without dedicated left turn lanes are highly dangerous and
installing them can reduce total intersection accidents by approximately twenty to seventy-
six percent, depending on the roadway.

HR NO. 207 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Transportation and
Development to conduct a traffic study to evaluate the benefits and feasibility of adding turn
lanes onto Louisiana Highway 1024.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Transportation and
Development shall submit a written report detailing its findings for the benefits and
feasibility of adding turn lanes onto Louisiana Highway 1024 to the House Committee on
Transportation, Highways and Public Works prior to January 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
