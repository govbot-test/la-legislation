1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 46:450.5.1, relative to kidney disease; to provide for legislative intent; to
3 provide for requirements for the Louisiana Department of Health; to provide for
4 annual reviews; to provide for reporting; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 46:450.5.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §450.5.1. Chronic and rare kidney diseases; coverage; annual review
8 A. The legislature hereby finds all of the following:
9 (1) More than thirty-five million Americans currently suffer from kidney
10 disease and millions more may not be aware that they are at risk for developing
11 kidney disease in the future.
12 (2) There is a new era emerging of clinical innovation in kidney disease
13 treatment, with the first-ever treatments approved by the United States Food
14 and Drug Administration to slow the decline of kidney function in patients with
15 chronic and rare kidney diseases.
16 (3) Although the main causes of kidney disease are diabetes and high
17 blood pressure, kidney disease also has genetic and hereditary causes, including
18 rare diseases that cause glomerulonephritis.
19 (4) According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, as
20 many as nine out of ten Americans with chronic kidney disease are unaware
21 they have it.
22 (5) If kidney disease is detected early through blood, urine, or other tests,
23 it may be possible to slow its progression and delay the need for dialysis or
24 transplant through lifestyle changes and pharmacological treatments.
25 (6) Dialysis and later stages of kidney disease are costly to the state and
26 its healthcare system and often result in a patient leaving the workforce.

1 B. The legislature further finds that, considering the detrimental health
2 effects of kidney disease in this state and the United States, its enormous impact
3 on the lives of patients and their caregivers, and the avoidable costs to the state,
4 its workforce, and the healthcare system of undetected or undertreated kidney
5 disease, it is in the interest of the state to encourage the prevention, early
6 screening, diagnosis, treatment, disparity of impact, and overall awareness and
7 understanding of kidney disease in Louisiana.
8 C.(1) The Louisiana Department of Health shall conduct an annual
9 review of all medications and forms of treatment for chronic and rare kidney
10 diseases and services for enrollees with a diagnosis of chronic or rare kidney
11 disease that are eligible for coverage under Medicaid. The purpose of the annual
12 review is to determine if the available covered medications, treatments, and
13 services are adequate to meet the needs of enrollees with a diagnosis of chronic
14 or rare kidney disease, and whether the department should seek to add
15 additional medications, treatments, or services.
16 (2) When conducting the annual review required by this Subsection, the
17 department shall solicit and consider input from the general public, with
18 specific emphasis on attempting to receive input from persons or groups with
19 knowledge and experience in the area of chronic and rare kidney diseases.
20 D. The department shall examine and pursue strategic partnerships with
21 state-based entities who represent or provide services to individuals living with
22 chronic and rare kidney diseases for the purpose of increasing education,
23 awareness, and diagnostic screening for chronic and rare kidney diseases,
24 through new or existing initiatives including but not limited to Well-Ahead
25 Louisiana.
26 E. On or before July 1, 2027, and on or before July first each year
27 thereafter, the department shall submit a report to the president of the Senate,
28 the speaker of the House of Representatives, the Senate Committee on Health
29 and Welfare, the House Committee on Health and Welfare, and the David R.
30 Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772. The
