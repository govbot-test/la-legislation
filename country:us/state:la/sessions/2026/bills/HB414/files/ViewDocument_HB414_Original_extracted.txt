HLS 26RS-691 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE CHENEVERT
HEALTH: Provides for prohibitions on hiring individuals with certain criminal convictions
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 40:1203.3(A)(1) and (C), to enact R.S. 40:1203.3(A)(5), and to
3 repeal R.S. 40:1203.3(B), relative to unlicensed healthcare workers and licensed
4 ambulance personnel; provides for hiring certain healthcare staff; prohibits hiring
5 individuals with certain criminal convictions; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 40:1203.3(A)(1) and (C) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
8 40:1203.3(A)(5) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §1203.3. Refusal to hire or contract; termination of employment; exceptions to
10 hiring prohibition; appeal procedure; waiver
11 A. Except as otherwise provided in R.S. 40:1203.2(C) and Subsection E of
12 this Section, no employer shall hire any licensed ambulance personnel or nonlicensed
13 person when the results of a criminal history check reveal that the licensed
14 ambulance personnel or nonlicensed person has been convicted of any of the
15 following offenses:
16 (1) R.S. 14:28.1, 30, 30.1, 31, 32.6, 32.7, 32.12, 34, 34.1, 34.7, 35.2, 37,
17 37.1, 37.4, 38.1, 42, 42.1, 43, 43.1, 43.2, 43.3, 43.5, 44, 44.1, 44.2, 46.2, 51, 60, 64,
18 64.1, 64.4, 66, 67.16, 80, 81.2, 89, 89.1, 93, 93.3, 93.4, 93.5, 283.3 or distribution or
19 possession with the intent to distribute controlled dangerous substances as listed in
20 Schedules I through V of the Uniform Controlled Dangerous Substances Act.
21 * * *

HB 414 Original 2026 Regular Session Chenevert
Abstract: Prohibits healthcare providers from hiring certain individuals with certain
criminal convictions.
Present law prohibits healthcare providers from hiring unlicensed healthcare workers and
licensed ambulance personnel convicted of certain criminal offenses provided for in present
law.
Present law further prohibits healthcare providers from hiring unlicensed healthcare workers
and licensed ambulance personnel convicted of criminal offenses provided by present law
if the healthcare provider provides care or services to any person under the age of 21.
Proposed law combines present law providing that a healthcare provider shall refuse to hire
an unlicensed healthcare worker or licensed ambulance personnel if he is convicted of an
offense listed in present law.
Proposed law extends the hiring prohibitions of present law to an unlicensed healthcare
worker or licensed ambulance personnel convicted of a federal offense or an offense in
another state that would satisfy the elements of an offense listed in present law.
Proposed law adds to the list of offenses that prohibit the hiring of an unlicensed healthcare
worker or licensed ambulance personnel:
(1) (R.S. 14:44.2) Aggravated kidnapping of a child.
(2) (R.S. 14:80) Felony carnal knowledge of a juvenile.
(3) (R.S. 14:81.2) Molestation of a juvenile or a person with a physical or mental
disability.
