9 "§1360.123. The Louisiana Naturopathic Advisory Council"
10 A. The Louisiana Naturopathic Advisory Council is created to assist the
11 Louisiana State Board of Medical Examiners in the administration and oversight of
12 the practice of naturopathic medicine.
13 B. The council consists of the following members:
14 (1) Three members who are licensed naturopathic physicians.
15 (2) One member who is a consumer.
16 C.(1) The members of the council shall be selected by the board.
17 (2) Each council member who is a naturopathic physician shall meet all of
18 the following criteria:
19 (a) Be licensed in this state as a naturopathic physician.
20 (b) Actively practice naturopathic medicine in this state.
21 (c) Be certified by the North American Board of Naturopathic Examiners.
22 (d) Have a minimum of two years of clinical experience in the practice of
23 naturopathic medicine.
24 (3) The consumer member shall represent consumer interests through
25 experience in public service, community advocacy, law, and ethics.
26 D.(1) A majority of the members of the council shall constitute a quorum.
27 (2) All members of the council shall serve a term of three years.
28 (3) The council shall elect a chairperson by a majority of the vote.
29 (4). The council shall meet, at a minimum, twice annually, or as called by
30 the board.
31 (5) The council shall maintain records of the content of its meetings and
32 submit a copy of the such records to the board.
33 E. The council has all of the following duties:
34 (1) Advise the board on licensure requirements and professional standards
35 for naturopathic physicians.
36 (2) Recommend rules and regulations governing the practice of naturopathic
37 medicine.
38 (3) Provide guidance to the board regarding prescribing authority as defined
39 in this Part.
40 (4) Advise on ethics, examinations, continuing education, and professional
41 conduct.
42 (5) Review licensure applications and make recommendations to the board.
43 (6) Review prescribing practices.
44 (7) Provide recommendations for updates or clarifications on law provided
45 for in this Part.
46 F. The council shall make recommendations only. The board retains full and
47 final authority over licensure, discipline, rulemaking, and approval of prescriptive
48 authority."

25 §1360.131. Prescriptive endorsement
26 A. In order to obtain the special license endorsement which shall authorize
27 a naturopathic physician to prescribe, dispense, and administer prescription
28 medicines, an otherwise qualified naturopathic physician will be required to pass the
29 naturopathic pharmacology examination or a substantially equivalent examination
30 approved by the board.
31 B. For no less than one year after receiving the special license endorsement,
32 prescriptions shall be reviewed by an objective and independent supervising
33 physician licensed, or a naturopathic physician licensed. The supervising physician
34 shall possess an unencumbered license and have been prescribing and administering
35 prescription drugs without limitation for five years or more. The supervising
36 physician shall evaluate the naturopathic physician’s ability to do all of the
37 following:
38 (1) Safely prescribe and administer prescription drugs within the
39 naturopath’s scope of practice.
40 (2) Comply with federal and state law.
41 (3) Comply with the applicable administrative rules of the Louisiana Board
42 of Pharmacy.
43 C. The naturopathic and supervising physicians shall have a formal written
44 agreement. The agreement shall address the requirements of Subsection B of this
45 Section. The agreement shall be available for inspection upon request by the board.
46 D. A naturopathic physician who satisfies the supervision requirement in
47 Subsection B of this Section shall notify the board that this requirement has been met
48 and file a certificate of completion signed by the supervisor.
49 E. The executive director of the board may waive the prescription review
50 requirement in Subsection B of this Section if the newly endorsed naturopathic
51 physician can show that he has experience in prescribing prescription medicines
52 under the laws of another jurisdiction that has standards and qualifications for a
53 naturopathic physician to prescribe prescription medications at least equal to those
