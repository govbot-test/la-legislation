DIGEST
SB 150 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Boudreaux
Present law establishes a central vital records registry within the office of public health and
a state registrar of vital records to provide for the collection, maintenance, issuance, and
preservation of all vital records covering births, deaths, marriages, divorce judgments,
adoptions, and change of names.
Proposed law authorizes the vital records registry and state registrar to scan original
documents submitted in support of vital records applications and in substantiation of
information contained in the vital records registry, electronically store the scanned original
documents, and return the original documents to the submitter.
Proposed law provides that scanned and electronically stored originals have the same
authenticity as the paper originals.
Proposed law authorizes the vital records registry and state registrar to issue certified copies
of documents in their custody in paper or electronic form, including but not limited to those
that are evidentiary in nature.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 40:42.1)
