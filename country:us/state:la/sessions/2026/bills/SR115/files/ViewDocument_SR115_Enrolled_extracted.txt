2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 115
BY SENATOR DUPLESSIS
A RESOLUTION
State Capitol.
WHEREAS, early childhood educators make up the workforce that supports the
employment of tens of thousands of working Louisiana parents; and
WHEREAS, early childhood education, from birth to kindergarten, is significantly
underfunded causing educators to leave the field to earn livable wages, resulting in many
families unable to access necessary care; and
WHEREAS, disruption in child care leaves families, providers, and businesses
vulnerable and costs Louisiana's economy one billion three hundred million dollars each
year; and
WHEREAS, For Providers By Providers (4PXP), Louisiana's only provider-led early
care in education (ECE) organization, is leading the statewide coordination of the National
Day Without Child Care to elevate the voices of educators, families, and providers; and
WHEREAS, for generations, early childhood educators, parents, families, and
caregivers have been fighting for equitable access to affordable child care, better pay, and
working conditions for providers; and
WHEREAS, less than half of child care providers offer staff paid sick days, and
fewer than one-third provide health insurance or retirement benefits, highlighting the
ongoing challenges in ensuring a stable and supported early childhood workforce; and
WHEREAS, among child care providers who lost staff in 2024, nearly half reported
that departing employees left for jobs offering higher wages, with lower-paying providers
experiencing staff turnover at higher rates than those able to offer more competitive
compensation; and
WHEREAS, most child care providers report increased staffing costs or challenges,

including the ability to pay higher wages and hire experienced staff and experience high
turnover as new staff leave within six months of employment; and
WHEREAS, early childhood education is essential to a functioning economy and
early childhood educators are among the lowest-paid professionals in the education sector,
earning an average of $10.63 an hour in Louisiana despite the critical role they play during
a child's formative years in supporting the child's development and learning; and
WHEREAS, many early childhood educators earn wages so low that they qualify for
public assistance programs, highlighting the disconnect between the value of their work and
the compensation they receive; and
WHEREAS, studies have shown that inadequate pay contributes to higher turnover
rates among early childhood educators, disrupting the stability and quality of care that young
children need to thrive; and
WHEREAS, low wages often make it difficult to recruit and retain skilled, passionate
professionals in the early childhood education workforce, placing additional strain on child
care providers and families; and
WHEREAS, the lack of accessible, high quality early childhood education hinders
Louisiana's capacity to attract and keep young professionals, resulting in a loss of talent,
particularly in vital sectors like healthcare and education; and
WHEREAS, on Tuesday, May 12, 2026, child care providers, educators, families,
and community members across Louisiana are joining the National Day Without Child Care
to raise awareness about the urgent need for investment in early childhood education and to
advocate for a fully funded child care system that powers the economy and supports working
families; and
WHEREAS, investing in early childhood education is critical to supporting
Louisiana's working families, strengthening businesses, and growing the state's economy,
ensuring every child has a strong start in life, which would build a more prosperous future
for all communities.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby designate Tuesday, May 12, 2026, as A Day Without Child Care Day at the
Louisiana State Capitol.
