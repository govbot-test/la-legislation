ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES: Provides for the standards of shipping containers for wines
and distilled spirits
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 26:351, relative to wines, distilled spirits, and powdered alcohol;
3 to provide for approved containers for wines and distilled spirits; to provide for case
4 configuration for wines and distilled spirits; to provide for application of federal
5 regulations; to provide for exceptions; to prohibit the sale or shipment of powdered
6 alcohol; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 26:351 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §351. Limitation on size of containers of beverages of high alcoholic content;
10 standards of fill
11 Except for wines, no manufacturer or wholesaler in this state shall have in his
12 possession any beverages of high alcoholic content outside of his bonded stockroom
13 unless they are in containers of no greater capacity than one and seventy-five one
14 hundredths liters. The standards of fill shall be the following:
15 (1)(a) All distilled spirits sold in or shipped into this state shall be in the
16 following containers: One and seventy-five hundredths liters, one liter, seven
17 hundred fifty milliliters, seven hundred milliliters, five hundred milliliters, three
18 hundred seventy-five milliliters, two hundred milliliters, and fifty milliliters.
19 (b) If any supplier ships a particular brand of a distilled spirit into this state
20 in a three hundred seventy-five milliliter container, he shall thereafter be prohibited

28 Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau is authorized for sale in this state.

1 (3) No additional limitation shall be imposed by the state on container size
2 or case configuration except as expressly provided in this Section.
3 B. (1) The state shall not prescribe the number of containers per shipping case
4 or shipping container for distilled spirits or wine, provided such container sizes
5 comply with federal law.

7 Procedure Act necessary for tax administration, public safety, or enforcement
8 purposes, provided such rules do not conflict with federally authorized standards of

12 otherwise authorized by federal standards of fill, provided that all of the following

14 (a) The container is not smaller than the equivalent of one hundred
15 eighty-seven milliliters.
16 (b) Written proof of the bottling date is furnished to the commissioner prior

19 (2) Nothing in this Subsection shall permit the sale of vintage wine in a
20 container smaller than one hundred eighty-seven milliliters.
21 D.(1) The container size restrictions prescribed in this Section do not apply
22 to alcohol beverages labeled and sold as "sake".
23 (2) Sake may be sold in any container size authorized for importation and
24 sale under federal law.
25 E. The container size restrictions prescribed in this Section do not apply to
26 cider products.
27 F. No alcoholic beverage sold in or shipped into this state shall contain
28 powdered alcohol.

HB 805 Original 2026 Regular Session Orgeron
Abstract: Provides for the standards for shipping containers for wine and distilled spirits.
Present law provides restrictions of containers, container sizes, and the number of containers
per shipping case for distilled spirits and wines sold in or shipped into the state of La.
Proposed law provides that regulations related to wines and distilled spirits relative to their
standards of fill and container sizes are governed by the U.S. Dept. of the Treasury, Alcohol
and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau, as set forth in Title 27 of the Code of Federal
Regulations, as amended.
Proposed law provides that the state cannot prescribe the number of containers per shipping
case or shipping container for wines and distilled spirits. Further allows the commissioner
to promulgate rules in accordance with the APA necessary for tax administration, public
safety, or enforcement purposes.
Present law provides limitations on the container sizes and shipping of vintage wines.
Defines "vintage wines" as wines bottled prior to December 31, 1978.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that the restrictions do not apply to sake or cider products.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law prohibits the sale or shipment of powdered alcohol in the state of La.
Proposed law retains present law.
(Amends R.S. 351)
