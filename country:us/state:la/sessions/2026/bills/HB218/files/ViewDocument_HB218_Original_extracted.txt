16 screening tool shall be incorporated into assessment protocols when a student visits
17 a school-based health clinic.
18 C. The program shall provide for standardized procedures in the event of
19 screening results that indicate that a student is at risk for food insecurity, including
20 but not limited to family outreach and referral to community resources such as

HB 218 Original 2026 Regular Session LaFleur
Abstract: Requires the administration of screenings for food insecurity to public school
students.
Proposed law:
(1) Requires school boards to establish a program for screening students for food
insecurity at schools and school-based health clinics, including the administration of
a screening tool to students in grades kindergarten through 12 when a student first
enrolls in a school and once during each semester of enrollment thereafter. Requires
incorporation of the screening tool into assessments at student visits to school-based
health clinics.
(2) Requires that program to provide for standardized procedures in the event of
screening results that indicate that a student is at risk for food insecurity, including
but not limited to family outreach and referral to community resources.
(3) Requires that results remain confidential except for disclosure to a student's parent
or legal guardian or to the student if he has reached the age of majority or is an
emancipated minor and to authorized school personnel responsible for the
implementation of procedures for such referral.
(4) Requires that the state Dept. of Education, in consultation with the La. Dept. of
Health, select a food insecurity screening tool that is available at no cost to the state
or to school boards and age-appropriate for students and provide information about
how to access this tool to each school board.
(5) Prohibits school employees from using the results of the screening to make any
decision relating to student instruction, academic opportunities, or discipline.
Proposed law is applicable to all public schools, including charter schools.
(Adds R.S. 17:2113 and 3996(B)(24))
