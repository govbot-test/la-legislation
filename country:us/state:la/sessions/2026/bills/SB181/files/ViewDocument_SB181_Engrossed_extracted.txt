DIGEST
SB 181 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Foil
Present law provides for definitions relative to the licensing of certain organizations to
conduct charitable raffles, bingo, and keno.
Proposed law adds the definition of "nonprofit healthcare organization" to mean any
organization that provides healthcare services, together with their parent organizations,
affiliates, subsidiaries and foundations, that is licensed or chartered in this state and is
exempt from federal and state taxation pursuant to the Internal Revenue Code and applicable
provisions of Louisiana law.
Proposed law provides that a nonprofit healthcare organization not be required to obtain a
license in order to hold, operate or conduct a raffle as defined in present law.
Proposed law provides that the nonprofit healthcare organization not be required to obtain
any license as described in present law, from a governing authority of any municipality or
parish.
Effective August 1, 2026
(Amends R.S. 4:707(E)(5), (6), (7), (8) and (9); adds R.S. 4:707(E)(10) and (H)(5))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by Senate
Committee Amendments Proposed by Senate Committee on Judiciary B to the
original bill
1. Limits the exemption from licensure for nonprofit healthcare organizations
to the operation of raffles only.
2. Eliminates a notification requirement contained in proposed law.
