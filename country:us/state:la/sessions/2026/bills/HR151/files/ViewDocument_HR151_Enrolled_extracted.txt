2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 151
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
To designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as Stuffed Shrimp Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, the city of Shreveport, Louisiana, has a long history with stuffed shrimp
dating back to the mid-1950s; and
WHEREAS, Freeman & Harris Cafe, a Creole and Black-owned restaurant in
Shreveport, became known for the dish at its original location on Texas Avenue; the avenue
was placed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1979, and is now the location of
the Texas Avenue Historic District; and
WHEREAS, former chefs of Freeman & Harris Cafe brought the iconic stuffed
shrimp recipe to various restaurant locations around the city, and the dish began appearing
in home kitchens and restaurants around the country; and
WHEREAS, stuffed shrimp is served by numerous Shreveport restaurants, including
Brother's Seafood, Eddie's Seafood & Soulfood, and Orlandeaux's Café; and
WHEREAS, historians have documented that stuffed shrimp was served during
meetings between Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., and local civil rights leaders, which is a
testament to the iconic status of the dish.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as Stuffed
Shrimp Day at the state capitol.
