HB 141 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Muscarello
Abstract: Removes full dates of birth from the list of prohibited information in a court
filing.
Present law (C.C.P. Art. 253(B)(2) and C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(B)) authorizes attorneys to
electronically file pleadings, documents, and exhibits for civil and criminal court matters.
Present law prohibits the following private information in such filings:
(1) First five digits of any social security number.
(2) Tax identification numbers.
(3) State identification numbers.
(4) Driver's license numbers.
(5) Financial account numbers.
(6) Full dates of birth.
(7) Any information protected from disclosure by state or federal law.
Proposed law generally retains present law, but removes full dates of birth from the list of
prohibited information in both civil and criminal court matters.
Proposed law provides that present law (C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(B)) does not apply to prosecuting
agencies described in present law (C.Cr.P. Art. 388), or to expungement proceedings.
(Amends C.C.P. Art. 253(B)(2) and C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(B))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Judiciary to the original
bill:
1. Clarify that present law (C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(B)) does not limit the information
required for submission by a prosecuting agency.
2. Make technical corrections.
