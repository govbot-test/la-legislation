HB 165 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Hebert
Abstract: Provides for the transfer of monies from the Lottery Proceeds Fund to the
Veterans Service Grant Fund.
Present constitution provides for lottery proceeds to be appropriated only for the purpose of
the minimum foundation program and no more than $500,000 for services related to problem
gaming.
Proposed constitutional amendment retains present constitution and adds a provision
requiring the state treasurer to annually transfer $500,000 from the Lottery Proceeds Fund
to the Veterans Service Grant Fund, for use as provided by law.
Provides for submission of the proposed amendment to the voters at the statewide election
to be held Nov. 3, 2026.
(Amends Const. Art. XII, §6(A)(1))

Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Appropriations to the
original bill:
1. Remove the requirement that the legislature annually appropriate monies from
the Lottery Proceeds Fund associated with a new lottery game for the benefit of
La. veterans.
2. Require the treasurer to annually transfer $500,000 from the Lottery Proceeds
Fund to the Veterans Service Grant Fund, for use as provided by law.
3. Change the ballot language by removing the language related to creating a new
lottery game and adding language allowing the legislature to appropriate up to
$500,000 from the Lottery Proceeds fund to be used for the benefit of La.
military veterans and their families.
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Civil Law and
Procedure to the engrossed bill:
1. Revise ballot language.
