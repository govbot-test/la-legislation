HLS 26RS-1325 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE MCFARLAND
LAW ENFORCEMENT: Provides for the establishment of the Louisiana Center for Safe
Schools within the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and Administration
of Criminal Justice
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 15:1216 and to repeal R.S. 29:726.5, 726.5.1, and 726.6, relative to school and
3 nonprofit security; to establish the Louisiana Center for Safe Schools within the
4 Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice;
5 to provide for duties, powers, administration, cooperative authority, procurement
6 authority, data governance, contract and license continuity, and an advisory council;
7 to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 15:1216 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1216. Louisiana Center for Safe Schools
11 A. The Louisiana Center for Safe Schools, referred to in this Section as the
12 "center", is hereby established within the Louisiana Commission on Law
13 Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice.
14 B. The center shall administer school and nonprofit security programs
15 formerly provided pursuant to Title 29 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950.
16 C. The center shall do all of the following:
17 (1) Identify and prioritize resources available to improve the safety and
18 security of schools of this state and nonprofit organizations.

1 (2) Facilitate the collection and dissemination of information on safety
2 vulnerabilities, safety needs, training standards, best practices, and available funding
3 programs.
4 (3) Develop, in consultation with the State Board of Elementary and
5 Secondary Education, a comprehensive statewide school safety framework, including
6 behavioral threat assessment models and trauma-informed support practices.
7 (4) Assist schools in developing and revising crisis management and
8 emergency response plans required pursuant to R.S. 17:416.16.
9 (5) Coordinate with the Department of Education and other appropriate state
10 agencies to ensure awareness of available safety resources and incorporation of best
11 practices.
12 (6) Provide training and technical assistance to schools, school governing
13 authorities, nonprofit organizations, and other appropriate entities on issues relating
14 to school and nonprofit security, including emergency operations planning,
15 emergency drills, threat assessment, facility security, digital safety, vulnerability
16 assessments, and hazard mitigation.
17 (7) Facilitate consistent and comprehensive information sharing between
18 schools, communities, nonprofit organizations, and governmental agencies to
19 promote safety and resiliency.
20 (8) Identify and offer training opportunities relating to school resource
21 officers and other personnel assigned to school and nonprofit security functions.
22 (9) Administer systems and coordinated processes relating to threat
23 assessment, violence prevention, risk mitigation, suicide prevention and intervention,
24 emergency preparedness, and response coordination.
25 D. The center may acquire, develop, license, hold, operate, maintain,
26 administer, and make available systems, platforms, tools, subscriptions, and related
27 infrastructure reasonably necessary to carry out its duties and may make such
28 systems available to authorized participants pursuant to agreements consistent with
29 applicable law.

1 E. The center may enter cooperative agreements, intergovernmental
2 agreements, cooperative endeavor agreements, contracts, or other lawful
3 arrangements with public entities or governmental bodies as necessary to carry out
4 its duties.
5 F. The center may apply for, receive, administer, and implement programs
6 supported by state, federal, interstate, or other lawful funding sources and may
7 expend such funds and carry out programmatic requirements consistent with this
8 Section.
9 G. The center may utilize cooperative or intergovernmental contract vehicles
10 authorized under state or federal law and existing statewide contracts to acquire
11 systems and services necessary to implement this Section. All procurements shall
12 be conducted in accordance with applicable state and federal procurement law.
13 H. The center shall adopt and maintain written policies governing data
14 management, confidentiality, access controls, authorized participation, and
15 compliance with applicable state and federal privacy law. Any public records
16 exemptions applicable to school security plans, critical incident data, or related
17 security information shall continue to apply to data and systems administered
18 pursuant to this Section.
19 I. The center may establish and administer emergency notification systems
20 and anonymous reporting systems to enhance school and nonprofit security.
21 J. The center shall provide an annual report to the governor, the State Board
22 of Elementary and Secondary Education, and the appropriate legislative committees
23 regarding the status of school and nonprofit security initiatives and the activities of
24 the center.
25 K. Nothing in this Section shall supersede the constitutional authority of the
26 State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education.
27 L. The center shall coordinate with the Governor’s Office of Homeland
28 Security and Emergency Preparedness regarding school-related critical incident
29 mapping and emergency response planning.

1 M.(1) There is hereby established within the Louisiana Commission on Law
2 Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice the School and Nonprofit
3 Security Advisory Council, referred to in this Subsection as the "council".
4 (2) The advisory council shall consist of the following members:
5 (a) The executive director of the Louisiana Commission on Law
6 Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice, or his designee.
7 (b) The state superintendent of education, or his designee.
8 (c) The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health, or his designee.
9 (d) The secretary of the Department of Children and Family Services, or his
10 designee.
11 (e) The superintendent of the Louisiana State Police, or his designee.
12 (f) The executive director of the Louisiana Sheriffs' Association, or his
13 designee.
14 (g) The executive director of a statewide association of chiefs of police, or
15 his designee.
16 (h) A representative of a statewide association of school boards, appointed
17 by that association.
18 (i) A representative of a statewide association of school principals or
19 superintendents, appointed by that association.
20 (j) The chair of the House Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice,
21 or his designee.
22 (k) The chair of the Senate Committee on Judiciary B, or his designee.
23 (l) The executive director of the Louisiana Interchurch Conference, or his
24 designee.
25 (m) The director of the Governor's Office of Homeland Security and
26 Emergency Preparedness, or his designee.
27 (3) The council shall elect a chair from among its members in accordance
28 with rules adopted by the council.
29 (4) A majority of the appointed members constitutes a quorum.

4 (a) Review and formally endorse statewide school and nonprofit security
5 frameworks developed by the center.

22 studies, pending matters, officers, and other actions of the prior commission are
23 transferred to and shall continue in effect under the council established by this
24 Section.

HB 821 Original 2026 Regular Session McFarland
Abstract: Establishes the La. Center for Safe Schools within the La. Commission on Law
Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice and the School and Nonprofit
Advisory Council.
Present law establishes the La. Center for Safe Schools (the "center") within the Governor's
Office of Homeland Security and Emergency Preparedness.
Proposed law recreates the center within the La. Commission on Law Enforcement and
Administration of Criminal Justice.
Proposed law provides for duties and powers of the center, including the following:
(1) Identifying and prioritizing resources to improve safety in schools.
(2) Collecting and disseminating information on safety needs, training standards, and
funding programs.
(3) Developing a comprehensive statewide school safety framework.
(4) Assisting schools in developing crisis management and emergency response plans.
(5) Coordinating with the Dept. of Education to ensure awareness of safety resources.
(6) Providing training to schools including emergency operations planning, emergency
drills, threat assessment, digital safety, and hazard mitigation.
(7) Facilitating consistent and comprehensive information sharing between schools.
(8) Identifying and offering training opportunities.
(9) Administering systems and coordinating processes relating to threat assessment,
violence prevention, risk mitigation, suicide prevention, emergency preparedness,
and response coordination.

are additions.

Proposed law allows the center to acquire platforms and tools and to enter into cooperative
agreements to carry out its duties.
Proposed law allows the center to enter into contracts to implement proposed law.
Proposed law allows the center to apply for and implement funding from state, federal, and
interstate sources.
Proposed law requires the center to adopt written policies regarding data management and
confidentiality except where public records law applies.
Proposed law requires the center to provide an annual report to the governor, State Bd. of
Elementary and Secondary Education, and certain legislative committees.
Proposed law establishes the School and Nonprofit Security Advisory Council and lists
members to be appointed to the council.
Proposed law provides duties of the council, including the following:
(1) Reviewing and endorsing statewide school and nonprofit security frameworks.
(2) Adopting recommendations regarding interagency coordination, threat assessment,
training, emergency preparedness, and implementation.
(3) Making recommendations regarding funding priorities relating to school and
nonprofit security.
(4) Issuing an annual report to the governor and legislature regarding school and
nonprofit security.
Proposed law prohibits the council from having policymaking and rulemaking authority,
entering into contracts, and procuring goods and services.
Proposed law states that the School and Nonprofit Security Council is the successor entity
to the La. Commission on School and Nonprofit Security established by R.S. 29:726.5.
Proposed law repeals present law (R.S. 29:726.5, 726.5.1, and 726.6).
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 15:1216; Repeals R.S. 29:726.5, 726.5.1, and 726.6)
