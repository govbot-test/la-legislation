2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR MIZELL
GAMING. Provides relative to the dedication of revenue from sports wagering. (7/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 27:625(G)(2), relative to sports wagering; to provide for the
3 crediting of funds from the proceeds of the tax on sports wagering net gaming
4 proceeds; to provide relative to the funds credited to the Louisiana Early Childhood
5 Education Fund; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 27:625(G)(2) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
8 §625. State tax; levy
9 * * *
10 G. After complying with the provisions of Subsection D of this Section, each
11 fiscal year the state treasurer shall credit the following amounts to the following
12 funds:
13 * * *
14 (2) Twenty-five percent of the monies collected pursuant to this Section, not
15 to exceed or twenty million dollars, whichever is greater, shall be credited to the
16 Louisiana Early Childhood Education Fund established by R.S. 17:407.30.
17 * * *

SLS 26RS-408 ORIGINAL
SB NO. 135
1 Section 2. This Act shall become effective on July 1, 2026; if vetoed by the governor
2 and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become effective on the day
3 following such approval by the legislature or July 1, 2026, whichever is later.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 135 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Present law requires that amounts collected from the tax on net gaming proceeds from sports
wagering be credited to various funds described in present law, including the La. Early
Childhood Education Fund.
Present law requires the amount credited to the La. Early Childhood Education Fund be
25%, not to exceed $20 million.
Proposed law removes the $20 million cap on amounts credited to the La. Early Childhood
Education Fund. Proposed law requires that 25%, or $20 million, whichever is greater, be
credited to the La. Early Childhood Education Fund.
Effective July 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 27:625(G)(2))
