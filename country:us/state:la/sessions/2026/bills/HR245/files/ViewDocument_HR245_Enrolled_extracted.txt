HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 245
BY REPRESENTATIVE BAYHAM
A RESOLUTION
To condemn political violence in the United States.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate to recognize the often volatile political division in the
United States and to condemn acts of politically-motivated violence; and
WHEREAS, attempts on any candidate or elected official's life cannot be tolerated;
curtailing political violence is essential for the health of the country; and
WHEREAS, unfortunately, there is a history of political violence in the United
States; and
WHEREAS, President Donald Trump has been the target of three separate
assassination attempts; he was grazed by a bullet during a July 2024 rally in Butler,
Pennsylvania, and faced a second attempt on his life in Florida later that year; the third
attempt came during the 2026 White House Correspondents' Dinner in Washington, D.C.;
and
WHEREAS, Minnesota House of Representatives Speaker Emerita Melissa Hortman
and her husband, Mark, were the victims of a targeted political assassination at their home
in June 2025; and
WHEREAS, Charlie Kirk, an American right-wing political activist, was assassinated
during a Turning Point USA event on the campus of Utah Valley University in Orem, Utah,
in September, 2025; and
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is familiar with acts of political violence; U.S.
Senator and former Louisiana Governor Huey P. Long was assassinated in the halls of the
state capitol on September 8, 1935; a bomb exploded in the Louisiana Senate Chamber in
the 1970s; U.S. House majority leader Steve Scalise was shot and wounded during the
annual Congressional Baseball Game in 2017; and
WHEREAS, political violence subverts the will of democracy and creates greater
division within the state and country, and it is vitally important that American citizens stand
against such acts.

HR NO. 245 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby condemn political violence in the United States and
does hereby encourage Americans to embrace discourse in the spirit of democracy as a
necessary alternative to acts of violence against elected officials and other political figures.
