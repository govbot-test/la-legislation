HB 1193 Original 2026 Regular Session Sawyer
Abstract: Establishes the use of Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity construction phase
contracts and Indefinite Delivery, Indefinite Quantity supply contracts by the Coastal
Protection and Restoration Authority. Provides procedures, definitions, and
requirements to govern the contracts while providing flexibility for project-specific
needs.
Proposed law (R.S. 49:214.6.11(A)) declares that improving efficiency in readiness in
performing integrated coastal protection is in the best interest of taxpayers. Authorizes the
Coastal Protection and Restoration Authority (CPRA) to award Indefinite Delivery,
Indefinite Quantity construction phase and supply contracts in relation to integrated coast
protection projects and related incidental work let or to be let by the authority.
Proposed law (R.S. 49:214.6.11(B)) provides for definitions of the following:
(1) "Best value" means a selection process in which proposals contain price and
qualitative components and award of the indefinite delivery, indefinite quantity
contract based upon a combination of price and qualitative considerations as
provided in the solicitation documents.

Proposed law (R.S. 49:214.6.11(C)) requires CPRA to comply with all applicable federal
regulations for projects and provides governed by federal law that federal regulations control

Proposed law (R.S. 49:214.6.11(D)) authorizes CPRA to use low bid, qualification-based,
or best value, or any other form of ID/IQ, to select a successful bidder or bidders. Further
allows procurement to include single award or multiple award. Additionally requires the
procurement documents include best value or qualification-based evaluation factors. Further
requires that proposed law supersedes La. Public Bid laws. Further allows that materials
acquired under ID/IQ supply contracts be provided to contractors for the use and as a
condition of any contractor for integrated coastal protection projects.
Proposed law (R.S. 49:214.6.11(E)) requires procurement and award of ID/IQ construction
phase contracts and ID/IQ supply contracts meet the following requirements:
(1) Single award ID/IQ construction phase contracts awarded on a low bid basis must
follow either the advertisement for low bid contracts pursuant to La. Public Bid law
(R.S. 38:2212) or the requirements for advertisements pursuant to proposed law.

advertised pursuant to La. Public Bid law, the award may also be made pursuant to
those provisions. Specifies that if award is made pursuant to La. Public Bid law, the
authority is not required to follow the provisions of proposed law.
(2) Single award ID/IQ supply contracts which will be awarded on a low bid basis must
follow either the applicable method set forth in the provisions of competitive sealed
bids (R.S. 39:1594) or the requirements for advertisements set forth in proposed law.
Specifies that if a single-award, low-bid ID/IQ supply contract is advertised pursuant
to competitive sealed bids, the award may also be made pursuant to competitive
