SLS 26RS-93 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR MIZELL
CRIMINAL PROCEDURE. Establishes minimum bond amounts for certain offenses.
(8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 319(A) and to enact Code of
3 Criminal Procedure Art. 315(D), relative to bail; to provide relative to schedules of
4 bail; to provide minimum bail amounts for certain offenses; and to provide for
5 related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 319(A) is hereby amended and reenacted
8 and Code of Criminal Procedure Art. 315(D) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 Art. 315. Schedules of bail
10 * * *
11 D. A bail schedule implemented by a district court shall fix the minimum
12 bail amount for the following offenses, and any court that does not utilize a bail
13 schedule shall not approve a bail undertaking that is less than the minimum
14 amount specified for the following offenses:
15 (1) For possession of child sexual abuse materials, R.S. 14:81.1, fifty
16 thousand dollars.
17 (2) For production of child sexual abuse materials, R.S. 14:81.1, one

3 Art. 319. Modifications of bail
4 A. The court having trial jurisdiction over the offense charged, on its own
5 motion or on motion of the prosecuting attorney or defendant, for good cause, may
6 either increase or reduce, subject to any minimum amount provided by law, the
7 amount of bail, or require new or additional security. For purposes of this Article,
8 good cause for increase of bail specifically includes but is not limited to the rearrest
9 of the defendant on offenses alleged to have been committed while out on a bail
10 undertaking. The modification of any bail order wherein a bail undertaking has been
11 posted by a criminal defendant and his sureties shall upon the modification terminate
12 the liability of the defendant and his sureties under the previously existing bail
13 undertaking. A new bail undertaking must be posted in the amount of the new bail
14 order.

The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part

The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 93 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Present law provides that a bail schedule may be fixed by a district court in noncapital felony
cases. Present law also provides that a bail amount fixed by schedule may be either increased
or reduced by the court in accordance with present law.
Proposed law provides that both a bail schedule implemented by a district court and a court
that does not utilize a bail schedule must impose bail in a minimum amount for the following
offenses:
(1) Possession of child sexual abuse materials - $50,000.
(2) Production of child sexual abuse materials - $100,000.
Proposed law also provides that the court cannot reduce the amount of a bail undertaking to
an amount that is less than any minimum amount provided in law.
Proposed law otherwise retains present law.
