HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 75
BY REPRESENTATIVE BOYD
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH: Designates February 27, 2025, as Caleb Wilson Day in
Louisiana
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate February 27, 2025, as Caleb Wilson Day in Louisiana.
3 WHEREAS, Caleb Jayden Wilson was born on November 1, 2004, to Corey Wilson,
4 Sr., and Urania Brown Wilson of Kenner, Louisiana; he was baptized into the Christian faith
5 by Reverend Mitchell J. Stevens at Pilgrim Baptist Church on January 3, 2010; and
6 WHEREAS, Caleb was a gifted student whose academic ability showed from an
7 early age; he discovered a passion for playing the trumpet while attending Edward Hynes
8 Charter School in New Orleans and was accepted into a program run by The Roots of Music,
9 a nonprofit organization that empowers kids through music education and mentorship, which
10 helped him hone his innate musical ability and gave him the opportunity to perform in
11 France; and
12 WHEREAS, Caleb graduated from Warren Easton High School, where he was a
13 member of the school band, band captain, and section leader for the trumpet players; he
14 maintained a desire to continue refining his musical craft, and as a result, he was accepted
15 into the Trombone Shorty Foundation; the foundation allowed him many opportunities to
16 perform across the United States; he performed in Havana, Cuba, in 2019; and
17 WHEREAS, before graduating from high school, Caleb earned a certificate in
18 general studies from Delgado Community College through Warren Easton High School's
19 dual enrollment program; he attended the Southern University Band Camp three times and
20 was recruited to become a future member of the university's world-renowned band, "The
21 Human Jukebox"; and

1 WHEREAS, Caleb was a founding member of the 110 Brass Band, and he performed
2 for Mademoiselle Dwyn's School of Dance, various cotillions, and other events; he was a
3 member of Nu Phi Omicron, Inc., a nonprofit organization that helps Louisiana's college and
4 high school musicians grow and excel in their craft; and
5 WHEREAS, Caleb was a member of the National Honor Society, student council,
6 and the Warren Easton baseball team; he competed on the New Orleans Recreation
7 Department (NORD) swim team and worked as a NORD lifeguard; and
8 WHEREAS, at the time of his passing, Caleb was a junior majoring in mechanical
9 engineering at Southern University and A&M College in Baton Rouge; he was a member
10 of the National Society of Black Engineers and a junior usher and active member of Pilgrim
11 Baptist Church's Youth Ministry; and
12 WHEREAS, Caleb was able to live out his dream as a member of the Southern
13 University Band, and he performed and marched on the field of the Caesars Superdome
14 during Super Bowl LIX; he also played during the nationally-televised "Bayou Classic"
15 games in New Orleans; and
16 WHEREAS, Caleb is survived by his parents, Corey and Urania Wilson; siblings,
17 Coren and Corey, Jr.; grandmothers, Eulalia Porter and Veronica Temple; grandfather,
18 Albert Brown; godparents, Reverend Richard Eugene Wilson, Yolanda Curtis Wilson, Alicia
19 Alexander, and Jeffrey Alexander; and a host of aunts, uncles, cousins, extended relatives,
20 and friends; he will be deeply missed, but his memory shall live on in the hearts and minds
21 of those who were privileged to have known him.
22 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
23 designate February 27, 2025, as Caleb Wilson Day in Louisiana; does hereby posthumously
24 commend Caleb's inspiring legacy as a mentor, musician, and scholar; and does hereby
25 recognize that the spirit of his music and the legacy of his love shall never fade.
