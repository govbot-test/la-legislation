HLS 26RS-771 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 17
BY REPRESENTATIVE TURNER
Development to study the state's return on investment with respect to the Taylor
Opportunity Program for Students in collaboration with the Kathleen Babineaux
Blanco Public Policy Center
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To authorize and direct the Board of Regents, Louisiana Works, and Louisiana Economic
3 Development to jointly study, in collaboration with the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco
4 Public Policy Center, the state's return on investment with respect to the Taylor
5 Opportunity Program for Students and submit a written report of findings and
6 recommendations to the House Committee on Education not later than
7 March 1, 2027.
8 WHEREAS, the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS) has enabled
9 thousands of Louisiana students to pursue postsecondary education and served as a
10 cornerstone of opportunity for nearly three decades; and
11 WHEREAS, TOPS represents one of Louisiana's most significant financial
12 commitments to postsecondary education; and
13 WHEREAS, TOPS has increased college access and retention for Louisiana students,
14 yet questions persist regarding the program's long-term return on investment for the state;
15 and
16 WHEREAS, the Board of Regents, Louisiana Works, and Louisiana Economic
17 Development each play a central role in aligning education and workforce needs to
18 strengthen Louisiana's economic competitiveness; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-771 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 17
1 WHEREAS, the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center at the University
2 of Louisiana at Lafayette conducts objective, data-driven policy research on complex state
3 programs; and
4 WHEREAS, a coordinated study among these agencies will enable the state to
5 identify whether current investments in TOPS are producing sufficient workforce and
6 economic outcomes and to determine how program funds could be better targeted to support
7 Louisiana's future workforce needs; and
8 WHEREAS, ensuring that Louisiana taxpayers receive an optimal return on their
9 investment in postsecondary education incentives is essential for the long-term fiscal health
10 and prosperity of the state.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
12 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby authorize and direct the Board of Regents, Louisiana
13 Works, and Louisiana Economic Development to jointly study the state's return on
14 investment with respect to the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS) and submit
15 a written report of findings and recommendations to the House Committee on Education not
16 later than March 1, 2027.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that these agencies shall work collaboratively with
18 the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center to design the research framework,
19 collect necessary data, and ensure transparency throughout the process.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, at a minimum, the
21 following:
22 (1) An analysis of post-graduation outcomes for TOPS recipients, including
23 workforce participation, in-state employment retention, and average earnings.
24 (2) A comparison of the economic and workforce outcomes of TOPS recipients
25 versus nonrecipients.
26 (3) An assessment of whether the various award levels and institutional distribution
27 maximize Louisiana's workforce needs and economic development priorities.
28 (4) Recommendations for policy adjustments needed to ensure TOPS funds are used
29 in a manner aligned with Louisiana's long-term workforce needs, economic competitiveness,
30 and the prosperity of its citizens.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the purpose of this study is not to review
2 individual TOPS eligibility criteria or award determinations but to examine long-term
3 economic and workforce trends to inform future state competitiveness.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
5 chairman of the Board of Regents, commissioner of higher education, secretary of Louisiana
6 Works, and secretary of Louisiana Economic Development.
