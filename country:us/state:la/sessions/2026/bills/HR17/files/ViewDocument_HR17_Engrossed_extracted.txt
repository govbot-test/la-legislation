HLS 26RS-771 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 17
BY REPRESENTATIVE TURNER
Development to study the state's return on investment with respect to the Taylor
Opportunity Program for Students in collaboration with the Kathleen Babineaux
Blanco Public Policy Center
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To authorize and direct the Board of Regents, Louisiana Works, and Louisiana Economic
3 Development to jointly study, in collaboration with the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco
4 Public Policy Center, the state's return on investment with respect to the Taylor
5 Opportunity Program for Students and submit a written report of findings and
6 recommendations to the House Committee on Education, the House Committee on
7 Appropriations, and the House Committee on Labor and Industrial Relations not later
8 than February 15, 2027.
9 WHEREAS, the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS) has enabled
10 thousands of Louisiana students to pursue postsecondary education and served as a
11 cornerstone of opportunity for nearly three decades; and
12 WHEREAS, TOPS represents one of Louisiana's most significant financial
13 commitments to postsecondary education; and
14 WHEREAS, TOPS has increased college access and retention for Louisiana students,
15 yet questions persist regarding the program's long-term return on investment for the state;
16 and
17 WHEREAS, the Board of Regents, Louisiana Works, and Louisiana Economic
18 Development each play a central role in aligning education and workforce needs to
19 strengthen Louisiana's economic competitiveness; and
Page 1 of 4
HLS 26RS-771 ENGROSSED
HR NO. 17
1 WHEREAS, the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center at the University
2 of Louisiana at Lafayette conducts objective, data-driven policy research on complex state
3 programs; and
4 WHEREAS, a coordinated study among these agencies will enable the state to
5 identify whether current investments in TOPS are producing sufficient workforce and
6 economic outcomes and to determine how program funds could be better targeted to support
7 Louisiana's future workforce needs; and
8 WHEREAS, ensuring that Louisiana taxpayers receive an optimal return on their
9 investment in postsecondary education incentives is essential for the long-term fiscal health
10 and prosperity of the state.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
12 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby authorize and direct the Board of Regents, Louisiana
13 Works, and Louisiana Economic Development to jointly study the state's return on
14 investment with respect to the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students (TOPS) and submit
15 a written report of findings and recommendations to the House Committee on Education, the
16 House Committee on Appropriations, and the House Committee on Labor and Industrial
17 Relations not later than February 15, 2027.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that these agencies shall use information provided
19 by the Louisiana Office of Student Financial Assistance when conducting the study.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that these agencies shall work collaboratively with
21 the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center to design the research framework,
22 collect necessary data, and ensure transparency throughout the process.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, at a minimum, the
24 following:
25 (1) An analysis of post-graduation outcomes for TOPS recipients, including
26 workforce participation, in-state employment retention, and average earnings.
27 (2) A comparison of the economic and workforce outcomes of TOPS recipients
28 versus nonrecipients.
29 (3) An assessment of whether the various award levels and institutional distribution
30 maximize Louisiana's workforce needs and economic development priorities.

1 (4) Recommendations for policy adjustments needed to ensure TOPS funds are used
2 in a manner aligned with Louisiana's long-term workforce needs, economic competitiveness,
3 and the prosperity of its citizens.
4 (5) A comparative analysis of the largest state-funded financial assistance programs
5 for students enrolled in postsecondary education institutions.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the report may be incorporated into the 2027
7 Launch Louisiana report developed and published by the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public
8 Policy Center for Louisiana's Foundational Integrated Research System for Transformation
9 as provided in R.S. 17:3138.12(D)(1).
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the purpose of this study is not to review
11 individual TOPS eligibility criteria or award determinations but to examine long-term
12 economic and workforce trends to inform future state competitiveness.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
14 chairman of the Board of Regents, commissioner of higher education, secretary of Louisiana
15 Works, secretary of Louisiana Economic Development, state superintendent of education,
16 and presidents of the Louisiana State University System, Southern University System,
17 University of Louisiana System, and Louisiana Community and Technical College System.

HR 17 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Turner
Authorizes and directs the Bd. of Regents, La. Works, and La. Economic Development to
jointly study, in coordination with the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center, the
state's return on investment with respect to the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students and
to submit a written report to the House Committee on Education, House Committee on
Appropriations, and House Committee on Labor and Industrial Relations not later than
Feb. 15, 2027. Provides for the agencies to use information from the La. Office of Student
Financial Assistance (LOSFA) when conducting the study, that the study include a
comparative analysis of the largest state-funded student financial assistance programs, and
that the report may be incorporated into the 2027 Launch La. report published the Blanco
Center for Louisiana's Foundational Integrated Research System for Transformation (LA
FIRST) required by present law.

Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Education to the
original bill:

Section 2:
1. Change report deadline from March 1, 2027, to Feb. 15, 2027.

Section 3:
2. Add the House Committee on Appropriations and House Committee on Labor
and Industrial Relations as report recipients.

Section 4:
3. Provide for the agencies to use information provided by LOSFA when
conducting the study.

Section 5:
4. Add that the report may be incorporated into the 2027 Launch La. report by the
Blanco Center for LA FIRST.

Section 6:
5. Provide for the study to include a comparative analysis of the largest state-funded
student financial assistance programs.
