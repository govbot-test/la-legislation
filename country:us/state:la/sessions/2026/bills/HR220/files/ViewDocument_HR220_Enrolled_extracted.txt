2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 220
BY REPRESENTATIVE BILLINGS
A RESOLUTION
To designate Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as St. Charles Parish Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, St. Charles Parish is nestled along the banks of the Mississippi River
and located approximately twenty-five miles north of New Orleans; it is one of the original
nineteen parishes in the territory of Orleans and one of nine parishes in the New Orleans
metropolitan area; and
WHEREAS, St. Charles Parish is often referred to as part of the German coast
because of the large population of German farmers that settled in the area during the 1700s
and injected new life into the city of New Orleans; and
WHEREAS, St. Charles Parish is home to the St. Charles Parish Rotary Club's
Alligator Festival, and there are more than fifteen tourist attractions and fifteen fairs and
festivals in the parish each year; and
WHEREAS, Des Allemands and Lac des Allemands in St. Charles Parish are
renowned as "the catfish capital of the universe"; and
WHEREAS, St. Charles Parish has an abundance of resources, including catfish,
shrimp, crabs, natural gas, and oil, and is home to fourteen energy, petrochemical, and
logistic terminals and facilities; the parish is an economic force in southeast Louisiana and
accounts for nearly thirty percent of the total value of exports in the region.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as St. Charles Parish
Day at the state capitol and does hereby extend sincerest wishes that the parish and its
residents continue to prosper in all of their endeavors.
