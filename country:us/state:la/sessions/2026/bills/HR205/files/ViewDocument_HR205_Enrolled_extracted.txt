Section 1:
ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 205
BY REPRESENTATIVE KERNER
A RESOLUTION
Procurement program under Section 32 of the Agricultural Adjustment Act to
purchase domestic shrimp from Louisiana shrimpers.
WHEREAS, Section 32 of the Agricultural Adjustment Act authorizes the United
States Department of Agriculture (USDA) Commodity Procurement program to purchase
surplus agricultural commodities to support domestic markets and provide nutritious food
to federal nutrition assistance programs; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana's shrimp industry is a vital component of the state's economy,
culture, and heritage, supporting thousands of jobs and sustaining coastal communities; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana shrimpers and processors are currently facing severe
economic hardship due to increased imports of foreign shrimp, rising operational costs, and
market instability; and
WHEREAS, imported shrimp often undercuts domestic shrimp prices, placing
Louisiana producers at a competitive disadvantage and threatening the long-term viability
of the domestic shrimp industry; and
WHEREAS, under Section 32, the USDA has purchased tens of millions of dollars
worth of Louisiana shrimp every year since at least 2020 as well as other domestically
sourced seafood in order to support domestic fishermen, manage surplus inventory, and
provide food to nutrition assistance programs, such as food banks; and
WHEREAS, purchasing Louisiana shrimp under Section 32 authority would provide
immediate economic relief to domestic shrimpers while ensuring that high-quality,
American-produced seafood reaches families in need; and

HR NO. 205 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, shrimp is a nutritious, high-protein food that can be effectively
incorporated into federal nutrition programs, including school meals, food banks, and
emergency feeding initiatives; and
WHEREAS, such action would strengthen domestic food security, support
sustainable fishing practices, and preserve an industry that is essential to Louisiana's coastal
economy; and
WHEREAS, Congressman Clay Higgins, in a letter to USDA secretary Brooke
Rollins in January, noted challenges domestic fishermen are facing due to imported seafood
dumping and unfair trade practices; and
WHEREAS, the impact on Louisiana shrimpers, should the USDA fail to purchase
shrimp for 2026, will be dire as the USDA purchase provides crucial stability for domestic
shrimp prices for the entire 2026 season; and
WHEREAS, exceptionally high fuel costs combined with the influx of imported
seafood from foreign countries have led to challenging times for hardworking Americans,
and the USDA Commodity Procurement program will provide a much needed lifeline to
sustain the shrimp industry for the 2026 season.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the USDA to utilize its Section 32
authority to purchase Louisiana shrimp and prioritize domestic seafood in its procurement
programs.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the USDA is encouraged to work with
Louisiana's shrimp industry stakeholders to ensure efficient procurement, processing, and
distribution of domestically harvested shrimp.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the United States Department of Agriculture and the Louisiana congressional
delegation.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
