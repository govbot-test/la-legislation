2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 205
BY REPRESENTATIVE KERNER
SEAFOOD: Urges and requests the United States Department of Agriculture to utilize the
Commodity Procurement program under Section 32 of the Agricultural Adjustment
Act to purchase domestic shrimp from Louisiana shrimpers
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the United States Department of Agriculture to utilize the Commodity
3 Procurement program under Section 32 of the Agricultural Adjustment Act to
4 purchase domestic shrimp from Louisiana shrimpers.
5 WHEREAS, Section 32 of the Agricultural Adjustment Act authorizes the United
6 States Department of Agriculture (USDA) Commodity Procurement program to purchase
7 surplus agricultural commodities to support domestic markets and provide nutritious food
8 to federal nutrition assistance programs; and
9 WHEREAS, Louisiana's shrimp industry is a vital component of the state's economy,
10 culture, and heritage, supporting thousands of jobs and sustaining coastal communities; and
11 WHEREAS, Louisiana shrimpers and processors are currently facing severe
12 economic hardship due to increased imports of foreign shrimp, rising operational costs, and
13 market instability; and
14 WHEREAS, imported shrimp often undercuts domestic shrimp prices, placing
15 Louisiana producers at a competitive disadvantage and threatening the long-term viability
16 of the domestic shrimp industry; and
17 WHEREAS, under Section 32, the USDA has purchased tens of millions of dollars
18 worth of Louisiana shrimp every year since at least 2020 as well as other domestically
19 sourced seafood in order to support domestic fishermen, manage surplus inventory, and
20 provide food to nutrition assistance programs, such as food banks; and

1 WHEREAS, purchasing Louisiana shrimp under Section 32 authority would provide
2 immediate economic relief to domestic shrimpers while ensuring that high-quality,
3 American-produced seafood reaches families in need; and
4 WHEREAS, shrimp is a nutritious, high-protein food that can be effectively
5 incorporated into federal nutrition programs, including school meals, food banks, and
6 emergency feeding initiatives; and
7 WHEREAS, such action would strengthen domestic food security, support
8 sustainable fishing practices, and preserve an industry that is essential to Louisiana's coastal
9 economy; and
10 WHEREAS, Congressman Clay Higgins, in a letter to USDA secretary Brooke
11 Rollins in January, noted challenges domestic fishermen are facing due to imported seafood
12 dumping and unfair trade practices; and
13 WHEREAS, the impact on Louisiana shrimpers, should the USDA fail to purchase
14 shrimp for 2026, will be dire as the USDA purchase provides crucial stability for domestic
15 shrimp prices for the entire 2026 season; and
16 WHEREAS, exceptionally high fuel costs combined with the influx of imported
17 seafood from foreign countries have led to challenging times for hardworking Americans,
18 and the USDA Commodity Procurement program will provide a much needed lifeline to
19 sustain the shrimp industry for the 2026 season.
20 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
21 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the USDA to utilize its Section 32
22 authority to purchase Louisiana shrimp and prioritize domestic seafood in its procurement
23 programs.
24 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the USDA is encouraged to work with
25 Louisiana's shrimp industry stakeholders to ensure efficient procurement, processing, and
26 distribution of domestically harvested shrimp.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this Resolution be transmitted to the
28 secretary of the United States Department of Agriculture and the Louisiana congressional
29 delegation.
