19 (a) Request proof of citizenship or qualified immigration as required by this
20 Section.
21 (b) Maintain a record indicating whether proof of citizenship or qualified
22 immigration was provided.
23 (c) Maintain a record indicating whether services or benefits were continued
24 or discontinued based on proof of citizenship or qualified immigration.
25 (d) Maintain any other data points required by rule or necessary for audit by
26 the legislative auditor.
27 (4) The records described in this Subsection shall constitute the only
28 information required to be produced to the legislative auditor for compliance with
29 this Section.
30 (5) Notwithstanding Paragraph (2) of this Subsection, if the United States
31 Immigration and Customs Enforcement submits a lawful request for information
32 regarding an individual who failed to provide proof of citizenship or qualified
33 immigration status, the entity shall provide the information in accordance with
34 applicable federal and state law.
35 (6) Nothing in this Subsection shall be construed to prohibit cooperation with
36 federal authorities when otherwise required by law.
37 I. This Section shall not apply, and no entity shall be subject to liability or
38 enforcement under this Section, for actions taken in good faith to protect life, health,
39 safety, or access to essential services during emergent circumstances, disaster
