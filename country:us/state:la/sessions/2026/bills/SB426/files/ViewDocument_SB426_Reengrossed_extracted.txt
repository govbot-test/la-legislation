4 §3386.1. Definitions
5 As used in this Chapter, the following terms have the meaning ascribed to
6 them in this Section:
7 (1) "Addiction counselor" means any person who is licensed, certified, or
8 registered in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter and procedures
9 established by the board and who, by means of his special knowledge acquired
10 through formal education or practical experience, is qualified to provide addiction
11 counseling services to those individuals afflicted with or suffering from an addictive
12 disorder or certain co-occurring disorders. The counseling services provided shall be
13 those which utilize KSA or core functions, as determined by the board to be
14 appropriate for the addictive disorder or disorders presented.
15 (2) "Addictive disorder" means the repeated pathological use of substances
16 including but not limited to alcohol, drugs, or tobacco, or repeated pathological
17 compulsive behaviors including but not limited to gambling, which cause physical,
18 psychological, emotional, economic, legal, social, or other harms to the individual
19 afflicted with the addiction or to others affected by the individual's affliction. As
20 used in this Chapter, "addictive disorder" shall include not only those instances
21 where withdrawal from or tolerance to the substance or behaviors are present but
22 also those instances involving use and abuse of substances.
23 (3) "Behavioral health condition" means a condition that affects an
24 individual's emotional, psychological, cognitive, behavioral, or social
25 functioning and may impact their ability to pursue health, wellness, recovery,
26 and quality of life, and includes substance use-related conditions, mental health
27 conditions, co-occurring conditions, trauma-related impacts, and patterns of
28 behavior that create functional impairment, distress, or increased risk of harm
29 to the individual or others.

21 §3386.2. Addictive Disorder Regulatory Authority
22 A. The legislature hereby finds it appropriate that the responsibility and
23 authority to regulate the practice of addictive disorder treatment, and prevention, and
24 peer support shall be exercised by a licensing board created for that purpose. It is
25 the intent of the legislature that a single authority issue and recognize
26 credentials for addictive disorder counseling, prevention, and peer support
27 professionals in Louisiana, and that such authority shall be the Addictive
28 Disorder Regulatory Authority.

12 §3387.15. Peer support specialist; scope of practice
13 A.(1) The practice of peer support within the meaning and intent of this
14 Chapter shall consist of the supervised rendering of mentorship, guidance, and
15 support services to individuals suffering from a behavioral health condition to
16 assist them in gaining an understanding of the nature of their disorder and
17 developing and maintaining a responsible lifestyle.
18 (2) A peer support specialist shall not practice autonomously.
19 Supervision shall be rendered by a licensed mental health professional or peer
20 support specialist supervisor. A peer support specialist shall not render clinical
21 services.
22 (3) The scope of practice, in addition to any other applicable provision
23 of this Chapter, shall include implementing strategic support, providing
24 mentorship to family members, and as appropriate, to others affected by the
25 individual's behavioral health condition.
26 B. The board shall adopt and promulgate rules which govern peer
27 support specialists in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter.
28 C. Any person seeking to be certified by the board as a peer support
29 specialist shall submit an application on a form and in a manner prescribed by

licensing board with the responsibility and authority to regulate the practice of addictive
disorder treatment and prevention.
Proposed law adds that ADRA shall issue and recognize credentials for professionals
engaged in providing substance use disorder counseling, prevention, and peer support
services for those who suffer from behavioral health conditions.
Present law provides for the membership of ADRA.
Proposed law changes the number of members from 8 to 11 and adds representation from
the office of behavioral health and higher education and a peer support specialist.
Proposed law provides for additional qualifications for board members, except for the
consumer member.
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law adds definitions for "behavioral health condition", "credentialing", "licensed
mental health professional", "peer support", "peer support specialist", "peer support
specialist associate", and "peer support specialist supervisor".
Proposed law establishes the qualifications, scope of practice, and status renewal process for
a person to be recognized by ADRA as a peer support specialist, peer support specialist
associate, and peer support specialist supervisor.
Proposed law provides for an application fee of not less than $50 and not more than $300,
an examination fee of not less than $100 and not more than $300, and a renewal fee of not
less than $100 and not more than $300 for a peer support specialist, associate, or supervisor.
Present law prohibits a person from being disqualified from employment as a peer support
specialist with a licensed behavioral health services provider if the person's criminal
background check reveals certain convictions if the person meets requirements established
in present law, including successfully completing the La. Dept. of Health's training and
requirements.
Proposed law expands present law to apply to certified addiction counselors, registered
addiction counselors, counselors in training, peer support specialists, or peer support
specialist associates and changes the reference from the La. Dept. of Health to ADRA.
Proposed law requires ADRA to coordinate with the La. Dept. of Health during the transition
of peer support specialists from the department to the board.
Proposed law provides that any rules promulgated by ADRA relative to peer support
specialist associates, peer support specialists, and peer support supervisors shall align with
federal requirements.

SB 426 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Stine
Present law establishes the Addictive Disorders Regulatory Authority (ADRA) as the

disorder treatment and prevention.
Proposed law adds that ADRA shall issue and recognize credentials for professionals
engaged in providing substance use disorder counseling, prevention, and peer support
services for those who suffer from behavioral health conditions.
Present law provides for the membership of ADRA.
Proposed law changes the number of members from 8 to 11 and adds representation from
the office of behavioral health and higher education and a peer support specialist.
Proposed law provides for additional qualifications for board members, except for the
consumer member.
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law adds definitions for "behavioral health condition", "credentialing", "licensed
mental health professional", "peer support", "peer support specialist", "peer support
specialist associate", and "peer support specialist supervisor".
Proposed law establishes the qualifications, scope of practice, and status renewal process for
a person to be recognized by ADRA as a peer support specialist, peer support specialist
associate, and peer support specialist supervisor.
Proposed law provides for an application fee of not less than $50 and not more than $300,
an examination fee of not less than $100 and not more than $300, and a renewal fee of not
less than $100 and not more than $300 for a peer support specialist, associate, or supervisor.
Present law prohibits a person from being disqualified from employment as a peer support
specialist with a licensed behavioral health services provider if the person's criminal
background check reveals certain convictions if the person meets requirements established
in present law, including successfully completing the La. Dept. of Health's training and
requirements.
Proposed law expands present law to apply to certified addiction counselors, registered
addiction counselors, counselors in training, peer support specialists, or peer support
specialist associates and changes the reference from the La. Dept. of Health to ADRA.
Proposed law requires ADRA to coordinate with the La. Dept. of Health during the transition
of peer support specialists from the department to the board.
Proposed law provides that any rules promulgated by ADRA relative to peer support
specialist associates, peer support specialists, and peer support supervisors shall align with
federal requirements.
Effective August 1, 2026.
