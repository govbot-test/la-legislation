3 §3386.1. Definitions
4 As used in this Chapter, the following terms have the meaning ascribed to
5 them in this Section:
6 (1) "Addiction counselor" means any person who is licensed, certified, or
7 registered in accordance with the provisions of this Chapter and procedures
8 established by the board and who, by means of his special knowledge acquired
9 through formal education or practical experience, is qualified to provide addiction
10 counseling services to those individuals afflicted with or suffering from an addictive
11 disorder or certain co-occurring disorders. The counseling services provided shall be
12 those which utilize KSA or core functions, as determined by the board to be
13 appropriate for the addictive disorder or disorders presented.
14 (2) "Addictive disorder" means the repeated pathological use of substances
15 including but not limited to alcohol, drugs, or tobacco, or repeated pathological
16 compulsive behaviors including but not limited to gambling, which cause physical,
17 psychological, emotional, economic, legal, social, or other harms to the individual
18 afflicted with the addiction or to others affected by the individual's affliction. As
19 used in this Chapter, "addictive disorder" shall include not only those instances
20 where withdrawal from or tolerance to the substance or behaviors are present but
21 also those instances involving use and abuse of substances.
22 (3) "Behavioral health condition" means a condition that affects an
23 individual's emotional, psychological, cognitive, behavioral, or social
24 functioning and may impact their ability to pursue health, wellness, recovery,
25 and quality of life, and includes substance use-related conditions, mental health
26 conditions, co-occurring conditions, trauma-related impacts, and patterns of
27 behavior that create functional impairment, distress, or increased risk of harm
28 to the individual or others.

25 credentials for addictive disorder counseling, prevention, and peer support
26 professionals in Louisiana, and that such authority shall be the Addictive
27 Disorder Regulatory Authority.

11 §3388.3. Applicability Peer support specialist renewal
12 A. It is the intent of the legislature that there be a single authority issuing
13 credentials for addictive disorder counseling and prevention professionals in
14 Louisiana. The authority for issuing and recognizing credentials for professionals
15 engaged in the providing of counseling and prevention services for those who suffer
16 from addictive disorders shall be the Addictive Disorder Regulatory Authority.
17 Each peer support specialist shall renew his certification annually. Each
18 peer support specialist associate shall renew his status annually. Each renewal
19 shall be for a twelve-month period.
20 B. Within the twelve-month period provided for in Subsection A of this
21 Section, each peer support specialist and peer support specialist associate shall
22 provide to the board documentation of successfully completing a minimum of
23 twenty board-approved continuing educational hours. Such hours shall be
24 directly related to the area of certification, including six continuing educational
25 hours in ethics.
26 C. After initial application, an individual may hold peer support
27 specialist associate status for up to sixty consecutive months. Failure to renew
28 his annual status shall disqualify an individual from reapplying for peer
29 support specialist associate status.

DIGEST
SB 426 Original 2026 Regular Session Stine
Present law establishes the Addictive Disorders Regulatory Authority (ADRA) as the
licensing board with the responsibility and authority to regulate the practice of addictive
disorder treatment and prevention.
Proposed law adds that ADRA shall issue and recognize credentials for professionals
engaged in providing substance use disorder counseling, prevention, and peer support
services for those who suffer from behavioral health conditions.
Present law provides for the membership of ADRA.
Proposed law changes the number of members from 8 to 11 and adds representation from
the office of behavioral health and higher education and a peer support specialist.
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law adds definitions for "behavioral health condition", "credentialing", "licensed
mental health professional", "peer support", "peer support specialist", "peer support
specialist associate", and "peer support specialist supervisor".
Proposed law establishes the qualifications, scope of practice, and status renewal process for
a person to be recognized by ADRA as a peer support specialist, peer support specialist
associate, and peer support specialist supervisor.
Proposed law provides for an application fee of not less than $50 and not more than $300,
an examination fee of not less than $100 and not more than $300, and a renewal fee of not
less than $100 and not more than $300 for a peer support specialist, associate, or supervisor.
Present law prohibits a person from being disqualified from employment as a peer support
specialist with a licensed behavioral health services provider if the person's criminal
background check reveals certain convictions if the person meets requirements established
in present law, including successfully completing the La. Dept. of Health's training and
requirements.
Proposed law expands present law to apply to certified addiction counselors, registered
addiction counselors, counselors in training, peer support specialists, or peer support
specialist associates and changes the reference from the La. Dept. of Health to ADRA.
Effective August 1, 2026.
