A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Louisiana State University Board of Supervisors to name the LSU
Gymnastics Training Center for Legendary LSU Gymnastics Coach "D-D" Breaux.
WHEREAS, Sara "D-D" Breaux was born and raised in Donaldsonville, Louisiana
and competed as a gymnast headed for the Olympics before an injury changed the course of
her career; and
WHEREAS, in 1977 she became the first full-time LSU Women's Gymnastics Coach
where she found a program with no resources, little respect and an uphill battle for survival;
and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux fought many battles that included threatened program
elimination, elimination of her assistant coaches and staff, no or little promotional support,
no basic resources such as competition leotards, and an inadequate and dangerous facility
for the first 39 years of her career; and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux fought for all Women's Sports and Athletes on the LSU
campus; and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux knew that in order to establish a championship program
that would be sustainable, she needed a world class facility, community support and
attendance at her meets and resources to recruit and compete promising athletes; and
WHEREAS, with the appointment of Skip Bertman as athletic director, support for
the program began to build; and

WHEREAS, in order to compete with her SEC and national competitors, Coach
Breaux had to have the same amount and quality of equipment as other institutions did in
order to maximize the practice time on each event allowed by the NCAA and so she sought
out every possible source of funds necessary for a competitive facility and equipment; and
WHEREAS, in 2014, she convinced the Tiger Athletic Foundation and the school's
athletic director to have a groundbreaking to convince donors that a facility would be built
and that would assist to attract donors and instill a belief in the community that this would
be done; and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux's efforts were successful in bringing a world class facility
to LSU which was celebrated on February 16, 2016, with a ribbon cutting ceremony for the
LSU Gymnastics Training Center; and
WHEREAS, for decades Coach Breaux pursued her vision for attendance by
appearing on the Buckskin Bill Show for a segment on exercise at 6:00 a.m., placing
schedules at cash registers throughout the city, handing out tickets to gymnastic events in
grocery store parking lots all in an effort to go from a few hundred gymnastic fans to sellout
crowds; and
WHEREAS, as attendance grew, she saw her vision realized and culminated when
the gymnastics program had its first sell-out crowd of 13,296 on March 14, 2016, for a meet
against Alabama which was about one month after the above-mentioned ribbon cutting
ceremony. On January 19, 2018, the program had a standing room only meet with 13,729
in attendance; and
WHEREAS, it is undeniable that the championship program and the world class
facility that exist today is a result of the tenacity, vision, courage and unshakeable
enthusiasm of Coach "D-D" Breaux; and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux's unwavering dedication to her mission and the university
she loves enabled her to overcome many obstacles to assist in developing a gymnastics
program whose numerous achievements in her 43 year tenure include: qualifying for 30
NCAA Championships, winning 15 Individual National Championships, winning 19
Regional titles, receiving 266 All-American Honors, receiving 160 Scholastic
All-Americans, winning 6 SEC Championships, producing 22 SEC Champions with 44 SEC

Titles; and
WHEREAS, during her last five years of coaching, the LSU Gymnastics team earned
National Championship Runners Up three times (2016, 2017, 2019); and
WHEREAS, in Coach Breaux's last three seasons, LSU won the regular season
competition and the SEC Championship (2017, 2018 and 2019); and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux has been inducted into numerous Halls of Fame
including the Louisiana Sportswriters Hall of Fame, named an SEC Trailblazer for Women's
Athletics and was the National Coach of the Year two times, SEC Coach of the Year six
times and Regional Coach of the Year nine times; and
WHEREAS, in 2018 LPB named "D-D" Breaux a Louisiana legend acknowledging
her creation of a championship team and her efforts to build a world class gymnastics'
facility; and
WHEREAS, four years after the world class facility was opened in 2016 and after
securing sellout crowds, a national powerhouse status for the LSU gymnastics program and
endowed scholarships, Coach Breaux believed the program was in a sustainable position at
LSU and retired in 2020; and
WHEREAS, in 2022, after her retirement from coaching, the LSU Alumni
Association bestowed their highest honor on Coach Breaux by naming her the LSU Alumnus
of the Year; and
WHEREAS, this legislature and governors have honored her and her teams for their
many accomplishments; and
WHEREAS, Coach Breaux continues to serve as an ambassador for the LSU
Athletics Department and volunteers her time as Chair of the Advisory Council for the
Department of Human Science and Education, member of the Ready Start Network in
Ascension Parish for Early Learning, member of the LSU College of Coast and Environment
Advisory Council, the Governor's Council on Physical Fitness, and serves on the board of
Woman's Hospital in Baton Rouge; and
WHEREAS, in 2025, ESPN chose Coach Breaux's unique and inspiring story for
their SEC Storied series describing Breaux as a "relentless trailblazer whose career mirrors
the evolution of women's collegiate athletics"; and

WHEREAS, in the film which premiered nationally on February 13, 2026, aptly
named "The Fighting Tiger", the ESPN summary states that Coach Breaux fought "against
institutional indifference, built community support, and pushed for equity as Title IX
reshaped the landscape, eventually transforming LSU from a struggling team practicing in
a cramped, outdated gym into a SEC Championship program and national powerhouse…and
answers the question, can one person unequivocally make a difference?"; and
WHEREAS, the directors of the film Lisa Lax and Nancy Stern Winters, who spent
almost a year researching, conducting interviews and filming in Louisiana in 2025, stated
that "D-D Breaux defines the ability to simply get things done. Her infectious energy is only
outshined by her engaging enthusiasm. To sustain this optimum level of passion and
purpose-filled work over four decades is beyond imagination. We are in awe."; and
WHEREAS, by naming this facility for Coach Breaux we create a permanent
reminder to anyone, especially young women, that one person can make a difference and can
overcome tremendous odds to achieve their goals.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request that Louisiana State University Board of Supervisors name the
LSU Gymnastics Center for this Louisiana legend and that the facility be named the Coach
"D-D" Breaux Gymnastics Training Center.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to Dr.
Wade Rousse, President of Louisiana State University and Lee Mallett, Chairman of the
LSU Board of Supervisors.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
