SLS 26RS-639 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE BILL NO. 348
BY SENATOR EDMONDS
MOTOR VEHICLES. Provides for third party service providers and enforcement of motor
vehicle liability security. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 33:1420.40, relative to law enforcement, to authorize local law enforcement
3 agencies to contract for administrative and logistical assistance related to motor
4 vehicle regulatory enforcement; to provide for limitations; to prohibit the delegation
5 of police powers; and to provide for related matters.
6 Section 1. R.S. 33:1420.40 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 A. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a sheriff,
8 municipal police department, or other local law enforcement agency may enter
9 into a contract with a licensed private service provider to provide
10 administrative and logistical assistance related to motor vehicle regulatory
11 enforcement, provided that a commissioned law enforcement officer has
12 initiated the enforcement action. A "service provider" for purposes of this
13 Section means any third party contractor providing services to law enforcement
14 for a city, municipality, or other local law enforcement entity.
15 B. A service provider operating pursuant to this Section may perform
16 the following functions:
17 (1) Plate processing and administrative handling of documentation.

2 compliance data using systems authorized by the office of motor vehicles.

9 required by law or by the contracting law enforcement agency.
10 C.(1) All services performed by a service provider pursuant to this
11 Section shall be performed only pursuant to documented direction of the
12 contracting law enforcement agency. After a lawful enforcement action has been
13 initiated by a commissioned law enforcement officer, a motorist may voluntarily
14 execute a written waiver consenting to:
15 (a) The voluntary surrender of the vehicle license plate by the motorist.
16 (b) The administrative handling of the license plate by a private service
17 provider.
18 (2) A waiver executed pursuant to this Subsection shall be voluntary,
19 documented in writing or electronic form, and retained in accordance with law
20 enforcement record keeping. No waiver executed pursuant to this Section shall
21 be construed to substitute for the lawful authority or actions of a commissioned
22 law enforcement officer.
23 D. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to grant police powers or
24 law enforcement authority to a service provider.

Proposed law provides for the definition of "service provider" and the functions that may be

(1) Plate processing and administrative handling of documentation.
(2) Electronic verification of motor vehicle registration, insurance, or compliance data

(3) Preparation, generation, mailing, or electronic transmission of notices of

(4) Administrative coordination with towing or storage facilities at the direction of a law

(5) Record keeping, data entry, reporting, and other tasks required by law or by the
contracting law enforcement agency.
Proposed law provides all services performed by a service provider be performed only
pursuant to documented direction of the contracting law enforcement agency. Proposed law
further provides after a lawful enforcement action has been initiated by a commissioned law
enforcement officer, a motorist may voluntarily execute a written waiver consenting to
voluntary surrender a license plate and administrative handling of the license plate by a
private service provider.
Proposed law provides a waiver be voluntary, documented in writing or electronic form, and
retained for record keeping.
