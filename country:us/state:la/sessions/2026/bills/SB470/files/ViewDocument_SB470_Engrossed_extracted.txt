2 Art. 894.6. Sentencing considerations and conditions of probation for pregnant
3 defendants
4 A. Nothing in this Article shall be construed to authorize the suspension
5 of sentence or probation for any offense for which such relief is otherwise
6 prohibited by law. This Article shall not be interpreted to expand or modify
7 eligibility for probation or suspension of sentence.
8 B. For any defendant who is pregnant at the time of sentencing and is
9 otherwise eligible for suspension of sentence or probation under existing law,
10 the court may consider the provisions of this Article.
11 C. For a defendant described in Paragraph B of this Article, the court
12 may suspend the imposition or execution of sentence and place the defendant
13 on probation in accordance with existing law, and may require, as a condition
14 of probation, that the defendant:
15 (1) Participate in and successfully complete a court-approved prenatal
16 care program, pregnancy support program, or maternal health program.
17 (2) Participate in and successfully complete a residential or outpatient
18 substance use disorder treatment program, including medically supervised
19 detoxification when appropriate.
20 (3) Comply with all recommended prenatal and postnatal medical care.
21 (4) Participate in parenting education, life skills training, or case
22 management services as deemed appropriate by the court.
23 (5) Comply with any additional conditions of probation authorized by
24 law.
25 D. In determining whether to impose a sentence pursuant to this Article,
26 the court shall consider public safety, the nature of the offense, the criminal
27 history of the defendant, and the availability of appropriate programs.
28 E. The Louisiana Department of Health shall maintain and make
29 available to courts, upon request, a list of publicly and privately available

2 Section 3.(A) The legislature hereby finds and declares that the state of Louisiana has
3 a compelling interest in protecting maternal health, promoting healthy births, and supporting

5 (B) Pregnant defendants in the criminal justice system may present unique medical

DIGEST
SB 470 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Present law allows the use of therapeutic restraints on a prisoner during childbirth.
Proposed law applies present law to prisoners during both the pregnancy and postpartum
recovery period. Proposed law also prohibits the use of shackles on a pregnant prisoner.
Proposed law otherwise retains present law.
Present law defines "postpartum".
Proposed law changes the definition of "postpartum" by extending the postpartum period and
defines "therapeutic restraint".
Proposed law requires every state and local correctional facility, during the intake process,
to offer a pregnancy test to any woman that is 55 years of age or younger who is arrested and
taken into custody. Proposed law also provides that the La. Dept. of Health will identify
funding sources for pregnancy tests and maintain certain records relating thereto.
