27 §960. Office of information and regulatory affairs
28 A. The Legislature of Louisiana makes the following findings:
29 (1) Executive branch agencies should promulgate rules only when required
30 by law, when necessary to interpret law, or when made necessary by compelling
31 public need.
32 (2) An efficient regulatory planning and review process is vital to ensure that
33 the state's regulatory system best serves the people of Louisiana.
34 (3) Coordinated review of agency rulemaking is necessary to ensure that
35 regulations are consistent with applicable law and the principles set forth in this
36 Section, and that the decisions made by one agency do not conflict with the policies
37 or actions taken or planned by another agency.
38 (4) The office of the governor is an appropriate agency to carry out the
39 review function.
40 B. The governor may establish in the office of the governor an office to be
41 known as the office of information and regulatory affairs.
42 C.(1) The office of information and regulatory affairs shall enhance planning
43 and coordination with respect to both new and existing rules, to restore the integrity
