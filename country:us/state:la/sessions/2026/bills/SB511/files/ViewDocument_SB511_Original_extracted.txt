AUTOMOBILE INSURANCE. Requires transparency of automobile repairs. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 22:1289.2, relative to transparency of automobile repairs; to prohibit insurers
3 from steering policyholders; to provide for duties of a repair shop; to provide for
4 insurer liability; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 22:1289.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §1289.2. Automobile repairs; insurers prohibition; liability transparency for
8 repair shops; insurer liability
9 A. As used in this Section, "steering" means any communication by an
10 insurer to a policyholder or third-party claimant to directly or indirectly
11 influence a policyholder or third-party claimant or provide the policyholder or
12 third-party claimant with the option to use an automobile repair shop that is a
13 preferred carve-out or is in a strategic relationship with the insurer or in which
14 the insurer maintains an ownership interest or controls or contracts with to
15 provide automobile repairs to its policyholders.
16 B. An insurer shall disclose the following to a policyholder or third-party
17 claimant:

1 (1) The right to choose his own repair shop and services.
2 (2) Preferred repair shops and services are optional and not a condition
3 of payment of a claim.
4 C. An insurer is prohibited from any of the following:
5 (1) Offering incentives or discounts to policyholders or third-party
6 claimants for using preferred shops.
7 (2) Requiring policyholders or third-party claimants to use preferred
8 repair shops or service providers.
9 (3) Steering policyholders or third-party claimants toward preferred
10 repair shops or service providers.
11 D.(1) A repair shop shall disclose when nonoriginal equipment
12 manufacturer parts (non-OEM) or nonstandard parts are used in making
13 repairs.
14 (2)(a) A repair shop shall obtain written consent from policyholders or
15 third-party claimants before using non-OEM parts or nonstandard parts in
16 making repairs.
17 (b) The written consent shall include a signed release by the policyholder
18 or third-party claimant acknowledging the risks and accepting liability for the
19 use of nonOEM or nonstandard parts in making repairs.
20 (3) If the insurer pressures the policyholder or third-party claimant into
21 accepting substandard repairs through the use of non-OEM or nonstandard
22 parts, liability for any resulting safety issues shall reside with the insurer.

DIGEST
SB 511 Original 2026 Regular Session Cathey
Proposed law provides transparency for automobile repairs.
Proposed law prohibits insurers from steering policyholders or third-party claimants toward
preferred repair shops or services.
Proposed law requires repair shops to obtain the consent of policyholders and third-party
