HLS 26RS-3252 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 52
BY REPRESENTATIVE LAFLEUR
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH: Designates April 14, 2026, as AKA Day at the state
capitol
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate Tuesday, April 14, 2026, as AKA Day at the state capitol and to commend the
3 members of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated.
4 WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, which has the distinction
5 of being the first Greek-letter sorority established by African American college women, was
6 founded at Howard University in Washington, D.C., in 1908; and
7 WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority's unending quest to be "Supreme in
8 Service to All Mankind" spans across domestic and international boundaries through the
9 service of members of more than one thousand chapters around the world; and
10 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana hosts chapters on college and university campuses
11 and active alumnae groups throughout the state; and
12 WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, is a union of over three
13 hundred thousand energetic, ambitious, and dedicated college-trained women who have
14 consciously chosen this affiliation as a means of service to promote community initiatives
15 in health preservation, preventative health education, academic excellence, civic
16 engagement, and economic empowerment; and
17 WHEREAS, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, chapters encourage
18 members to become involved and committed community volunteers in five primary service
19 target areas that actively contribute to the educational, civic, and social lives of the citizens
20 of Louisiana; and

1 WHEREAS, the current international program, Soaring to Greater Heights of Service
2 and Sisterhood, has five initiatives: Empower Our Families, Build Our Economic Wealth,
3 Enhance Our Environment, Advocate for Social Justice, and Uplift Our Local Community;
4 and
5 WHEREAS, AKA Day at the state capitol is a national program through which
6 Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority members throughout the nation meet with their legislators to
7 address issues that are pertinent to their communities; and
8 WHEREAS, it is appropriate to commend the members of Alpha Kappa Alpha
9 Sorority, Incorporated, for one hundred eighteen years of exemplary leadership and
10 outstanding community service, and to designate Tuesday, April 14, 2026, as AKA Day at
11 the state capitol.
12 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
13 designate Tuesday, April 14, 2026, as AKA Day at the state capitol; does hereby commend
14 the members of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated, and does hereby commend and
15 express enduring appreciation to the members of Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority, Incorporated,
16 for their exemplary leadership and outstanding community service and for the tremendous
17 honor and pride these women bring to their families, friends, communities, the state of
18 Louisiana, and our nation and to communities across the globe.
