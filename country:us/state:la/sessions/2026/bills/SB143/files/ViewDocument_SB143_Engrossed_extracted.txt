DIGEST
SB 143 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Edmonds
Proposed law creates the "Commission on Law Enforcement Bulletproof Vest Fund", as a
special fund, in the state treasury to be administered by the Louisiana Commission on Law
Enforcement and Administration of Criminal Justice.
Proposed law defines "commission", "eligible law enforcement agency" , "fund", and "peace
officer".
Proposed law requires monies in the fund to be invested in the same manner as monies in
the general fund. Proposed law provides that interest earned on investment of monies be
credited to the fund and requires that unexpended and unencumbered monies in the fund at
the end of the fiscal year remain in the fund.
Proposed law requires the treasurer to deposit into the fund any monies transferred, donated,
or appropriated to the fund by the legislature. The source of monies deposited into the fund
may include grants, donations, gifts, and any federal funds received for the purchase of
bulletproof vests, or any other monies that are made available from any other sources that
are collected for the purposes of proposed law.
Proposed law authorizes the legislature to appropriate monies from the fund to the
commission for any purpose authorized pursuant to proposed law.
Proposed law requires monies in the fund to be used solely to provide financial assistance
to eligible law enforcement agencies for the purchase of bulletproof vests for commissioned
peace officers.
Proposed law provides that in awarding funds, the commission must give priority to eligible
law enforcement agencies that demonstrate a lack of adequate bulletproof vests for their
commissioned peace officers.
Proposed law authorizes the commission to consider the extent to which an agency lacks
bulletproof vests, the number of officers without vests, and the agency’s financial needs
when determining awards. The commission may establish matching fund requirements or
cost-sharing provisions as it deems appropriate.
Proposed law requires the commission to adopt rules with respect to all of the following:
(1) Standards for the issuance of bulletproof vests.
(2) Application procedures and requirements.
(3) Criteria for awarding funds.

Summary of Amendments Adopted by Senate
Committee Amendments Proposed by Senate Committee on Finance to the original
bill
1. Remove provision creating the "Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections
Special Protective Equipment Fund".
2. Remove provision requiring the state treasurer, beginning July 1, 2026, and
each July first thereafter, to deposit into the fund $8,500,000 from the state
general fund.
3. Create the "Commission on Law Enforcement Bulletproof Vest Fund" and
provide for the transfer, deposit, and use of monies in the fund.
4. Authorize the legislature to appropriate monies from the fund to the
commission for any purpose authorized pursuant to proposed law.
5. Require monies in the fund to be used solely to provide financial assistance
to eligible law enforcement agencies for the purchase of bulletproof vests for
commissioned peace officers and establish funding priorities and criteria.
6. Require the commission to adopt rules.
7. Make technical changes.
