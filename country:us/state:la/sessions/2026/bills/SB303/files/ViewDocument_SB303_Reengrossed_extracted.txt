SLS 26RS-441 REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR CLOUD
PROCUREMENT CODE. Creates the "Interstate Technology Collaboration Act" to provide
for use of cooperative purchasing for information technology system procurement. (gov sig)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 39:1702(A)(1), relative to the Louisiana Procurement Code; to
3 provide for cooperative purchasing for information technology systems; to provide
4 for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 39:1702(A)(1) is hereby amended and enacted to read as follows:
7 §1702. Cooperative purchasing authorized; participation in federal General Services
8 Administration vendor list
9 A.(1) Any public procurement unit may either participate in, sponsor,
10 conduct, or administer a cooperative purchasing agreement for the acquisition of any
11 supplies, services, personal, professional, consulting, and social services,
12 information technology services and information technology systems as
13 provided in Part V-A of Chapter 1 of Subtitle I of this Title, major repairs, or
14 construction with one or more public procurement units or external procurement
15 activities or one or more private procurement units in accordance with an agreement
16 entered into between the participants. Such cooperative purchasing may include but
17 is not limited to joint or multi-party contracts between public procurement units and

1 open-ended state public procurement unit contracts which are made available to local
2 public procurement units. Such cooperative purchasing may include but is not
3 limited to:
4 (a) Joint or multi-party contracts between public procurement units.
5 (b) Open-ended state public procurement unit contracts which are made
6 available to public procurement units.
7 (c) Contracts between a public procurement unit and an external
8 procurement activity under which the public procurement unit accesses and
9 utilizes an existing information technology system belonging to the external
10 procurement activity in exchange for payment of a share of the costs associated
11 with the system. The public procurement unit shall not be required to
12 participate in a competitive procurement process for such contracts, provided
13 that the external procurement activity originally acquired the system by means
14 of a contract procured through a competitive procurement process.
15 * * *
16 Section 2. This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Interstate Technology
17 Collaboration Act".
18 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
19 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
20 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
21 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
22 effective on the day following such approval.

DIGEST
SB 303 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Cloud
Present law allows public procurement units to participate, sponsor, conduct, or administer
cooperative purchasing agreements with other entities to purchase any supplies, personal
services, professional services, consulting services, social services, major repairs, or
construction services with one or more public procurement units, external procurement
activities, or private procurement units in accordance with an agreement between the parties.
Page 2 of 3
Coding: Words which are struck through are deletions from existing law;
words in boldface type and underscored are additions.
SLS 26RS-441 REENGROSSED
SB NO. 303
Proposed law retains present law and adds information technology services and information
technology systems to the list.
Present law provides that cooperative purchasing may include but is not limited to joint or
multi-party contracts between public procurement units and open-ended state public
procurement unit contracts made available to other public procurement units.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that cooperative purchasing may include but is
not limited to contracts between a public procurement unit and an external procurement
activity allowing the public unit to access and use an existing information technology system
owned by the external procurement activity in exchange for payment of a share of system
costs.
Proposed law exempts a public procurement unit from conducting a separate competitive
procurement process for information technology services, provided the external procurement
activity originally acquired the system through a competitive procurement process.
Proposed law shall be known and may be cited as the "Interstate Technology Collaboration
Act".
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 39:1702(A)(1))
