2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 54
BY REPRESENTATIVE CHASSION
President Dr. Norman C. Francis and designates February 18, 2026, as Norman
Francis Day at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To honor the life of former Xavier University of Louisiana President Dr. Norman C. Francis
3 and designates February 18, 2026, as Norman Francis Day at the state capitol.
4 WHEREAS, Norman C. Francis was born in 1931 in Lafayette, Louisiana, and he
5 moved to New Orleans to attend Xavier University of Louisiana; and
6 WHEREAS, while studying at Xavier, Dr. Francis was elected class president during
7 each of his undergraduate years and student body president during his senior year; and
8 WHEREAS, after graduating in 1952, Dr. Francis enrolled in Loyola University
9 School of Law, where he became the school's first African American graduate in 1955;
10 shortly after earning a juris doctor, he received his draft order and enlisted in the United
11 States Army; and
12 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis was hired by Xavier University in 1957 and served as dean
13 of men, the first of his many roles with the educational institution; he served as an attorney
14 litigating civil rights cases in Louisiana prior to Brown v. Board of Education and played a
15 key role in the civil rights movement; and
16 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis was chosen to serve as the first lay president of Xavier
17 University in 1968 at the age of thirty-seven; under his leadership, the university
18 strengthened its Catholic and historically Black identity and expanded its national reputation,
19 particularly in the sciences and health professions; and
Page 1 of 3
HLS 26RS-1473 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 54
1 WHEREAS, as president, Dr. Francis led Xavier University through a period of
2 unparalleled growth, new partnerships, and prominence as one of the country's premier
3 HBCUs; today, the university is recognized as a leading producer of Black graduates who
4 go on to earn medical degrees; he retired in 2015, after forty-seven years of impactful
5 service, and his presidency stands among the longest in U.S. higher education history; and
6 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis cofounded Liberty Bank and Trust Company, one of the
7 oldest and largest Black-owned banks in the United States, to provide financial services to
8 marginalized Black communities; he also helped the city of New Orleans acquire its first
9 NFL franchise by investing in the New Orleans Saints and the development of the
10 Superdome; and
11 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis was hailed as a monumental figure in philanthropy and
12 education; he served as chair of the United Negro College Fund and held leadership roles
13 on numerous corporate, foundation, and nonprofit boards; and
14 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis was a leading voice among Catholic higher education
15 presidents and helped shape the conversation on the implications of the papal encyclical Ex
16 Corde Ecclesiae and the role of Catholic identity in American higher education; he also
17 founded the Institute for Black Catholic Studies; and
18 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis was a trusted civic leader who played a pivotal role in the
19 rebuilding of New Orleans following Hurricane Katrina; his presence helped anchor the
20 city's recovery with integrity, collaboration, and hope, and his work was an important factor
21 in his receipt of the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President George W. Bush in 2006;
22 and
23 WHEREAS, Dr. Francis deservedly received countless honors and accolades, but his
24 most lasting legacy is found in the memory of his inspiring character, which was defined by
25 quiet strength, deep faith, disciplined leadership, and steadfast belief in students; and
26 WHEREAS, Dr. Norman C. Francis led without spectacle and was guided by the
27 unshakeable conviction that excellence and ethics are inseparable; he was a truly singular
28 figure in Louisiana and American history, and his life is worthy of the highest recognition.
29 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
30 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby honor the life of former Xavier University of Louisiana
