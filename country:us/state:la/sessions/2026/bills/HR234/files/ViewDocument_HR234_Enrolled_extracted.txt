HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 234
BY REPRESENTATIVE MOORE
A RESOLUTION
To express the condolences of the House of Representatives upon the death of Dr. Johnny
Earl Drumgole, Sr.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on April 27,
2026, of Dr. Johnny Earl Drumgole, Sr.; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Drumgole was born and raised in Monroe, Louisiana, and was a
1969 graduate of Carroll High School, where he starred as a running back on the football
team; he maintained a strong connection with the city throughout his life; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Drumgole and his wife, Dr. Pamela Cage-Drumgole, began hosting
a Bible study in their home in 1979 and founded Liberty Christian Center on August 8, 1982;
and
WHEREAS, Dr. Drumgole fully transitioned into ministry in 1982, after a
distinguished career at General Motors; under his stewardship, Liberty Christian Center
enjoyed growth and stability and expanded its facilities; and
WHEREAS, outside of ministry, Dr. Drumgole was involved in local education and
social services; he founded Excelsior Christian School and advocated for youth mentorship
to challenge young men to pursue higher education and personal excellence; he held
leadership roles with the Northeast Louisiana Food Bank, Habitat for Humanity, and
numerous regional ministerial alliances; and
WHEREAS, Dr. Johnny Earl Drumgole, Sr., is survived by his wife, Dr. Pamela
Cage-Drumgole; his children, Daphne Drumgole, Jasmine Drumgole, and Johnny Drumgole,
Jr.; and his grandchildren; he will be deeply missed, but the legacy of his ministerial and
community service shall live on in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged to
have known and loved him.
