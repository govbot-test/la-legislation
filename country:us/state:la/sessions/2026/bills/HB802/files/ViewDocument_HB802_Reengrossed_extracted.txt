WATER/RESOURCES: Provides with respect to the management of watershed restoration
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 38:3310, relative to watershed restoration and conservation; to create the
3 Watershed Restoration and Conservation Fund and establish its mechanism for
4 funding; to provide for the expenditures, administration, and reporting of the fund;
5 to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 38:3310 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §3310. Watershed Restoration and Conservation Fund
9 A. There is hereby established in the state treasury as a special fund the
10 Watershed Restoration and Conservation Fund, hereafter referred to in this Section
11 as "the fund".
12 B. The purpose of the fund shall be to support the reclamation, restoration,
13 and long-term stewardship of lands and watersheds within the Amite River Basin
14 impacted by sand and gravel mining operations, with priority given to flood-prone
15 areas and impaired watersheds.
16 C. After allocation of money to the Bond Security and Redemption Fund as
17 provided in Article VII, Section 9(B) of the Constitution of Louisiana, the treasurer
18 shall deposit into the fund one hundred percent of the portion of recurring severance
19 tax revenues collected on sand severed from within the geographic boundaries of the
20 district that would have been deposited into the state general fund after allocations

HB 802 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Sawyer
Abstract: Establishes the Watershed Restoration and Conservation Fund (fund) and
provides for the deposit, use, and investment of monies in the fund. Further
establishes reporting requirements regarding deposit and use of monies in the fund.
Proposed law establishes the fund to support the reclamation, restoration, and long-term
stewardship of lands and watersheds within the Amite River Basin impacted by sand and
gravel mining operations, with priority given to flood-prone areas and impaired watersheds.
Present law provides for allocation of severance tax revenues collected on sand.
Proposed law retains present law provisions and adds requirement for the treasurer to deposit
into the fund 100% of the portion of recurring severance tax revenues collected on sand
severed from within the boundaries of the Amite River Basin Drainage and Water
Conservation District (district) that would have otherwise been deposited into the state
general fund after the allocations of revenue required by present law.

Proposed law provides that monies in the fund be invested in the same manner as monies in
the state general fund with interest earned on investment of monies in the fund credited to
the fund and any unexpended and unencumbered monies in the fund at the end of the fiscal
year remaining in the fund.
Present law authorizes the board of the Amite River Basin Commission (board) to manage
and control the watersheds within the district to establish adequate drainage, flood control,
and water resources development, including the construction of reservoirs, diversion canals,
gravity and pumped drainage systems, and other flood control works. Proposed law retains
present law.
Proposed law requires appropriation of monies in the fund to the Coastal Protection and
Restoration Authority for disbursement to the board of the district for use as provided in
present and proposed law.
Proposed law requires the board to submit an annual report detailing the receipts,
expenditures, and funded activities of the fund to the House Committee on Natural
Resources and Environment and the Senate Committee on Natural Resources.
(Adds R.S. 38:3310)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Natural Resources and
Environment to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Move proposed law from Title 30 of the Revised Statutes to Chapter 16 of Title
38 of the Revised Statutes, which is the chapter of laws for the Amite River
Basin Drainage and Water Conservation District.

Section 3:
2. Limit the purpose of the fund to address only those watersheds within the Amite
River Basin impacted by sand and gravel mining, rather than any watershed
impacted by sand and gravel mining.

Section 4:
3. Remove gravel severance as a source of revenue for the fund.

Section 5:
4. Limit severance revenue deposited into the fund to the revenue collected from
sand severed from within the geographic boundaries of the Amite River Basin
Drainage and Water Conservation District.

Section 6:
5. Remove a section of proposed law providing for entities that may be eligible to
use the fund.

Section 7:
6. Authorize the Amite River Basin Commission to use the fund for any purpose
authorized by present law regarding the Amite River Basin Drainage and Water
Conservation District.

Section 8:
7. Specify that the board of commissioners for the Amite River Basin Drainage and
Water Conservation District is responsible for submitting the report required by
proposed law.

Section 9:
8. Change the author of the bill from Representative McMakin to Representative
Sawyer.

Section 10:
9. Make technical changes.
