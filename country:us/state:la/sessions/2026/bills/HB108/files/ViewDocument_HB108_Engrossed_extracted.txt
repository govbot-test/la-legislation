HLS 26RS-725 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE COX
CRIMINAL/JURY TRIALS: Provides relative to juror qualifications
1 AN ACT
2 To enact Code of Criminal Procedure Article 401(A)(6), relative to juror qualifications; to
3 prohibit persons convicted of violent crimes and sex crimes from serving as a juror;
4 and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Article 401(A)(6) is hereby enacted to read
7 as follows:
8 Art. 401. General qualifications of jurors
9 A. In order to qualify to serve as a juror, a person shall meet all of the
10 following requirements:
11 * * *
12 (6) Not have been convicted at any time of a crime of violence as defined in
13 R.S. 14:2(B) or a sex offense as defined in R.S. 15:541(24).
14 * * *

DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part

HB 108 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Cox
Abstract: Prohibits persons convicted of a crime of violence or a sex offense from serving
as a juror.
Present law provides for the qualifications a person must meet to serve as a juror.
Proposed law provides that in order to serve as a juror, a person must never have been
convicted of a crime of violence or a sex offense.
(Adds C.Cr.P. Art. 401(A)(6))
