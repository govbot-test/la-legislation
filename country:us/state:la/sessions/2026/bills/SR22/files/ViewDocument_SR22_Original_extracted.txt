SLS 26RS-451 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 22
BY SENATOR FOIL
MATRIMONIAL REGIMES. Requests the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and
recommend legislation relative to the division of retirement benefits of a former spouse in
the partition of community property.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and recommend legislation
3 relative to the division of retirement benefits of a former spouse in the partition of
4 community property.
5 WHEREAS, R.S. 9:2801 provides for the partition of community property and
6 settlement claims arising from matrimonial regimes; and
7 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Supreme Court in Sims v. Sims, 358 So.2d 919 (La. 1978)
8 determined in a partition of community assets following the dissolution of the community
9 property regime as the result of a divorce, a divorced spouse has an interest in a former
10 spouse's pension rights, insofar as the benefits are attributable to his employment during the
11 existence of the community; and
12 WHEREAS, in Sims, the Louisiana Supreme Court held that the divorced spouse was
13 entitled to have recognized an interest in the pension funds or rights of the former spouse,
14 when the benefits become actually payable to, or for the other, in the proportion that they
15 are attributable to the former spouse's employment during the community; and
16 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Supreme Court determined that the following formula by
17 fraction, known as the "Sims Formula" is the proper formula to apply for the division of
18 retirement benefits attributable to employment during the existence of the community, the

1 numerator of which is the years of creditable service during the existence of the community,
2 divided by the denominator of which is the total years of creditable service, multiplied by
3 one-half; and
4 WHEREAS, the Sims Formula is established by jurisprudence and applies to the
5 division of retirement benefits, as the result of a divorce, in all retirement systems in the state
6 of Louisiana; and
7 WHEREAS, the Louisiana State Law Institute has the capability and expertise to
8 study and make recommendations for potential changes relative to the laws governing the
9 division of retirement benefits and applicable to the partition of community property as the
10 result of a divorce.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
12 does hereby urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and recommend
13 legislation relative to the division of retirement benefits of a former spouse in the partition
14 of community property as the result of a divorce.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
16 director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute shall submit
18 one printed copy and one electronic copy of any report produced pursuant to this Resolution
19 to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772.
