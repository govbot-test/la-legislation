HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 229
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
Taylor.
WHEREAS, Johnnie Taylor was born in Crawfordsville, Arkansas; he began singing
as a child in church and graduated to performing with gospel groups as a teenager; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor was a versatile and charismatic singer and his music blends
the sweetness of gospel with the grittiness of blues; known as "The Philosopher of Soul", he
was a prolific recording artist who delivered multiple hits during the height of his career in
the 1960s and 1970s; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released his first chart-topping R&B hit in the fall of 1968
and earned his first certified gold record and first GRAMMY nomination; in 1976, he
released the biggest hit of his career, "Disco Lady", which is distinguished as the first-ever
single to be certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released nearly thirty albums over the course of his four-
decade career and received three GRAMMY nominations; he was invited, along with James
Brown, Tony Bennett, and Dinah Shore, to perform at President Nixon's 1969 inaugural ball;
in 1999, he was honored by the Rhythm & Blues Foundation and presented with the
prestigious Pioneer Award; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released the album Good Love! with Malaco Records on
May 21, 1996; "Last Two Dollars" is a noteworthy track on the album that showcases his
incredible blend of rhythm and blues, and it is fitting to commemorate the day of its release.

HR NO. 229 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commemorate the thirtieth anniversary of the song
"Last Two Dollars" by Johnnie Taylor and does hereby extend enduring appreciation for
Mr. Taylor's many contributions to rhythm and blues and the American music industry.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
