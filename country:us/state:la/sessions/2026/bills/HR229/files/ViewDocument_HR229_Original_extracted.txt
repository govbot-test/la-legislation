HLS 26RS-1586 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 229
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
COMMENDATIONS: Commemorates the thirtieth anniversary of the song "Last Two
Dollars" by Johnnie Taylor
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commemorate the thirtieth anniversary of the song "Last Two Dollars" by Johnnie
3 Taylor.
4 WHEREAS, Johnnie Taylor was born in Crawfordsville, Arkansas; he began singing
5 as a child in church and graduated to performing with gospel groups as a teenager; and
6 WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor was a versatile and charismatic singer and his music blends
7 the sweetness of gospel with the grittiness of blues; known as "The Philosopher of Soul", he
8 was a prolific recording artist who delivered multiple hits during the height of his career in
9 the 1960s and 1970s; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released his first chart-topping R&B hit in the fall of 1968
11 and earned his first certified gold record and first GRAMMY nomination; in 1976, he
12 released the biggest hit of his career, "Disco Lady", which is distinguished as the first-ever
13 single to be certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America; and
14 WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released nearly thirty albums over the course of his four-
15 decade career and received three GRAMMY nominations; he was invited, along with James
16 Brown, Tony Bennett, and Dinah Shore, to perform at President Nixon's 1969 inaugural ball;
17 in 1999, he was honored by the Rhythm & Blues Foundation and presented with the
18 prestigious Pioneer Award; and
19 WHEREAS, Mr. Taylor released the album Good Love! with Malaco Records on
20 May 21, 1996; "Last Two Dollars" is a noteworthy track on the album that showcases his
21 incredible blend of rhythm and blues, and it is fitting to commemorate the day of its release.
