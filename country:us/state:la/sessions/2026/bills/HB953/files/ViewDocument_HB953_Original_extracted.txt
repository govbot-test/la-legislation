12 §1377. Definitions; exceptions
13 A. Master plumber. A "master plumber" is a natural person who possesses
14 the necessary qualifications and knowledge to plan and lay out plumbing systems;
15 who supervises journeyman plumbers in the installation, alteration, and/or repair of
16 plumbing systems; and who is may be licensed as such by the board.
17 B.(1) Journeyman plumber. A "journeyman plumber" is a natural person
18 who possesses the necessary qualifications and knowledge to install, alter, repair, and
19 maintain plumbing systems; is may be licensed as such by the board; is supervised
20 by a master plumber; and is in the employ of an employing entity.
21 (2) Residential plumber limited. A " residential plumber limited" is a natural
22 person who possesses the necessary qualifications and knowledge to install, alter,
23 repair, and maintain plumbing systems and is may be licensed by the board to install,
24 alter, repair, and maintain plumbing systems in one- and two-family dwellings at the
25 direction of a master plumber without the supervision of a journeyman plumber.
26 C.(1) Apprentice plumber. An "apprentice plumber" is a natural person
27 engaged in learning the plumbing trade by working under the direct, on-the-job
28 supervision of a journeyman plumber and in the employ of an employing entity.

HB 953 Original 2026 Regular Session Fontenot
Abstract: Grants concurrent authority to license plumbers to the State Board for
Contractors in addition to the authority held by the State Plumbing Board.
Present law provides the State Plumbing Board is the sole and exclusive agency with power
to license plumbers and plumbing businesses in this state.
Proposed law makes the State Plumbing Board one of the agencies with this power.
Present law provides that the State Plumbing Board adopt tests of qualifications to be
possessed by any person engaged in the duties of residential plumbers, journeyman
plumbers, master plumbers, and water protection supply specialists.
Proposed law provides that persons engaged in these duties may possess such tests of
qualifications.
Present law provides that no natural person shall engage in the work of a residential plumber
unless he holds a residential plumber limited license, with similar licensing requirement
needed for the work of journeyman plumbers, apprentices, master plumbers, and water
supply protection specialists.
Proposed law amends present law to allow a person to engage in this work with a similar or
equivalent license issued pursuant to proposed law.
Present law allows a master plumber to be licensed only if he was previously licensed as a
journeyman plumber by the State Plumbing Board.

Proposed law also allows a person to be licensed as a master plumber pursuant to provisions
of proposed law.
Present law provides no entity shall engage in the business of plumbing without the employ
of a master plumber.
Proposed law allows such a business to employ a master plumber licensed pursuant to
proposed law.
Present law provides the fees the State Plumbing Board may fix for the examination,
licensing, and renewal fees for various plumbing licenses.
Proposed law specifies that these fees may be fixed only for licenses and examinations
pursuant to present law.
Present law provides the State Plumbing Board with the power to enjoin any person from
engaging in plumbing work when it appears that person is violating provisions of present
law.
Proposed law clarifies that this power extends only to those licensed pursuant to present law.
Present law provides for the State Plumbing Board to fine any person performing plumbing
work without a license issued by the Board.
Proposed law provides that the State Plumbing Board may only fine those performing
plumbing work without any license whatsoever.
Present law provides for the licensing of journeymen who have not been previously licensed
who come under the jurisdiction of the State Plumbing Board.
Proposed law specifies that these provisions do not apply to persons who hold relevant
licenses pursuant to proposed law.
Present law provides for definitions of the various grades of plumbers, who each shall hold
a license from the State Plumbing Board to do that particular work.
Proposed law changes this requirement from mandatory to permissive.
Present law provides that the State Plumbing Board may revoke licenses from plumbers who
knowingly allow persons not licensed by that board to perform work.
Proposed law makes an exception for plumbers licensed by the State Board for Contractors.
Present law provides that plumbing licensees must provide proof of insurance to the State
Plumbing Board.
Proposed law specifies that this refers only to licenses issued pursuant to present law.
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law defines "plumber".
Present law provides that nothing in present law regarding contractors shall be presumed to
permit plumbing contractors to engage in plumbing work without licenses issued by the
State Plumbing Board.
