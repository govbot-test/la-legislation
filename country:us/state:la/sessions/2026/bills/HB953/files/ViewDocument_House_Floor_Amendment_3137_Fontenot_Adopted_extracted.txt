22 §2150. Purpose; legislative intent
23 The purpose of the legislature in enacting this Chapter is the protection of the
24 health, safety, and general welfare of all those persons dealing with persons engaged
25 in the contracting and plumbing vocation vocations, and the affording of such
26 persons of an effective and practical protection against the incompetent,
27 inexperienced, unlawful, and fraudulent acts of contractors or plumbers with whom
28 they contract. Further, the legislative intent is that the State Licensing Board for
29 Contractors shall monitor construction and plumbing projects to ensure compliance
30 with the licensure requirements of this Chapter.

32 A. There is hereby established within the board the Plumbing Contractors
33 Subcommittee, hereinafter referred to as "plumbing subcommittee", consisting of
34 the following eleven members appointed by the governor, each of whom shall be a

38 (1) Three members, each of whom shall be appointed from the membership

44 Louisiana Associated General Contractors.
45 (4) One member who shall be appointed from the membership of the
46 Associated Builders and Contractors of Louisiana.
47 (5) One member who shall be appointed from the membership of the
48 Louisiana Home Builders Association.
49 (6) Two members, each of whom shall not be associated with the plumbing
50 industry, to serve as members-at-large.
51 B.(1) The terms of office for the initial members appointed to the plumbing
52 subcommittee is three members for a three-year term, four members for a two-year
53 term, and four members for a one-year term, to be determined by the governor.
54 Thereafter, all members are to be appointed for six-year terms.
55 (2) All terms commence thirty days after appointment, and all members shall
56 serve until their successors have been appointed and qualified. Vacancies occurring
57 in the membership of the plumbing subcommittee for any reason shall be filled by
58 appointment by the governor for the unexpired term. No member appointed may
59 serve more than two consecutive six-year terms.

27 §2156.1.1. Requirements for issuance of a plumber's license; violations; penalties;
28 classifications
29 A. Any natural person who engages in any plumbing work of any kind,
30 including as an employee of a plumbing contractor, is required to possess the
31 appropriate plumber's license. Licenses for plumbers shall be issued in accordance
32 with any of the following:
33 (1) An applicant for licensure as an apprentice plumber shall register with
34 the board in a method prescribed by the board and shall semi-annually verify his
35 employment in the trade of apprentice plumber with the board in a method
36 prescribed by the board.
37 (2) An applicant for licensure as a journeyman plumber may obtain that
38 license from the board after the completion of two thousand hours of verified work
39 as an apprentice plumber, and after passing both a written examination and a
40 practical administration approved, recognized, or administered on behalf of the
41 board.
42 (3) An applicant for licensure as a master plumber may obtain that license
43 from the board after first becoming a licensed journeyman plumber, the completion
44 of one thousand hours of verified work as a licensed journeyman plumber, and after
45 successfully passing a written examination approved, recognized, or administered
46 on behalf of the board.
47 B. A person may not perform plumbing work, or act as a plumber as defined
48 in this Chapter, unless he holds an active license as a plumber in accordance with the
49 provisions of this Chapter. The board or plumbing subcommittee may revoke,
50 suspend, or refuse to renew a license; issue cease and desist orders to stop work;
51 issue fines and penalties; or debar any person licensed pursuant to the provisions of
52 this Chapter for a violation of any of the following requirements:
53 (1) An apprentice plumber shall work only under the supervision of a

28 §2164. Violations; civil penalty; jurisdiction
29 A. Any person who violates any provision of this Chapter shall, after notice
30 and a hearing, be liable to the board for a fine of up to ten percent of the total
31 contract or the value of the work bid or being performed for which there is a
32 violation. In addition to the fine, the board may impose administrative costs and
33 attorney fees for each offense. In determining the amount of the administrative
34 penalty, the board, plumbing subcommittee, or residential subcommittee shall
35 consider the circumstances and seriousness of the violation, cooperation on the part
36 of the contractor, and the history of previous violations.
37 B. A monetary penalty assessed by the board, plumbing subcommittee, or
38 the residential subcommittee is payable within ninety days or as provided by law.
39 Failure to pay an outstanding penalty may be cause to deny issuance or renewal of
40 a license or registration and may be subject to collection efforts as provided by law.
