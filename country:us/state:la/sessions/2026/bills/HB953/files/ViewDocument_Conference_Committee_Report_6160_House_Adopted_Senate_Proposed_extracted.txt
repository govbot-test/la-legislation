CONFERENCE COMMITTEE REPORT DIGEST
HB 953 2026 Regular Session Fontenot
Keyword and oneliner of the instrument as it left the House
PLUMBERS: Provides relative to plumbing licenses
Report rejects Senate amendments which would have:
1. Removed provisions of proposed law creating the Plumbing Contractors
Subcommittee within the State Licensing Board for Contractors, abolishing the State
Plumbing Board of Louisiana, and transferring all powers, duties, property, and
obligations of the plumbing board to the contractors board.
2. Increased State Plumbing Board membership from 11 members to 12 members by
removing one residential plumber limited board member and adding two licensed
contractors selected by the governor, one selected from a list of three names
submitted by the Associated Builders and Contractors of Louisiana, and one selected
from a list of three names submitted by the Louisiana Home Builders Association or
its successor, and increases the number of members that constitute a quorum from six
to seven.
3. Exempted the two licensed contractor board members from the requirement that
members be licensed by the State Plumbing Board.
4. Required the board to recognize the qualification or registration of apprentices
through enrollment in an approved training program administered by the Louisiana
Community and Technical College System (LCTCS).
5. Provided that the State Plumbing Board shall assist in developing journeyman
training programs, imposes a $100 contractor renewal fee dedicated to LCTCS
plumbing training, and established requirements for the use and annual disbursement
of LCTCS training funds.
6. Removed references to residential plumber limited licensure in proposed law and
provides that any residential plumber limited licensees holding a valid license on
August 1, 2026, shall become journeyman plumber licensees on that date by
operation of law.
7. Amended journeyman licensure apprenticeship work hour requirements to 2,000
indentured apprenticeship hours, or 2,500 unindentured apprenticeship hours, or
1,000 unindentured apprenticeship hours plus approved LCTCS training.
8. Amended requirements for master plumber licensure to 1,500 work hours as a
journeyman, completion of a 24-hour responsibility course, passage of exams, a
criminal background check, and payment of fees.
9. Authorized administrative fines of not less than $500 nor more than $5000 for
violations of proposed law, after notice and hearing. Further provided that
administrative fines imposed shall not require the filing of a consumer complaint and
shall be independent of any criminal prosecution.
10. Removed the definition of "residential limited plumber", and amended the definition
of "apprentice plumber" and "apprentice gas fitter".
11. Stated legislative intent to expand plumbing recruitment, particularly of minority
students, and required a report on minority workforce data, license issuance,
demographics, and recruitment efforts by April 12, 2027, to the House and Senate
commerce committees.

Report amends the bill to:
1. Remove from definition of "residential plumbing contractor" a provision that would
have restricted that definition to persons performing plumbing work on residences
for which the project value is $50,000 or more, and licensed by the board to do the
same.
2. Add to the membership of the State Licensing Board for Contractors one member
who is an active licensed plumber, and shall be appointed by the governor from a list
of three names submitted by the Louisiana Pipe Trades Association.
3. Amend provision providing for appointment to the Plumbing Subcommittee three
members from providing those members shall be appointed from the membership of
the United Association of Journeymen and Apprentices of the Plumbing and
Pipefitting Industry of the United States and Canada to providing that those members
be appointed from the membership of the Louisiana Pipe Trades Association.
4. Remove exemption for plumbing contractors from $100 fee assessed on each license
renewal issued to a contractor to be dedicated towards certain public universities or
community college schools of construction management or construction technology
and instead maintain present law.
5. Remove provision that would have assessed on plumbing contractors an additional
fee of $100 on each license renewal per year to be dedicated to the Louisiana
Community and Technical College System for plumbing training programs and
instead maintain present law.
6. Remove provisions allowing a master plumbing contractor or a residential plumbing
contractor to contract to supervise certain plumbing work.
7. Increase work hour requirement for journeyman plumber licensure from 2,000
verified hours to 2,500 verified hours.
8. Remove provision allowing the Plumbing Subcommittee to impose additional
licensing fees by rule promulgation.
9. Remove provision that would have directed monies collected pursuant to violations
related to plumbing or plumbing contracting to the Louisiana Community and
Technical College System to be used for plumbing training programs and instead
maintains present law regarding allocation of monies collected pursuant to
contracting violations.
Digest of the bill as proposed by the Conference Committee
Present law provides for the legislative purpose relative to the State Licensing Bd. for
Contractors.
Proposed law amends present law to provide for legislative intent of that board regarding
plumbers.
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law amends definitions of "contractor" and "plumbing contractor" and adds
definitions of "apprentice plumber license", "journeyman plumber license", "master plumber
license", "master plumbing contractor", "plumber", and "residential plumbing contractor".
Present law provides for the State Licensing Bd. for Contractors and provides for its
membership.

Proposed law adds to the membership of the State Licensing Bd. for Contractors one member
to be selected by the governor from a list of three names selected by the Louisiana Pipe
Trades Association.
Present law creates the State Plumbing Bd., provides for its domicile and its membership,
provides for the term limits, compensation, and voting of members, and provides for officers
of that board.
Proposed law abolishes the State Plumbing Bd. and creates the Plumbing Contractors
Subcommittee of the State Licensing Bd. for Contractors, provides for the membership,
terms, voting, and reimbursement of its members, and provides for the officers of that
subcommittee.
Present law provides for the powers and duties of the State Plumbing Bd., and provides that
the State Plumbing Bd. is the sole and exclusive agency with the power to license plumbers
and plumbing businesses and the power to enjoin any person from engaging in plumbing
work when it appears that the person is violating provisions of present law.
Proposed law abolishes the State Plumbing Bd. and transfers its powers to license plumbers
and plumbing businesses to the Plumbing Contractors Subcommittee of the State Licensing
Bd. for Contractors, including all of the following:
(1) To adopt rules and regulations to govern all plumbing contractors and plumbers in
this state, including all specialty classifications and sub-classifications.
(2) To issue, suspend, or revoke licenses to do plumbing or plumbing business in this
state pursuant to proposed law.
(3) To prescribe and adopt regulations and policies for continuing education for
plumbers.
(4) To enforce against, prosecute, or enjoin all persons violating plumbing or plumbing
contracting provisions of proposed law, and to issue fines and penalties for
violations.
Present law provides for licensing requirements of plumbers relative to certain kinds of work
and business, provides relative to the grades of apprentice plumber, journeyman plumber,
master plumber, residential plumber limited, medical gas and vacuum systems verifier,
medical gas piping installer, and water protection supply specialists, with enforcement power
by the State Plumbing Bd.
Proposed law repeals the licensing requirements in present law and provides for licensing of
individual plumbers in apprentice, journeyman, and master plumber license grades, and for
plumbing contractors who may offer or contract to undertake plumbing work. Proposed law
provides for any specialty classifications or sub-classifications to be promulgated by rule
issued by the Plumbing Contractors Subcommittee.
Present law provides for temporary working permits for certain plumbers from other states.
Proposed law repeals present law and provides for the Plumbing Contractors Subcommittee
to create temporary working permits by rulemaking.
Present law provides the fees the State Plumbing Board may fix for the examination,
licensing, and renewal fees for various plumbing licenses, and for fees the State Licensing
Bd. for Contractors may charge for contractor licenses.
Proposed law repeals the fees that may be issued by State Plumbing Bd. and amends present
law regarding contractors to provide that the fees charged for contractor licenses shall also
apply to plumber licenses.

Present law provides for the State Plumbing Bd. to perform inspections, assess violations,
issue injunctions, or fine any person performing plumbing work without a license issued by
that board.
Proposed law repeals the State Plumbing Bd. and provides the Plumbing Contractors
Subcommittee of the State Licensing Bd. for Contractors with power to perform inspections,
assess violations, or issue injunctions and fines.
Proposed law provides for the transfer of all powers, duties, functions, references in law or
documents, obligations, property, titles, revenues, and employees from State Plumbing Bd.
to State Licensing Bd. for Contractors on effective date of proposed law.
Provides for multiple effective dates for provisions of proposed law.
