SLS 26RS-340 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE BILL NO. 155
BY SENATOR TALBOT
HEALTH/ACC INSURANCE. Provides for coverage of certain dental care when connected
with cancer treatment. (gov sig)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 22:1077.5, relative to dental care and cancer treatment; to provide for
3 healthcare coverage for certain medically necessary dental procedures as a result of
4 cancer treatment; to clarify healthcare insurers are not required to provide coverage
5 for routine preventative dental care; to provide for applicability; to provide for an
6 effective date; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 22:1077.5 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §1077.5. Coverage for dental services for cancer patients
10 A. Except as provided in Subsection C of this Section, an insurer offering
11 a health coverage plan in this state shall provide the following coverage for
12 medically necessary dental procedures for an insured who has been diagnosed
13 with cancer:
14 (1) Fluoride treatment and dental procedures that are medically
15 necessary to reduce the risk of infection, eliminate infection, or treat tooth loss
16 or decay within thirty days prior to beginning cancer treatment, including
17 chemotherapy, biological therapy, or radiation therapy treatment.

1 (2) Dental procedures that are medically necessary to reduce the risk of
2 infection, eliminate infection, or treat tooth loss or decay that is the direct or
3 indirect result of cancer treatment, including chemotherapy, biological therapy,
4 or radiation therapy treatment.
5 (3) An obturator as medically necessary for treatment of an insured that
6 has a defective oral cavity as a direct or indirect result of cancer treatment,
7 including chemotherapy, biological therapy, or radiation therapy treatment.
8 B. The coverage required under this Subsection shall include coverage
9 for laboratory assessments, medications, and treatments.
10 C. A healthcare insurer is not required to provide coverage for routine
11 preventive dental care, including cleaning and sealants.
12 D. The provisions of this Section do not include a plan providing
13 coverage for excepted benefits as defined in R.S. 22:1061, limited benefit health
14 insurance plans, and short-term policies that have a term of less than twelve
15 months.
16 Section 2. The provisions of this Act shall apply to any new policy, contract,
17 program, or health coverage plan issued on or after January 1, 2027. Any policy, contract,
18 or plan in effect prior to January 1, 2027 shall convert to conform to the provisions of this
19 Act on or before the renewal date thereof, but in no event later than January 1, 2028.
20 Section 3. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
21 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
22 by the governor, as provided by Article II, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
23 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
24 effective on the day following such approval.

Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires healthcare insurance provide coverage for medically necessary dental
procedures for an insured diagnosed with cancer.
Proposed law requires coverage for certain procedures medically necessary to reduce or
eliminate infection or to treat tooth loss or decay prior to or as a result of cancer treatment.
Proposed law requires coverage for laboratory assessments, medications, and treatments.
Proposed law requires coverage for an obturator for treatment of an insured with a defective
oral cavity when medically necessary as a result of cancer treatment.
Proposed law clarifies that a healthcare insurer is not required to provide coverage for
routine dental care.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 22:1077.5)
