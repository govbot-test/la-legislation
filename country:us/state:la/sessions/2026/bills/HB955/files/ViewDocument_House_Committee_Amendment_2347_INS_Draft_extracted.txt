10 "Section 1. R.S. 15:571.36.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §571.36.1. Electronic monitoring provider registration
12 A. As used in this Section, the following terms have the meaning ascribed to them:
13 (1) "Commission" means the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and
14 Administration of Criminal Justice.
15 (2) "Electronic monitoring provider" means any person or entity or provider that
16 provides electronic monitoring services including but not limited to global positioning
17 system monitoring, to individuals pursuant to a court order, condition of bond, probation,
18 parole, or other lawful authority within this state.
19 B. No person or entity shall provide electronic monitoring services within this state
20 unless such person or entity is registered with the commission.
21 C. An electronic monitoring provider applying for registration shall submit an
22 application on forms provided by the commission and shall certify in writing all of the
23 following:
24 (1) The provider has reviewed and understands the provisions of R.S. 15:571.36 and
25 all applicable laws governing electronic monitoring.
26 (2) The provider agrees to comply with all statutory requirements and rules adopted
27 pursuant to this Section.
28 (3) The equipment and monitoring systems utilized by the provider meet the
29 standards and operational requirements established by law.
30 (4) The provider maintains the operational capability to monitor individuals, detect
31 violations, and report such violations in accordance with law.
32 D. The provider shall provide identifying business information, including ownership
33 and principal place of business, designate a registered agent within this state, and submit to
34 criminal background checks for owners and principal operators.
35 E. Electronic monitoring providers shall retain all monitoring records, violation
36 records, device status records, and related documentation for a minimum of five years and
37 shall make such records available upon lawful request by a court, law enforcement agency,
38 prosecutorial authority, or other authorized entity.
39 F. Electronic monitoring providers shall comply with any reporting, data-sharing, or
40 system participation requirements established by law or by rule of the commission including
41 participation in any centralized monitoring or oversight system established by the state.
42 G. The commission shall maintain the registration status of each electronic
43 monitoring provider. The commission shall deny, suspend, or revoke the registration of a
44 provider only upon receipt of official documentation any of the following:
45 (1) The provider or its owner has been convicted of, or entered a plea of guilty or
46 nolo contendere to, a felony offense or any offense directly related to fraud, public safety,
47 or the provision of electronic monitoring services.
