HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 101
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
A RESOLUTION
To commend Coca-Cola UNITED for its legacy, economic impact, and commitment to the
people and communities of Louisiana and to designate Tuesday, April 7, 2026, as
Coca-Cola Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, Coca-Cola UNITED's story in Louisiana began in 1902 in the city of
New Orleans as Crescent City Coca-Cola, soon followed by the Baton Rouge Coca-Cola
Bottling Company in 1906, both locations establishing a cornerstone for a company that
would grow to become an integral part of Louisiana's economic and cultural fabric; and
WHEREAS, Coca-Cola UNITED is a family-owned Coca-Cola bottler that has
proudly operated in Louisiana with a commitment to evolving consumer needs and
health-conscious lifestyles for more than one hundred twenty-four years; and
WHEREAS, Coca-Cola UNITED currently operates seven sales center facilities and
two production plants in Louisiana, including Alexandria, Baton Rouge, Lafayette, Lake
Charles, Monroe, New Orleans, and Shreveport; and
WHEREAS, Coca-Cola UNITED is a family-owned and locally-operated Louisiana
employer that sustains thousands of Louisiana jobs and provides meaningful employment
to more than two thousand three hundred dedicated citizens across the state; and
WHEREAS, together with The Coca-Cola Company, Coca-Cola UNITED
contributes over $937 million annually in economic impact and an additional $38 million
in purchases from local suppliers, including the Domino Sugar refinery in Chalmette,
Louisiana, which indirectly supports more than 14,730 jobs across the state; the company
invests approximately $6.5 million annually in community programs, which demonstrates
a deep commitment to community well-being; and

WHEREAS, over the past decade, Coca-Cola UNITED has demonstrated its
long-term commitment to strengthening infrastructure and ensuring continued growth in
Louisiana by investing more than two hundred eleven million dollars in capital
improvements statewide, including eighty-nine million dollars in Baton Rouge; and
WHEREAS, the Coca-Cola System continues to be a leader in sustainability by
investing in and supporting programs such as state waterway litter gitter devices, Keep
Louisiana Beautiful's "Love the Boot" initiative, and Recycle World in partnership with
Recycle Dat and the Osprey Initiative; these efforts have resulted in the collection of more
than one hundred thirty thousand pounds of Mardi Gras parade recyclables over the past two
years and more than two hundred thirty tons of plastic bottles and aluminum cans annually;
and
WHEREAS, the company has also taken a national leadership role in addressing
childhood obesity; in 2006, Coca-Cola UNITED, as part of the American Beverage
Association, partnered with the Alliance for a Healthier Generation to reduce beverage
calories in schools by ninety-four percent and voluntarily removed all full-calorie beverages
from elementary, middle, and high schools, implementing policies to support healthier
environments for children; and
WHEREAS, Coca-Cola UNITED's actions are guided by the enduring core values
of quality, excellence, integrity, and respect, which have shaped its relationships with
employees, consumers, customers, and communities and advanced initiatives in
sustainability, education, technology, and innovation.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Coca-Cola UNITED for its legacy,
economic contributions, community engagement, and efforts to promote healthier lifestyles
and address childhood obesity across Louisiana and beyond and does hereby designate
Tuesday, April 7, 2026, as Coca-Cola Day at the state capitol.
