7 §3242. Certification of commercial applicators
8 A. No person shall apply or supervise the application of any restricted use
9 pesticide as a commercial applicator unless that person has the proper certification.
10 Certification shall be issued only after the applicant has satisfactorily passed an
11 examination. The commissioner shall establish categories and subcategories for
12 certification in different areas of commercial application and shall establish specific
13 standards for each category and subcategory. Certification shall be valid for three
14 years and shall be renewed in accordance with rules adopted by the commissioner.
15 The commissioner by rule shall provide for the issuance of annual certification cards
16 every three years to coincide with the certification renewal period. Commercial
17 applicators may supervise the ground application of restricted use pesticides within
18 the commercial applicator's certification by competent uncertified individuals who
19 are under the direct supervision of the commercial applicator. Commercial
20 applicators shall not supervise the aerial application of any pesticide by any
21 uncertified person.

HB 505 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Chance Henry
Abstract: Modifies the issuance of certification cards for commercial pesticide applicators,
pesticide salespersons, and agricultural consultants to coincide with the certification
renewal periods and increases the fee for certification card renewals. Further
modifies the disposition of unexpended and unencumbered monies in the Pesticide
Fund.
Present law requires that all unexpended and unencumbered monies in the Pesticide Fund
be returned to the state general fund at the end of each fiscal year.

Proposed law modifies present law by requiring all unexpended and unencumbered monies
in the Pesticide Fund remain in the fund at the end of each fiscal year.
Present law prohibits any person without proper certification from applying or supervising
the application of restricted use pesticides as commercial applicators. Requires that
certification be issued only after an applicant passes an examination and that this
certification be renewed every three years. Further requires the commissioner to establish
certification standards and provides for the issuance of annual certification cards.
Additionally, authorizes commercial applicators to supervise ground applications by
competent uncertified individuals under their direct supervision.
Proposed law modifies present law by changing the issuance of certification cards from
annually to every three years to coincide with the certification renewal period.
Present law prohibits the sale or supervision of the sale of restricted use pesticides as a
pesticide salesperson unless one is properly certified. Requires that certification be issued
only after an applicant passes an examination and be renewed every three years. Further
requires the commissioner to provide for the issuance of annual certification cards.
Additionally, authorizes pesticide salespersons to supervise the sale of restricted use
pesticides by competent uncertified individuals under their direct supervision.
Proposed law modifies present law by changing the issuance of certification cards from
annually to every three years to coincide with the certification renewal period.
Present law prohibits any person from being employed as an agricultural consultant unless
that person has proper certification. Further requires that certification be issued only after
an applicant passes an examination and be renewed every three years. Additionally, requires
the commissioner to provide for the issuance of annual certification cards.
Proposed law modifies present law by changing the issuance of certification cards from
annually to every three years to coincide with the certification renewal period.
Present law requires a fee of $20 for issuing annual certification cards and excludes private
applicators from this requirement.
Proposed law modifies present law by increasing the fee from $20 to $60 and changing the
reference from annual certification cards to triennial certification cards.
(Amends R.S. 3:3210(B), 3242(A), 3244, 3246(A), and 3251(B)(4))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
