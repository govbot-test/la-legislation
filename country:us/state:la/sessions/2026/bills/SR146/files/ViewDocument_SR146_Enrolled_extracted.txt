2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 146
BY SENATOR MIGUEZ
A RESOLUTION
To express the sincere and heartfelt condolences of the Senate of the Legislature of
Louisiana upon the passing of Louis "Thomas" Nelson.
WHEREAS, it is with great regret that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana has
learned of the passing of Louis "Thomas" Nelson, a beloved husband, father, brother,
grandfather, and respected leader, on April 25, 2026 at the age of eighty-nine; and
WHEREAS, Thomas Nelson's life was dedicated to public service, integrity, and an
unwavering commitment to helping others; and
WHEREAS, a devoted public servant for nearly forty years, Thomas believed that
true leadership meant showing up for the community every single day, and he exemplified
that belief throughout his career, including serving for twenty-six years as a police juror,
tirelessly representing St. Martinville and the Cade area, as well as for twelve years as the
mayor of the city of St. Martinville, where he governed with steadfast leadership; and
WHEREAS, Thomas worked diligently on behalf of his constituents to establish the
Cade Water System, ensuring families in the area have access to this essential resource for
years to come, and to build and maintain the Cade Community Center, which continues to
serve as a hub for community life; and
WHEREAS, beyond his elected service, Thomas dedicated over forty years to bus
driving for the St. Martin Parish School Board, touching the lives of countless students with
his kindness, dedication, and care, and he also owned and operated Nelson's Lawnmower
Repair Shop and Service Station, continuing to serve the people of his beloved community;
and
WHEREAS, Thomas was leader, trusted friend, and pillar of the community, where
he was known for his kind heart and generous spirit, creating a lasting impact for generations
to come; and

WHEREAS, he is survived by his wife of sixty-seven years, Ursula St. Andre'
Nelson; daughters, Francisca Taylor (Carrol), Lisa Nelson, Vonda LeBlanc, and Sherri Sigue
(Lurry); brothers, Lloyd Nelson, John Nelson, and McKinley Nelson (Nam); and a host of
grandchildren and great-grandchildren; and
WHEREAS, Louis "Thomas" Nelson lived his life with dignity and humility, always
placing family, faith, and service above all else, and his legacy will live on in the countless
lives he touched with his wisdom and kindness as well as in the strong and vibrant
community he helped build.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby express sincere and heartfelt condolences to the family of Louis "Thomas"
Nelson upon his passing and does hereby commend his dedication to public service and his
many accomplishments.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
family of Louis "Thomas" Nelson.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
