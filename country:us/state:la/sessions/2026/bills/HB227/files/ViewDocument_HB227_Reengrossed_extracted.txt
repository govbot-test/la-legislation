HB 227 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Ventrella
Abstract: Permits court filings to clerks of court on letter size paper.
Present law authorizes paper or electronic filings of pleadings, documents, and exhibits.
Proposed law retains present law and provides that any pleading or document filed
electronically or in paper form is acceptable on legal or letter size paper.
Present law prohibits the following private information in such filings:
(1) First five digits of any social security number.
(2) Tax identification numbers.
(3) State identification numbers.
(4) Driver's license numbers.
(5) Financial account numbers.
(6) Full dates of birth.
(7) Any information protected from disclosure by state or federal law.
Proposed law generally retains present law, but removes full dates of birth from the list of
prohibited information in both civil and criminal court matters.
(Amends C.C.P. Art. 253(B)(2) and C.Cr.P. Art. 14.1(B))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Remove full dates of birth from the list of prohibited information in both civil
and criminal court matters.
