HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 119
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
Nancy Metayer of Coral Springs, Florida.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the tragic passing of Nancy
Metayer; and
WHEREAS, Nancy Metayer was a distinguished public servant who became the first
Black and Haitian-American female commissioner of Coral Springs, Florida; she was elected
in 2020 and reelected in 2024, and on November 13, 2025, was appointed by her fellow
commissioners to serve a one-year term as the city's vice mayor; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer held a bachelor of science degree from Florida
Agricultural and Mechanical University and a master of health science degree from Johns
Hopkins University; and
WHEREAS, prior to serving on the Coral Springs City Commission, Ms. Metayer
led environmental justice efforts across the state of Florida and focused on enhancing
community resilience; as an environmental scientist and former member of the Broward
County Soil and Water Conservation District, she applied her strategic expertise across
government agencies and nonprofit organizations; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer's work was instrumental in addressing water crises and
responding to major natural disasters, including Hurricanes Irma, Michael, and Dorian; she
was deeply committed to improving environmental conditions, water quality, sanitation, and
public health, all of which were reflected by her extensive professional experience; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer envisioned making Coral Springs a leader in sustainability
and resilience, and she was dedicated to supporting small businesses and exploring
opportunities in solar manufacturing, technology hubs, and other local industries; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer was guided by her core values of community service,
philanthropy, and stewardship, and she sought to amplify the voices of all citizens and work
towards a better future for Coral Springs; and

WHEREAS, in addition to Ms. Metayer's responsibilities as vice mayor, she served
as a commission liaison to several committees, including the Accessibility Advisory Group,
Affordable Housing Advisory Committee, Charter School Advisory Board and Board of
Directors, Environmental Sustainability Committee, Solid Waste Authority, Multicultural
Advisory Committee, Broward League of Cities, and the Broward County Climate Change
Task Force; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer was a member of the Utilities, Natural Resources, and
Public Works Committee, which is a Florida League of Cities legislative committee, and an
executive board member for Avanse Ansanm and Friends of the Earth, and she was
appointed to the Florida League of Cities' 2025-2026 Federal Action Strike Team; she also
served on the board of directors for ICLEI USA, which is a non-governmental organization
that promotes sustainable development, and later transitioned to the role of senior equity
officer to further support the organization's mission; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Metayer received numerous awards and honors in recognition of
her outstanding public service career; she was listed on the Haitian American Chamber of
Commerce's Top 20 Under 40, received the United Nations Clean Water Advocacy Award,
and was inducted into the CLEO Institute Leadership Circle; and
WHEREAS, Nancy Metayer was an inspiring woman and trailblazing public servant;
although her life was tragically cut short, her outstanding professional achievements and
extensive legacy of dedicated public service shall live in perpetuity in the hearts and minds
of those who were privileged to have known, loved, and served alongside her.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Nancy
Metayer; does hereby recognize her trailblazing achievement as the first Black and Haitian-
American female commissioner of Coral Springs; and does hereby extend sincere wishes
that her family, friends, colleagues, and loved ones find comfort in their shared memories
of her beautiful life.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
