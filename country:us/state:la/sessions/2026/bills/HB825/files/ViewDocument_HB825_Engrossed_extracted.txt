1 §1346.2. Requirement to offer stated Stated value policy offer
2 A. Insurers offering residential property policies within this state may offer
3 a stated value policy option to homeowners upon the request of the homeowner or
4 his authorized agent, allowing for the insurance coverage to be based on a stated

6 rather than solely on the market value of the property the replacement cost value of

9 provided in Subsection A of this Section shall prominently disclose stated value
10 policy offerings in all insurance policy proposals, agreements, and renewal
11 documents provided to consumers.

13 A. A homeowner electing a stated value policy shall submit to his insurer a
14 written payoff statement from the financial institution or any other entity holding a
15 mortgage or lien on the property. This statement shall accurately reflect the
16 outstanding balance or the nature of the mortgage or lien on the homeowner's
17 property at the time the homeowner applies for or renews an insurance policy. In
18 addition to the financial statement required in this Subsection, a homeowner shall
19 also provide to his insurer a mortgage certificate from the clerk of court indicating
20 the presence or absence of a mortgage on the property.

HB 825 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Braud
Abstract: Amends the Stated Value Homeowner's Policy Act.
Proposed law redefines "stated value homeowner's policy" as a residential insurance policy
under which the insured may declare a stated value for the insured residential property,
which is agreed upon by the insurer as the amount of insurance coverage, irrespective of the
replacement cost value of the property.
Present law permits insurance coverage to be based on a stated value of the residential
property as declared by the homeowner or authorized agent, rather than solely on the market
value of the property. Proposed law permits insurance coverage to be based on a stated
value of the residential property as declared by the homeowner or authorized agent, rather
than the replacement cost value of the property.
Proposed law removes present law that an insurer is required to prominently disclose stated
value policy offerings in all insurance policy proposals, agreements, and renewal documents
provided to consumers.
Present law provides that a homeowner electing a stated value homeowner's policy shall
submit to his insurer a written payoff statement from the financial institution or any other
entity holding a mortgage on the property. Proposed law adds that a homeowner electing
a stated value homeowner's policy shall submit to his insurer a written payoff statement from
the financial institution or any other entity holding a mortgage or lien on the property.
Proposed law requires that prior to issuing any stated value policy, the insurer shall obtain
a statement signed by insureds which contains the notice language within the bill and
boldfaced type no smaller than 18-point font.
