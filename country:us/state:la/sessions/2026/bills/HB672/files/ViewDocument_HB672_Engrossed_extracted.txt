8 Section 1. Part VI of Chapter 3 of Title 51 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
9 comprised of R.S. 51:971 through 972, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 PART VI. BOLSTERING BRICK MANUFACTURING IN LOUISIANA ACT
11 §971. Findings; purpose
12 A. The legislature finds that brick manufacturing has been integral to this
13 state's economy since its earliest days, yet this state currently ranks thirty-third
14 nationally in clay brick production despite possessing substantial clay deposits,
15 favorable natural gas pricing, superior port and rail infrastructure, and an
16 experienced manufacturing workforce. The legislature declares that expanding this
17 industry would create quality manufacturing jobs, increase supply of and demand for
18 locally-sourced building materials, and position this state to capture a greater share
19 of regional and national markets.

1 B. It is the purpose of this Act to bolster the establishment of brick
2 manufacturing facilities in this state by reducing regulatory barriers, lowering entry
3 costs, and providing targeted support to qualifying enterprises, thereby enabling this
4 state to leverage its natural resources and infrastructure for the benefit of local
5 workers and this state's economic competitiveness.
6 §972. Brick manufacturing; definitions; priority industry; rail and port access
7 A. Louisiana Economic Development may support the recruitment, retention,
8 and expansion of brick manufacturing facilities in this state within existing statutory
9 authority and subject to the availability of funds.
10 B. In accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act, Louisiana
11 Economic Development may develop policies, guidance, or program criteria
12 consistent with existing economic development programs, when appropriate and
13 within existing authority, including but not limited to the following:
14 (1) Identifying sites that possess characteristics suitable for brick
15 manufacturing operations, such as proximity to clay deposits, for pre-certification.
16 (2) Establishing specialized workforce development and training initiatives
17 for brick manufacturing.
18 (3) Developing job creation and hiring initiatives for brick manufacturing
19 enterprises.
20 (4) Providing clear regulatory pathways for the establishment of brick
21 manufacturing facilities throughout this state.
22 (5) Coordinating with ports, rail providers, and local governments regarding
23 infrastructure needs associated with brick manufacturing projects.
24 (6) Other lawful economic development activities consistent with this Part
25 and existing programs.

Abstract: Permits La. Economic Development to support recruitment, retention, and
expansion of brick manufacturing facilities in this state within existing authority and
subject to the availability of funds.
Proposed law provides legislative findings and purpose.
Proposed law permits La. Economic Development to support recruitment, retention, and
expansion of brick manufacturing facilities in this state within existing authority and subject
to the availability of funds, and to develop policies consistent with existing economic
development programs, when appropriate and within existing authority, including but not
limited to the following:
(1) Identifying sites that possess characteristics suitable for brick manufacturing
operations, such as proximity to clay deposits, for pre-certification.
(2) Establishing specialized workforce development and training initiatives for brick
manufacturing.
(3) Developing job creation and hiring initiatives for brick manufacturing enterprises.
(4) Providing clear regulatory pathways for the establishment of brick manufacturing
facilities throughout this state.
(5) Coordinating with ports, rail providers, and local governments regarding
infrastructure needs associated with brick manufacturing projects.
(6) Other lawful economic development activities consistent with proposed law and
existing programs.
(Adds R.S. 51:971-972)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Commerce to the
original bill:
1. Make technical changes.
2. Amend proposed law mandating that La. Economic Development (LED)
prioritize brick manufacturing to permitting LED to support recruitment,
retention, and expansion of brick manufacturing facilities in this state within
existing authority and subject to the availability of funds, and to develop policies
consistent with existing economic development programs, when appropriate and
within existing authority.
3. Amend proposed law directing LED to promulgate related rules and regulations,
including prioritizing brick manufacturing enterprises for access to this state's
rail lines and port facilities and any and all other related economic development
initiatives, to permitting LED to develop policies, guidance, or program criteria,
