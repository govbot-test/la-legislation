HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 107
BY REPRESENTATIVE JACKSON
A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health (LDH) to require enhanced
reporting and legislative oversight of the Medicaid Care Incentive Payment program.
WHEREAS, the Medicaid Care Incentive Payment (MCIP) program is designed to
improve healthcare quality and outcomes through performance-based incentive payments
to participating providers; and
WHEREAS, the program utilizes public funds and involves multiple entities,
including managed care organizations and quality networks, necessitating clear and ongoing
accountability; and
WHEREAS, the legislature has a compelling interest in ensuring that incentive
payments are utilized in a manner that achieves measurable improvements in healthcare
quality and patient outcomes; and
WHEREAS, current information available to the legislature regarding the
performance and financial activity of the MCIP program is limited and often delayed; and
WHEREAS, routine reporting will allow the legislature to better evaluate program
effectiveness and make informed policy decisions in a timely manner; and
WHEREAS, periodic legislative briefings will further strengthen oversight and
ensure accountability of all entities participating in the MCIP program.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to require the preparation and
submission of biannual MCIP reports.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that such reports shall be prepared separately by each
Quality Improvement Network (QN), the Louisiana Quality Network (LQN), and each
Medicaid managed care organization (MCO) participating in the MCIP program in this state.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each report shall be submitted to LDH and
transmitted by LDH to the House and Senate committees on health and welfare, the Joint
Legislative Committee on the Budget, and the David R. Poynter Legislative Research
Library.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each report shall include, at a minimum, all of
the following:
(1) The total amount of incentive payments received, distributed, retained, or
recaptured through the MCIP program.
(2) Identification of participating hospital networks and participating hospitals.
(3) Performance results for MCIP measures, including baseline performance,
established targets, and current results on all initiatives.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the purpose of this Resolution is to ensure that
the legislature receives timely, consistent, and comprehensive information regarding the
performance of the MCIP program and the use of incentive funds, thereby enhancing
oversight between formal audit cycles.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health.
