1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as "Anti-Hunger Day" in Louisiana.
3 WHEREAS, hunger and food insecurity affect approximately 825,000 Louisiana
4 residents, impacting individuals and families regardless of age, race, or background; and
5 WHEREAS, Louisiana had one of the country's highest rates of child poverty in 2024
6 with almost 25% of Louisiana's children experiencing food insecurity; and
7 WHEREAS, childhood hunger is of particular concern for the many children who
8 face daily challenges in accessing nutritious meals needed for healthy development and
9 education; and
10 WHEREAS, senior hunger is a serious concern as many elderly citizens face the
11 choice of buying food, medicine, or paying for housing; and
12 WHEREAS, Louisiana's rate of food insecurity among seniors is 14%, close to the
13 highest in the nation; and
14 WHEREAS, food insecurity is shown to increase serious, lifelong health
15 complications in children and adults; and
16 WHEREAS, Feeding Louisiana, the association of the state's five regional food
17 banks including Food Bank of Northwest Louisiana, Food Bank of Northeast Louisiana,
18 Food Bank of Central Louisiana, Greater Baton Rouge Food Bank, and Second Harvest Food

1 Bank, has demonstrated outstanding leadership in the fight against hunger through advocacy,
2 resource procurement, and partnership development; and
3 WHEREAS, Feeding Louisiana collaborates with agencies and individuals across the
4 state to distribute food, support nutrition programs, and advocate for policies that reduce
5 hunger and improve food security; and
6 WHEREAS, Feeding Louisiana and the regional food banks serve as critical lifelines
7 for Louisiana families facing increased food insecurity during federal funding lapses and
8 disasters by providing nourishing food to residents in all sixty-four parishes; and
9 WHEREAS, Feeding Louisiana places dedicated Supplemental Nutrition Assistance
10 Program (SNAP) Outreach Coordinators at each of the regional food banks who help
11 residents understand how SNAP works, determine eligibility, and complete applications and
12 recertifications; and
13 WHEREAS, the efforts of Feeding Louisiana and its partners are vital in addressing
14 both the symptoms and the root causes of hunger and make a tangible difference in the lives
15 of countless Louisianians; and
16 WHEREAS, the need for food and support in our communities continues to grow
17 despite the significant contributions of Feeding Louisiana and its affiliated food banks and
18 charitable entities, local organizations, charities, schools, and community groups; and
19 WHEREAS, Feeding Louisiana works together with these groups to bring statewide
20 awareness, collaboration, and action to address the issue of food insecurity; and
21 WHEREAS, the declaration of "Anti-Hunger Day" in Louisiana serves as a powerful
22 statement of the collective commitment to end hunger, raise awareness about the issue of
23 food insecurity, and strengthen the bonds of community and compassion that define our
24 state.
25 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
26 designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as "Anti-Hunger Day" in Louisiana and extends heartfelt
27 gratitude to Feeding Louisiana for its leadership and tireless efforts in the fight against
28 hunger.
