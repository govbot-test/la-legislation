SLS 26RS-133 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 30
BY SENATOR MORRIS
CONDOLENCES. Expresses condolences upon the death of former district attorney, judge,
and Louisiana State Senator Robert W. "Bob" Kostelka.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the sincere and heartfelt condolences of the Senate of the Legislature of
3 Louisiana upon the death of the Honorable Robert W. "Bob" Kostelka, former
4 district attorney, judge, and Louisiana state senator, and to commend his legacy of
5 many years of dedicated public service.
6 WHEREAS, the Honorable Robert W. "Bob" Kostelka passed away peacefully on
7 the morning of January 10, 2026, aged ninety-two, surrounded by love and leaving behind
8 the legacy of a life well-lived; and
9 WHEREAS, Bob was a devoted husband, father, grandfather, and great-grandfather,
10 a respected jurist and public servant, and a man whose strength, integrity, and warmth left
11 a lasting mark on all who knew him; and
12 WHEREAS, he was a gentleman of abundant grace and civic acumen who was
13 guided throughout his life by a deep sense of duty, fairness, and commitment to doing what
14 was right; and
15 WHEREAS, Bob was born in Shreveport, Louisiana, attended Centenary College,
16 matriculated to Louisiana State University, and earned his Juris Doctor degree from the
17 Louisiana State University School of Law in 1957; and
18 WHEREAS, he dedicated much of his life to public service, as the district attorney

1 and district court judge for the 4th Judicial District Court for Ouachita and Morehouse
2 parishes, as a judge on the Louisiana 2nd Circuit Court of Appeal from 1998 to 2003, and
3 ultimately as a Louisiana state senator from 2003 to 2016, possessing perhaps the most
4 comprehensive legal background of any legislator; and
5 WHEREAS, utilizing his expertise and interests, Senator Kostelka garnered
6 assignments to the Committee on Senate and Governmental Affairs as chairman, the
7 Committee on Judiciary C as chairman, the Committee on Judiciary A as vice chairman, and
8 seats on the committees on Labor and Industrial Relations, Revenue and Fiscal Affairs,
9 Transportation, Highways and Public Works, and Retirement, as well as the Select
10 Committee on Vocational and Technical Education; and
11 WHEREAS, in addition to his keen interest in governmental affairs and criminal
12 procedure, he authored legislation that addressed child support, consumer protection, health
13 care, election reform, education, courts and judicial funding, taxation, and governmental
14 ethics for public officials and employees; and
15 WHEREAS, his integrity, legal wisdom, and principled leadership earned Bob a
16 nomination by President George H. W. Bush to the United States District Court for the
17 Western District of Louisiana in 1992, reflecting the deep respect he commanded throughout
18 his career; and
19 WHEREAS, he was inducted into the Louisiana Political Hall of Fame in 2022,
20 honored for his life and career of service by his peers; and
21 WHEREAS, Bob was affiliated with numerous professional associations, including
22 Phi Delta Phi Legal Fraternity, the American Bar Association, the Association of Appellate
23 Judges, the Louisiana State Bar Association, the Fourth District Bar Association, and the
24 Louisiana State Law Institute; and
25 WHEREAS, beyond his professional accomplishments, he was, above all else, a
26 family man who remained steadfastly the "man of the house," a protector and provider
27 whose presence brought a sense of security, steadiness, and love; and
28 WHEREAS, at home in Monroe, numerous civic and philanthropic organizations
29 benefitted from Bob's active participation, including the Salvation Army as a three-time
30 chairman, for which he received the Citizen of the Year Award; Rotary International,

1 District 6190, as board member and treasurer; the Boy Scouts of America as scout master
2 and district chairman; the YMCA; and the Arthritis Foundation and Muscular Dystrophy
3 Association, acting as past campaign chairman for both nonprofits; and
4 WHEREAS, as a faithful member of the First Presbyterian Church of Monroe, he
5 served as a witness to the power of God's grace, as an elder of the church, and as a Sunday
6 school teacher; and
7 WHEREAS, Bob found deep joy in travel, especially journeys out West, where the
8 mountains and open skies of Montana held a special place in his heart and reflected his own
9 spirit of grounded and enduring strength; and
10 WHEREAS, he was a warm and devoted husband and father, who led by example
11 and instilled values of integrity, hard work, and faithfulness in his family; and
12 WHEREAS, Bob was married to the former Felicia Marie Danna, his helpmate in
13 all things, who accompanied him to the Louisiana State Capitol during legislative sessions
14 and served as his guiding light, as he served as her rock; inseparable sweethearts always; and
15 WHEREAS, he is survived by his beloved wife, Felicia; his children: Kathryn
16 Lemoine, William "Bill" Kostelka (Donna), Carol Gros (Terry), and Claire Texada (Chase);
17 his grandchildren: Evan Kostelka (Caroline), Katie Collandreau (Pierre), and Collins Marie
18 Texada; his great-grandchildren: Cooper, Molly, Carter, Eleanore, and Madeleine; and many
19 extended family members and friends who will miss him deeply and remember him always;
20 and was preceded in death by his first wife, Bobbie, son, Cliff, sister, Billie, and countless
21 friends and associates; and
22 WHEREAS, Bob leaves behind a remarkable legacy, not only in the justice he upheld
23 and the service he gave, but in the lives he touched at home and beyond, and his family and
24 friends will forever cherish his wisdom, steady guidance, stories, and the love that anchored
25 them through generations; and
26 WHEREAS, his family can take comfort in knowing that he lived a full and
27 honorable life, that he now rests in peace, reunited with those who went before him, and that
28 his legacy will continue to live on through the family he loved, the values he passed down,
29 and the countless lives he influenced through his service and example.
30 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
