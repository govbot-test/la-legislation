HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 354
BY REPRESENTATIVE DESHOTEL
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Public Service Commission to study the feasibility and potential
benefits of incorporating Louisiana One Call and 811 services into a dedicated state
division or office for the purpose of improving underground utility coordination,
mapping, damage prevention, public safety, and infrastructure planning, and to report
its findings to the legislature.
WHEREAS, Louisiana's underground infrastructure network continues to expand
rapidly due to broadband deployment, water and sewer improvements, electric and gas
infrastructure upgrades, and transportation projects; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Underground Utilities and Facilities Damage Prevention
Law, R.S. 40:1749.11 et seq., establishes requirements for notification and coordination
prior to excavation activities in order to protect underground facilities and public safety; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana One Call, commonly known through the 811 system, plays
a critical role in facilitating communication between excavators and utility operators to
reduce damage to underground infrastructure; and
WHEREAS, increased infrastructure activity across the state has highlighted the
growing importance of utility coordination, accurate locating, mapping, and enforcement
efforts; and
WHEREAS, damage to underground utilities can result in disruptions to essential
services, public safety risks, increased costs to taxpayers and utilities, and delays to critical
infrastructure projects; and
WHEREAS, the state may benefit from evaluating whether Louisiana One Call and
related 811 coordination functions should be incorporated into a dedicated division, office,
or enhanced state structure to improve oversight, coordination, data sharing, enforcement,
training, and long-term planning; and

WHEREAS, a study could help identify opportunities for improved efficiency,
enhanced protection of underground infrastructure, better coordination between state and
local entities, and potential integration with broadband deployment and utility mapping
initiatives.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Public Service Commission to
study the feasibility and advisability of incorporating Louisiana One Call and 811-related
functions into a dedicated state division, office, or administrative structure.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Public Service Commission shall coordinate
with and receive input from the following entities to assist in the study:
(1) The Department of Transportation and Development.
(2) The office of broadband, development and connectivity.
(3) Louisiana One Call.
(4) Utility providers.
(5) Local governments.
(6) Other relevant stakeholders.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include but not be limited to the
following:
(1) The current structure, governance, and funding of Louisiana One Call and the
811 system.
(2) The feasibility of creating a dedicated division or office focused on underground
utility coordination, damage prevention, utility mapping, and excavation safety.
(3) Potential improvements in coordination between utilities, excavators, state
agencies, local governments, and broadband providers.
(4) Opportunities for enhanced enforcement, data collection, reporting, and
accountability related to underground utility damages.
(5) The potential integration of underground utility mapping initiatives and digital
infrastructure coordination efforts.
(6) Best practices from other states regarding the administration and oversight of 811
systems and underground utility protection programs.
