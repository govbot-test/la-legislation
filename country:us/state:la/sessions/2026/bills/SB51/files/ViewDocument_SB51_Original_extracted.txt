SLS 26RS-200 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR KLEINPETER
CRIME/PUNISHMENT. Creates the crime of fraudulent representation of military service
or awards. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 14:67.30, relative to misappropriation without violence; to create the crime
3 of fraudulent representation of military service or awards; to provide for elements
4 of the crime; to provide for definitions and penalties; and to provide for related
5 matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 14:67.30 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §67.30. Fraudulent representation of military service or awards
9 A.(1) A person commits fraudulent representation of military service or
10 awards when he makes a false representation that he has received or otherwise
11 holds a military service record or award that was either revoked or not granted
12 to, achieved by, assigned to, or earned by the person in order to injure, defraud,
13 obtain economic gain, advantage, benefit, or secure any privilege for himself or
14 another.
15 (2) Whoever commits the crime of fraudulent representation of military
16 service or awards shall be fined not more than one thousand dollars, or
17 imprisoned for not more than six months, or both.

1 B. Whoever commits the crime of fraudulent representation of military
2 service or awards with regard to any of the following military awards or honors
3 shall be fined not more than five thousand dollars, imprisoned with or without
4 hard labor for not more than one year, or both:
5 (1) Congressional Medal of Honor as defined in 18 U.S.C. 704(c)(2).
6 (2) Distinguished-service cross awarded under 10 U.S.C. 7272.
7 (3) Navy cross awarded under 10 U.S.C. 8292.
8 (4) Air Force cross awarded under 10 U.S.C. 9272.
9 (5) Silver star awarded under 10 U.S.C. 7276, 8294, or 9276.
10 (6) Purple Heart awarded under 10 U.S.C. 1129.
11 (7) A replacement of or duplicate of any of the awards listed in
12 Paragraphs (1) through (6) of this Subsection.
13 C. Whoever commits the crime of fraudulent representation of military
14 service or awards when the fraudulent representation results in the issuance of
15 a benefit reserved to active duty service members, veterans, or the family
16 members of active duty service members or veterans shall be fined not more
17 than ten thousand dollars, imprisoned with or without hard labor for not more
18 than five years, or both. In addition, the offender shall be required to pay full
19 restitution of the benefit received in violation of this Subsection.
20 D. As used in this Section:
21 (1) "Award" means any medal, service ribbon, ribbon device, badge,
22 rank insignia, or other honor awarded by the armed services or reserve forces
23 of the United States or the national guard of any state.
24 (2) "Service record" means the actual record compiled and maintained
25 by the armed services or reserve forces of the United States or the national
26 guard of any state that documents the service rank, deeds, achievements,
27 qualifications, or actions performed by a person during his time in service.
28 (3) "Veteran" means a person who has served in the armed services or
29 reserve forces of the United States or the national guard of any state.

DIGEST
SB 51 Original 2026 Regular Session Kleinpeter
Proposed law creates the crime of fraudulent representation of military service or awards and
makes it unlawful for a person to make a false representation that he has received or
otherwise holds a military service record or award that was either revoked or not granted,
achieved, assigned, or earned in order to injure, defraud, obtain economic gain, advantage,
benefit, or secure any privilege for himself or another.
Proposed law provides that a person who commits the crime of fraudulent representation of
military service or awards will be fined not more than $1,000, or imprisoned for not more
than six months, or both.
Proposed law provides that whoever commits the crime of fraudulent representation of
military service or awards with regard to any of the following military awards or honors will
be fined up to $5,000, imprisoned with or without hard labor for up to one year, or both:
(1) Congressional Medal of Honor.
(2) Distinguished-service cross.
(3) Navy cross.
(4) Air Force cross.
(5) Silver star.
(6) Purple Heart.
(7) A replacement or duplicate of any of these awards.
Proposed law provides that whoever commits the crime of fraudulent representation of
military service or awards when the fraudulent representation results in the issuance of a
benefit reserved to active duty service members, veterans, or the family members of active
duty service members or veterans will be fined up to $10,000, imprisoned with or without
hard labor for not more than five years, or both. Proposed law further provides that the
offender must pay full restitution of the benefit received in violation of proposed law.
Proposed law provides that:
(1) "Award" means any medal, service ribbon, ribbon device, badge, rank insignia, or
other honor awarded by the U.S. armed forces, reserve forces of the U.S., or the
national guard forces of any state.
(2) "Service record" means the actual record compiled and maintained by the U.S. armed
forces, reserve forces of the U.S., or the national guard forces of any state, that
documents the service rank, deeds, achievements, qualifications, or actions
performed by a person during his time in service.
(3) "Veteran" means a person who has served in the U.S. armed forces, reserve forces
of the U.S., or the national guard forces of any state.
