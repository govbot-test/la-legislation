ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 239
BY REPRESENTATIVE BOYD
A RESOLUTION
commend The Links, Incorporated, and its seven chapters in Louisiana for their
dedicated service and lasting contributions to the state and its citizens.
WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated, is an international, not-for-profit corporation
that was established in 1946 and is committed to enriching, sustaining, and ensuring the
cultural and economic survival of African Americans and other persons of African ancestry;
and
WHEREAS, the organization is one of the nation's oldest and largest volunteer
service organizations of women who are committed to enriching the lives of others through
a strong focus on community service and civic engagement; and
WHEREAS, the mission of The Links, Incorporated, is to promote and engage in
educational, civic, and intercultural activities that enrich the lives of members and the larger
community through five program facets: Services to Youth, the Arts, National Trends and
Services, International Trends and Services, and Health and Human Services; and
WHEREAS, The Links, Incorporated, comprises seventeen thousand professional
women of African descent in two hundred ninety-nine chapters located in forty-one states,
the District of Columbia, the Commonwealth of the Bahamas, and the United Kingdom;
there are seven chapters in the great state of Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, the seven Louisiana-based chapters belong to the Southern Area of The
Links, Incorporated, and include the Baton Rouge Chapter, Crescent City Chapter, La
Capitale Chapter, Monroe-Grambling Chapter, New Orleans Chapter, Pontchartrain Chapter,
and Shreveport Chapter; they represent decades of leadership and service across the state;
and
Page 1 of 2

WHEREAS, the volunteerism and impactful programming of the Louisiana chapters
have significantly contributed to the advancement of education, health equity, cultural
preservation, and community development in the state; and
WHEREAS, the observance of Links Day at the Capitol in each state is a key
initiative aimed at educating, empowering, and engaging members in the legislative process
while advocating for public policy that improves the well-being of the communities they
serve; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Links Day at the Capitol offers an opportunity for
members to engage with lawmakers, highlight issues critical to underserved communities,
and reinforce the importance of civic involvement and public service.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, May 6, 2026, as Louisiana
Links Day at the Capitol; does hereby commend The Links, Incorporated, for its exemplary
service; and does hereby applaud the seven Louisiana chapters of the organization for their
enduring commitment to uplifting communities across the state.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
