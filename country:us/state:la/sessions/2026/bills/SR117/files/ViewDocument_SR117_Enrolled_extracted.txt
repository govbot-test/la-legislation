2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 117
BY SENATOR KLEINPETER
A RESOLUTION
Division II Non-Select State Championship.
WHEREAS, the Brusly High School Panthers boys basketball team has achieved the
highest level of success in Louisiana high school athletics by capturing the 2026 Louisiana
High School Athletic Association (LHSAA) Division II Non-Select State Championship
title; and
WHEREAS, the Panthers secured the championship with a hard-fought 45-38 victory
over the two-time defending champion Wossman Wildcats on March 13, 2026, at Burton
Coliseum in Lake Charles; and
WHEREAS, this victory marks only the second state championship in program
history for Brusly High School and the first since 1984, making the achievement especially
meaningful for the school and its community; and
WHEREAS, the Panthers' championship performance was highlighted by senior
forward Ben Radford, who was named Most Outstanding Player after a dominant showing
of 17 points, 17 rebounds, and 7 blocks; and
WHEREAS, senior Micah Bryant made critical contributions to the victory, sparking
a decisive 7-0 run in the third quarter and finishing with 12 points, including a key
three-pointer to begin the fourth quarter; and
WHEREAS, senior Kazi Murray added a crucial layup with 54 seconds remaining
to extend the Panthers' lead and help secure the championship; and
WHEREAS, the team's defensive effort and composure under pressure proved
decisive, as head coach Kirby Loupe credited the Panthers' ability to handle Wossman's
defensive intensity as the key to the game, particularly following their semifinal loss to the
Wildcats in the previous season; and
WHEREAS, the Panthers displayed exceptional teamwork, resilience, and
sportsmanship, representing Brusly High School and the community of West Baton Rouge
Parish with pride and distinction; and

WHEREAS, the team's success reflects the outstanding leadership and commitment
of its coaching staff, whose guidance and mentorship have been instrumental in developing
both the athletic and personal character of the players; and
WHEREAS, the support of families, faculty, students, and the Brusly community
played a vital role in encouraging and sustaining the team's championship run; and
WHEREAS, the Brusly High School basketball team's achievement serves as an
inspiration to future student-athletes and stands as a proud moment in the school's history.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby commend the Brusly High School boys basketball team on winning the 2026
LHSAA Division II Non-Select State Championship and further recognizes the players,
coaches, and supporters for their outstanding accomplishment.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
principal of Brusly High School and the head coach of the boys basketball team.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
