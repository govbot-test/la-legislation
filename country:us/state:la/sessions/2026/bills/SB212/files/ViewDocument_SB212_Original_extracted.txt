malpractice actions. (8/1/26)

DIGEST
SB 212 Original 2026 Regular Session Miller
Present law (R.S. 40:1231.2(B)) provides that the total amount recoverable for all
malpractice claims for injuries to or death of a patient, exclusive of future medical care and
related benefits as provided in present law, shall not exceed $500,000 plus interest and cost.
Proposed law provides that the total amount recoverable for all malpractice claims for
injuries to or death of a patient, exclusive of past and future medical care and related benefits
as provided in present law shall not exceed $500,000 plus interest and costs for each
claimant having a right of action.
Proposed law provides that when there is an increase in the federal Consumer Price Index,
with respect to the amount of the foregoing limitation of liability set forth in present law
from September 1, 1975, the liability limit prescribed in proposed law shall be increased by
a sum equal to the amount of such limit multiplied by the percentage increase in the
Consumer Price Index for Urban Wage Earners and Clerical Workers (CPI-W) (seasonally
adjusted, U.S. City Average, All Items), as published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics,
between September 1, 1975 and the time at which damages subject to such limits are
awarded by final judgment.
Present law provides that the total amounts paid in accordance with present law shall not
exceed the limitation as provided in present law.
Proposed law provides that the total amounts paid in accordance with proposed law shall not
exceed the limitation as provided in proposed law.
Present law (R.S. 40:1231.3(A)) provides that, with respect to future medical care and
related benefits, if the total amount is for the maximum amount recoverable, exclusive of the
value of future medical care and related benefits that will be incurred after the date of the
response to the special interrogatory by the jury or the court's finding, the cost of all future
medical care and related benefits that will be incurred after the date of the response to the
special interrogatory by the jury or the court's finding shall be paid in accordance with
present law.
Proposed law repeals present law and provides that in all malpractice claims which proceed
to trial in which the plaintiff has established fault and causation of injury and damages, and
in which the plaintiff is found in need of future medical care and related benefits, and the
amount thereof has been established, the court within 30 days of the date of the judgment
shall hold a post-trial evidentiary hearing on the issue of competence of the patient, the tutor
of a minor patient, or, if interdicted, the patient's appointed curator, to manage the future
medical care and related benefits awarded by the judge or jury.
Proposed law provides that if the court determines at the evidentiary hearing that the patient,
the tutor of the minor patient, or the patient's appointed curator is competent to manage the
future medical care and related benefits awarded by the jury, such award shall be made
executory, due and payable by the defendant provider found liable at trial and by the Patient
Compensation Fund as any other award of damages in accordance with present law.
Proposed law provides that if the court determines at the evidentiary hearing that the patient,
the tutor of the minor patient, or the patient's appointed curator is not competent to manage
the future medical care and related benefits awarded by the jury, the court shall set forth its
