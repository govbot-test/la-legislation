SLS 26RS-3027 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 56
BY SENATOR FOIL
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and congratulates the Young Men's Christian Association
on the occasion of its 175th anniversary.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate the 175th anniversary of the Young Men's Christian
3 Association, recognize its role in strengthening communities, and encourage
4 continued efforts to promote well-being and community connection.
5 WHEREAS, the Young Men's Christian Association (YMCA) marks its 175th
6 anniversary of establishment in the United States, by Thomas Valentine Sullivan, in Boston
7 Massachusetts on December 29, 1851, with a mission to put Christian principles into
8 practice by building a healthy spirit, mind, and body for all; and
9 WHEREAS, the YMCA has continuously adapted to meet the evolving needs of
10 American communities, strengthening society by bringing people together, fostering
11 relationships, and creating a sense of belonging; and
12 WHEREAS, the YMCA's legacy includes providing safe housing during the
13 Industrial Revolution, pioneering the first night school, and English as a Second Language
14 classes, inventing basketball, and delivering the first group swim lessons; and
15 WHEREAS, the YMCA's legacy also includes establishing the Youth and
16 Government program, serving as the birthplace of Father's Day, and becoming the nation's
17 leading nonprofit provider of child care and after-school programs to support working
18 families; and

1 WHEREAS, today nearly 2,600 YMCAs serve more than 10,000 communities, with
2 more than 300,000 staff and 350,000 volunteers, delivering essential programs to over 17
3 million people annually; and
4 WHEREAS, the YMCAs of Louisiana foster a commitment to strengthening
5 communities by connecting people to their potential, purpose, and each other; and
6 WHEREAS, the YMCA of the Capital Area, founded in 1917 in Baton Rouge,
7 Louisiana, has served as a cornerstone of the community for more than a century, advancing
8 youth development, healthy living, and social responsibility; and
9 WHEREAS, the YMCA of the Capital Area engages dedicated staff and volunteers
10 across seven branches, delivering essential programs and services that strengthen
11 individuals, families, and communities throughout the Capital Area; and
12 WHEREAS, the YMCA is a leader in aquatics activities such as water safety and
13 swim lessons, as well as child care, youth sports, meal programs, and health and wellness
14 initiatives, and is the largest provider of fitness programs for older adults; and
15 WHEREAS, the YMCA creates welcoming spaces for people of all ages,
16 backgrounds, and abilities.
17 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
18 commend and congratulate the YMCA for 175 years of service to the nation and recognizes
19 the YMCA's role in responding to community needs, fostering relationships, and building
20 a sense of belonging.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana commends the staff
22 and volunteers who create connected communities and advance the YMCA's mission and
23 encourages continued support of their efforts to address awareness of health, wellness,
24 nutrition and social interaction by creating places that promote well-being and help people
25 build relationships and thrive.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
27 president and chief executive officer of the YMCA of the Capital Area, Sean Elliott.
