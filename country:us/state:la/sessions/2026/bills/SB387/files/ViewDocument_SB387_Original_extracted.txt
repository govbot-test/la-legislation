2 §1867.1 PBM Duty; compensation; audits; contract and other requirements
3 A. PBM Duty
4 (1) A pharmacy benefit manager shall owe the pharmacy benefit
5 manager duty to any enrollee, health plan, or provider that receives pharmacy
6 benefit management services from the pharmacy benefit manager or that
7 furnishes, covers, receives, or is administered a unit of a prescription drug for
8 which the pharmacy benefit manager has provided pharmacy benefit
9 management services.
10 (a) Duty to enrollees. The pharmacy benefit manager duty owed to
11 enrollees shall include duties of care and good faith and fair dealing. The
12 commissioner shall adopt rules and regulations defining the scope of the duties
13 owed to enrollees, including by obligating pharmacy benefit managers to
14 provide all pharmacy benefit management services related to formulary design,
15 utilization management, and grievances and appeals in a transparent manner
16 to enrollees that is consistent with the best interest of enrollees and to disclose
17 all conflicts of interest to enrollees.
18 (b) Duty to health plans. The pharmacy benefit manager duty owed to
19 health plans shall include duties of care and good faith and fair dealing. The
20 commissioner shall adopt rules and regulations defining the scope of the duties
21 owed to health plans, including by obligating pharmacy benefit managers to
22 provide transparency to health plans about amounts charged or claimed by the
23 pharmacy benefit manager in a manner that is adequate to identify all instances
24 of spread pricing and to disclose all conflicts of interest to health plans.
25 (c) Duty to providers. The pharmacy benefit manager duty owed to
26 providers shall include duties of care and good faith and fair dealing. The
27 commissioner shall adopt rules and regulations defining the scope of the duties
28 owed to providers, including by obligating pharmacy benefit managers to
29 provide transparency to providers about amounts charged or claimed by the

DIGEST
SB 387 Original 2026 Regular Session Bass
Present law provides for definitions.
Proposed law retains present law and adds definitions for "enrollee", "healthcare service",
"person", "pharmacy benefit management fee", "pharmacy benefit management service",
"provider", and "related entity". Proposed law also amends the definition for "rebates".
Proposed law provides for a PBM's duty of care and good faith and fair dealing to enrollees,
health plans, and providers.
Proposed law provides for PBM compensation through pharmacy benefit manager fees and
prohibits a PBM from retaining rebates and fees.
Proposed law allows the commissioner of insurance and any health insurance issuer or health
plan contracted with a PBM to audit the PBM once per calendar year. Proposed law further
provides for information that may be requested as part of the audit and provides for the
protection of confidential and proprietary information through a public records exemption.
Proposed law requires PBM contracts to specify all forms of revenue to be paid by the health
insurance issuer or health plan to the pharmacy benefit manager and to acknowledge that
spread pricing is not permitted.
Proposed law provides that, in addition to any other civil or criminal penalty authorized by
law, a violation of proposed law is punishable by the commissioner through a civil monetary
penalty of $25,000 for each and every act or violation, with no aggregate penalty maximum.
Proposed law further provides that if a violation is not corrected within 30 days after notice
of the violation is received by the PBM, the commissioner must suspend or revoke the
pharmacy benefit manager's license.
Proposed law is to be implemented to regulate a pharmacy benefit manager or health
insurance issuer only to the extent permissible under applicable law.
Effective January 1, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 22:1863 and R.S. 44:4.1(B)(11); adds R.S. 22:1867.1; repeals R.S.22:1868.1)
