6 §1868.2. Pharmacy benefit manager formularies
7 A. As used in this Section, the following terms have the following
8 meanings:
9 (1) "Affiliated manufacturer" means a drug or biological product
10 manufacturer that, either directly or indirectly through one or more
11 intermediaries, meets one or more of the following criteria:
12 (a) Has an investment or ownership interest greater than five percent in
13 a pharmacy benefit manager.
14 (b) Shares common ownership with a pharmacy benefit manager.
15 (c) Has an investor or a holder of an ownership interest in a pharmacy
16 benefit manager.
17 (2) "Biological product" has the same meaning as in the Public Health
18 Service Act, 42 U.S.C. 262.
19 (3) "Biosimilar" has the same meaning as in the Public Health Service
20 Act, 42 U.S.C. 262.
21 (4) "Interchangeable" has the same meaning as in the Public Health
22 Service Act, 42 U.S.C. 262.
23 B.(1) A pharmacy benefit manager revising the formulary of covered
24 prescription drugs at the beginning of a plan year shall provide a sixty day
25 continuity-of-care period in which the covered prescription drug that is being
26 revised from the formulary continues to be provided at the same cost for the
27 insured for a period of sixty days.
28 (2) The sixty day continuity-of-care period commences upon notification
29 to the insured by the insurer.
30 (3) This Subsection does not apply if any of the following have occurred
31 regarding the covered prescription drug:
32 (a) The prescription drug has been made available over the counter by
33 the United States Food and Drug Administration and has entered the
34 commercial market as such.
35 (b) The prescription drug has been removed or withdrawn from the
36 commercial market by the manufacturer.
37 (c) The prescription drug is subject to an involuntary recall by state or
38 federal authorities and is no longer available on the commercial market.
39 C. A pharmacy benefit manager shall not require an insured to receive
40 a drug or biological product that is manufactured by an affiliated manufacturer
41 when there is an available generically equivalent drug, or an available biological
42 product that is biosimilar to and interchangeable for the prescribed biological
43 product.
44 D. A pharmacy benefit manager shall not require an insured to receive
45 a more expensive name brand drug when less expensive name brand drugs,
46 biosimilars, generic drugs, or any other drug in the same class of drugs are
47 available.
48 E. Other than at the time of coverage renewal, while an insured is taking
49 a prescription drug a pharmacy benefit manager shall not do any of the
50 following:
51 (1) Remove the prescription drug from its list of covered drugs during
52 the policy year unless any of the following have occurred:
53 (a) The United States Food and Drug Administration has issued a
54 statement about the drug which calls into question the clinical safety of the
55 drug.
56 (b) The manufacturer of the drug has notified the United States Food
57 and Drug Administration of a manufacturing discontinuance or potential
58 discontinuance of the drug as required by the Federal Food, Drug, and
59 Cosmetic Act, 21 U.S.C. 356.

6 for prescription drug benefits, or reclassify the drug to a higher cost-sharing
7 tier during the policy year.
8 F. This Section does not prohibit the addition of prescription drugs to the
