SLS 26RS-304 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR CATHEY
TRAFFIC. Provides for speed enforcement measures. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 32:47(A)(3), (4), and (5), to enact R.S. 32:47(A)(8), and to
3 repeal R.S. 32:43(A)(4), relative to speed enforcement measures; to provide for
4 pavement markings in school zones; to provide for pavement markings in
5 compliance with the federal requirements; to provide for compliance language with
6 federal requirements; to repeal certain exemptions; and to provide for related
7 matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 32:47(A)(3),(4), and (5) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
10 32:47(A)(8) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §47. Use of speed recording devices; restrictions
12 A. Automated speed enforcement devices or mobile speed cameras that are
13 used to issue a citation of an alleged violation by mail shall only be used by local
14 municipal authorities or local parish authorities in the following instances:
15 * * *
16 (3) There are forty-eight-inch by forty-eight-inch signs, the bottom of which
17 shall be at least seven feet from the ground, indicating that a device or camera is

1 present, no less than five hundred feet and no more than one thousand feet before the
2 device or camera, in a manner as to be clearly visible to approaching traffic. The
3 signs shall be bright yellow and shall read "Photo enforcement devices at use".
4 (4) Upon the entrance to each school zone, there shall be painted a two- foot-
5 wide yellow stripe completely across the roadway with pavement word markings
6 in accordance with the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices indicating
7 "Entering School Zone" painted in twelve-inch white lettering visible therein.
8 (5) Two hundred feet before the entrance to each school zone, there shall be
9 painted a two-foot-wide yellow stripe completely across the roadway with pavement
10 word markings in accordance with the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control
11 Devices indicating "School Zone Ahead" painted in twelve-inch white lettering
12 visible therein.
13 * * *
14 (8) Nothing in this Section shall be construed to require the use of any
15 traffic control device or pavement marking that is inconsistent with or not
16 authorized by the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices.
17 * * *
18 Section 2. R.S. 32:43(A)(4) is hereby repealed in its entirety.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 138 Original 2026 Regular Session Cathey
Present law provides signs are to be bright yellow and read "Photo enforcement devices at
use". Proposed law removes provision that signs be bright yellow.
Present law provides that an entrance to a school zone have a painted a two- foot-wide
yellow stripe completely across the roadway with"Entering School Zone" painted in 12-inch
white lettering. Proposed law removes provision for a two foot wide yellow stripe painted
in 12 inch white lettering and adds the provision that pavement word markings be in
accordance with the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices (MUTCD).
Present law provides for a painted, two-foot-wide yellow stripe completely across the
roadway with "School Zone Ahead" painted in 12 inch white lettering. Proposed law
removes the provision for two foot wide yellow stripes and painted 12 inch white lettering
and adds the provision that pavement word markings be in accordance with the MUTCD.

SLS 26RS-304 ORIGINAL
SB NO. 138
Proposed law provides there are no requirements in present law or proposed law that will
conflict with the MUTCD.
Present law prohibits local municipal or parish authorities from using automated speed
enforcement devices on interstate highways.
Present law exempts a governing authority of a municipality with a population of less than
sixteen thousand and more than fifteen thousand according to the latest federal decennial
census within a parish with a population of less than 90,000 and more than 70,000 according
to the latest federal decennial census from the prohibition in present law. Proposed law
repeals the exemption.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 32:47(A)(3), (4), and (5); adds R.S. 32:47(A)(8); repeals R.S. 32:43(A)(4))
