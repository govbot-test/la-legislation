BY REPRESENTATIVE BOYER
ELECTIONS/BOND & TAX: (Constitutional Amendment) Provides for eligible election
dates for bond and tax elections
1 A JOINT RESOLUTION
2 Proposing to amend Article VI, Section 22 of the Constitution of Louisiana and to repeal
3 Article VI, Section 30(B) of the Constitution of Louisiana, relative to the procedure
4 for certain special elections; to provide for local government bond and tax elections,
5 parcel fee elections, and gaming elections; to provide for such elections to be held
6 only during a regularly scheduled open primary election; to repeal provisions
7 prohibiting a political subdivision from submitting the same tax proposition to the
8 electorate more than once in a six month period except in the case of an emergency;
9 to authorize the legislature to provide for a special election; to provide for
10 submission of the proposed amendment to the electors; and to provide for related
11 matters.
12 Section 1. Be it resolved by the Legislature of Louisiana, two-thirds of the members
13 elected to each house concurring, that there shall be submitted to the electors of the state of
14 Louisiana, for their approval or rejection in the manner provided by law, a proposal to
15 amend Article VI, Section 22 of the Constitution of Louisiana, to read as follows:
16 §22. Procedure for Certain Special Elections
17 Section 22.(A) When an election is required in a political subdivision under
18 the provisions of this constitution which require submission to the electors of a
19 proposition or question, the election shall be called, conducted, and the returns
20 thereof canvassed, in accordance with the procedures established by the law then in
21 effect pertaining to elections for incurring bonded indebtedness and special taxes
22 relative to local finance, or as may be otherwise provided by law.

1 (B) When an election is required in a political subdivision under the

HB 446 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Boyer
Abstract: Provides that local tax, bond, parcel fee, and gaming elections may be held only
during a regularly scheduled open primary election, except as otherwise provided by
law enacted by two-thirds vote of the legislature.
Present constitution (Art. VI, §22) provides that when an election is required in a political
subdivision under the provisions of the constitution which require submission to the electors
of a proposition or question, the election shall be called, conducted, and the returns thereof
canvassed, in accordance with the procedures established by the law then in effect pertaining
to elections for incurring bonded indebtedness and special taxes relative to local finance, or
as may be otherwise provided by law.
Proposed constitutional amendment retains present constitution.
Present constitution (Art. XI, §1) provides that the legislature shall adopt an election code
which shall provide for the conduct of all elections.
Proposed constitutional amendment retains present constitution.
Proposed constitutional amendment provides that when an election is required in a political
subdivision under the provisions of present constitution or other law regarding bonded
indebtedness or special taxes relative to local finance, a parcel fee, or gaming, gambling, or
wagering, the election may be held only during a regularly scheduled open primary election.
Further provides that the legislature may provide by law enacted by two-thirds of the elected
members of each house of the legislature for the calling of special elections in cases of
emergency.
Present constitution (Art. VI, §30(B)) provides that no political subdivision shall submit the
same tax proposition, or a new tax proposition that includes such a tax proposition, to the
electorate more than once within a six month period except in the case of an emergency as
determined by the governing authority of the political subdivision.
Proposed constitutional amendment repeals present constitution.
Provides for submission of the proposed amendment to the voters at the statewide election
to be held on November 3, 2026.
Effective January 1, 2029.
(Amends Const. Art. VI, §22; Repeals Const. Art. VI, §30(B))
