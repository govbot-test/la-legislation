SLS 26RS-3873 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 137
BY SENATOR PRESSLY
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and recognizes U.S. Highway 80 for historical
significance.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend and recognize the historical significance of U.S. Highway 80 and to
3 commemorate the approaching 100th anniversary of its designation.
4 WHEREAS, U.S. Highway 80 was officially designated on November 11, 1926, as
5 one of the original numbered highways in the United States Highway System and became
6 one of America's earliest transcontinental automobile routes, extending from the Atlantic
7 coast near Savannah, Georgia, to the Pacific coast in San Diego, California; and
8 WHEREAS, U.S. Highway 80 was established in 1926 as the Dixie Overland
9 Highway, also known by other auto trail names depending on the region, most notably the
10 Broadway of America and the Bankhead Highway; and
11 WHEREAS, U.S. Highway 80 was promoted as an all-weather southern corridor that
12 increased commerce, tourism, and connectivity throughout the South; and
13 WHEREAS, during the twentieth century, U.S. Highway 80 served as a principal
14 east-west transportation corridor and contributed to the growth of roadside commerce, travel
15 culture, and economic development across Louisiana and the nation; and
16 WHEREAS, U.S. Highway 80 passes through numerous Louisiana communities,
17 including Monroe, Rayville, Ruston, Minden, Haughton, Bossier City, and Shreveport,
18 where it functioned as an important commercial corridor; and

1 WHEREAS, U.S. Highway 80 contributed to the cultural and economic development
2 of North Louisiana and is associated with the region's musical heritage, including the
3 influence of the Louisiana Hayride in Shreveport, a highly influential live country and
4 western radio broadcast, during the era when U.S. Highway 80 served as a major travel
5 route; and
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana has recognized portions of Historic U.S. Highway 80 as a
7 scenic byway, preserving elements of the state's roadside and transportation heritage,
8 including a one hundred mile stretch that extends from Ruston to the Mississippi River
9 designated as the Historic U.S. 80 Byway, with preserved neon signs and roadside
10 architecture that defined the golden age of American travel.
11 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
12 does hereby commend and recognize the historical, cultural, and economic significance of
13 U.S. Highway 80 and commemorates the approaching 100h anniversary of its designation.
