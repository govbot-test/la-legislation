HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 48
BY REPRESENTATIVE BUTLER
A RESOLUTION
To designate the week of March 23-27, 2026, as Louisiana Agriculture Week at the state
capitol and in the state of Louisiana and to commend the farmers, ranchers, and
agribusiness leaders who participate in this vital educational initiative.
WHEREAS, agriculture is the backbone of Louisiana's economy, contributing
billions of dollars annually and providing the essential food, fiber, and fuel that sustains our
citizens and drives our state's prosperity; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana Agriculture Week is dedicated to providing an opportunity
for citizens across the state to honor the essential role agriculture plays in providing food,
maintaining a strong economy, and driving innovation for a sustainable future and fostering
a deeper connection between consumers and the sources of their food, fuel, and fiber; and
WHEREAS, this week serves as a critical bridge between the field and the capitol,
allowing lawmakers and the public to gain firsthand insight into the daily operations,
technological advancements, and economic challenges faced by our agricultural community;
and
WHEREAS, this week provides a unique opportunity for legislators to walk the land,
visit with producers, and understand the direct impact of legislative decisions on the
sustainability of family farms and rural life; and
WHEREAS, the House Committee on Agriculture, Forestry, Aquaculture, and Rural
Development continues to emphasize the importance of "boots-on-the-ground" advocacy and
the strengthening of personal relationships between farmers and their elected officials; and
WHEREAS, fostering a deeper understanding of the labor and dedication required
to maintain Louisiana's diverse agricultural landscape, from timber and sugarcane to rice,
poultry, and crawfish, is essential for the development of sound public policy.

HR NO. 48 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate the week of March 23-27, 2026, as Louisiana
Agriculture Week at the state capitol and in the state of Louisiana and does hereby commend
the farmers, ranchers, and agribusiness leaders of this state for their participation in this
initiative and for their invaluable contributions to Louisiana's economy, food security, and
cultural heritage.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Legislature
of Louisiana does hereby encourage all citizens and lawmakers to participate in activities
that celebrate and support the hardworking men and women of Louisiana agriculture.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
commissioner of agriculture and the president of the Louisiana Farm Bureau Federation.
