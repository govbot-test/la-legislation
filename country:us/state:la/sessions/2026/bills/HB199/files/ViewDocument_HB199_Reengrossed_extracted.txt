HB 199 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Miller
Abstract: Extends the moratorium on nursing facilities and additional beds for nursing
facilities.
Present law establishes a facility need review process within the La. Dept. of Health (LDH)
to determine if there is a need for any new or additional nursing facilities or beds.
Present law provides that a facility need review must be satisfied before a nursing facility
can proceed with licensure and Medicaid provider certification.
Present law provides for a nursing facility moratorium with certain exceptions and provides
that nursing facilities are subject to facility need review in the absence of the moratorium.
Present law provides that the nursing facility moratorium shall be in effect until July 1, 2027.
Proposed law retains present law but extends the moratorium until July 1, 2031.
Proposed law requires LDH to collect information from nursing facilities and hospitals to
determine the need for a nursing home moratorium.
Proposed law requires LDH to submit a monthly report of the information collected to the
House and Senate health and welfare committees beginning February 15, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 40:2116.1(B)(1))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Health and Welfare to
the original bill:
1. Change the termination date for the moratorium for nursing facilities to July 1,
2031.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Add a requirement that LDH collect information and prepare a report.
