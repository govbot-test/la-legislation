1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 9:3251(A), relative to advances or deposits for a residential or
3 dwelling premises; to permit the extension of a return of advance or deposits to a
4 tenant or lessee; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 9:3251(A) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §3251. Lessee's deposit to secure lease; retention by lessor; conveyance of leased
8 premises; itemized statement by lessor
9 A. Any advance or deposit of money furnished by a tenant or lessee to a
10 landlord or lessor to secure the performance of any part of a written or oral lease or
11 rental agreement shall be returned to the tenant or lessee of residential or dwelling
12 premises within one month after the lease shall terminate. except that However, the
13 landlord or lessor may retain all or any portion of the advance or deposit which is
14 reasonably necessary to remedy a default of the tenant or to remedy unreasonable
15 wear to the premises. If any portion of an advance or deposit is retained by a
16 landlord or lessor, he shall forward to the tenant or lessee, within one month after the
17 date the tenancy terminates or within fifteen days after the date that is one month
18 after the tenancy terminates, an itemized statement accounting for the proceeds

HB 292 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Boyd
Abstract: Permits the extension of a return on deposits and advances by a landlord or lessor
to a tenant or lessee of a residential or dwelling premises.
Present law requires a landlord or lessor of a residential or dwelling premises to return any
advance or deposit furnished by a tenant or lessee within one month after the lease
termination. If the landlord or lessor retains all or a portion of the advance or deposit, the
landlord or lessor is required to send an itemized statement accounting of the retained
proceeds and reasons why the proceeds were not returned within one month after the tenancy
terminates.
Proposed law retains present law generally but permits the landlord or lessor to send the
itemized statement accounting for the retained proceeds within an additional 15 days after
the date that is one month after the tenancy terminates.
(Amends R.S. 9:3251(A))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Civil Law and
Procedure to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Remove proposed language that would permit the landlord or lessor and tenant
or lessee to agree upon a date for the return of the security deposit.

Section 3:
2. Permit the landlord or lessor to send the itemized statement accounting for the
retained security deposit within an additional 15 days after the date that is one
month after the tenancy terminates.
