WHEREAS, the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival (MVAAFF) was
founded in 2002 by the husband-and-wife team, Stephanie and Floyd Rance, to celebrate and

WHEREAS, the MVAAFF has grown into a nationally recognized and highly
regarded nine-day annual cultural event held in Oak Bluffs, Massachusetts, featuring
screenings, panel discussions, networking events, and special celebrations such as the
Summer White Party and Black Excellence Brunch; and
WHEREAS, the MVAAFF is recognized as an Oscar-qualifying festival for short
films, underscoring its prominence in the film industry, while serving as a platform for
independent and studio films, including documentaries and short films that highlight the
creative achievements of Black artists and Black voices; and
WHEREAS, for more than two decades, the MVAAFF has attracted influential
figures in the film industry and cultural leaders, fostering community engagement and
supporting emerging Black filmmakers through educational programs, mentorship, and
workshops; and
WHEREAS, the MVAAFF draws national and international audiences to Martha's
Vineyard, contributing to the local economy and promoting the island as a center for cultural
celebration and artistic excellence; and

WHEREAS, films and filmmakers presented at the festival have earned national
recognition, awards, and acclaim, with the MVAAFF playing a key role in amplifying Black
voices in the film industry; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana wishes to
recognize and honor the MVAAFF for its enduring contributions to celebrating and
advancing Black artistry in film, culture, and education.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend and congratulate the Martha's Vineyard
African-American Film Festival on the occasion of its twenty-fourth anniversary for its
significant impact on the film industry and the elevation of Black voices in global cinema.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Legislature
of Louisiana encourages the continued growth and success of the festival in advancing Black
artistry, storytelling, and cultural excellence.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
founders of the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival, Stephanie and Floyd
Rance, in recognition of their outstanding contributions to cinema and the arts.

HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 113
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
To commend and recognize the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival for
twenty-four years of Black excellence in film and for its significant contributions to
cinema, culture, and education.
WHEREAS, the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival (MVAAFF) was
founded in 2002 by the husband-and-wife team, Stephanie and Floyd Rance, to celebrate and
showcase films created by or prominently featuring Black filmmakers, actors, and
storytellers; and
WHEREAS, the MVAAFF has grown into a nationally recognized and highly
regarded nine-day annual cultural event held in Oak Bluffs, Massachusetts, featuring
screenings, panel discussions, networking events, and special celebrations such as the
Summer White Party and Black Excellence Brunch; and
WHEREAS, the MVAAFF is recognized as an Oscar-qualifying festival for short
films, underscoring its prominence in the film industry, while serving as a platform for
independent and studio films, including documentaries and short films that highlight the
creative achievements of Black artists and Black voices; and
WHEREAS, for more than two decades, the MVAAFF has attracted influential
figures in the film industry and cultural leaders, fostering community engagement and
supporting emerging Black filmmakers through educational programs, mentorship, and
workshops; and
WHEREAS, the MVAAFF draws national and international audiences to Martha's
Vineyard, contributing to the local economy and promoting the island as a center for cultural
celebration and artistic excellence; and
