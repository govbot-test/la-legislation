2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 113
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
Festival on the occasion of its twenty-fourth anniversary and its role in elevating
Black excellence in film
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend and recognize the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival for
3 twenty-four years of Black excellence in film and for its significant contributions to
4 cinema, culture, and education.
5 WHEREAS, the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival (MVAAFF) was
6 founded in 2002 by the husband-and-wife team, Stephanie and Floyd Rance, to celebrate and
7 showcase films created by or prominently featuring Black filmmakers, actors, and
8 storytellers; and
9 WHEREAS, the MVAAFF has grown into a nationally recognized and highly
10 regarded nine-day annual cultural event held in Oak Bluffs, Massachusetts, featuring
11 screenings, panel discussions, networking events, and special celebrations such as the
12 Summer White Party and Black Excellence Brunch; and
13 WHEREAS, the MVAAFF is recognized as an Oscar-qualifying festival for short
14 films, underscoring its prominence in the film industry, while serving as a platform for
15 independent and studio films, including documentaries and short films that highlight the
16 creative achievements of Black artists and Black voices; and
17 WHEREAS, for more than two decades, the MVAAFF has attracted influential
18 figures in the film industry and cultural leaders, fostering community engagement and
19 supporting emerging Black filmmakers through educational programs, mentorship, and
20 workshops; and

1 WHEREAS, the MVAAFF draws national and international audiences to Martha's
2 Vineyard, contributing to the local economy and promoting the island as a center for cultural
3 celebration and artistic excellence; and
4 WHEREAS, films and filmmakers presented at the festival have earned national
5 recognition, awards, and acclaim, with the MVAAFF playing a key role in amplifying Black
6 voices in the film industry; and
7 WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana wishes to
8 recognize and honor the MVAAFF for its enduring contributions to celebrating and
9 advancing Black artistry in film, culture, and education.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
11 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend and congratulate the Martha's Vineyard
12 African-American Film Festival on the occasion of its twenty-fourth anniversary for its
13 significant impact on the film industry and the elevation of Black voices in global cinema.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Legislature
15 of Louisiana encourages the continued growth and success of the festival in advancing Black
16 artistry, storytelling, and cultural excellence.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
18 founders of the Martha's Vineyard African-American Film Festival, Stephanie and Floyd
19 Rance, in recognition of their outstanding contributions to cinema and the arts.
