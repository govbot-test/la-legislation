16 PART XIII. VISION BENEFIT INSURANCE AND VISION DISCOUNT PLANS
17 §1809. Short Title
18 This Part shall be cited as the "Louisiana Vision Plan Transparency and
19 Fair Practice Act".
20 §1809.1. Purpose
21 The purpose of this Part is to ensure patient access to eye care services
22 and materials through transparent and fair business practices by vision benefit
23 managers. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to expand or limit the scope
24 of practice of any licensed healthcare professional. The provisions of this Part
25 shall not apply to medical benefits provided by an insurer under a health benefit
26 plan that is not a vision benefit plan or vision benefit discount plan as defined
27 in this Part.
28 §1809.2. Legislative findings; access to eye care services
29 A. The legislature finds and declares that access to timely, affordable,

1 and appropriate eye care services is a matter of public health and patient safety
2 within the state of Louisiana.
3 B. The legislature further finds that:
4 (1) Vision benefit managers and vision benefit plans increasingly
5 influence where patients may obtain eye care services and materials, which
6 providers may participate in care delivery, and which treatments or materials
7 may be practically available to patients.
8 (2) Business practices that steer patients to affiliated providers, restrict
9 provider participation, limit access to independent laboratories or suppliers, or
10 interfere with clinical judgment may reduce access to care, particularly in rural
11 and underserved areas of this state.
12 (3) Independent eye care providers are essential to maintaining access
13 to vision and medical eye care services for Louisiana residents, including in
14 communities where alternative providers are limited or unavailable.
15 C. It is therefore the intent of the legislature that this Part be construed
16 and applied primarily to:
17 (1) Protect patient access to eye care services and materials.
18 (2) Preserve the integrity of the doctor-patient relationship.
19 (3) Ensure that clinical and prescriptive decisions remain under the
20 control of licensed healthcare professionals.
21 (4) Prevent practices that impair access to care through coercion,
22 steering, or anti-competitive conduct.
23 (5) Support a competitive healthcare environment that serves the health
24 needs of Louisiana residents.
25 D. The provisions of this Part are enacted pursuant to the state's
26 authority to regulate the professional delivery of healthcare services and to
27 protect public health, safety, and welfare.

DIGEST
SB 404 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session McMath
Present law provides for requirements and prohibitions for medical eye care or vision care
benefits provided by or available through a health benefit plan, health maintenance
organization, preferred provider organization, managed care organization, accountable care
organization, or contract of insurance or any medical hospital service contract that are within
the lawful scope of practice of a duly licensed optometrist.
Proposed law expands the applicability of present law to vision benefit plans, vision benefit
discount plans, and any agent acting on behalf of any plan or entity that offers an agreement
or contract of insurance that provides medical eye care or vision care benefits.
Proposed law provides that proposed law shall be cited as the "Louisiana Vision Plan
Transparency and Fair Practice Act".
Proposed law provides that proposed law shall not be construed to expand or limit the scope
of practice of any healthcare provider.
Proposed law provides that proposed law does not apply to medical benefits provided by an
insurer under a health benefit plan that is not a vision benefit plan or vision discount plan.
Proposed law provides for legislative findings and definitions relative to eye care providers
and services, vision benefit managers, and vision benefit plans.
Proposed law provides for requirements and prohibitions relative to covered and noncovered
services and fee schedules in contracts or agreements between insurers or vision benefit
managers and eye care providers.
Proposed law provides for requirements and prohibitions for eye care provider participation
and credentialing by an insurer or vision benefit manager.
Proposed law provides for transparency and disclosure requirements for insurers and vision
benefit managers.
Proposed law establishes requirements for amending contracts and agreements between eye
care providers and insurers or vision benefit managers.
Proposed law prohibits an insurer or vision benefit manager from using extrapolation to
complete an audit of a participating eye care provider.
Proposed law establishes a private right of action for any eye care provider adversely
affected by a violation of proposed law, including injunctive relief and monetary damages
of up to $10,000 for each violation, plus attorney fees and costs.
Proposed law provides for the authority of the Dept. of Insurance and the attorney general
to enforce the provisions of proposed law.
Proposed law provides for the applicability, severability, and effectiveness of proposed law.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
