§1109.5. Power to levy taxes, incur debt, and issue bonds
* * *
D. The board shall provide written notice, at least thirty days prior to the date
of any meeting, at which the board may consider and take action on a matter set forth
in this Section, and such written notice shall be provided to the governing authority
of each of the parishes that comprise the Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District.
E. Notwithstanding the provisions of R.S. 15:1109.3(C), any action or
resolution authorizing the levy of a tax pursuant to this Section shall be required to
be approved by every member of the board and shall be subsequently approved, by
written resolution or other authorized form, by the governing authority of each of the
parishes that comprise the Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District, prior to any
such measure being placed on the ballot."

HB 36 2026 Regular Session Butler
Keyword and oneliner of the instrument as it left the House
JUVENILES: Removes Evangeline Parish as a member of the Acadiana Regional Juvenile
Justice District
Report rejects Senate amendments which would have:
1. Added the president of each parish member's police jury as a joint appointing
authority for each respective commissioner who serves on the board of commissioner
for the Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District Commission.
2. Made technical changes.
Report amends the bill to:
1. Add each president of parishes with certain population thresholds as a joint
appointing authority for each respective commissioner who serves on the board of
commissioners for the Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District Commission.
2. Provide termination procedures for appointed board members who fail to attend two
consecutive meetings, provides for notice of vacancy, and provides the appointing
authority with 90 days to fill the vacancy.
3. Require the board to provide written notice to each member of the Acadiana Regional
Juvenile Justice District at least 30 days prior to the date of any meeting at which the
board may consider and take action on a matter relative to the levying of taxes,
incurrence of debts, or issuance of bonds.
4. Require that any action or resolution that authorizes the levy of a tax be approved by
every board member and be subsequently approved by the governing authority of
each of the parishes that comprise the district before placement of such measure on
the ballot.
5. Make technical changes.
Digest of the bill as proposed by the Conference Committee
Present law (R.S. 15:1109.1) provides for the membership of the Acadiana Regional Juvenile
Justice District Commission for each respective commissioner who serves on the
commission.
Proposed law amends present law to add the president of parishes with certain population
thresholds as a joint appointing authority.
Proposed law provides that the failure of the appointed members to attend two consecutive
meetings is cause for termination from the commission. Further provides for the vacancy of
the position by notifying the appropriate appointing party and provides the appointing
authority with 90 days to fill the vacancy.

Present law (R.S. 15:1109.5) provides for the power of the board of commissioners of the
Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District to levy taxes, incur debt, and issue bonds.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires the board to provide written notice at least 30 days prior to the date
of any meeting at which the board may consider and take action on a matter relative to the
levying of taxes, incurrence of debts, or issuance of bonds as set forth in present law (R.S.
15:1109.5). Further requires that such written notice be provided to the governing authority
of each of the parishes that comprise the Acadiana Regional Juvenile Justice District
(district).
Proposed law requires that any action or resolution that authorizes the levy of a tax pursuant
to present law (R.S. 15:1109.5) be approved by every member of the board and be
subsequently approved, by written resolution or other authorized form, by the governing
authority of each of the parishes that comprise the district before the measure is placed on
the ballot.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 15:1109.1(A)(1)-(8); Adds R.S. 15:1109.1(C) and 1109.5(D) and (E))
