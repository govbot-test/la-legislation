2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 241
BY REPRESENTATIVE MARTINEZ
A RESOLUTION
To designate Wednesday, April 29, 2026, as Assumption Parish Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, Assumption Parish was created on March 31, 1807; prior to its
founding, the parish was a part of the Lafourche settlement, which is one of the earliest
Louisiana settlements; and
WHEREAS, in the early eighteenth century, Assumption Parish was home to a
variety of Native American tribes, the largest of which was the Chitimacha Tribe; by the mid
1800s, the area was primarily inhabited by French and Spanish settlers; and
WHEREAS, the village of Napoleonville is the parish seat of Assumption Parish; and
WHEREAS, Assumption Parish has a long history of agriculture, and in 1861, sugar
cane became the principal crop grown in the parish; today, the parish stands as an important
contributor to Louisiana sugar production, and large sugar cane farms have operated in the
area for generations; and
WHEREAS, agriculture is a nearly $50 million industry in Assumption Parish, and
sugar cane and soybeans are grown on nearly forty thousand acres in the parish; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize the historic contributions of Assumption
Parish to the agriculture industry of the state.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 29, 2026, as Assumption
Parish Day at the state capitol and does hereby express enduring appreciation for the
contributions of the parish to the agriculture industry and cultural landscape of Louisiana.
