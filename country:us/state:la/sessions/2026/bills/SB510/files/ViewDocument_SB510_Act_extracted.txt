SENATE BILL NO. 510
BY SENATOR MORRIS
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 26:90(A)(3)(a) and 286(A)(3)(a), relative to alcoholic beverage
3 permits; to provide for acts prohibited on licensed premises; to prohibit enticement
4 of minors on premises with certain substances; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 26:90(A)(3)(a) and 286(A)(3)(a) are hereby amended and reenacted
7 to read as follows:
8 §90. Acts prohibited on licensed premises; suspension or revocation of permits
9 A. No person holding a retail dealer's permit, and no person permitted to sell
10 alcoholic beverages at retail to consumers, and no agent, associate, employee,
11 representative, or servant of any such person shall do or permit any of the following
12 acts to be done on or about the licensed premises:
13 * * *
14 (3)(a) Intentionally entice, aid, or permit any person under the age of eighteen
15 years to visit or loiter in or about any place where alcoholic beverages or beer , beer,
16 or consumable hemp products as defined in R.S. 3:1481 are the principal
17 commodities sold, handled, or given away. The provisions of this Section shall in no
18 way prohibit the presence of any person under the age of eighteen years on or about
19 a licensed premises for any function sponsored by a religious or charitable
20 organization with tax exempt status under Section 501(3) 501(c)(3) of the Internal
21 Revenue Code of the United States, or by a fraternal beneficiary society with tax
22 exempt status under 501(8) 501(c)(8) of the said code, and no alcoholic beverages
23 are sold, handled, given away, or accessible during the presence of any such person.
24 * * *
25 §286. Acts prohibited on licensed premises; suspension or revocation of permits
26 A. No person holding a retail dealer's permit, and no person permitted to sell
27 alcoholic beverages at retail to consumers, and no servant, agent, or employee of the
