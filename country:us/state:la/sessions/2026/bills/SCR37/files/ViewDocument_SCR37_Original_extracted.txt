1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To request the surgeon general to review Louisiana's informed consent laws and submit a
3 report to the legislature on whether any gaps exist in current laws relative to
4 vaccinations.
5 WHEREAS, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, while
6 there is a requirement for certain vaccine information to be given to a patient prior to an
7 immunization, there is no federal requirement for informed consent relating to
8 immunizations; and
9 WHEREAS, the National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act of 1986, as amended,
10 created the National Vaccine Injury Compensation Program, in response to lawsuits against
11 vaccine manufacturers and healthcare providers that threatened to cause vaccine shortages
12 and reduce vaccination rates; and
13 WHEREAS, one component of the program is a requirement for a healthcare
14 provider to provide a copy of the current Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) to the legal
15 representative of any child or to any other individual receiving certain vaccines; and
16 WHEREAS, while a VIS provides information, informed consent is an ethical and
17 legal process involving a discussion and explicit, voluntary patient permission to proceed
18 with any medical or surgical procedure, which may be required by state law; and

1 WHEREAS, Louisiana has uniform consent laws and medical consent laws as
2 provided for in R.S. 40:1157.1, et seq., that provide for the means for obtaining patient
3 consent and the persons eligible to give consent for a patient to receive surgical or medical
4 treatment; and
5 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Medical Disclosure Panel is established in R.S. 40:1157.2
6 to determine which risks and hazards related to medical care and surgical procedures must
7 be disclosed by a physician or other healthcare provider to a patient or person authorized to
8 consent for a patient and to establish the general form and substance of such disclosure; and
9 WHEREAS, the panel is required to prepare separate lists of those medical
10 treatments and surgical procedures that do and do not require disclosure and, for those
11 treatments and procedures that do require disclosure, establish the degree of disclosure
12 required and the form in which the disclosure will be made; and
13 WHEREAS, the list of medical and surgical procedures requiring disclosures are
14 provided for in the Louisiana Administrative Code at LAC 48:I.2301 through 2463; and
15 WHEREAS, vaccinations are not included in the list.
16 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
17 request the surgeon general to review Louisiana's informed consent laws and submit a report
18 to the legislature on whether any gaps exist in current law or if there is a need for a specific
19 law relative to vaccinations.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
21 surgeon general.
