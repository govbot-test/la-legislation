BY REPRESENTATIVES GLORIOSO AND CHASSION
INSURANCE CLAIMS: Provides relative to the verification of contractors
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 22:1892(A)(1) and to enact R.S. 22:1892(L), relative to the
3 payment and adjustment of insurance claims; to require insurers to verify contractor
4 licensing before issuing certain payments; to provide for a safe harbor from penalties
5 for payment delays related to contractor verification; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 22:1892(A)(1) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 22:1892(L)
9 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1892. Payment and adjustment of claims; policies other than life and health and
11 accident; good faith duty; breach of good faith duty; vehicle damage claims;
12 extension of time to respond to claims during emergency or disaster;
13 penalties; arson-related claims suspension; definitions
14 A.(1) All insurers issuing any type of contract, other than those specified in
15 R.S. 22:1811, 1821, and Chapter 10 of Title 23 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of
16 1950, shall pay the amount of any claim due any insured within thirty days pursuant
17 to R.S. 22:1892(B) or R.S. 22:1892.2, as applicable, after receipt of satisfactory
18 proofs of loss from the insured or any party in interest. The insurer shall notify the
19 insurance producer of record of all such payments for property damage claims made
20 in accordance with this Paragraph. However, if a contractor is a named payee on a

1 check or draft intended for the repair or restoration of immovable property, the
2 insurer or its adjuster shall first verify the contractor's license status through the
3 website or portal of the Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors. The
4 amount of any claim due shall be paid pursuant to R.S. 22:1892(B) or R.S.
5 22:1892.2, as applicable, after receipt of satisfactory proof of loss and, if applicable,
6 completion of the verification required by this Paragraph.
7 * * *
8 L.(1) Notwithstanding any other provision of this Section or of R.S.
9 22:1892.2 to the contrary, an insurer shall not be deemed to have acted in bad faith,
10 nor shall that insurer be subject to any penalty or attorney fees, for a delay in
11 transmitting payment if that delay is directly and reasonably attributable to the
12 insurer's inability to verify a contractor's license through the portal or website of the
13 Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors.
14 (2) To qualify for the safe harbor provided in this Subsection, the insurer
15 shall do all of the following:
16 (a) Document the attempt to verify the license in the claim file, including the
17 date, time, and result of the search.
18 (b) Provide written notice to the insured within five business days of the
19 failed verification attempt, stating that payment is being withheld or delayed pending
20 verification of the contractor's license status as required by Subsection (A)(1) of this
21 Section.

HB 1162 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Glorioso
Abstract: Requires the verification of contractors under certain circumstances.
Present law provides that all insurers issuing any type of contract, other than those specified
in present law, shall pay the amount of any claim due any insured pursuant to present law
(R.S. 22:1892(B) or R.S. 22:1892.2), as applicable, after receipt of satisfactory proofs of loss
from the insured or any party in interest.

are additions.

Proposed law provides that if a contractor is a named payee on a payment intended for the
repair or restoration of immovable property, the insurer or its adjuster shall first verify the
contractor's license status through the website or portal of the La. State Licensing Bd. for
Contractors. The amount of any claim due shall be paid pursuant to present law (R.S.
22:1892(B) or R.S. 22:1892.2), as applicable, after receipt of satisfactory proof of loss and,
if applicable, completion of the verification required by proposed law.
Proposed law provides that an insurer shall not be deemed to have acted in bad faith and
shall not be subject to any penalty for a delay in transmitting this payment to a contractor if
that delay is directly and reasonably attributable to the insurer's inability to verify that
contractor's license through the website or portal of the La. State Licensing Bd. for
Contractors, if the insurer documents the attempt to verify the license and provides written
notice to the insured within five business days of the failed verification attempt stating the
payment is being withheld or delayed in accordance with proposed law.
(Amends R.S. 22:1892(A)(1); Adds R.S. 22:1892(L))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:

Section 2:
1. Remove the 30-day provision relative to the claims payment period from
proposed law.

Section 3:
2. Add that claims payment periods are subject to present law.
