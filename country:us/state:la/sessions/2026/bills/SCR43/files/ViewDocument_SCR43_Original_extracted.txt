SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 43
BY SENATOR SEABAUGH
the 2026 Distinguished Service Award in Sports Journalism and on being inducted into the
2026 class of the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate John James Marshall upon receiving the 2026 Distinguished
3 Service Award in Sports Journalism and on being inducted into the 2026 class of the
4 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
5 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame in Natchitoches, Louisiana, each year
6 collaborates with the Louisiana Sports Writers Association in selecting a limited number of
7 sports figures who embody the character, skill, and talent worthy of being named to the hall
8 of fame; and
9 WHEREAS, the Distinguished Service Award is the most prestigious honor offered
10 to sports media in the state and recipients are carefully chosen after an extensive deliberation
11 by a forty-member expert panel based on their professional accomplishments in local, state,
12 regional, and national arenas; and
13 WHEREAS, John James Marshall is the recipient of the 2026 Distinguished Service
14 Award in Sports Journalism and a member of the 2026 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame
15 Induction Class; and
16 WHEREAS, John James Marshall, a native of Shreveport, has dedicated more than
17 five decades to sports journalism and broadcasting, becoming one of the most respected and
18 versatile voices in Louisiana sports media; and
Page 1 of 3
SLS 26RS-224 ORIGINAL

1 WHEREAS, Mr. Marshall began his career in local media in 1974 at the age of
2 fifteen when he started covering American Legion baseball games for the Shreveport Times,
3 demonstrating an early passion for storytelling and sports reporting that would define his
4 professional life; and
5 WHEREAS, after graduating from Louisiana Tech University in 1981, where he
6 served as sports editor of The Tech Talk, Mr. Marshall began his professional career with the
7 Shreveport Journal and rose to become executive sports editor in 1988; and
8 WHEREAS, Mr. Marshall and his brother, Ben Marshall, have achieved recognition
9 in broadcasting, twice winning the LSWA's Best Radio Show award for their program
10 SportsTalk with Bonzai Ben and JJ on 50,000-watt KWKH-AM and holds the distinction of
11 being the longest-running sports talk radio show in Louisiana; and
12 WHEREAS, following the closing of the Shreveport Journal in 1991, Mr. Marshall
13 joined the Shreveport Times and continued his work in sports journalism before ultimately
14 choosing to pursue his growing radio career; and
15 WHEREAS, despite not entering the LSWA writing contest for twenty-seven years,
16 Mr. Marshall has earned multiple awards in recent years for his writing with the
17 Shreveport-Bossier Journal; and
18 WHEREAS, over the course of his distinguished career, Mr. Marshall has been
19 recognized repeatedly for excellence in journalism, earning four Louisiana Sports Writers
20 Association (LSWA) Prep Writer of the Year awards, two LSWA Columnist of the Year
21 honors, and the LSWA Story of the Year award in 2023; and
22 WHEREAS, Mr. Marshall's career has included numerous accomplishments across
23 several forms of media, including winning a national Associated Press Sports Editors Best
24 Feature Sports Story award in 1987 and earning two Council for Advancement and Support
25 of Education awards for documentary video production and storytelling; and
26 WHEREAS, he has served as a play-by-play announcer in both radio and television
27 for professional football with the Canadian Football League's Shreveport Pirates and for
28 NCAA Division I basketball with Centenary College, and contributing to the fan experience
29 as a public address announcer for the Shreveport Captains for eight years and as a part-time
30 public address announcer for the Houston Astros; and
Page 2 of 3
SLS 26RS-224 ORIGINAL

1 WHEREAS, Mr. Marshall has served as media director and instructor at Loyola
2 College Prep, his alma mater, for twenty-nine years, and his connection to Loyola College
3 Prep extends beyond journalism and education, as he was a standout student-athlete, serving
4 as quarterback for the school's 1976 Class 3A state championship football team and earning
5 All-City honors as a third baseman in baseball; and
6 WHEREAS, in recognition of his many contributions to journalism, broadcasting,
7 athletics, and education, Mr. Marshall was inducted into the Loyola College Prep Hall of
8 Honor in 2014 and the baseball press box at the school bears his name; and
9 WHEREAS, Mr. Marshall's service to Louisiana sports journalism has included
10 leadership roles within the Louisiana Sports Writers Association, where he became the
11 youngest individual ever elected president at the age of thirty-one and where he continues
12 to serve on the organization's Hall of Fame committee; and
13 WHEREAS, in 2026, Mr. Marshall's outstanding career and dedication to sports
14 media were fittingly recognized with his induction into the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame
15 as the recipient of the Distinguished Service Award in Sports Journalism.
16 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
17 commend and congratulate John James Marshall upon receiving the 2026 Distinguished
18 Service Award in Sports Journalism and on being inducted into the 2026 class of the
19 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to John
21 James Marshall.
