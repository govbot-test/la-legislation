HLS 26RS-1896 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 62
BY REPRESENTATIVE DOMANGUE
to modify flood map review frequency
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) to review
3 Louisiana's floodplain maps and implement updates every five years.
4 WHEREAS, FEMA is responsible for helping people before, during, and after
5 disasters; and
6 WHEREAS, Congress enacted the National Flood Insurance Program to better
7 protect the United States from flooding by providing a unified national program for
8 floodplain management; and
9 WHEREAS, catastrophic flooding from Hurricane Katrina in Louisiana precipitated
10 drastic changes with FEMA and its oversight and management of floods and flood risks; and
11 WHEREAS, FEMA's floodplain maps are a fundamental element of the flood
12 insurance program, providing a basis for flood insurance rates and floodplain management
13 regulations; and
14 WHEREAS, the maps are utilized by governments officials, developers, and planners
15 to determine the vulnerability to flooding when building communities; and
16 WHEREAS, many existing Flood Insurance Rate Maps (FIRMs) may not fully
17 reflect current flood risks due to changing environmental conditions, infrastructure
18 developments, and evolving climate patterns; and
19 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is highly vulnerable to flooding due to its
20 low-lying geography, coastal erosion, subsidence, and increased frequency of severe weather
21 events; and
Page 1 of 2
HLS 26RS-1896 ORIGINAL
HCR NO. 62
1 WHEREAS, accurate and up-to-date floodplain maps are essential tools for
2 protecting lives, guiding responsible development, determining flood insurance
3 requirements, and informing emergency preparedness efforts; and
4 WHEREAS, outdated or inaccurate floodplain maps can lead to increased property
5 damage, higher recovery costs, and insufficient risk awareness among residents and local
6 governments.
7 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
8 urge and request FEMA to conduct a comprehensive review and update of floodplain maps
9 across the state of Louisiana, incorporating the most recent data, scientific methods, and risk
10 projections.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that frequent reviews of the floodplain maps utilizing
12 the most updated and accurate data available be conducted and updates be implemented
13 every five years.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that FEMA is encouraged to collaborate closely with
15 state agencies, parish governments, academic institutions, and local stakeholders to ensure
16 that updated maps reflect on-the-ground conditions and community knowledge.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that updated floodplain maps should be made easily
18 accessible to the public and be accompanied by clear guidance to help residents understand
19 their flood risk and insurance implications.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
21 administrator of FEMA, the secretary of the Department of Homeland Security, and the
22 members of the Louisiana congressional delegation.
