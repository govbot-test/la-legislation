SLS 26RS-1955 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 103
BY SENATOR FOIL
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH. Designates Monday, May 4, 2026, as Disability
Awareness Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Monday, May 4, 2026, as Disability Awareness Day at the Louisiana State
3 Capitol and to recognize the contributions, rights, and dignity of individuals with
4 disabilities.
5 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is committed to promoting accessibility,
6 inclusion, and opportunity for all its citizens, including individuals with disabilities; and
7 WHEREAS, more than one in four adults in Louisiana live with a disability,
8 contributing significantly to the cultural, economic, and civic fabric of our state; and
9 WHEREAS, individuals with disabilities face daily barriers in transportation,
10 healthcare, education, employment, and public infrastructure that require greater awareness
11 and action; and
12 WHEREAS, the disability community exemplifies resilience, strength, and
13 determination, and reminds us that anyone's life can change in a split second; and
14 WHEREAS, organizations like the Split Second Foundation and the Governor's
15 Advisory Council on Disability Affairs work tirelessly to promote inclusion, accessibility,
16 and increase the quality of life for Louisianians with disabilities; and
17 WHEREAS, Disability Awareness Day provides an opportunity for residents,
18 lawmakers, and community leaders to learn from and uplift the voices of those living with

1 disabilities; and
2 WHEREAS, organizations such as the Split Second Foundation, Louisiana
3 Rehabilitation Services, McNeese Prime, Louisiana Developmental Disabilities Council,
4 Families Helping Families of Louisiana, The Arc of Louisiana, People First of Louisiana,
5 Statewide Independent Living Council, Louisiana Speech-Language-Hearing Association,
6 Lighthouse Louisiana, Louisiana Department of Veteran Affairs, Train Unique-Adaptive
7 Fitness, the Louisiana Special School District-Louisiana Schools for the Deaf and Visually
8 Impaired, LSU Health New Orleans - Human Development Center, and the Brain Injury
9 Association of Louisiana/United Spinal Association of Louisiana help to promote awareness
10 of the importance of accessibility, inclusion, and the elimination of barriers that hinder full
11 participation in society.
12 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
13 does hereby recognize Monday, May 4, 2026, as Disability Awareness Day at the Louisiana
14 State Capitol and does hereby acknowledge the dedication of the various organizations that
15 work toward ensuring equal rights and opportunities for individuals with disabilities.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
17 Governor's Advisory Council on Disability Affairs.
