2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 103
BY SENATOR FOIL
A RESOLUTION
To designate Monday, May 4, 2026, as Disability Awareness Day at the Louisiana State
Capitol and to recognize the contributions, rights, and dignity of individuals with
disabilities.
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is committed to promoting accessibility,
inclusion, and opportunity for all its citizens, including individuals with disabilities; and
WHEREAS, more than one in four adults in Louisiana live with a disability,
contributing significantly to the cultural, economic, and civic fabric of our state; and
WHEREAS, individuals with disabilities face daily barriers in transportation,
healthcare, education, employment, and public infrastructure that require greater awareness
and action; and
WHEREAS, the disability community exemplifies resilience, strength, and
determination, and reminds us that anyone's life can change in a split second; and
WHEREAS, organizations like the Split Second Foundation and the Governor's
Advisory Council on Disability Affairs work tirelessly to promote inclusion, accessibility,
and increase the quality of life for Louisianians with disabilities; and
WHEREAS, Disability Awareness Day provides an opportunity for residents,
lawmakers, and community leaders to learn from and uplift the voices of those living with
disabilities; and

WHEREAS, organizations such as the Split Second Foundation, Louisiana
Rehabilitation Services, McNeese Prime, Louisiana Developmental Disabilities Council,
Families Helping Families of Louisiana, The Arc of Louisiana, People First of Louisiana,
Statewide Independent Living Council, Louisiana Speech-Language-Hearing Association,
Lighthouse Louisiana, Louisiana Department of Veteran Affairs, Train Unique-Adaptive
Fitness, the Louisiana Special School District-Louisiana Schools for the Deaf and Visually
Impaired, LSU Health New Orleans - Human Development Center, and the Brain Injury
Association of Louisiana/United Spinal Association of Louisiana help to promote awareness
of the importance of accessibility, inclusion, and the elimination of barriers that hinder full
participation in society.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby recognize Monday, May 4, 2026, as Disability Awareness Day at the Louisiana
State Capitol and does hereby acknowledge the dedication of the various organizations that
work toward ensuring equal rights and opportunities for individuals with disabilities.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
Governor's Advisory Council on Disability Affairs.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
