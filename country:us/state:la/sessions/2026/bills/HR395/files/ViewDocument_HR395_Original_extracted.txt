HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 395
BY REPRESENTATIVE NEWELL
Alumnae Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commemorate the ninetieth anniversary of the 1936 charter of the New Orleans Alumnae
3 Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.
4 WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.,
5 was chartered in June 1936 and supports the sorority's mission of service, scholarship, and
6 social action; the chapter's history reflects resilience, leadership, and a dedication to uplifting
7 generations through education, advocacy, and civic engagement; 2026 marks the ninetieth
8 year of sisterhood, growth, and public service in the Greater New Orleans area; and
9 WHEREAS, the chapter's charter members were Ema Joyce Adams Bailey, Melva
10 Dier Borris, Julia Washington Bond, Sadie Nelson Boyce, Elizabeth Stubbs Davis, Jessie
11 Covington Dent, Irene Colbert Edmonds, Oralee Baranco Mitchell, Sylvia Labat Randall,
12 Estelle Baranco Turpin, and Naomi Evans Washington, eleven exceptional women who
13 brought the sorority's first chapter to the city of New Orleans; and
14 WHEREAS, the legacy of leadership continued through the line of past presidents
15 and leaders such as Cynthia Butler-McIntyre, Gloria Bryant-Banks, Carolyn E. Lewis,
16 Pamela M. Rogers, and many others who have shaped the direction of the chapter and
17 contributed to the sorority's global impact; and
18 WHEREAS, while the chapter was chartered as the Alpha Eta Sigma Chapter of
19 Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., its level of excellence continued after the name was
20 changed to the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter; the sorority's Five-Point Programmatic
Page 1 of 3

HR NO. 395
1 Thrust is economic development, educational development, international awareness and
2 involvement, physical and mental health, and political awareness and involvement; and
3 WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter has organized nine service projects
4 that have positively impacted citizens in the parishes of Orleans, Jefferson, and St. Bernard;
5 such programs include gathering to worship and venerating the Christian principles on which
6 the sorority was founded, sharing stories, recording reflections, and preserving the chapter's
7 history; there are more than seven hundred active members whose individual acts of service
8 benefit the community at large; and
9 WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter has generated millions of dollars in
10 economic activity and brought thousands of visitors to New Orleans by hosting three
11 regional conferences and three national conventions; the chapter has awarded more than five
12 hundred thousand dollars in scholarships to students attending colleges and universities in
13 the Greater New Orleans area and sponsored numerous signature programs designed to
14 develop local youth; and
15 WHEREAS, the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter has provided compassionate care
16 and support to communities experiencing natural disasters and other hardships across the
17 world; the chapter promotes health education, community health fairs, healthcare access, and
18 research on health disparities; it also promotes voter registration, voter education, voter
19 participation, and civic engagement with state and local government officials; and
20 WHEREAS, the ninetieth anniversary of the New Orleans Alumnae Chapter is not
21 only a celebration of the past but a catalyst for renewed commitment to the future of the
22 chapter and the broader community; it is fitting to reflect on the chapter's history, mission,
23 and vision for years to come.
24 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
25 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commemorate the ninetieth anniversary of the New
26 Orleans Alumnae Chapter of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc., and does hereby express best
27 wishes as its members reflect, celebrate, and renew their commitment to the sorority’s
28 mission of service, scholarship, and social action.
