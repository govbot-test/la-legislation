HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 188
BY REPRESENTATIVES BUTLER, COATES, AND OWEN
A RESOLUTION
To memorialize the United States Congress to oppose any provision in the federal Farm Bill,
or any other federal legislation or regulation, that would prohibit, restrict, or
undermine lawful hunting, including hunting with dogs, field trials, dog training, or
the use of working dogs in traditional sporting activities and to urge Louisiana's
congressional delegation to protect the culture, heritage, and rights of sportsmen and
women.
WHEREAS, H.R. 7567, the "Farm, Food, and National Security Act" (Farm Bill) of
2026, is a major omnibus instrument filed to enact a multi-year law that dictates agricultural,
food, and conservation policies every five years, approximately; and
WHEREAS, Congress is currently negotiating a new Farm Bill to address
conservation efforts, commodity supports, crop insurance, and nutrition programs with an
anticipated floor vote by the United States House of Representatives as early as the end of
April; and
WHEREAS, proposed amendments to the Farm Bill include language from the
Greyhound Protection Act of 2025 (H.R. 5017) that contain vague language that leaves both
hunters and conservationists concerned; and
WHEREAS, hunting is a longstanding tradition in the state of Louisiana and across
the nation, deeply rooted in the culture, heritage, and way of life of countless families and
communities; and

WHEREAS, hunting with dogs has been for generations a lawful and valued part of
that tradition, especially in rural communities where such practices are passed down from
one generation to the next; and
WHEREAS, working dogs used in hunting, field trials, and related sporting activities
are highly trained and serve an important role in lawful outdoor recreation, wildlife
management, and conservation practices; and
WHEREAS, sportsmen and women contribute substantially to local economies
through the purchase of equipment, feed, veterinary care, training supplies, travel, lodging,
and other related expenditures; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana citizens have expressed concern that language proposed in
the federal Farm Bill, or related federal measures, could be interpreted in a way that restricts
or bans lawful hunting practices involving dogs; and
WHEREAS, any attempt to broadly prohibit or impair hunting with dogs would
represent an attack on lawful sporting traditions and would negatively affect hunters, dog
owners, breeders, trainers, and rural communities; and
WHEREAS, the House of Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana recognizes
the importance of preserving lawful hunting traditions and protecting the rights of citizens
to continue these time-honored practices in compliance with state law; and
WHEREAS, such federal overreach could negatively impact Louisiana’s hunting
economy, rural communities, and long-standing cultural traditions; and
WHEREAS, decisions regarding the use of hunting dogs are best left to individual
states, which are more familiar with their unique ecosystems, traditions, and regulatory
needs.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby memorialize the United States Congress to oppose any
provisions in H.R. 7567, the "Farm, Food, and National Security Act" of 2026, that would
restrict or limit the lawful use of hunting dogs.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Louisiana
Legislature urges members of the Louisiana congressional delegation to work diligently to
prevent the inclusion of such provisions in any final version of the Farm Bill.
