SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 120
BY SENATOR HODGES

WHEREAS, on May 4, 2026, President Donald J. Trump issued a proclamation
recognizing May 2026 as Jewish American Heritage Month; calling upon the nation, in
anticipation of the 250th anniversary of the United States of America, to observe a national
Shabbat from sundown on May 15, 2026, through nightfall on May 16, 2026, as a time of
prayer, reflection, rest, gratitude, and thanksgiving to Almighty God, and as an opportunity
for families and communities to gather in recognition of the blessings of faith, freedom, and
national unity; and
WHEREAS, Jewish-Americans have contributed immeasurably to the cultural,
economic, scientific, educational, military, and civic development of the United States since
the founding of the nation; and
WHEREAS, among the early patriots who supported the cause of American liberty
and independence was Haym Salomon, a Jewish patriot and financier who played a critical
role in supporting the Continental Congress and aiding the Revolutionary War effort through
financial services and personal sacrifice, helping to sustain the cause of liberty during the
nation's founding struggle for freedom; and

WHEREAS, President George Washington, in his historic 1790 letter to the Hebrew
Congregation in Newport, Rhode Island, articulated one of the enduring principles of
American religious liberty, declaring that the United States "gives to bigotry no sanction, to
persecution no assistance", a statement that continues to reflect the nation's commitment to
religious freedom and equal protection under the law for all people; and
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is enriched by Jewish citizens and institutions that
have contributed to the arts, business, medicine, law, education, charitable endeavors, public
leadership, and the preservation of religious liberty throughout the state's history; and
WHEREAS, Christians acknowledge with gratitude the enduring witness and
heritage of the Jewish people, through whom the Scriptures were preserved and through
whose long historical experience and testimony the broader principles of faith, religious
identity, and religious liberty have been strengthened and advanced; and
WHEREAS, Israel and in particular Jerusalem, Judea, and Samaria are historically
and Biblically significant regions of Israel that hold profound religious and cultural
importance in the shared heritage of Judaism and Christianity and are among the principal
places where the Holy Bible records many events of the Hebrew prophets and the life and
ministry of Jesus Christ; and
WHEREAS, the enduring influence of Israel, and especially Jerusalem, Judea, and
Samaria, is reflected throughout Louisiana and the United States in the names of
communities, churches, religious institutions, and places inspired by the history, geography,
and traditions of the Holy Land and our shared Judeo-Christian heritage; and
WHEREAS, May is nationally recognized as Jewish American Heritage Month, first
proclaimed in 2006 and observed annually to celebrate the accomplishments and heritage
of Jewish-Americans; and
WHEREAS, in further recognition of Jewish American Heritage Month and in
anticipation of the upcoming 250th anniversary of the United States of America, the Senate
of the Legislature of Louisiana joins with other states across the nation in encouraging
Christians and persons of all faith traditions to join with their Jewish neighbors in observing
a Shabbat from sundown on May 15, 2026, through nightfall on May 16, 2026, as a time of
prayer, reflection, rest, gratitude, and thanksgiving to Almighty God.
Page 2 of 3
SR NO. 120 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby commend Louisiana's Jewish-American citizens for their contributions to the
state and nation, recognizes May 2026 as Jewish American Heritage Month in Louisiana,
and joins with other states in encouraging Christians and persons of all faith traditions to join
with their Jewish neighbors in observing a Shabbat from sundown on May 15, 2026, through
nightfall on May 16, 2026, as a time of prayer, reflection, gratitude, and thanksgiving to
Almighty God in celebration of the upcoming 250th anniversary of the United States.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
Consulate General of Israel to the Southwest, in Houston, Texas, for distribution to
appropriate representatives of the Jewish communities in Louisiana and such other officials
or entities as deemed appropriate.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
