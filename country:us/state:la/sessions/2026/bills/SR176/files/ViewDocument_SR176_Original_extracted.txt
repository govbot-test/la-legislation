SLS 26RS-3982 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 176
BY SENATOR MIZELL
Regents to study the feasibility of student identification cards being used for digital
identification verification.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the office of alcohol and tobacco control and the Board of Regents to
3 jointly study the feasibility, security implications, and administrative requirements
4 of enabling student identification cards issued by Louisiana postsecondary
5 institutions to function as a form of digital identification verification, and to report
6 findings to the legislature.
7 WHEREAS, Louisiana's public and private postsecondary institutions issue student
8 identification cards that are widely used for campus access, meal plans, library services, and
9 other institutional functions; and
10 WHEREAS, rapid advancements in digital identity technology have expanded the
11 potential uses of secure, institution-issued identification credentials; and
12 WHEREAS, the office of alcohol and tobacco control (ATC) is responsible for
13 regulating and enforcing state laws related to the sale of alcohol and tobacco products,
14 including the verification of legally required identification; and
15 WHEREAS, the Board of Regents oversees the state's public higher education
16 systems and maintains statewide policies related to student records, data security, and
17 institutional operations; and
18 WHEREAS, several states and private-sector entities have begun exploring or

1 implementing digital identification systems capable of verifying age or identity through
2 secure, encrypted platforms; and
3 WHEREAS, Louisiana may benefit from evaluating whether student identification
4 cards, physical or digital, could be integrated into a secure digital verification framework
5 that meets ATC standards for age and identity verification; and
6 WHEREAS, such a study would allow policymakers to assess potential benefits,
7 including convenience for students, modernization of verification processes, and reduction
8 of fraudulent identification, while also evaluating risks related to privacy, cybersecurity, and
9 institutional capacity.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
11 does hereby urge and request the office of alcohol and tobacco control and the Board of
12 Regents to conduct a joint study on the feasibility of allowing student identification cards
13 issued by Louisiana postsecondary institutions to be used for digital identification
14 verification, including but not limited to age verification for alcohol and tobacco purchases.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, at minimum:
16 (1) An evaluation of technological requirements and security standards necessary for
17 digital verification.
18 (2) An assessment of privacy and data-protection implications for students and
19 institutions, as well as savings for both.
20 (3) A review of potential costs, funding needs, and implementation timelines.
21 (4) Consultation with Louisiana higher education systems, campus security officials,
22 relevant technology experts, and best practices from other states.
23 (5) Identification of any statutory or regulatory changes required to authorize such
24 use.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the office of alcohol and tobacco control and the
26 Board of Regents shall submit a written report of findings and recommendations to the
27 Senate Committee on Judiciary B and the Senate Committee on Education no later than
28 February 1, 2027.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
30 commissioner of the office of alcohol and tobacco control and the commissioner of higher
