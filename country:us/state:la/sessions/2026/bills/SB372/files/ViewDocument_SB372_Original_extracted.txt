BY SENATOR BASS
HEALTH/ACC INSURANCE. Provides relative to pharmacy benefit managers. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 22:1870(D)(1), relative to pharmacy benefit managers; to
3 provide authority to the commissioner of insurance; and to provide for related
4 matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 22:1870(D)(1) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §1870. Pharmacy benefit manager transparency report; examination by
8 commissioner
9 * * *
10 D.(1)(a) The commissioner may examine the books or records of a pharmacy
11 benefit manager to determine the accuracy of the transparency report. The
12 commissioner shall have access to any information he considers necessary to
13 determine the accuracy of the transparency report including but not limited to
14 individual amounts paid by a health insurance issuer to the pharmacy benefit
15 manager for drugs, devices, or services provided by a pharmacist or pharmacy, and
16 the individual amount a pharmacy benefit manager paid to a pharmacist or pharmacy
17 for the same drug, device, or service.

1 (b) The commissioner may also examine the books or records of any
2 entity in a pharmacy benefit manager's corporate vertical structure, including
3 but not limited to the insurer, group purchasing organization, manufacturer,
4 wholesale distributor, special or mail order pharmacy, retail or long-term care
5 pharmacy, and provider.

The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 372 Original 2026 Regular Session Bass
Present law requires a pharmacy benefit manager (PBM) to submit an annual transparency
report to the commissioner of insurance. Further allows the commissioner of insurance to
examine the books or records of a PBM to determine the accuracy of the report. Proposed
law retains present law.
Proposed law allows the commissioner to also examine the books or records of any entity
in the PBM's corporate vertical integration structure.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 22:1870(D)(1))
