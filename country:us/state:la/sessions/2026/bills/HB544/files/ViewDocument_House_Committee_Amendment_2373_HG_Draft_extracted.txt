20 "§1300.46. Notice of election; expenses
21 A. When a citizens advisory referendum election is called pursuant to the
22 provisions of this Chapter, the governing authority shall give written notice of the
23 election to the secretary of state, the commissioner of elections, and each clerk of
24 court and registrar of voters in the area affected by the election. If the election is to
25 be held on a primary election date, such notice shall be received by the secretary of
26 state at least four weeks prior to the opening of the qualifying period for the primary
27 election. If the election is not to be held on a primary election date, such notice shall
28 be received by the secretary of state on or before the fifty-fourth day prior to the
29 election. If the deadline for receipt by the secretary of state falls on a Saturday,
30 Sunday, or legal holiday, then the next day which is not a Saturday, Sunday, or legal
31 holiday shall be deemed to be the deadline for receipt by the secretary of state.
32 B.(1) The state shall pay one-half of the cost of ballots and election materials
33 used in a citizens advisory referendum election. The remaining one-half of the cost
34 shall be prorated between the state and all parish governing authorities participating
35 in the citizens advisory referendum election.
