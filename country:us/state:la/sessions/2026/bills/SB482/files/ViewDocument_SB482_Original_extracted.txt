DIGEST
SB 482 Original 2026 Regular Session Reese
Present law provides for middle and high school students and their parents to receive career
counseling from a school counselor to develop a career plan so that every student is afforded
the opportunity to obtain a high-demand and high-wage job after high school graduation or
after college. Present law provides for the development and use of Individual Graduation
Plans (IGPs) for public school students to achieve those career outcomes. Present law
requires that, by the end of the eighth grade, each student, with assistance from a parent or
legal guardian and a school counselor, begin development of an IGP to guide coursework
and postsecondary planning. Present law provides for an annual review of the plan and that,
upon completion of the review, the plan shall be signed by the student, the parent or legal

Proposed law retains present law and requires career coaches to also assist the students, the
parents, and school counselors in the preparation and review of the student's IGP.
Present law requires the state Board of Elementary and Secondary Education (BESE) to
develop an advisement policy for middle and high schools to use to equip school counselors
with the information needed to help each student do all of the following:
(1) Identify the student's career goals.
(2) Develop and revise the student's individualized graduation plan.
(3) Identify high-demand, high-wage jobs in the region.
(4) Identify dual enrollment opportunities whereby a student may attend college courses
while still enrolled in high school so as to advance the student's career goals.
Present law requires the state Dept. of Education to provide professional development and
guidance for school counselors in furtherance of BESE policy to equip school counselors to
assist students in achieving their career goals.
Proposed law retains present law but requires that students also receive counseling from
career coaches to assist students with career planning.
Proposed law provides that the state Dept. of Education is to publish on its website a list of
high quality career coaching vendors from which schools may select a vendor. Proposed law
allows public school boards to form regional consortia to share the costs of contracting with
career coaches.
Proposed law provides that each public school board shall include a description of its plan
to provide career coaching to each student as part of the pupil progression plan developed
for accountability purposes.
Proposed law requires that the Dept. of Education annually publish on its website a list of
each public school board that has not spent more than 95% of the career development fund
dollars appropriated for the previous fiscal year.
Effective August 1, 2026.
