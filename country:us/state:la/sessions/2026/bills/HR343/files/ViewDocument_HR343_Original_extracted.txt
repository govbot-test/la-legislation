HLS 26RS-5920 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 343
BY REPRESENTATIVE FIRMENT
CONGRESS: Memorializes Congress to oppose any effort to transfer, divest, privatize, or
dispose of Kisatchie National Forest lands or otherwise remove such lands from
federal ownership and management
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To memorialize the United States Congress to oppose to any effort to transfer, divest,
3 privatize, or otherwise remove portions of the Kisatchie National Forest located
4 within Grant Parish from federal public ownership; to recognize the extraordinary
5 natural, cultural, recreational, and historical importance of the Kisatchie National
6 Forest to Grant Parish and the state of Louisiana; to express the unwavering
7 opposition of any transfer efforts by the citizens of Grant Parish and the state of
8 Louisiana; and to further urge increased federal and state investment in outdoor
9 recreation, conservation, tourism, and family-friendly opportunities associated with
10 the forest.
11 WHEREAS, the Kisatchie National Forest stands as one of Louisiana's greatest
12 natural treasures and represents a vast and irreplaceable landscape of towering pines, rolling
13 hills, hardwood bottoms, winding creeks, pristine lakes, scenic trails, and abundant wildlife
14 that together form a living testament to the beauty and spirit of central Louisiana; and
15 WHEREAS, Grant Parish occupies a uniquely honored place within the state of
16 Louisiana because it contains more acreage of the Kisatchie National Forest than any other
17 parish in the state, a distinction that generations of Grant Parish citizens have embraced with
18 profound pride, gratitude, and reverence; and

1 WHEREAS, the people of Grant Parish have long understood that the Kisatchie
2 National Forest is far more than merely public land, but is instead an essential part of the
3 parish's identity, heritage, culture, economy, and way of life; and
4 WHEREAS, countless families throughout Grant Parish have built lifelong memories
5 within the forest through hunting, fishing, camping, hiking, horseback riding, birdwatching,
6 boating, trail riding, wildlife observing, and countless other outdoor traditions that have been
7 lovingly passed down from one generation to the next; and
8 WHEREAS, the forests, streams, and wilderness areas of Kisatchie provide a refuge
9 from the pressures of modern life and offer families, children, sportsmen, conservationists,
10 veterans, and visitors alike the rare opportunity to experience quiet solitude, natural beauty,
11 outdoor adventure, and a meaningful connection to God's creation; and
12 WHEREAS, the Kisatchie National Forest serves as an economic driver for Grant
13 Parish and surrounding communities through tourism, outdoor recreation, sporting activities,
14 hospitality, forestry, and small business development, while simultaneously preserving the
15 scenic character and rural heritage that make Grant Parish so exceptional; and
16 WHEREAS, the preservation of large, publicly accessible tracts of undeveloped
17 wilderness is becoming increasingly rare throughout the United States, making the Kisatchie
18 National Forest an even more valuable and irreplaceable asset for future generations of
19 Louisianians; and
20 WHEREAS, the citizens of Grant Parish deeply value the principle that the Kisatchie
21 National Forest belongs to the people and should remain protected and accessible for the
22 enjoyment, recreation, and benefit of present and future generations; and
23 WHEREAS, recent discussions and proposals concerning the possible transfer of
24 portions of the Kisatchie National Forest from federal ownership to ownership by the state,
25 local governmental entities, private interests, or other nonfederal ownership structures have
26 generated significant concern among the citizens of Grant Parish and throughout central
27 Louisiana; and
28 WHEREAS, while the authors and supporters of such proposals may be motivated
29 by sincere and well-intended concerns regarding land management, economic development,
30 local control, or governmental efficiency, the citizens of Grant Parish nevertheless firmly

1 believe that the long-term risks associated with fragmentation, privatization, restricted public
2 access, commercial overdevelopment, and potential loss of conservation protections
3 outweigh any perceived benefits of such transfers; and
4 WHEREAS, the desire of the people of Grant Parish is not the reduction or
5 divestiture of the Kisatchie National Forest, but rather renewed investment in the forest and
6 its surrounding communities through enhanced recreational infrastructure, expanded
7 family-friendly activities, improved campgrounds and trails, increased conservation efforts,
8 tourism promotion, and greater opportunities for outdoor education and public enjoyment;
9 and
10 WHEREAS, the Kisatchie National Forest represents a sacred inheritance entrusted
11 to the current generation for careful stewardship and preservation so that future generations
12 may continue to experience the same freedom, beauty, outdoor traditions, and sense of
13 wonder that have defined life in Grant Parish for decades.
14 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
15 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby memorialize the United States Congress to oppose any
16 effort to transfer, privatize, divest, or otherwise remove portions of the Kisatchie National
17 Forest located within Grant Parish from federal public ownership.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives does hereby
19 recognize the extraordinary importance of the Kisatchie National Forest to the heritage,
20 identity, culture, economy, recreation, and quality of life of the citizens of Grant Parish and
21 the state of Louisiana.
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives respectfully urges
23 and requests the Congress of the United States to oppose proposals seeking to transfer or
24 privatize portions of the Kisatchie National Forest and to instead work collaboratively with
25 local communities to preserve and strengthen public access, conservation, and responsible
26 stewardship of the forest.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives urges and requests
28 increased federal and state investment in outdoor recreation, tourism, conservation, trails,
29 campgrounds, youth programs, family-oriented activities, wildlife habitat management, and

otherwise remove portions of the Kisatchie National Forest located within Grant Parish from

historical importance of the Kisatchie National Forest to Grant Parish and the state of

Grant Parish and the state of Louisiana; and urges increased federal and state investment in
outdoor recreation, conservation, tourism, and family-friendly opportunities associated with
the forest.
