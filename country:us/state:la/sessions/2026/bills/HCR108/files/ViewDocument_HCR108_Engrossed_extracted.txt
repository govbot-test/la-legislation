HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 108
BY REPRESENTATIVE WRIGHT
INTERNATIONAL AFFAIRS: Encourages further economic ties and friendship between
Louisiana and Taiwan
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To encourage further economic ties and friendship between the state of Louisiana and the
3 Republic of China (Taiwan).
4 WHEREAS, the longstanding bonds of friendship, economic ties, and bonds of
5 friendship between the United States and the Republic of China (Taiwan), embodied in the
6 Taiwan Relations Act of 1979, continue to yield important economic, social, strategic, and
7 cultural benefits; and
8 WHEREAS, in 2025, Taiwan ranked fourth on the Heritage Foundation's Index of
9 Economic Freedom, holds an important position in the global economy as a top player in the
10 world's information and communications technology industry, and is a trusted partner for
11 economic cooperation and exchanges in industries like smart machinery, the Internet of
12 Things, biotechnology, energy technology, national defense, semiconductors, and the
13 circular economy; and
14 WHEREAS, U.S.-Taiwan trade reached two hundred fifty-six billion dollars in total
15 goods trade according to 2025 U.S. data, and Taiwan ranks as the United States' eighth
16 largest export market for American agricultural products and as the United States' fifth
17 largest merchandise trading partner; and
18 WHEREAS, the robust U.S.-Taiwan trade and investment partnership provides a
19 solid foundation for a mutually beneficial bilateral trade agreement to spur job creation,
20 expand the global market, and secure international, democratic supply chains; and

1 WHEREAS, Taiwan is a pivotal force for stability in the Indo-Pacific that amplifies
2 the values of democracy, peace, and prosperity by partnering with like-minded countries
3 through integrated diplomacy to address global challenges; and
4 WHEREAS, Taiwanese officials and civil society groups partner with the United
5 States on a range of issues to promote our common values of freedom, democracy, and
6 human rights; and
7 WHEREAS, since 1985, Louisiana and Taiwan have maintained a sister-state
8 relationship and enjoy particularly close economic ties; and
9 WHEREAS, Louisiana and the Unites States have benefitted immeasurably through
10 decades of unwavering friendship with Taiwan and the wider world is sure to reap
11 tremendous rewards as Taiwan works to enhance its presence in the international
12 community.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 express support for strengthening our partnership with Taiwan, for the expansion of Taiwan's
15 role on the global stage, and for its efforts to preserve peace and stability in the Taiwan Strait
16 through meaningful dialogue.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana supports a tax
18 agreement between the United States and Taiwan, supports the goals of the Critical Undersea
19 Infrastructure Resilience Initiative Act to prioritize the protection and resilience of undersea
20 cables near Taiwan, with a focus on countering gray zone tactics from the People's Republic
21 of China, and maintains that United Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758 cannot be
22 used to exclude Taiwan from participation in the United Nations System and other
23 international fora such as the International Civil Aviation Organization, the International
24 Criminal Police Organization, and the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate
25 Change.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana celebrates the
27 forty-seventh anniversary of the Taiwan Relations Act and the forty-first anniversary of the
28 sister-state relationship between the state of Louisiana and Taiwan.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
30 Taipei Economic and Cultural Office in Houston.
