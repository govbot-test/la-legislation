CONFERENCE COMMITTEE REPORT DIGEST
HB 302 2026 Regular Session Chassion
Keyword and oneliner of the instrument as it left the House
TOBACCO/TOBACCO PRODUCTS: Prohibits the sale of vapor products near schools
Report adopts Senate amendments to:
1. Provide that proposed law does not apply to lawful marijuana or marijuana products
authorized pursuant to present law and regulated by the La. Dept. of Health.
Report rejects Senate amendments which would have:
1. Authorized a municipality or parish to impose requirements for the sale of vapor,
tobacco, or alternative nicotine products that are more stringent than the requirements
in proposed law.
2. Required an applicant for a tobacco retail license to demonstrate compliance with
applicable municipal or parish ordinances.
Digest of the bill as proposed by the Conference Committee
Present law defines "vapor product" as any non-combustible product containing nicotine or
other substances that employs a heating element, power source, electronic circuit, or other
electronic, chemical, or mechanical means, regardless of shape or size, that can be used to
produce vapor from nicotine in a solution or other form.
Present law further provides that "vapor product" includes any electronic cigarette, electronic
cigar, electronic cigarillo, electronic pipe, or similar product or device and any vapor
cartridge or other container of nicotine in a solution or other form that is intended to be used
with or in an electronic cigarette, electronic cigar, electronic cigarillo, electronic pipe, or
similar product or device.
Present law provides relative to retail dealer permits and the renewal of retail dealer permits
for the sale of vapor products.
Proposed law retains present law and prohibits, pursuant to a municipal or parish ordinance,
the sale, offer for sale, storage, or maintenance of stock of vapor products on a premises
situated within 300 feet of a school
Proposed law provides that in municipalities and in unincorporated areas divided into
subdivisions that the measurement of the 300 feet is to be measured as a person walks using
the sidewalk from the nearest point of the property line of the school to the nearest point of
the premises to be licensed.
Proposed law authorizes the municipality or parish to establish an alternate method of
measurement of the 300 foot limitation by measuring in a straight line from the nearest point
of the property line of the school to the nearest point of the premises to be licensed.

Proposed law provides that the alternative method of measurement applies only prospectively
to a new permit issued on or after the date the alternative method of measurement has been
adopted.
Proposed law does not apply to lawful marijuana or marijuana products authorized pursuant
to present law and regulated by the La. Dept. of Health.
