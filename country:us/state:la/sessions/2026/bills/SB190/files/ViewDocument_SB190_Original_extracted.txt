SLS 26RS-259 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR MIZELL
NURSING HOMES. Provides relative to nursing home licenses. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:2009.10.2, relative to nursing facilities; to provide for the Centers for
3 Medicare and Medicaid Services Special Focus Facility Program; to provide for
4 nursing facility candidates for the program; to provide for state requirements for
5 nursing facilities; to provide for the duties and responsibilities of the Louisiana
6 Department of Health; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 40:2009.10.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §2009.10.2. Special Focus Facility Program; candidates; state requirements
10 A. Any nursing facility licensed by the Louisiana Department of Health
11 that is identified by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services as a
12 candidate for participation in the Special Focus Facility Program due to
13 persistent poor performance on surveys which include obtaining an immediate
14 jeopardy deficiency, a deficiency cited as abuse or actual harm, or a deficiency
15 for the potential for more than minimal harm to residents, shall be subject to
16 enhanced state oversight regardless of whether the nursing facility is selected
17 for participation in the Special Focus Facility Program.

1 B. Any nursing facility identified in Subsection A of this Section shall:
2 (1) Provide notice to both prospective and current residents that the
3 facility is a candidate for placement in the Special Focus Facility Program due
4 to the scope and severity of persistent noncompliance with state and federal
5 requirements that could put their health and safety at risk.
6 (2) Provide current residents the opportunity to transfer to another
7 available facility if the resident can be transferred safely and there is an
8 alternative nursing facility with availability that meets the resident's
9 geographical, physical health, and mental health needs.
10 (3) Comply with the following state requirements in addition to any
11 requirements imposed by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services:
12 (a) Be subject to state-appointed monitoring.
13 (b) Be subject to a minimum of quarterly in-person unannounced
14 inspections.
15 (c) Enter into a directed plan of correction with the department.
16 (d) Hold directed, in-service training.
17 (e) Be subject to a root cause analysis to determine the basis for its poor
18 performance.
19 (f) Be subject to an owner or license holder analysis to identify a pattern
20 of poor performance under the same owner.
21 (g) Be responsible for immediate staffing increases as needed to address
22 deficiencies.
23 (h) Engage with a Quality Improvement Organization approved by the
24 Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services.
25 C. If a nursing facility is selected for the Special Focus Facility Program
26 and fails to meet the requirements for graduation from the program and the
27 additional requirements imposed by the department in accordance with
28 Subsection B of this Section within eighteen months, the Louisiana Department
29 of Health shall revoke the facility's license.

1 D. A nursing facility shall not be allowed to enter into the Special Focus
2 Facility Program more than once. If a nursing facility graduates from the
3 Special Focus Facility Program and continues to be a poor-performing facility,
4 the department shall take action to best protect the facility's residents, including
5 revoking the owner's license and securing a new license holder and owner to
6 continue operating at the same location or revoking the license, closing the
7 facility, and relocating the residents to alternative nearby facilities.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 190 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Proposed law establishes state requirements to correspond with the federal requirements
established by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) Special Focus
Facility (SFF) program which focuses on nursing homes that have a persistent record of
noncompliance leading to poor quality of care.
Proposed law requires that any nursing facility licensed by the La. Dept. of Health that is
identified by CMS as a candidate for participation in the Special Focus Facility Program be
subject to enhanced state oversight regardless of whether the nursing facility is selected for
participation in the program.
Proposed law requires the nursing facilities to notify prospective and current residents of the
candidacy for the program and give the residents an opportunity to transfer to another
nursing facility.
Proposed law requires the nursing facilities to comply with state requirements in addition
to CMS requirements for the program.
Proposed law requires the La. Dept. of Health to revoke the license of any nursing facility
that fails to meet the requirements for graduation from the program and additional
requirements imposed by the department within 18 months.
Proposed law prohibits a nursing facility to enter into the program more than once and
requires the La. Dept. of Health to take action to protect the residents of a continuously poor-
performing facility.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 40:2009.10.2)
