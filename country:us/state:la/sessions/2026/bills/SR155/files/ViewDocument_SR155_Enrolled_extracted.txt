2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 155
BY SENATOR MILLER
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and evaluate legislation
relative to the creation of mineral servitudes in connection with certain economic
development projects.
WHEREAS, R.S. 31:149 generally addresses the circumstances under which a
mineral servitude will be imprescriptible, such that the prescription accruing against a
mineral servitude will not run for the period of time, and under the circumstances, specified
in R.S. 31:149; and
WHEREAS, Subsection I of R.S. 31:149 exists as an exception to Subsection A
through H of R.S. 31:149 under circumstances involving certain economic development
projects in which a cooperative endeavor agreement is executed and the project is certified
by the secretary of Louisiana Economic Development, and the mineral servitude is subject
to the prescription of nonuse for a period of twenty years; and
WHEREAS, significant issues have arisen concerning the circumstances proposed
in Senate Bill No. 492 of the 2026 Regular Session of the Legislature as they pertain to the
established regime of mineral servitudes.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and recommend
legislation relative to the issues raised in Senate Bill No. 492 of the 2026 Regular Session
of the Legislature and make recommendations and to report its findings to the Legislature
of Louisiana no later than February 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
