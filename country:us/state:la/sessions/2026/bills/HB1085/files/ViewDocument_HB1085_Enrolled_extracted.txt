13 provide for severability; and to provide for related matters.

15 §357. Windshields required
16 A. Every passenger vehicle, other than a motorcycle, and every motor truck
17 or truck tractor, and every authorized emergency vehicle shall be equipped with an
18 adequate windshield.
19 B. Every noncommercial vehicle registered in this state shall be required to
20 maintain the integrity and condition of the windshield. The standards for
21 windshields are as follows:
22 (1) In the eight and one-half inch by eleven inch "acute area" directly in the
23 driver's line of vision there shall be no cracks and there shall be no more than two
24 stars, nicks, chips, bullseyes, or half-moons in excess of one-half inch.
25 (2) In the "critical area" cleaned by the normal sweep of the windshield
26 wiper blade on the driver's side of the windshield there shall be no star which is
27 larger than two inches in diameter; there shall be no more than two stars larger than
28 one and one-half inches in diameter; and there shall be no more than two cracks
29 longer than eight inches; or any combination thereof.

15 §1301. Vehicles without required equipment or in unsafe condition
16 No person shall drive or move on any highway any motor vehicle, low-speed
17 vehicle as defined in R.S. 32:1(40)(50), trailer, semitrailer, or pole trailer, or any
18 combination thereof, unless the equipment upon any such vehicle is in good working
19 order and adjustment as required in this Chapter and the vehicle is in such safe
20 mechanical condition as not to endanger the driver or other occupant or any person
21 upon the highway. Every motor vehicle, low-speed vehicle, trailer, semitrailer, and
22 pole trailer vehicle registered in this state which is required to be inspected pursuant
23 to the provisions of this Chapter shall bear a valid safety inspection certificate issued
24 in the state of Louisiana, except as otherwise provided by this Chapter.
