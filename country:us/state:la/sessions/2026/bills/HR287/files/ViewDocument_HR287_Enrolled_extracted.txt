HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 287
BY REPRESENTATIVE SCHLEGEL
A RESOLUTION
To commend the Ready Louisiana Coalition and to designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as
Early Ed Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, the Ready Louisiana Coalition is a bipartisan statewide organization that
was formed in 2018 and is comprised of more than two hundred businesses, chambers,
advocacy organizations, and early childhood education professionals seeking sufficient
investment in quality and affordable early childhood education in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, members of the Ready Louisiana Coalition believe that investing in
quality and affordable early childhood education will prepare students for a smart start in
life, enable families to be productive in the workforce, and serve as an economic engine for
local communities; and
WHEREAS, research shows that early experiences affect the development of the
brain's architecture and provide the foundation for future learning, health, and social
behaviors; and
WHEREAS, early years form the basis for cognition, literacy and learning, social and
emotional development, physical development, and more; and
WHEREAS, ninety percent of brain development takes place between birth and age
four, and because the early years are when the foundation of the brain is built, children who
begin school behind generally remain behind; and
WHEREAS, children who receive quality and affordable early childhood education
experience long-term improved outcomes in education, health, and social behaviors; and

WHEREAS, in Louisiana, the average family can spend as much on child care as
they do on in-state college tuition, and these expenses are often a considerable part of their
household budget; and
WHEREAS, the current lack of quality and affordable early childhood education
harms all families and disproportionately impacts children of color and those in poverty
during this critical age for early brain development; and
WHEREAS, lack of quality and affordable early childhood education affects the
future workforce and has a substantial impact on worker participation and productivity of
the current workforce in the state; and
WHEREAS, research shows that the state can achieve a thirteen percent return on
public investment in quality and affordable early childhood education; and
WHEREAS, child care issues and lost workforce participation and productivity have
resulted in more than $1.3 billion in losses for Louisiana's economy; and
WHEREAS, the Ready Louisiana Coalition works to provide access to quality and
affordable early childhood education to improve children's outcomes, strengthen their
families, and build the economy.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Ready Louisiana Coalition and does
hereby designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as Early Ed Day at the state capitol.
