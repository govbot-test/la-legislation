HLS 26RS-5283 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 287
BY REPRESENTATIVE SCHLEGEL
COMMENDATIONS: Commends the Ready Louisiana Coalition and designates May 19,
2026, as Early Ed Day at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend the Ready Louisiana Coalition and to designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as
3 Early Ed Day at the state capitol.
4 WHEREAS, the Ready Louisiana Coalition is a bipartisan statewide organization that
5 was formed in 2018 and is comprised of more than two hundred businesses, chambers,
6 advocacy organizations, and early childhood education professionals seeking sufficient
7 investment in quality and affordable early childhood education in Louisiana; and
8 WHEREAS, members of the Ready Louisiana Coalition believe that investing in
9 quality and affordable early childhood education will prepare students for a smart start in
10 life, enable families to be productive in the workforce, and serve as an economic engine for
11 local communities; and
12 WHEREAS, research shows that early experiences affect the development of the
13 brain's architecture and provide the foundation for future learning, health, and social
14 behaviors; and
15 WHEREAS, early years form the basis for cognition, literacy and learning, social and
16 emotional development, physical development, and more; and
17 WHEREAS, ninety percent of brain development takes place between birth and age
18 four, and because the early years are when the foundation of the brain is built, children who
19 begin school behind generally remain behind; and
20 WHEREAS, children who receive quality and affordable early childhood education
21 experience long-term improved outcomes in education, health, and social behaviors; and

1 WHEREAS, in Louisiana, the average family can spend as much on child care as
2 they do on in-state college tuition, and these expenses are often a considerable part of their
3 household budget; and
4 WHEREAS, the current lack of quality and affordable early childhood education
5 harms all families and disproportionately impacts children of color and those in poverty
6 during this critical age for early brain development; and
7 WHEREAS, lack of quality and affordable early childhood education affects the
8 future workforce and has a substantial impact on worker participation and productivity of
9 the current workforce in the state; and
10 WHEREAS, research shows that the state can achieve a thirteen percent return on
11 public investment in quality and affordable early childhood education; and
12 WHEREAS, child care issues and lost workforce participation and productivity have
13 resulted in more than $1.3 billion in losses for Louisiana's economy; and
14 WHEREAS, the Ready Louisiana Coalition works to provide access to quality and
15 affordable early childhood education to improve children's outcomes, strengthen their
16 families, and build the economy.
17 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
18 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Ready Louisiana Coalition and does
19 hereby designate Tuesday, May 19, 2026, as Early Ed Day at the state capitol.
