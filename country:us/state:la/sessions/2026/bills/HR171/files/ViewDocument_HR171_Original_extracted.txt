HLS 26RS-3460 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 171
BY REPRESENTATIVE TURNER
Louisiana Works, the state Department of Education, the Louisiana Community and
Technical College System, and the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center,
to study the effectiveness, use, and workforce outcomes of the M.J. Foster Promise
Program and the TOPS-Tech award
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Board of Regents, in coordination with Louisiana Works, the state
3 Department of Education, the Louisiana Community and Technical College System,
4 the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center, and other appropriate
5 stakeholders, to study the effectiveness, utilization, and workforce outcomes of the
6 M.J. Foster Promise Program and the Taylor Opportunity Program for Students Tech
7 Award, including how they align with other financial aid programs, and to report
8 findings to the legislature.
9 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana has made significant investments in workforce
10 development programs intended to increase access to postsecondary training and
11 industry-based credentials for residents seeking employment in high-demand fields; and
12 WHEREAS, the M.J. Foster Promise Program provides financial assistance to
13 eligible students who enroll in a qualified program at a two-year public college or university,
14 or an accredited proprietary school licensed by the Board of Regents to pursue an associate
15 degree or a shorter-term postsecondary education credential required for certain
16 high-demand, high-wage occupations aligned with Louisiana’s workforce priorities as
17 determined by an advisory council pursuant to R.S. 17:3047.1; the Taylor Opportunity
18 Program for Students Tech (TOPS-Tech) Award provides tuition support for up to two years
19 of skill or occupational training at any campus of the Louisiana Community and Technical
Page 1 of 4
HLS 26RS-3460 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 171
1 College System, accredited two- or four-year public colleges or universities, or members of
2 the Louisiana Association of Independent Colleges and Universities to pursue a vocational
3 or technical education certificate, diploma, or non-academic degree program; and
4 WHEREAS, these programs engage a wide network of institutions and workforce
5 training providers, including institutions within the Louisiana Community and Technical
6 College System, Louisiana State University at Eunice, Southern University at Shreveport,
7 approved proprietary schools, and other credentialing providers; and
8 WHEREAS, these programs must remain aligned with industry demand, workforce
9 shortages, and regional economic development priorities in order to maximize the return on
10 investment for the state and its taxpayers; and
11 WHEREAS, an evaluation of the M.J. Foster Promise Program and TOPS-Tech
12 would help determine participation trends, completion outcomes, workforce placement rates,
13 and opportunities for improvement in program delivery and coordination across state
14 agencies; a study and recommendations on state-funded financial aid to simplify, align,
15 streamline, and optimize is crucial to achieving the best return on investment for Louisiana
16 taxpayers.
17 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
18 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Board of Regents, in coordination
19 with Louisiana Works, the state Department of Education, the Louisiana Community and
20 Technical College System, the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center, and other
21 appropriate stakeholders, to study the effectiveness, utilization, and workforce outcomes of
22 the M.J. Foster Promise Program and the TOPS-Tech Award, including how they align with
23 other financial aid programs, and to report findings to the legislature.
24 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that these agencies shall work collaboratively to
25 design the research framework, collect necessary data, and ensure data protection and data
26 security throughout the process.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include but need not be limited
28 to the following:
29 (1) Program participation levels and demographic trends among recipients.

1 (2) The types of credentials, certificates, and training programs funded through each
2 program.
3 (3) Completion and credential attainment rates for program participants.
4 (4) Workforce outcomes, including job placement, wage growth, and retention in
5 high-demand occupations.
6 (5) Program cost comparisons with a return on investment analysis, including
7 estimated state fiscal impact such as tax revenue generated by participants and comparison
8 of program costs to economic outcomes.
9 (6) Possible alignment opportunities between funded programs and current and
10 projected workforce shortages in Louisiana.
11 (7) Recommendations for more efficient coordination between secondary education,
12 postsecondary institutions, and workforce development programs.
13 (8) Recommendations for improving program accessibility, efficiency, and
14 alignment with the workforce needs of employers and the education and training needs of
15 students.
16 (9) Outcomes of program participants disaggregated by age, including but not
17 limited to participants under the age of twenty-one.
18 (10) Comparative analysis of similarly situated individuals who did not receive
19 program funding, including individuals of comparable age, educational attainment, and
20 workforce status, in order to assess differences in employment, earnings, and career
21 progression associated with participation in the M.J. Foster Promise Program and the TOPS-
22 Tech Award.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Board of Regents shall provide a
24 comparative analysis of all state-funded financial aid programs including an evaluation of
25 any gaps in financial support that create barriers to participant access.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Board of Regents shall submit a written
27 report of its findings and recommendations to the House Committee on Education, the House
28 Committee on Labor and Industrial Relations, and the House Committee on Appropriations,
29 not later than February 15, 2027.

2 commissioner of higher education, the secretary of Louisiana Works, the state
3 superintendent of education, the president of the Louisiana Community and Technical

HR 171 Original 2026 Regular Session Turner
Requests the Bd. of Regents, in coordination with La. Works, the state Dept. of Education,
La. Community and Technical College System, the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public
Policy Center, and other appropriate stakeholders, to study the effectiveness, utilization, and
workforce outcomes of the M.J. Foster Promise Program and the TOPS-Tech Award,
including how they align with other financial aid programs, and to report findings to the
legislature not later than Feb. 15, 2027.
