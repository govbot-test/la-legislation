HB 1214 Original 2026 Regular Session Miller
Abstract: Establishes the office of state health care facilities within the La. Dept. of Health
and provides for the administration of certain state healthcare facilities.
The Office of State Health Care Facilities
Proposed law establishes the office of state health care facilities (OSHCF) within the La.
Dept. of Health (LDH).
Proposed law provides that OSHCF is responsible for leading and coordinating LDH in the
operation of state healthcare facilities.
Proposed law provides that OSHCF shall administer and coordinate all of the following
healthcare facilities:
(1) Eastern Louisiana Mental Health System.
(2) Central Louisiana State Hospital.
(3) Feliciana Forensic Facility.
(4) Villa Feliciana Medical Complex.
(5) Pinecrest Supports and Services Center.
(6) Central Louisiana Supports and Services Center.
(7) Any other licensed state healthcare facility upon appropriation by the legislature.
Proposed law provides that LDH shall employ an executive director of OSHF.
Proposed law establishes that each administrator or chief executive officer of a facility under
the authority of OSHF shall report directly to the executive director of OSHF.
Proposed law provides that OSHF may establish and administer additional state healthcare
facilities subject to funding by the legislature.

Psychiatric Hospitals
Present law provides that the office of behavioral health, within LDH, may oversee the
operation of Eastern Louisiana Mental Health System and Central Louisiana State Hospital.
Present law further provides that the office of behavioral health may establish residential
facilities as satellite facilities to such health system or hospital with funds allocated to the
hospitals by the legislature.
Proposed law transfers the responsibilities of present law to OSHCF and provides that
OSHCF may open independent facilities.
Present law provides that the site of a satellite facility selected by the assistant secretary of
the office of behavioral health must be approved by the local governing authority.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Present law provides that LDH may establish additional forensic facilities, for the treatment
of patients deemed incompetent to stand trial or were otherwise incarcerated, in New
Orleans, Baton Rouge, Shreveport, and Alexandria.
Proposed law adds that additional forensic facilities may be established in areas surrounding
the locations provided for in present law.
Facilities for Individuals with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities
Present law provides that facilities, known as "developmental centers" for individuals with
intellectual and developmental disabilities shall be operated and administered by the office
of citizens with developmental disabilities.
Present law establishes that LDH shall operate the following developmental centers:
(1) Pinecrest Supports and Services Center.
(2) Northwest Supports and Services Center.
(3) North Lake Supports and Services Center.
(4) Central Louisiana Supports and Services Center.
Proposed law removes Northwest Supports and Services Center and North Lake Supports
and Services Center.
Proposed law provides that OSHCF shall operate and administer the Pinecrest Supports and
Services Center and Central Louisiana Supports and Services center with the support of the
office of citizens with developmental disabilities.
Present law provides that the facility administrators of each developmental center shall
recommend polices and procedures to the secretary of LDH for the operation of the center.
Proposed law modifies present law providing that the facility administrator shall recommend
polices and procedures to the executive director of OSHCF.
Present law provides that by receiving services from and living in a licensed, state-operated,
24-hour residential facility operated by or through the office of citizens with developmental
disabilities the person shall be deemed to have made an assignment to any property owned
to the office up to the cost of services.
Proposed law modifies present law providing that LDH shall receive the assignment of
property.

Miscellaneous
Proposed law provides that the secretary shall directly control the executive office of the
secretary and OSHCF.
Proposed law provides that in the event of an extended absence or vacancy in the position
of an assistant secretary at LDH, the secretary has the authority to act on behalf or in lieu of
the assistant secretary including as appointing authority for personnel actions.
Present law provides that the office of aging and adult services shall administer state-
operated nursing homes and Villa Feliciana Medical Complex.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Proposed law provides that LDH may transfer funding and positions from within the
department to establish and implement the provisions of proposed law.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
