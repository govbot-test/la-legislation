SLS 26RS-2127 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 35
BY SENATOR FOIL
NOTARIES. To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use of
remote online notarization for executing certain authentic acts.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and formulate proposed
3 legislation to amend the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and related laws, as it
4 deems appropriate, to authorize the use of remote online notarization for executing
5 authentic acts as defined in Civil Code Article 1833, excluding those instruments
6 prohibited in R.S. 35:623(B) or authentic acts deemed inappropriate for remote
7 online notarization, and to report its findings and proposed legislation to the
8 Louisiana Legislature.
9 WHEREAS, Act No. 254 of the 2020 Regular Session of the Louisiana Legislature
10 created the "Remote Online Notarization Act"; and
11 WHEREAS, the "Remote Online Notarization Act" provides for the process and
12 requirements necessary to become and remain authorized to perform remote online
13 notarizations, including the requirement that the secretary of state may only authorize a
14 regularly commissioned notary public who holds a valid notarial commission in this state
15 to conduct remote online notarizations; and
16 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:626 sets forth requirements for the locations of the notary
17 public, the parties, and any witnesses involved in a remote online notarization; and
18 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:627 provides procedures for a notary public to verify the

1 identity of parties and witnesses to a remote online notarial act and requires a notary public
2 to electronically and digitally sign the act; and
3 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:629 requires a notary public to maintain electronic copies and
4 audio-video recordings of each remote online notarization for at least ten years from the date
5 of the notarization; and
6 WHEREAS, audio and video conferencing technology has evolved since the passage
7 of the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and is becoming more widely available and used
8 by notaries throughout Louisiana and in other states to electronically authenticate documents
9 through remote online notarization; and
10 WHEREAS, for many years certain rural areas of Louisiana have faced a shortage
11 of notaries, which makes the facilitation of commerce more difficult and costly for residents
12 of those areas; and
13 WHEREAS, the use of remote online notarization provides an opportunity to better
14 serve residents in rural areas and facilitate commerce and banking by authentic act; and
15 WHEREAS, in Louisiana, an authentic act is required to be executed before a notary
16 public and two witnesses, including signatures of all parties, witnesses, and the notary; and
17 WHEREAS, Louisiana law further provides that certain instruments, including
18 authentic acts, are not permitted to be executed by remote online notarization; and
19 WHEREAS, other civil law jurisdictions like France and Quebec permit the
20 execution of authentic acts via remote online notarization; and
21 WHEREAS, allowing the execution of authentic acts in Louisiana through remote
22 online notarization may expand the business case and possibilities for using remote online
23 notarization.
24 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
25 urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and formulate proposed
26 legislation to amend the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and related laws, as it deems
27 appropriate, to authorize the use of remote online notarization for executing authentic acts
28 as defined in Civil Code Article 1833, excluding those instruments prohibited in R.S.
29 35:623(B) or authentic acts deemed inappropriate for remote online notarization, and to
30 report its findings and proposed legislation to the Louisiana Legislature.

SCR 35 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Foil
Requests the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and formulate proposed legislation to
amend the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and related laws, as it deems appropriate, to
authorize the use of remote online notarization for executing authentic acts as defined in
Civil Code Article 1833, excluding those instruments prohibited in R.S. 35:623(B) or
authentic acts deemed inappropriate for online notarization, and to report its findings and
proposed legislation to the Louisiana Legislature.
