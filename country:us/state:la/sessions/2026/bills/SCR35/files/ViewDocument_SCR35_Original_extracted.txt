SLS 26RS-2127 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 35
BY SENATOR FOIL
remote online notarization for executing certain authentic acts.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and formulate proposed
3 legislation to amend the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and related laws, as
4 necessary, to authorize the use of remote online notarization for executing authentic
5 acts as defined in Civil Code Article 1833, excluding those instruments prohibited
6 in R.S. 35:623(B), and to report its findings and proposed legislation to the Louisiana
7 Legislature.
8 WHEREAS, Act No. 254 of the 2020 Regular Session of the Louisiana Legislature
9 created the "Remote Online Notarization Act"; and
10 WHEREAS, the "Remote Online Notarization Act" provides for the process and
11 requirements necessary to become and remain authorized to perform remote online
12 notarizations, including the requirement that the secretary of state may only authorize a
13 regularly commissioned notary public who holds a valid notarial commission in this state
14 to conduct remote online notarizations; and
15 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:626 sets forth requirements for the locations of the notary
16 public, the parties, and any witnesses involved in a remote online notarization; and
17 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:627 provides procedures for a notary public to verify the
18 identity of parties and witnesses to a remote online notarial act and requires a notary public
Page 1 of 3

SCR NO. 35
1 to electronically and digitally sign the act; and
2 WHEREAS, R.S. 35:629 requires a notary public to maintain electronic copies and
3 audio-video recordings of each remote online notarization for at least ten years from the date
4 of the notarization; and
5 WHEREAS, audio and video conferencing technology has evolved since the passage
6 of the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and is becoming more widely available and used
7 by notaries throughout Louisiana and in other states to electronically authenticate documents
8 through remote online notarization; and
9 WHEREAS, for many years certain rural areas of Louisiana have faced a shortage
10 of notaries, which makes the facilitation of commerce more difficult and costly for residents
11 of those areas; and
12 WHEREAS, the use of remote online notarization provides an opportunity to better
13 serve residents in rural areas and facilitate commerce and banking by authentic act; and
14 WHEREAS, in Louisiana, an authentic act is required to be executed before a notary
15 public and two witnesses, including signatures of all parties, witnesses, and the notary; and
16 WHEREAS, Louisiana law further provides that certain instruments, including
17 authentic acts, are not permitted to be executed by remote online notarization; and
18 WHEREAS, other civil law jurisdictions like France and Quebec permit the
19 execution of authentic acts via remote online notarization; and
20 WHEREAS, allowing the execution of authentic acts in Louisiana through remote
21 online notarization may expand the business case and possibilities for using remote online
22 notarization.
23 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
24 urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and formulate proposed
25 legislation to amend the "Remote Online Notarization Act" and related laws, as necessary,
26 to authorize the use of remote online notarization for executing authentic acts as defined in
27 Civil Code Article 1833, excluding those instruments prohibited in R.S. 35:623(B), and to
28 report its findings and proposed legislation to the Louisiana Legislature.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge
30 and request the Louisiana State Law Institute study group to include representatives of the

1 secretary of state, Louisiana Notary Association, Louisiana Bankers Association, Louisiana
2 State Bar Association, Louisiana Association of Independent Land Title Agents, Louisiana
3 Land Title Association, Louisiana Clerks of Court Association, and Louisiana Public Tag

5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the Louisiana State Law Institute shall submit its
6 findings and proposed legislation to the Louisiana Legislature no later than March 1, 2027.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
8 director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
