HB 1072 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Bourriaque
Abstract: Modifies certain powers, duties, and responsibilities of the office of Louisiana
Highway Construction, including in cases of emergencies.
Present law (R.S. 48:196(C)) requires that monies appropriated from the State Hwy.
Improvement Fund be exclusively used by the Dept. of Transportation and Development
(DOTD) for funding projects for any road which is part of the state hwy. system but not part
of the federal system and ineligible for federal hwy. funding assistance.
Proposed law modifies present law by changing from the DOTD to office of La. Hwy.
Construction (OLHC).
Present law (R.S. 49:220.51) creates the OLHC within the office of the governor to be
responsible for the study, planning, engineering, design, construction, operation,
maintenance, repair, rehabilitation, and replacement of any nonfederal aid hwy. that is part
of the state hwy. system.
Proposed law authorizes the OLHC to enter into agreements with the DOTD for the design,
construction, remediation, rehabilitation, or replacement of any segment of a federal-aid
hwy. if no federal funds are used for the work as permitted by federal law. Requires a letter
of agreement outline the scope of the work, funding allocations, and regulatory compliance
standards required for the administration of federal-aid hwy. projects.
Present law (R.S. 49:220.52) provides for certain powers, duties, and responsibilities of the
OLHC.
Proposed law authorizes the OLHC to solicit, receive, and administer interagency transfers,
state general fund allocations, or any other sources of funding allowed under the law
necessary to support the delivery of its functions. Extends the office's ability to utilize
emergency procurement procedures from Jan. 1, 2026 to Dec. 31, 2027.
Proposed law (R.S. 49:220.54) specifies that in emergency cases, when certified by the
executive director, the requirements of bidding for the OLHC projects may be waived to
meet the minimum demands of the emergency. Further specifies that upon the executive
director's recommendation, certain resources may be negotiated and made without requesting
bids, except for purchases or leases, which must be in writing with the explicit reasons
supporting the necessity of the leases or purchases. Authorizes the executive director to
waive bidding for necessary emergency purchases to temporarily repair certain damages to
nonfederal aid hwys. and associated components that are under the control of the OLHC
until permanent repairs can be made.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 48:196(C) and R.S. 49:220.51(D) and 220.52(C)(2) and (4); Adds R.S.
49:220.51(E) and 220.54)

Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Transportation,
Highways and Public Works to the original bill:
1. Make technical changes.
2. Remove references to the exercise of procurement procedures, including
emergency procurement.
3. Remove the provisions that would have empowered the office of Louisiana
Highway Construction to execute the design, construction, remediation,
rehabilitation, or replacement of federal-aid highways through a mutual letter of
agreement between the office and the secretary of the Department of
Transportation and Development.
4. Modify present law by changing from the Department of Transportation and
Development to the office of Louisiana Highway Construction as the entity
authorized to exclusively use monies appropriated from the State Highway
Improvement Fund for projects related to any road apart of the state highway
system but not apart of the federal system.
5. Add the authorization that the office of Louisiana Highway Construction may
enter into agreements with the Department of Transportation and Development
for the design, construction, remediation, rehabilitation, or replacement of any
segment of a federal-aid highway if no federal funds are used for the work as
permitted by federal law.
6. Remove a provision that would have given the office of Louisiana Highway
Construction plenary control and administrative oversight over the State
Highway Improvement Fund.
7. Remove a provision that would have created a restricted special account within
the division of administration for deposit of proceeds designated for the State
Highway Fund.
8. Remove a provision that would have authorized the office of Louisiana Highway
Construction to transfer or share funds, maintained by the division of
administration, via agreement or memorandum of understanding with any
department, political subdivision, or public entity, including the Department of
Transportation and Development.
9. Remove a provision that would have granted the executive director emergency
powers to unilaterally issue formal emergency declarations, to invoke emergency
protocols when public health, safety, and welfare was at risk, and to execute
summary contracts for remediation of infrastructure.
10. Add that the office of Louisiana Highway Construction may solicit, receive, and
administer interagency transfers, state general fund allocations, or any other
sources of funding allowed under the law necessary to support the delivery of its
functions.
11. Add that the executive director may waive certain requirements in emergency
circumstances to meet the demands of the emergency, including certain
resources, with the exception of purchases or leases, which must be in writing
with the explicit reasons supporting the necessity of the leases or purchases. Add
the authorization of the executive director to waive bidding for necessary
emergency purchases to temporarily repair certain damages to nonfederal aid
