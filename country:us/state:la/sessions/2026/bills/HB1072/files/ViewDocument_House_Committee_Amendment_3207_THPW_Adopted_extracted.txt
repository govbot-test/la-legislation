23 "§220.54. Emergency authority
24 A. In cases of emergency where the emergency has been certified by the
25 executive director, the requirements of R.S. 48:252 et seq. may be waived by the
26 executive director to the minimum extent necessary to meet the demands of the
27 emergency.
28 B. Upon the recommendation of the executive director, emergency purchases
29 of commodities, materials, supplies, equipment and miscellany, or purchases or
30 leases of noncompetitive or patented articles, devices, equipment, or commodities
31 may be negotiated and made without requesting bids, except that essential documents
32 authorizing these purchases or leases shall have written on their face the explicit
33 reasons supporting the necessity for these leases or purchases.
34 C. When sections of nonfederal aid highway, the component parts thereof,
35 or any facility under the control of the office are damaged, obstructed, or destroyed
36 by flood or other disaster, so as to impede or interrupt the normal use of the
37 nonfederal aid highway or facility, the executive director may make, or authorize to
38 be made, without requesting bids, such emergency purchases as are required to
39 temporarily repair the highway or facility until permanent repairs can be made. The
40 temporary repairs shall be held to minimum necessities and planning shall
41 immediately begin for the permanent rehabilitation of the highway or facility."
