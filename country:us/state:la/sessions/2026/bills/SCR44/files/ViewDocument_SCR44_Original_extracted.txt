SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 44
BY SENATOR SEABAUGH
the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend Sylvia Fowles on being inducted into the 2026 class of the Louisiana Sports
3 Hall of Fame.
4 WHEREAS, each year the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame in Natchitoches, Louisiana
5 collaborates with the Louisiana Sports Writers Association in selecting a limited number of
6 sports figures who embody the character, skill, talent, and accomplishment worthy of being
7 named to the Hall of Fame; and
8 WHEREAS, the 2026 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame Induction Class includes Sylvia
9 Fowles; and
10 WHEREAS, Sylvia Fowles, a native of Miami, Florida, has distinguished herself as
11 one of the most dominant and accomplished players in the history of women's basketball,
12 earning global recognition for her exceptional athletic ability, leadership, and dedication to
13 excellence; and
14 WHEREAS, standing six feet six inches tall, the McDonald's All-American center
15 became a cornerstone of the women's basketball program at Louisiana State University
16 (LSU), where she helped lead the LSU Tigers women's basketball to four consecutive
17 appearances in the national semifinals of the NCAA Division I Women's Basketball
18 Tournament from 2005 through 2008; and

1 WHEREAS, during her remarkable collegiate career, the Tigers compiled an
2 outstanding record of 129 wins and 22 losses, and Fowles averaged a double-double across
3 her four seasons with 15.5 points and 10.9 rebounds per game and shot an impressive 58.4
4 percent from the field; and
5 WHEREAS, she steadily improved throughout her collegiate career, averaging 11.8
6 points and 9.0 rebounds as a freshman, 15.9 points and 11.6 rebounds as a sophomore, 16.9
7 points and 12.6 rebounds as a junior, and 17.4 points and 10.3 rebounds as a senior, while
8 also averaging 2.2 blocked shots and 1.5 steals per game over 144 career contests; and
9 WHEREAS, Fowles earned first-team All-American honors as both a junior and
10 senior and, in 2008, she was named Southeastern Conference Player of the Year,
11 Southeastern Conference Defensive Player of the Year, and National Defensive Player of
12 the Year; and
13 WHEREAS, selected as the second overall pick in the 2008 WNBA Draft, Fowles
14 enjoyed an extraordinary fifteen-year professional career in the Women's National
15 Basketball Association with the Chicago Sky and the Minnesota Lynx, becoming an
16 eight-time All-Star and one of the league's most dominant centers; and
17 WHEREAS, she helped lead the Minnesota Lynx to WNBA championships in 2015
18 and 2017, earning Finals Most Valuable Player honors in both title runs and later receiving
19 the league's Most Valuable Player award in 2017, while also being named WNBA Defensive
20 Player of the Year four times and earning three selections to the All-WNBA First Team; and
21 WHEREAS, over the course of her professional career, Fowles averaged 15.7 points
22 and 9.8 rebounds per game while setting a WNBA record by shooting 59.9 percent from the
23 field across 408 games and establishing herself as one of the league's greatest rebounders
24 and defenders, retiring as the WNBA's all-time leader in rebounds per game and with 4,006
25 total rebounds; and
26 WHEREAS, internationally, she represented the United States with distinction as a
27 member of United States women's national basketball team, winning four Olympic gold
28 medals at the 2008 Summer Olympics, 2012 Summer Olympics, 2016 Summer Olympics,
29 and 2020 Summer Olympics; and
30 WHEREAS, in recognition of her historic contributions to the sport, she was named

1 in 2021 among the WNBA's Top 25 Players of All Time, honoring the league's most
2 influential athletes during its first twenty-five seasons; and
3 WHEREAS, Louisiana State University retired her No. 34 jersey in 2017, an honor
4 shared by only one other LSU women's basketball legend, and, in 2023, the Minnesota Lynx
5 likewise retired her number in recognition of her extraordinary impact; and
6 WHEREAS, beyond her achievements on the court, Fowles has demonstrated a deep
7 commitment to community service, founding the Sylvia Fowles Family Fund in 2010 to
8 assist children in need and serving as a spokesperson for Citizens United for Research in
9 Epilepsy; and
10 WHEREAS, through her remarkable athletic accomplishments, her leadership, and
11 her dedication to charitable causes, Sylvia Fowles has brought great pride to the state of
12 Louisiana and has secured her place among the greatest players in the history of basketball.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 commend Sylvia Fowles on being inducted into the 2026 class of the Louisiana Sports Hall
15 of Fame.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
17 Sylvia Fowles.
