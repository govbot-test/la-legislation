16 "Section 1. R.S. 3:4743(14) through (18) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
17 3:4744.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
18 §4743. Definitions
19 As used in this Part:
20 * * *
21 (14) "Non-lactational dairy protein product" means a food product
22 containing dairy-identical or dairy-derived proteins produced through microbial
23 fermentation and not derived from the natural lactation of a mammal.
24 (14) (15) "Person" means an individual, partnership, limited liability
25 company, limited liability partnership, corporation, trust, firm, company, or other
26 entity doing business in Louisiana.
27 (15) (16) "Pork" means the flesh of a domesticated swine that is edible by
28 humans.
29 (16) (17) "Pork product" means a type of agricultural product that is edible
30 by humans and produced in whole or in part from pork, including bacon, bratwurst,
31 ground pork, ham, pork chops, ribs, roast, and sausage.
32 (17) (18) "Poultry" means domesticated birds that are edible by humans.
33 (18) (19) "Rice" means the whole or broken kernels obtained from the species
34 Oryza sativa L. or Oryza glaberrima, or wild rice, which is obtained from one of the
35 four species of grasses from the genus Zizania or Porteresia.
36 * * *
37 §4744.1 Labeling of non-lactational dairy protein products
38 A.(1) Any non-lactation dairy protein product offered for sale within this
39 state shall bear a clear and conspicuous disclosure statement that reads: "Produced
40 through microbial fermentation. Not derived from mammalian lactation."
41 (2) The disclosure shall also meet all of the following requirements:

9 "Section 2. R.S. 3:4744(C) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §4744. Applicability; prohibited activities
11 * * *
12 C.(1) No person, firm, or corporation shall manufacture for sale, sell, hold
13 or offer for sale, or distribute any non-lactational dairy protein product in this state.
14 (2) No non-lactational dairy protein product shall be labeled or marketed as
15 milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, ice cream, or any other dairy product intended for
16 human consumption.
17 (3) Any violation of this Section shall constitute a violation of this Part and
18 shall be subject to all enforcement authority, penalties, and remedies otherwise
19 provided by law.
20 Section 3. The provisions of Section 1 and 4 and this Section of this Act shall
21 become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not signed by the governor, upon
22 expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature by the governor, as provided
23 by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If vetoed by the governor and
24 subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become effective on the day
25 following such approval.”
