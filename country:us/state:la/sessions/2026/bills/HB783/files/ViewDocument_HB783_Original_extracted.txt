BY REPRESENTATIVE COATES
HEALTH/LDH: Provides relative to the regulation of food and food safety
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 3:4102(1) and (10)(b), R.S. 38:2251.1, and R.S. 40:881(A) and
3 to enact R.S. 3:4102(19) and 4109(H), relative to the regulation of food and food
4 safety; to provide relative to the Dairy Stabilization Law; to provide for definitions;
5 to prohibit state agencies from purchasing or serving any non-lactational dairy
6 protein product; to prohibit certain entities from manufacturing, processing,
7 packaging, storing, or distributing non-lactational dairy protein products; to provide
8 for labeling requirements; to prohibit certain products from being labeled as milk;
9 and to provide for related matters.
10 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
11 Section 1. R.S. 3:4102(1) and (10)(b) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
12 3:4102(19) and 4109(H) are hereby enacted to read as follows:
13 §4102. Definitions
14 As used in this Subpart, unless the context otherwise requires:
15 (1) "Milk" means the lacteal secretion, practically free from colostrum,
16 obtained by the complete milking of one or more healthy cows (including such
17 secretions when raw, cooled, pasteurized, standardized, homogenized, recombined,
18 or concentrated) which meets applicable requirements of the state health officer. The
19 word "milk" shall be interpreted to include goat, sheep, water buffalo, camel milk
20 and the milk from other hooved animals.

3 B. No state agency, including a public school, university, correctional

6 Section 3. R.S. 40:881(A) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §881. Labeling of milk products; disclosures; prohibition; definition; enforcement
8 A.(1) The Louisiana Department of Health shall enforce the United States
9 Food and Drug Administration's standard of identity for milk, as set forth in 21 CFR
10 131.110, the Pasteurized Milk Ordinance, and the provisions of this Section to
11 prohibit the sale of plant-based or non-lactational dairy protein products mislabeled
12 as milk.
13 (2) Any non-lactational dairy protein product offered for sale within this
14 state shall bear a clear and conspicuous disclosure statement that reads: "Produced
15 through microbial fermentation. Not derived from mammalian lactation."
16 (3) The disclosure shall also meet the following requirements:
17 (a) Appear on the principal display panel.
18 (b) Be in a font size not less that fourteen-point type.
19 (c) Be in a typeface and color that contrasts with the background.
20 (d) Be placed in close proximity to the product name.

HB 783 Original 2026 Regular Session Coates
Abstract: Provides for the definitions "milk" and "non-lactational dairy protein products".
Prohibits certain entities from manufacturing, processing, packaging, storing, or
distributing, purchasing, or serving non-lactational dairy protein products. Provides
for the labeling requirement of non-lactational dairy protein products.

Present law (R.S. 3:4102(1)) defines "milk" as the lacteal secretion from one or more cows,
including milk that is raw, cooled, pasteurized, standardized, homogenized, recombined, or
concentrated, and that meets the requirements of the state health officer.
Proposed law modifies present law by clarifying that "milk" is the lacteal secretion that is
practically free from colostrum and is obtained by the complete milking of one or more
healthy cows. Further requires that “milk” be interpreted to include the milk of goats, sheep,
water buffalo, camels, and other hooved animals.
Present law (R.S. 3:4102(10)(b)) defines "dairy product" to include any product that contains
milk solids not fat, butterfat, or a milk derivative, and that is manufactured to resemble a
dairy product listed in the preceding subparagraph. Clarifies that the term "dairy product"
does not include butter; cheese (except cottage cheese or Creole cream cheese); nonfat dry
milk; skim condensed milk; whole condensed milk; or sweetened condensed milk.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that non-lactational dairy protein products are not
considered "milk".
Present law (R.S. 3:4102) provides for definitions under the Dairy Stabilization Law.
Proposed law retains present law and adds a definition of "non-lactational dairy protein
product" to mean a food product not containing dairy-identical or dairy-derived proteins
produced through microbial fermentation or other manufacturing processes and not derived
from the natural lactation of a mammal.
Present law (R.S. 3:4109) provides licensing requirements for processors.
Proposed law retains present law but adds a prohibition for any licensed processor,
distributor, supplier, or retailer to manufacture, process, package, store, or distribute a
non-lactational dairy protein product.
Present law (R.S. 38:2251.1) provides for the preference of milk and dairy products
produced or processed in the state over out-of-state products for state and local government
purchases, provided that the in-state products are of equal quality and reasonably priced.
Proposed law retains present law but prohibits a state agency, including a public school,
university, correctional facility, or other publicly funded institution, from purchasing or
serving any non-lactational dairy protein product, as defined in present law (R.S. 3:4102).
Present law (R.S. 40:881(A)) requires the La. Dept. of Health to enforce the U.S. Food and
Drug Administration’s standard of identity for milk (21 CFR 131.110), the Pasteurized Milk
Ordinance, and the provisions of present law to prohibit the sale of plant-based products that
are mislabeled as milk.
Proposed law modifies present law by also prohibiting the sale of non-lactational dairy
protein products that are mislabeled as milk. Further requires that any non-lactational dairy
protein product offered for sale bear a clear and conspicuous disclosure statement that
complies with the required formatting of proposed law.
(Amends R.S. 3:4102(1) and (10)(b), R.S. 38:2251.1, and R.S. 40:881(A); Adds R.S.
3:4102(19) and 4109(H))
