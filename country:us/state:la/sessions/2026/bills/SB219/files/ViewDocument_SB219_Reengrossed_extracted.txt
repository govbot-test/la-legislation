DIGEST
SB 219 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session McMath
Present law provides for the following composition of the La. Dept. of Health: the executive
office of the secretary, the office of the surgeon general, the office of management and
finance, the office of public health, the office of behavioral health, the office for citizens
with developmental disabilities, the office of aging and adult services, and the office on
women's health and community health.
Present law provides that the office of public health is responsible for functions which relate
to the general health of the people of the state, including physical fitness and supplemental
food programs for women, infants, and children.
Proposed law creates the office of health and nutrition within the La. Dept. of Health.
Proposed law provides that the office is responsible for leading and coordinating efforts
intended to promote health and nutrition, healthcare access, preventative healthcare services,
chronic disease prevention and management programs, physical fitness, and health
outcomes, including supplemental nutrition assistance services and commodity programs for
women, infants, and children.
Proposed law further requires the office to coordinate with the office of aging and adult
services and the office of public health to provide public education on risk reduction
strategies for Alzheimer's and related dementias, included the diseases' links to other chronic
diseases.
Proposed law provides for an executive director for the office that reports directly to the
secretary.
Proposed law provides that the executive office of the secretary and the office of health and
nutrition shall be under the direct supervision, direction, and control of the secretary of the
La. Dept. of Health.

Summary of Amendments Adopted by Senate
Committee Amendments Proposed by Senate Committee on Health and Welfare to
the original bill
1. Adds chronic disease prevention and management programs, healthcare
access, and preventative healthcare services to the purview of the office of
health and nutrition.
2. Changes the office's responsibility regarding the Supplemental Nutrition
Assistance Program.
3. Adds coordination with the office of adult and aging services and the office
of public health to provide public education on risk reduction strategies for
Alzheimer's and related dementias to the office's purview.
