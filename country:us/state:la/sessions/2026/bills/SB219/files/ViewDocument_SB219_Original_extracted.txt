HEALTH DEPARTMENT. Creates the office of health and nutrition within the Louisiana
Department of Health. (gov sig)

6 of the office; to provide for an executive director of the office; to provide for offices
7 under the direct supervision of the secretary; to provide for an effective date; and to

9 §258. Offices; purposes and functions
10 A. The purposes for which the offices of the Louisiana Department of Health,
11 other than the executive office of the secretary and the office of management
12 and finance, are created shall be set forth in this Section.
13 B. The office of public health shall perform the functions of the state which
14 relate to the general health of the people of the state, including but not limited to
15 responsibility for the preparation and supervision of the Sanitary Code, local health
16 units, sewage treatment and disposal within the state, physical fitness, supplemental
17 food programs for women, infants, and children, emergency medical services, and
18 related functions affecting the public health. It shall also perform those functions of
19 the state provided by law relating to environmental quality and pollution control
20 which are related to the public health and which are specifically assigned to the
21 department, including but not limited to functions relating to the treatment and
22 disposal of sewage within the state, with the exception of those functions assigned
23 by law to the Department of Environmental Quality including, but not limited to, the
24 licensing of sewage sludge transporters or haulers.
25 * * *
26 N. The office of health and nutrition shall be responsible for leading and
27 coordinating efforts within the Louisiana Department of Health that are
28 intended to promote supplemental nutrition assistance services and commodity
29 programs for women, infants, children, physical fitness, and health outcomes.

2 §258.1. Office of health and nutrition
3 A. The department shall have an executive director for the office of
4 health and nutrition that reports directly to the secretary.
5 B. The office of health and nutrition shall be responsible for:
6 (1) Leading and coordinating the Governor's Council on Physical Fitness
7 and Sports, the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program for Women,
8 Infants, and Children (WIC), and the Commodity Supplemental Food Program
9 (CSFP).
10 (2) Developing policy for the implementation of the Supplemental
11 Nutrition Assistance Program within the department.
12 (3) Leading and coordinating department initiatives that include
13 addressing health and nutrition, health care access, and preventative health
14 care services.
15 Section 2. R.S. 46:450.3(A) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
16 §450.3. WIC participants; homeless
17 A. The office of public health health and nutrition within the Louisiana
18 Department of Health shall adopt rules and regulations for the WIC program in
19 accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act to provide nonperishable food
20 items to women and children. Such rules shall provide for the distribution of WIC
21 vouchers, in small quantities on a frequent basis, for food items that do not require
22 refrigeration or storage to eligible WIC participants that have been identified by
23 qualified organizations as being homeless. Such rules shall comply with
24 requirements of federal laws and regulations in order to ensure receipt of federal
25 funding for the program.
26 * * *
27 Section 3. The Louisiana Department of Health is authorized to transfer funding and
28 positions within the department to establish and implement the office of health and nutrition.
29 Section 4. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not

DIGEST
SB 219 Original 2026 Regular Session McMath
Present law provides for the following composition of the La. Dept. of Health: the executive
office of the secretary, the office of the surgeon general, the office of management and
finance, the office of public health, the office of behavioral health, the office for citizens
with developmental disabilities, the office of aging and adult services, and the office on
women's health and community health.
Proposed law adds the office of health and nutrition within the La. Dept. of Health.
Proposed law provides that the office is responsible for leading and coordinating the
Governor's Council on Physical Fitness and Sports, the Supplemental Nutrition Assistance
Program for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC), and the Commodity Supplemental Food
Program (CSFP) and for policy implementation of the SNAP within the department.
Proposed law provides for an executive director for the office that reports directly to the
secretary.
Proposed law provides that the executive office of the secretary and the office of health and
nutrition shall be under the direct supervision, direction, and control of the secretary of the
La. Dept. of Health.
Proposed law authorizes the La. Dept. of Health to transfer funding and positions within the
department to establish and implement the office of health and nutrition.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 36:251(C)(1), 254(B)(1)(a)(i), 257(A), and 258(A) and (B) and R.S.
46:450.3(A); adds R.S. 36:253.1, 258(N), and 258.1)
