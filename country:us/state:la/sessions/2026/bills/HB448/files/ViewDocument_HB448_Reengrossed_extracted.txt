HB 448 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Adams
Abstract: Authorizes the use of certain scholarships for former firefighters, spouses of
former firefighters, former police officers, and spouses of former police officers for
any postgraduate degree.
Present law provides for scholarships for the following categories of people:
(1) A firefighter who is injured in his line of duty in which injury results in the inability
to remain employed.
(2) The spouse of a firefighter who is killed in the line of duty or injured in the line of
duty and which injury results in the inability to remain employed.
(3) A police officer who is injured in his line of duty in which injury results in the
inability to remain employed.
(4) The spouse of a police officer who is killed in the line of duty or injured in the line
of duty and which injury results in the inability to remain employed.
Present law requires each scholarship recipient to meet all entrance requirements to the
college or university to which he applies, and to maintain a "C" average each semester.
Present law limits the use of the scholarship to 10 semesters. Requires scholarship to cover
all tuition and fees.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law limits use of scholarship to obtaining one bachelor degree. Proposed law adds
option to use scholarship to obtain any postgraduate degree.
(Amends R.S. 17:1681.2(A) and (C) and 1682.2(A) and (C))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Education to the
original bill:
1. Add option to use scholarship to obtain any postgraduate degree.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Add police officer who is injured in his line of duty in which injury results in the
inability to remain employed, the spouse of such a police officer, and the spouse
of a police officer who is killed in the line of duty.
2. Make technical changes.
