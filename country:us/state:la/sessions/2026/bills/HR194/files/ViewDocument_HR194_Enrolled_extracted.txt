ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 194
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREEMAN
A RESOLUTION
Education to provide de-identified visual acuity screening data collected annually in
Louisiana schools from 2018 through the present to qualified independent
researchers for the purpose of evaluating longitudinal trends.
WHEREAS, visual acuity screenings are conducted annually in Louisiana schools
as part of routine student health assessments; these screenings result in the collection of
objective data reflecting changes in student vision over time; and
WHEREAS, the period beginning in 2018 corresponds with significant changes in
educational practices, including increased reliance on digital devices for instruction; and
WHEREAS, a longitudinal analysis of visual acuity data may provide valuable
insight into trends affecting student health, learning outcomes, and overall well-being; and
WHEREAS, independent analysis of de-identified data is a recognized best practice
to ensure objectivity, transparency, and scientific integrity; and
WHEREAS, access to such data would assist policymakers, educators, health
professionals, and parents in making informed decisions related to student health and
educational environments.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health
and the state Department of Education to provide de-identified visual acuity screening data
collected annually in Louisiana schools from 2018 through the present to qualified
independent researchers for the purpose of evaluating longitudinal trends.
Page 1 of 2
HR NO. 194 ENROLLED
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the data provided shall include, to the extent
available and in compliance with state and federal privacy laws, the following:
(1) Year of screening.
(2) Grade level or age range of students.
(3) Visual acuity measurements or screening outcomes.
(4) Aggregated demographic categories.
(5) Any standardized screening protocols used.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the data shall be made available to qualified
independent researchers, including academic institutions and public interest research
organizations, for the purpose of conducting longitudinal analyses of student visual acuity.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that all data shared pursuant to this Resolution shall
be fully de-identified and provided in a manner consistent with the Family Educational
Rights and Privacy Act, the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, and
applicable state privacy protections.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health and the state
Department of Education are requested to cooperate in facilitating access to the data and to
identify any limitations or gaps in the data set that may affect interpretation.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the findings of any completed analyses or
studies conducted pursuant to this Resolution be summarized and reported to the legislature,
including any observed trends, limitations, and recommendations for further study or policy
consideration.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health and the state superintendent of education.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
