SLS 26RS-2617 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 52
BY SENATORS WOMACK, DUPLESSIS AND MORRIS AND REPRESENTATIVES
MCFARLAND, MENA, ST. BLANC AND TURNER
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH. Designates Wednesday, May 6, 2026, as Main Street Day
at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To designate Wednesday, May 6, 2026, as "Main Street Day" at the Louisiana State Capitol.
3 WHEREAS, since 1984, Louisiana Main Street has helped communities revitalize
4 their historic downtowns and neighborhood commercial districts-celebrating community
5 character and generating impressive economic returns; and
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana Main Street has produced a strong return on investment, with
7 every one dollar invested into the state program generating $67 of new investment back into
8 downtowns and commercial corridors in Fiscal Year 2024-2025; and
9 WHEREAS, Louisiana's thirty-eight designated communities have generated
10 impressive results over the last 42 years, logging 17,491 new jobs, 4,086 new businesses,
11 $606 million in private investments, $438 million in public investments, and 1.3 million
12 volunteer hours; and
13 WHEREAS, Louisiana Main Street leads a statewide network of designated
14 communities: Abbeville, Bastrop, Broussard, Columbia, Crowley, Denham Springs,
15 DeRidder, Donaldsonville, Eunice, Franklin, Hammond, Homer, Houma, Kenner, Leesville,
16 Minden, Monroe, Morgan City, Natchitoches, New Iberia, New Roads, Opelousas,
17 Plaquemine, Ponchatoula, Ruston, Slidell, Springhill, St. Francisville, St. Martinville,
18 Thibodaux, West Monroe, Winnfield, and Winnsboro, and districts in New Orleans

1 including Broad Street, Lower Ninth Ward, North Rampart Street, O. C. Haley Boulevard,
2 and Old Algiers; and
3 WHEREAS, Louisiana Main Street also works with communities on the pathway to
4 potential designation, and currently Jonesville and Washington are "Lagniappe"
5 communities; and
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana Main Street's communities are also part of a national network
7 of over twelve hundred Main Street communities, led by Main Street America; and
8 WHEREAS, the Main Street program is a proven model for economic
9 development-when downtown and neighborhood commercial districts grow, the whole
10 community grows; and
11 WHEREAS, leaders from Main Street communities from all corners of Louisiana are
12 gathering at the Louisiana State Capitol today.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 designate Wednesday, May 6, 2026, as Main Street Day at the Louisiana State Capitol and
15 does hereby commend Louisiana Main Street and its thirty-eight designated communities
16 for their outstanding downtown revitalization efforts throughout the state.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
18 assistant secretary of the office of Cultural Development in the Department of Culture,
19 Recreation, and Tourism and the lieutenant governor of Louisiana.
