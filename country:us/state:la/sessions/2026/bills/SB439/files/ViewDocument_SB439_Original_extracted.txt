SLS 26RS-1152 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR BOUDREAUX
LOCAL EMPLOYEES. Provides for cancer screenings for firefighters and fire service
employees. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 33:2581.4, relative to certain firefighters and fire service
3 employees; to provide relative to precancer screenings for esophageal cancer for
4 certain firefighters and fire service employees; to provide relative to the time period
5 for cancer screening examinations; to provide relative to screens for retired
6 firefighters and fire service employees; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 33:2581.4 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §2581.4. Cancer screenings; certain firefighters and fire service employees
10 A. A classified firefighter employed as a full-time paid employee of a
11 municipal or parish fire department or fire protection district and fire service
12 employees employed by a nonprofit corporation under contract with a fire protection
13 district shall be provided a cancer screening examination and precancer screening
14 conducted by a physician. The initial cancer screening examination shall be
15 conducted not less than three years after the start of employment, and subsequent
16 examinations shall be conducted during the course of employment in accordance
17 with the following schedule:

2 (4) Every ten years for every firefighter and fire service employee
3 beginning with the first ten years of employment and in ten year intervals
4 thereafter for precancer screening or testing.
5 * * *
6 C. Effective January 1, 2027, precancer screenings for esophageal cancer
7 shall be made available to every firefighter and fire service employee and
8 scheduled as provided in Subsection A of this Section, but terminable in all
9 cases at the age of sixty.
10 D. Any firefighter or fire service employee who retires from active service,
11 including a firefighter or fire service employee who retires due to a disability, shall
12 be provided cancer and precancer screening examinations by his former employer
13 pursuant to the schedule provided in Subsection A of this Section.
14 D.E. All costs associated with cancer screening and precancer examinations
15 provided for in this Section shall be paid by the firefighter's or fire service
16 employee's employer or former employer. No copayment, deductible, coinsurance,
17 or any out-of-pocket expense shall be required for such examinations.
18 E. F. No firefighter or fire service employee shall be required to participate
19 in cancer or precancer screening examinations.
20 F. G. The provisions of the Section shall not be applicable to any firefighter
21 or fire service employee who resigned or was terminated by his employer.
22 G. H. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to require a personal health
23 insurance company to pay for cancer screening examinations for firefighters or fire
24 service employees that are conducted more frequently than such examinations are
25 recommended in the most recently published recommendations established by the
26 American Cancer Society or the National Comprehensive Cancer Network.

DIGEST
SB 439 Original 2026 Regular Session Boudreaux
Present law (R.S. 33:2581.4) provides that a classified firefighter employed as a full-time
paid employee of a municipal or parish fire department or fire protection district and fire
service employees employed by a nonprofit corporation under contract with a fire protection
district shall be provided a cancer screening examination conducted by a physician.
Present law provides that the initial examination shall be conducted not less than three years
after the start of employment, and subsequent examinations shall be conducted during the
course of employment in accordance with the following schedule:
(1) Every five years for firefighters and fire service employees who are not less than 20
years of age and not more than 39 years of age.
(2) Every four years for firefighters and fire service employees who are not less than 40
years of age and not more than 49 years of age.
(3) Every three years for firefighters and fire service employees who are 50 years of age
or older.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that subsequent examinations shall be conducted
for precancer screenings or testing, every 10 years for every firefighter and fire service
employee beginning with the first 10 years of employment and in 10 year intervals
thereafter.
Proposed law provides that effective on January 1, 2027, precancer screenings for
esophageal cancer shall be made available to every firefighter and fire service employee and
scheduled as provided in present law, but terminable in all cases at the age of 60.
Present law provides that any firefighter or fire service employee who retires from active
service, including a firefighter or fire service employee who retires due to a disability, shall
be provided cancer screening examinations by his former employer pursuant to the schedule
provided in present law. Proposed law retains present law and includes precancer screening
examinations for retired firefighters and fire service employees.
Present law provides that all costs associated with cancer screening examinations provided
for in present law shall be paid by the firefighter's or fire service employee's employer or
former employer. Further provides that no copayment, deductible, coinsurance, or any out-
of-pocket expense shall be required for such examinations. Proposed law retains present law
and includes that all costs associated with precancer screening examinations shall be paid
by the firefighter's or fire service employee's employer or former employer.
Present law provides that no firefighter or fire service employee shall be required to
participate in cancer screening examinations. Proposed law retains present law and provides
that no firefighter or fire service employee shall be required to participate in precancer
screening examinations.
Present law provides that the provisions of present law shall not be applicable to any
firefighter or fire service employee who resigned or was terminated by his employer.
Proposed law retains present law and provides that no firefighter or fire service employee
who resigned or was terminated shall be required to participate in precancer screening
