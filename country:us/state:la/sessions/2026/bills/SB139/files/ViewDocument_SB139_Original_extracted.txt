SENATE BILL NO. 139
BY SENATOR FOIL
SUITS AGAINST STATE. Provides for the amount of interest awarded on claims for
personal injury or wrongful death in suits against the state or political subdivisions. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 13:5112(C), relative to suits against the state or a political
3 subdivision; to provide relative to the accrual rate of legal or judicial interest on any
4 claim for personal injury or wrongful death; to provide relative to requirements,
5 conditions, and procedures; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 13:5112(C) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
8 §5112. Suits against the state or political subdivision; court costs; interest
9 * * *
10 C. Legal interest on any claim for personal injury or wrongful death shall
11 accrue at the lesser of six percent per annum or the rate of judicial interest in
12 accordance with R.S. 13:4202. Legal interest shall accrue from the date service
13 is requested following judicial demand until the judgment thereon is signed by the
14 trial judge in accordance with Code of Civil Procedure Article 1911. Legal interest
15 accruing subsequent to the signing of the judgment shall be at the rate fixed by R.S.
16 9:3500.
17 * * *

DIGEST
SB 139 Original 2026 Regular Session Foil
Present law (R.S. 13:5112) provides that in any suit against the state or any department,
board, commission, agency, or political subdivision in which a judgment is rendered, the
court may award the successful party court costs and interest.
Present law (R.S. 13:5112(C)) provide that legal interest on any claim for personal injury
or wrongful death accrues at 6% per annum from the date service is requested following
judicial demand until the signing of the judgment pursuant to present law (C.C.P. Art. 1911).
Further provides that legal interest accruing subsequent to the signing of the judgment will
be at the rate fixed by present law, (R.S. 9:3500).
Proposed law provides that legal interest on any claim for personal injury or wrongful death
accrues at the lesser of 6% per annum or the rate of judicial interest in accordance with
present law, (R.S. 13:4202).
Proposed law provides that legal interest accrues from the date service is requested following
judicial demand until the signing of the judgment pursuant to present law (C.C.P. Art. 1911).
Proposed law retains present law requirement that legal interest accruing subsequent to the
signing of the judgment will be at the rate fixed by present law, (R.S. 9:3500).
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 13:5112(C))
