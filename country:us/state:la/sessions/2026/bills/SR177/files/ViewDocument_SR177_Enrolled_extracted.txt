2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 177
BY SENATOR MIZELL
A RESOLUTION
of Criminal Justice to study and make recommendations regarding the development,
adoption, and implementation of lethal assessment protocols for peace officers in
Louisiana.
WHEREAS, the safety of both peace officers and the public is a matter of critical
importance to the state of Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, peace officers frequently encounter situations involving individuals who
may pose a risk of serious harm to themselves or others, requiring rapid and accurate
assessment of potential lethality; and
WHEREAS, lethal assessment protocols are structured tools designed to help law
enforcement identify indicators of imminent danger, particularly in cases involving domestic
violence, mental health crises, or escalating threats; and
WHEREAS, several states and jurisdictions have implemented or piloted lethal
assessment models to improve officer decision-making, enhance victim safety, and reduce
the likelihood of fatal encounters; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and the Administration
of Criminal Justice is uniquely positioned to evaluate best practices, consult with
subject-matter experts, and assess the feasibility of statewide standards; and
WHEREAS, a comprehensive study would allow the legislature to consider whether
Louisiana should adopt uniform protocols, training requirements, or data-collection practices
to support peace officers in high-risk encounters.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and the
Administration of Criminal Justice to conduct a study on the feasibility, benefits, and
potential implementation of lethal assessment protocols for peace officers.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include, at a minimum:
(1) Review of existing models, including domestic violence lethality assessments,
crisis-intervention frameworks, and threat-assessment tools used in other states.
(2) Evaluation of training needs, including curriculum development, certification, and
continuing education.
(3) Assessment of data collection, including reporting requirements, privacy
considerations, and integration with existing criminal justice databases.
(4) Consultation with stakeholders, including sheriffs, police chiefs, district
attorneys, victim-advocacy organizations, and mental-health professionals.
(5) Identification of statutory or regulatory changes necessary to implement statewide
standards.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement
and the Administration of Criminal Justice shall submit a written report of its findings and
recommendations to the Senate Committee on Judiciary B, no later than February 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
chairman of the Louisiana Commission on Law Enforcement and the Administration of
Criminal Justice.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
