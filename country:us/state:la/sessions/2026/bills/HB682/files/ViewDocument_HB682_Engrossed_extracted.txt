HLS 26RS-607 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
VETERANS: Authorizes the use of veterans as resource officers at schools
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:416.19(B)(1) and to enact R.S. 17:3996(B)(24), relative to
3 school resource officers; to provide for the use of veterans as school resource
4 officers; to provide for the definition of veteran; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 17:416.19(B)(1) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
7 17:3996(B)(24) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §416.19. School resource officers
9 * * *
10 B.(1)(a) A "school resource officer" shall be means a peace officer as
11 defined in R.S. 40:2402 or a veteran as defined in R.S. 13:5364, who meets all of the
12 following criteria:
13 (i) He was honorably discharged from the Armed Forces.
14 (ii) He successfully completed the requisite background check.
15 (iii) He meets all minimum training requirements established by the Council
16 on Peace Officer Standards and Training or an equivalent program approved by the
17 State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education, and mandatory reporter
18 training provided for in Children's Code Article 603.1.
19 (iv) He is employed or contracted by a city, parish, or other local public
20 school system, a nonpublic school, or a law enforcement agency.

1 (v) He operates under policies adopted by the public school governing
2 authority or nonpublic school regarding scope of duties, authority, and use of force.
3 (vi) He is physically and mentally capable of performing assigned duties.

5 Paragraph shall not be considered a peace officer, and shall not have arrest powers,

15 Chapter and its approved charter and the school's officers and employees shall be
16 exempt from all statutory mandates or other statutory requirements that are
17 applicable to public schools and to public school officers and employees except for
18 the following laws otherwise applicable to public schools with the same grades:

HB 682 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Dickerson
Abstract: Authorizes certain veterans to serve as school resource officers.
Present law authorizes public school governing authorities and nonpublic schools to enter
into agreements with local law enforcement agencies to provide for school resource officers.
Present law defines "school resource officer" as a peace officer as defined by present law
(R.S. 40:2402).

HLS 26RS-607 ENGROSSED
HB NO. 682
Proposed law retains present law and expands the definition of "school resource officer" to
include a veteran, as defined by present law (R.S. 13:5364).
Proposed law requires such a veteran to meet certain criteria, including the following:
(1) An honorable discharge.
(2) A successful background check.
(3) Completion of minimum training by the Council on Peace Officer Standards
and Training or an equivalent program approved by the State Bd. of
Elementary and Secondary Education.
(Amends R.S. 17:416.19(B)(1); Adds R.S. 17:3996(B)(24))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Education to the
original bill:

Section 2:
1. Add certain criteria required for a veteran to be qualified to serve as a school
resource officer.

Section 3:
2. Include charter schools.
