SLS 26RS-1471 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR EDMONDS
SCHOOLS. Creates the School Safety Drone Response Pilot Program to supplement school
crisis management and response plans. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 17:416.16.2, relative to school crisis management and response plans; to
3 provide for the School Safety Drone Response Pilot Program; to establish eligibility
4 to participate in the pilot program; to provide for administration of the program; and
5 to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 17:416.16.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §416.16.2. School Safety Drone Response Pilot Program
9 A.(1) The state Department of Education may establish a School Safety
10 Drone Response Pilot Program to evaluate the use of drone technology as a
11 supplement to existing school safety and law enforcement response protocols at
12 each city, parish, and other local public schools.
13 (2) The State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education shall
14 establish criteria for participation by each city, parish, and other local public
15 schools.
16 (3) The department may choose a vendor to operate the pilot program
17 and provide the drone response technology.

1 B. The program shall be conducted as part of the crisis management and
2 response plan in accordance with the provisions of R.S. 17:416.16.
3 C. Each city, parish, and other local public schools shall be eligible to
4 participate in the pilot program. The department shall select at least one but no
5 more than four schools to participate in the pilot program, based upon a first
6 come, first serve basis.
7 D. As a condition to participate in the program, a school shall provide
8 school mapping data to the vendor that is chosen by the department to operate
9 a drone safety program, subject to the provisions of R.S. 17:416.16.1. The
10 vendor shall enter into a confidentiality agreement with the department, the
11 school, and the Louisiana Center for Safe Schools to provide strict
12 confidentiality with respect to the school's mapping data.
13 E. The department may accept grants or private funding to establish and
14 operate the pilot program.
15 F. Beginning December 31, 2027, the department shall submit an annual
16 report to the Senate Committee on Education and the House of Representatives
17 Committee on Education as to the findings and effectiveness of the pilot
18 program.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 488 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Edmonds
Present law (R.S. 17:416.16 and 416.16.1) provides for school crisis management and
response plans for public schools and authorizes the collection and protection of school
mapping data, including provisions for confidentiality and limited sharing of such data.
Proposed law authorizes the Dept. of Education to establish a School Safety Drone Response
Pilot Program to evaluate the use of drone technology as a supplement to existing school
safety and law enforcement response protocols at public schools. Proposed law also requires
the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education (BESE) to establish criteria for
school participation and authorizes the department to select a vendor to operate the program
and provide drone technology.
Proposed law provides that the program shall be conducted as part of existing school crisis
management and response plans. Proposed law authorizes any public school to apply, but

Proposed law requires participating schools to provide school mapping data to the selected
