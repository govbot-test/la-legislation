HLS 26RS-4001 REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE WILEY
LAW ENFORCEMENT: Provides relative to disclosure of certain law enforcement records
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 44:4(65), relative to disclosure of certain records; to prohibit the disclosure
3 of certain records pertaining to law enforcement; to provide for a public records
4 exception; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 44:4(65) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §4. Applicability
8 This Chapter shall not apply:
9 * * *
10 (65)(a) To the social security number, home address, or date of birth of an
11 employee of a criminal justice agency contained in the criminal history records of
12 the employee who serves as a witness in a criminal proceeding and which records are
13 in the custody of the clerk of court, unless the records are voluntarily released by the
14 employee, otherwise available to the general public, or released by court order.
15 (b) For purposes of this Paragraph, "criminal history records" means records
16 collected and maintained by the Criminal Justice Information Services Division of
17 the Federal Bureau of Investigation or the Louisiana Computerized Criminal History
18 System operated by the Department of Public Safety and Corrections.
19 (c) For purposes of this Paragraph, "criminal justice agency" shall have the
20 same meaning as provided in R.S. 14:283.2.

HB 1245 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Wiley
Abstract: Prohibits the disclosure of the social security number, home address, or date of
birth contained in the criminal history records of an employee of a criminal justice
agency who serves as a witness in a criminal proceeding and which records are in the
custody of the clerk of court.
Present law (Public Records Law) generally provides that any person of the age of majority
may inspect, copy, or reproduce any public record, and places certain duties on the custodian
of a public record to make such records available upon request.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law (R.S. 44:4) provides certain exceptions to the Public Records Law.
Proposed law provides an additional exception for the social security number, home address,
or date of birth contained in the criminal history records of an employee of a criminal justice
agency who serves as a witness in a criminal proceeding and which records are in the
custody of the clerk of court, unless the records are voluntarily released by the employee,
otherwise available to the general public, or released by court order.
Proposed law provides that "criminal history records" means records collected and
maintained by the Criminal Justice Information Services Division of the Federal Bureau of
Investigation or the La. Computerized Criminal History System operated by the Dept. of
Public Safety and Corrections.
Present law defines "criminal justice agency" as any government agency or subunit thereof,
or private agency that, through statutory authorization or a legal formal agreement with a
governmental unit or agency, has the power of investigation, arrest, detention, prosecution,
adjudication, treatment, supervision, rehabilitation, or release of persons suspected, charged,
or convicted of a crime; or that collects, stores, processes, transmits, or disseminates criminal
history records or crime information.
Proposed law retains present law and applies the same definition to proposed law.
(Adds R.S. 44:4(65))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on House and
