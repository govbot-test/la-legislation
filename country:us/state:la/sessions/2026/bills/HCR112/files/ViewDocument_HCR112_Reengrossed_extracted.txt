study the recent flooding on Louisiana Highway 1 in Shreveport
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request that the Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development
3 (DOTD), in conjunction with DOTD District 4, study the recent occurrences of
4 rainwater flooding along the Louisiana Highway 1 corridor in Shreveport, Louisiana,
5 extending from its intersection with East Stoner Avenue to its intersection with East
6 Kings Highway and Louisiana Highway 3032.
7 WHEREAS, Louisiana Highway 1 is a major north-south business and residential
8 thoroughfare in Shreveport; and
9 WHEREAS, this segment of Louisiana Highway 1 has experienced recent recurring
10 and significant flooding during rain events; and
11 WHEREAS, the recurring flooding along this segment of Louisiana Highway 1 has
12 impeded access to residences and businesses, disrupted vehicular traffic, and posed threats
13 to public safety; and
14 WHEREAS, a coordinated study with the participation of affected neighborhoods
15 and businesses and relevant local municipal entities is necessary to identify the causes of and
16 solutions to the flooding along this corridor.
17 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
18 urge and request that the Louisiana DOTD, in conjunction with DOTD District 4, study the
19 recent occurrences of rainwater flooding along the Louisiana Highway 1 corridor in
20 Shreveport, Louisiana, extending from its intersection with East Stoner Avenue to its
21 intersection with East Kings Highway and Louisiana Highway 3032.
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall organize and conduct no less
23 than two public forums with affected residents and business owners to gather feedback and
24 insight regarding the impacts of the recent recurring flooding along the corridor.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall incorporate the input, expertise,
2 and support of relevant local municipal entities, including but not limited to the Shreveport
3 Public Works Department, the Shreveport Department of Operational Services, Office of
4 Water and Sewerage, Shreveport Traffic Engineering, and Caddo Parish Office of Homeland
5 Security.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall submit a written report detailing
7 its findings and recommendations, including proposed plans to mitigate the flooding during
8 rain events, for inclusion in DOTD's Statewide Flood Control Program, to the joint
9 transportation, highways, and public works committee at least sixty days prior to the
10 convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the DOTD shall submit a copy of the report to
12 the state representative for Louisiana House District No. 2 and the state senator for Louisiana
13 Senate District No. 39 at least sixty days prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of the Resolution be transmitted to the
15 secretary of the DOTD.

HCR 112 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Jackson
Urges and requests that the Dept. of Transportation and Development (DOTD) study the
recent occurrences of rainwater flooding along a segment of the Louisiana Highway 1
corridor in Shreveport, La. Requires the DOTD to submit a written report detailing its
findings and recommendations to the joint transportation committee, the representative for
La. House District No. 2, and the senator for La. Senate District No. 39 at least 60 days prior
to the convening of the 2027 R.S.
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:

Section 2:
1. Adds the Caddo Parish Office of Homeland Security as an entity that the
Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development is urged and
requested to work in conjunction with to study the recent occurrences of
rainwater flooding along the Louisiana Highway 1 corridor in Shreveport,
Louisiana.

Section 3:
2. Clarifies that the Department of Transportation and Development's written report
that includes the findings and recommendations resulting from its study, along
with proposed plans to mitigate flooding during rain events, also be incorporated
into its Statewide Flood Control Program.
