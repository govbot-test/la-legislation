14 On page 1, delete lines 11 through 17 and insert:
15 " (8) "Environmental well or hole" includes but is not limited to the
16 observation, monitor, piezometer, recovery, horizontal injection, cathodic
17 protection, or geotechnical boreholes for the purposes of environmental
18 assessment, sampling, or analyzation.
19 (9) "Executive director" means the person appointed by the board to serve as
20 the chief operating officer in connection with the day-to-day operation of the board's
21 business. The executive director is the appointing authority for all employees of the
22 board.
23 (9)(a)(10)(a) "General contractor" means a person who contracts directly
24 with the owner. The term "general contractor" includes the term "primary contractor"
25 and wherever used in this Chapter or in regulations promulgated thereunder "primary
26 contractor" means "general contractor".
27 (b) "General contractor" does not mean any person, supplier, manufacturer,
28 or employee of such person who assembles, repairs, maintains, moves, puts up, tears
29 down, or disassembles any patented or proprietary equipment supplied by such
30 person to a contractor to be used solely by the contractor for a construction
31 undertaking.
32 (10)(11) "Home improvement" means the reconstruction, alteration,
33 renovation, repair, modernization, conversion, improvement, removal, or demolition,
34 or the construction of an addition to any preexisting residential structure which
35 building is used or designed to be used as a residence or dwelling unit, or to
36 structures which are adjacent to such residence or building for which the project
37 value is seven thousand five hundred dollars or more. "Home improvement " does
38 not include services rendered gratuitously.
39 (11)(12) "Home improvement contractor" means any person who undertakes
40 or attempts to undertake or submits a price or bid or offers to construct, supervise,
41 superintend, oversee, direct, perform, or in any manner assume charge of a home
42 improvement project for which the project value is at least seven thousand five
43 hundred dollars but less than fifty thousand dollars. A home improvement contractor
44 shall not perform any structural work that is integral to the structural integrity of any
Page 1 of 4

1 new or existing structure, including but not limited to footings, foundations, outside
2 walls, skeleton, bearing columns and interior load bearing walls, floor slabs, or
3 roofing systems to any type.
4 (12)(13) "License" means any form of license or registration the board is
5 authorized to issue in accordance with this Chapter.
6 (13)(14) "Mechanical contractor" means any person who undertakes to,
7 attempts to, or submits a price or bid or offers to construct, supervise, superintend,
8 oversee, direct, perform, or in any manner assume charge of the construction,
9 alteration, repair, improvement, movement, demolition, putting up, tearing down, or
10 furnishing labor, or furnishing labor together with material and equipment, or
11 installing the same for the construction, installation, maintenance, testing, and repair
12 of air conditioning, refrigeration, heating systems, and plumbing for all residential,
13 commercial, and industrial applications as well as ventilation systems, mechanical
14 work controls, boilers and other pressure vessels, steam and hot water systems and
15 piping, gas piping and fuel storage, and chilled water and condensing water systems
16 and piping, including but not limited to any type of industrial process piping and
17 related valves, fittings, and components, for which the project value is ten thousand
18 dollars or more. This Paragraph is not deemed or construed to limit the authority of
19 a contractor, general contractor, or residential contractor, as those terms are defined
20 in this Section, nor to require such individuals to become a mechanical contractor.
21 (14)(15) "Mold remediation contractor" means any person who engages in
22 removal, cleaning, sanitizing, demolition, or other treatment, including preventative
23 activities, of mold or mold-contaminated matter that was not purposely grown at that
24 location for which the project value is seven thousand five hundred dollars or more.
25 Mold remediation applies only to the regulation of mold-related activities that affect
26 indoor air quality and does not apply to routine cleaning when not conducted for the
27 purpose of mold-related activities intended to affect indoor air quality.
28 (15)(16) "Person" means any individual, firm, partnership, association,
29 cooperative, corporation, limited liability company, limited liability partnership, or
30 any other entity recognized by Louisiana law; and whether or not acting as a
31 principal, trustee, fiduciary, receiver, or as any other kind of legal or personal
32 representative, or as a successor in interest, assignee, agent, factor, servant,
33 employee, director, officer, or any other representative of such person; or any state
34 or local governing authority or political subdivision.
35 (16)(17) "Plumbing contractor" means any person who installs, maintains,
36 and repairs potable and nonpotable tap water or sewer systems within a building
37 structure or residential structure for which the project value is ten thousand dollars
38 or more.
39 (17)(18) "Principal" means an owner, shareholder, or an officer or director
40 of a corporation; a member or manager of a limited liability company; a general
41 partner of a partnership; a sole proprietor; a trustee; or a full-time employee with
42 similar operational control or significant influence with respect to any person as
43 determined by the board.
44 (18)(19) "Qualifying party" means a natural person designated by the
45 contractor to represent the contractor for the purpose of complying with the
46 provisions of this Chapter including but not limited to meeting the requirements for
47 the initial license and any continuation thereof.
48 (19)(a)(20)(a) "Residential contractor" means any person who constructs a
49 fixed building or structure for sale or use by another as a residence or who, for a
50 price, commission, fee, wage, or other compensation, undertakes or offers to
51 undertake the construction or superintending of the construction of any residential
52 structure which is not more than three floors in height, to be used by another as a
53 residence, for which the project value is fifty thousand dollars or more. The term
54 "residential contractor" includes all persons who receive an additional fee for the
55 employment or direction of labor, or any other work beyond the normal architectural
56 or engineering services.
57 (b) "Residential contractor" includes both of the following:
58 (i) Any person bidding or performing home improvement for which the
59 project value is seven thousand five hundred dollars or more.
60 (ii) Any person performing the installation of a modular home with a value
61 equal to or greater than fifty thousand dollars for which the total project value shall

1 not include the cost of the component parts of the modular home in the condition
2 each part leaves the factory pursuant to R.S. 40:1730.71.
3 (c) "Residential contractor" does not include any person engaged in building
4 residential structures that are built to the United States Department of Housing and
5 Urban Development's construction standards for manufactured housing as outlined
6 at 42 U.S.C. 5401 et seq., or mounted on a metal chassis and wheels.
7 (20)(21) "Residential roofing" means the construction, alteration, repair,
8 improvement, demolition, putting up, tearing down, furnishing labor, or furnishing
9 labor together with materials or equipment, or the installation of materials or
10 equipment for any phase of roofing specific to a residential structure for which the
11 project value is seven thousand five hundred dollars or more.
12 (21)(22) "Residential roofing contractor" means any person who undertakes
13 to, attempts to, or submits a price or bid, or offers to construct, supervise,
14 superintend, oversee, direct, perform, or in any manner assume charge of a
15 residential roofing project for which the project value is seven thousand five hundred
16 dollars or more.
17 (22)(23) "Residential structure" means a building or structure that is used
18 primarily for occupancy by a person as a residence. Such structures or buildings
19 include but are not limited to single family dwellings and duplexes which are not
20 more than three floors in height and structures that are part of or adjacent to the
21 building or structures to be used as a residence. A residential structure more than
22 three floors in height may be built by a person holding a building construction and
23 residential construction license.
24 (23)(24) "Subcontract" means an agreement to perform a portion of the scope
25 of work contained in the principal contract including the entire cost of labor and
26 materials of that part of the principal contract which is performed by the
27 subcontractor.
28 (24)(a)(25)(a) "Subcontractor" means a person who contracts to perform a
29 scope of work that is a part of the scope of work contained in the principal contract.
30 (b) "Subcontractor" does not include any person, supplier, or manufacturer
31 who assembles, repairs, maintains, moves, puts up, tears down, or disassembles any
32 patented or proprietary equipment supplied by such person to a contractor to be used
33 solely by the contractor for a construction undertaking.
34 (26) "Water well drilling" means drilling for the purposes of extracting
35 ground water which includes but is not limited to the drilling, construction,
36 maintenance, repair, abandoning, plugging, or sealing water wells for potable
37 and nonpotable water."
