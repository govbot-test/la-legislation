18 Advocates (CASA).

20 district court shall provide each juror with an option to donate their per diem
21 compensation to CASA the designated organization as described in Subsection A of

HB 1011 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Freiberg
Abstract: Permits jurors to donate their per diem compensation to the formal program of
Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA).
Present law authorizes a pilot program that allows jurors in the 19th JDC to donate their per
diem compensation in whole or in part to an organization that is either formally recognized
under Ch.C. Art. 424 for its role in advocating for the best interests of abused and neglected
children in the court system, or specifically designated as Capital Area Court Appointed
Special Advocates (CASA).
Present law requires the clerk of court for the 19th JDC to provide each juror an option to
donate the per diem compensation to CASA during jury orientation or the juror service
process.
Present law requires the clerk of court for the 19th JDC to establish procedures for the
collection and timely distribution of donated per diem compensation to the designated
organization.
Present law requires that the procedures for the collection and timely distribution of donated
per diem compensation to the designated organization include provisions for donor
acknowledgment and compliance with financial management standards.
Present law also requires a form to be distributed to each person registered for the jury pool
that contains the mission of CASA and the voluntary option to assign fees due to them as a
tax-deductible donation to the organization.
Present law provides for the purpose of the program and requires the juror office of the 19th
JDC to send a monthly check of individual juror payments to CASA along with the names,
addresses, and amounts donated of the individual juror for a mailed tax-deductible receipt.
Present law requires any information provided to CASA by the juror office to remain
confidential and not be subject to disclosure under the public records law.
Present law requires that CASA be designated exclusively for the per diem donations during
the two-year pilot program.

Proposed law deletes present law that provides for the "pilot" program in the 19th JDC.
Proposed law amends present law to authorize a program in any judicial district court to
allow jurors to voluntarily donate per diem compensation to CASA.
Proposed law requires the clerk of court for the judicial district court to provide each juror
an option to donate the per diem compensation to CASA during jury orientation or the juror
service process.
Proposed law requires the clerk of court for the judicial district court to establish procedures
for the collection and timely distribution of donated per diem compensation to CASA.
Proposed law requires the juror office of the judicial district court to send a monthly check
of individual juror payments to CASA along with the names, addresses, and amounts
donated of the individual juror for a mailed tax-deductible receipt. Provides that such
information is exempt from public records.
Proposed law requires donations made to CASA to be utilized exclusively for supporting the
mission of CASA.
(Amends R.S. 13:3049.2 and R.S. 44:4.1(B)(6))
