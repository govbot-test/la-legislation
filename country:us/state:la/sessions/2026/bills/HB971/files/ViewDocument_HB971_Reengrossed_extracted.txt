BY REPRESENTATIVE STAGNI
MEDICAID: Provides relative to Medicaid reimbursement for rural health clinics
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:2197.1, relative to rural health clinics; to provide for Medicaid
3 reimbursement rates of rural health clinics; to provide for the promulgation and
4 adoption of rules; to provide for a state plan amendment; to provide for an
5 implementation date; and to provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 40:2197.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §2197.1. Equality in Medicaid reimbursement rates for rural health clinics
9 A. The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health shall take all
10 necessary actions to equalize the Medicaid reimbursement rate between independent
11 rural health clinics, also known as type one rural health clinics, and provider-based
12 rural health clinics, also known as type two and three rural health clinics. The
13 Louisiana Department of Health shall ensure that independent rural health clinics are
14 reimbursed by Medicaid at the same rate as provider-based rural health clinics.
15 B. The Louisiana Department of Health shall prepare a state plan amendment
16 or promulgate and adopt rules, as necessary, to implement the provisions of this
17 Section.
18 Section 2. Not later than ninety days after the effective date of this Act, the
19 Louisiana Department of Health shall take all necessary actions to implement the provisions
20 of Section 1 of this Act.

HB 971 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Stagni
Abstract: Requires the La. Dept. of Health (LDH) to take all necessary actions to ensure
that independent rural health clinics are reimbursed by Medicaid at the same rate as
provider-based rural health clinics.
Present law defines a rural health clinic as a facility that provides primary healthcare
services to outpatients located in an area that is not an urbanized area as defined by the U.S.
Census Bureau.
Proposed law requires the secretary of LDH to take all necessary actions to equalize
Medicaid reimbursement rates between independent rural health clinics and provider-based
rural health clinics.
Proposed law requires LDH to take any necessary action to implement the provisions of
proposed law not later than 90 days after the effective date of proposed law including filing
a Medicaid state plan amendment or promulgating and adopting rules.
Effective upon appropriation of funds by the legislature.
(Adds R.S. 40:2197.1)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Appropriations to the
engrossed bill:

Section 2:
1. Change deadline by which LDH must take action to implement proposed law
from Oct. 1, 2026, to 90 days after proposed law becomes effective.

Section 3:
2. Make effectiveness of proposed law subject to appropriation of monies by the
legislature for the implementation of proposed law.
