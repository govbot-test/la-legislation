SLS 26RS-116 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR FESI
PUBLIC HEALTH. Provides relative to water fluoridation. (1/1/27)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 40:5.11, relative to public water systems; to repeal mandatory
3 fluoridation pursuant to the Louisiana Department of Health's water fluoridation
4 program; to prohibit the fluoridation of public water systems unless approved in a
5 local election; to provide for exceptions; to provide for an effective date; and to
6 provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 40:5.11 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §5.11. Water fluoridation program; public water systems; prohibition
10 A. The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health shall establish in the
11 office of public health a fluoridation program for the purpose of protecting the dental
12 health of the citizens of this state, and especially children, through the prevention of
13 tooth decay. Fluoridation of any public water system in Louisiana is prohibited
14 unless authorized pursuant to Subsection B of this Section.
15 B.(1) Each public water system that has at least five thousand service
16 connections and natural levels of fluoride that are outside the optimal range
17 established in the rules and regulations adopted pursuant to this Section shall acquire,

2 the level of fluoride in its water system in the optimal range for the purpose of
3 protecting the dental health of citizens of this state, as established in the rules and
4 regulations adopted pursuant to this Section.
5 (2) Each public water system with at least five thousand service connections

7 regulations adopted pursuant to this Section shall provide to the Louisiana

12 pursuant to this Section.
13 (3) No public water system shall be required to comply with Paragraph (1)
14 of this Subsection unless sufficient funds have been identified by the state, whether
15 by appropriation, capital outlay, grants or similar means or source of funds, as
16 available to that system for the cost of acquiring and installing fluoridation
17 equipment and the cost of material required to fluoridate said system for at least six
18 months from the date of initial installation.
19 (4) A public water system that has never used fluoridation to adjust fluoride
20 levels in its water and whose water contains fluoride in amounts outside the range
21 established in the rules and regulations adopted pursuant to this Section as optimal
22 for the purpose of protecting the dental health of the citizenry shall be exempt from
23 compliance with Paragraph (1) of this Subsection provided all of the following apply
24 Fluoridation of a public water system is authorized only if all of the following
25 conditions are met:
26 (a)(1) A petition requesting the exemption fluoridation has been signed by
27 at least fifteen percent of the registered voters who reside in the precincts served by
28 said the water system, as certified by the parish registrar of voters, and has been
29 presented to the governing authority of each municipality or parish in whose

2 (b)(2) A local election has been called on the exemption after the petition has
3 been received by each governing authority pursuant to Subparagraph (a) Paragraph
4 (1) of this Paragraph Subsection. Participation in the election shall be limited to

20 in the four years immediately prior to the election in which the exemption
21 fluoridation was approved.

27 levels of fluoride in public water systems within the optimal range for the purpose
28 of protecting the oral health of the citizens of this state.
29 Section 2. This Act shall become effective on January 1, 2027.

DIGEST
SB 4 Original 2026 Regular Session Fesi
Present law requires the La. Dept. of Health to establish a water fluoridation program.
Proposed law repeals the program.
Present law requires public water systems with at least 5,000 service connections to
fluoridate the water and provides exemptions for water systems that meet certain criteria.
Present law requires the La. Dept. of Health to establish optimal levels of fluoridation for
public water systems.
Present law authorizes certain water systems to opt out of fluoridation if 15% of registered
voters in the precincts served by the water system sign a petition and a local election is
called by the governing authority in which a majority of registered voters approve the
exemption.
Proposed law prohibits the fluoridation of any public water systems unless 15% of registered
voters in the precincts served by the water system sign a petition and a local election is
called by the governing authority in which a majority of registered voters approve
fluoridation.
Proposed law provides that systems are not required to remove any naturally occurring
fluoride from their water.
Effective January 1, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 40:5.11)
