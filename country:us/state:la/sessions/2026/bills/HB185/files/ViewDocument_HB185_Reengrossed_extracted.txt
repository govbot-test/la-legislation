WORKERS COMPENSATION: Provides relative to the definition of an independent
contractor
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 23:1021(introductory paragraph) and (7), relative to workers'
3 compensation; to provide for the definition of an independent contractor; to expand
4 the definition of an independent contractor; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 23:1021(introductory paragraph) and (7) are hereby amended and
7 reenacted to read as follows:
8 §1021. Terms defined
9 As used in this Chapter, unless the context clearly indicates otherwise, the
10 following terms shall be given have the meaning ascribed to them in this Section:
11 * * *
12 (7)(a) "Independent contractor" means any person who renders service, other
13 than manual labor, for a specified recompense for a specified result either as a unit
14 or as a whole, under the control of his principal as to results of his work only, and not
15 as to the means by which such result is accomplished, and are expressly excluded
16 from the provisions of this Chapter unless a substantial part of the work time of an
17 independent contractor is spent in manual labor by him in carrying out the terms of
18 the contract, in which case the independent contractor is expressly covered by the
19 provisions of this Chapter. The operation of a truck tractor or truck tractor trailer,
20 including fueling, driving, connecting and disconnecting electrical lines and air

HB 185 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Melerine
Abstract: For the purposes of workers' compensation, expands the definition of an
independent contractor by providing additional individuals who shall also be
considered independent contractors.
Present law defines an individual who is considered an independent contractor for the
purposes of workers' compensation.
Proposed law expands the definition of independent contractor to also include employees of
independent contractors or other independent contractors contracting with the independent
contractor, who are also substantially engaged in manual labor. Proposed law clarifies that
the aforementioned definition expansion applies to an independent contractor who is
substantially engaged in manual labor and is expressly covered by the provisions of present
law.
(Amends R.S. 23:1021(intro. para.) and (7))
