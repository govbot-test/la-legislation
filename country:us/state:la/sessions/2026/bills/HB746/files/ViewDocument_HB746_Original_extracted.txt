2026 Regular Session
HOUSE BILL NO. 746
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
LOCAL GOVERNMENT: Provides relative to oversized trucking permits issued by local
governments
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 32:387(A), relative to statewide uniform permits for oversized
3 vehicles; to restrict local governments from adopting permit requirements for
4 oversized vehicles; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 32:387(A) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §387. Special permits
8 A.(1) No vehicle or combination of vehicles that does not meet the
9 requirements of R.S. 32:380 through 386 shall use the public highways of this state
10 without first obtaining a special permit from the department in addition to any other
11 special permit which may be required from any other agency or department of the
12 state or a political subdivision, and the special permit herein provided for in this
13 Section shall be issued at the discretion of the secretary. If a permit issued pursuant
14 to Subsection B of this Section authorizes a vehicle to operate on interstate
15 highways, the secretary may allow the vehicle to operate on interstate highways at
16 night if requested by the owner or operator.
17 (2) No political subdivision shall adopt any special permit standard that
18 exceeds the requirements contained in Paragraph (1) of this Subsection.

HB 746 Original 2026 Regular Session Owen
Abstract: Prohibits political subdivisions from issuing special permits outside of the
permits issued by the Dept. of Transportation and Development.
Present law requires oversized vehicles or combination of vehicles that do not meet the
requirements of present law obtain a special permit when using public highways of the state.
Further specifies that the Dept. of Transportation and Development be the entity to issue
special permits. Further authorizes any other agencies or departments of the state or political
subdivisions to issue other special permits.
Proposed law prohibits political subdivisions from adopting any special permit standard that
exceeds the requirements contained in present law.
(Amends R.S. 32:387(A))
