TECHNOLOGY. Authorizes creation of the Pilot Innovation Hub. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 38:2211.3, relative to innovation and economic development; to create the
3 Pilot Innovation Hub; to provide for designation of a Pilot Innovation Hub; relative
4 to fostering and testing innovative technologies; to provide for requirements to
5 complete the program; to provide for public notice; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 38:2211.3 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §2211.3. Pilot Innovation Hub; creation; jurisdiction
10 A. The governing authority of a parish is hereby authorized to enact a
11 resolution designating the parish as a Pilot Innovation Hub.
12 B. The Pilot Innovation Hub designation shall permit the validation and
13 implementation of innovative technologies to address public challenges,
14 stimulate local economic activity, and create job opportunities. Local business
15 growth and technology commercialization is hereby recognized as a key
16 component to efficiently and safely address public challenges, an essential factor
17 in economic development, and a primary contributor to an all-of-government

2 C.(1) Any political subdivision within the jurisdiction designated as a
3 Pilot Innovation Hub may do all of the following:
4 (a) Establish reasonable procedures governing the competitive selection
5 of the pilot innovation contractor.
6 (b) Enter into a contractual agreement with the pilot innovation
7 contractor for the purposes of directly managing the programming.
8 (c) Use public property and resources and align procedures including
9 permitting and zoning, to implement programming.
10 (2) Pilot innovation contractors, when and if authorized by a political
11 subdivision with a Pilot Innovation Hub shall do all of the following:
12 (a) Establish reasonable procedures governing the competitive selection
13 and vetting of program participants.
14 (b) Develop and implement programming.
15 (c) Define benchmarks and deployment metrics.
16 (d) Implement programming in a manner that does not adversely impact
17 the political subdivision or the health, safety, or welfare of the residents of the
18 parish.
19 D. Successful completion of programming shall be demonstrated by a
20 comprehensive pilot report, compiled by the pilot innovation contractor and
21 delivered to the parish governing authority or political subdivision. The
22 comprehensive pilot report shall:
23 (1) Provide performance data, roadmap for scaled deployment including
24 integration requirements and regulatory considerations.
25 (2) Demonstrate that usage of technology delivers a valid, useful public
26 purpose and address the goals outlined in the agreement.
27 E. Upon successful completion of programming through the Pilot
28 Innovation Hub, the parish governing authority or political authority may
29 award a sole-source contract to the innovator without competitive bidding,

2 (1) The parish shall give notice to the public of its intended sole-source

4 (2) The business has dedicated operations in the state.
5 F. For the purposes of this Section:
6 (1) "Programming" means any activities related to the piloting and
7 validation of technologies intended to address public challenges located within
8 the jurisdictional boundaries of the Pilot Innovation Hub, including but not
9 limited to flooding, water management, subsidence, infrastructure challenges,

13 nongovernmental organization, or venture platform with demonstrated
14 experience in developing and managing pilot designed programs to validate
15 start-up technologies.

DIGEST
SB 384 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Harris
Proposed law authorizes the governing authority of any parish to enact a resolution to
designate the parish as a Pilot Innovation Hub.
Proposed law provides that the Pilot Innovation Hub designation permits the validation and
implementation of innovative technologies to address public challenges, stimulate local
economic activity, and create job opportunities.
Proposed law recognizes local business growth and technology commercialization as a key
component of economic development and contributors to statewide economic
competitiveness.
Proposed law authorizes any political subdivision within a Pilot Innovation Hub, to establish
procedures for competitive selection of a pilot innovation contractor, enter into contracts
with a pilot innovation contractor to manage programming, and utilize public property and
align permitting and zoning to implement programming.
Proposed law allows pilot innovation contractors to establish competitive selection and
vetting procedures for program participants, develop and implement programming, define
benchmarks and deployment metrics, and ensure that programming does not adversely

impact public health, safety, or welfare.
Proposed law provides that successful completion of programming shall be demonstrated
by a comprehensive pilot report that includes performance data and a roadmap for scaled
deployment and demonstrates a valid public purpose and achievement of program goals.
Proposed law provides that, upon successful completion of programming, the parish
governing authority or political subdivision may award a sole-source contract to the
innovator without competitive bidding, subject to public notice and a thirty-day protest
period and a requirement that the business maintain operations in the state.
Proposed law defines the terms "programming" and "pilot innovation contractor".
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 38:2211.3)
