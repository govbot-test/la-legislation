SLS 26RS-2401 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 158
BY SENATOR WOMACK
LOCAL AGENCIES. Requests local governing authorities to consider adopting local
ordinances to require unlicensed residential facilities for seniors or persons with disabilities
to have generators or alternative electrical power sources.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request local governing authorities, in consultation with parish offices of
3 homeland security and emergency preparedness, to consider adopting local
4 ordinances to require unlicensed residential facilities for seniors or persons with
5 disabilities to have generators or alternative electrical power sources.
6 WHEREAS, Louisiana has experienced multiple devastating hurricanes that have
7 caused extensive power outages in the past five years; and
8 WHEREAS, during the severe winter storm in January 2026, many residents in north
9 Louisiana were left without power for days; and
10 WHEREAS, certain populations, including the elderly and disabled, are highly
11 vulnerable during extended power outages caused by natural disasters, with an increased risk
12 for cardiovascular and respiratory crises, hypothermia or hyperthermia, and the failure of
13 life-sustaining medical equipment; and
14 WHEREAS, due to certain unlicensed facilities housing these vulnerable
15 populations, considerations of backup power to essential systems like heating, air
16 conditioning, fire alarms, and emergency lighting are critical to stopping the creation of
17 additional life-threatening situations; and
18 WHEREAS, these measures are designed to prevent such tragedies and keep

1 residents and staff safe during emergencies while minimizing the use of 911 and impacts on
2 public safety; and
3 WHEREAS, Act No. 253 of the 2022 Regular Session of the Legislature of Louisiana
4 required all nursing homes to have a generator or other Louisiana Department of Health
5 approved alternate electrical power source; and
6 WHEREAS, Senate Bill No. 145 of the 2026 Regular Session would require all adult
7 residential care providers to have a generator or other alternate electrical power source; and
8 WHEREAS, rental housing specifically designated for the elderly and persons with
9 disabilities receiving supportive housing services, including but not limited to housing
10 facilities that receive funding or financing support from the United States Department of
11 Housing and Urban Development, are not regulated by the state and do not have to meet the
12 same requirements as state-regulated and licensed facilities; and
13 WHEREAS, pursuant to R.S. 29:729, each parish office of homeland security and
14 emergency preparedness is required to prepare and maintain an all hazards emergency
15 operations plan that includes the prevention and minimization of injury and damage caused
16 by disaster or emergency and recommendations for zoning, building, and other land use
17 controls and other preventive and preparedness measures designed to eliminate or reduce
18 disasters or their impact; and
19 WHEREAS, local governing authorities can regulate by ordinance critical safety
20 measures for any setting in which multiple unrelated individuals are residing in a home and
21 those individuals are paying rent or other compensation for such shelter.
22 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
23 does hereby urge and request local governing authorities, in consultation with parish offices
24 of homeland security and emergency preparedness, to consider adopting local ordinances to
25 require unlicensed residential facilities for seniors or persons with disabilities to have
26 generators or alternative electrical power sources.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
28 Louisiana Municipal Association and the Policy Jury Association of Louisiana.
