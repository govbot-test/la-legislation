WHEREAS, the commencement of the American Revolution in 1775 led to the
liberation of the American colonies and people from British rule; and
WHEREAS, the American victory at Yorktown and the signing of the Treaty of Paris
led to the creation of the United States of America and the founding documents of American
liberty, justice, and freedom, the Declaration of Independence, the Constitution of the United
States of America, including the Bill of Rights, and the Federalist Papers; and
WHEREAS, the constitution creates the framework of our government and
establishes the separation of powers and system of checks and balances that protects
America's freedoms and system of justice; and
WHEREAS, the Legislature of Louisiana has demonstrated its commitment to the
education of Louisiana's people on these historical documents and events by enacting laws
requiring their inclusion in the education of the state's students.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commemorate the commencement of the American
Revolution and does hereby recognize the importance of America's founding documents.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Legislature
of Louisiana encourages the people of Louisiana to study and acknowledge the significance
of America's founding documents and remember and honor those who fought in, and gave
aid and comfort to, the cause of the American Revolution.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
