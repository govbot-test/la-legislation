SENATE FLOOR AMENDMENTS
2026 Regular Session
Representative Jackson
1 AMENDMENT NO. 1
2 Delete all Amendments proposed by the Senate Committee on Judiciary B and adopted by
3 the Senate on May 27, 2026.
4 AMENDMENT NO. 2
5 On page 1 line 2, after "To" delete the remainder of the line, delete line 3, and insert the
6 following:
7 "enact R.S. 27:393.2, relative to gaming activity; to provide for an enhanced
8 promotional play pilot program; to provide for limitations; to provide for a
9 termination date; and to"
10 AMENDMENT NO. 3
11 On page 1, line 6, after "Section 1." delete the remainder of the line, delete lines 7 through
12 17, delete page 2, and insert the following:
13 "Section 1. R.S. 27:393.2 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
14 § 393.2. Enhanced promotional play pilot program
15 A. Notwithstanding R.S. 27:353(9) or any other law to the contrary,
16 beginning on July 1, 2026 and terminating on June 30, 2027, for the purposes of this
17 Chapter, "net slot machine proceeds shall mean the total of all cash and property
18 received by a licensee from slot machine gaming operations minus the amount of
19 cash or prizes paid to winners and seven million dollars directly attributable to
20 promotional play wagers.
21 B. The additional two million dollars directly attributable to promotional play
22 wagers provided by this Section shall not be assigned to another licensee or casino
23 gaming operator as provided by Act No. 258 of the 2026 Regular Session.
24 C. No later than March 1, 2027, the licensees shall provide a comprehensive
25 report to the Gaming Control Board, the Senate Committee on Judiciary B, and the
26 House Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice demonstrating any
27 additional net slot machine proceeds directly attributable to the enhanced
28 promotional play for the period of July 1 through December 31, 2026, and the effect
29 on the collection and disposition of fees and taxes on credits deposited into local
30 governing authority funds described in R.S. 27:392.
31 Section 2. The provisions of this Act shall cease as of June 30, 2027."
