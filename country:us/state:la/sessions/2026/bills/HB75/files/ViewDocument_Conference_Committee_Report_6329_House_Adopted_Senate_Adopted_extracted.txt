CONFERENCE COMMITTEE REPORT DIGEST
HB 75 2026 Regular Session Jackson
Keyword and oneliner of the instrument as it left the House
GAMING: Provides for the allowable amount of promotional play offered by certain gaming
operators and for deductions regarding promotional play
Report adopts Senate amendments to:
1. Create a pilot program that increases the amount of untaxable promotional play
wagers from five million dollars to seven million dollars.
Report rejects Senate amendments which would have:
1. Increased the amount of untaxable promotional play wagers from five million dollars
to seven million dollars for riverboat casinos, the official gaming establishment in
New Orleans, and racetracks.
Report amends the bill to:
1. Clarify that the increase in promotional play wagers shall not diminish the amount
of locally dedicated funds relative to the 2025-2026 fiscal year.
Digest of the bill as proposed by the Conference Committee
Present law provides for promotional play wagers by licensees operating slot machines at
racing facilities. Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law temporarily redefines "net slot machine proceeds," notwithstanding present law
(R.S. 27:353(9)), and provides for the application and distribution of such proceeds from July
1, 2026, through June 30, 2027.
Proposed law creates a pilot program to increase the amount of promotional play wagers
available to licensees from $5M to $7M.
Proposed law prohibits the additional $2M directly attributable to promotional play wagers
from being assigned to another licensee or casino gaming operator.
Proposed law requires licensees, by March 1, 2027, to provide a report to the Gaming
Control Bd., the Senate Committee on Judiciary B, and the House Committee on
Administration of Criminal Justice demonstrating any additional net slot machine proceeds
and the effect on collecting and disposing of fees and taxes.
Proposed law ensures, notwithstanding a force majeure, that local entities and authorities
receive an amount not less than the amount received in the 2025-2026 fiscal year.
Proposed law terminates on June 30, 2027.
