HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 243
BY REPRESENTATIVES DICKERSON, MARTINEZ, AND SAWYER
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request that the parish governing authorities surrounding the Tickfaw River
erect safety warning signage on the Tickfaw River.
WHEREAS, the Tickfaw River is a one hundred thirteen mile long river that flows
from southwest Mississippi through southeast Louisiana, emptying into Lake Maurepas; and
WHEREAS, there are currently no parish governing authority signs erected for the
safety of boaters on the Tickfaw River; and
WHEREAS, on May 20, 2020, a single vessel incident occurred on the Tickfaw
River when the vessel attempted to turn from the Natalbany River into the Tickfaw River
but struck the west bank of the Tickfaw River; and
WHEREAS, two injuries were reported and contributing factors were careless and
reckless operation and operator inattention; and
WHEREAS, on April 16, 2023, another a single vessel incident occurred on the
Tickfaw River when the vessel attempted to turn at a high rate of speed but lost control,
traveling into the trees and coming to rest on its side; and
WHEREAS, two injuries were reported and contributing factors were excessive
speed, a rules of the road violation, and operator inexperience; and
WHEREAS, on July 28, 2024, there was a fatal two-boat collision on the Tickfaw
River near Kings Point in Livingston Parish, Louisiana, which resulted in two deaths and six
injuries; and
WHEREAS, on December 15, 2024, a two vessel incident occurred on the Tickfaw
River when vessel one was headed upstream on the Tickfaw River struck vessel two, headed

downstream and contributing factors were a rules of the road violation, sharp turn, and
operator inattention; and
WHEREAS, on June 10, 2025, a single vessel incident occurred on the Tickfaw
River, when the vessel attempted to turn from the Natalbany River into the Tickfaw River
but struck a tree on the bank of the Tickfaw River; and
WHEREAS, no injuries were reported and a contributing factor was careless and
reckless operation; and
WHEREAS, a total of thirteen boating incidents occurred on the Tickfaw River from
2020 to 2025, resulting in two fatalities and eighteen injuries, with various contributing
factors; and
WHEREAS, in the interest of public safety, there is a necessity to erect safety
warning signage on the Tickfaw River as a means of making boaters aware of potential
hazards on the river.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the parish governing authorities
surrounding the Tickfaw River to erect safety warning signage on the Tickfaw River.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
presidents of the parish governing authorities surrounding the Tickfaw River.
