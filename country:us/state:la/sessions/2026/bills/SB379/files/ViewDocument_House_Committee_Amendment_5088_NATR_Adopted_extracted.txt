1 §214.35. Reconsiderations, judicial review
2 * * *
3 B. A decision or determination shall be subject to reconsideration by the
4 secretary if a petition for reconsideration is filed in writing with the secretary within
5 ten days following public notice of a posting of the final coastal use permit, or local
6 program approval decision, or determination made under R.S. 49:214.34 on the
7 official website for the Department of Conservation and Energy or receipt of
8 written notice of a determination made under R.S. 49:214.30(C)(1) or R.S.
9 49:214.34. The grounds for reconsideration shall be either that one of the following:
10 (1) The decision or determination is clearly contrary to the law or the
11 evidence before the secretary;.
12 (2) The petitioner has discovered, since the decision or determination,
13 evidence important to the issues which he could not, with due diligence, have
14 presented to the secretary prior to the decision;.
15 (3) There is a showing that issues not previously considered, through no fault
16 of the petitioner, ought to be examined in order to properly dispose of the matter; or.
17 (4) There exist other good grounds for further consideration of the issues and
18 the evidence in the public interest.
19 * * *
20 E. Proceedings for review may be instituted by filing a petition in the district
21 court of the parish in which the proposed use is to be situated within thirty days after
22 mailing of notice of the final decision by the secretary or, if a reconsideration is
23 requested, within thirty days after the decision thereon. the following time periods:
24 (1) If a petition for reconsideration is not filed, the proceeding for review
25 shall be instituted within thirty days after the final decision by the secretary
26 under this Subpart is posted on the official website for the Department of
27 Conservation and Energy.
28 (2) If a petition for reconsideration is filed, the proceeding for review
29 shall be instituted within thirty days after the decision regarding the
30 reconsideration is posted on the official website for the Department of
31 Conservation and Energy.
32 F. A third person having an interest in the matter subject to review has
33 the right to intervene as a party in an action for judicial review under this
34 Section when the intervention is unlikely to unduly broaden the issues or to
35 unduly impede the resolution of the action. A "third person having an interest"
36 with the right to intervene under this Section shall include but not be limited to
37 any applicant, permittee, or party to the underlying administrative process,
38 action, or proceeding that resulted in the issuance of the rule, regulation,
39 permit, decision, or determination that is the subject of the suit for review. For
40 avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this Subsection shall be given prospective
41 and retroactive application.
42 G. Judicial review shall otherwise be pursuant to the Louisiana
43 Administrative Procedure Act, provided that all such cases shall be tried with
44 preference and priority. Trial de novo shall be held upon request of any party.
45 * * *
46 §978.1. Judicial review of adjudication
47 A. * * *
48 (3) A third person having an interest in the matter subject to review has
49 the right to intervene as a party in an action under this Section when the
50 intervention is unlikely to unduly broaden the issues or to unduly impede the
51 resolution of the action. A "third person having an interest" with the right to
52 intervene under this Section shall include but not be limited to any applicant,
53 permittee, or party to the underlying administrative process, action, or
54 proceeding that resulted in the issuance of the decision or order that is the
55 subject of the suit for review. For avoidance of doubt, the provisions of this
56 Paragraph shall be given prospective and retroactive application.
57 * * *"
