DIGEST
SB 517 Original 2026 Regular Session Barrow
Present law (R.S. 33:2740.67) created the Baton Rouge North Economic Development
District.
Present law (R.S. 33:2740.67(C)) provides that the district shall be managed by a nine-
member board of commissioners. The board shall be comprised of members as follows:
(1) One member shall be appointed by the state representative for House District No. 29,
who shall be a representative of the business community, on a rotating basis
beginning in 2022 and every other year thereafter.
(2) Two members shall be appointed by the state senator for Senate District No. 15.
(3) One member with a background in economic development shall be appointed by the
mayor-president of the parish of East Baton Rouge.
(4) One member shall be appointed with a community development background located
within the district to be selected by a subcommittee formed by the board.
(5) One member shall be appointed by the owner of the largest parcel of land located

(6) One member shall be appointed by the state representative for House District No. 61,

(7) One member shall be appointed by the state representative for House District No. 63,
on a rotating basis beginning in 2022 and every other year thereafter.
(8) One member shall be appointed by the state representative for House District No. 67,
on a rotating basis beginning in 2023 and every other year thereafter.
(9) One member shall be appointed by the state representative for House District No.
101, on a rotating basis beginning in 2023 and every other year thereafter.
(10) One member shall be appointed by the state senator for Senate District No. 14.
Proposed law changes present law and provides that the member appointed by the state
representative for House District No. 29, does not have to be a representative of the business
community.
Proposed law changes present law and provides that the member with the community
background shall be selected from a list of three nominees submitted by a nominating
subcommittee established by the board.
Proposed law removes the rotating terms of board members, resulting in the board of
commissioners being composed of eleven members.
Present law provides that board members shall serve two-year terms.
Proposed law increases the term of board members from two-year terms to four-year terms.
Proposed law provides that no member shall serve more than two-consecutive four-year
terms. Provides that a partial term of two years or less shall not count toward the term limit.
Further provides that service for more than two years of a partial term limit shall count as
one consecutive term.
Proposed law provides that two members shall serve an initial term of one year; two member
shall serve an initial term of two years; three member shall serve an initial term of three
years; four member shall serve an initial term of four years, all as determined by lot at the
first meeting of the board. Further provides that each reappointment to a seat shall be for
a four-year term expiring in the same cycle year applicable to that seat
Present law provides that board members shall be eligible for reappointment, but the
cumulative term of service shall not exceed four years.
Proposed law retains present law but removes the four year cumulative term restriction.
Present law provides that any vacancy which occurs prior to the expiration of the term for
which a member of the board has been appointed shall be filled for the remainder of the
unexpired term in the same manner as the original appointment.
Proposed law retains present law but provides that if a vacancy is not filled within 60 days,
the governor shall appoint an interim member from a list of three nominees provided by the
original appointing authority.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 33:2740.67(C)(1), (2), (3) and (4))
