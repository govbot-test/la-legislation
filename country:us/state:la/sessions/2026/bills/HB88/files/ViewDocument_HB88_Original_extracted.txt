1 A JOINT RESOLUTION
2 Proposing to amend Article III, Section 13(A) of the Constitution of Louisiana, to provide
3 relative to notice of intent to introduce a bill to enact a local or special law; to

5 legislator who intends to introduce a bill to enact a local or special law under certain

7 notice to be published if a legislator is not responsible for the notice; to provide for
8 submission of the proposed amendment to the electors; and to provide for related
9 matters.

11 elected to each house concurring, that there shall be submitted to the electors of the state of

13 amend Article III, Section 13(A) of the Constitution of Louisiana, to read as follows:
14 §13. Local or Special Laws; Notice of Intent; Publication
15 Section 13. (A)(1) Except as otherwise provided in this Section, no local or
16 special law shall be enacted unless notice of the intent to introduce a bill to enact
17 such a law has been published on two separate days, without cost to the state, in the
18 official journal of the locality where the matter to be affected is situated. The last
19 day of publication shall be at least thirty days prior to introduction of the bill.

HB 88 Original 2026 Regular Session Bayham
Abstract: Requires that the notice for a local or special bill that affects an area outside of
the district of a member of the legislature who intends to introduce the bill include
the name of at least one member who intends to introduce the bill and requires that
the notice include the name of the person causing the notice to be published if done
so by a person other than a legislator.
Present constitution provides that no local or special law shall be enacted unless notice of
the intent to introduce a bill to enact such a law has been published on two separate days,
without cost to the state, in the official journal of the locality where the matter to be affected
is situated. Requires that the notice state the substance of the contemplated law.

Proposed constitutional amendment retains present constitution.
Proposed constitutional amendment requires that if the notice is for a bill that affects an area
outside of the district of a member of the legislature who intends to introduce the bill, the
notice shall include the name of at least one member who intends to introduce the bill.
Further requires that if the notice is caused to be published by a person other than a member
of the legislature, the notice shall include the name of the person who caused the notice to
be published.
Provides for submission of the proposed amendment to the voters at the statewide election
to be held November 3, 2026.
(Amends Const. Art. III, §13(A))
