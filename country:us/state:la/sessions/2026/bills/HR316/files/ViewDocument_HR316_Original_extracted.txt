2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 316
BY REPRESENTATIVE WYBLE
AGRICULTURE: Urges and requests the LSU AgCenter, in coordination with the Southern
Ag Center and the Louisiana Department of Agriculture and Forestry, to study and
develop strategies to strengthen small and mid-scale agricultural production and to
increase opportunities for young adults to participate in and pursue careers in
agriculture
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the LSU AgCenter, in coordination with the Southern Ag Center and
3 the Louisiana Department of Agriculture and Forestry, to study and develop
4 strategies to strengthen small and mid-scale agricultural production and to expand
5 opportunities for young adults to participate in and pursue careers in agriculture.
6 WHEREAS, agriculture is a vital component of Louisiana's economy, culture, and
7 rural communities and is directly tied to workforce development, economic growth,
8 employment opportunities, long-term food security, and the overall stability of the state; and
9 WHEREAS, small and mid-scale farms that are generally under two hundred fifty
10 acres are critical to local food systems, rural development, and direct-to-consumer markets;
11 and
12 WHEREAS, supporting the continued viability and expansion of such agricultural
13 operations is essential to sustaining and strengthening local food systems in the state; and
14 WHEREAS, there are barriers to entry, particularly for individuals under forty years
15 old, that limit participation in agriculture and hinder growth in agricultural production; and
16 WHEREAS, addressing these challenges requires attention to infrastructure, training,
17 and market access, which producers have identified as key needs for agricultural operations.
18 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
19 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the LSU AgCenter, in coordination

1 with the Southern Ag Center and the Louisiana Department of Agriculture and Forestry, to
2 study and develop strategies or best practices, including those from other states, to expand
3 production on farms under two hundred fifty acres, increase entry into agriculture among
4 young adults, and strengthen direct-to-consumer and value-added agricultural markets.
5 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include but not be limited to the
6 following areas:
7 (1) Sales and marketing training for direct-to-consumer producers.
8 (2) Access to processing facilities, particularly for poultry and small livestock.
9 (3) Cold storage infrastructure, including refrigerator and freezer capacity.
10 (4) Evaluation and improvement of the state's "Certified Louisiana" branding
11 program.
12 (5) Development of a statewide peer-to-peer training conference for producers.
13 (6) Expanded technical assistance through the LSU AgCenter focused on small-scale
14 agricultural production and value-added "clean food" production.
15 (7) Support for farmer mental health and wellness resources.
16 (8) Opportunities to expand agritourism, including farm-based events, on-site sales,
17 and other revenue-generating activities that connect consumers to local agriculture.
18 (9) Best practices and mentorship programs to support, expand, and encourage
19 participation among young and beginning farmers.
20 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the LSU AgCenter shall identify potential policy
21 recommendations, including incentives, partnerships, and workforce development strategies
22 to support these efforts.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the LSU AgCenter shall submit a written report
24 of its findings and recommendations to the House Committee on Agriculture, Forestry,
25 Aquaculture, and Rural Development no later than February 1, 2027.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
27 vice president for agriculture for the Louisiana State University, the chancellor of the
28 Southern University Agricultural Research and Extension Center, and the commissioner of
29 agriculture and forestry.

HR 316 Original 2026 Regular Session Wyble
Urges and requests the LSU AgCenter, in coordination with the Southern Ag Center and the
La. Dept. of Agriculture and Forestry, to study and develop strategies to strengthen small
and mid-scale agricultural production and increase opportunities for young adults to
participate in and pursue careers in agriculture. Requires the LSU AgCenter submit a
written report to the House Committee on Agriculture, Forestry, Aquaculture, and Rural
Development on or before Feb. 1, 2027.
