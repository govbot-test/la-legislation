HOUSE BILL NO. 847
BY REPRESENTATIVE BUTLER
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 3:1206(A) and 1207(C) and (G) and to enact R.S. 3:1206(C),
3 relative to the election of district supervisors for the state soil and water conservation
4 commission; to establish the method and administration of elections; to provide for
5 supervisor terms, the filling of vacancies, and eligibility requirements for
6 reappointment or reelection; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 3:1206(A) and 1207(C) and (G) are hereby amended and reenacted
9 and R.S. 3:1206(C) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1206. Election of three supervisors for each district
11 A. Within thirty days after the date of issuance by the secretary of state of
12 a certificate of organization of a soil and water conservation district, nomination
13 petitions may be filed with the state soil and water conservation commission to
14 nominate candidates for supervisors of the district. The commission shall have
15 authority to extend the time within which nominating petitions may be filed. No
16 nominating petition shall be accepted by the board, unless it shall be the registrar of
17 voters in the parish where the signers reside has certified, pursuant to R.S. 18:3, that
18 the petition has been subscribed by twenty-five or more qualified voters within the
19 district. who are qualified to vote under the constitution and law of this state.
20 Qualified voters may sign more than one such nominating petition to nominate more
21 than one candidate for supervisor. The commission shall give due notice of an
22 election to be held for the election of three supervisors for the district. The names

14 * * *
15 C. The secretary of state and the commissioner of agriculture and forestry
16 may adopt rules and regulations as may be necessary to carry out the intent and
17 purposes of this Section.
18 §1207. Appointment, qualifications, tenure of supervisors
19 * * *
20 C. The three elected supervisors of the district shall be elected annually, one
21 each year., These elections of supervisors shall be held throughout the state
22 regularly on the second Saturday in June, each year, in accordance with the general
23 procedure and in the manner prescribed in R.S. 3:1206. Terms of office of all
24 supervisors so elected shall begin on July 1st first, following the date of their
25 election. All supervisors presently in office and serving on the governing body of
26 a conservation district, shall remain in office until their terms expire, but from and
27 after August 1st, 1956, all vacancies shall be filled in the manner above prescribed,
28 and whenever the term of any supervisor expires, it shall be filled by an election to
29 be held on the second Saturday in June after the expiration of said term.
