DIGEST
SB 499 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Proposed law defines the term "Certified biometrically-bound age assurance credential" as
a digital or physical credential issued by the commissioner that meets all of the following
criteria:
(1) Establishes or verifies an individual's age or age eligibility in compliance with
Digital Identity Guidelines, published by the National Institute of Standards and
Technology (NIST).
(2) Is cryptographically bound to one or more biometric identifiers of the individual so
that the credential cannot be transferred, shared, or used by any other person.
(3) Meets applicable age assurance, identity-proofing, and security standards.
Proposed law requires any certified biometrically-bound age assurance credential presented
in digital-wallet or printed form be accepted by any permittee as lawful proof of age when
the quick QR code is scanned and the live facial match confirms the holder.
Proposed law prohibits any full identity data or raw biometric image from being transmitted
to or retained by the relying party beyond the age indicator.
Proposed law requires that, subject to appropriation, the commissioner of the office of
alcohol and tobacco control (commissioner) provide approved applicable equipment,
including but not limited to computer hardware and software necessary for permittees to
confirm the certified biometrically-bound age assurance credential of a patron before
entering the premises.
Proposed law requires the commissioner to promulgate rules in accordance with the APA
to implement the provisions of proposed law.
Proposed law requires that proposed law be cited as the "Madison Brooks Law."
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 26:90(L))
