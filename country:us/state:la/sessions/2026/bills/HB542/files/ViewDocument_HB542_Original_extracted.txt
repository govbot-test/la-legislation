HB 542 Original 2026 Regular Session McMakin
Abstract: Provides relative to supplemental pay to certain law enforcement officers whose
agency is headquartered in Baton Rouge
Present law requires the payment of extra compensation in the amount of $600 per month
for any full-time commissioned law enforcement officer employed by a bona fide police
agency of the state or its political subdivisions, other than the Department of Public Safety
and Corrections and the Department of Wildlife and Fisheries, and headquartered in either
the city of New Orleans or Lake Charles, who serves the welfare of the public by providing
police services to the general public by effecting arrests, issuing citations, and serving
warrants, while patrolling levees, waterways, and riverfront areas, or while patrolling bridges
that are within the boundaries of the city of New Orleans or Lake Charles. Eligible officers
are required to have completed one year of service, be hired after March 31, 1986, and have
completed and passed a council-certified training program.
Proposed law retains present law and adds law enforcement agencies headquartered in the
city of Baton Rouge that patrol levees, waterways, and riverfront areas, or patrol bridges to
the list of law enforcement officers eligible to receive the extra compensation of $600 a
month.
Effective July 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 40:1667.1(A)(2)(a))
