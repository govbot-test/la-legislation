WHEREAS, the members of the House of Representatives of the Legislature of
Louisiana recognize the need to address domestic violence; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence is a serious crime that affects people of all races,
ages, gender, and income levels; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence is widespread and affects thousands of Louisiana
families each year; and
WHEREAS, the act of domestic violence violates an individual's privacy, dignity,
security, and humanity due to the systematic use of physical, emotional, sexual,
psychological, and economic abuse; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence victims deserve access to safety, resources, and legal
protections; and
WHEREAS, The Louisiana Coalition Against Domestic Violence is the federally
designated statewide coalition of shelters, non-residential programs, and individuals working
to end domestic violence in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, on April 28, 2026, the Louisiana Coalition Against Domestic Violence
will host its annual day at the state capitol with a display in Memorial Hall; and
WHEREAS, Domestic Violence Prevention Advocacy Day at the state capitol
provides an opportunity for citizens of Louisiana to learn more about preventing domestic
violence and to show support for the organizations and individuals who provide critical
advocacy, services and assistance to victims.

HR NO. 204 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Tuesday, April 28, 2026, as Domestic
Violence Prevention Advocacy Day in the state of Louisiana; does hereby commend and
encourage diligence in the efforts to prevent violence against women; and does hereby urge
all citizens and all community organizations to join in this observance.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 204
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
A RESOLUTION
To designate Tuesday, April 28, 2026, as Domestic Violence Prevention Advocacy Day in
the state of Louisiana.
WHEREAS, the members of the House of Representatives of the Legislature of
Louisiana recognize the need to address domestic violence; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence is a serious crime that affects people of all races,
ages, gender, and income levels; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence is widespread and affects thousands of Louisiana
families each year; and
WHEREAS, the act of domestic violence violates an individual's privacy, dignity,
security, and humanity due to the systematic use of physical, emotional, sexual,
psychological, and economic abuse; and
WHEREAS, domestic violence victims deserve access to safety, resources, and legal
protections; and
WHEREAS, The Louisiana Coalition Against Domestic Violence is the federally
designated statewide coalition of shelters, non-residential programs, and individuals working
to end domestic violence in Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, on April 28, 2026, the Louisiana Coalition Against Domestic Violence
will host its annual day at the state capitol with a display in Memorial Hall; and
WHEREAS, Domestic Violence Prevention Advocacy Day at the state capitol
provides an opportunity for citizens of Louisiana to learn more about preventing domestic
violence and to show support for the organizations and individuals who provide critical
advocacy, services and assistance to victims.
