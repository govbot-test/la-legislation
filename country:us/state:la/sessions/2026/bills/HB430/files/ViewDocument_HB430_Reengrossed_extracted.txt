HB 430 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Chassion
Abstract: Extends health insurance coverage for the families of law enforcement officers
and firemen killed in the line of duty employed by the city of Lafayette.
Present law requires the employer of a law enforcement officer or fireman who is killed in
the course of the performance of his official duties or arising out of any activity on or off
duty in his capacity as a law enforcement officer or fireman, in the protection of life or
property, to provide and pay for health insurance coverage for the law enforcement officer's
or fireman's surviving spouse for two years following the death of the law enforcement
officer or fireman. Requires the employer to provide and pay for health insurance coverage
for the law enforcement officer's or fireman's surviving children, stepchildren, and adopted
children for as long as the child remains qualified for the benefit or two years, whichever
comes first.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law enacts the "MJ 911 Act" in memory of officers lost in the line of duty.
Proposed law requires the employer of a law enforcement officer or fireman employed by
the city of Lafayette to provide health insurance coverage until the law enforcement officer's
surviving spouse or the surviving spouse of a fireman reaches Medicare eligibility. Extends
health insurance coverage to the law enforcement officer's or fireman's child, stepchild, or
adopted child who is any of the following:
(1) Under the age of 26.
(2) Physically or mentally disabled.
Proposed law requires health insurance coverage for children until a child no longer meets
the specified qualifications.

Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Appropriations to the
original bill:
1. Changes the termination of health insurance coverage for a deceased fireman or
law enforcement officer's surviving spouse from the remainder of the surviving
spouse's life to until the surviving spouse reaches Medicare eligibility.
2. Changes the age until which a deceased law enforcement officer or fireman's
child, stepchild, or adopted child qualifies for heath insurance coverage pursuant
to proposed law from 18 years of age to 26 years of age.
3. Removes provision requiring coverage if a child, stepchild, or adopted child is
under the age of 23 and enrolled in secondary school or an accredited college or
university.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Changes the employer required to provide health insurance coverage under
proposed law from Lafayette Parish to the city of Lafayette.
