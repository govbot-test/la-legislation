4 assistance in conducting the nursing home early voting program; to provide for the

18 the secretary the number of commissioners that is required for such assistance. A
19 commissioner who assists the registrar in the conduct of early voting shall take an

HB 551 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Billings
Abstract: Provides for the use of commissioners by the registrar of voters during the early
voting period to assist with conducting the nursing home early voting program.
Present law (R.S. 18:1333) provides for the nursing home early voting program. Provides
that a person who resides in a nursing home and who is entitled to vote may vote early
during the period extending at least two weeks prior to the beginning day for early voting
through the last day for early voting established by present law.
Present law (R.S. 18:1309(J)(1)) provides that, upon approval of the secretary of state, a
registrar of voters may utilize commissioners selected and trained by the registrar of voters
to assist the registrar during early voting period in the conduct of early voting by his office.
Provides that a registrar of voters shall, in seeking the approval of the secretary of state,
indicate to the secretary the number of commissioners that is required for such assistance.
Provides a commissioner who assists the registrar in the conduct of early voting shall take
an oath of office as a deputy registrar for the early voting period and shall complete an
affidavit prepared by the secretary of state that contains the name, address, and last four
digits of the social security number of the early voting commissioner and an
acknowledgment that the law prohibits the disclosure of confidential voter information listed
in the precinct register or early voting list kept by the registrar. Provides that a
commissioner who assists the registrar in the conduct of early voting shall be paid $150 for
each day of such assistance.
Proposed law retains present law and specifies that present law includes conducting the
nursing home early voting program.
Proposed law retains present law.
(Amends R.S. 18:1309(J)(1))
