HCASB201 4274 5432
HOUSE COMMITTEE AMENDMENTS
2026 Regular Session
Engrossed Senate Bill No. 201 by Senator Morris
1 AMENDMENT NO. 1
2 On page 1, line 2, change "Art. 878.1(D)" to "Article 878.1(A) and (D)"
3 AMENDMENT NO. 2
4 On page 1, line 6, change "Art. 878.1(D) is" to "Article 878.1(A) and (D) are"
5 AMENDMENT NO. 3
6 On page 1, between lines 8 and 9, insert the following:
7 "A.(1) If an offender is indicted on or after August 1, 2017, for the crime of
8 first degree murder (R.S. 14:30) where the offender was under the age of eighteen
9 years at the time of the commission of the offense, the district attorney may file a
10 notice of intent to seek a sentence of life imprisonment without possibility of parole
11 within one hundred eighty days after the indictment. If the district attorney timely
12 files the notice of intent, a hearing shall be conducted after conviction and prior to
13 sentencing to determine whether the sentence shall be imposed with or without
14 parole eligibility. If the court determines that the sentence shall be imposed with
15 parole eligibility, the offender shall be eligible for parole pursuant to the provisions
16 of R.S. 15:574.4(E). If the district attorney fails to timely file the notice of intent, the
17 sentence shall be imposed with parole eligibility and the offender shall be eligible
18 for parole pursuant to the provisions of R.S. 15:574.4(E) without the need of a
19 judicial determination pursuant to the provisions of this Article. If the court
20 determines that the sentence shall be imposed without parole eligibility, the offender
21 shall not be eligible for parole.
22 (2) If an offender is indicted on or after August 1, 2026, for the crime of
23 second degree murder (R.S. 14:30.1) where the offender was under the age of
24 eighteen years at the time of the commission of the offense, the district attorney
25 may file a notice of intent to seek a sentence of life imprisonment without
26 possibility of parole within one hundred eighty days after the indictment. If the
27 district attorney timely files the notice of intent, a hearing shall be conducted
28 after conviction and prior to sentencing to determine whether the sentence shall
29 be imposed with or without parole eligibility. If the court determines that the
30 sentence shall be imposed with parole eligibility, the offender shall be eligible
31 for parole pursuant to R.S. 15:574.4(F). If the district attorney fails to timely
32 file the notice of intent, the sentence shall be imposed with parole eligibility and
33 the offender shall be eligible for parole pursuant to R.S. 15:574.4(F) without the
34 need of a judicial determination pursuant to this Article. If the court
35 determines that the sentence shall be imposed without parole eligibility, the
36 offender shall not be eligible for parole."
