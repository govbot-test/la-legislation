DIGEST
SB 201 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Morris
Present law provides for a hearing to determine parole eligibility for certain juvenile
offenders.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law limits the hearing to the sole purpose of determining whether the sentence shall
be imposed with or without parole eligibility.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law requires the court to state for the record the considerations taken into account
and the factual basis for its determination.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that sentences imposed without parole eligibility and determinations
that an offender is not entitled to parole eligibility should normally be reserved for the worst
offenders and the worst cases.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Proposed law requires a reviewing court to give paramount consideration to the information
considered by the trier of fact that rendered the original sentence.
Proposed law prohibits the reviewing court, in cases where a juvenile offender was originally
sentenced to death after the court considered but rejected youth as a mitigating factor, from
substituting its judgment for the judgment of the trier of fact unless the trier of fact clearly
abused its discretion and could not have reasonably reached that result.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends C.Cr.P. Art. 878.1(D))
