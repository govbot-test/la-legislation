PUBLIC PROPERTY: Provides for the transfer of certain state property in Caddo Parish
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 17:1518.3 and to repeal Sections 5 through 8 of Act No. 93 of the 2025
3 Regular Session of the Legislature, relative to the transfer of certain state property
4 in Caddo Parish; to repeal authority granted to the commissioner of administration
5 and the secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development to transfer
6 certain property in Caddo Parish; to require the transfer of certain state property in
7 Caddo Parish from the Department of Transportation and Development to the
8 Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center Shreveport; to provide a property
9 description; to provide terms and conditions; to provide for an effective date; and to
10 provide for related matters.

17 Development to the Louisiana State University Health Sciences Center Shreveport:
18 Four (4) certain tracts or parcels of land, together with all
19 improvements situated thereon, consisting of Lots 52 through 66 of Salisbury
20 Park Subdivision and Lots 50, 51 and 52 of Samford Place Subdivision,

DIGEST
The digest printed below was prepared by House Legislative Services. It constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument. The keyword, one-liner, abstract, and digest do not constitute
part of the law or proof or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
HB 735 Original 2026 Regular Session Jackson
Abstract: Requires the Dept. of Transportation and Development (DOTD) transfer certain
state property to the LSU Health Sciences Center Shreveport.
Present law authorizes the commissioner of administration and the secretary of the DOTD
to transfer certain state property in Caddo Parish to either the LSU Health Sciences Center
Shreveport or the LSU Health Sciences Foundation Shreveport.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Present law authorizes the commissioner of administration to administer and supervise state
property and requires that the commissioner be a party to any transaction involving
immovable property of the state.
Proposed law requires that the DOTD transfer certain state property in Caddo Parish to the
LSU Health Sciences Center Shreveport, in accordance with present law.
Proposed law provides that the commissioner of administration and the secretary of the
DOTD are authorized to execute any documents necessary to effectuate the transfer.
Proposed law requires the LSU Health Sciences Center Shreveport to record the transfer in
the conveyance records for Caddo Parish.
Proposed law requires that the transfer be effective July 1, 2026.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 17:1518.3; Repeals Act No. 93 of 2025 R.S., §§5-8)
