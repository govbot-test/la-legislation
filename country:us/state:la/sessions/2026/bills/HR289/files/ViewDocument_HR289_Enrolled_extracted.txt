HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 289
BY REPRESENTATIVE MACK
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Department of Conservation and Energy to review policies and
practices related to the redaction of proposed well site locations.
WHEREAS, pursuant to R.S. 30:1 et seq., the Department of Conservation and
Energy is charged with regulating the siting, construction, and operation of wells; and
WHEREAS, the department is required to provide the public and interested parties
with an opportunity to participate in the permitting process; and
WHEREAS, a recent trend has emerged among Class VI permit applications that
include a request from the applicant that proposed well site locations not be made public;
and
WHEREAS, a prior version of the Louisiana Geologic Storage of Carbon Dioxide
Act contained a provision, R.S. 30:1104(F), which provided for confidentiality of
information contained in Class VI permit applications; and
WHEREAS, this provision was repealed by Act 458 of the 2025 Regular Session;
and
WHEREAS, R.S. 44:3.2 regarding proprietary and trade secret information is also
cited by applicants as justification for requesting the redaction of proposed well sites; and
WHEREAS, the department has previously notified Class VI applicants that the
location of their proposed Class VI wells should not be claimed as confidential and that the
department plans to upload the well locations on its website; and
WHEREAS, the department requires that the Class VI well location be publicly
available prior to progressing its review of a Class VI application towards the required public
notice and comment period that precedes the department's permit decision; and
WHEREAS, the department continues to look into ways to make the well locations
within Class VI applications publicly available; and

WHEREAS, the redaction of proposed well sites for any class of permit prevents the
public and parties who may have a direct interest in the site from being able to participate
in the permitting process; and
WHEREAS, public notice, hearing, and comment periods required by law cannot
perform their intended function if the public is not permitted to know about a proposed well
site; and
WHEREAS, it is imperative that the department review the current laws, regulations,
and policies regarding confidentiality of information contained in well permit applications
as well as the practices of permit applicants so that the department may effectively carry out
its duties and the public can participate in an informed way.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Conservation and
Energy to review all applicable and relevant laws, regulations, policies, and practices
regarding the redaction of proposed well sites in permit applications and to submit a written
report of its findings to the House Committee on Natural Resources and Environment,
together with any recommendations for legislative action required to allow the department
to carry out its mandates and the public to have a meaningful opportunity to participate in
the permitting process by March 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Conservation and Energy.
