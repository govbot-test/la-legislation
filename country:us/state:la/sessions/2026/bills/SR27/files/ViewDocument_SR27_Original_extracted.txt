SLS 26RS-850 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 27
BY SENATOR BOUDREAUX
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and congratulates Trinity Christian Methodist Episcopal
Church on the occasion of its 154th Anniversary.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate Trinity Christian Methodist Episcopal Church on the
3 occasion and celebration of its 154th Anniversary.
4 WHEREAS, originally named the Colored Methodist Episcopal Church in America,
5 Trinity Christian Methodist Episcopal Church is the oldest African American church in
6 Lafayette and began when free blacks found hope and meaning in the gospel preached by
7 former slave owners and started worshiping throughout the town of Vermilion, now
8 Lafayette, Louisiana; and
9 WHEREAS, on May 3, 1872, Governor Alexander Mouton granted a plot of ground
10 known as Lot Number 235 on the old Corporation of Vermilionville to the congregation of
11 the Colored Methodist Episcopal Church in America for "a church, parsonage or public
12 school house" to aid in the promotion and morality among the black population of the parish;
13 and
14 WHEREAS, a small group of free blacks erected a small frame building for worship
15 services which, despite the adversities of the 1880's and 1890's, grew and prospered, many
16 times being noted for its role providing education and other needed support systems for the
17 black citizens in the community; and
18 WHEREAS, beginning its spiritual journey in a tent, the congregation, guided by

1 faith and determination, through God's grace and hard work, the church was rebuilt in 1954
2 and located at 604 Lee Avenue, its current location, and the name "Colored" was officially
3 changed in 1956 to "Christian" and the church became known as "Trinity Christian
4 Methodist Episcopal Church", which today continues to be a special place in the lives of its
5 congregation; and
6 WHEREAS, throughout its long and distinguished history, Trinity Christian
7 Methodist Episcopal Church has become a beacon of hope and spiritual guidance within the
8 Lafayette community, providing leadership and compassion through numerous outreach
9 ministries that serve the needs of the people and is blessed with leadership by a number of
10 exceptional bishops, presiding elders, musicians, lay leaders, and faithful members; and
11 WHEREAS, Trinity is committed to service through meaningful programs that
12 include music ministry, participation in the downtown ministerial alliance, ministries
13 supporting the homeless and senior citizens, and outreach efforts to assist the poor and
14 underserved through its guiding motto, "We walk by faith, and not by sight", which reflects
15 the steadfast faith that has sustained the congregation through generations of worship,
16 service, and fellowship; and
17 WHEREAS, in June 2013, Bishop Thomas L. Brown, Sr. made history in assigning
18 Reverend Maggie C. Banks as the first woman pastor of Trinity Christian Methodist
19 Episcopal Church, and under her dedicated leadership, Trinity continues its mission of
20 spiritual growth, service, and community engagement, building upon the strong foundation
21 laid by generations of faithful members over the past one hundred fifty-four years; and
22 WHEREAS, the theme for the 154th Anniversary celebration, inspired by Philippians
23 1:3, "I thank my God for Trinity Christian Methodist Episcopal Church: A Look in Two
24 Directions", reflects both gratitude for the church's rich history and faith in its promising
25 future.
26 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
27 does hereby commend and congratulate Trinity Christian Methodist Episcopal Church of
28 Lafayette, Louisiana, on the occasion and celebration of its 154th Anniversary, recognizing
29 its historic contributions, spiritual leadership, and unwavering commitment to faith and
30 community service.
