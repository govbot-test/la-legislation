HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 193
BY REPRESENTATIVE FIRMENT
A RESOLUTION
To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of James David
"Cowboy" Robertson.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on April 2, 2026,
of James David "Cowboy" Robertson; and
WHEREAS, Cowboy was born on January 31, 1946, to William Roy Robertson, Sr.,
and Josie Boyd Robertson; he was the youngest of six children and was raised in a close and
loving family; and
WHEREAS, Cowboy was a lifelong resident of LaSalle Parish and a longtime
member of Temple Baptist Church in Jena, Louisiana, where faith and fellowship became
a central focus of his life; and
WHEREAS, Cowboy was a highly decorated United States Army veteran who served
honorably during the Vietnam War, Operation Desert Storm, and Operation Iraqi Freedom;
rather than referring to himself as a hero, he shifted focus to the many soldiers who gave
their lives in service to the country; and
WHEREAS, Cowboy was an active member of the Whatley-Flaherty Veterans of
Foreign Wars Post 5002; he co-founded the LaSalle Parish Veteran's Honor Guard with the
assistance of fellow veterans J.W. Thomas, Ralph McCrory, and Willie "Sap" Tradewell; the
organization, which is still in operation, provides military funeral services free of charge for
all veterans, and it has conducted over one thousand military funerals; and

WHEREAS, Cowboy was a fixture at all military events in LaSalle Parish and played
an instrumental role in starting Memorial Day services; he was also actively involved in
Veterans Day and other patriotic events; and
WHEREAS, Cowboy was a deeply loving and devoted husband, father, and
grandfather who cherished his wife and children and took great pride in his family; he was
known for his warm smile and kind spirit, and all who knew him considered him to be a
beloved presence in their lives; and
WHEREAS, James David "Cowboy" Robertson leaves to cherish his memory his
loving wife of fifty-six years, Carol Beagan Robertson; sons, Jamie "Cowboy, Jr."
Robertson (Amie), Johnathan Robertson, and Jason Robertson (Rachel); sixteen
grandchildren; five great-grandchildren; and a host of extended family members and dear
friends; he will be deeply missed, but the legacy of his deep patriotism and service to his
country shall live on in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged to have known
and loved him.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of James
David "Cowboy" Robertson; does hereby offer enduring appreciation for his unwavering
dedication and military service to the United States; and does hereby extend sincere wishes
that his family, friends, and loved ones find comfort in their shared memories of his beautiful
life.
