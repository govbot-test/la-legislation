2 §420. Declaration of purpose
3 A. The legislature hereby recognizes that it is a special privilege afforded to
4 athlete agents and other persons acting in a representative, advisory, or
5 managerial capacity to contact student athletes to secure their athletic abilities for
6 professional sports teams, professional sports organizations, or for endorsement
7 or commercial opportunities. The legislature also feels that with that privilege
8 should come certain duties, obligations, and responsibilities. The legislature hereby
9 enacts the laws of this Chapter to regulate those contacts and relationships and to
10 protect young, inexperienced student athletes in this state from unscrupulous athlete
11 agents and other persons who would seek to exploit such athletes, or their
12 educational institutions or schools, when entering or seeking to enter into an agent
13 contract, endorsement contract, or professional sport services contract or when
14 obtaining or seeking to obtain employment with a professional sports team, or
15 commercial opportunities.
16 B. It is the further purpose of the legislature in enacting this Chapter to
17 protect student athletes from contacts with and accepting things of value from athlete
18 agents and other persons in a manner so as to cause such athletes to unintentionally
19 lose their eligibility to compete in interscholastic or intercollegiate athletics,
20 otherwise suffer financial or reputational harm.
21 C. It is the further purpose of the legislature to protect the educational
22 institutions and schools of this state from unscrupulous athlete agents, athletes, and
23 other persons who would cause such institutions educational institution or school
24 to suffer various sanctions, penalties, or other adverse consequences as a result of
25 unlawful activities in connection with athletes participating in sports at such
26 educational institutions and schools.
27 D. Another purpose of the legislature in enacting this Chapter is to recognize
28 that the relationship between a student athlete and their institution or school is one
29 of contract.

DIGEST
SB 389 Original 2026 Regular Session Connick
Present law provides for the licensing and regulation of agents representing professional and
prospective professional athletes.
Proposed law retains present law with respect to licensing and regulation of agents
representing professional and prospective professional athletes and adds those agents
representing student athletes in college and high school.
Present law defines an "athlete agent" as any person who recruits or solicits an athlete to
enter into an agent contract or professional sport services contract with any person, or who
offers anything of value to any person to induce an athlete to enter into an agreement by
which any person will represent the athlete, or who for anything of value procures, offers,
promises, or attempts to obtain employment for an athlete with a professional sports team.
Proposed law defines an "athlete agent" as an individual who does any of the following:
(1) Enters into an agent contract with an athlete or, directly or indirectly, recruits or
solicits an athlete to enter into an agent contract for compensation or in anticipation
of compensation.
(2) Represents or advises an athlete in connection with an endorsement contract, or
endorsement activities, including name, image, and likeness matters for
compensation or in anticipation of compensation.
Proposed law defines an "endorsement contract"as an agreement under which an athlete is
employed or receives anything of value to permit the use, promotion, or association of the
athlete's name, image, and likeness, publicity, reputation, following, fame, or other personal
or commercial attributes, whether or not related to the athlete's athletic ability or
performance.
Present law requires an athlete agent to register with the Dept. of Justice public protection
division.

Proposed law authorizes the divisions to charge the following fees to produce sufficient
revenue to offset the expenses incurred by the division in administering proposed law, as

(1) $1,000 for an initial application for registration.
(2) $500 for registration based on a certificate of registration issued by another state.

another state.
Proposed law requires the attorney general to prescribe training for all applicants for

Proposed law requires each applicant to consent to and submit to a background check
conducted by or on behalf of the division.

with any requirement of proposed law, or the rules the division adopts.
