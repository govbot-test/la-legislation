Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ACT No. 504 ENROLLED
BY SENATOR CLOUD
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact Children's Code Art. 906(B)(1) and (3) and to enact Children's Code
3 Art. 906(B)(5), relative to the office of juvenile justice; to require review hearings
4 via secure video conference; to reclassify detention centers; to provide for
5 confidential virtual conferencing with the child's counsel of record; and to provide
6 for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. Children's Code Art. 906(B)(1) and (3) are hereby amended and reenacted
9 and Children's Code Art. 906(B)(5) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 Art. 906. Required review hearings; commitment; children in custody of the office
11 of juvenile justice
12 * * *
13 B.(1) Any child committed by a court to the custody of the office of juvenile
14 justice must shall be physically transported to the committing court for an in-person
15 review hearing not more than six months after the child's commitment, and every six
16 months thereafter, unless such an in-person hearing is waived by counsel for the
17 child and by the committing court. The date of the initial review hearing shall be set
18 by the court at the time of disposition. The court may order review hearings set
19 more frequently than the in-person hearings every six months, but for such
20 additional hearings the child shall participate via a video conferencing platform.
21 Staff of the office of juvenile justice and contract staff shall participate in each
22 review hearing in the same manner as the child who is required to participate
23 in accordance with this Subparagraph.
24 * * *
25 (3) For the purposes of this Paragraph, a child is deemed "committed by a
26 court to the custody of the office of juvenile justice" if he is judicially committed to
27 the legal custody of the office of juvenile justice, regardless of where the child is

1 physically held, including but not limited to state-run secure facilities, state-run
2 nonsecure facilities, private facilities with which the office of juvenile justice
3 contracts, and juvenile detention centers.
4 * * *
5 (5) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any child
6 committed by a court to the custody of the office of juvenile justice shall have
7 the right to participate in a private and confidential virtual consultation with
8 his counsel of record prior to any review hearing or other proceeding. The
9 consultation shall be conducted through a secure video conferencing platform
10 that ensures end-to-end confidentiality.
