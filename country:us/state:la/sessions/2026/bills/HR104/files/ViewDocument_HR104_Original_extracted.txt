HLS 26RS-2617 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 104
BY REPRESENTATIVE BRASS
at the state capitol
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate Monday, April 13, 2026, as My Sister's Keeper Day at the state capitol and to
3 commend the Black Women's Health Imperative and the Southern University and
4 A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper for its contributions to uplift, educate,
5 and empower Black women and girls throughout Louisiana and beyond.
6 WHEREAS, the Black Women's Health Imperative, founded by Byllye Y. Avery in
7 1983 at Spelman College in Atlanta, is the first nonprofit organization created by Black
8 women to protect and advance the health and wellness of Black women and girls; and
9 WHEREAS, for more than four decades, the Black Women's Health Imperative has
10 worked to ensure that Black women have access to equitable healthcare, education, and
11 resources necessary to live healthier and more empowered lives; and
12 WHEREAS, My Sister's Keeper, a signature program of the Black Women's Health
13 Imperative, empowers young Black women ages eighteen through thirty to become
14 advocates, leaders, and change-makers within their communities, engaging participants
15 through leadership development, storytelling, policy and advocacy training, and wellness
16 strategies; and
17 WHEREAS, the My Sister's Keeper Chapter at Southern University and A&M
18 College is a student-led organization dedicated to empowering, educating, and supporting
19 women to move from average to excellence while promoting health awareness, advocacy,
20 and leadership within the campus community; and
Page 1 of 2
HLS 26RS-2617 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 104
1 WHEREAS, through educational programming, community outreach, and advocacy
2 initiatives, the chapter raises awareness about health concerns impacting Black women and
3 girls, promotes a culture of wellness and empowerment, and encourages dialogue and action
4 on issues including sexual and reproductive health, mental and emotional wellness,
5 menstrual equity, gender discrimination and violence, anti-racism and allyship, and tobacco
6 and vaping prevention; and
7 WHEREAS, through their leadership, service, and advocacy, the members of the
8 Southern University and A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper advance the mission
9 of the Black Women's Health Imperative to promote health justice and equity for Black
10 women and girls and strengthen their campus community; it is most fitting to recognize and
11 commend their outstanding efforts.
12 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
13 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Monday, April 13, 2026, as My Sister's
14 Keeper Day at the state capitol and does hereby commend the Black Women's Health
15 Imperative and the Southern University and A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper
16 for its exemplary commitment to advancing the health, wellness, and empowerment of Black
17 women and girls in the state of Louisiana and across our nation.
