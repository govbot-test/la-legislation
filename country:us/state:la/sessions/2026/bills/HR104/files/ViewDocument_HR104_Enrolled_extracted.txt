ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 104
BY REPRESENTATIVE BRASS
A RESOLUTION
commend the Black Women's Health Imperative and the Southern University and
A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper for its contributions to uplift, educate,
and empower Black women and girls throughout Louisiana and beyond.
WHEREAS, the Black Women's Health Imperative, founded by Byllye Y. Avery in
1983 at Spelman College in Atlanta, is the first nonprofit organization created by Black
women to protect and advance the health and wellness of Black women and girls; and
WHEREAS, for more than four decades, the Black Women's Health Imperative has
worked to ensure that Black women have access to equitable healthcare, education, and
resources necessary to live healthier and more empowered lives; and
WHEREAS, My Sister's Keeper, a signature program of the Black Women's Health
Imperative, empowers young Black women ages eighteen through thirty to become
advocates, leaders, and change-makers within their communities, engaging participants
through leadership development, storytelling, policy and advocacy training, and wellness
strategies; and
WHEREAS, the My Sister's Keeper Chapter at Southern University and A&M
College is a student-led organization dedicated to empowering, educating, and supporting
women to move from average to excellence while promoting health awareness, advocacy,
and leadership within the campus community; and
WHEREAS, through educational programming, community outreach, and advocacy
initiatives, the chapter raises awareness about health concerns impacting Black women and
girls, promotes a culture of wellness and empowerment, and encourages dialogue and action

on issues including sexual and reproductive health, mental and emotional wellness,
menstrual equity, gender discrimination and violence, anti-racism and allyship, and tobacco
and vaping prevention; and
WHEREAS, through their leadership, service, and advocacy, the members of the
Southern University and A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper advance the mission
of the Black Women's Health Imperative to promote health justice and equity for Black
women and girls and strengthen their campus community; it is most fitting to recognize and
commend their outstanding efforts.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Monday, April 13, 2026, as My Sister's
Keeper Day at the state capitol and does hereby commend the Black Women's Health
Imperative and the Southern University and A&M College Chapter of My Sister's Keeper
for its exemplary commitment to advancing the health, wellness, and empowerment of Black
women and girls in the state of Louisiana and across our nation.
