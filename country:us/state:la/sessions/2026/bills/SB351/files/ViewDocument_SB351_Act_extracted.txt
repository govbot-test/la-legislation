2026 Regular Session ACT No. 383 ENROLLED
BY SENATOR JACKSON-ANDREWS AND REPRESENTATIVES BOYD,
BROUSSARD, CARRIER, FREEMAN, FREIBERG, DANA HENRY,
LAFLEUR, TERRY LANDRY, MARCELLE, MARTINEZ, MILLER,
MOORE, MURRAY, TAYLOR, WALTERS AND YOUNG
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:407.30(D)(1) and 407.51(H)(1) and to enact R.S. 17:407.29.1,
3 relative to the Child Care Assistance Program; to provide for a provisional eligibility
4 process; to provide for approval by the Senate and House committees on education;
5 to authorize the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education to promulgate
6 rules and regulations; to provide relative to the State Board of Elementary and
7 Secondary Education; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 17:407.30(D)(1) and 407.51(H)(1) are hereby amended and reenacted
10 and R.S. 17:407.29.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §407.29.1. Provisional eligibility; child care assistance program application
12 A. An expectant mother may apply for assistance from the Child Care
13 Assistance Program, through the Department of Education, prior to birth of the
14 child in order to preliminarily determine whether the applicant will be eligible.
15 B. The department shall develop a process to determine provisional
16 eligibility of an applicant and to later confirm eligibility after the birth of the
17 child. The process shall be subject to approval by the Senate and House
18 committees on education, meeting separately or jointly.

1 C. The State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education shall
2 promulgate rules and regulations in accordance with the Administrative
3 Procedure Act as necessary for the implementation of this Section.
4 §407.30. Louisiana Early Childhood Education Fund
5 * * *
6 D.(1)(a) The fund shall be administered by the State Board of Elementary and
7 Secondary Education. Subject to legislative appropriation, monies in the fund shall
8 be awarded annually to local entities approved by the board State Board of
9 Elementary and Secondary Education for the purpose of funding projects that will
10 expand the number of early childhood care and education quality slots for families
11 who are eligible for the Child Care Assistance Program in Type III early learning
12 centers that have at least one classroom with children age fifteen months or younger
13 if a local entity provides funds from nonstate and nonfederal sources. No such award
14 shall be made unless the local entity's financial commitment to the project proposed
15 for funding provides matching funds from nonstate and nonfederal sources at a rate
16 of at least one-to-one.
17 (b) Each local entity shall establish eligibility criteria for the early childhood
18 care and education quality slots financed by local funds that serve as the match for
19 the award. At a minimum, only children whose family income makes them
20 economically disadvantaged as defined by the board State Board of Elementary
21 and Secondary Education shall be eligible for the slots.
22 * * *
23 §407.51. Advisory Council
24 * * *
25 H.(1) The council shall provide input and guidance to the board State Board
26 of Elementary and Secondary Education and the department on matters pertaining
27 to the development and implementation of rules, regulations, bulletins, policies, or
28 standards related to all early care and education programs, including early learning
29 centers, enrollment in early learning centers, the Cecil J. Picard LA 4 Early
30 Childhood Program, the Child Care and Development Fund Block Grant, the Child
