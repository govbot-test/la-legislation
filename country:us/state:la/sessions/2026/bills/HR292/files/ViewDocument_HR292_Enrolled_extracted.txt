HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 292
BY REPRESENTATIVES KERNER AND CHASSION
A RESOLUTION
To memorialize the United States Department of Commerce and the National Oceanic and
Atmospheric Administration to take such actions as are necessary to properly inspect
and ensure the safety of imported seafood.
WHEREAS, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) discovered in August of 2025
that imported shrimp being sold in United States grocery stores was radioactive; and
WHEREAS, several days later, at different grocery stores, the FDA found again that
tens of thousands of packages of radioactive frozen shrimp products were being sold to
United States consumers; and
WHEREAS, the FDA confirmed in August that imported seafood was contaminated
with Caesium-137, which is a radioactive isotope; and
WHEREAS, inspections of imported seafood are conducted by the National Oceanic
and Atmospheric Administration which is within the United States Department of
Commerce; and
WHEREAS, only one or two percent of imported seafood products are actually
inspected on a daily basis while countries such as the United Kingdom and China inspect
around half of their imported seafood products; and
WHEREAS, the domestic shrimp industry has long been critical of foreign shrimp
imports pointing out the amount of shrimp stopped by United States Customs and Border
Control for containing antibiotics and other contaminants; and
WHEREAS, in 2024 alone, eighty-one shipments of foreign shrimp were refused by
the FDA over the presence of banned narcotics; and
WHEREAS, the United States, and Louisiana particularly, has robust fishing and
shrimping operations that catch and distribute clean, safe domestic seafood that is not just
safer for American consumers, but also provides jobs for hard-working Americans and
boosts the economies of the country and of the state; and

WHEREAS, United States Senator John N. Kennedy and Lieutenant Governor Billy
Nungesser have both urged the federal government to implement additional measures to
deter the use of imported shrimp and to better ensure the safety of the seafood imports we
do allow to be distributed to consumers in the United States; and
WHEREAS, President Donald J. Trump signed an executive order in 2025 entitled
"Restoring American Seafood Competitiveness" and the first step to restoring the
competitiveness of domestic seafood is to stop importing unsafe and potentially tainted
seafood from overseas.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby memorialize the United States Department of
Commerce and the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration to take such actions
as are necessary to properly inspect and ensure the safety of imported seafood.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the United States Department of Commerce, to the undersecretary of Commerce
for Oceans and Atmosphere, and to each member of the Louisiana congressional delegation.
