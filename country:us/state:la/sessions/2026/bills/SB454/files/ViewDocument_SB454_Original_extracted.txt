attorney positions in the state. (7/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 16:51(A) and 54(A), relative to assistant district attorneys; to
3 provide for the number of assistant district attorneys per judicial district; to provide
4 for a population based allocation of assistant district attorney positions; to provide
5 relative to the approval of additional assistant district attorney positions; to provide
6 exceptions; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:

1 membership; appointments; expenses; rules; meetings
2 A. There is hereby created within the office of the governor the Governor's
3 Advisory and Review Commission on Assistant District Attorneys, hereafter referred
4 to as the commission, to advise the governor and the legislature with respect to state
5 funded additional assistant district attorney positions in the respective judicial
6 districts and the parish of Orleans. Notwithstanding the provisions of R.S. 16:53, the
7 determination of the commission as to the necessity for each additional district
8 attorney position in each judicial district shall be final, and no position of additional
9 assistant district attorney to be paid by the state shall be enacted without the approval
10 of the commission; however, such approval shall not be required for any
11 reallocation of authorized assistant district attorney positions among judicial
12 districts in R.S. 16:51, provided that the total number of such positions
13 authorized statewide is not increased.
14 * * *
15 Section 2. The provisions of this Act shall become effective on July 1, 2026.
The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part
of the legislative instrument, were prepared by Senate Legislative Services.
The keyword, summary, and digest do not constitute part of the law or proof
or indicia of legislative intent. [R.S. 1:13(B) and 24:177(E)]
DIGEST
SB 454 Original 2026 Regular Session Morris
Present law provides for the number of assistant district attorneys for the various judicial
districts.
Proposed law changes the number of assistant district attorneys for the various judicial
districts to be based on the population of a judicial district according to the latest federal
decennial census as follows:
(1) Judicial districts with a population of 10,000 or less persons, one assistant district
attorney.
(2) Judicial districts with a population of 10,000 or more but not more than 100,000
persons, one assistant district attorney per each 7,000 persons.
(3) Judicial districts with a population greater than 100,000 persons, one assistant district
attorney per each 7,500 persons.
Present law provides for the review of additional assistant district attorneys by the
Governor's Advisory and Review Commission on Assistant District Attorneys. Further
prohibits the legislature from enacting an additional position of assistant district attorney to
