1 C.(1) The district is established for the primary object and purpose of
2 promoting and encouraging economic and commercial development and tourism
3 opportunities, stimulating the economy through renewed commerce, industry,
4 research, and tourism, and for the utilization and development of natural and human
5 resources of the area by providing job opportunities.
6 (2) Nothing shall prohibit the district from providing public infrastructure
7 outside the boundaries of the district that generally benefits the public.
8 D.(1) The district shall be governed by a board of commissioners, referred
9 to in this Section as the "board", consisting of five members as follows:
10 (a) The member of the governing authority of the parish of Jefferson who
11 represents District No. 5 shall appoint two members.
12 (b) The member of the governing authority of the city of New Orleans who
13 represents District A shall appoint two members.
14 (c) The president of the governing authority of the city of New Orleans shall
15 appoint one member.
16 (2) Members of the board shall serve five-year terms after initial terms as
17 provided in this Paragraph. One member shall serve an initial term of one year; one
18 shall serve two years; one shall serve three years; one shall serve four years; and one
19 shall serve five years, as determined by lot at the first meeting of the board.
20 (3) Any vacancy which occurs prior to the expiration of the term for which
21 a member of the board has been appointed shall be filled for the remainder of the
22 unexpired term in the same manner as the original appointment.
23 (4) The board shall elect from its members a president, a vice president, a
24 secretary, a treasurer, and such other officers as it may deem necessary. The duties
25 of the officers shall be fixed by the bylaws adopted by the board.
26 (5) The minute books and archives of the district shall be maintained by the
27 secretary of the board. The monies, funds, and accounts of the district shall be in the
28 official custody of the board.
29 (6) The board shall adopt such rules and regulations as it deems necessary
30 or advisable for conducting its business affairs. It shall hold regular meetings as shall

1 be provided for in the bylaws and may hold special meetings at such times and
2 places within the district as may be prescribed in the bylaws.
3 (7) A majority of the members of the board shall constitute a quorum for the
4 transaction of business. The board shall keep minutes of all meetings and shall make
5 them available through the secretary of the board to residents of the district.
6 (8) The members of the board shall serve without compensation; however,
7 they may be reimbursed for expenses incurred while attending to the business of the
8 board or the district.
9 E. The district, acting by and through its board, shall have and exercise all
10 powers of a political subdivision necessary or convenient for carrying out its objects
11 and purposes, including but not limited to the following:
12 (1) To sue and to be sued.
13 (2) To adopt, use, and alter at will a corporate seal.
14 (3) To adopt bylaws and rules and regulations.
15 (4) To receive by gift, grant, or donation any sum of money, property, aid,
16 or assistance from the United States, the state of Louisiana, or any political
17 subdivision thereof, or any person, firm, or corporation.
18 (5) To enter into contracts, agreements, or cooperative endeavors with the
19 state and its political subdivisions or political corporations and with any public or
20 private association, corporation, business entity, or individual.
21 (6) To appoint officers, agents, and employees; prescribe their duties; and
22 fix their compensation.
23 (7) To acquire by purchase, gift, grant, donation, or lease property as may
24 be necessary or desirable for carrying out the objectives and purposes of the board.
25 (8) To perform any other necessary and ancillary acts to effectuate its
26 functions, to perform its duties, or to give effect to its powers in accordance with this
27 Section.
28 F.(1) In addition to the authority provided to the district by this Section, the
29 district may levy and collect a sales and use tax within the boundaries of the district
30 not to exceed two percent.
