2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE CREWS
BANKS/BANKING: Creates the Bayou Gold Program
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 6:342, relative to creating the Bayou Gold Program; to provide for definitions;
3 to create the Bayou Gold Program; to provide for duties of the Department of the
4 Treasury; to provide for certification requirements; to provide for application to the
5 department; to provide for fees; to provide for penalties; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 6:342 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §342. Bayou Gold Program; certification
10 A. For the purposes of this Section, the following definitions have the
11 following meanings:
12 (1) "Account holder" means a person with a single account or joint account
13 in a bullion depository.
14 (2) "Allocated precious metals" means gold or silver bullion held in custody
15 on a fully reserved, one-to-one basis, owned by the account holder, and not subject
16 to lending, rehypothecation, or encumbrance.
17 (3) "Bullion depository" means an entity providing vault facilities within the
18 United States for the storage of gold bullion and silver bullion that do all of the
19 following:

1 (a) Complies with the prescribed London Bullion Market Association or
2 equivalent best practice guidelines.
3 (b) Provides allocated accounts that do all of the following:
4 (i) Hold gold bullion and silver bullion.
5 (ii) Allow account holders to buy, sell, save, or spend gold bullion and silver
6 bullion.
7 (4) "Business" means a private entity that provides financial services based
8 on gold- and silver-backed transactional accounts rather than fiat currency accounts.
9 (5) "Certification" means formal recognition granted by the department that
10 a business meets the standards established pursuant to this Section.
11 (6) "Department" means the Louisiana Department of the Treasury.
12 (7) "Fiat currency" means government-issued currency that is declared legal
13 tender by a governmental authority and is not backed by a physical asset.
14 (8) "Program" means the Bayou Gold Program established pursuant to this
15 Section.
16 B.(1) The department shall establish and administer the Bayou Gold Program
17 in accordance with the provisions of this Section.
18 (2) The program shall provide a voluntary certification framework for
19 qualifying businesses that offer gold- and silver-backed transactional services for
20 account holders.
21 (3) The department shall maintain a publicly accessible registry of certified
22 businesses.
23 (4) The department may develop and authorize the use of a "Bayou Gold
24 Program" name, logo, or certification mark for use by certified businesses.
25 (5) The department shall not operate, manage, or own a gold or silver
26 depository or payment processor pursuant to this Section.
27 C. To be eligible for certification under the program, a business shall meet
28 all of the following criteria:
Page 2 of 6

HLS 26RS-1049 ORIGINAL
HB NO. 797
1 (1) Maintain allocated precious metals stored in a secure bullion depository
2 facility meeting industry best-practice standards for security, insurance, fraud
3 prevention, and auditing.
4 (2) Utilize a bullion depository located within the state of Louisiana or a
5 bordering state.
6 (3) Ensure that a customer's precious metals:
7 (a) Remain the sole legal property of the customer.
8 (b) Do not appear as assets on the business's balance sheet.
9 (c) Are not subject to claims of creditors of the business.
10 (d) Are not lent, pledged, or rehypothecated.
11 (4) Provide proof that all customer holdings are backed on a fully reserved,
12 one-to-one basis.
13 (5) Maintain policies and procedures consistent with allocated ownership.
14 (6) Ensure precious metals held under the program fulfill all of the following
15 requirements:
16 (a) Maintain a documented chain of integrity.
17 (b) Are refined by a London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) or
18 COMEX-approved refinery.
19 (c) Meet recognized-industry grade standards.
20 (d) Are in globally accepted bullion forms.
21 (7) Provide the ability for customers to redeem their gold or silver in
22 physical form within five business days of notice, subject to reasonable logistics and
23 security procedures.
24 (8) Provide real-time or near real-time transactional capability allowing
25 customers to conduct payments based on allocated precious metals.
26 (9) Clearly disclose all fees, spreads, and compensation structures to
27 customers.

1 D.(1) A business seeking certification pursuant to this Section shall submit
2 an application to the department, which shall include but not be limited to the
3 following:
4 (a) Proof of fully allocated reserves.
5 (b) Most recent independent audit reports.
6 (c) Evidence of insurance coverage.
7 (d) Description of custody arrangements.
8 (e) Description of payment system capabilities.
9 (2) The department may request additional documentation reasonably
10 necessary to evaluate compliance.
11 (3) The department shall either grant or deny certification within sixty days
12 of receiving a completed application.
13 E.(1) The department may establish reasonable application and renewal fees
14 sufficient to cover administrative costs of the program.
15 (2) The department may conduct periodic reviews to ensure continued
16 compliance with this Section.
17 (3) The department may revoke certification for any business upon a finding
18 of material noncompliance pursuant to this Section.
19 F. Certification is valid for two years from the date of issuance and a
20 certified business may reapply for renewal prior to expiration.
21 G. A business that falsely claims certification under the Bayou Gold
22 Program shall be subject to penalties as determined by the department, including
23 civil fines and public removal from the registry.
24 H. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to do any of the following:
25 (1) Create a state guarantee of deposits.
26 (2) Impose financial liability upon the state for actions of certified
27 businesses.
28 (3) Establish a state-operated precious metals financial system.
