8 Section 1. R.S. 6:342 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §342. Bayou Gold Program; certification
10 A. For the purposes of this Section, the following definitions have the
11 following meanings:
12 (1) "Account holder" means a person with a single account or joint account
13 in an electronic payment platform.
14 (2) "Allocated precious metals" means gold or silver bullion held in custody
15 on a fully reserved, one-to-one basis, owned by the account holder, and not subject
16 to lending, rehypothecation, or encumbrance.
17 (3) "Bullion depository" means an entity providing vault facilities within the
18 United States for the storage of gold bullion and silver bullion that do all of the
19 following:

1 (a) Complies with the prescribed London Bullion Market Association or
2 equivalent best practice guidelines.
3 (b) Holds gold bullion and silver bullion.
4 (4) "Certification" means formal recognition granted by the department that
5 an electronic payment platform meets the standards established pursuant to this
6 Section.
7 (5) "Department" means the Department of the Treasury.
8 (6) "Electronic payment platform" means a private entity that provides
9 financial services based on gold- and silver-backed electronic transfer transactional
10 accounts rather than fiat currency accounts.
11 (7) "Fiat currency" means government-issued currency that is declared legal

13 (8) "Program" means the Bayou Gold Program established pursuant to this
14 Section.
15 B.(1) The department shall establish and administer the Bayou Gold Program
16 in accordance with the provisions of this Section.
17 (2) The program shall provide a voluntary certification framework for
18 qualifying electronic payment platforms that offer gold- and silver-backed
19 transactional services for account holders.
20 (3) The department shall maintain a publicly accessible registry of certified
21 electronic payment platforms.
22 (4) The department may develop and authorize the use of a "Bayou Gold
23 Program" name, logo, or certification mark for use by certified electronic payment
24 platforms.
25 (5) The department shall not operate, manage, or own a gold or silver
26 depository or payment processor pursuant to this Section.
27 C. To be eligible for certification under the program, an electronic payment
28 platform shall meet all of the following criteria:

1 (1) Maintain allocated precious metals stored in a secure bullion depository
2 facility meeting industry best-practice standards for security, insurance, fraud

4 (2) Utilize a bullion depository located within the state of Louisiana or a

21 (d) Are in globally accepted bullion forms.
22 (7) Provide the ability for customers to redeem their gold or silver in

25 (8) Provide real-time or near real-time transactional capability allowing

1 D.(1) An electronic payment platform seeking certification pursuant to this
2 Section shall submit an application to the department, including but not limited to
3 the following:
4 (a) Proof that the account holder's gold and silver bullion are fully allocated.
5 (b) Most recent independent audit reports.
6 (c) Evidence of insurance coverage.
7 (d) A description of custody arrangements.
8 (e) A description of payment system capabilities.
9 (2) The department may request additional documentation reasonably
10 necessary to evaluate compliance.
11 (3) The department shall either grant or deny certification within sixty days
12 of receiving a completed application.
13 E.(1) The department may establish reasonable application and renewal fees
14 sufficient to cover administrative costs of the program.
15 (2) The department may conduct periodic reviews to ensure continued
16 compliance with this Section.
17 (3) The department may revoke certification for any electronic payment
18 platform upon a finding of material noncompliance pursuant to this Section.
19 F. Certification is valid for two years from the date of issuance and a
20 certified electronic payment platform may reapply for renewal prior to expiration.
21 G. An electronic payment platform that falsely claims certification under the
22 Bayou Gold Program shall be subject to penalties as determined by the department,
23 including civil fines and public removal from the registry.
24 H. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to do any of the following:
25 (1) Create a state guarantee of deposits.
26 (2) Impose financial liability upon the state for actions of certified electronic
27 payment platforms.
28 (3) Establish a state-operated precious metals financial system.

HB 797 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Crews
Abstract: Creates the Bayou Gold Program.
Proposed law defines "account holder", "allocated precious metals", "bullion depository",
"certification", "department", "electronic payment platform", "fiat currency", and "program".
Proposed law provides that the Dept. of the Treasury (department) shall establish and
administer the Bayou Gold Program.
Proposed law provides that the program shall provide a voluntary certification framework
for qualifying electronic payment platforms that offer gold- and silver-backed transactional
services for account holders.
Proposed law provides that the department shall maintain a publicly accessible registry of
certified electronic payment platforms and that the department may develop and authorize
the use of a "Bayou Gold Program" name, logo, or certification mark for use by certified
electronic payment platforms.
Proposed law provides that the department shall not operate, manage, or own a gold or silver
depository or payment processor pursuant to proposed law.
Proposed law provides for the criteria an electronic payment platform shall meet to be
eligible for certification under the program.
Proposed law that an electronic payment platform seeking certification pursuant to proposed
law shall submit an application to the department, which shall include certain information.
Proposed law further provides that the department may request additional documentation
reasonably necessary to evaluate compliance.
Proposed law provides that the department shall either grant or deny certification within 60
days of receiving a completed application.
Proposed law provides that the department may establish reasonable application and renewal
fees sufficient to cover administrative costs of the program, may conduct periodic reviews
to ensure continued compliance with proposed law, and may revoke certification for any
electronic payment platform upon a finding of material noncompliance pursuant to proposed
law.
Proposed law provides that certification is valid for two years from the date of issuance, and
a certified electronic payment platform may reapply for renewal prior to expiration.
Proposed law provides that an electronic payment platform that falsely claims certification
under the Bayou Gold Program shall be subject to penalties as determined by the
department, including civil fines and public removal from the registry.
Proposed law provides that nothing in proposed law shall be construed to do any of the
following:
(1) Create a state guarantee of deposits.
