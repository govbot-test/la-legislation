15 As used in this Part, the following terms have the following meanings:

16 §2351.1. Rule of construction
17 A. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to permit the recovery of an
18 unpaired vital organ in a manner that causes or hastens the death of the donor,
19 or prior to death being determined in accordance with R.S. 9:111.
20 B. Nothing in this Part shall be construed to prohibit living organ or
21 tissue donation that does not result in the death of the donor and is otherwise
22 authorized by law.
23 C. Unless expressly provided by law, authorization to make an
24 anatomical gift after death shall not be construed as consent to any act
25 undertaken prior to death.

17 §2354.11. Premortem interventions; definitions; prohibitions
18 A. For purposes of this Section, "premortem intervention" means any
19 medical procedure, test, medication, cannulation, or other intervention
20 undertaken prior to the determination of death for the purpose of facilitating
21 organ or tissue recovery.
22 B. No premortem intervention shall be performed unless it is medically
23 indicated for the direct benefit of the patient or is performed pursuant to a
24 legally effective consent applicable to premortem interventions, as provided in
25 this Section.
26 C. Regardless of consent, no person shall perform or direct any
27 premortem intervention that has a material likelihood of any of the following:
28 (1) Hastening death.
29 (2) Manufacturing, accelerating, or securing irreversibility of
30 circulatory, respiratory, or neurologic cessation.

1 (3) Preventing or materially interfering with the restoration or

3 D. Any physician or individual who performs or directs a premortem
4 intervention shall be independent of, and shall not participate in, the recovery
5 or transplantation of any organ or tissue from the individual, and shall not be

7 E. Authorization to make an anatomical gift after death does not

1 §2354.12. Post-determination handling and transport of the decedent
2 A. This Section applies following the determination and pronouncement
3 of death under R.S. 9:111 and the recovery of any organ or tissue pursuant to
4 an anatomical gift.
5 B. Prior to the recovery of organs or tissue, the individual authorized to
6 make an anatomical gift, or the individual's authorized representative where
7 applicable, shall be informed in writing of any plan to do any of the following:
8 (1) Transport the decedent from the hospital or facility where death was
9 determined to another facility for further organ or tissue recovery, processing,
10 or handling.
11 (2) Continue mechanical ventilation, perfusion, or other artificial
12 support of bodily functions following the determination of death for purposes
13 related to organ or tissue recovery.
14 C. No transport or continuation of mechanical support described in
15 Subsection B of this Section shall occur unless expressly authorized as part of
16 a legally effective authorization applicable to post-determination handling and
17 transport.
18 D. Authorization to make an anatomical gift after death shall not, by
19 itself, constitute authorization for transport of the decedent to a nonhospital
20 facility or for continuation of mechanical support following death
21 determination.
22 E. Any handling or transport of the decedent following death
23 determination and organ or tissue recovery shall be conducted in a manner
24 consistent with the dignity of the decedent and with the reasonable expectations
25 of the family, as informed by the disclosures made under this Section.
26 F. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to limit the recovery of

17 §2354.11. Premortem interventions; definitions; prohibitions
18 A. For purposes of this Section, "premortem intervention" means any
19 medical procedure, test, medication, cannulation, or other intervention
20 undertaken prior to the determination of death for the purpose of facilitating
21 organ or tissue recovery.
22 B. No premortem intervention shall be performed unless it is medically
23 indicated for the direct benefit of the patient or is performed pursuant to a
24 legally effective consent applicable to premortem interventions, as provided in
25 this Section.
26 C. Regardless of consent, no person shall perform or direct any
27 premortem intervention that has a material likelihood of any of the following:
28 (1) Hastening death.
29 (2) Manufacturing, accelerating, or securing irreversibility of
30 circulatory, respiratory, or neurologic cessation.

1 §2354.12. Post-determination handling and transport of the decedent
2 A. This Section applies following the determination and pronouncement
3 of death under R.S. 9:111 and the recovery of any organ or tissue pursuant to
4 an anatomical gift.
5 B. Prior to the recovery of organs or tissue, the individual authorized to
6 make an anatomical gift, or the individual's authorized representative where
7 applicable, shall be informed in writing of any plan to do any of the following:
8 (1) Transport the decedent from the hospital or facility where death was
9 determined to another facility for further organ or tissue recovery, processing,
10 or handling.
11 (2) Continue mechanical ventilation, perfusion, or other artificial
12 support of bodily functions following the determination of death for purposes
13 related to organ or tissue recovery.
14 C. No transport or continuation of mechanical support described in
15 Subsection B of this Section shall occur unless expressly authorized as part of
16 a legally effective authorization applicable to post-determination handling and
17 transport.
18 D. Authorization to make an anatomical gift after death shall not, by
19 itself, constitute authorization for transport of the decedent to a nonhospital
20 facility or for continuation of mechanical support following death
21 determination.
22 E. Any handling or transport of the decedent following death
23 determination and organ or tissue recovery shall be conducted in a manner
24 consistent with the dignity of the decedent and with the reasonable expectations
25 of the family, as informed by the disclosures made under this Section.
26 F. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to limit the recovery of
27 organs or tissue otherwise authorized by law, to alter the standards for
28 determination of death under R.S. 9:111, or, except to the extent necessary to
29 carry out a valid anatomical gift, to alter or transfer the custodial authority of
30 the person or persons authorized by law to control disposition of the body.

1 §2354.13. Effect of anatomical gift decisions
2 A. An authorization recorded in the registry constitutes a legally
3 effective expression of intent and supersedes any prior refusal.
4 B. A refusal recorded in the registry constitutes a legally effective
5 expression of intent and supersedes any prior authorization.
6 C. The most recent authorization or refusal recorded in the registry shall
7 control.
8 D. The absence of a recorded authorization or refusal indicates that no
9 anatomical gift decision has been recorded in the registry and shall not
10 constitute authorization or refusal.
11 E. A response to a query by the office of motor vehicles or other state
12 agency indicating that no anatomical gift decision should be recorded shall not
13 constitute an authorization or refusal and shall have no legal effect.
14 F. An informational indication made by a minor shall not constitute a
15 legally effective authorization or refusal.
16 §2354.14. Out-of-state anatomical gift decisions; effect
17 A. An anatomical gift decision, including an authorization or refusal,
18 made in another state that is valid under the laws of that state or of this state
19 shall be recognized as a legally effective anatomical gift decision in this state.
20 B. The effect of an anatomical gift decision recognized under this Section
21 shall be determined in accordance with the laws of this state.
22 C. If multiple anatomical gift decisions exist, including decisions made
23 in another state, the most recent legally effective authorization or refusal shall
24 control.
25 D. If the laws of another state conflict with the provisions of this Part, the
26 provisions of this Part shall govern the determination of the effect of the
27 anatomical gift decision.
28 E. Notwithstanding any provision of another state's law, an anatomical
29 gift decision made by a minor shall not constitute a legally effective
30 authorization or refusal in this state.
