HB 575 Original 2026 Regular Session Carver
Abstract: Establishes a priority preference for persons in extended foster care pursuant to
present law in the purchase of surplus state vehicles.
Present law grants the commissioner of administration control over surplus state property.
Establishes a priority order of preference for acquisition of state surplus property that is
authorized for disposition. Generally, that order is:
(1) Other state agencies.
(2) Political subdivisions, private elementary, secondary, or proprietary schools; housing
authorities; and independent colleges and universities.
(3) Charitable, educational, or religious organizations.
(4) Private persons, firms, or corporations.
For surplus computer equipment, present law establishes a first priority preference for public
elementary and secondary schools and community and technical colleges under the
management and control of the Board of Supervisors of La. Community and Technical
Colleges.
For surplus state vehicles, proposed law establishes a first priority preference for persons
participating in the extended foster care program established pursuant to present law (R.S.
46:288.1 et seq).
Effective July 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 39:330(B)(4))
