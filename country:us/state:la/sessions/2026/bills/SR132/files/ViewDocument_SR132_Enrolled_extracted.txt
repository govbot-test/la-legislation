Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 132
BY SENATOR ABRAHAM
A RESOLUTION
requiring employers to provide occupational accident insurance to independent
contractors and whether single member business entities should be required to
acquire workers' compensation insurance coverage.
WHEREAS, Louisiana workers' compensation system was established to provide a
structured and predictable mechanism for compensating employees who suffer injuries
arising out of and in the course of employment; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana workers' compensation law excludes independent contractors
and single member business entities from coverage with certain exceptions; and
WHEREAS, today's workforce includes an increasing number of sole proprietors,
independent contractors, owner-operators, gig workers, and single-member business entities
operating in industries such as transportation, construction, logistics, delivery services, and
home services; and
WHEREAS, occupational accident insurance products are currently offered in
various states and industries as an alternative or supplemental form of injury protection for
certain independent contractors and nontraditional work arrangements; and
WHEREAS, questions exist regarding the extent to which occupational accident
insurance products may lawfully be offered, structured, regulated, and utilized under existing
Louisiana insurance and labor laws; and
WHEREAS, current law exempts a single member business entity from the
requirement of workers' compensation insurance for itself if it has no employees; and
WHEREAS, concerns have also been raised regarding whether certain classifications
of workers as independent contractors or sole proprietorships may result in gaps in coverage,
increased uncompensated medical costs, and inconsistent treatment among similarly situated
workers; and
Page 1 of 2
SR NO. 132 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, there is a need to study whether existing workers' compensation laws
adequately address modern business structures and labor arrangements while maintaining
fairness for small businesses, independent workers, contractors, and consumers; and
WHEREAS, given that Louisiana has a compelling interest in ensuring that injured
workers have access to medical care and wage replacement benefits following workplace
injuries, the potential economic impact and other public policy challenges associated with
requiring workers' compensation or occupational accident coverage for single member
business entities, 1099 employees, or independent contractors should be studied.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study and recommend
legislation to require all employers, including single member business entities and 1099
employees to provide workers' compensation or occupational accident insurance coverage.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that this study should be informed by input from a
broad cross-section of stakeholders, including chambers of commerce, local and regional
economic development organizations, labor representatives, and business and industry
leaders.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall be in consultation with local
workforce development boards, the secretary of Louisiana Works, or her designee, workers'
compensation plaintiff and defense attorneys, and the commissioner of the Louisiana
Department of Insurance, or his designee.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute shall submit
a written report of its findings and recommendations, including potential statutory,
regulatory, or programmatic changes, to the House and Senate committees on labor and
industrial relations by January 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
director of the Louisiana State Law Institute, the secretary of Louisiana Works, and the
commissioner of the Louisiana Department of Insurance.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
