HLS 26RS-84 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE VILLIO
CRIMINAL/PROCEDURE: Provides relative to disclosure of juror information
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 44:4.1(B)(39) and to enact Code of Criminal Procedure Article
3 794.1, relative to juror information; to prohibit the disclosure of juror information;
4 to provide exceptions; to provide penalties; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Article 794.1 is hereby enacted to read as
7 follows:
8 Art. 794.1. Disclosure of Juror Information
9 A. All persons having access to or knowledge of the identity, name, address,
10 telephone number, email address, image, or likeness of any currently or formerly
11 serving grand or petit juror shall not publicly disclose such information, except as
12 provided in this Article.
13 B. Upon motion by any person and finding of good cause by the court,
14 information collected by the court or by a prosecuting attorney during the jury
15 selection process about a person who serves as a juror, including the juror's name,
16 home address, or other identifying information, may be disclosed by the court to the
17 person that made the motion. Nothing in this Article shall prohibit the court from
18 limiting the disclosure or providing additional measures as may be necessary to
19 accomplish release while protecting juror identity.

HB 55 Original 2026 Regular Session Villio
Abstract: Provides for the disclosure of identifying information of jurors.
Proposed law provides that no person shall intentionally disclose certain information that
would identify someone who served on a jury.
Proposed law creates an exception for when a court finds good cause following a motion
to disclose the identifying information.
Proposed law provides a penalty of up to $1,000 or six months imprisonment, or both.
Proposed law adds an exception to present law regarding public records.
(Amends R.S. 44:4.1(B)(39); Adds C.Cr.P. Art. 794.1)
