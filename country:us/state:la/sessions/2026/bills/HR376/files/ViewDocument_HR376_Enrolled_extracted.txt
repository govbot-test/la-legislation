HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 376
BY REPRESENTATIVE DOMANGUE
A RESOLUTION
To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Charles Joseph
"CJ" Christ.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on May 20, 2026,
of Charles Joseph "CJ" Christ; and
WHEREAS, Charles Joseph "CJ" Christ was born in 1929 on a farm in Mowata,
Louisiana, and was one of seven children born to Lawrence and Ada Christ; he moved to
Lake Charles, Louisiana, with his family during World War II and attended McNeese State
University; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Christ cherished his siblings, Ruth, Bobby, Francis, Geraldine,
Julie, and Jeanette; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Christ joined the United States Air Force in 1949 and flew twenty-
three combat missions in B-29 bombers over North Korea; after completing his military
service, he moved to Houma, Louisiana, and resided there for the remainder of his life; and
WHEREAS, during the mid-1950s, Mr. Christ performed impromptu air shows
above Houma in his P-51 Mustang fighter aircraft; he married Patricia Wurzlow in 1959 and
fathered three children; and
WHEREAS, in the 1970s, Mr. Christ operated a successful aviation business, Houma
Aviation, at the Houma Airport; in the 2000s, he founded the Regional Military Museum,
which has grown into a publicly-funded, veteran-run institution that receives overwhelming
support from citizens of the tri-parish area; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Christ was a well-respected author and historian who studied the
history of the United States Gulf during World War II; he was also an avid aviator, mariner,
and scuba diver; he traveled the world and touched the lives of all who met him with his
infectious sense of humor and passion for adventure; and

WHEREAS, Charles Joseph "CJ" Christ is survived by his children, Cheryl (Steve),
Bobby (Karen), and Linda; stepson, Jack (Ida); and host of grandchildren and extended
family members; he will be deeply missed, but the legacy of his adventurous spirit and
passion for life shall live on in perpetuity in the hearts and minds of those who were
privileged enough to have known and loved him.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Charles
Joseph "CJ" Christ and does hereby extend sincere wishes that his family, friends, and loved
ones find comfort in their shared memories of his beautiful life.
