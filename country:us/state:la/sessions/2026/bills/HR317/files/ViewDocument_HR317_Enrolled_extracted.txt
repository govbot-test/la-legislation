ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 317
DICKERSON, EDMONSTON, EGAN, FISHER, GLORIOSO, TRAVIS
JOHNSON, MANDIE LANDRY, TERRY LANDRY, MENA, MURRAY,
MUSCARELLO, NEWELL, OWEN, SCHLEGEL, TAYLOR, THOMPSON,
VENTRELLA, AND WYBLE
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to convene a
diagnostic review team to study any potential hazards of the increasing railroad
activity on the Norfolk Southern Railway originating at the Alabo Street Wharf.
WHEREAS, Norfolk Southern Railway is a Class I freight railroad operating in the
Eastern United States, with key infrastructure that includes approximately a six mile rail
bridge on the Lake Pontchartrain Bridge; and
WHEREAS, the Alabo Street Wharf in New Orleans was originally a slaughterhouse
that was purchased by the Port of New Orleans to transport river and train cargo, including
lumber, copper, and sugar; and
WHEREAS, this rail corridor has been largely dormant for decades; and
WHEREAS, the Port of New Orleans has entered into a fifteen-year lease agreement
with Sunrise Foods International to convert the Alabo Street Wharf in the Lower Ninth
Ward, Holy Cross, and other St. Bernard Parish neighborhoods into a specialized terminal
for importing organic grain, transported by Norfolk Southern trains; and
WHEREAS, in 2026, there were multiple incidents involving Norfolk Southern trains
colliding with vehicles in Arabi, Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, on March 23, 2026, an eighteen-wheeler truck collided with a Norfolk
Southern train; and
WHEREAS, on April 7, 2026, a Norfolk Southern train collided with a vehicle,
leading to the closure of Judge Perez Drive and Patricia Street; and
WHEREAS, there are multiple incidents in Alabama involving Norfolk Southern
trains; and

HR NO. 317 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, some tracks run within fifteen feet of the neighborhood residents's front
doors; and
WHEREAS, grain dust is an S-1 high-hazard classified material and can become
combustible under certain conditions and poses a potential environmental and safety hazard;
and
WHEREAS, residents surrounding these tracks argue there has been no recent
comprehensive assessment of the risks associated with this high-intensity industrial use of
these trains; and
WHEREAS, these tracks may pose a safety hazard due to their dangerous
infrastructure, including tight tracks and non-standard curves on narrow residential streets;
and
WHEREAS, R.S. 48:390 authorizes the secretary of the Department of
Transportation and Development to complete a study of all public railroad grade crossings
to establish priorities for improvement, relocation, or closure in compliance with federal
guidelines and requires the development of a prioritized plan for implementing railroad
grade crossing improvements, relocations or closures pursuant to the Railroad Highway
Crossing Program, 23 U.S.C. 130; and
WHEREAS, 23 CFR Part 646 outlines regulations for Federal-aid highway projects
involving railroad facilities, specifically focusing on safety improvements, crossing
elimination, insurance requirements, and cost-sharing between states and railroads; and
WHEREAS, there is a potential environmental and public safety issue arising out of
expansion of rail activity in this area.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Transportation and
Development to convene a diagnostic review team to conduct a study to assess the potential
hazards of increasing activity on the Norfolk Southern Railway originating at the Alabo
Street Wharf and potential ways to prevent any foreseeable harm to the community.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Transportation and
Development shall submit a written report detailing its findings to the House Committee on
Transportation, Highways and Public Works prior to January 1, 2027.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.
