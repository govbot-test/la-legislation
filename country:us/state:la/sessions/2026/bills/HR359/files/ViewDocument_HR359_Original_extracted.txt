HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 359
BY REPRESENTATIVE RISER
H. "Bob" Baumann
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the sincere condolences of the House of Representatives of the Legislature of
3 Louisiana upon the death of Robert H. "Bob" Baumann, longtime director of the
4 Louisiana State University Center for Energy Studies and trusted advisor on
5 Louisiana energy policy.
6 WHEREAS, it was with deep regret and profound sorrow that the citizens of
7 Louisiana learned of the death of Robert H. "Bob" Baumann on Thursday, November 6,
8 2025, at the age of seventy-four; and
9 WHEREAS, it is fitting to express enduring gratitude for Bob Baumann's outstanding
10 contributions to this state, particularly during more than three decades of service to
11 Louisiana State University and to the industries that serve as the foundation of the Louisiana
12 economy; and
13 WHEREAS, born and raised in the Queens borough of New York City, Bob
14 Baumann graduated from the University of Rhode Island and arrived in Baton Rouge in
15 1974 to pursue graduate studies at Louisiana State University, where he earned his Master
16 of Science degree in geography in 1980 under Dr. Jess Walker, with a thesis examining the
17 interrelationships of marsh elevation, water level, sedimentation, and other selected variables
18 in the Lower Barataria Basin; and
19 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann fell in love with LSU and the Baton Rouge community
20 and never returned to the northeast, putting down deep roots in Louisiana and devoting the
21 whole of his professional life to the people, institutions, and industries of this state; and
Page 1 of 4
HLS 26RS-6137 ORIGINAL

1 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann was a founding member of the LSU Center for Energy
2 Studies upon its establishment in 1982 and over the course of his tenure, rose to lead the
3 center, building it into an indispensable source of energy information and analysis for the
4 legislature, public agencies, business and civic groups, and the academic community, and
5 there was no part of his long career of which he was more justifiably proud than his work
6 on behalf of Louisiana State University; and
7 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann served as a confidant, friend, and advisor to multiple
8 leaders of the LSU system, beginning with Chancellor James Wharton, who recognized his
9 quiet capability and brought him into his inner circle during the 1980s campaign to institute
10 more selective admission standards at the university; and
11 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann served for many years as LSU's representative at the state
12 capitol, earning the trust of legislators across the political spectrum and securing critical
13 support for the university's teaching, research, and outreach missions until his retirement
14 from LSU in 2007; and
15 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann's blend of intellectual rigor and steady, plain-spoken
16 clarity made him one of the most trusted voices on energy policy in the state; as Tyler Gray,
17 a director of the LSU Energy Institute, observed, Bob "authored influential research, advised
18 governors and legislatures, and shaped major debates with a calm, steady clarity that made
19 even the most complex oil and gas issues understandable" and was "a tremendous mentor
20 to generations of students, colleagues, and policymakers"; and
21 WHEREAS, in 2014, in recognition of a career spent bettering the working
22 environment of Louisiana's oil and gas industry, Bob Baumann received the Lifetime
23 Achievement Award of the Louisiana Independent Oil and Gas Association, becoming only
24 the third individual so honored in the association's history; and
25 WHEREAS, in 2023, the Louisiana Mid-Continent Oil and Gas Association
26 presented Bob Baumann with the Hard Hat Award in recognition of his exemplary and
27 outstanding contributions to Louisiana's oil and natural gas industry, an honor reflecting a
28 lifetime of service to the sector; and
29 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann was a champion of Louisiana industry at every level of
30 the legislative process and a persistent voice of reason on questions of pipelines, refining,

1 petrochemicals, and the broader industrial activity that drives the state's economy, drawing
2 on a depth of technical and historical knowledge that made him a trusted resource for
3 legislators, agency officials, and industry advocates alike, on whichever side of an issue they
4 happened to find themselves; and
5 WHEREAS, following his retirement from the university, Bob Baumann opened the
6 next chapter of his career as a principal of Hood, Baumann and Associates, where he
7 counseled and represented some of the most significant energy enterprises operating in
8 Louisiana, including Shell Oil, ConocoPhillips, Enbridge, TC Energy, Phillips 66, and PBF
9 Energy, and continued to bring his characteristic care and seriousness to the work of
10 Louisiana energy policy until the final days of his life; and
11 WHEREAS, beyond his roles in research and advocacy, Baumann remained a
12 stalwart supporter of his academic home in the LSU Department of Geography and
13 Anthropology, securing critical funding for the renovation of the Departmental Alumni
14 Seminar Room and, with his family, establishing the Robert H. Baumann Family
15 Scholarship Fund to support outstanding students in the sciences for years to come; and
16 WHEREAS, all who worked with Bob Baumann over the course of his long career
17 appreciated his graciousness, his integrity, and his unmistakable northeast accent softened
18 by decades of Louisiana hospitality and knew him as a generous mentor, a candid counselor,
19 and a true gentleman of the capitol; and
20 WHEREAS, from the front porch of his office on Spanish Town Road, affectionately
21 known to those who passed through it as the "Policy Porch," Bob Baumann hosted
22 legislators, lobbyists, agency officials, students, reporters, and friends for wide-ranging
23 conversations on anything and everything happening at the capitol, holding open a kind of
24 standing seminar on Louisiana public life that no other forum could replicate; and
25 WHEREAS, Bob Baumann was a mentor in the fullest sense of the word; generous
26 with his time, his sage advice, and his wealth of knowledge and a great many of those who
27 today carry forward Louisiana's energy and industrial policy work owe some part of their
28 professional formation to an afternoon, an introduction, or a candid piece of counsel from
29 him; and

1 WHEREAS, above all, Bob Baumann was a devoted family man with an unwavering
2 commitment to his wife, Brenda, and their two sons, Frank and Kyle, who will be missed
3 profoundly by his beloved family and the many other individuals whose lives he touched
4 with his wisdom and generosity of spirit; and
5 WHEREAS, with the death of Bob Baumann, the state has lost a faithful adopted son,
6 a builder of institutions, and a larger-than-life figure whose counsel shaped a generation of
7 Louisiana energy policy and whose absence will be felt at the Louisiana State Capitol every
8 day of every session to come.
9 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
10 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express sincere and heartfelt condolences upon the
11 death of Robert H. "Bob" Baumann; does hereby recognize and record for posterity his
12 notable accomplishments and singular contributions to Louisiana State University, to the
13 LSU Center for Energy Studies, and to the broader cause of sound energy and industrial
14 policy in this state; and does hereby extend enduring appreciation for the pride and honor
15 that he will forever bring to his family, to LSU, to the Baton Rouge community, and to the
16 state of Louisiana.
