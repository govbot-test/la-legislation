HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 337
BY REPRESENTATIVE PHELPS
A RESOLUTION
To commend Green Whitaker, Sr., on the occasion of his one hundredth birthday.
WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize Green Whitaker, Sr., who has been a
witness to history throughout his long life, and to commend the occasion of his one
hundredth birthday; and
WHEREAS, Green Whitaker, Sr., was born on February 2, 1926, to Estella and Isaac
Whitaker; he was the second youngest of nineteen children who were raised in the
agricultural community of Holly in DeSoto Parish, Louisiana; his family lived on eighty
acres of land, purchased by his father, where they cultivated nearly all of their food; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Whitaker's daughter, Anita "Nita" Whitaker, chronicled her father's
upbringing in her award-winning book, When Your Hand is in the Lion's Mouth: The Life
and Wisdom of a Man named Green; and
WHEREAS, the Whitaker family property was found to be a source of oil, and the
family established a drilling rig and began pumping and selling oil; the discovery drew
attention from oil drillers who unsuccessfully attempted to purchase the property; drillers
tapped into the source of oil from an adjacent property, which led to salt contamination of
the Whitaker property and marginalized the once-arable farmland; and
WHEREAS, the Whitaker family sold the property and pivoted to sharecropping on
the Frierson Plantation; Isaac Whitaker, a man of great character, compassion, intelligence,
generosity, and love for his family, maintained his family's resilience and instilled these
qualities in his children; and
WHEREAS, Green emerged as a budding entrepreneur in his adulthood; he was
married to the former Ola Mae White for twenty-eight years before her passing on
November 21, 1977, and they raised four children: Green, Jr.; Alene; Kathy; and Anita; he
established his family on 73rd Street in the Cedar Grove community in Shreveport,
Louisiana, and supported many others in the community; and

WHEREAS, Green dedicated thirty-five years of service as a school bus operator for
the Caddo Parish School Board, and during one period of his life, worked five part-time
jobs; he established a takeout food business with his wife, served as the founder and co-
owner of Good Samaritan Funeral Home for twenty-one years, and worked as a successful
insurance salesman for more than sixty-eight years; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Whitaker has served as a deacon for more than seventy-five years,
and he is known in the Cedar Grove community and beyond as a man of great insight,
kindness, and integrity; he is a lifelong singer and was a member and manager of a local
gospel group named The Spiritual Jubilee; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Whitaker volunteered as a foster grandparent in his late eighties and
played the role of Jesus in a musical production in his nineties; he continues to serve as an
incredible father and mentor to his children and others; and
WHEREAS, the last-living member of his large family, Mr. Whitaker remains a
beloved and highly respected resident of the city of Shreveport, where he is treasured as a
consistently positive role model, faith warrior, and source of fatherly inspiration; and
WHEREAS, Green Whitaker, Sr., is an inspiring Louisianian with a wealth of
experience spanning countless disciplines; he is truly deserving of the highest recognition
for his incredible contributions throughout his life.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Green Whitaker, Sr., on the occasion of his
one hundredth birthday and does hereby extend sincere wishes that he remain prosperous
and in good health as he joins the exclusive club of Louisiana centenarians.
