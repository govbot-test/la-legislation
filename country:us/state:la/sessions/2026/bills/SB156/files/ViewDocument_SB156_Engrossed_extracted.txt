DIGEST
SB 156 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Kleinpeter
Present law provides that whoever commits the crime of negligent homicide will be
sentenced as follows:
(1) When the victim was 10 years of age or older, the offender may be fined not more
than $5,000, or imprisoned, with or without hard labor, for 10 years, or both.
(2) When the victim was less than 10 years of age, the offender will be imprisoned at
hard labor, without benefit of probation, parole, or suspension of sentence, for not
less than two years nor more than 10 years.
Proposed law increases the maximum period of imprisonment from 10 years to 15 years
regardless of the victim's age and otherwise retains present law.
Proposed law provides that an offender will be imprisoned at hard labor for not less than two
years nor more than 20 years if:
(1) The victim was less than 13 years of age.
(2) The death was the result of criminally negligent mistreatment, abuse, or neglect.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends 14:32(C)(1) and (2))

Committee Amendments Proposed by Senate Committee on Judiciary C to the

proposed law.

20 years.
