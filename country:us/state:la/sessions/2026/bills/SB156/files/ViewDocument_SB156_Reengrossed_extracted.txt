2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR KLEINPETER
CRIME/PUNISHMENT. Increases the maximum period of imprisonment for negligent
homicide. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 14:32(C)(1) and (2), relative to negligent homicide; to provide
3 relative to penalties; to increase the maximum term of imprisonment for negligent
4 homicide under certain circumstances; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 14:32(C)(1) and (2) are hereby amended and reenacted to read as
7 follows:
8 §32. Negligent homicide
9 * * *
10 C.(1)(a) Except as provided for in Paragraph (2) of this Subsection, whoever
11 commits the crime of negligent homicide shall be imprisoned with or without hard
12 labor for not more than ten years, fined not more than five thousand dollars, or
13 imprisoned, with or without labor, for not more than fifteen years, or both.
14 (b) If the court does not order the offender to a term of imprisonment
15 when the following two factors are established, the court shall state, both orally
16 and in writing at the time of sentencing, the reasons for not sentencing the
17 offender to a term of imprisonment:

1 (i) The fatality was caused by a person engaged in the operation of, or in
2 actual physical control of, any motor vehicle, aircraft, watercraft, or other
3 means of conveyance; and
4 (ii) The offender's blood alcohol concentration contributed to the fatality.
5 (2)(a) If the victim killed was under the age of ten years, the offender shall
6 be imprisoned at hard labor, without benefit of probation, parole, or suspension of
7 sentence, for not less than two years nor more than ten fifteen years.
8 (b) If the court does not order the offender to a term of imprisonment when
9 the following two factors are established, the court shall state, both orally and in
10 writing at the time of sentencing, the reasons for not sentencing the offender to a
11 term of imprisonment:
12 (i) The fatality was caused by a person engaged in the operation of, or in
13 actual physical control of, any motor vehicle, aircraft, watercraft, or other means of
14 conveyance; and
15 (ii) The offender's blood alcohol concentration contributed to the fatality.
16 If the victim was under the age of thirteen years and the fatality was the
17 result of criminally negligent mistreatment, abuse, or neglect, the offender shall
18 be imprisoned at hard labor for not less than two years nor more than twenty
19 years.

DIGEST
SB 156 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Kleinpeter
Present law provides that whoever commits the crime of negligent homicide will be
sentenced as follows:
(1) When the victim was 10 years of age or older, the offender may be fined not more
than $5,000, or imprisoned, with or without hard labor, for 10 years, or both.
(2) When the victim was less than 10 years of age, the offender will be imprisoned at
hard labor, without benefit of probation, parole, or suspension of sentence, for not
less than two years nor more than 10 years.

Proposed law increases the maximum period of imprisonment from 10 years to 15 years
regardless of the victim's age and otherwise retains present law.
Proposed law provides that an offender will be imprisoned at hard labor for not less than two
years nor more than 20 years if:
(1) The victim was less than 13 years of age.
(2) The death was the result of criminally negligent mistreatment, abuse, or neglect.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends 14:32(C)(1) and (2))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by Senate
Committee Amendments Proposed by Senate Committee on Judiciary C to the
original bill

Section 2:
1. Delete proposed law provision relative to the offender's age.

Section 3:
2. Reduce the minimum age of the victim for the proposed law enhanced
penalty from 17 to 13.

Section 4:
3. Delete the element of intentional mistreatment, abuse, or neglect from
proposed law.

Section 5:
4. Reduce the proposed law maximum period of incarceration from 40 years to
20 years.
