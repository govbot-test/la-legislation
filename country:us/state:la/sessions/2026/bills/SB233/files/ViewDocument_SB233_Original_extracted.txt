DIGEST
SB 233 Original 2026 Regular Session Mizell
Proposed law authorizes the office of technology services to create the Louisiana Statewide
Data Exchange Compact (LSDEC), to establish a single legal and technical framework for
secure, privacy-compliant data sharing between the office of technology services and
participating agencies.
Proposed law provides that the compact shall facilitate the disclosure of confidential
information and set forth each participating agency's rights and obligations regarding such
information, including the limited purposes for which each agency may use or have access
to confidential information.
Proposed law defines "agency", "data exchange", and "Louisiana Statewide Data Exchange
Compact".
Proposed law requires the office of technology services to serve as the administrator for
LSDEC.
Proposed law requires the office of technology services to establish a committee of
participating agencies to provide input on the development of the LSDEC and review the
compact at least twice per year to ensure alignment with changes in state and federal law and
developments in data security practices.
Proposed law provides that participation in the LSDEC is voluntary and authorizes an
agency, at any time, to make the determination that continued use of the compact is no
longer appropriate for specific data and to amend or terminate its participation or pursue an
alternative method to protect data shared by the agency.
Proposed law requires participating agencies to sign the LSDEC, which shall serve as the
primary governance document for safeguarding data shared among agencies.
Proposed law requires the compact to specify that a receiving agency shall establish,
implement, and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to
ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of confidential information received
from another agency.
Proposed law authorizes the office of technology services to promulgate rules to implement
the provisions of proposed law.
Effective upon signature of the governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 39:18.1-18.3)
