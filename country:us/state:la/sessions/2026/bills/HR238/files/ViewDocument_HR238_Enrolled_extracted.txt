2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 238
BY REPRESENTATIVE DEVILLIER
A RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on May 1, 2026,
of Jane Couvillion; and
WHEREAS, Jane was born on May 7, 1942, and her life was defined by her
Christian faith and devotion to her family; and
WHEREAS, Jane married the love of her life, Dr. John Lee Couvillion, on June 1,
1963; they shared fifty-three years of marriage together before his passing; she was a
devoted homemaker who raised her children and grandchildren in a home filled with love
and laughter; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Couvillion gave her family the gift of faith, and she and John set
a wonderful example of service to their church; she dedicated many years as a CCD teacher
at St. Thomas More, taught confirmation and pre-cana classes, and was involved in
St. Thomas More Couples Weekend retreats; and
WHEREAS, Jane cherished her monthly gatherings with friends; she shared meals
and Christian fellowship with her supper club group for forty years; she also enjoyed
meeting with her six sisters for their monthly "sister breakfast", during which they shared
many conversations; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Couvillion loved spending time with her children and
grandchildren, and she was blessed to live long enough to meet her first great-grandchild,
Emrie George, ten months before her passing; her great-grandchild was a true blessing that
brought much joy and laughter to her life; and
Page 1 of 2
HR NO. 238 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, Jane Couvillion is survived by her beloved children, Darren Couvillion
(Leah), Sue Andrepont (Chuck), Michelle George (Mike), and Jeanelle Fruge (Nick);
grandchildren, Peyton Couvillion, John Christian Lyons, Nathan Andrepont, Jacob George,
Ian George, Kaleb George, Grace Fruge, and Zachary Fruge; great-grandchild, Emrie
George; and a host of extended relatives and dear friends; she will be deeply missed, but the
legacy of her loving spirit shall live on in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged
to have known and loved her.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Jane
Couvillion and does hereby extend sincere wishes that her family, friends, and loved ones
find comfort in their shared memories of her beautiful life.
