To amend and reenact R.S. 14:35(B) and 38(B) and to enact Part III of Chapter 3 of Title 23
of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to be comprised of R.S. 23:271 through
274, relative to workplace violence in retail and food service establishments; to
provide a short title; to provide definitions; to prohibit workplace violence against
an employee of a regulated establishment; to provide enhanced penalties for
offenders who commit certain crimes against an employee of a regulated
establishment; to provide for signage; and to provide for related matters.
Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:

Section 2:
Section 1. R.S. 14:35(B) and 38(B) are hereby amended and reenacted to read as
follows:

Section 3:
§35. Simple battery
* * *
B.(1) Whoever commits a simple battery shall be fined not more than one
thousand dollars or imprisoned for not more than six months, or both.
(2) Whoever commits a simple battery against an employee of a regulated
establishment as defined in R.S. 23:272 shall be fined not more than two thousand
dollars or imprisoned, with or without hard labor, for not more than two years, or
both.
* * *

Section 4:
§38. Simple assault
* * *
B.(1) Whoever commits a simple assault shall be fined not more than two
hundred dollars, or imprisoned for not more than ninety days, or both.

PART III. THE LOUISIANA BEHIND THE COUNTER PROTECTION ACT

Section 6:
§271. Short Title
This Part shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Behind the
Counter Protection Act".

Section 7:
§272. Definitions
As used in this Part, the following terms have the meanings ascribed to them:
(1) "Department" means Louisiana Works.
(2) "Employee" means an employee who engages in duties at or behind a
checkout station, service counter, drive-through window, customer service desk, or
similar place or structure utilized as the point of transaction for an employee and a
customer.
(3)(a) "Regulated establishment" means any retail store, convenience store,
pharmacy, restaurant, fast food outlet, gas station with a food or retail counter, or
other licensed commercial establishment in this state where employees regularly
interact with the public at a service counter or cash register for the sale of goods or
food for consumption on or off premises.
(b) "Regulated establishment" does not include establishments primarily
engaged in office-based or nonpublic-facing operations and regulated entities as
provided for in R.S. 40:2199.11 et seq.
(4) "Workplace violence" means any act of violence or credible threat of
violence, including but not limited to assault, battery, robbery, intimidation, verbal
abuse, threats with a weapon, or any conduct that places an employee in reasonable
fear of physical harm or is directed at an employee while he is performing his duties
at a regulated establishment.

Section 8:
§273. Prohibition against workplace violence; penalties

No person shall commit the offense of workplace violence against an
employee of a regulated establishment. Whoever commits the offense of workplace
violence against an employee of a regulated establishment shall be charged with any
of the following crimes as appropriate:
(1) Simple assault as defined in R.S. 14:38(B)(2).
(2) Simple battery as defined in R.S. 14:35(B)(2).

Section 9:
§274. Signage
A. A regulated establishment may display at its premises at least one sign
that contains the following statement in forty-eight boldface font:
"WARNING: Workplace violence against an employee is a crime and will not be
tolerated. Such acts may result in arrest and criminal conviction under Louisiana law."
B. The department shall prescribe and furnish a sign that conforms with the
specifications provided for in Subsection A of this Section. The department shall
make the sign readily accessible for or distribute the sign to a regulated
establishment.

HB Draft 2026 Regular Session
Abstract: Enacts the Louisiana Behind the Counter Protection Act, which prohibits
workplace violence against an employee of a regulated establishment.
Present law defines and provide penalties for the crime of simple battery.
Proposed law adds an enhanced penalty, which includes enforcing a fine of not more than
$2,000 or imprisonment, with or without hard labor, for not more than two years, or both
against a person who commits such crime against an employee of a regulated establishment.
Proposed law otherwise retains present law.
Present law defines and provide penalties for the crime of simple assault.
Proposed law adds an enhanced penalty, which includes enforcing a fine of not more than
$1,000 or imprisonment for not more than six months, or both against a person who commits
such crime against an employee of a regulated establishment. Proposed law otherwise
retains present law.
Proposed law states that proposed law shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana
Behind the Counter Protection Act".
