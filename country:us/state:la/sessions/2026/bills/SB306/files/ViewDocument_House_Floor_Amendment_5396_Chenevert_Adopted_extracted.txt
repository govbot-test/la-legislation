6 Section 2. Civil Code Art. 2315.14 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 Art. 2315.14. Liability for damages caused by offenses related to obstruction
8 of freedom of worship in a church or other place of worship
9 A. In addition to general and special damages, a prevailing plaintiff shall
10 be awarded court costs and reasonable attorney fees upon proof that the
11 injuries on which the action is based were caused by conduct prohibited by R.S.
12 14:38.6 resulting in the injury to the person, obstruction of the freedom of
13 worship in a church or other place of worship, or damage to property,
14 regardless of whether the defendant was arrested, charged, or prosecuted for
15 such conduct.
16 B. Nothing in this Article shall be construed to limit any other cause of
17 action or remedy provided by law.
18 C. As used in this Article, a defendant includes any person who
19 knowingly aided, abetted, financed, funded, solicited, conspired, or otherwise
20 provided material support or assistance for conduct prohibited by R.S. 14:38.6.
21 D.(1) Recoverable damages pursuant to this Article may include but are
22 not limited to damages for physical injury, mental anguish, emotional distress,
23 property damage, lost income, interference with the free exercise of religion,
24 and any other damages authorized by law.
25 (2) In addition to any other remedy provided by law, a court may grant
26 temporary restraining orders, preliminary injunctions, permanent injunctions,
27 or declaratory relief, as well as court costs and reasonable attorney fees, to
28 prevent the conduct, or to prevent the continuation of the conduct, that is
29 prohibited by R.S. 14:38.6."
