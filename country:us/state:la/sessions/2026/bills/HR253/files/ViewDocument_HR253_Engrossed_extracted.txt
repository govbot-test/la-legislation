HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 253
BY REPRESENTATIVE OWEN
BUILDING CODES: Creates the Task Force on Residential Construction Cost and
Inspection Practices
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To create the Task Force on Residential Construction Cost and Inspection Practices, to study
3 and make recommendations regarding the impact of post-2005 building code
4 additions and inspection requirements on the cost of residential construction in this
5 state.
6 WHEREAS, homeowners, homebuilders, and the public have expressed concern that
7 onerous and inconsistent inspection regimes increase housing costs without commensurate
8 gains in public safety, while also creating uncertainty and financial risk for both builders and
9 buyers; and
10 WHEREAS, the Louisiana State Uniform Construction Code was originally enacted
11 pursuant to Act No. 12 of the 2005 First Extraordinary Session with the clear legislative
12 intent to limit mandatory requirements for one- and two-family dwellings to basic building
13 integrity and life-safety standards; and
14 WHEREAS, since Hurricanes Katrina and Rita, many parishes and municipalities
15 have imposed additional and often conflicting code requirements, energy-efficiency
16 mandates, inspection regimes, and permitting processes that were never contemplated in the
17 original 2005 law; and
18 WHEREAS, these additional requirements have driven significant inflation in the
19 cost of constructing an average single-family home in this state and have created wide
20 disparities in inspection fees, timelines, and enforcement practices across different regions
21 of the state; and

1 WHEREAS, the legislature finds that a comprehensive, data-driven study is
2 necessary to quantify the increase in the average cost of building an average home since
3 2005, to document geographic disparities in inspection costs and practices, and to determine
4 whether new legislation is needed to protect home buyers, home builders, and public safety
5 while restoring the original limited scope of the residential building code.
6 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
7 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby establish the Task Force on Residential Construction
8 Cost and Inspection Practices to study the impact of additions and amendments to the
9 building codes and inspection requirements since 2005 on the cost of residential construction
10 in this state, and provide that this Task Force may propose recommendations, together with
11 specific proposals for legislation. Any recommendations or proposals shall be submitted by
12 written report to the legislature and to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as
13 required by R.S. 24:771 and 772.
14 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the legislature recommends that the task force
15 include consideration of all of the following:
16 (1) The average cost of constructing an average single-family home in Louisiana
17 from 2005 to the present, including a breakdown of how code and inspection requirements
18 have contributed to those costs.
19 (2) Disparities in inspection fees, permitting timelines, and enforcement practices
20 among parishes and municipalities, with particular attention to differences that emerged after
21 Hurricanes Katrina and Rita.
22 (3) Whether current law adequately protects home buyers, home builders, and public
23 safety, and whether legislation is needed to reaffirm the original intent of Act No. 12 of the
24 2005 First Extraordinary Session of the Legislature, limit mandatory residential requirements
25 to core integrity and safety standards, address inspection cost disparities, and reduce
26 unnecessary regulatory burdens.
27 (4) A list of legislative recommendations that the task force proposes to affect both
28 near- and long-term change for homeowners, homebuilders, and the economy of the this
29 state.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
2 members:
3 (1) Five members of the Louisiana State Uniform Construction Code Council
4 appointed by the chairman of the council.
5 (2) Three active homebuilders, licensed in this state appointed by the president of
6 the Louisiana Home Builders Association.
7 (3) One representative of the Louisiana Home Builders Association appointed by the

9 (4) One representative of the Police Jury Association of Louisiana appointed by the

13 (6) One representative of the Department of Insurance appointed by the
14 commissioner of insurance.
15 (7) One representative of the State Licensing Board for Contractors appointed by the

17 (8) One member of the Building Officials Association of Louisiana appointed by its
18 president.
19 (9) One active licensed private inspector of the Louisiana Association of Third-Party
20 Inspectors appointed by its president.
21 (10) Two active homebuilders appointed by the speaker of the House of
22 Representatives.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each appointing authority shall submit the
24 names of the appointees of the task force to the Louisiana State Uniform Construction Code
25 Council no later than July 1, 2026.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that at the first meeting, the members shall elect a
27 chairman and other officers as the task force may deem appropriate.

29 no later than August 1, 2026, at the call of the Louisiana Uniform Construction Code
