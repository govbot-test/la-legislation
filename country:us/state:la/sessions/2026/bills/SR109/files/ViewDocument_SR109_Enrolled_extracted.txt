2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 109
BY SENATOR STINE
A RESOLUTION
To recognize Monday, May 11, 2026, as Louisiana Arts Day in Louisiana.
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is known for its rich and diverse cultural heritage,
shaped by generations of artists, musicians, writers, performers, and tradition bearers whose
creativity defines the spirit and identity of our people; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana's arts and culture sector is a vital economic engine, generating
an estimated six billion six hundred million dollars in annual economic impact, supporting
more than forty-nine thousand jobs, and contributing approximately two point one percent
to the state's gross domestic product; and
WHEREAS, artists and cultural workers in this state are the driving force behind the
state's thriving tourism industry, attracting millions of visitors each year who come to
experience the state's unique music, cuisine, festivals, visual arts, historic sites, and cultural
traditions; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana tourism is powered by the creativity, innovation, and
authenticity of Louisiana's artists and culture bearers, whose work not only enriches the lives
of residents but also strengthens communities and local economies across all sixty-four
parishes; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Partnership for the Arts works to advocate on behalf of
artists in this state, ensuring they are heard and recognized for their contributions to our
cultural economy and the vibrancy of art throughout the state; and
WHEREAS, investing in and supporting the arts ensures continued economic growth,
educational enrichment, workforce development, and the preservation of Louisiana's
irreplaceable cultural legacy; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana Arts Day serves as an opportunity to recognize, celebrate, and
advocate for the importance of the arts and culture sector and the individuals who sustain it.

THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
