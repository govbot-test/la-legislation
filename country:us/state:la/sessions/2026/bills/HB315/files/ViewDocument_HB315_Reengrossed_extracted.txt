HB 315 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Melerine
Abstract: Prohibits noncompete contracts and agreements for interns and apprentices.
Present law provides that, generally, every contract or agreement or provision that restrains
a person from exercising a lawful profession, trade, or business shall be null and void.
However, present law provides an illustrative list of exceptions where noncompete contracts
and agreements are permitted.
Proposed law prohibits noncompete contracts, agreements, or provisions for unpaid or paid
interns and apprentices.
Proposed law prohibits noncompete contracts, agreements, or provisions from restricting an
intern or apprentice from pursuing employment, educational, or professional opportunities
following the completion of the internship or apprenticeship.
Proposed law states that an intern or apprentice shall remain subject to the employer's
confidentiality, intellectual property, and data protection agreements.
Proposed law defines "intern".
(Adds R.S. 23:921(P))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Labor and Industrial
Relations to the original bill:
1. Prohibit a noncompete contract, agreement, or provision from restricting an
intern or apprentice from pursuing employment, educational, or professional
opportunities following the completion of the internship or apprenticeship.
2. Provide that an intern or apprentice shall still be subject to the employer's
confidentiality, intellectual property, and data protection agreements.
3. Make a title change.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Define the word "intern".
