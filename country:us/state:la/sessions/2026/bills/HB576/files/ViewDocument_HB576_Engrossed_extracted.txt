STATE DEPARTMENT: Transfers the Old Governor's Mansion to the Department of State
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 49:141 and to enact Chapter 5-S of Title 25 of the Louisiana
3 Revised Statutes of 1950, to be comprised of R.S. 25:380.161 through 380.166,
4 relative to the Old Governor's Mansion; to transfer the Old Governor's Mansion to
5 the Department of State; to provide for the powers, duties, functions, and
6 responsibilities of the secretary of state and the department; to provide relative to
7 operations and funding; to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for related
8 matters.
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. Chapter 5-S of Title 25 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
11 comprised of R.S. 25:380.161 through 380.166, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
12 CHAPTER 5-S. OLD GOVERNOR'S MANSION
13 §380.161. Definitions
14 For the purposes of this Chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings
15 indicated unless the context clearly provides otherwise:
16 (1) "Department" means the Department of State.
17 (2) "Secretary" means the secretary of state.

1 §380.162. Establishment and location; purpose and use
2 A. The Old Governor's Mansion is established as a facility in the City of
3 Baton Rouge under the overall jurisdiction of the department, as more specifically
4 provided in this Chapter.
5 B. The secretary shall have custody, control, and supervision of maintenance
6 of the Old Governor's Mansion and its contents. Title to the Old Governor's Mansion
7 shall remain in the state of Louisiana, along with all buildings and other
8 improvements constructed thereon. All of the management, functions, programs, and
9 operations of every kind of the Old Governor's Mansion shall be exercised and
10 performed by the secretary.
11 C. For purposes of administration and supervision of the Old Governor's
12 Mansion, the secretary shall have full authority over its grounds and all other
13 buildings and improvements constructed thereon, and shall control any use made
14 thereof and the space therein.
15 D. The Old Governor's Mansion shall be a historical, cultural, and
16 educational institution whose primary purpose shall be to collect, preserve, and
17 present, as an educational resource, documents, artifacts, objects of art, and the like,
18 that reflect the politics, history, art, and culture of Louisiana.
19 §380.163. Operations
20 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary and in particular the
21 provisions of R.S. 41:1212, the secretary may enter into contracts and agreements
22 with any public agency or nonprofit corporation which has been established for the
23 primary purpose of supporting the programs and activities of the Old Governor's
24 Mansion for the establishment of offices, stores, or exhibits on the property of and
25 in the Old Governor's Mansion. The secretary may also enter into contracts and
26 agreements for joint construction, equipment, maintenance, and financing thereof,
27 and may enter into contracts and agreements for the joint financing, supervision, and
28 conduct of cooperative enterprises and undertakings. Any public agency may enter

1 into contracts and agreements with the secretary for the purposes mentioned in this
2 Section.
3 §380.164. Operating funds
4 A. The legislature shall provide sufficient funds for the proper operation of
5 the Old Governor's Mansion.
6 B.(1) If sufficient funds necessary for the operation of the Old Governor's
7 Mansion are not appropriated by the legislature, and funds from other local, public,
8 or private sources are not available for this purpose, the department may temporarily
9 close the Old Governor's Mansion to the public until such time as sufficient funds
10 necessary for the operation of the Old Governor's Mansion are appropriated by the
11 legislature. The department shall maintain the Old Governor's Mansion during any
12 period of temporary closure.
13 (2) The department may accept and use, in accordance with law, gifts,
14 grants, bequests, donations, endowments, or funds from any public or private source
15 to support operations of the Old Governor's Mansion.
16 §380.165. Donations and loans; disposition of abandoned loans
17 A. The secretary may solicit and accept funds, governmental grants,
18 donations, and contributions of lands, buildings, monies, relics, works of art, or other
19 property on behalf of and as additions to the Old Governor's Mansion either in the
20 form of loans or in the form of donations inter vivos or mortis causa, and the
21 secretary may acquire them by purchase, lease, or otherwise, subject to the terms,
22 conditions, or limitations contained in the instrument by which such property is
23 acquired. However, the secretary shall be empowered and authorized to enter into
24 any and all contracts with any person, lender, or donor that he may deem fit, proper,
25 and necessary to accept and receive such lands, buildings, monies, relics, works of
26 art, or other property on behalf of and as additions to the Old Governor's Mansion.
27 The Old Governor's Mansion is specifically empowered to serve as the beneficiary
28 of any public trust heretofore or hereafter created for such purpose pursuant to the
29 provisions of R.S. 9:2341 through 2347.

1 B. The secretary is granted the authority of deaccession with respect to any
2 collections of the Old Governor's Mansion and for such purpose is specifically
3 exempted from the laws of the state relative to the sale or disposal of surplus
4 property. The secretary shall establish policies and procedures necessary to carry out
5 this authority in an orderly manner.
6 C.(1) Any property which has been deposited with the Old Governor's
7 Mansion, by loan or otherwise, and which has been held by the Old Governor's
8 Mansion for more than ten years, and to which no person has made a claim shall be
9 deemed to be abandoned and, notwithstanding the provisions of Chapter 1 of Title
10 XII of Book III of the Civil Code, shall become the property of the Old Governor's
11 Mansion, provided that the secretary of state has published a notice and listing of the
12 property in at least one newspaper of general circulation in the parish at least once
13 per week for two consecutive weeks. The notice shall contain:
14 (a) The name and last known address, if any, of the last known owner or
15 depositor of the property.
16 (b) A description of the property.
17 (c) A statement that if proof of claim is not presented by the owner to the
18 secretary and if the owner's right to receive such property is not established to the
19 satisfaction of the secretary within sixty-five days from the date of the second
20 published notice, the property will be deemed abandoned and shall become the
21 property of the Old Governor's Mansion.
22 (2) If no valid claim is made to the property within sixty-five days from the
23 date of the second published notice, title to the property shall vest in the Old
24 Governor's Mansion free from all claims of the owner and all persons claiming
25 through or under him.
26 §380.166. Use of collections
27 The collections of the Old Governor's Mansion may be lent in part subject to
28 approval by the secretary. The secretary shall ensure proper safeguards for their
29 maintenance and return and shall ensure that proper records are kept.

HB 576 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Gadberry
Abstract: Transfers the Old Governor's Mansion to the Dept. of State.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(A)) creates that Division of State Buildings in the office of the
governor. Provides that the division is administered by a superintendent of state buildings
appointed by the governor. Provides that the superintendent shall have charge of the
management, operation, and maintenance of general public buildings of the state, including
both the old and new governor's mansions.
Proposed law removes the Old Governor's Mansion from its inclusion among the general
public buildings of the state that are under the jurisdiction of the superintendent of the
Division of State Buildings. Otherwise retains present law.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(B)) provides that the Division of State Buildings shall have
custody and supervision of maintenance of the Old Governor's Mansion with its buildings
and improvements. Provides that the superintendent of state buildings shall maintain the
building and grounds with money appropriated by the legislature for such purposes.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(C)) provides for the management and control of the property of the
Old Governor's Mansion by the commissioner of administration. Authorizes the
commissioner to transfer custody, supervision, and general administrative control to any
public agency or non-profit corporation with sufficient resources to preserve the mansion
and its contents. Proposed law repeals present law.
Proposed law establishes the Old Governor's Mansion under the overall jurisdiction of the
Dept. of State. Provides that the secretary of state shall have custody, control, and
supervision of maintenance of the building and its contents, including all of its management,
functions, programs, and operations. Provides that the secretary shall have full authority
over the building's grounds and all other buildings and improvements constructed thereon.
Provides that title to the Old Governor's Mansion remains in the State of La., along with all
buildings and other improvements constructed thereon.
Proposed law provides that the Old Governor's Mansion shall function as a historical,
cultural, and educational institution with a primary purpose to collect, preserve, and present
documents, artifacts, and objects of art that reflect the politics, history, art, and culture of La.
Proposed law authorizes the secretary of state to enter into contracts and agreements with
public agencies or nonprofit corporations that are established for the primary purpose of
supporting the programs and activities of the Old Governor's Mansion for the creation of
offices, stores, or exhibits on the property of and within the building. Authorizes the
secretary to enter into contracts and agreements for joint construction, equipment,
maintenance, and financing thereof, and for the joint financing, supervision, and conduct of

are additions.

cooperative enterprises and undertakings. Authorizes any public agency to enter into
contracts and agreements with the secretary for such purposes.
Proposed law requires the legislature to provide sufficient funds for the proper operation of
the Old Governor's Mansion. Authorizes the Dept. of State to temporarily close the building
to the public until the legislature is able to appropriate sufficient funds for its operation.
Requires the department to maintain the building during any period of temporary closure.
Proposed law authorizes the secretary of state to solicit and accept funds, governmental
grants, donations. Authorizes the secretary to accept contributions of lands, buildings,
monies, relics, works of art, or other property on behalf of and as additions to the Old
Governor's Mansion in the form of loans or donations. Authorizes the secretary to acquire

limitations contained in the loan or donation agreement by which the property is acquired.
Authorizes the secretary to enter into any contracts with any person, lender, or donor that he
deems fit, proper, and necessary to accept and receive such property on behalf of and as
additions to the Old Governor's Mansion.
Proposed law provides that the Old Governor's Mansion is authorized to serve as the
beneficiary of any public trust created pursuant to present law.
Proposed law grants the secretary of state the authority of deaccession with respect to any
collections of the Old Governor's Mansion. Exempts the secretary, for such purposes, from
the laws of the state relative to the sale or disposal of surplus property. Requires the
secretary to establish policies and procedures necessary to carry out this authority.
Proposed law provides a procedure for acquiring property deposited with the Old Governor's
Mansion, by loan or otherwise, which has been in its possession for more than 10 years
without claim.
Proposed law authorizes, subject to approval by the secretary of state, the collections of the
Old Governor's Mansion to be lent in part. Requires the secretary to ensure proper
safeguards for the maintenance and return of such collections and to ensure that proper
records of such lending are kept.

Proposed law authorizes, subject to approval by the secretary of state, the collections of the
Old Governor's Mansion to be lent in part. Requires the secretary to ensure proper
safeguards for the maintenance and return of such collections and to ensure that proper
records of such lending are kept.
(Amends R.S. 49:141; Adds R.S. 25:380.161- 380.166)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on House and
Governmental Affairs to the original bill:
1. Make technical changes.
2. Provide that the title to the Old Governor's Mansion shall remain in the state of
La., along with all buildings and other improvements constructed thereon.
