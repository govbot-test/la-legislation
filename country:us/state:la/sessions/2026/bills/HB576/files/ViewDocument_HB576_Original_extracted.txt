STATE DEPARTMENT: Transfers the Old Governor's Mansion to the Department of State
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 49:141 and to enact Chapter 5-T of Title 25 of the Louisiana
3 Revised Statutes of 1950, to be comprised of R.S. 25:380.171 through 380.176,
4 relative to the Old Governor's Mansion; to transfer the Old Governor's Mansion to
5 the Department of State; to provide for the powers, duties, functions, and
6 responsibilities of the secretary of state and the department; to provide relative to
7 operations and funding; to provide for effectiveness; and to provide for related
8 matters.
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. Chapter 5-T of Title 25 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
11 comprised of R.S. 25:380.171 through 380.176, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
12 CHAPTER 5-T. OLD GOVERNOR'S MANSION
13 §380.171. Definitions
14 For the purposes of this Chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings
15 indicated unless the context clearly provides otherwise:
16 (1) "Department" means the Department of State.
17 (2) "Secretary" means the secretary of state.

1 §380.172. Establishment and location; purpose and use
2 A. The Old Governor's Mansion is established as a facility in the City of
3 Baton Rouge under the overall jurisdiction of the Department of State, as more
4 specifically provided in this Chapter.
5 B. The secretary of state shall have custody, control, and supervision of
6 maintenance of the Old Governor's Mansion and its contents. All of the
7 management, functions, programs, and operations of every kind of the Old
8 Governor's Mansion shall be exercised and performed by the secretary.
9 C. For purposes of administration and supervision of the Old Governor's
10 Mansion, the secretary shall have full authority over its grounds and all other
11 buildings and improvements constructed thereon, and shall control any use made
12 thereof and the space therein.
13 D. The Old Governor's Mansion shall be a historical, cultural, and
14 educational institution whose primary purpose shall be to collect, preserve, and
15 present, as an educational resource, documents, artifacts, objects of art, and the like,
16 that reflect the politics, history, art, and culture of Louisiana.
17 §380.173. Operations
18 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary and in particular the
19 provisions of R.S. 41:1212, the secretary may enter into contracts and agreements
20 with any public agency or nonprofit corporation which has been established for the
21 primary purpose of supporting the programs and activities of the Old Governor's
22 Mansion for the establishment of offices, stores, or exhibits on the property of and
23 in the Old Governor's Mansion. The secretary may also enter into contracts and
24 agreements for joint construction, equipment, maintenance, and financing thereof,
25 and may enter into contracts and agreements for the joint financing, supervision, and
26 conduct of cooperative enterprises and undertakings. Any public agency may enter
27 into contracts and agreements with the secretary for the purposes mentioned in this
28 Section.

1 §380.174. Operating Funds

4 B.(1) If sufficient funds necessary for the operation of the Old Governor's
5 Mansion are not appropriated by the legislature, and funds from other local, public,

27 provisions of R.S. 9:2341 through 2347.

HB 576 Original 2026 Regular Session Gadberry
Abstract: Transfers the Old Governor's Mansion to the Dept. of State.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(A)) creates that Division of State Buildings in the office of the
governor. Provides that the division is administered by a superintendent of state buildings
appointed by the governor. Provides that the superintendent shall have charge of the
management, operation, and maintenance of general public buildings of the state, including
both the old and new governor's mansions.
Proposed law removes the Old Governor's Mansion from its inclusion among the general
public buildings of the state that are under the jurisdiction of the superintendent of the
Division of State Buildings. Otherwise retains present law.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(B)) provides that the Division of State Buildings shall have
custody and supervision of maintenance of the Old Governor's Mansion with its buildings
and improvements. Provides that the superintendent of state buildings shall maintain the
building and grounds with money appropriated by the legislature for such purposes.
Proposed law repeals present law.
Present law (R.S. 49:141(C)) provides for the management and control of the property of the
Old Governor's Mansion by the commissioner of administration. Authorizes the
commissioner to transfer custody, supervision, and general administrative control to any
public agency or non-profit corporation with sufficient resources to preserve the mansion
and its contents. Proposed law repeals present law.
Proposed law establishes the Old Governor's Mansion under the overall jurisdiction of the
Dept. of State. Provides that the secretary of state shall have custody, control, and
supervision of maintenance of the building and its contents, including all of its management,
functions, programs, and operations. Provides that the secretary shall have full authority over
the building's grounds and all other buildings and improvements constructed thereon.
Proposed law provides that the Old Governor's Mansion shall function as a historical,
cultural, and educational institution with a primary purpose to collect, preserve, and present
documents, artifacts, and objects of art that reflect the politics, history, art, and culture of La.
Proposed law authorizes the secretary of state to enter into contracts and agreements with
public agencies or nonprofit corporations that are established for the primary purpose of
supporting the programs and activities of the Old Governor's Mansion for the creation of
offices, stores, or exhibits on the property of and within the building. Additionally authorizes
the secretary to enter into contracts and agreements for joint construction, equipment,
maintenance, and financing thereof, and for the joint financing, supervision, and conduct of
cooperative enterprises and undertakings. Authorizes any public agency to enter into
contracts and agreements with the secretary for such purposes.
Proposed law requires the legislature to provide sufficient funds for the proper operation of
the Old Governor's Mansion. Authorizes the Dept. of State to temporarily close the building

Proposed law provides a procedure for acquiring property deposited with the Old Governor's
Mansion, by loan or otherwise, which has been in its possession for more than 10 years
without claim.
Proposed law authorizes, subject to approval by the secretary of state, the collections of the
Old Governor's Mansion to be lent in part. Requires the secretary to ensure proper
