HB 1057 Original 2026 Regular Session Walters
Abstract: Provides that an application to vote absentee by mail made by a member of the
U.S. Service who is stationed or deployed outside of the U.S. remains valid until he
is no longer stationed or deployed outside of the U.S.
Present law (R.S. 18:1307(C)) provides for the application by mail to vote absentee for a
member of the U.S. Service or someone who resides outside of the U.S. Provides the
member of the U.S. Service or person residing outside of the U.S. may use the federal
postcard application or an application electronically transmitted by the registrar or sec. of
state, and the application shall be received by the registrar no later than 4:30 p.m. on the day
before the election. Provides that such application shall be valid for a period extending at
least one year from the date the application is received in the office of the registrar of voters;
such period shall include at least one regularly scheduled federal general election. Further
provides that if the registrar rejects the application of an applicant who is a member of the
U.S. Service or resides outside of the U.S., the registrar shall provide the applicant with
written reasons for the rejection.
Proposed law retains present law.
