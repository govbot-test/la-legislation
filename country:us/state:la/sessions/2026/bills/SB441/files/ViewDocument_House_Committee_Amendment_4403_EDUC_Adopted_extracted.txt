10 "(2) The child-to-staff ratios shall not exceed the following:
11 (a) Infants and under one year - 5 to 1
12 (b) One year - 7 to 1
13 (c) Two years - 10 to 1
14 (d) Three years - 13 to 1
15 (e) Four years - 15 to 1
16 (f) Five years - 19 to 1
17 (2)(a) The child-to-staff ratios shall be in compliance with those set in
18 rules that the State Board of Elementary of Education shall adopt for this
19 purpose. The rules shall provide ratio requirements for prekindergarten
20 students who are three years old, four years old, and five years old in order to
21 provide for their health, safety, and welfare. Both public and nonpublic schools
22 operating prekindergarten programs shall comply with the ratio requirements
23 provided in these rules.
24 (b) Such rules may be waived if the state board determines, upon clear
25 and convincing evidence, that the demonstrated economic impact is sufficiently
26 great to make compliance impractical for the school despite diligent efforts, and
27 that alternative means have been put in place that ensure the health, safety, and
28 well-being of students and staff."

32 On page 6, delete lines 15 through 25 and insert the following:
33 "(6) Only staff members directly providing care, supervision, or guidance to
34 children shall be counted in the child-to-staff ratio. The same staff members shall
35 not be used to meet the ratio requirements for two different groups of children at the
36 same time.
37 (7) Sufficient staffing needed to satisfy child-to-staff ratios shall be present
38 on the premises during rest time and available to assist as needed. Children ages one
39 and older may be grouped together at rest time with one staff member in each room
40 supervising the resting children.
