1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 17:11(B), 24.8(A), (B), and (D)(1), 407.33(1) and (5),
3 407.35(A), and 407.41(A), (B)(2) and (3), and (C) and to enact R.S. 17:24.8(E) and
4 407.41(D), (E), and (F), relative to programs for prekindergarten-aged children; to
5 provide for nonpublic and public prekindergarten programs; to provide for approval
6 of nonpublic prekindergarten programs; to provide for child safety and welfare
7 standards; to provide for violations; to provide for camps; to provide for exemptions;
8 to provide for rulemaking; to provide for terms, definitions, conditions, and
9 procedures; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
10 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
11 Section 1. R.S. 17:11(B), 24.8(A), (B), and (D)(1), 407.33(1) and (5), 407.35(A),
12 407.41(A), (B)(2) and (3), and (C) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 17:24.8(E)
13 and 407.41(D), (E), and (F) are hereby enacted to read as follows:
14 §11. Approval of nonpublic schools by board
15 * * *
16 B.(1) The board shall approve any nonpublic elementary or secondary school
17 which makes application therefor on a form furnished by the board, if such school
18 meets and maintains a sustained curriculum or specialized course of study of quality
19 at least equal to that prescribed for similar public schools.
20 (2) The board shall approve a prekindergarten program developed by
21 a nonpublic school pursuant to R.S. 17:24.8 if the program meets the
22 requirements of this Subsection and maintains substantial compliance with the
23 child safety and welfare standards provided for in R.S. 17:407.41. In its annual
24 application, each nonpublic school shall attest to the prekindergarten program's
25 compliance with the child safety and welfare standards.

20 A. The provisions of this Part shall not apply to grades kindergarten and

1 All school prekindergarten programs established pursuant to R.S. 17:24.8 shall,
2 at a minimum, meet the following minimum standards to ensure the safety and

4 (1) There shall be a minimum of two staff members present at any facility
5 when more than four children are present, except under an extenuating circumstance
6 that temporarily prevents compliance with this Paragraph.
7 (2) The child-to-staff ratios shall not exceed the following:
8 (a) Infants and under one year - 5 to 1
9 (b) One year - 7 to 1
10 (c) Two years - 10 to 1
11 (d) Three years - 13 to 1
12 (e) Four years - 15 to 1
13 (f) Five years - 19 to 1
14 (a) The child-to-staff ratios shall be in compliance with those set in rules
15 that the State Board of Elementary and Secondary Education shall adopt for
16 this purpose. The rules shall provide ratio requirements for prekindergarten
17 students who are three years old, four years old, and five years old in order to
18 provide for the health, safety, and welfare of those students. Both public and
19 nonpublic schools operating prekindergarten programs shall comply with the
20 ratio requirements provided in these rules.
21 (b) The rules may be waived if the state board determines, upon clear
22 and convincing evidence, that the demonstrated economic impact is sufficiently
23 great to make compliance impractical for the school despite diligent efforts, and
24 that alternative means have been put in place that ensure the health, safety, and
25 well-being of students and staff.
26 (3) An average of the child-to-staff ratios may be applied to mixed age
27 groups of children only for groups that include no children under the age of two.
28 (4) When a mixed age group includes children younger than age two, the age
29 of the youngest child determines the child-to-staff ratio for the group.
