1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 47:337.51(A)(1), the introductory paragraph of (2), and (3), and

13 for applicability; and to provide for related matters.

11 A. Having assessed the amount determined to be due, the secretary shall send

26 International with Electronic United States Postal Service Delivery Confirmation.

10 §1625. Appeals from the collector's disallowance of refund claim
11 A.(1) If the collector fails to act on a properly filed claim for refund or credit
12 within one year from the date received by him or if the collector denies the claim in
13 whole or in part, the taxpayer claiming such the refund or credit may appeal to the
14 Board of Tax Appeals for a hearing on the claim filed. No appeal may be filed before
15 the expiration of one year from the date of filing such of the claim unless the
16 collector renders a decision thereon within that time, nor after the expiration of sixty
17 ninety days from the date of mailing by registered certified mail by the collector to
18 the taxpayer of a notice of the disallowance of the part of the claim to which such the
19 appeal relates.
20 (2) A taxpayer's proper appeal to the Board of Tax Appeals within sixty
21 ninety days from the date on any notice of disallowance issued shall also establish
22 that the appeal was filed within sixty ninety days from the date of certified or
23 registered mailing of the notice.
24 B. A notice of disallowance, if issued, shall inform the taxpayer that he has
25 sixty ninety days from the date of the certified or registered mailing of that notice
26 to appeal to the Board of Tax Appeals, and that any consideration, reconsideration,
27 or action by the collector with respect to such the claim following the mailing of a
28 notice by registered certified mail of disallowance shall not operate to extend the
29 period within which an appeal may be taken.
