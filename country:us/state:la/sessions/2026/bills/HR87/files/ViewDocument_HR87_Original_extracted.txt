HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 87
BY REPRESENTATIVE DEVILLIER
of Roger D. Boudreaux
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Roger D.
3 Boudreaux.
4 WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
5 Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on March 28,
6 2026, of Roger D. Boudreaux; and
7 WHEREAS, Mr. Boudreaux served as mayor of Church Point, Louisiana, for sixteen
8 years and was an energetic supporter of the town, its schools, and the local Filipino
9 community; he also cherished following his descendants' sports competitions; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. Boudreaux was a member of the Bears Booster Club for over thirty
11 years; he was a founder and twenty-two-year member of Cajun Woodstock; and
12 WHEREAS, Roger D. Boudreaux is survived by his wife, Seina Campos Boudreaux;
13 daughter, Stephanie Aguillard; sons, Nathan Boudreaux (Lisa) and Blake Boudreaux (Skie);
14 "bonus" daughter, Mia Dominguez (Jesus); grandchildren, Drake Boudreaux, Hannah
15 Boudreaux, Gabrielle Boudreaux, Josh Boudreaux, Caiden Boudreaux, Landon Boudreaux,
16 Bentley Boudreaux, Liam Boudreaux, Dylan Stelly, Destiny Stelly, and Slade Stelly; and
17 "bonus" grandchildren, Haven Dominguez, Dontae Dominguez, Izaiah Dominguez, and
18 Andria Dominguez; he will be deeply missed, but the legacy of his love and service to the
19 town of Church Point shall live on in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged to
20 have known and loved him.

HR NO. 87
1 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
2 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Roger
3 D. Boudreaux; does hereby offer enduring appreciation for his contributions to the Church
4 Point community; and does hereby extend sincere wishes that his family, friends, and loved
5 ones find comfort in their shared memories of his beautiful life.
