HLS 26RS-5540 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 283
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
SPECIAL DAY/WEEK/MONTH: Commends Jewish-Americans in Louisiana on their
contributions to the state and nation, recognizes May 2026 as Jewish-American
Heritage Month, and encourages observance of Shabbat on May 15-16, 2026
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend Jewish-Americans in Louisiana for their contributions to the state and nation;
3 to recognize May 2026 as Jewish-American Heritage Month in Louisiana; and to join
4 with other states in encouraging Christians and persons of all faith traditions to join
5 with their Jewish neighbors in observing a Shabbat from sundown on May 15, 2026,
6 through nightfall on May 16, 2026, as a time of prayer, reflection, gratitude, and
7 thanksgiving to Almighty God in celebration of the two hundred fiftieth anniversary
8 of the United States.
9 WHEREAS, on May 4, 2026, President Donald J. Trump issued a proclamation
10 recognizing May 2026 as Jewish-American Heritage Month, calling upon the nation in
11 anticipation of the two hundred fiftieth anniversary of the United States of America, to
12 observe a national Shabbat from sundown on May 15, 2026, through nightfall on May 16,
13 2026, as a time of prayer, reflection, rest, gratitude, and thanksgiving to Almighty God, and
14 as an opportunity for families and communities to gather in recognition of the blessings of
15 faith, freedom, and national unity; and
16 WHEREAS, Jewish-Americans have contributed immeasurably to the cultural,
17 economic, scientific, educational, military, and civic development of the United States since
18 the founding of the nation; and
19 WHEREAS, among the early patriots who supported the cause of American liberty
20 and independence was Haym Salomon, a Jewish patriot and financier who played a critical

1 role in supporting the Continental Congress and aiding the Revolutionary War effort through
2 financial services and personal sacrifice, helping to sustain the cause of liberty during the
3 nation's founding struggle for freedom; and
4 WHEREAS, President George Washington in his historic 1790 letter to the Hebrew
5 Congregation in Newport, Rhode Island, articulated one of the enduring principles of
6 American religious liberty by declaring that the United States "gives to bigotry no sanction,
7 to persecution no assistance", a statement that continues to reflect the nation's commitment
8 to religious freedom and equal protection under the law for all people; and
9 WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana is enriched by Jewish citizens and institutions that
10 have contributed to the arts, business, medicine, law, education, charitable endeavors, public
11 leadership, and the preservation of religious liberty throughout the state's history; and
12 WHEREAS, Christians acknowledge with gratitude the enduring witness and
13 heritage of the Jewish people, through whom the Scriptures were preserved and through
14 whose long historical experience and testimony the broader principles of faith, religious
15 identity, and religious liberty have been strengthened and advanced; and
16 WHEREAS, Israel and in particular Jerusalem, Judea, and Samaria are historically
17 and Biblically significant regions of Israel that hold profound religious and cultural
18 importance in the shared heritage of Judaism and Christianity and are among the principal
19 places where the Holy Bible records many events of the Hebrew prophets and the life and
20 ministry of Jesus Christ; and
21 WHEREAS, the enduring influence of Israel, and especially Jerusalem, Judea, and
22 Samaria, is reflected throughout Louisiana and the United States in the names of
23 communities, churches, religious institutions, and places inspired by the history, geography,
24 and traditions of the Holy Land and our shared Judeo-Christian heritage; and
25 WHEREAS, May is nationally recognized as Jewish-American Heritage Month, first
26 proclaimed in 2006 and observed annually to celebrate the accomplishments and heritage
27 of Jewish Americans; and
28 WHEREAS, in further recognition of Jewish-American Heritage Month and in
29 anticipation of the upcoming two hundred fiftieth anniversary of the United States of
30 America, it is appropriate to join with other states across the nation in encouraging
