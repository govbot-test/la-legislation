DIGEST
SB 102 Original 2026 Regular Session Pressly
Present law provides for licensing and compliance requirements by the Louisiana Motor
Vehicle Commission for all license applicants and holders and that no license shall be
transferable. Proposed law provides for an exception where a license may be transferable;
such as when affiliated dealers are under common ownership or control; or when a motor
vehicle salesman or a motor vehicle lessor agent is employed with multiple affiliated dealers
with common ownership.
Present law establishes licensing and compliance requirements for manufacturers,
distributors, wholesalers, converters or secondary manufacturers, distributors or wholesalers,
factory branches and distributor branches. Proposed law removes provisions that would
require a new license and application including a change in the name of a licensee, a change
by a licensee in the makes, models, or classifications, requiring an addendum to a franchise
agreement, of motor vehicles, recreational products, or specialty vehicles manufactured,
distributed, or converted, or the addition of a make of motor vehicle or recreational product
manufactured, distributed, or converted shall require a new license and application.
Proposed law further provides a change of location, corporate ownership or majority
ownership or change in the name of a licensee only require the licensee to file an amendment
to the existing license.
Present law provides for additional licensing and compliance requirements for motor vehicle
and recreational products dealers. Present law further provides applications for a license as
a motor vehicle and recreational products dealer must also include a contract or franchise
in effect between the applicant and a manufacturer or distributor of the new motor vehicle
in the state of Louisiana. Proposed law adds a provision that the contract must also identify
the individual named as a dealer-operator.
Present law requires the application to identify the individual named as dealer-operator and
provide information as the commission deems necessary. Proposed law retains present law
and provides for technical matters.
Present law provides for fees and penalties. Proposed law retains present law and also
provides for licenses and temporary licenses.
Present law provides for defraying the cost of issuing licenses and reasonable fees assessed
by the commission in the amounts of:
(1) A maximum of $1000 for each manufacturer, distributor, converter, motor vehicle

(2) A maximum of $300 for each motor vehicle dealer, specialty vehicle dealer,

(3) A maximum of $30 license fee for each motor vehicle salesman and motor vehicle

Proposed law retains present law amounts and adds the cost of amending licenses and
administering the provisions of the law and provides for technical matters.
Present law provides that persons failing to be licensed as required by the commission will
pay a penalty that may be waived by the commission. Proposed law retains present law and
provides for technical changes.
Proposed law provides for a licensed motor vehicle dealer in this state who is aggrieved by
a violation to institute a civil action against any manufacturer, distributor, or other person.
Proposed law further allows a trade association that meets certain requirements to bring a
civil action or intervene in a civil action in its own name or on behalf of one or more of its
members for declaratory or injunctive relief.
Proposed law provides for remedies for motor vehicle dealer prevailing pursuant to proposed
law of declaratory relief, injunctive relief, actual damages and treble damages, upon proof
by clear and convincing evidence that the violation was willful, malicious, or in bad faith
and reasonable costs and attorney fees. Proposed law further provides a prevailing trade
association may be granted any of the following remedies: declaratory relief, injunctive
relief, and reasonable costs and attorney fees.
Proposed law provides a civil action pursuant to proposed law may be brought before the
commission or in any district court of competent jurisdiction. Proposed law further provides
exhaustion of administrative remedies is not required prior to filing a civil action in district
court.
Proposed law provides the district courts have concurrent jurisdiction to hear any claim
arising pursuant to proposed law.
