Section 1:
HLS 26RS-197 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE MIKE JOHNSON
UTILITIES: Creates the Broadband and Cable Price Notice Act
1 AN ACT
2 To enact Chapter 12-B of Title 51 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to be
3 comprised of R.S. 51:1381 through 1389, relative to the Broadband and Cable Price
4 Notice Act; to provide for definitions; to provide for written notice; to provide for
5 method of delivery; to provide for the rights of customers; to provide for
6 enforcement; to provide for authority; to provide for rulemaking; to provide for
7 applicability; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. Chapter 12-B of Title 51 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
10 comprised of R.S. 51:1381 through 1389, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 CHAPTER 12-B. THE BROADBAND AND CABLE PRICE NOTICE ACT
12 §1381. Short title
13 This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Broadband and Cable
14 Price Notice Act."
15 §1382. Definitions
16 (1) "Broadband internet access service" means a mass-market retail service
17 that provides the capability to transmit data to and receive data from substantially all
18 internet endpoints, including any functional equivalents.

1 (2) "Cable or video service" means the one-way transmission to subscribers
2 of video programming or other programming service by a cable operator or video
3 service provider, including services provided pursuant to a state or local franchise.
4 (3) "Customer" means a natural person who receives cable or video service
5 or broadband internet access service at a residential address in this state.
6 (4) "Price increase" means any increase to a customer's monthly recurring
7 charge or mandatory fee for service, including but not limited to base service
8 charges, equipment rental fees, modem or router fees, "broadcast TV" or "regional
9 sports" network access, or similar surcharges. "Price increase" does not include
10 taxes or fees imposed by federal, state, or local government.
11 (5) "Provider" means any person or entity that offers cable or video service
12 or broadband internet access service to residential customers in this state, whether
13 pursuant to a local franchise, a state-issued certificate, or otherwise.
14 (6) "Written notice" includes delivery by United States mail, email, or text
15 message, if the customer has elected to receive billing or account communications
16 through that channel.
17 §1383. Advance notice required; timing
18 A. A provider shall furnish clear and conspicuous written notice to each
19 affected customer at least thirty calendar days before the effective date of any price
20 increase.
21 B. When a price increase results from circumstances outside the provider's
22 reasonable control including but not limited to programmer pass-through charges
23 imposed with less than thirty days' notice to the provider, the provider shall give
24 written notice as soon as practicable, but in no event later than the customer's next
25 regular billing cycle after the provider learns of the change.
26 C. A customer may cancel or downgrade the affected service without early
27 termination fees or other penalties triggered solely by the price increase, if the
28 customer gives notice of cancellation on or before the effective date or within thirty
29 days after receiving a written notice pursuant to this Section.

1 D. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to shorten or limit any federal
2 notice requirement that provides greater advance notice for cable service.
3 §1384. Content of notice
4 A. The written notice described in this Chapter shall include all of the
5 following:
6 (1) The customer's current monthly recurring charges and each new charge
7 and fee, itemized by line item.
8 (2) The effective date of the increase.
9 (3) A brief statement of the reason for the increase.
10 (4) A statement of the customer's right to cancel or downgrade the affected
11 service without early termination fees or other penalties triggered solely by the price
12 increase, provided the customer gives notice of cancellation on or before the
13 effective date or within thirty days after receiving a written notice in accordance with
14 R.S. 51:1383.
15 (5) A toll-free telephone number and web address where a customer may
16 obtain additional information or exercise cancellation and downgrade rights.
17 B. The written notice outlined in this Chapter shall be sent to a customer
18 separately from any bill from the provider.
19 §1385. Method of delivery; recordkeeping
20 A. Written notice may be provided by any channel the customer has selected
21 for billing or account communications; if no election exists, notice shall be sent by
22 United States mail to the service address or billing address on file.
23 B. A provider shall retain records sufficient to demonstrate compliance with
24 this Chapter, including samples of notices and logs of the dates and channels used,
25 for two years from the effective date of each price increase.
26 §1386. Customer rights preserved
27 A. A provider shall not treat a customer's exercise of rights pursuant to R.S.
28 51:1384 as a breach of contract or impose any fee or penalty triggered solely by the
29 customer's cancellation or downgrade in response to a price increase.

1 B. Nothing in this Chapter prohibits a provider from recovering unreturned
2 equipment charges or other amounts lawfully owed that are not early termination
3 fees.
4 §1387. Enforcement; unfair trade practice; remedies
5 A. A violation of this Chapter is an unfair or deceptive trade practice in
6 accordance with the Louisiana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law,
7 R.S. 51:1401 et seq.
8 B. The attorney general may investigate and bring actions to enforce this
9 Chapter including civil penalties, injunctive relief, restitution, and attorneys' fees as
10 authorized by Louisiana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law, R.S.
11 51:1401 et seq.
12 C. The remedies provided by this Chapter are in addition to any other
13 remedies available at law or in equity, including private actions authorized by
14 Louisiana Unfair Trade Practices and Consumer Protection Law, R.S. 51:1401 et
15 seq.
16 §1388. Construction; federal and local law
17 A. This Chapter is a disclosure and customer-notice requirement only and
18 shall not be construed as rate regulation or to require any specific price, term, or
19 offering.
20 B. For cable or video services, this Chapter shall be construed in harmony
21 with federal law, including 47 U.S.C. 521 et seq. and 47 CFR 76.1603, and shall not
22 diminish any federal or franchise-authority notice obligations that are equal to or
23 greater than.
24 C. Nothing in this Chapter shall limit the authority of a local franchise
25 authority to adopt or enforce customer-service standards or notice provisions, if a
26 provider that complies with the more stringent applicable requirement is deemed in
27 compliance with this Chapter.

1 §1389. Rulemaking
2 The attorney general may promulgate rules under the Administrative
3 Procedure Act to implement the provisions of this Chapter, including rules
4 specifying reasonable notice formats and record keeping standards.
5 Section 2. This Act applies to price increases first noticed to customers on or after
6 the effective date of this Act. It does not require re-noticing of increases that took effect
7 before the effective date of this Act.
8 Section 3. This Act shall become effective on January 1, 2027.
