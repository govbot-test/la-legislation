Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
BY SENATOR MILLER AND REPRESENTATIVE CHASSION
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact the introductory paragraph of R.S. 13:10.3(D) and 103 and to enact
3 R.S. 13:10.3(D)(3) and (4), relative to funding of the Judges' Supplemental
4 Compensation Fund; to provide relative to public retirement systems; to provide
5 relative to certain justices, judges, and commissioners; to provide relative to funding
6 for certain reserves; to provide for the reimbursement of unvouchered expenses; to
7 provide for the adjustment of the amount payable from self-generated funds; to
8 provide for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. The introductory paragraph of R.S. 13:10.3(D) and 103 are hereby
11 amended and reenacted and R.S. 13:10.3(D)(3) and (4) are hereby enacted to read as follows:
12 §10.3. Judges' Supplemental Compensation Fund; creation; sources of funds
13 * * *
14 D. After making provisions for necessary and associated administrative
15 expenses, including those invoiced by the judicial administrator for services
16 actually provided, the board shall authorize the judicial administrator to set aside
17 and transmit monthly an amount to provide the additional employer's retirement
18 contribution due by the state on the supplemental compensation to the relevant State
19 Employees' Retirement System on behalf of the judges who are members of the a
20 public retirement system. The board, through the judicial administrator, shall then
21 distribute the proceeds from the fund monthly, as follows:
22 * * *

SB NO. 232 ENROLLED
1 (3) The provisions of this Subsection shall apply only to a justice, judge,
2 or commissioner serving in an office listed in Paragraph (D)(1) or (2) who has
3 either served in any of those offices on or before December 31, 2026, or has
4 served in any of those offices for at least three years after December 31, 2026.
5 (4) Any savings as a result of the provisions of this Subsection shall not
6 be used to increase the amounts provided for under this Subsection to any
7 amount higher than the amount paid during Fiscal Year 2025-26 and shall be
8 retained to provide a reserve to maintain the stability of the fund. The reserve
9 may be used during any reduction in the fund's receipts to maintain stability in
10 payments.
11 * * *
12 §103. Expenses of chief justice and associate justices of the supreme court;
13 reimbursement
14 A. The chief justice and each associate justice of the supreme court shall be
15 reimbursed for reasonable housing and other otherwise unreimbursed
16 unvouchered expenses incurred while on official duty, payable out of the self-
17 generated funds provided for in R.S. 13:124 in amounts not to exceed the amounts
18 allocated under the authority of this Section as of May 1, 1989.
19 B. The allowance payable from self-generated funds as provided for in
20 this Section shall be adjusted for inflation on January first of each year by the
21 change in the consumer price index - U.S. city average for all urban consumers
22 (CPI-U) as prepared by the United States Department of Labor, Bureau of
23 Labor Statistics, applied annually, and shall reflect the change, if any, since the
24 amount was last modified, provided that any adjustment in a monthly amount
25 shall be made only in whole increments of one hundred dollars.
26 Section 2. Section 1 of this Act shall become effective upon signature by the
27 governor or, if not signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become
28 law without signature by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the
29 Constitution of Louisiana. If vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the
30 legislature, this Act shall become effective on the day following such approval.
