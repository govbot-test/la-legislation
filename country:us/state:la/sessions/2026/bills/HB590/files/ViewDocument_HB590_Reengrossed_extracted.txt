HB 590 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Glorioso
Abstract: Authorizes the Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of motor
vehicles, to create specially designed envelopes for improving traffic stop
communication between law enforcement and those with certain disorders and
disabilities.
Present law authorizes the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections (DPS&C), office of motor
vehicles (OMV), to issue identification stickers or decals to symbolize awareness for certain
disabilities, at no cost to the driver or parent or legal guardian.
Proposed law authorizes the DPS&C, OMV, to create specially designed envelopes to be
provided to drivers with mental, physical, developmental disabilities, or autism spectrum
disorder, or their parents or legal guardians, that provide proof as required by present law.
Specifies that the envelopes include guidelines to assist officers in recognizing and
effectively communicating with drivers with disabilities during traffic stops.
(Adds R.S. 32:403.3(B)(2)(c))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Transportation,
Highways and Public Works to the original bill:
1. Make technical changes.
2. Add the requirement that the Department of Public Safety and Corrections,
office of motor vehicles, create specially designed envelopes to be provided
to drivers with mental, physical, developmental disabilities, or autism
spectrum disorder, or their parents or legal guardians, that provide proof of
the disability as required by present law. Specifies that the envelopes include
guidelines to assist officers in recognizing and effectively communicating
with drivers with disabilities during traffic stops.
The House Floor Amendments to the engrossed bill:
1. Change the Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of motor
vehicles, creation of specially designed envelopes from mandatory to
permissive.
2. Remove the provisions that created the Green and Blue Envelop Programs.
3. Remove the upon signature of the governor effective date.
4. Make technical changes.
