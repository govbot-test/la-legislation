HOUSE COMMITTEE AMENDMENTS
2026 Regular Session
Committee on Administration of Criminal Justice
This document reflects the content of a substitute bill but is not in a bill form; page
numbers in this document DO NOT correspond to page numbers in the substitute bill
itself.
1 To amend and reenact R.S. 14:100(C)(2) and (3), relative to driving offenses; to provide
2 relative to the crime of hit-and-run driving; to provide for penalties; and to provide
3 for related matters.
4 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
5 Section 1. R.S. 14:100(C)(2) and (3) are hereby amended and reenacted to read as
6 follows:
7 §100. Hit-and-run driving
8 * * *
9 C.
10 * * *
11 (2)(a) Whoever commits the crime of hit-and-run driving when death or
12 serious bodily injury is a direct result of the accident, when the driver knew or should
13 have known that death or serious bodily injury has occurred, and the driver's vehicle
14 was directly involved in the accident, shall be fined not more than five thousand
15 dollars or imprisoned with or without hard labor for not less than two years nor more
16 than ten years, two of which shall be served without the benefit of parole, probation,
17 or suspension of sentence, or both.
18 (b) If the victim is a pedestrian or a bicyclist, at least two years of the
19 sentence imposed shall be served without the benefit of parole, probation, or
20 suspension of sentence.
21 (3)(a) Whoever commits the crime of hit-and-run driving, where all of the
22 following conditions are met, shall be imprisoned, with or without hard labor, for not
23 less than five years nor more than twenty years:
24 (a) (i) Death or serious bodily injury is a direct result of the accident.

1 (b) (ii) The driver knew or must have known that the vehicle he was
2 operating was involved in an accident or that his operation of the vehicle was the
3 direct cause of an accident.
4 (c) (iii) The driver had been previously convicted of any of the following:
5 (i) (aa) A violation of R.S. 14:98, or a law or an ordinance of any state or
6 political subdivision prohibiting operation of any vehicle or means of transportation
7 or conveyance while intoxicated, impaired, or while under the influence of alcohol,
8 drugs, or any controlled dangerous substance on two or more occasions within ten
9 years of this offense.
10 (ii) (bb) A violation of R.S. 14:32.1-vehicular homicide. (vehicular
11 homicide)
12 (iii) (cc) A violation of R.S. 14:39.1-vehicular negligent injuring. (vehicular
13 negligent injuring)
14 (iv) (dd) A violation of R.S. 14:39.2-first degree vehicular negligent injuring.
15 (first degree vehicular negligent injuring)
16 (b) If the victim is a pedestrian or a bicyclist, at least five years of the
17 sentence imposed shall be served without the benefit of parole, probation, or
18 suspension of sentence.
19 Section 2. This Act shall be cited and referred to as "The Christina Larsen Act".

HB Draft 2026 Regular Session
Abstract: Provides relative to penalties for hit-and-run driving.
Present law provides for the crime of hit-and-run driving and provides for penalties.
Proposed law retains present law and provides for penalties as follows:
(1) For a violation of present law (R.S. 14:100(C)(2)), a requirement that at least two
years of the sentence imposed be served without the benefit of parole, probation, or
suspension of sentence if the victim is a pedestrian or a bicyclist.

Proposed law shall be cited and referred to as "The Christina Larsen Act".
