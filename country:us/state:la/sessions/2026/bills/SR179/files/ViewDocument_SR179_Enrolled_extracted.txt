WHEREAS, the Louisiana Department of Health's Safe Drinking Water Program
enforces the drinking water regulations for the state and provides several ways to search
water quality and regulatory information; and
WHEREAS, in 2021, the legislature passed "The Community Drinking Water
Infrastructure Sustainability Act" which requires the Louisiana Department of Health Safe
Drinking Water Program to provide for a statewide system of accountability for community
water systems and establish a letter grade schedule reflective of community water system
quality, performance, and sustainability; and
WHEREAS, the law provides for seven standards evaluating the infrastructure,
accountability, and overall health risk of drinking water to consumers, but gave the
department flexibility in allocating individual point values for each of the standards through
rulemaking; and
WHEREAS, the standards and point values include:
(1) Federal water quality violations - 30 points.
(2) State water quality violations - 10 points.
(3) Financial sustainability - 10 points.
(4) Operation and maintenance performance - 15 points.
(5) Infrastructure violations - 20 points.
(6) Customer satisfaction - 10 points.
(7) Level of and secondary contaminants - 5 points.
WHEREAS, water systems also have the opportunity to earn up to 10 bonus points
for things including an asset management plan, storage assessment and maintenance
program, well assessment and maintenance program, participation in a capacity development

program, or management training program; and
WHEREAS, in May 2026, the department published the fourth annual community
water system grades offering a transparent look at the quality and performance of more than
nine hundred systems serving Louisiana residents; and
WHEREAS, the May 2026 report shows continued progress statewide, with fewer
water systems receiving failing grades and more systems demonstrating measurable
improvements in performance; and
WHEREAS, for consumers, notice of a poor water system grade does not imply
unsafe water and does not pose an immediate health risk, but rather can indicate significant
concerns with the long-term viability of a water system that requires major improvements
or upgrades in order to continuously provide safe drinking water; and
WHEREAS, the most noticeable factor for consumers is the color and odor of their
drinking water, which is considered under the customer satisfaction and secondary
contaminants factors; and
WHEREAS, the maximum number of points that a water system can have deducted
for color and odor is 15 points; and
WHEREAS, it is appropriate after compiling four years of water system grades for
the department to conduct a review of the Community Water System Accountability letter
grade point values and make modifications as needed to ensure the public is getting an
accurate and reliable letter grade for their water systems, including whether the point values
allocated for each standard should be modified.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to review the Community
Water System Accountability letter grade point values and make modifications as needed
to ensure the public is getting an accurate and reliable letter grade for Louisiana water
systems.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health shall submit
a report of its review and any modifications made to the grading system to the Senate
Committee on Health and Welfare and the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library,
as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772, no later than March 1, 2027.

rulemaking; and
WHEREAS, the standards and point values include:
(1) Federal water quality violations - 30 points.
(2) State water quality violations - 10 points.
(3) Financial sustainability - 10 points.
(4) Operation and maintenance performance - 15 points.
(5) Infrastructure violations - 20 points.
(6) Customer satisfaction - 10 points.
(7) Level of and secondary contaminants - 5 points.

for things including an asset management plan, storage assessment and maintenance
