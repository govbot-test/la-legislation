Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:

Section 2:
Section 1. R.S. 56:129 is hereby enacted to read as follows:

Section 3:
§129. Possession of white-tailed deer; prohibitions
Wildlife rehabilitators permitted pursuant to LAC 76:V.131 may possess
white-tailed deer subject to the following provisions:
(1) Rehabilitation of white-tailed deer originating from outside of Louisiana
is strictly prohibited.
(2) The rehabilitation of injured or orphaned white-tailed deer originating
from within a Chronic Wasting Disease (CWD) Management Zone shall be
permitted; however, any such deer shall be rehabbed and released only within the
boundaries of the same CWD Management Zone from which the animal originated
(a) Any person or entity engaged in the rehabilitation of such deer shall be
strictly responsible for the monitoring of the animal's health during rehabilitation and
shall maintain a diligent watch for any neurological symptoms or other indicators
consistent with CWD.
(b) Failure to comply with the provisions of this Paragraph shall constitute
a violation subject to penalties as provided by law, and may result in the revocation
of any applicable rehabilitation permit.
(3) Any animal that displays abnormal neurological symptoms shall be
euthanized and a CWD sample submitted to the department. Until a negative CWD
result is received all deer within the facility shall remain quarantined.

(4) If any deer from a rehabilitation facility receives a positive CWD result,
all live white-tailed deer shall be euthanized and CWD samples collected. That
rehabilitation facility shall be prohibited from participating in deer rehabilitation
activities in the future.
(5) Any white-tailed deer displaying open fractures, neurological symptoms,
or symptoms of epizootic hemorrhagic disease, or blue tongue, are ineligible for
rehabilitation.
(6) Any white-tailed deer that display signs of being habituated to humans
is ineligible for rehabilitation.
(7) The minimum enclosure size shall be fifteen feet by twenty feet by eight
feet for a single fawn. The pen shall be increased by fifty square feet for each
additional animal. The maximum number of fawns allowed per enclosure shall be
four, regardless of size. The enclosure shall be constructed of wood or wire with a
minimum thickness of twelve gauge with no greater than two-inch mesh or other
sturdy materials to prevent escape and to prevent domestic animals or predators from
gaining access to the pen. The enclosure shall be constructed in an area out of sight
of high traffic human and pet activity. If wire mesh fencing is used, a privacy or
shade cloth must cover all sides.
(8) Once fawns are accustomed to drinking from a bottle, they must be fed
only from a hanging rack.
(9) Predetermined release location sites shall be specified by the Department
of Wildlife and Fisheries prior to each release. The wildlife rehabilitator shall notify
the department permits coordinator at least seventy-two hours in advance of a
planned release.
(10) No tags, collars, or other visual marks on white-tail deer to be released
shall be allowed.

Abstract: Permits the possession of white-tailed deer by wildlife rehabilitators and

Proposed law permits the possession of white-tailed deer by wildlife rehabilitators permitted
pursuant to LAC 76:V.131.
Proposed law prohibits the rehabilitation of white-tailed deer originating outside of La.
Proposed law allows the rehabilitation of white-tailed deer from a Chronic Wasting Disease
Management Zone provided the deer only be released in the same zone from which it
originated and the rehabilitator monitor closely for symptoms of the disease subject to
revocation of the rehabilitation permit.
Proposed law provides for the euthanization of any deer showing symptoms of chronic
wasting disease and for quarantine and testing for all deer at the facility.
Proposed law prohibits possession of deer showing symptoms of illness or displaying open
fractures and deer that display signs of habituation to humans.
Proposed law provides specifications for enclosure sizes, location, and material for
construction of enclosures.
Proposed law provides that fawns be fed from a hanging rack once accustomed to drinking
from a bottle.
Proposed law provides for notification of permits coordinator of planned release location and
prohibits tags, collars, or visual marks on deer to be released.
