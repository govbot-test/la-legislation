WHEREAS, Etta Jane Harris began her distinguished career in state government in

WHEREAS, throughout her remarkable tenure, Ms. Harris has been a steadfast

University and A&M College; and

numerous policy areas, including education, juvenile justice, special schools, social services,

WHEREAS, Ms. Harris' exceptional leadership led to her appointment as deputy
undersecretary of the Louisiana Department of Children and Family Services, where she
often presented information to, or testified before, legislators, members of the judiciary,
advocacy organizations, and professional audiences; and
WHEREAS, she was appointed to serve as a member on several significant bodies,
including the Education Estimating Conference Committee, the Children's Cabinet, and the
Juvenile Justice Committee, further demonstrating her trusted expertise and meaningful
influence in state governance; and
WHEREAS, widely respected by colleagues and peers, Ms. Harris is known not only
for her professional accomplishments but also for her mentorship, impeccable style,
commanding presence, and ability to uplift those around her; and

SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 118
BY SENATOR BARROW
A RESOLUTION
To commend and congratulate Etta Jane Harris for more than forty-four years of exceptional
public service upon the occasion of her retirement.
WHEREAS, Etta Jane Harris began her distinguished career in state government in
1979, at the age of nineteen, embarking upon more than forty-four years of dedicated public
service; and
WHEREAS, throughout her remarkable tenure, Ms. Harris has been a steadfast
advocate for single-family housing, access to public services, children in the child welfare
system, individuals with disabilities, and people who are hungry and in need; and
WHEREAS, she earned a Bachelor of Science in Business Management and a Master
of Public Administration, with a concentration in budget and finance, from Southern
University and A&M College; and
WHEREAS, she has served with distinction as a state budget manager for the
commissioner of administration under several administrations, providing expertise across
numerous policy areas, including education, juvenile justice, special schools, social services,
and coordinated system care; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris' exceptional leadership led to her appointment as deputy
undersecretary of the Louisiana Department of Children and Family Services, where she
often presented information to, or testified before, legislators, members of the judiciary,
advocacy organizations, and professional audiences; and
WHEREAS, she was appointed to serve as a member on several significant bodies,
including the Education Estimating Conference Committee, the Children's Cabinet, and the
Juvenile Justice Committee, further demonstrating her trusted expertise and meaningful
influence in state governance; and
WHEREAS, widely respected by colleagues and peers, Ms. Harris is known not only
