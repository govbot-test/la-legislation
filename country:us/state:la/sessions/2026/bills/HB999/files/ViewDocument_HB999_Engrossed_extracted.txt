6 for an effective date; and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 32:863.1(C)(1)(a) and (I)(1) are hereby amended and reenacted to
9 read as follows:
10 §863.1. Evidence of compulsory motor vehicle liability security contained in
11 vehicle; enforcement; penalty; fees
12 * * *
13 C.(1)(a) If the operator of a motor vehicle is unable to show compliance with
14 the provisions of this Part by displaying the required document when requested to
15 do so, the motor vehicle shall be impounded and the operator shall be issued a notice
16 of noncompliance with the provisions of this Part on a physical or electronic form
17 provided by the department. Notwithstanding the provisions of R.S. 32:852(E), the
18 notice may be sent by first class mail. A copy of the notice of noncompliance shall
19 be provided to the towing or storage company and a copy shall be forwarded to the
20 office of motor vehicles within three calendar days after the notice of noncompliance

2 hearings rights. In addition, the law enforcement officer shall remove the license

18 noncompliance shall be issued. The provisions of this Section, including the
19 requirements for proof of motor vehicle liability security and the enforcement,
20 impoundment, and due process procedures established herein, apply to any motor
21 vehicle registered in another state when operated upon the public highways of this
22 state.

HB 999 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Firment
Abstract: Establishes proof-of-insurance requirements for out-of-state vehicles and
expands compulsory motor vehicle liability security enforcement, impoundment, and
due process procedures to include all vehicles operating within Louisiana.
Present law provides procedures for verifying proof of motor vehicle liability security,
issuing notices of noncompliance, removing license plates, and impounding vehicles when
an operator fails to provide proof of insurance as required by R.S. 32:861. Present law
further provides due process protections, including notice, reinstatement procedures, and
administrative review.
Present law limits the application of these procedures to out-of-state vehicles only when the
vehicle is involved in an accident or collision in this state.
Proposed law removes the accident-only limitation and provides that the proof of insurance,
enforcement, and impoundment procedures in present law apply to any motor vehicle
operated in this state, including vehicles registered in another state.
Proposed law clarifies that the impoundment and license plate removal procedures apply
uniformly to all vehicles subject to a notice of noncompliance.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 32:863.1(C)(1)(a) and (I)(1))
