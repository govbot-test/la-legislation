HB NO. 992
Present law requires each local public school board to assign a student identification number
to every student enrolled in a public elementary or secondary school.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires the state Dept. of Education (DOE) to assign an early childhood
education number to every child participating in a publicly funded program at one of the
following:
(1) A prekindergarten program at a school.
(2) An early learning center as defined by present law (R.S. 17:407.33).
Proposed law authorizes DOE to assign such a number to a child in a program that is not
publicly funded at one of the above early childcare settings with parental consent.
Proposed law requires DOE to assign only one number to each child, and such number shall
be linked to a student and his student identification number upon his entering kindergarten
at a public elementary school.
Requires proposed law to be initially implemented no later than August 1, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 17:3914(C)(3))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Education to the
original bill:
1. Distinguish between early childhood education numbers and student
identification numbers.
2. Specify that proposed law applies to a child who is participating in a program at
a prekindergarten program at a school or early learning center.
3. Require each local public school board to link the early childhood education
number to the student identification number for each student by Dec. 31, 2027.
