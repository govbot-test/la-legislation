DIGEST
SB 252 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Pressly
Present law provides for the Dept. of Public Safety (DPS) to give information relating to
anatomical donations relating to eyes, kidneys, and entire bodies. Proposed law provides the
information is provided and presented in a manner without characterization,
recommendation, or emphasis favoring authorization or refusal, regarding the option to make
or refuse an anatomical gift, and must provide the applicant the opportunity to indicate
authorization for an anatomical gift, refusal to make an anatomical gift, or no selection at all,
in addition, no applicant can be required to make a selection as a condition of issuance or
renewal of a driver's license or identification card.
Present law provides information for an anatomical gift statement indicating the organs
donated for the purpose of transplantation or transplantation research, a space for indicating
any limitation or special wish of the donor, and the signature of the donor and the witnesses
to the signature of the donor must be made available free of charge to applicants for a
driver's license. Present law further provides DPS will promulgate rules relative to the form
and maintain the form on file with the department.
Proposed law retains present law and adds the provision for the applicant to have the option
to make an anatomical gift for transplantation or transplantation research, refusal to make
an anatomical gift or to not make a selection at all including any limitations or special
instructions.
Present law provides if the applicant elects not to execute an anatomical gift statement at the
time of application, the statement will remain blank when presented to the licensee so it can
be executed by the licensee at any time after the license is issued and no other form or
statement shall be required for the purpose of providing for the donation of organs. Proposed
law provides if an applicant makes no selection at the time of application or renewal that the
absence of a selection does not infer authorization or refusal and does not revoke previous
authorization.
Proposed law provides for severability.
Effective January 1, 2027.
(Amends R.S. 32:410(B))
