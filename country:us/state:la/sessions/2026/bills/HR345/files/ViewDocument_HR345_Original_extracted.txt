HLS 26RS-5993 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 345
BY REPRESENTATIVE SCHAMERHORN
ENVIRONMENT: Creates the Task Force on Seismic Activity
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To create the Task Force on Seismic Activity to study the prevalence and cause of
3 earthquakes in North Louisiana, to propose written recommendations to the
4 legislature, and to present its findings to the public at a duly noticed public meeting.
5 WHEREAS, seismic activity has been reported in and around DeSoto, Caddo,
6 Sabine, Natchitoches, Bienville, and Red River Parishes in recent months, including
7 earthquakes of sufficient magnitude to be felt by residents, damage property, and be detected
8 by regional and national seismic monitoring networks; and
9 WHEREAS, earthquakes arise from a variety of natural processes, including
10 movement along subsurface faults, regional geologic stresses, and other naturally occurring
11 structural processes; and
12 WHEREAS, seismic events may originate at varying depths and distances from areas
13 in which potential contributing factors are located; and
14 WHEREAS, scientific literature recognizes that certain human activities may, in
15 some circumstances, contribute to or trigger seismic activity; and
16 WHEREAS, earthquakes of only moderate magnitude have the potential to threaten
17 the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of Louisiana and may result in damage to
18 homes, infrastructure, public facilities, pipelines, transportation systems, and other property;
19 and

1 WHEREAS, understanding the geologic structures, fault systems, subsurface
2 conditions, and potential human influences associated with seismic activity are essential to
3 assessing risks and protecting public safety; and
4 WHEREAS, the precise causes, frequency, distribution, and magnitude of seismic
5 events in Northwest Louisiana and elsewhere in the state have not been fully studied or
6 documented through a comprehensive scientific investigation; and
7 WHEREAS, cooperation between state and federal scientific agencies would
8 expedite a determination of causes and characteristics and aid in developing
9 recommendations for the future.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
11 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby establish and provide for the Task Force on Seismic
12 Activity to study the prevalence and cause of earthquakes in North Louisiana, and may
13 propose recommendations, together with specific proposals for legislation. Any
14 recommendations or proposals shall be submitted by written report to the legislature and to
15 the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772.
16 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
17 members:
18 (1) The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Conservation and Energy or his
19 designee, who shall serve as the chairman.
20 (2) The associate director of the United States Geological Survey, natural hazards
21 or his designee, who shall serve as an ex officio member.
22 (3) The dean of Tulane University, school of science and engineering, or his
23 designee.
24 (4) The director of the Red River Office of Homeland Security and Emergency
25 Preparedness, or his designee.
26 (5) The secretary of the Louisiana Department of Environmental Quality, or his
27 designee.
28 (6) The president of the Louisiana Wildlife Federation, or his designee.
29 (7) The acting director of the Louisiana Geological Survey, Louisiana State
30 University, or his designee.
