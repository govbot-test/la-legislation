SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 136
BY SENATOR HARRIS
A RESOLUTION
governance, financing, jurisdiction, duties, coordination, transparency, and
public-benefit obligations of boards, commissions, districts, authorities, and related
public or quasi-public entities operating on, adjacent to, or materially affecting the
riverfront and adjacent neighborhoods and districts in Orleans Parish.
WHEREAS, the Mississippi Riverfront includes public land in Orleans Parish that
is historic and is among the most significant public, economic, cultural, historic,
transportation, and tourism assets in the state; and
WHEREAS, these public lands preserve historic landscapes and offer abundant
tourism and opportunities that draw millions of visitors annually stimulating local and state
economies while offering social and recreational opportunities and benefits to residents; and
WHEREAS, there is a need and desire to support efforts to develop, to provide and
encourage public access, and to coordinate through responsible and sustainable stewardship
of these assets; and
WHEREAS, the riverfront and adjacent neighborhoods and districts in Orleans Parish
are supported by multiple boards, commissions, districts, authorities, and related public or
quasi-public entities that play integral roles, with differing legal authorities, geographic
boundaries, governance structures, revenue streams, operational duties, and public
responsibilities that often cross boundary management; and
WHEREAS, consultation, coordination, and alignment of the roles and
responsibilities between the multiple entities along the riverfront and adjacent
neighborhoods and districts in Orleans Parish, is essential to the long term economic vitality
of further economic development, tourism and recreation of the riverfront assets, the city of
New Orleans, and the state of Louisiana; and
Page 1 of 4
SR NO. 136 ENROLLED
WHEREAS, there is an interest in ensuring that the stewardship of public assets
along the riverfront and adjacent neighborhoods and districts in Orleans Parish are well
maintained, and that public entities collaborate for increased efficiency and transparency
ensuring that maintenance and operational cost, public safety, investment in public
infrastructure, governance and investment to enhance the economic, cultural, and social
vitality of the areas are transparent, efficient, publicly accountable, and structured in a
manner that supports sound stewardship of public assets, coordination, cooperation,
understanding of capacity, and the delivery of measurable economic and public benefit.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request Louisiana Economic Development to work with the city of
New Orleans, New Orleans & Company, and GNO, Inc. to study and report on the
governance, jurisdiction, duties, coordination, public-benefit obligations of boards,
commissions, districts, authorities, and related public or quasi-public entities operating on,
adjacent to, or materially affecting the riverfront in Orleans Parish.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include but not be limited to the
following:
(1) An inventory of boards, commissions, districts, authorities, public benefit entities,
nonprofit public instrumentalities, special taxing districts, and other public or quasi-public
bodies with jurisdiction, authority, operational responsibility, ownership, management
responsibility, taxing authority, financing authority, leasing authority, maintenance duties,
programming responsibilities, or other material influence over property, facilities,
improvements, operations, public access, or development on, adjacent to, or materially
affecting the riverfront in Orleans Parish.
(2) Identification of the legal basis for each entity, including constitutional, statutory,
charter, ordinance, contractual, cooperative endeavor, intergovernmental, or other authority.
(3) Identification of the geographic boundaries, service areas, or functional
jurisdiction of each entity.
(4) Identification of the governing structure of each entity, including board
composition, appointing authority, reporting obligations, and lines of oversight or
accountability.
Page 2 of 4
SR NO. 136 ENROLLED
(5) Identification of the assets, facilities, public spaces, improvements, leases, or
projects under the authority, ownership, management, control, or material influence of each
entity and revenues and financing mechanisms associated with each entity.
(6) Evaluation of opportunities to improve coordination, alignment of
responsibilities, and clarity of roles among entities, including where multiple entities may
operate within the same geography or serve related public purposes.
(7) Evaluation of whether public obligations are clearly assigned, including
obligations related to public access, maintenance, capital improvements, infrastructure
coordination, redevelopment, economic development, neighborhood impacts, public safety,
and whether the relationship between basic municipal services and enhanced services is
clearly defined and effectively coordinated.
(8) Evaluation of land management practices, roles, and responsibilities and
coordination across entities and whether existing revenues and responsibilities are aligned
for sustainable stewardship of the area, aligned to further leverage and attract private
investment for improving and expanding public access and economic development in the
area.
(9) Identification of any entity, agreement, or financing structure for which enhanced
coordination, reporting, or potential statutory or administrative adjustments may be
beneficial.
(10) Recommendations for any legislation, local ordinances, cooperative endeavor
agreements, memoranda of understanding, governance protocols, to improve delivery,
efficiency, coordination, fiscal accountability, and public-benefit alignment, including
clarifying and strengthening the alignment between basic municipal services and enhanced
services.
(11) Evaluation of the provision of core or basic municipal services delivered by the
city of New Orleans, including but not limited to sanitation, public safety, infrastructure
maintenance, and code enforcement, and an assessment of how enhanced services, including
supplemental cleaning, public space management, security, beautification, programming,
and economic development activities provided by districts, authorities, and other public or
quasi-public entities are aligned, coordinated, and complementary to basic services.
Page 3 of 4
SR NO. 136 ENROLLED
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, in conducting the study, Louisiana Economic
Development may solicit information, cooperation, comments, records, and
recommendations from all relevant stakeholders with assets or jurisdictional or operational
authority on or near the riverfront and adjacent neighborhoods and districts in Orleans
Parish.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that all state agencies, political subdivisions, boards,
commissions, districts, authorities, public bodies, and public officials are urged to cooperate
with Louisiana Economic Development and to provide information and assistance necessary
to carry out the purposes of this Resolution.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Louisiana Economic Development shall submit
a written report of its findings and recommendations to the Legislature of Louisiana and the
David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772 not later than
February 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of Louisiana Economic Development.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
