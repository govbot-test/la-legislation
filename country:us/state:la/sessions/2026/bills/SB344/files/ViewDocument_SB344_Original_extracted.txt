SLS 26RS-604 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR BARROW
WEAPONS. Creates the crime of unsecured storage of firearms. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 14:95.4, relative to weapons; to create the crime of unsecured storage of
3 firearms; to provide for elements of the crime; to provide definitions; to provide
4 penalties; to provide for exceptions; to guarantee constitutional rights; and to provide
5 for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 14:95.4 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §95.4. Unsecured storage of firearms
9 A. It shall be unlawful for any person to store a firearm in an unsecured
10 manner on premises under his control if he either knows or should reasonably
11 know that a minor or prohibited person may gain access to the firearm.
12 B. For purposes of this Section:
13 (1) "Minor" means any person under the age of eighteen years.
14 (2) "Prohibited person" means any person who is prohibited from
15 owning or possessing a firearm pursuant to the provisions of R.S. 14:95.1 or any
16 other state or federal law, any person who has been adjudicated mentally ill or
17 committed to a mental institution, or any person subject to a protective order

1 prohibiting the owning or possessing of firearms.
2 (3) "Tamper-resistant trigger lock" means a mechanical locking device
3 that is affixed to a firearm so as to block operation of the trigger and that
4 requires a key, combination, biometric input, or other secure authentication
5 method to remove or disengage the device.
6 (4) "Unsecured" means not stored either in a locked container or with
7 a tamper-resistant trigger lock properly attached.
8 C. The provisions of this Section shall not apply in any of the following
9 circumstances:
10 (1) The firearm is carried on the person of the owner.
11 (2) The minor or prohibited person obtains the firearm as a result of an
12 unlawful entry onto the premises.
13 (3) The minor obtains the firearm with the permission of the owner for
14 lawful hunting or recreational shooting activity while under adult supervision.
15 D.(1) Whoever violates the provisions of this Section shall be fined not
16 more than five hundred dollars, imprisoned for not more than six months, or
17 both.
18 (2) If a violation of this Section results in bodily injury being caused to
19 another person by a minor or prohibited person who obtained an unsecured
20 firearm, the offender shall be fined not more than one thousand dollars,
21 imprisoned, with or without hard labor, for not more than one year, or both.
22 E. The provisions of this Section shall not be construed to impair the
23 right of any person to keep and bear arms as provided by Article I, Section 11
24 of the Constitution of Louisiana or the Second Amendment of the United States
25 Constitution.

DIGEST
SB 344 Original 2026 Regular Session Barrow
Proposed law creates the crime of unsecured storage of firearms and makes it unlawful for
a person to store a firearm in an unsecured manner on premises under his control when he
either knows or should reasonably know that a minor or prohibited person may gain access
to the firearm.
Proposed law defines "minor", "prohibited person", "tamper-resistant trigger lock", and
"unsecured".
Proposed law provides exceptions for when:
(1) The firearm is carried on the person of the owner.
(2) When a minor or prohibited person obtains the firearm as a result of an unlawful
entry onto the premises.
(3) The minor obtains the firearm with the permission of the gun owner for lawful
hunting or recreational shooting activity while under adult supervision.
Proposed law provides an offender will be fined not more than $500, imprisoned for not
more than six months, or both. Proposed law also provides that, if a violation results in
bodily injury being caused to another person by a minor or prohibited person who obtained
an unsecured firearm, the offender will be fined not more than $1,000, or imprisoned, with
or without hard labor, for not more than one year, or both.
Proposed law cannot be construed to impair the right to keep and bear arms under La. Const.
Art. I, Sec. 11, or the 2nd Amendment of the U.S. Constitution.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 14:95.4)
