HLS 26RS-1834 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 31
BY REPRESENTATIVE DOMANGUE
COMMERCE: Requests the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use and application
of the term "foreign" with respect to juridical persons provided for in state law
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To authorize and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use and application
3 of the term "foreign" in state law as it pertains to banking institutions, pipeline
4 operations and other critical infrastructure, and juridical persons generally, and to
5 report its findings and make specific recommendations for legislation to the
6 Legislature of Louisiana prior to the convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
7 WHEREAS, the laws of this state frequently employ the term "foreign" to describe
8 corporations, limited liability companies, financial institutions, and other juridical persons
9 organized in jurisdictions outside of this state; and
10 WHEREAS, the use of the term "foreign" appears throughout various titles of the
11 Louisiana Revised Statutes and Codes, including but not limited to provisions governing
12 banking, commerce, corporate law, and pipeline operations and other critical infrastructure;
13 and
14 WHEREAS, the meaning, scope, and implications of the term "foreign" may vary
15 depending on the context in which it is used, potentially leading to inconsistencies in
16 interpretation and application; and
17 WHEREAS, such inconsistencies may have significant legal, regulatory, and
18 economic consequences for businesses operating in this state, as well as state citizens; and
19 WHEREAS, a comprehensive review of the use and application of the term "foreign"
20 in state law would assist the legislature in determining whether clarification, modernization,
21 or standardization of terminology is warranted; and

1 WHEREAS, the Louisiana State Law Institute is uniquely qualified to conduct such
2 a study and to provide recommendations based on thorough legal research and analysis.
3 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
4 authorize and request the Louisiana State Law Institute to study the use and application of
5 the term "foreign" in state law as it pertains to banking institutions, pipeline operations and
6 other critical infrastructure, and juridical persons generally, and to report its findings and
7 make specific recommendations for legislation to the Legislature of Louisiana prior to the
8 convening of the 2027 Regular Session.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in conducting this study, the Louisiana State
10 Law Institute may consider, including but not limited to, the following:
11 (1) The historical origins and evolution of the term "foreign" in Louisiana statutes
12 and jurisprudence.
13 (2) Variations in the definition and application of the term across different areas of
14 law.
15 (3) The legal and practical effects of usage with respect to regulated industries and
16 juridical persons.
17 (4) The extent to which entities from countries considered "foreign adversaries" of
18 the United States might appropriately be specifically defined and differentiated from other
19 international entities.
20 (5) Whether alternative terminology or standardized definitions would improve
21 clarity and consistency.
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana State Law Institute shall submit
23 one print copy and one electronic copy of any report produced pursuant to this Resolution
24 to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:772.
25 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
26 director of the Louisiana State Law Institute.
