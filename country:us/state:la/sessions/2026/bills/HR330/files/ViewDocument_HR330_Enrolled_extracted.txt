HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 330
BY REPRESENTATIVE WILFORD CARTER
A RESOLUTION
To commend Louise Cole for more than four decades of community service in Cameron
Parish.
WHEREAS, Louise Cole is a native of Alabama who moved to Cameron Parish in
Southwest Louisiana, where she has made outstanding contributions to the Cameron and
Lake Charles communities, the state of Louisiana, and the United States; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole is devoted to working for the betterment of her community;
she has long been passionate about justice and equality and took particular interest in the
work of the NAACP, one of the oldest nonviolent civil rights organizations in the world; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole took on the responsibility of establishing a branch of the
NAACP in Cameron Parish; she successfully established the Cameron Parish Branch with
the help of officers from the Louisiana State Conference and the Lake Charles Branch,
including Ethel Floyd and Florce J. Floyd, Sr.; and
WHEREAS, on June 12, 1978, the Cameron Parish Branch of the NAACP received
its charter from Emmitt Douglas, president of the Louisiana State Conference of Branches,
and members of the NAACP National Board of Directors, and Ms. Cole was elected
president of branch; and
WHEREAS, under Ms. Cole's leadership, the Cameron Parish Branch became deeply
involved in social justice efforts within the parish; the branch conducted membership drives,
voter education, registration, and get-out-the-vote drives; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole rallied people of all races and backgrounds and local marching
bands, churches, and organizations to achieve social justice in Southwest Louisiana; she
tirelessly and courageously lobbied local and state legislators to rename the Monkey Island
Ferry after Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.; and she initiated, coordinated, and implemented the
Annual Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., Parade in Cameron Parish; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole served as a mentor, teacher, leader, and role model for the
youth chapter of the Cameron Parish Branch of the NAACP; the branch also provided

scholarships and educational assistance to local youth and supported the future leaders of the
community; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole is a strong believer in the power and importance of united
prayer and praise; in the early 1970s, she recognized the need to unite people of diverse
racial, faith, and national backgrounds on the National Day of Prayer, and each year she
brought together ministers of a diverse range of faiths and religious beliefs to join together
in united prayer; and
WHEREAS, in her youth, Ms. Cole loved fashion and was a fashion model; she
shared her love by establishing an annual style and fashion show, where young people
compete in various categories and are recognized for their poise, style, and grace; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole and her son, Dr. Charles Cole, have traveled across the United
States to attend numerous meetings, conventions, and gatherings, and they share a consistent
message to love one another; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Cole has received numerous awards and honors for her outstanding
service; the Cameron Parish Branch has been recognized as the Outstanding Branch of the
Year, and she received the Champion of the Cause Award and numerous other honors; and
WHEREAS, Louise Cole has forged a legacy of service to civil rights efforts in
Cameron Parish, and she is deserving of recognition for her exceptional contributions to the
communities of Southwest Louisiana.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Louise Cole for more than four decades of
community service in Cameron Parish and does hereby extend sincere wishes that she
remain prosperous in all of her endeavors.
