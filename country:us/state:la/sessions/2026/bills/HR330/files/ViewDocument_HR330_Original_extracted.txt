HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 330
BY REPRESENTATIVE WILFORD CARTER
service in Cameron Parish
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend Louise Cole for more than four decades of community service in Cameron
3 Parish.
4 WHEREAS, Louise Cole is a native of Alabama who moved to Cameron Parish in
5 Southwest Louisiana, where she has made outstanding contributions to the Cameron and
6 Lake Charles communities, the state of Louisiana, and the United States; and
7 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole is devoted to working for the betterment of her community;
8 she has long been passionate about justice and equality and took particular interest in the
9 work of the NAACP, one of the oldest nonviolent civil rights organizations in the world; and
10 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole took on the responsibility of establishing a branch of the
11 NAACP in Cameron Parish; she successfully established the Cameron Parish Branch with
12 the help of officers from the Louisiana State Conference and the Lake Charles Branch,
13 including Ethel Floyd and Florce J. Floyd, Sr.; and
14 WHEREAS, on June 12, 1978, the Cameron Parish Branch of the NAACP received
15 its charter from Emmitt Douglas, president of the Louisiana State Conference of Branches,
16 and members of the NAACP National Board of Directors, and Ms. Cole was elected
17 president of branch; and
18 WHEREAS, under Ms. Cole's leadership, the Cameron Parish Branch became deeply
19 involved in social justice efforts within the parish; the branch conducted membership drives,
20 voter education, registration, and get-out-the-vote drives; and

1 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole rallied people of all races and backgrounds and local marching
2 bands, churches, and organizations to achieve social justice in Southwest Louisiana; she
3 tirelessly and courageously lobbied local and state legislators to rename the Monkey Island
4 Ferry after Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.; and she initiated, coordinated, and implemented the
5 Annual Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., Parade in Cameron Parish; and
6 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole served as a mentor, teacher, leader, and role model for the
7 youth chapter of the Cameron Parish Branch of the NAACP; the branch also provided
8 scholarships and educational assistance to local youth and supported the future leaders of the
9 community; and
10 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole is a strong believer in the power and importance of united
11 prayer and praise; in the early 1970s, she recognized the need to unite people of diverse
12 racial, faith, and national backgrounds on the National Day of Prayer, and each year she
13 brought together ministers of a diverse range of faiths and religious beliefs to join together
14 in united prayer; and
15 WHEREAS, in her youth, Ms. Cole loved fashion and was a fashion model; she
16 shared her love by establishing an annual style and fashion show, where young people
17 compete in various categories and are recognized for their poise, style, and grace; and
18 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole and her son, Dr. Charles Cole, have traveled across the United
19 States to attend numerous meetings, conventions, and gatherings, and they share a consistent
20 message to love one another; and
21 WHEREAS, Ms. Cole has received numerous awards and honors for her outstanding
22 service; the Cameron Parish Branch has been recognized as the Outstanding Branch of the
23 Year, and she received the Champion of the Cause Award and numerous other honors; and
24 WHEREAS, Louise Cole has forged a legacy of service to civil rights efforts in
25 Cameron Parish, and she is deserving of recognition for her exceptional contributions to the
26 communities of Southwest Louisiana.
27 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
28 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Louise Cole for more than four decades of
29 community service in Cameron Parish and does hereby extend sincere wishes that she
30 remain prosperous in all of her endeavors.
