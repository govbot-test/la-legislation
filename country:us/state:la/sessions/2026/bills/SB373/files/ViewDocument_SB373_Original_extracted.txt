DIGEST
SB 373 Original 2026 Regular Session Cloud
Present law requires any individual subject to confinement in a state adult penal or
correctional institution to be committed to the Department of Public Safety and Corrections
(department). Further authorizes the department to house inmates at a parish jail or
institution in certain circumstances and requires the department to pay a daily sum per
inmate to each parish sheriff or to the governing authority that operates the jail or institution.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law requires that any subsequent increase in the daily sum provided for in present
law on or after January 1, 2026, is contingent upon compliance with basic jail guidelines
approved by the department, all applicable safety and sanitation laws and regulations, and
criteria established by the department by rule.
Proposed law requires the department to adopt rules and regulations, in consultation with the
sheriff or the governing authority that operates the parish jail, for the implementation of
proposed law.
Proposed law requires the rules to establish criteria for eligibility to receive additional per
diem funding, including benchmarks and reporting requirements. Such criteria shall include
but not be limited to requirements designed to:
(1) Mitigate the state's exposure to liability risks such as addressing critical facility
upgrades and repairs.
(2) Ensure safe, sanitary, and code-compliant conditions.
(3) Provide and upgrade adequate technology infrastructure, if applicable.
Proposed law creates the "Transitional Workforce and Vocational Training Pilot Program"
within the department for state inmates that are in custody of the sheriff or the governing
authority that operates the parish jail. Requires the department to implement the pilot
program no later than January 1, 2027.
Proposed law requires the pilot program to provide workforce and vocational training to
inmates in high-demand skilled trades to address critical workforce gaps and industry
shortages while strengthening reentry outcomes.
