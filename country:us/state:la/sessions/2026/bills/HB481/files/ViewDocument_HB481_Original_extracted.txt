HLS 26RS-1216 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE RISER
PUBLIC PRINTING: Provides for the cost of publishing official proceedings and public
notices
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 43:147.1(A) and (B)(1) and (2), relative to official journals of
3 parishes, municipalities, and school boards; to provide relative to the publication of
4 public notices and proceedings; to require parish and municipal governing authorities
5 and school boards to publish their official proceedings and public notices by
6 contract; to provide with respect to compensation for printing; and to provide for
7 related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 43:147.1(A) and (B)(1) and (2) are hereby amended and reenacted
10 to read as follows:
11 §147.1. Compensation for printing; per character
12 A. The parish and municipal governing authorities and school boards of this
13 state may shall have their official proceedings and public notices published by
14 contract, compensation for which shall not exceed the amounts provided by this
15 Section unless agreed to by both parties in writing. Payment may be made monthly
16 or quarterly at the option of the governing authority or school board.
17 B.(1) In all parishes that do not contain a city with a population of more than
18 one hundred thousand, the cost of publishing official proceedings and public notices
19 shall not exceed the rate of one and one-half three cents per character. When the
20 insertion contains material to be set in tabular form or is submitted prebuilt, the rate

HB 481 Original 2026 Regular Session Riser
Abstract: Requires parish and municipal governing authorities and school boards to publish
their official proceedings and public notices by contract and increases the maximum
rates based on price per character.
Present law authorizes parishes, municipalities, and school boards to publish their official
proceedings and public notices by contract. Provides that payment may be made monthly
or quarterly.
Proposed law requires parishes, municipalities, and school boards to publish their official
proceedings and public notices by contract. Otherwise retains present law.
Present law provides for maximum rates based on price per character, unless parties agree
to a higher rate. Provides that "character" does not include spaces, letting, or kerning.
Provides that the maximum rate is two cents per character if the parish has a municipality
with a population of more than 100,000 and one and one-half cents per character if the parish
does not have a municipality with a population of more than 100,000.
