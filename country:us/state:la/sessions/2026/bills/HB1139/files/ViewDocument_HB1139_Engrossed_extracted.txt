BY REPRESENTATIVE FISHER
MENTAL HEALTH: Requires certain behavioral health services for patients receiving
treatment pursuant to an emergency certificate
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 28:53.1, relative to behavioral healthcare services; to require
3 certain procedures upon admission and discharge; to provide for patients admitted
4 to treatment pursuant to an emergency certificate; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 28:53.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §53.1. Discharge Requirements; medical evaluation; discharge plan; healthcare
8 provider notification requirements
9 A. If a patient is admitted for inpatient behavioral healthcare services
10 pursuant to an emergency certificate issued in accordance with R.S. 28:53, the
11 admitting healthcare facility shall provide certain services upon admission and
12 discharge.
13 B. Within seventy-two hours of admission, the healthcare facility shall do
14 all of the following:
15 (1) Ensure that a licensed healthcare professional conducts a behavioral
16 health evaluation of the patient for underlying behavioral conditions.
17 (2) Contact the patient's primary healthcare provider or licensed healthcare
18 professional providing behavioral health services, if known, to establish a patient
19 treatment history.

1 C. Upon discharge, the individual who is responsible for discharge planning
2 at the healthcare facility where the patient has been admitted shall make a reasonable
3 effort to do all of the following:
4 (1) Provide written or telephonic notification to any healthcare professional
5 that is currently providing behavioral health services or primary care services to the
6 patient, if known, of the date and time that the patient is scheduled to be discharged
7 unless the patient objects to that information being communicated. If the patient
8 does not have a healthcare professional who is currently providing behavioral health
9 services, the discharging healthcare facility shall refer the patient to the appropriate
10 healthcare service, including behavioral and primary care providers.
11 (2) Provide written or telephonic notification within twenty-four hours of
12 discharge to any healthcare professional that the patient is being referred to for
13 follow-up behavioral health services. The healthcare professional that the patient is
14 being referred to shall be provided with a summary of the patient's medical history
15 and any current mental health conditions that the patient is suffering from at the time
16 of discharge. The summary shall be transmitted no later than the date that the patient
17 has been scheduled for follow-up behavioral services.
18 (3) Provide to the patient educational documents published by the Louisiana
19 Department of Health prior to or at the time of discharge. The Louisiana Department
20 of Health shall create and publish educational documents that provide information
21 directed to the patient and the patient's family members, caregivers, or friends about
22 the warning signs of self-harm and the importance of seeking behavioral health
23 services after an admission pursuant to an emergency certificate has ended. The
24 documentation shall instruct the patient and the patient's family members, caregivers,
25 or friends to seek assistance from a healthcare professional to provide ongoing care
26 and clarify that medical privacy laws do not prevent a family member, friend, or
27 other loved one from communicating the patient's condition to a healthcare
28 professional treating the patient.
29 (4) Ensure that the patient is provided with the appropriate prescriptions.

HB 1139 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Fisher
Abstract: Requires a healthcare facility treating a patient admitted by an emergency
certificate to provide certain behavioral health services.
Proposed law provides that if a patient is admitted to a healthcare facility, the facility is
required, within 72 hours of admission, to do all of the following:
(1) Ensure that the licensed healthcare professional conducts a behavioral health
evaluation of the patient for underlying behavioral conditions.
(2) Contact the patient's primary healthcare provider or licensed healthcare professional,
who provides behavioral health services, if known, in order to establish a patient
treatment history.
Present law provides that if a patient is admitted for inpatient behavioral healthcare services
pursuant to an emergency certificate issued in accordance with present law (R.S. 28:53), the
individual who is responsible for discharge planning at the healthcare facility where the
patient has been admitted shall make a reasonable effort to do all of the following:
(1) Provide written or telephonic notification to any healthcare professional that is
currently providing behavioral health services to the patient, if known, of the date
and time that the patient is scheduled to be discharged, unless the patient objects to
that information being communicated.
(2) Provide written or telephonic notification within 24 hours of discharge to any
healthcare professional that the patient is being referred to for follow-up behavioral
health services. Present law requires the healthcare professional to be provided with
a summary of the patient's medical history and current mental health conditions.
(3) Provide the patient with educational documents published by the La. Dept. of Health
(LDH) prior to or at the time of discharge. Present law provides what information
shall be included in the educational documents.
Proposed law modifies present law by adding additional requirements upon discharge. A
healthcare facility is required to do all of the following:
(1) Refer the patient to the appropriate healthcare professional if the patient does not
have a healthcare professional who is currently providing primary or behavioral
health services.
(2) Ensure that the patient is provided with the appropriate prescriptions.
(Amends R.S. 28:53.1)
