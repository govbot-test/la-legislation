2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 145
BY SENATOR HODGES
A RESOLUTION
To recreate and provide for the Task Force on Protecting Louisiana's Critical Infrastructure
from Foreign Adversaries to study real-world threats that foreign adversaries pose
to Louisiana's critical infrastructure, including threats to our United States' military
bases, oil and gas production and refining facilities, industrial manufacturing plants,
energy generation infrastructure, and agriculture and food production lands.
WHEREAS, Senate Resolution No. 204 of the 2025 Regular Session of the
Legislature of Louisiana created the Task Force on Protecting Louisiana's Critical
Infrastructure from Foreign Adversaries; and
WHEREAS, in a recent interview by Federal Bureau of Investigation Director Kash
Patel stated that over the last several years that an alarming number of parcels of land and
buildings that are surrounding United States' military bases have been bought by foreign
adversaries, including Russian citizens and members of the Chinese Communist Party; and
WHEREAS, on June 3, 2025, Director Kash Patel also announced that a Chinese
national, who allegedly expressed loyalty to the Chinese Communist Party and was funded
by the Chinese government, reportedly smuggled a dangerous biological pathogen into the
United States as an act of agroterrorism; and
WHEREAS, the biological pathogen that Director Patel referred to is a fungus that
causes a disease known as "head blight" which infects wheat, barley, corn, and rice and can
cause significant health issues in both humans and livestock and is responsible for billions
of dollars in economic losses worldwide each year; and
WHEREAS, Director Patel also stated that, while the federal government has a role
to play in protecting our nation from the threats imposed by the Chinese Communist Party
efforts to infiltrate American institutions, critical infrastructure, and our food supply, each
state must also take proactive measures to address these concerns in order to protect state
infrastructures from the Chinese Communist Party's actions; and
WHEREAS, the People's Republic of China (PRC) is a one-party rule nation that is

controlled by the Chinese Communist Party; and
WHEREAS, on April 18, 2024, former Federal Bureau of Investigation Director
Christopher Wray stated that the PRC "has made it clear that it considers every sector that
makes our society run as fair game in its bid to dominate on the world stage, and that its plan
is to land low blows against a civilian infrastructure to try to induce panic and break
America's will to resist"; and
WHEREAS, in the same speech, Former Director Wray stated that "the PRC's
targeting of our critical infrastructure is both broad and unrelenting" and that the PRC is
working to "give itself the ability to physically wreak havoc on our critical infrastructure at
a time of its choosing"; and
WHEREAS, the purpose of compromising this infrastructure is "to cause real-world
harm to American citizens and communities in an event of conflict" between the United
States and the PRC, according to Wray; and
WHEREAS, this real-world harm directly threatens Louisianians because Louisiana
is home to a significant number of the United States' military bases, oil and gas production
and refining facilities, industrial manufacturing plants, energy generation infrastructure, and
agriculture and food production lands; and
WHEREAS, study and more work is needed to protect Louisiana's critical
infrastructure from foreign threats, in particular threats from the Russian government and
the Chinese Communist Party.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby establish and provide for the Task Force on Protecting Louisiana's Critical
Infrastructure from Foreign Adversaries to study real-world threats that foreign adversaries
pose to Louisiana's critical infrastructure, including threats to our United States' military
bases, oil and gas production and refining facilities, industrial manufacturing plants, energy
generation infrastructure, and agriculture and food production lands, and to propose
recommendations, together with specific proposals for legislation, which may be done by
written report to the Senate and to the David R. Poynter Legislative Research Library as
required by R.S. 24:771 and 772, no later than February 15, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force should include consideration of

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
members:
(1) The senator who represents Senate District 13, who shall serve as chairman.
(2) Two members of the Senate who are appointed by the president of the Senate.
(3) The superintendent of the Louisiana State Police, or his designee.
(4) The Senate President Pro Tempore, or her designee.
(5) The director of the Governor's Office of Homeland Security and Emergency
Preparedness, or his designee.
(6) The attorney general, or his designee.
(7) The special agent in charge of the New Orleans field office of the Federal Bureau
of Investigation, or his designee.
(8) The special agent in charge of the New Orleans field office of the United States
Secret Service, or his designee.
(9) The officer in charge of the Baton Rouge office of the United States Department
of Homeland Security, or his designee.
(10) The adjutant general of the Louisiana Army National Guard, or his designee.
(11) The member of the House of Representatives who represents District 30, who
shall be appointed and serve if authorized by the speaker of the House of Representatives.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that each designating authority shall submit the
names of designees to the task force to the senator who represents Senate District 13 no later
than August 15, 2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the task force shall convene for its first meeting no
later than September 15, 2026, at the call of the chairman, and the members may elect other
officers to assist the chairman as the task force may deem appropriate.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in conducting this study the task force will seek
input, advice, information, and data from any persons, agencies, entities, or stakeholders that
it deems necessary to fulfill the purposes of this Resolution.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in conducting this study the task force may
request staff support from the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force shall constitute a
quorum for the transaction of business. All official actions of the task force shall require the
affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be
individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate on the date in the
event of a submission of its report or March 1, 2027, whichever occurs first.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
superintendent of the Louisiana State Police, the director of the Governor's Office of
Homeland Security and Emergency Preparedness, the attorney general, the special agent in
charge of the New Orleans field office of the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the special
agent in charge of the New Orleans field office of the United States Secret Service, the
officer in charge of the Baton Rouge office of the United States Department of Homeland
Security, and the adjutant general of the Louisiana Army National Guard.
