WHEREAS, Anita Whitaker-LaFontaine is a native of Shreveport, Louisiana, and
grew up in the Cedar Grove community; she is the daughter of Green Whitaker, Sr., and the
late Ola Mae Whitaker; and
WHEREAS, Anita is a 1977 graduate of Captain Shreve High School, a 1981
graduate of Northeast Louisiana University, and a former registered intensive care unit
nurse; and
WHEREAS, Anita became the first African American Miss Louisiana in 1984, and
she was Star Search Hall of Fame Grand Champion; and
WHEREAS, Anita is an accomplished singer and songwriter who has performed in
four world premiere musicals, countless charity galas, television and concert venues, and
theatrical productions; she has recorded and co-produced four studio albums to date; and
WHEREAS, Anita has performed with many of the most famous voices and
musicians of her time, including duets with Andrea Bocelli, Michael Bolton, Josh Groban,
Dave Koz, and Chris Botti; she sang the original demos for Whitney Houston's songs "I Will
Always Love You", "I Have Nothing", and "I Look to You"; she is a favorite singer of the
legendary producer, David Foster; and
WHEREAS, Anita has worked with Carole Bayer Sager, Michael Bublé, Kenneth
"Babyface" Edmonds, Patti Austin, Burt Bacharach, Carole King, James Ingram, Steve
Tyrell, Patti LaBelle, and Barbara Streisand; and
WHEREAS, Anita has created cabaret shows and undertaken work on memoirs; she
co-wrote Sophisticated Lady: The Songs of Natalie Cole with Kenneth L. Roberson; she also
penned two award-winning books, including When Your Hand is in the Lion's Mouth: The
Life and Wisdom of a Man named Green, which won the award for Best African American

WHEREAS, Ms. Whitaker-LaFontaine made her Broadway debut as Wiletta Mayers
