SLS 26RS-223 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 48
BY SENATOR SEABAUGH
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and congratulates Gil LeBreton upon receiving the 2026
Distinguished Service Award in Sports Journalism and on being inducted into the 2026 class
of the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate Gil LeBreton upon receiving the 2026 Distinguished Service
3 Award in Sports Journalism and on being inducted into the 2026 class of the
4 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
5 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame in Natchitoches, Louisiana, each year
6 collaborates with the Louisiana Sports Writers Association in selecting a limited number of
7 sports figures who embody the character, skill, and talent worthy of being named to the hall
8 of fame; and
9 WHEREAS, the Distinguished Service Award is the most prestigious honor offered
10 to sports media in the state and recipients are carefully chosen after an extensive deliberation
11 by a forty-member expert panel based on their professional accomplishments in local, state,
12 regional, and national arenas; and
13 WHEREAS, Gil LeBreton is the recipient of the 2026 Distinguished Service
14 Award in Sports Journalism and a member of the 2026 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame
15 Induction Class; and
16 WHEREAS, Gil LeBreton, a native of New Orleans and graduate of Louisiana State
17 University, has enjoyed a sportswriting career spanning more than five decades and has
18 earned widespread recognition as one of the most respected and accomplished sports

1 journalists in the United States; and
2 WHEREAS, Mr. LeBreton began his career with The Times-Picayune in his
3 hometown of New Orleans and later served as a beat reporter covering Louisiana State
4 University athletics and the New Orleans Saints for both The Times-Picayune and the Baton
5 Rouge Morning Advocate; and
6 WHEREAS, after honorable service in the United States Army during the Vietnam
7 War, Mr. LeBreton returned home to Louisiana and earned a degree in journalism from
8 Louisiana State University, where he also spent three years working in the university's Sports
9 Information Office; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. LeBreton went on to serve for thirty-seven years as an
11 award-winning sports columnist with the Fort Worth Star-Telegram, where his commentary
12 and reporting earned national acclaim and cemented his reputation as one of the premier
13 voices in sports journalism; and
14 WHEREAS, over the course of his remarkable career, Mr. LeBreton covered some
15 of the most significant sporting events in the world, including twenty-six Super Bowls,
16 thirteen World Series, sixteen Olympic Games, sixteen NCAA Basketball Final Fours, the
17 FIFA World Cup, the Masters Tournament, the Tour de France, the NBA Finals, the Stanley
18 Cup Finals, and the Wimbledon Championships; and
19 WHEREAS, his work carried him across the globe, covering sporting events in
20 France, Spain, Germany, Norway, Greece, the United Kingdom, Australia, Japan, South
21 Korea, Mexico, Argentina, and Canada, as well as in forty of the fifty United States; and
22 WHEREAS, throughout his career, Mr. LeBreton conducted notable one-on-one
23 interviews with many legendary figures in sports and public life; and
24 WHEREAS, Mr. LeBreton's reporting from the Olympic Games earned numerous
25 state and national honors, and his early reporting for The Times-Picayune on Louisiana
26 Olympic hopefuls ahead of the 1976 Montreal Games was recognized by the Louisiana
27 Sports Writers Association as the top series of the year, while his column marking the
28 one-year anniversary of the World Football League's demise was named the LSWA Story
29 of the Year; and
30 WHEREAS, he remains the only sportswriter to be named Sportswriter of the Year
