HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 142
BY REPRESENTATIVE MARCELLE
A RESOLUTION
To commend Etta Jane Harris for her contributions to the state of Louisiana on the occasion
of her retirement as deputy undersecretary of the Department of Children and Family
Services.
WHEREAS, it is appropriate to honor Etta Jane Harris for her service as deputy
undersecretary of the Department of Children and Family Services and to commend her on
the occasion of her retirement; and
WHEREAS, Etta Jane Harris was born on April 10, 1961, and is one of three
children born to James and Marie Harris; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris began her state employment journey at the age of nineteen,
and she has served the state with dedication and advocated for the well-being of its citizens;
and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris has served with distinction for more than forty-four years;
her advocacy efforts have been focused on single-family housing, access to public services,
Louisiana children served by the division of child welfare, disabled individuals, and the
hungry and needy; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris received a bachelor of science degree in business
management and a master's degree in public administration with a concentration in budget
and finance from Southern University; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris served as a state budget manager for the commissioner of
administration under numerous governors and worked to support education, juvenile justice,
social services, coordinated system care services, and other aspects of state administration;
and

WHEREAS, Ms. Harris served as undersecretary during Governor Bobby Jindal's
administration; in this role, she testified before legislators, judges, advocacy groups, and
conferences; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris has been blessed with two sons, McArthur and Micah
Whitmore; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Harris has served this state with professionalism, dignity, and style;
she is well respected and mentors others to be successful, and her sense of humor, kindness,
energy, and graciousness have enriched those fortunate enough to know and work with her;
and
WHEREAS, Etta Jane Harris has been a dedicated public servant since 1979 and
retires as deputy undersecretary of the Department of Children and Family Services; she is
truly most deserving of the highest recognition for her meritorious career.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend Etta Jane Harris on the occasion of her
retirement as deputy undersecretary of the Department of Children and Family Services;
does hereby express enduring appreciation for her contributions to the state of Louisiana;
and does hereby extend sincere wishes that she remain prosperous in her endeavors as she
enters a new stage of life in retirement.
