HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 406
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREEMAN
A RESOLUTION
Wisdom Benjamin.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on May 30, 2026,
of Adelaide Wisdom Benjamin; and
WHEREAS, Adelaide Wisdom Benjamin was born in New Orleans, Louisiana, to
William Bell Wisdom and Mary Freeman Wisdom and raised to understand the importance
of community involvement; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Benjamin graduated from Louise S. McGehee School and enrolled
at Hollins College; after two years, she returned home and enrolled at Newcomb College,
where she graduated in 1954; during this period of her life, she reigned as the Krewe of Rex
queen on Mardi Gras night; she then earned a law degree at Tulane University School of
Law; and
WHEREAS, after completing law school, Ms. Benjamin joined the firm of Wisdom,
Stone, Pigman, and Benjamin and practiced law from 1956 to 1958; she married Edward
Benjamin and retired from practicing law to focus on raising a family; she graduated from
the University of the South's Theological School extension program entitled "Education for
Ministry" and was a mentor in the program for six years; and
WHEREAS, after retiring, Ms. Benjamin pursued her passion for volunteering; she
joined the board of the New Orleans Symphony in 1983; she was a music lover whose
grandparents helped found the orchestra in 1936, and she led the board for five years; and

WHEREAS, in 1991, the orchestra became the Louisiana Philharmonic Orchestra,
and it holds the distinction of being the oldest full-time musician-governed and
collaboratively operated orchestra in the United States; Ms. Benjamin has contributed
through endowments for the orchestra's music director and principal conductor, the latter of
which is named in her honor; in 1997, the orchestra dedicated a week of concerts to her; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Benjamin sat on numerous boards, including the Louisiana
Museum Foundation, the National WWII Museum, the Louise S. McGehee School, and
numerous family foundations; and
WHEREAS, Ms. Benjamin was presented with an honorary doctorate from Loyola
University New Orleans, and the university's president's council gave her its Integritas Vitae
Award in 1994; and
WHEREAS, Adelaide Wisdom Benjamin is survived by her sons, Edward Wisdom
"Ned" Benjamin and Stuart Minor Benjamin; daughters, Mary Dabney Benjamin and Ann
Leith Benjamin Hill; ten grandchildren; and a great-granddaughter who is named after her;
she will be deeply missed, but the legacy of her outstanding service to the city of New
Orleans and love of her family shall live on in the hearts and minds of those who were
privileged to have known and loved her.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Adelaide
Wisdom Benjamin; does hereby express enduring appreciation for her contributions to the
city of New Orleans; and does hereby extend sincere wishes that her family, friends, and
loved ones find comfort in their shared memories of her beautiful life.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
