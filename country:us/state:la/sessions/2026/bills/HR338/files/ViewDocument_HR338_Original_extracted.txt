HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 338
BY REPRESENTATIVE PHELPS
CONDOLENCES: Expresses the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death
of Ponder "P.C." Clinton, Jr.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Ponder "P.C."
3 Clinton, Jr.
4 WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
5 Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on December 3,
6 2025, of Ponder "P.C." Clinton, Jr.; and
7 WHEREAS, Ponder Clinton, Jr., was born on February 9, 1926, in DeSoto Parish,
8 Louisiana, to Ponder Clinton, Sr., and Lucinda Jiles Clinton; he was the eldest of twelve
9 children; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton was known as "P.C." by his family and friends, and as the
11 oldest sibling, he provided a wealth of guidance to his brothers and sisters; and
12 WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton was educated at Red Lick Community School and raised
13 in a Christian household, where he received daily instruction in the teachings of Christianity;
14 and
15 WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton enlisted in the United States Army on April 4, 1944, at the
16 age of eighteen; he served honorably during his tenure and was stationed at Camp
17 Beauregard in Rapides Parish, Louisiana; and

1 WHEREAS, following his military service, Mr. Clinton continued the legacy of his
2 ancestors by pursuing a career in agriculture as a farmer and cattleman; he was recognized
3 as the first Black Louisiana farmer to produce a premium bale of cotton, achieving such
4 recognition in 1967, 1968, 1969, 1970, 1972, 1973, and 1975; he attributed his success in
5 agriculture to his steadfast faith; and
6 WHEREAS, in addition to agriculture, Mr. Clinton served as a school bus operator
7 in DeSoto and Caddo Parishes, transporting hundreds of students during his tenure;
8 afterwards, he began a bush-hogging business and worked as the head custodian at the
9 Caddo Career and Technology Center; and
10 WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton played an instrumental role in the formation of a
11 community action committee that organized and participated in daily marches throughout
12 the Cedar Grove neighborhood; these marches included singing and praying and declared
13 the AB Palmer Park a drug-free zone; the group's efforts garnered national recognition and
14 caught the attention of civil rights activist Dick Gregory, who traveled to Shreveport and
15 joined in the marches; the group's efforts ultimately resulted in the revitalization of the park
16 and created a safe atmosphere for the surrounding community; and
17 WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton and his wife, Dorothy Mae, were married for seventy-four
18 years before her passing on July 10, 2023, and they had ten children: Brenda, Robert,
19 Albert, Emmanuel, Lawrence, Gloria, Harry, Perry, Dennis, and Barbara; and
20 WHEREAS, Ponder "P.C." Clinton, Jr., was a pillar of the various communities he
21 served, and while he will be deeply missed, the legacy of his contributions to the state of
22 Louisiana shall live on in perpetuity in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged
23 to have known him.
24 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
25 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Ponder
26 "P.C." Clinton, Jr.; does hereby offer enduring appreciation for his contributions to the
27 Louisiana's agricultural industry, including his status as a pioneer among Black farmers and
28 cattlemen in the state; and does hereby extend sincere wishes that his family, friends, and
29 loved ones find comfort in their shared memories of his inspiring life.
