2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 338
BY REPRESENTATIVE PHELPS
A RESOLUTION
Clinton, Jr.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on December 3,
2025, of Ponder "P.C." Clinton, Jr.; and
WHEREAS, Ponder Clinton, Jr., was born on February 9, 1926, in DeSoto Parish,
Louisiana, to Ponder Clinton, Sr., and Lucinda Jiles Clinton; he was the eldest of twelve
children; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton was known as "P.C." by his family and friends, and as the
oldest sibling, he provided a wealth of guidance to his brothers and sisters; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton was educated at Red Lick Community School and raised
in a Christian household, where he received daily instruction in the teachings of Christianity;
and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton enlisted in the United States Army on April 4, 1944, at the
age of eighteen; he served honorably during his tenure and was stationed at Camp
Beauregard in Rapides Parish, Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, following his military service, Mr. Clinton continued the legacy of his
ancestors by pursuing a career in agriculture as a farmer and cattleman; he was recognized
as the first Black Louisiana farmer to produce a premium bale of cotton, achieving such
recognition in 1967, 1968, 1969, 1970, 1972, 1973, and 1975; he attributed his success in
agriculture to his steadfast faith; and
WHEREAS, in addition to agriculture, Mr. Clinton served as a school bus operator
in DeSoto and Caddo Parishes, transporting hundreds of students during his tenure;
afterwards, he began a bush-hogging business and worked as the head custodian at the
Caddo Career and Technology Center; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton played an instrumental role in the formation of a
community action committee that organized and participated in daily marches throughout
the Cedar Grove neighborhood; these marches included singing and praying and declared
the AB Palmer Park a drug-free zone; the group's efforts garnered national recognition and
caught the attention of civil rights activist Dick Gregory, who traveled to Shreveport and
joined in the marches; the group's efforts ultimately resulted in the revitalization of the park
and created a safe atmosphere for the surrounding community; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Clinton and his wife, Dorothy Mae, were married for seventy-four
years before her passing on July 10, 2023, and they had ten children: Brenda, Robert,
Albert, Emmanuel, Lawrence, Gloria, Harry, Perry, Dennis, and Barbara; and
WHEREAS, Ponder "P.C." Clinton, Jr., was a pillar of the various communities he
served, and while he will be deeply missed, the legacy of his contributions to the state of
Louisiana shall live on in perpetuity in the hearts and minds of those who were privileged
to have known him.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of Ponder
"P.C." Clinton, Jr.; does hereby offer enduring appreciation for his contributions to the
Louisiana's agricultural industry, including his status as a pioneer among Black farmers and
cattlemen in the state; and does hereby extend sincere wishes that his family, friends, and
loved ones find comfort in their shared memories of his inspiring life.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
