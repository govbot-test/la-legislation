HLS 26RS-4252 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
Resolution No. 15 by Representative LaCombe)
BY REPRESENTATIVE LACOMBE
WILDLIFE/HABITAT: Expresses legislative support for a request by Senator John N.
Kennedy to the United States Fish and Wildlife Service to conduct a study
addressing the impact of land use changes and habitat loss on migratory waterfowl
behavior
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To express legislative support for a request by Senator John N. Kennedy of Louisiana to the
3 United States Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS) to initiate a formal study to
4 evaluate how land use changes and habitat loss affect migratory waterfowl
5 productivity and breeding population distribution, behavior, wintering distributions,
6 and associated economic outcomes in the Mississippi Flyway.
7 WHEREAS, Senator John N. Kennedy of Louisiana wrote a letter to the USFWS
8 concerning the recent decline in annual waterfowl migration to Louisiana; and
9 WHEREAS, as a result of continental wetland loss and changes to land use and
10 policy, substantial landscape alteration has occurred in areas important to Mississippi
11 Flyway waterfowl hunters; and
12 WHEREAS, data from the USFWS shows that the Louisiana wintering mallard
13 population declined from approximately eight hundred thousand in 1999, to three hundred
14 thousand in 2018, representing a three and two-tenths of a percent average annual decline
15 over those years, the largest decline in mallards among the fourteen states in the Mississippi
16 Flyway; and
17 WHEREAS, this same data also shows that states to the north, from Iowa to
18 Mississippi, have observed average annual increases in their mallard populations of roughly
19 eleven percent; and
Page 1 of 2
HLS 26RS-4252 ENGROSSED
HCR NO. 86
1 WHEREAS, the annual mallard harvest in Louisiana declined by approximately
2 ninety-five percent between 1999 and 2021, marking the most significant decline among
3 states within the Mississippi Flyway; and
4 WHEREAS, fewer waterfowl migrating south will likely accelerate this decline in
5 harvest, leading to additional losses in conservation revenues and other funding sources that
6 support wildlife habitat programs; and
7 WHEREAS, in anticipation of improving habitat in the breeding grounds and in
8 Louisiana to increase the numbers of wintering waterfowl in Louisiana, Senator John N.
9 Kennedy has requested that the USFWS conduct a study examining migratory waterfowl
10 behavior, their habitats, winter distribution patterns, and related economic effects within the
11 Mississippi Flyway.
12 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
13 express its support for the request by Senator John N. Kennedy to the USFWS to conduct
14 a study to evaluate variables that contribute to the observed changes in migratory waterfowl
15 behavior, winter distributions, continental populations, and related economic outcomes in
16 the Mississippi Flyway.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be forwarded to the
18 director of the United States Fish and Wildlife Service.

HCR 86 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session LaCombe
Expresses the support of the La. Legislature for the request by Senator John N. Kennedy to
the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service to initiate a formal study to evaluate, study, and research
changes to migratory waterfowl behavior, wintering distributions, continental populations,
and associated economic outcomes in the Mississippi Flyway.
