BOATS/BOATING. Provides for requirements to stop and board a vessel. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 34:851.29, relative to vessel stops and boardings; to provide for
3 vessel operation and equipment; to provide for safety of persons and property; to
4 provide for law enforcement; to provide for compliance; to provide for authority to
5 stop and board vessels; to provide for probable cause; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 34:851.29 is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
9 §851.29. Enforcement
10 It shall be the duty and responsibility of every wildlife agent and peace
11 officer of this state and its subdivisions to enforce the provisions of this Part, and in
12 the exercise thereof, they are hereby. However, no officer is authorized to perform
13 a vessel stop and or board any vessel for the purpose of addressing inquiries to those
14 on board, requiring appropriate proof of identification therefrom, examining the
15 certificate of numbers issued under this Part or in the absence of such certificate
16 requiring appropriate proof of identification of the owner or operator of the vessel,
17 and in addition, examining such vessel for compliance with enforcing the

The original instrument and the following digest, which constitutes no part

DIGEST
SB 243 Original 2026 Regular Session Kleinpeter
Present law authorizes wildlife agents and peace officers, with or without a warrant, to stop
and board any vessel to address inquiries, require identification of persons and vessels, and
examine vessels for compliance with operation and equipment safety laws. Further requires
officers to first identify themselves and exempts officers from liability for trespass.
Proposed law retains the duty of wildlife agents and peace officers to enforce the provisions
of present law but prohibits an officer from stopping or boarding a vessel for the purpose of
enforcing present law unless the officer has probable cause to believe that a violation has
occurred or is occurring.
Proposed law retains the requirement that an officer identify himself prior to boarding and
retains the exemption from liability for trespass while performing official duties.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 34:851.29)
