2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 129
BY SENATOR EDMONDS
A RESOLUTION
to the family and friends of Kathleen "Kathy" Sessums upon the occasion of her
passing.
WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow and deep regret that the citizens of Louisiana
have learned of the passing of Kathleen "Kathy" Sessums, at the age of sixty-eight, after a
long and courageous battle with cancer on April 22, 2026; and
WHEREAS, Kathy was born on August 4, 1957, and raised in Shreveport, Louisiana;
and
WHEREAS, at thirteen she met the love of her life, John Thomas Sessums, whom
she married in 1974, and soon after they welcomed twin boys beginning a life centered on
love, family, and unwavering devotion; and
WHEREAS, during Kathy's forty-six year tenure in public service in a clerical
position, her supervisors recognized her potential and encouraged her to pursue college
credits required to qualify for jobs in the accounting field; and
WHEREAS, upon earning the necessary education, she became an agency accountant
for the Department of Health and Human Resources, now known as the Louisiana
Department of Health (LDH), and quickly advanced to various managerial positions across
the state, undertaking a multitude of tasks, responsibilities, and challenges; and
WHEREAS, Kathy lead a milestone project to build the state's first cost allocation
system at LDH, a system critical in establishing the foundation for the modern cost
allocation systems used statewide, thus enabling the state to orchestrate funding resources
in a way that continues to benefit all residents; and
WHEREAS, Kathy's experience, analytical brilliance, and near-photographic
memory ultimately led to her becoming the director of Louisiana's Enterprise Resource
Planning system, also known as LaGov; and
WHEREAS, under her leadership, LaGov became a massive, multi-year initiative

resulting in the implementation of a statewide information system that integrates accounting,
finance, budgeting, procurement, staff training, payroll, inventory control, capital projects,
travel management, and grant services, those of which Kathy had a keen understanding of
detail and how they all worked together; and
WHEREAS, Kathy worked tirelessly with various governmental departments to
incorporate data from essential agency stand-alone systems into the LaGov system, which
has replaced obsolete systems and modernized state operations and secured millions in
taxpayer savings. LaGov provides a common base for all state activities, and has become key
to all statewide operations today; and
WHEREAS, in 2022 she received a well deserved award, the "Charles E. Dunbar,
Jr. Career Civil Service Award", which is the highest honor classified state employees can
receive for their service to the citizens of Louisiana; and
WHEREAS, Kathy will be remembered not only for her professional
accomplishments, but also for her leadership, mentorship, and the relationships she built
throughout her time in public service; and
WHEREAS, Kathy was a devoted wife, mother, and Mimi and enjoyed spending
time with her children, grandchildren, dogs, and close friends, and whether in Florida or on
the river, weekends on the water, surrounded by loved ones, was where she was happiest;
and
WHEREAS, she is survived by her beloved husband of fifty-two years, John Thomas
Sessums; her twin sons and daughter-in-laws, Brady (Cinclare) Sessums, Casey (Rebecca)
Sessums; and four grandchildren, Elizabeth Sessums, John Brady "JB" Sessums Jr., Marcelle
Sessums, and Thomas Sessums; and
WHEREAS, Kathy shall be remembered as a visionary who helped modernize state
operations through LaGov, leaving a lasting impact on Louisiana's state agencies, as the
cornerstone of Louisiana's statewide operations; and
WHEREAS, Kathleen "Kathy" Sessums will be remembered for her strength,
intelligence, warmth, and the deep love she had for her family, friends, and she leaves a
legacy in the lives she touched, the family she cherished, and the enduring impact of her
life's work for Louisiana through LaGov.

SR NO. 129 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby express sincere and heartfelt condolences to the family and friends of Kathleen
"Kathy" Sessums on the occasion of her passing and does extend enduring appreciation for
the tremendous pride and honor that her memories will forever bring to the state of
Louisiana.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to her
husband, Mr. John Thomas Sessums.
