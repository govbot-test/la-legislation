7 Section 1. R.S. 15:827.2.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §827.2.1. Collection of certain correctional census data
9 A. The legislature finds that the Department of Public Safety and Corrections
10 collects and maintains correctional census, demographic, admissions, release,
11 supervision, mortality, and operational data. The legislature further finds that this
12 data is used to generate publicly available reports and informational dashboards on
13 the website of the department. The legislature finds that public access to this data
14 in machine-readable form promotes transparency, accountability, and informed
15 policymaking. The legislature previously adopted Senate Concurrent Resolution No.
16 32 of the 2024 Regular Session, which urged and requested the Department of Public
17 Safety and Corrections to publish certain correctional census data in downloadable
18 format. The legislature now finds that codification of these transparency
19 requirements is necessary to ensure uniform, consistent, and timely public access to
20 correctional system data.

HB 525 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Carpenter
Abstract: Provides for the online publication and maintenance of certain data used and
compiled by the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections (DPS&C).
Proposed law provides for legislative findings.
Proposed law provides for duties of the DPS&C relative to the publishing of certain data and
reports.
Proposed law provides for the contents and format of the information and data to be
published on a monthly basis.
Proposed law provides that nothing in proposed law requires the disclosure of personally
identifiable information that is protected under state or federal law.
Proposed law permits the DPS&C to redact identifying information as necessary to comply
with applicable confidentiality provisions, provided that aggregate and anonymized data are
published to the maximum extent permitted by law.
Proposed law requires the DPS&C to submit a written report that certifies compliance with
proposed law to certain legislative committees no later than March 31, 2027.
Proposed law requires the DPS&C to implement the provisions of proposed law no later than
Oct. 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 15:827.2.1)
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Administration of
Criminal Justice to the original bill:
1. Modify format requirements to provide for publishing of reports and data in
Microsoft Excel format.
2. Change the frequency update of admission, release, and death information from
a monthly basis to an annual basis.
3. Remove proposed law relative to the offender census at parish prisons or jails as
information required to be published on the DPS&C's website.
4. Make technical changes.
