ENROLLED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 161
BY REPRESENTATIVE WALTERS
A RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, Black Maternal Health Week was founded and is led by the Black
Mamas Matter Alliance to build awareness, activism, and community-driven solutions to
improve Black maternal health outcomes and is observed annually from April 11-17; and
WHEREAS, Black women in the United States continue to experience
disproportionately high rates of maternal morbidity and mortality, regardless of income or
education, due to systemic inequities, implicit bias, and gaps in access to quality care; and
WHEREAS, in Louisiana, maternal health outcomes reflect significant disparities
and pregnancy-related mortality rates remain among the highest in the nation; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana has consistently ranked among the worst states in the nation
for maternal health outcomes, which underscores the urgency for targeted intervention and
sustained policy action; and
WHEREAS, Black women in Louisiana are approximately two to three times more
likely to die from pregnancy-related causes than white women, which reflects persistent
racial disparities in health outcomes; and
WHEREAS, the leading causes of pregnancy-related death include cardiovascular
conditions, hemorrhage, and complications associated with chronic health conditions, and
the majority of pregnancy-related deaths in Louisiana are considered preventable; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana experiences elevated rates of severe maternal morbidity,
including life-threatening complications during pregnancy and childbirth, which
disproportionately impact Black women; and

WHEREAS, social determinants of health, including access to prenatal and
postpartum care, insurance coverage, transportation, and provider bias, contribute
significantly to adverse maternal health outcomes across the state; and
WHEREAS, culturally competent, community-centered care models, including the
integration of doulas, midwives, and community health workers, have proven essential in
improving maternal and infant health outcomes, particularly for Black families; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana-based organizations, health care providers, doulas, midwives,
and advocates continue to play a critical role in advancing equity, improving outcomes, and
supporting families before, during, and after pregnancy; and
WHEREAS, observing Black Maternal Health Week serves as an opportunity to
amplify the voices of Black mothers, recognize community leadership, and recommit to
policies and investments that ensure safe, respectful, and equitable maternal healthcare for
all.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate April 11- 17, 2026, as Black Maternal Health
Week in Louisiana; does hereby commend the work of community-based organizations,
health care providers, and advocates across Louisiana for their dedication to improving
maternal health outcomes and advancing equity; does hereby affirm its commitment to
supporting policies, programs, and partnerships that eliminate maternal health disparities and
ensure that every mother in Louisiana has access to high-quality, culturally competent care;
and does hereby encourage continued public-private partnerships, data-driven strategies, and
innovative care delivery models that expand access, improve outcomes, and strengthen
accountability across Louisiana's maternal health system.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
