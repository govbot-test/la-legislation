12 "§961. Court reporters, generally
13 * * *
14 J. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, a
15 stenographer-reporter of the Board of Tax Appeals as authorized pursuant to R.S.
16 47:1404 and 1416 shall prepare, certify, and charge for a transcript in accordance
17 with the board's rules. The stenographer-reporter shall be considered to have the
18 authority of an official reporter for the board in the same manner as if appointed by
19 the district court for the parish of the board's domicile and shall be considered a
20 certified digital reporter during his or her continued employment in that capacity.
21 * * *
22 §981. Court reporter pools
23 A. There is hereby established a court reporter pool of thirty emergency court
24 reporters employed or approved by the supreme court from which said court
25 reporters may be assigned from time to time to any of the various district courts in
26 the state as the need arises in order that transcripts for criminal appeals and other
27 necessary purposes may be timely lodged in the appellate courts.
28 * * *
29 D. In addition to all fees for court reporters provided for in R.S. 13:961, each
30 court reporter in the pool shall be paid an annual salary by the state in the amount of
31 fifteen thousand dollars. The supreme court shall provide for the salaries, related
32 costs, and travel expenses of the court reporters in its annual budget for operations
33 of the court. Reimbursement for travel expenses of pool court reporters shall not
34 exceed the amount allowed other state employees. Notwithstanding any other
35 provision of law to the contrary, an emergency court reporter appointed pursuant to
36 the authority of this Section shall prepare, certify, and charge for a transcript in
37 accordance with rules duly adopted by the Supreme Court for that purpose. An
38 emergency court reporter under this Section shall be considered to have the authority
39 of an official reporter for a district court in the same manner as if appointed by it.
40 * * *
41 Section 2. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
42 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
43 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
44 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
45 effective on the day following such approval."
