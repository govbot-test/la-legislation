SLS 26RS-516 RE-REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR KLEINPETER
ELECTIONS DEPARTMENT. Provides for the increase of parish board of election
supervisor meetings in certain parishes for certain races. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S.18:423(E), relative to parish board of election supervisors; to
3 provide for eligible compensation days during an election; to provide for
4 implementation; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 18:423(E) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
7 §423. Parish boards of election supervisors
8 * * *
9 E. Compensation. Each member of the parish board of election supervisors
10 shall receive one hundred fifty dollars for each day, not to exceed six seven days,
11 actually spent in the performance of his duties in preparing for and supervising each
12 election held in the parish, except that each member of the board may be
13 compensated for not more than eight nine days for a presidential or regularly
14 scheduled or special congressional primary or general election. In addition, each
15 member of the board who is not a public official shall receive fifty dollars for each
16 day spent in court as a subpoenaed witness in litigation concerning the performance
17 of his duties as a member of the parish board of election supervisors in connection

DIGEST
SB 202 Re-Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Kleinpeter
Present law provides that parish board of election supervisors receive one hundred fifty
dollars for each day not to exceed six days spent in the performance of his duties.
Proposed law retains present law but changes the time period from six to seven days.
Present law provides that each member of the board may be compensated for not more than
eight days for a presidential or regularly scheduled or special congressional primary or
general election.
Proposed law changes this maximum compensation time to nine days.
Implementation of the provisions of proposed law is subject to the appropriation of funds
by the legislature for this purpose.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends R.S. 18:423(E))
