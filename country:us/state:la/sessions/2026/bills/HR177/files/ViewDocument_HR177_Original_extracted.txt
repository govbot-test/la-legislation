2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 177
BY REPRESENTATIVE CARLSON
Louisiana
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To designate May 2026 as ALS Awareness Month in Louisiana.
3 WHEREAS, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, commonly known as ALS or Lou Gehrig's
4 disease, is a progressive fatal neurodegenerative disease in which a person's brain loses
5 connection with their muscles, which slowly reduces a person's ability to walk, talk, eat, and
6 eventually breathe; and
7 WHEREAS, thousands of new ALS cases are reported every year, and estimates
8 show that, every ninety minutes, someone is diagnosed with ALS and someone passes away
9 from ALS; on average, patients diagnosed with ALS survive only two to five years from the
10 time of diagnosis; and
11 WHEREAS, the exact causes of ALS are unknown and there is no known cure for
12 the disease; and
13 WHEREAS, people who have served in the military are more likely to develop and
14 die from ALS than those with no history of military service; and
15 WHEREAS, securing access to new therapies, durable medical equipment, and
16 communication technologies is of vital importance to people living with ALS; and
17 WHEREAS, clinical trials play a pivotal role in evaluating new treatments,
18 enhancing quality of life, and fostering assistive technologies for those living with ALS; and
19 WHEREAS, the ALS Association is the largest philanthropic funder of ALS research
20 globally and has committed more than one hundred fifty-four million dollars to support more
21 than five hundred fifty projects across the United States and eighteen other countries; and
Page 1 of 2

1 WHEREAS, the ALS Association is committed to making amyotrophic lateral
2 sclerosis livable and curing the disease; ALS Awareness Month provides an opportunity to
3 increase public awareness of the dire circumstances of people living with ALS, acknowledge
4 the terrible impact the disease has on those individuals and their families, and support
5 research to eradicate the disease.
6 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
7 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate May 2026 as ALS Awareness Month in
8 Louisiana and does hereby encourage the citizens of Louisiana to support ALS research,
9 advocate for increased funding, and stand in solidarity with those affected by the disease.
