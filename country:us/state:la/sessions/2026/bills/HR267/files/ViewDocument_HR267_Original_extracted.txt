HLS 26RS-2148 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 267
BY REPRESENTATIVE SPELL
HEALTH: To study prevention and reduction of diabetes-related amputation
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health, in collaboration with the
3 University of Louisiana at Lafayette and other stakeholders, to study the prevention
4 and reduction of diabetes-related amputations and to report its findings and
5 recommendations to the legislature.
6 WHEREAS, diabetes is a major public health issue in Louisiana and a leading cause
7 of preventable lower-limb amputations; and
8 WHEREAS, amputations resulting from diabetes and related complications result in
9 significant human suffering and increased healthcare costs to the state; and
10 WHEREAS, many diabetes-related amputations are preventable through early
11 detection, coordinated care, patient education, and access to appropriate medical services;
12 and
13 WHEREAS, improved data collection, research, and collaboration among public and
14 private entities are necessary to reduce the incidence of such amputations; and
15 WHEREAS, institutions of higher education with significant research capacity are
16 uniquely positioned to conduct comprehensive studies and develop evidence-based
17 recommendations; and
18 WHEREAS, multidisciplinary input from healthcare professionals, public health
19 experts, and individuals impacted by diabetes is essential to developing effective prevention
20 strategies.

1 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
2 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health,
3 hereinafter referred to as the department, to conduct, in collaboration with the Louisiana
4 Center for Health Innovation at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette which has
5 substantial research activity, a study on the prevention and reduction of amputations
6 resulting from diabetes and related complications.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Center for Health Innovation at
8 the University of Louisiana at Lafayette shall utilize existing resources and collaborate with
9 the department in conducting the study.
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include but not be limited to the
11 following:
12 (1) The prevalence of diabetes-related amputations in Louisiana.
13 (2) The cost to the state for treatment and care associated with those amputations.
14 (3) Evidence-based best practices for the prevention of diabetes-related amputations,
15 including early detection and screening programs.
16 (4) Strategies to expand access to limb-saving services, including multidisciplinary
17 care teams, wound care, podiatry, and patient education.
18 (5) Opportunities for public-private partnerships to reduce the rate of amputations.
19 (6) Preventative services including but not limited to topical oxygen therapies,
20 advanced therapies including skin substitutions, autologous 3D bio-printing, and in-home
21 services.
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall also include:
23 (1) Examination of amputations related to diabetic foot ulcers and peripheral artery
24 disease.
25 (2) Development of recommendations regarding:
26 (a) Methods to increase public awareness of diabetes-related amputations.
27 (b) Best practices for reducing the number of amputations performed.
28 (c) Policy solutions to ensure access to treatments to prevent amputations, including
29 insurance coverage for therapies designed to treat diabetic foot ulcers.
30 (d) Access to evidence-supported medical tools, technologies, and services.

1 (e) Methods to improve and incentivize quality care to avoid amputations.
2 (f) Strategies to promote collaboration among healthcare providers and stakeholders.
3 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health and the
4 Louisiana Center for Health Innovation at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette shall
5 establish or appoint a workgroup of subject-matter experts and stakeholders, which may
6 include but is not limited to physicians, nurses, podiatrists, endocrinologists, vascular
7 specialists, public health representatives, and individuals affected by diabetes, to assist in
8 conducting the study.
9 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Louisiana Department of Health shall submit
10 a report of the findings and recommendations of the study to the House Committee on
11 Health and Welfare and the Senate Committee on Health and Welfare no later than
12 December 1, 2026, and shall make the report publicly available.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
14 secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health and the director of the Louisiana Center for
15 Health Innovation at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette.
