VACCINES/VACCINATION: Prohibits the use of vaccine status to determine admission
to public buildings
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:1300.81 through 1300.86, relative to vaccination status and admission to
3 public buildings; to provide for individual rights regarding medical decisions; to
4 provide for definitions; to provide for the prohibition of medical mandates; and to
5 provide for related matters.
6 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
7 Section 1. R.S. 40:1300.81 through 1300.86 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 §1300.81. Short title
9 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Louisiana Medical
10 Freedom Act".
11 §1300.82. Purpose
12 The purpose of this Act is to protect the right of individuals to make
13 autonomous decisions regarding medical interventions without discrimination,
14 coercion, or exclusion by public entities.
15 §1300.83. Definitions
16 As used in this Section:
17 (1) "Business entity" means any individual or group engaged in any activity,
18 profession, or enterprise for gain, benefit, or livelihood, whether for-profit or
19 nonprofit, including self-employed individuals, corporations, partnerships, limited

14 §1300.84. Prohibition of medical mandates
15 (1) No business entity shall deny, restrict, or otherwise penalize any
16 individual’s access to services, products, venues, education, training, trade, or
17 transportation based on the individual’s refusal of a medical intervention.
18 (2) A business entity shall not require a medical intervention as a condition
19 of employment.
20 (3) No ticket issuer shall discriminate against or deny access to any person
21 at an event based on his status with respect to any medical intervention.
22 (4) No government entity or official shall require a medical intervention for
23 any of the following:
24 (a) Access to government services.
25 (b) Use of public buildings, facilities, infrastructure, or transportation.
26 (c) Employment by government entities.
27 (5) Any requirements for medical interventions which are allowed under this
28 law remain subject to any applicable laws or legal precedents providing for or
29 protecting exemptions and reasonable accommodations.

HB 926 Original 2026 Regular Session Bayham
Abstract: Provides for the prohibition of the use of vaccination status to prevent admission
to public buildings and events, provides for individual rights regarding medical
intervention decisions, and provides for the prohibition of medical mandates.
Proposed law names the proposed law the Louisiana Medical Freedom Act.
Proposed law states the legislative purpose of the proposed law.
Proposed law lists relevant definitions.
Proposed law prohibits business entities, ticket issuers, and government entities or officials
from requiring medical intervention or refusing an individual's access to services, products,
venues, trade, transportation, public buildings, public infrastructure, or government
employment.
Proposed law provides for enforcement through the attorney general's office or appropriate
local prosecutor and remedies if the proposed law is violated.
Proposed law prevents healthy individuals or alleged asymptomatic individuals from being
excluded from public activities based upon the individual declining medical intervention
during an outbreak or public health emergency.
