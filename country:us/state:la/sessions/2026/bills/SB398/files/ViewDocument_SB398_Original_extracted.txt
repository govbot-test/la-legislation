4 §2176. Short title
5 This Part shall be known and may be cited as "Uniform Standards Code
6 for Manufactured and Factory-Built Housing".
7 §2176.1. Definitions
8 As used in this Part, unless the context requires a different definition:
9 (1)(a) "Code" for manufactured housing means the National
10 Manufactured Home Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C.
11 5401 et seq., as amended, and federal regulations promulgated pursuant
12 thereto, along with any construction or installation-related standards adopted
13 by the Residential Subcommittee of the Louisiana State Licensing Board of
14 Contractors.
15 (b) "Code" for modular housing means the International Residential
16 Code as adopted by the Louisiana State Uniform Construction Code Council.
17 (2) "Residential Subcommittee" means the Residential Subcommittee of
18 the Louisiana State Licensing Board of Contractors.
19 (3) "Criminal history record information" means conviction information
20 collected by criminal justice agencies on individuals.
21 (4)(a) "Factory-built housing developer" means any person, group of
22 persons, firm, partnership, corporation, association, company, or legal entity
23 that sells, leases, or offers for sale or lease to the public a lot together with the
24 sale of a manufactured home permanently installed and fixed on a foundation
25 on the lot and designed as a single family residence. For purposes of this Part,
26 "factory-built housing developer" includes "contractors" and "residential
27 contractors" as defined in R.S. 37:2150.1.
28 (b) "Factory-built housing developer" shall not include any of the
29 following:

9 §2176.3. License required; qualifications; application; issuance; transfer;
10 criminal history record information; bond
11 A.(1) No manufacturer, factory-built housing residential contractor, or
12 salesman within or without this state shall sell or offer for sale in Louisiana any
13 mobile home, modular home, or manufactured housing unless he has obtained
14 a valid manufacturer's, factory-built housing residential contractor's, or
15 salesman's license, whichever is applicable, from the residential subcommittee
16 as provided in this Part.
17 (2) No factory-built housing developer shall sell or offer for sale to the
18 public any manufactured or modular home unless he has obtained a license
19 from the residential subcommittee, as provided in this Part. No employee of a
20 factory-built housing developer shall offer manufactured or modular housing
21 for sale to the general public without first obtaining a salesman license or being
22 a licensed real estate agent.
23 (3) No manufacturer, within or without this state, shall sell, or offer for
24 sale to a person, any manufactured or modular housing for resale to the public
25 unless the person has obtained a valid factory-built housing residential
26 contractor's or factory-built housing developer's license from the residential
27 subcommittee as provided in this Part.
28 (4) A license shall be issued when the requirements of this Part are met
29 as herein provided.

DIGEST
SB 398 Original 2026 Regular Session Foil
Present law establishes the Residential Contractors Subcommittee (residential
subcommittee), within the State Licensing Board for Contractors (board), consisting of five
members. Three members appointed by the governor from a list of names submitted by the
La. Homebuilders Association, one member from the 1st congressional district, one member
from the 2nd congressional district, one member from the 3rd congressional district, one
member representing the 4th and 5th congressional district, and one member representing
the 6th congressional district.
Proposed law increases the membership of the residential subcommittee from five members
to nine members and provides that the membership shall consist of three members appointed
by the governor from a list of names submitted by the La. Homebuilders Association, three
members appointed by the governor from a list of names submitted by the La. Manufactured
Housing Association, one member appointed by the governor to represent each
congressional district and two at-large members appointed by the governor.
Proposed law redesignates present law provisions from Title 51 of the La. Revised Statutes
of 1950 relative to manufactured an modular homes to Title 37 of the La Revised Statutes
of 1950, relative to the La. State Licensing Board for Contractors.
Present law provides for terms and definitions.
Proposed law retains present law terms and definitions except changes the term "developer"
to "factory-built housing developer", and changes the term "retailer" to "factory-built
housing developer". Further defines "factory-built housing" and removes the terms
"distributor", "distributor sales representative" and "manufactured home broker".
Present law created the La. Manufactured Housing Commission.

Proposed law repeals present law that created the La. Manufactured Housing Commission
(commission) and redesignates the commission's powers and duties to the Residential
Subcommittee of the State Licensing Board for Contractors.
Present law provides that the provisions of the La. State Uniform Construction Code shall
not conflict with federal HUD standards governing manufactured housing construction or
with state law governing manufactured housing installation and that services, renovation,
repair, or warranty work performed on manufactured housing are also governed by federal
HUD standards under the jurisdiction of commission.
Present law extends the exemption for manufactured housing to related accessory structures,
including driveways, steps, desks, and similar work, but excludes additional living areas or
other type of heated and cooled spaces outside the original footprint of the manufactured
home.
Proposed law retains present law except deletes the reference to services, renovation, repair,
or warranty work performed on manufactured housing being under the jurisdiction of the
commission and removes the exemption for manufactured housing as it relates to accessories
structures.
Proposed law requires municipalities and parishes to inspect the installation and setup of
manufactured and modular homes within their jurisdiction.
Proposed law requires inspections to be conducted by a certified building code enforcement
officer or certified third-party provider using federal HUD construction standards.
Proposed law requires all disputes regarding interpretation of federal or state construction
standards be resolved by the residential subcommittee, whose decision is final and binding.
Proposed law requires the residential subcommittee to offer educational courses on federal
HUD construction and installation standards for certification purposes.
Proposed law allows local governments to charge a reasonable inspection fee and permits
licensees to hire a certified third-party provider to conduct installation inspections.
