9 §2155. Residential Contractors Subcommittee; membership; terms; powers; duties
10 A. There is hereby established within the board the Residential Contractors
11 Subcommittee, hereinafter referred to as "residential subcommittee", consisting of
12 five nine members who shall be residents of this state and who have been actively
13 engaged in residential contracting for at least five years prior to appointment by the
14 governor. Three members of the residential subcommittee shall be appointed by the
15 governor from a list of not less than ten names submitted by the Louisiana
16 Homebuilders Association as certified by its president and secretary. One member
17 of the residential subcommittee shall be from and appointed to represent the first
18 congressional district, one member shall be from and appointed to represent the
19 second congressional district, one member shall be from and appointed to represent
20 the third congressional district, one member shall be from and appointed to represent
21 the fourth and fifth congressional districts, and one member shall be from and
22 appointed to represent the sixth congressional district. Three members of the
23 residential subcommittee shall be appointed by the governor from a list of not
24 less than ten names submitted by the Louisiana Manufactured Housing
25 Association as certified by its president and secretary. One member of the
26 residential subcommittee shall be appointed by the governor to represent each
27 congressional district. Two members shall be appointed by the governor as at-
28 large members.

2 §2176. Short title
3 This Part shall be known and may be cited as "Uniform Standards Code
4 for Manufactured and Factory-Built Housing".
5 §2176.1. Establishment of uniform standards codes
6 All new manufactured or modular homes, as defined in R.S. 37:2150.1,
7 which are sold or offered for sale in this state shall be in compliance with the
8 manufactured housing code or the modular housing code and the requirements
9 of this Part.
10 §2176.2. License required; qualifications; application; issuance; transfer;
11 criminal history record information; bond
12 A.(1) No manufacturer, factory-built housing dealer, or salesman within
13 or without this state shall sell or offer for sale in Louisiana any mobile home,
14 modular home, or manufactured housing unless he has obtained a valid
15 manufacturer's, factory-built housing dealer's, or salesman's license, whichever
16 is applicable, from the residential subcommittee as provided in this Part.
17 (2) No factory-built housing developer shall sell or offer for sale to the
18 public any manufactured or modular home unless he has obtained a license
19 from the residential subcommittee, as provided in this Part. No employee of a
20 factory-built housing developer shall offer manufactured or modular housing
21 for sale to the general public without first obtaining a salesman license or be a
22 licensed real estate agent.
23 (3) No manufacturer, within or without this state, shall sell, or offer for
24 sale to a person, any manufactured or modular housing for resale to the public
25 unless the person has obtained a valid factory-built housing dealer's or factory-
26 built housing developer's license from the residential subcommittee as provided
27 in this Part.
28 (4) A license shall be issued when the requirements of this Part are met
29 as herein provided.

2 §2177.1. Purpose
3 The legislature finds a need to promote commerce in Louisiana by
4 providing clear, concise, and mandatory warranties for the purchasers and
5 occupants of new manufactured and modular homes in Louisiana and by
6 providing for the use of homeowners' insurance as additional protection for the
7 public against defects in the construction of new manufactured and modular
8 homes. This need can be met by providing a warranty for a new manufactured
9 or modular home purchaser defining the responsibility of the builders to that
10 purchaser and subsequent purchasers during the warranty periods provided
11 herein. The warranty, which is mandatory in most cases, shall promote
12 uniformity of defined building standards. Additionally, all provisions of this
13 Part shall apply to any defect, although there is no building standard directly
14 regulating the defective workmanship or materials.

conflict with federal HUD standards governing manufactured housing construction or with

2 §2177.1. Purpose
3 The legislature finds a need to promote commerce in Louisiana by
4 providing clear, concise, and mandatory warranties for the purchasers and
5 occupants of new manufactured and modular homes in Louisiana and by
6 providing for the use of homeowners' insurance as additional protection for the
7 public against defects in the construction of new manufactured and modular
8 homes. This need can be met by providing a warranty for a new manufactured
9 or modular home purchaser defining the responsibility of the builders to that
10 purchaser and subsequent purchasers during the warranty periods provided
11 herein. The warranty, which is mandatory in most cases, shall promote
12 uniformity of defined building standards. Additionally, all provisions of this
13 Part shall apply to any defect, although there is no building standard directly
14 regulating the defective workmanship or materials.

9 A. If a builder violates this Part by failing to perform as required by the

11 against the builder for actual damages, including attorney fees and court costs,
12 arising out of the violation. The damages with respect to a single defect shall not
13 exceed the reasonable cost of repair or replacement necessary to cure the defect,
14 and damages with respect to all defects in the home shall not exceed the original
15 purchase price of the home.
16 B. The parties may provide for the arbitration of any claim in dispute.
17 Any arbitration shall comply with and may be binding only to the extent

19 §2177.10. Exclusiveness
20 This Part provides the exclusive remedies, warranties, and peremptive

22 as defined in this Part, and no other provisions of law relative to warranties and
23 redhibitory vices and defects shall apply. Nothing herein shall be construed as
24 affecting or limiting any warranty of title to land or improvements.
