SENATE BILL NO. 72
BY SENATOR HODGES
MOTOR VEHICLES. Provides for electronic signatures for vehicle titles. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 32:714, relative to electronic signatures and the office of motor vehicles; to
3 provide for the acceptance of digital signatures; to provide for the acceptance of
4 electronic signatures; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 32:714 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §714. Titling; electronic signature
8 A. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, any document required
9 or permitted to be executed in connection with the sale, transfer, titling,
10 registration, or encumbrance of a motor vehicle may be created, executed, and
11 submitted in electronic form when the office of motor vehicles or its agents have
12 the technological capability to do so, and shall not deny legal effect or
13 enforceability to a document solely due to its electronic form.
14 B. An electronic signature shall satisfy any requirement of law that a
15 document be signed, endorsed, or executed. Nothing in this Section shall be
16 construed to waive or eliminate any witnessing, acknowledgment, notarization,
17 or other execution formality otherwise required by law.

DIGEST
SB 72 Original 2026 Regular Session Hodges
Proposed law provides for documents required in connection with the sale, transfer, titling,
registration, or encumbrance of a motor vehicle to be submitted in electronic form when the
office of motor vehicles or its agents have the technological capability to do so.
Proposed law provides that the legal effect or enforceability of a document cannot be
disputed solely due to its electronic form.
Proposed law provides that when the technology exists any document required to be
notarized in connection with a motor vehicle transaction may be notarized electronically or
by remote online notarization in accordance with the Remote Online Notarization Act.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Adds R.S. 32:714)
