15 §707.2.1. Electronic signatures and records for motor vehicle transactions
16 A. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any document,
17 record, or instrument required in connection with the sale, lease, transfer,
18 titling, registration, or financing of a motor vehicle, unless otherwise exempted
19 by this Section shall be created, executed, transmitted, and stored electronically,
20 including but not limited to the following:
21 (1) Titling documents.
22 (2) Registration applications.
23 (3) Odometer disclosures.
24 (4) Secure and limited powers of attorney.
25 (5) Electronic attestations.
26 B.(1) Any documents used to transfer ownership of a vehicle to an
27 insurance company after payment of damages may be signed electronically and
28 reproduced in paper form and shall not require notarization. The supporting
29 documents include but are not limited to the following:
30 (a) Limited powers of attorney.
31 (b) Applications for duplicate titles.
32 (c) Odometer disclosure forms.
33 (2) Electronic signatures shall comply with the Authentication
34 Assurance Level 2 (AAL2) security standard as defined in the National Institute
35 of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800-63-3.
36 C.(1) The Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of motor
37 vehicles, shall accept electronic records and electronic signatures for the
38 documents and records identified in Subsection A of this Section and any
39 electronic signature or electronic record accepted by the department pursuant
40 to this Section shall have the same force and effect as an original written
41 document or handwritten signature and shall satisfy any legal requirement for
42 a written record or signature.
43 (2) The department shall promulgate rules and regulations in
44 accordance with the Administrative Procedure Act as may be necessary to
45 implement the provisions of this Section, including but not limited to rules
46 governing:
47 (a) Electronic signatures and authentication standards.
48 (b) Security and identity verification procedures.
49 D. An electronic signature that is authenticated in a manner approved
50 by the department shall satisfy any requirement for a written signature and
51 shall not require notarization.
52 E. An electronically signed, or a legibly scanned or imaged copy thereof,
53 shall have the same force and effect as an original paper document bearing a
54 handwritten signature and shall be admissible for all legal and evidentiary
55 purposes to the same extent as the original paper document.
56 F.(1) A dealer or authorized officer, as set forth in R.S. 32:702, using an
57 approved system in good faith and without actual knowledge of fraud or
58 unauthorized access, shall be immune from civil liability arising solely from the
59 use of electronic signatures, remote notarization of powers of attorney or
60 electronic records, including claims alleging improper execution, authentication,
61 or transmission, provided the dealer complied with the requirements of this
62 Section and any rules promulgated by the department.

3 §707.2.2. Secure digital motor vehicle titles
4 A. The Department of Public Safety and Corrections, office of motor
5 vehicles, shall implement a system that offers vehicle title holders a fully
6 paperless, secure digital vehicle title that may be issued in lieu of a paper
7 certificate of title.
8 B. Once implemented, the secure digital title system shall:
9 (1) Include all functions related to title reassignments.
10 (2) Meet or exceed applicable security requirements as set forth in
11 regulations promulgated by the department.
12 (3) Provide title holders with online, real-time access to motor vehicle
13 titles and status of titles in process.
14 C. A secure digital vehicle title produced under this Section shall be
15 regarded as an official title from the state of Louisiana and fully recognized
16 legally for any motor vehicle related transactions within the state and all other
17 United States jurisdictions.
18 D. The secure digital title system shall permit the electronic transfer,
19 reassignment, and recording of ownership of a motor vehicle. Ownership
20 transfers executed through an approved electronic title system shall have the
21 same legal effect as transfers executed through a paper certificate of title.
22 Licensed motor vehicle dealers may complete title assignments, reassignments
23 of ownership, and related documents through the electronic system without
24 requiring issuance of a paper certificate of title.
25 E. A duly certified copy of the office of motor vehicle's secure digital
26 title shall be admissible in any civil, criminal, or administrative proceeding in
27 this state as evidence of the existence of the title.
28 F. The commissioner of the office of motor vehicles shall promulgate any
29 rules and regulations necessary to implement the provisions of this Section.
