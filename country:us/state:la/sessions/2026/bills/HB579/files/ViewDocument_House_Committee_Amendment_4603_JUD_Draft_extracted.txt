13 §1844. Basic rights for victim and witness
14 * * *
15 O. Notification of pardon or parole.
16 (1) The Board of Pardons or the committee on parole, respectively, shall
17 notify the victim or the victim's family and all persons who file a victim registration
18 and notification form and the appropriate district attorney that a hearing has been set
19 for the person convicted of the crime. The victim or victim's family shall have the
20 right to make written and oral statements as to the impact of the crime at any hearing
21 before the board or committee and to rebut any statements or evidence introduced
22 by the inmate or defendant. The victim or the victim's family, a victim advocacy
23 group, and the district attorney or his representative may also appear before the board
24 or committee in person or by means of teleconference or telephone communication.
25 The board shall not prohibit the statement from being directed toward the inmate or
26 defendant unless the statement disturbs the decorum of the hearing.
27 * * *
28 W. Confidentiality of crime victims who are minors, victims of sex offenses,
29 and victims of human trafficking-related offenses.
30 (1)(a) In order to protect the identity and provide for the safety and welfare
31 of crime victims who are minors under the age of eighteen years and of victims of
32 sex offenses or human trafficking-related offenses, notwithstanding any provision
33 of law to the contrary, all public officials and officers and public agencies, including
34 but not limited to all law enforcement agencies, sheriffs, district attorneys, judicial
35 officers, clerks of court, the Crime Victims Reparations Board, and the Department
36 of Children and Family Services or any division thereof, shall not publicly disclose
37 the name, address, contact information, or identity of crime victims who at the time
38 of the commission of the offense are minors under eighteen years of age or of
39 victims of sex offenses or human trafficking-related offenses, regardless of the date
40 of commission of the offense. The confidentiality of the identity of the victim who
41 at the time of the commission of the offense is a minor under eighteen years of age
42 or the victim of a sex offense or human trafficking-related offense may be waived
43 by the victim. The public disclosure of the name of the juvenile crime victim by any
44 public official or officer or public agency is not prohibited by this Subsection when
45 the crime resulted in the death of the victim. Nothing in this Subsection shall be

6 (i) Except as provided in Subparagraph (d)(ii), a minor under the age of

9 (ii) A victim of any sex offense or human trafficking-related offense who is
10 also the victim of any criminal homicide enumerated in R.S. 14:29 or of any other
11 criminal offense which has as an element the death of the victim that is related to the
12 sex offense or human trafficking-related offense."

14 On page 1, delete lines 17 through 20 in their entirety and insert the following:
15 "(13) The right to be informed of these rights.
16 D. Any complaint about a violation of this Section may be submitted directly
17 to the Senate Select Committee on Women and Children or the House Select
18 Committee on Women and Children for legislative oversight.
19 Section 2. Any discretionary funds provided to the state through the Services,
20 Training, Officers, and Prosecutors (STOP) Violence Against Women Formula Grant
21 Program, administered by the Office on Violence Against Women (OVW), shall be used to
22 update and provide information on the notice required in accordance with this Act. The
23 notice shall be developed in consultation with sexual assault service providers and is subject
24 to approval by the Louisiana Sexual Assault Oversight Commission."
