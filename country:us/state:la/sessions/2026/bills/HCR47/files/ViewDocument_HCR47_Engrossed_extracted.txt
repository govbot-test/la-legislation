HLS 26RS-3002 ENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 47
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREEMAN
SCHOOLS/EMPLOYEES: Requests that the Department of Children and Family Services,
in consultation with the state Department of Education, study the implementation of
online training requirements for school employees relative to reporting child abuse
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To urge and request the Department of Children and Family Services, in consultation with
3 the state Department of Education, to study the implementation of the provisions of
4 Children's Code Article 603.1(B) relative to online training requirements pertaining
5 to reporting child abuse and to submit a report of findings and conclusions, including
6 any recommendations for related legislation, to the House Committee on Education
7 and the Senate Committee on Education no later than March 1, 2027.
8 WHEREAS, Children's Code Article 603 provides that the definition of "mandatory
9 reporter" includes a "teaching or child care provider", which is defined as any person who
10 provides or assists in the teaching, training, and supervision of a child, including any public
11 or private teacher, teacher's aide, instructional aide, school principal, school staff member,
12 bus driver, coach, professor, technical or vocational instructor, technical or vocational school
13 staff member, college or university administrator, college or university staff member, social
14 worker, an employee or an operator of an early learning center, an operator or employee of
15 a registered family child day care home, a licensed or unlicensed day care provider, or any
16 individual who provides these services to a child in a voluntary or professional capacity; and
17 WHEREAS, Children's Code Article 603.1(B) requires each teaching or child care
18 provider to complete an online training course provided by the Department of Children and
19 Family Services between June first and August thirty-first annually and requires that a record
20 of completion of the course by the teaching or child care provider be provided to each entity
21 at which he is employed; and

1 WHEREAS, Children's Code Article 603.1(B) further requires, no later than
2 September thirtieth of each year and in a manner prescribed by the state Department of
3 Education (DOE), each entity at which a teaching or child care provider is employed to
4 submit a list to DOE of all teaching and child care providers employed by the entity and
5 identify the employees that have complied with the training requirements and those
6 employees that have not complied; additionally, the law requires DOE to publish the
7 completion rates for each entity on its website; and
8 WHEREAS, concerns have been raised about the logistics of the implementation of
9 these requirements.
10 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
11 urge and request the Department of Children and Family Services, in consultation with the
12 state Department of Education, to study the implementation of the provisions of Children's
13 Code Article 603.1(B) relative to online training requirements pertaining to reporting child
14 abuse.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall include the consideration of the
16 option of offering the training in person so that it can be completed by a group of individuals
17 assembling for the training and possibly streamlining the training so that it can be completed
18 in a shorter period of time.
19 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Children and Family Services
20 shall consult with the state Department of Education which shall provide recommendations
21 concerning how to maximize the efficiency of the reporting requirements, including but not
22 limited to the feasibility of implementing a searchable database relative to training
23 completion.
24 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Children and Family Services
25 shall include in its report an estimation of any costs associated with any recommended
26 changes.
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Children and Family Services
28 shall submit a report of findings and conclusions, including any recommendations for related
29 legislation, to the House Committee on Education and the Senate Committee on Education
30 no later than March 1, 2027.
