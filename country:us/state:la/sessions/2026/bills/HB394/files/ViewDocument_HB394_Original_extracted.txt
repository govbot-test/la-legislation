HB 394 Original 2026 Regular Session Chenevert
Abstract: Provides relative to conditional parole for prisoners who need additional
rehabilitation prior to release.
Present law provides for parole consideration and hearings.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that the release date of a prisoner may be extended to no later than nine
months after the parole hearing or the most recent reconsideration of the prisoner's case if
the committee on parole determines that it is necessary for the prisoner to complete one or
more rehabilitative programs prior to his release to ensure public safety and enhance the
prisoner's opportunity for success.
Proposed law amends present law to change the maximum length of extension for the release
date from nine months to 12 months. Otherwise retains present law.
Proposed law provides that if the committee on parole determines that it is necessary for the
prisoner to complete a rehabilitative program prior to his release in order to ensure public
safety and enhance the prisoner's opportunity for success, the committee may grant
conditional parole that delays release for not more than 24 months pending the prisoner's
completion of any of the following programs:
(1) A regional reentry program administered through the Dept. of Public Safety and
Corrections (DPS&C).
(2) A reentry and workforce development program administered at the La. State
Penitentiary.
