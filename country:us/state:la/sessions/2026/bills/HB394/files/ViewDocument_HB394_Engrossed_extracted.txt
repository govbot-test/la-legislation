DIGEST

HB 394 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Chenevert
Abstract: Provides relative to conditional parole for prisoners who need additional
rehabilitation prior to release.
Present law provides for parole consideration and hearings.
Proposed law retains present law.
Present law provides that the release date of a prisoner may be extended to no later than nine
months after the parole hearing or the most recent reconsideration of the prisoner's case if
the committee on parole determines that it is necessary for the prisoner to complete one or
more rehabilitative programs prior to his release to ensure public safety and enhance the
prisoner's opportunity for success.
Proposed law amends present law to change the maximum length of extension for the release
date from nine months to 24 months. Otherwise retains present law.
(Amends R.S. 15:574.4.1(D)(2))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Administration of
Criminal Justice to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Remove proposed law relative to participation and completion of certain
rehabilitation programming and the prisoner's appearance before the committee
on parole for a final determination regarding the conditional grant of parole.

Section 3:
2. Change the maximum length of extension for the release date of a prisoner on
parole from 12 months to 24 months.

Section 4:
3. Make technical changes.
