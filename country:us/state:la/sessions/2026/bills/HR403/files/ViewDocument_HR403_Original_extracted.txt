HLS 26RS-6314 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 403
BY REPRESENTATIVE TERRY LANDRY
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To create the Task Force on Gestational Carrier Agreements and Assisted Reproductive
3 Regulation to study the implementation and regulation of gestational carrier
4 agreements in this state.
5 WHEREAS, advances in assisted reproductive technology and fertility treatment
6 have increased the use of gestational carrier arrangements throughout the United States; and
7 WHEREAS, Louisiana law presently contains provisions governing in vitro
8 fertilization and human embryos pursuant to R.S. 9:121 through 133, but does not provide
9 a comprehensive legal or regulatory framework governing gestational carrier agreements;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, issues related to gestational carrier arrangements implicate matters of
12 public health, family law, medical ethics, informed consent, parentage, child welfare,
13 privacy protections, and constitutional and federal nondiscrimination requirements; and
14 WHEREAS, the legislature recognizes the importance of protecting the health,
15 safety, dignity, and legal rights of gestational carriers, intended parents, and children born
16 through assisted reproductive arrangements; and
17 WHEREAS, the legislature further recognizes the need for multidisciplinary study
18 and stakeholder input prior to the consideration of comprehensive legislation concerning
19 gestational carrier agreements and assisted reproductive regulation in Louisiana.
20 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
21 Legislature of Louisiana does hereby establish and provide for the Task Force on Gestational
Page 1 of 5
HLS 26RS-6314 ORIGINAL
HR NO. 403
1 Carrier Agreements and Assisted Reproductive Regulation to study the implementation and
2 regulation of gestational carrier agreements in this state, and may propose recommendations,
3 together with specific proposals, for legislation. Any recommendations or proposals shall
4 be submitted by written report to the legislature and to the David R. Poynter Legislative
5 Research Library as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the Legislature
7 of Louisiana recommends that the task force include consideration of all of the following:
8 (1) Whether and how this state should recognize and regulate gestational carrier
9 agreements.
10 (2) Development of a regulatory framework consistent with the Human Embryo
11 Statutes, R.S. 9:121 through 133.
12 (3) Compliance with applicable federal nondiscrimination and privacy laws,
13 including the Affordable Care Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, and the Health
14 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act.
15 (4) Appropriate regulatory authority and oversight responsibilities among the
16 Louisiana Department of Health, the courts, the Department of Justice, and other state
17 agencies.
18 (5) Financial eligibility standards, escrow requirements, compensation protections,
19 and related safeguards for gestational carriers that protect participants without creating
20 discriminatory barriers.
21 (6) Standards governing informed consent and medical decision-making authority
22 during assisted reproductive and gestational carrier arrangements.
23 (7) Development of data collection, reporting, and record retention standards relating
24 to gestational carrier arrangements and assisted reproductive practices.
25 (8) Ethical considerations relating to reproductive medicine, family formation, and
26 maternal and child health.
27 (9) Development of a comprehensive legal and regulatory framework governing the
28 ethical creation, licensure oversight, and operation of private gestational carrier agencies in
29 this state.

1 (10) Procedures for the establishment of legal parentage and judicial recognition of
2 intended parents in gestational carrier arrangements.
3 (11) Birth certificate issuance and amendment procedures for children born pursuant
4 to gestational carrier agreements.
5 (12) A review of regulatory and statutory frameworks adopted in other states
6 governing gestational carrier agreements and assisted reproductive technology.
7 (13) Recommendations regarding judicial procedures, venue, confidentiality
8 protections, and evidentiary standards applicable to gestational carrier proceedings.
9 (14) The interaction between gestational carrier agreements and existing state laws
10 governing in vitro fertilization, embryo disposition, and juridical persons.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
12 members:
13 (1) One member who is the secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health or his
14 designee.
15 (2) One member who is the attorney general or his designee.
16 (3) One member who is the secretary of the Department of Children and Family
17 Services or his designee.
18 (4) One member who is the assistant secretary of the office on women's health and
19 community health or his designee.
20 (5) One member who is a healthcare professional specializing in maternal and fetal
21 health care appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives.
22 (6) One member who is a board-certified maternal-fetal medicine physician
23 appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives.
24 (7) One member who is a licensed attorney with expertise in family law and
25 reproductive law appointed by the speaker of the House of Representatives.
26 (8) One member who is a representative of the Louisiana State Law Institute with
27 expertise in family law or persons law appointed by the president of the Louisiana State Law
28 Institute.

HLS 26RS-6314 ORIGINAL

1 (9) One member who is a licensed psychologist or licensed professional counselor
2 with experience in third-party reproduction appointed by the speaker of the House of
3 Representatives.
4 (10) One member who advocates for reproductive rights appointed by the speaker
5 of the House of Representatives.
6 (11) One member appointed by the chair of the House Committee on Health and
7 Welfare.
8 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the chair of the task force shall be selected by
9 a majority vote of the members of the task force.
10 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force shall constitute a
11 quorum for the transaction of business, and all official actions of the task force shall require
12 the affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
13 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall convene for its first meeting
14 no later than September 1, 2026.
15 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall be staffed by the Louisiana
16 State Law Institute.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that in conducting this study, the task force is
18 encouraged to seek input and advice from members of the judiciary, medical licensing
19 boards, fertility clinics, legal scholars, bioethicists, vital records officials, and any other
20 stakeholders the task force deems necessary.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Rules of Order of the House of
22 Representatives or applicable Joint Rules shall apply to all meetings of the task force.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
24 without compensation, except per diem or expense reimbursement to which they may be
25 individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
26 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that meetings of the task force shall be subject to the
27 Open Meetings Law, R.S. 42:11 et seq., and records of the task force shall be subject to the
28 Public Records Law, R.S. 44:1 et seq.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that all members of the task force shall receive
30 written agenda materials and issue memoranda in advance of each meeting.

1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall submit its final written
2 report, including findings, recommendations, and proposed statutory language, not later than
3 February 15, 2027.
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate upon submission
5 of its final report or February 28, 2027, whichever occurs first.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
7 secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health, the attorney general, the secretary of the
8 Department of Children and Family Services, and the executive director of the Louisiana
9 Conference of Catholic Bishops.
