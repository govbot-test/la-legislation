23 "§73.14.1. Unlawful possession of images of another created by artificial
24 intelligence
25 A. It is unlawful for any person to possess any video or still image created
26 by artificial intelligence that depicts another person who is under the age of
27 seventeen years and is totally nude or in a state of undress so as to expose the
28 genitals, pubic area, buttocks, or female breasts, when the person possessing the
29 video or still image knows or has reason to know that the person is not licensed or
30 authorized to possess such video or still image.
31 B. For purposes of this Section, the following terms have the following
32 meanings:
33 (1) "Another person" includes a person whose image was used in creating,
34 adapting, or modifying a video or still image with the intent to depict an actual

1 person and who is recognizable as an actual person by the person's face, likeness, or
2 other distinguishing characteristic.
3 (2) "Artificial intelligence" means an artificial system developed in computer
4 software, physical hardware, or other context that solves tasks requiring human-like
5 perception, cognition, planning, learning, communication, or physical action.
6 (3) "Electronic mail service provider" means any person or entity, including
7 an internet service provider, that is an intermediary in sending or receiving electronic
8 mail or that provides to end users of the electronic mail service the ability to send or
9 receive electronic mail.
10 (4) "Interactive computer service" means any information service, system,
11 or access software provider that provides or enables computer access by multiple
12 users to a computer server, including specifically a service or system that provides
13 access to the internet and such systems operated or services offered by libraries or
14 educational institutions.
15 C. Whoever violates the provisions of this Section shall be imprisoned with
16 or without hard labor for not more than five years. At least one year of the sentence
17 of imprisonment shall be imposed without benefit of parole, probation, or suspension
18 of sentence.
19 D. This Section do not apply to an interactive computer service, electronic
20 mail service provider, or a provider of a telecommunications service or any
21 information service as defined in 47 U.S.C. 153, or a system or access software
22 provider that provides or enables computer access by multiple users to a computer
23 server that was used by a person to violate this Section.
