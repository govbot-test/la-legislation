SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 172
BY SENATOR REESE
A RESOLUTION
evaluate the funding structure of Louisiana's public postsecondary education
enterprise and the allocation of funding to higher education's two-year and four-year
institutions through the outcomes-based funding formula, and to provide
recommendations to the legislature and the Louisiana Board of Regents regarding
potential reforms, structure, and funding considerations for public postsecondary
education institutions.
WHEREAS, the state of Louisiana recognizes postsecondary education as a critical
driver of workforce development, economic competitiveness, civic engagement, and upward
mobility; and
WHEREAS, Article VIII, Section 5 of the Constitution of Louisiana provides that
the Louisiana Board of Regents shall plan, coordinate, and recommend funding for public
postsecondary education, and develop a formula for equitable distribution of funds to public
institutions of postsecondary education; and
WHEREAS, Act 462 of the 2014 Regular Session of the Legislature first required
the Board of Regents to adopt an outcomes-based funding formula for public postsecondary
education to allocate state appropriations among public postsecondary education institutions;
and
WHEREAS, funding for public postsecondary education is derived from multiple
sources; and
WHEREAS, a review of the history of higher education funding in Louisiana is a
critical part of a comprehensive analysis of resources provided and allocated to public
institutions across the state; and
WHEREAS, postsecondary institutions are facing a changing fiscal landscape,
characterized by higher operating, auxiliary, and athletic costs, funding deficits, outstanding
debt, fluctuating enrollment, concerns regarding affordability, student debt, institutional

stability, and changing perceptions regarding the value of obtaining a degree or credential;
and
WHEREAS, members of the legislature have recently raised questions and concerns
regarding the structure, implementation, transparency, and outcomes associated with the
current funding formula; and
WHEREAS, it is the responsibility of the legislature to appropriate funding for public
higher education and to ensure that funds are distributed in a fair, transparent, accountable,
and effective manner that advances critical performance outcomes such as student success
and workforce development across the state; and
WHEREAS, a comprehensive review of public postsecondary funding and the
funding formula will provide the legislature the opportunity to both measure the adequacy
of funding provided and examine whether the formula appropriately accounts for
institutional missions, enrollment trends, student outcomes, regional needs, workforce
priorities, and operational costs.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby establish and provide for the Legislative Higher Education Funding and
Formula Task Force to study the Louisiana Board of Regents' funding formula and related
funding methodologies for public postsecondary education institutions, and to propose
recommendations, together with specific proposals for legislation, by written report to the
Senate Committee on Finance, House Committee on Appropriations, Senate Committee on
Education, House Committee on Education, and to the David R. Poynter Legislative
Research Library as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772, no later than February 26, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall conduct, at minimum, the
following:
(1) An assessment of historical and current funding structures and primary
sources of financially supporting public postsecondary education including
State General Fund appropriations, statutory dedications, federal grants,
tuition and mandatory fees, auxiliary enterprises, private philanthropy, and
other institutional revenues.
(2) An assessment of historical and current formula methodologies and metrics
Page 2 of 4
SR NO. 172 ENROLLED
used in the calculation of Louisiana's public higher education funding
formula for two-year and four-year institutions.
(3) A comparative analysis of Louisiana's funding structures and funding
formula with national best practices and those of regional peer states.
(4) A longitudinal evaluation of Louisiana's postsecondary education state
funding, performance-based funding metrics, accountability measures, and
the changing costs of educating students.
(5) Recommendations for legislation, if any, necessary to strengthen higher
education funding to provide financial stability in Louisiana and improve the
public postsecondary formula structure to drive outcomes and institutional
performance in alignment with workforce needs.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall consist of the following
members:
(1) The chair of the House Committee on Appropriations, or his designee.
(2) The chair of the Senate Committee on Finance, or his designee.
(3) Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the speaker of
the House of Representatives.
(4) Two members of the Senate appointed by the president of the Senate.
(5) The commissioner of higher education, or his designee.
(6) One chancellor of a two-year community or technical college appointed by
the president of the Louisiana Community and Technical Colleges System.
(7) The president of the Louisiana State University and Agricultural and
Mechanical College System, or his designee, as an ex officio nonvoting
member.
(8) The president of the Southern University and Agricultural and Mechanical
College System, or his designee, as an ex officio nonvoting member.
(9) The president of the University of Louisiana System, or his designee, as an
ex officio nonvoting member.
(10) The president of the Louisiana Community and Technical Colleges System,
or his designee, as an ex officio nonvoting member.

2026.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED the task force shall convene for its first meeting no
later than September 1, 2026, at the call of the commissioner of higher education, and at the
first meeting, the president of the Senate shall appoint a chairman and the members shall
elect other officers as the task force may deem appropriate.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a majority of the task force shall constitute a
quorum for the transaction of business. All official actions of the task force shall require the
affirmative vote of a majority of the members.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the members of the task force shall serve
without compensation, except per diem or expenses reimbursement to which they may be
individually entitled as members of their constituent organizations.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the task force shall terminate on the date of the
submission of its report or February 26, 2027, whichever occurs first.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
commissioner of higher education, the president of the Louisiana State University System,
the president of the Southern University System, the president of the University of Louisiana
System, and the president of the Louisiana Community and Technical Colleges System.
