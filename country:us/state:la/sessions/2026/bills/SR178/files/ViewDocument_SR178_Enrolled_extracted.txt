adults; and

organizations may play an important role in supporting prevention, awareness, and early
intervention efforts; and
WHEREAS, modern public health approaches increasingly utilize digital education
tools, voluntary self-assessment resources, and mobile support services to improve
accessibility and engagement; and
WHEREAS, Louisiana has the opportunity to evaluate innovative and
evidence-informed approaches to problem gambling prevention and support that may
improve access to information, education, and behavioral health resources across the state.

SR NO. 178 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to study opportunities to
modernize Louisiana's problem gambling prevention and education efforts, with a particular
focus on youth, college-age populations, and young adults.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the department is urged to examine opportunities
for age-appropriate educational resources and curriculum addressing gambling-related
harms, financial literacy, decision-making, online gaming environments, and responsible
behavior.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the department is urged to examine the use of
voluntary digital tools and mobile resources designed to support prevention, self-assessment,
awareness, early intervention, and connection to behavioral health and recovery support
services.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the department is urged to consult with the
Louisiana Department of Education, the Louisiana Board of Regents, colleges and
universities, behavioral health providers, community organizations, researchers, and other
relevant stakeholders in developing its findings and recommendations.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the department is urged to submit a written
report of its findings and recommendations to the Senate and the David R. Poynter

2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 178
BY SENATOR MIZELL
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Louisiana Department of Health to study and make
recommendations regarding modernization of the state's problem gambling
prevention and education efforts, particularly among youth and young adults, and to
examine opportunities for school-based education, higher education engagement,
digital support resources, and early intervention strategies.
WHEREAS, legalized sports wagering and other forms of gambling have expanded
significantly in recent years across Louisiana and the United States; and
WHEREAS, young people and young adults are increasingly exposed to
gambling-related content through sports, online gaming, social media, advertising, and
mobile applications; and
WHEREAS, problem gambling may negatively impact mental health, financial
well-being, academic performance, family stability, and workforce participation; and
WHEREAS, many individuals experiencing gambling-related harm do not seek
formal treatment or support until significant personal or financial consequences have
occurred; and
WHEREAS, prevention and early education efforts may help improve awareness of
gambling-related risks and encourage healthier decision-making among youth and young
adults; and
WHEREAS, schools, colleges, universities, healthcare providers, and community
organizations may play an important role in supporting prevention, awareness, and early
intervention efforts; and
WHEREAS, modern public health approaches increasingly utilize digital education
