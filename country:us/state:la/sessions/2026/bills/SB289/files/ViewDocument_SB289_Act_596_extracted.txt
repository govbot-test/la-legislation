SENATE BILL NO. 289
BY SENATOR ABRAHAM
1
2 AN ACT
3 To enact R.S. 44:3.7 and to repeal R.S. 44:4(56), relative to public records; to provide for
4 exemptions to the public records law for certain university records; to provide for
5 confidentiality of applicants for certain executive-level university positions; to
6 provide for the privacy of university donors; to provide for the confidentiality of
7 research and proprietary information at universities; and to provide for related
8 matters.
9 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
10 Section 1. R.S. 44:3.7 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 §3.7. Certain records of public postsecondary education institutions
12 A. Nothing in this Chapter shall be construed to require the disclosure
13 of records, or the information contained therein, held by a public postsecondary
14 education institution, which records are:
15 (1) Applications, resumes, or the personally identifiable information of
16 an applicant for president, chancellor, senior vice chancellor, or athletic coach
17 at the public postsecondary education institution, unless the applicant has
18 authorized the disclosure of such records and information. This exemption shall
19 expire once an applicant is approved for the position by the governing board,
20 at which time the records of the top three finalists, including the approved
21 applicant, shall become public. This exemption shall continue for any applicant
22 that is not among the top three finalists for the position, unless the applicant has
23 authorized the disclosure of such records and information.
24 (2) Any portion of a record that identifies the name of a donor to the

20 to exceed an additional six months.

23 policy intended for the safety of students or employees of a postsecondary
24 education institution, personally identifiable information of any reported
25 witness to the reported violation, and, if the reported violation involves violence
26 or abuse, personally identifiable information of any person who may be a victim
27 of violence or abuse directly related to the reported violation if such information
28 is in the custody or control of the postsecondary education institution or
29 management board and was received in accordance with adopted, written
