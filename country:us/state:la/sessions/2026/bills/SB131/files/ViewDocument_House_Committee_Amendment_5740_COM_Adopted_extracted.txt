HOUSE COMMITTEE AMENDMENTS

Amendments proposed by House Committee on Commerce to Reengrossed Senate Bill No.

1 AMENDMENT NO. 1
2 On page 3, delete lines 14 through 22 in their entirety and insert in lieu thereof the following:
3 "D.(1) When a professional or occupational board initiates a
4 disciplinary proceeding against a licensee and the final disposition of the
5 proceeding results in a consent order, judgment, or finding in which the licensee
6 is the prevailing party, the board shall not collect from the licensee any attorney
7 fees or costs incurred by the board after a written offer of judgment.
8 (2) For purposes of this Subsection, a licensee is deemed the prevailing
9 party when either of the following occurs:
10 (a) The licensee made a written offer of judgment, and the matter is
11 subsequently terminated, dismissed, or resolved without an adverse finding
12 against the licensee.
13 (b)(i) The consent order, judgment, or finding results in a disposition
14 more favorable to the licensee than the terms previously offered by the licensee
15 pursuant to this Subsection.
16 (ii) A result is considered more favorable to the licensee under any of the
17 following circumstances:
18 (aa) The licensee offered a suspension period, and the final consent
19 order, judgment, or finding imposes a suspension period shorter than or equal
20 to that offered by the licensee.
21 (bb) The licensee offered a probationary period, and the final consent
22 order, judgment, or finding imposes a probationary period shorter than or
23 equal to that offered by the licensee.
24 (cc) The licensee offered payment of a monetary fine, and the final
25 consent order, judgment, or finding imposes only a monetary fine in an amount
26 less than or equal to that offered by the licensee.
27 (dd) The licensee offered a suspension of licensure, and the final consent
28 order, judgment, or finding imposes no suspension of licensure, regardless of
29 whether the final disposition includes a fine, probation, or both. For purposes
30 of this Subitem, one day of suspension shall be deemed equivalent to fifteen days
31 of probation when comparing disciplinary sanctions that include both
32 suspension and probationary periods.
33 (ee) The final consent order, judgment, or finding imposes a combined
34 suspension and probationary period that is less severe than or equal to that
35 offered by the licensee. For purposes of this Subitem, one day of suspension
36 shall be deemed equivalent to fifteen days of probation when comparing
37 disciplinary sanctions that include both suspension and probationary periods.
38 (3) The provisions of this Subsection supersede any agency rule,
39 regulation, custom, or practice governing the payment of attorney fees and costs
40 to the extent that such rule, regulation, custom, or practice is less favorable to
41 the licensee."
