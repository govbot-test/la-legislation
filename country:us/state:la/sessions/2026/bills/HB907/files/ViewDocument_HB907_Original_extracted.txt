HEALTH CARE/PROVIDERS: Provides civil and criminal immunity for the distribution
or use of Naloxone and other opioid antagonists beyond their shelf-life end date
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:978.2.3, relative to prescriptions; to provide for civil and criminal
3 immunity for the distribution or use of Naloxone and other opioid antagonists
4 beyond their shelf-life end date; and to provide for related matters.
5 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
6 Section 1. R.S. 40:978.2.3 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
7 §978.2.3. Naloxone; distribution or use after shelf-life end date; limitation of
8 liability
9 A. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a person who
10 prescribes, dispenses, distributes, or furnishes naloxone or another opioid antagonist
11 shall not be subject to criminal or civil liability solely on the basis that any labeled
12 shelf-life end date for such product has passed.
13 B. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a person acting in
14 good faith who furnishes or administers naloxone or another opioid antagonist,
15 including one for which any labeled shelf-life end date has passed, to an individual
16 reasonably believed to be undergoing an opioid-related drug overdose shall be
17 immune from criminal and civil liability for such furnishing or administration except
18 in the event of gross negligence or willful or wanton misconduct.

HB 907 Original 2026 Regular Session Miller
Abstract: Provides for civil and criminal immunity for the distribution or use of opioid
antagonists beyond their shelf-life end dates and prohibits disciplinary actions by
professional licensing boards for healthcare professionals granted civil and criminal
immunity.
Proposed law prohibits civil and criminal liability for the use of naloxone and other opioid
antagonists solely on the basis that any labeled shelf-life end date for such product has
passed.
Proposed law prohibits civil or criminal liability for any person acting in good faith who
furnishes or administers naloxone or another opioid antagonist to an individual reasonably
believed to be undergoing an opioid-related drug overdose except in the event of gross
negligence or willful or wanton misconduct.
Proposed law prohibits healthcare professionals from disciplinary action by professional
licensing boards when granted civil and criminal immunity under the proposed provisions.
Proposed law defines the term "shelf-life end date".
Proposed law mandates that a federally certified health care provider, facility, or entity
comply with federal regulations and requirements regarding prohibiting the prescribing,
