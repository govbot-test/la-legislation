BY REPRESENTATIVE PHELPS

Association's 2026 Mission: Lifeline EMS - Gold Achievement Award.
WHEREAS, the American Heart Association's Mission: Lifeline EMS program
focuses on quality of life care and outcomes for pre-hospital stroke, heart attack, and cardiac
arrest patients by connecting all care components into a seamlessly integrated care system;
the key tenets of these systems of care reinforce evidence-based guidelines and measure
performance, identify gaps, and engage in quality improvement; and
WHEREAS, the Mission: Lifeline EMS recognition program is designed to showcase
pre-hospital agencies across the nation for their excellence in heart attack and stroke care;
and
WHEREAS, the Shreveport Fire Department has more than five hundred firefighters
who are certified as an EMT; and
WHEREAS, some months there are as many as fifty cardiac arrests within the city's
jurisdictional boundaries; the Shreveport Fire Department managed to revive 60% of its
cardiac arrest patients in one month; and
WHEREAS, the Shreveport Fire Department fields nearly fifty thousand calls for
service each year, and its response time averages around three minutes; the department uses
evidence-based diagnosis in the field to coordinate its care with hospitals; and
WHEREAS, the Shreveport Fire Department provides national health standards for
all of its parish members; the department's firefighters and paramedics are trained to
recognize symptoms quickly, perform 12-lead EKGs in the field, and activate hospital stroke
or cardiac teams before the patient arrives; and

WHEREAS, the Shreveport Fire Department has received the 2026 Mission:
Lifeline EMS - Gold Achievement Award from the American Heart Association for the
second year in a row, and the department and its members are most deserving of the highest
recognition for their commitment to providing the highest level of pre-hospital cardiac arrest
and stroke care.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby commend the Shreveport Fire Department on being
awarded the American Heart Association's 2026 Mission: Lifeline EMS - Gold Achievement
Award and does hereby extend enduring appreciation for the department's lifesaving efforts,
which exceed expectations set by national standards.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
