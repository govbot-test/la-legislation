HB 632 Original 2026 Regular Session Spell
Abstract: Provides relative to information sharing for purposes of the Louisiana's
Foundational Integrated Research System for Transformation (LA FIRST).
LA FIRST, generally
Present law requires the Bd. of Regents to establish and maintain Louisiana's Foundational
Integrated Research System for Transformation (LA FIRST) at the University of La. at
Lafayette within the Kathleen Babineaux Blanco Public Policy Center (Blanco Center).
Provides for data sharing agreements between various entities, including specified state
agencies, for the purpose of the publication of annual reports on various topics, including
education, workforce development, and criminal justice. Proposed law retains present law.

Present law provides that the shared data shall be separated into two parts, personal
information and nonpersonal information. Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law, for present law and proposed law purposes, defines "personal information"
as a person's social security number, full name, and date of birth and "nonpersonal
information" as all other data housed in the LA FIRST data system.
Present law provides that personal information shall be used only to generate a unique
identifier for each record, which shall be combined with nonpersonal information to allow
for the matching of records across the data system. Proposed law removes the requirement
that the identifier be combined for such purpose and instead allows personal information to
be combined for such purpose or for enhanced analysis. Further requires that the personal
information be removed from the files used in the research and analysis after the identifier
has been assigned.
Present law prohibits research and analysis from being conducted on data that includes
personally identifiable information. Proposed law instead prohibits this on data that includes
personal information.
LA FIRST, K-12 student information
Present law requires each city, parish, or other local public school board to directly share
student data with the Blanco Center and agencies specified in present law. Proposed law
broadens the application of present law to the governing authorities of all public schools
(including charter schools) and instead requires that they share personal information for their
students directly with the state Dept. of Education and requires the department to share the
information with the Blanco Center.
Proposed law further requires that the initial set of personal information that governing
authorities share with the department include existing personal information for current
students as well as at least five years of historical personal information available for former
students. Requires the department to share this information with the Blanco Center and that
shared data be updated at least annually.
Proposed law authorizes the department to implement a streamlined information sharing
process for proposed law purposes, including incorporating proposed law information
sharing requirements into existing data-sharing agreements.
Present law relative to student data privacy prohibits sharing a student's personally
identifiable information and provides exceptions. Proposed law adds an exception for
present law and proposed law relative to LA FIRST.
(Amends R.S. 17:3138.12(F) and (H)(2); Adds R.S. 17:3914(P) and 3996(B)(24))
