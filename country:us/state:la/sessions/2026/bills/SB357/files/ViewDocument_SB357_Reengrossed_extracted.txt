SLS 26RS-636 REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY SENATOR REESE
COURTS. Provides relative to the courts and the judiciary. (8/1/26)
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 13:72.1 and 74, and R.S. 40:2405(B), to enact R.S. 13:103.1, and
3 to repeal R.S. 13:75, relative to the courts and the judiciary; to provide relative to the
4 supreme court; to provide relative to courts of last resort of other states; to provide
5 relative to adoption of rules; to provide relative to criers for the supreme court; to
6 provide relative to the court's security personnel; to provide relative to statewide
7 police power; to provide relative to the deputy secretary of public safety services; to
8 provide relative to a justice's primary residence; to provide relative to certain
9 vouchered expenses; to provide relative to reimbursement for official expenses; to
10 provide relative to duly adopted rules of the court; to provide relative to employee
11 compensation; to provide relative to public retirement systems; to provide relative
12 to retirement of a crier of the supreme court; to provide for commissions issued to
13 court security personnel; and to provide for related matters.
14 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
15 Section 1. R.S. 13:72.1 and 74 are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 13:103.1
16 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
17 §72.1. Declaration of state law to federal or other courts

1 A. The supreme court of this state may, by rule of court, provide that when
2 it shall appear to the Supreme Court of the United States, or to any court of appeals
3 of the United States, or to the courts of last resort of other states, that there are
4 involved, in any proceeding before it, questions or propositions of the laws of this
5 state, which are determinative of the said cause, and there is no clear controlling
6 precedent in the decisions of the supreme court of this state, such federal appellate
7 court may certify such questions or propositions of the laws of this state to the
8 supreme court of this state for instructions concerning such questions or propositions
9 of state law, which certificate the supreme court of this state may, by written opinion,
10 answer.
11 B. The supreme court of this state is hereby authorized and empowered to
12 adopt all rules it deems appropriate to administer this Section and to collaborate
13 with any and all other courts of last resort of other states and of the United States in
14 the preparation and approval of uniform rules of court to make effective this and
15 similar laws. The provisions of Subsection A of this Section are applicable and
16 operative only to the extent of any rules duly adopted by the supreme court.
17 * * *
18 §74. Crier for Supreme Court; salary
19 There shall may be a crier one or more criers for the supreme court, who
20 shall be appointed by the judges of the supreme court from among the court's
21 security personnel, and be commissioned by the civil sheriff for the parish of
22 Orleans as a deputy sheriff. The crier shall receive from the state treasury on his own
23 warrant approved by the chief justice the sum of thirty-one hundred dollars, per
24 annum, payable monthly. Security personnel commissioned pursuant to this
25 Section shall be considered members of a bona fide police agency and may
26 additionally be designated by the court as requiring statewide police power
27 pursuant to R.S. 40:1379.1 in the same manner as provided for in Subsection N
28 of that Section. Upon request, the deputy secretary of public safety services, or
29 his designee, may also facilitate additional security assistance for the court.

2 §103.1. Expenses of the supreme court
3 A. Any justice whose primary residence is more than fifty miles from the
4 courthouse may elect all or part of any amount due under R.S. 13:103 to be
5 reimbursed as a vouchered expense or otherwise allowed pursuant to an
6 accountable plan maintained in accordance with Title 26 of the United States
7 Code, provided that the election shall be for an entire fiscal year and the total
8 amount elected or claimed shall not exceed the amounts otherwise provided for
9 under that Section.
10 B. Reimbursement for other official expenses paid from available funds
11 shall be subject to the court's duly adopted rules.
12 C. Any amounts reimbursed under this Section shall not be considered
13 employee compensation for the purposes of any public retirement system.
14 Section 2. R.S. 40:2405(B) is hereby amended and reenacted to read as follows:
15 §2405. Peace officer training requirements; reimbursement by peace officer
16 * * *
17 B.(1) Additionally, the receipt of supplemental pay from the municipal police
18 officers fund, the deputy sheriffs' fund, the state police fund, or out of the funds
19 appropriated for military police by the Military Department, state of Louisiana, shall
20 be contingent upon the recipient's beginning employment as a peace officer after
21 January 1, 1986, successfully completing a council-approved training program, and
22 passing the council-approved examination within one calendar year from date of
23 initial employment.
24 (2) Certified security personnel of the Supreme Court of Louisiana or of any
25 court of appeal of the state shall not be eligible to receive supplemental pay benefits
26 even though the peace officer has successfully completed a council-approved
27 training program. The commission issued to court security personnel shall remain
28 in force and in effect at the pleasure of the employing court.

Present law (R.S. 13:72.1) provides that the supreme court of this state may, by rule of court,
provide that when it shall appear to the U.S. Supreme Court, or to any U.S. court of appeals,
that there are involved, in any proceeding before it, questions or propositions of the laws of
this state, which are determinative of the said cause, and there is no clear controlling
precedent in the decisions of the supreme court of this state, such federal appellate court may
certify such questions or propositions of the laws of this state to the supreme court of this
state for instructions concerning such questions or propositions of state law, which certificate
the supreme court of this state may, by written opinion, answer.
Proposed law retains present law, and provides that the supreme court of this state may, by
rule of court, provide that when it shall appear to the U.S. Supreme Court, or to any U.S.
court, or to the courts of last resort of other states, that there are involved, in any proceeding
before it, questions or propositions of the laws of this state, which are determinative of the
said cause, and there is no clear controlling precedent in the decisions of the supreme court
of this state, such court may certify such questions or propositions of the laws of this state
to the supreme court of this state for instructions concerning such questions or propositions
of state law, which certificate the supreme court of this state may, by written opinion,
answer.
Present law provides that the supreme court of this state is hereby authorized and empowered
to collaborate with any and all other courts of last resort of other states and of the U.S. in the
preparation and approval of uniform rules of court to make effective this and similar laws.
Proposed law provides that the supreme court of this state is hereby authorized and
empowered to adopt all rules it deems appropriate to administer proposed law and to
collaborate with any and all other courts of last resort of other states and of the U.S. in the
preparation and approval of uniform rules of court to make effective this and similar laws.
Further provides that the provisions of proposed law are applicable and operative only to the
extent of any rules duly adopted by the supreme court.
Present law (R.S. 13:74) provides that there shall be a crier for the supreme court, who shall
be appointed by the judges of the supreme court, and be commissioned by the civil sheriff
for the parish of Orleans as a deputy sheriff. Provides that the crier shall receive from the
state treasury on his own warrant approved by the chief justice the sum of $3,100.00, per
annum, payable monthly.
Proposed law repeals the present law requirement that the crier shall receive from the state
treasury on his own warrant approved by the chief justice the sum of $3,100.00, per annum,
payable monthly.
Proposed law provides that there may be one or more criers for the supreme court, who shall
be appointed by the judges of the supreme court from among the court's security personnel,
and be commissioned by the civil sheriff for the parish of Orleans as a deputy sheriff.
Provides that the crier shall receive from the state treasury on his own warrant approved by
the chief justice the sum of $3,100.00, per annum, payable monthly. Further provides that
security personnel commissioned pursuant to proposed law shall be considered members of
a bona fide police agency and may additionally be designated by the court as requiring
