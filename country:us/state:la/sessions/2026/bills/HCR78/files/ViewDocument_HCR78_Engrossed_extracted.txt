2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 78
BY REPRESENTATIVES DOMANGUE, BRAUD, BRYANT, GEYMANN, KERNER,
MACK, ORGERON, AND ZERINGUE
SEAFOOD: Memorializes Congress to pass the American Seafood Competitiveness Act of
2026
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To memorialize the United States Congress to take such actions as are necessary to pass the
3 American Seafood Competitiveness Act of 2026.
4 WHEREAS, Louisiana's commercial fisheries are a vital part of the state's culture
5 and economy, with one out of every seventy jobs in the state related to the seafood industry
6 and an economic impact of over two billion dollars annually for Louisiana, and
7 WHEREAS, commercial fishing industries in the state have faced increasing pressure
8 from imported products and their impact on the domestic seafood market; and
9 WHEREAS, the American Seafood Competitiveness Act of 2026, an important step
10 in fulfilling President Trump's Executive Order on Restoring American Seafood
11 Competitiveness, expands access to United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) loans,
12 grants, and credit programs for commercial fishing and seafood processing businesses; and
13 WHEREAS, the Act would amend the Consolidated Farm and Rural Development
14 Act to include commercial fishing and fish processing as activities eligible for USDA
15 programs; and
16 WHEREAS, the Act would include wild-caught fish and shellfish as eligible
17 agricultural products under the USDA's Local Agriculture Market Program; and
18 WHEREAS, under the Act, commercial fishermen would be eligible for USDA farm
19 ownership loans, which they could use to purchase commercial fishing licenses and vessels,

1 and for farm operating loans, which they could use to cover operating and maintenance
2 costs; and
3 WHEREAS, under the Act, fish processors would also be eligible for farm ownership
4 loans, which they could use to acquire or upgrade processing facilities, and for farm
5 operating loans, which they could use to help address the significant operating and
6 maintenance costs that continue to rise; and
7 WHEREAS, the Act would extend eligibility for farm credit to those businesses that
8 provide services to commercial fishermen and fish processing facilities so that businesses
9 and rural communities that rely on the coastal fishing economy would gain an additional
10 option to finance their businesses in the same way that businesses and communities that rely
11 on the farming economy do; and
12 WHEREAS, to ensure effective implementation, the Act further directs the USDA
13 to conduct outreach, provide technical assistance, train agency staff, and coordinate with the
14 National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration and other federal and state agencies to
15 make sure the USDA programs are accessible to the commercial fishing industry; and
16 WHEREAS, Louisiana's commercial fishermen and processors, as well as the coastal
17 communities they support, would gain improved access to capital, strengthened domestic
18 seafood markets, and support for the long-term competitiveness of domestic commercial
19 fishing and processing.
20 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
21 memorialize the United States Congress to take such actions as are necessary to pass the
22 American Seafood Competitiveness Act of 2026.
23 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
24 presiding officers of the Senate and the House of Representatives of the Congress of the
25 United States of America and to each member of the Louisiana congressional delegation.
