2026 Regular Session ENROLLED
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 161
BY SENATOR BARROW
A RESOLUTION
To direct the office of motor vehicles, Department of Public Safety and Corrections, to study
options for regulating and reducing fees charged by licensed driver education and
training schools operating in Louisiana.
WHEREAS, driver education is required by law for Louisiana residents seeking
drivers' licenses and specifically and overwhelmingly affects minors and first-time drivers;
and
WHEREAS, the cost of private driver instruction programs varies substantially
across regions of the state and may create financial barriers for low-income and working
families; and
WHEREAS, limited statewide data exists regarding the comparative cost of driver
education programs; and
WHEREAS, the state has an interest in ensuring driver training remains accessible
while maintaining instructional quality, public safety standards, and affordability.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
does hereby direct the office of motor vehicles to conduct a study concerning driver
education and training schools and regulating costs in Louisiana.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the study shall examine, at minimum, the
following:
(1) Current tuition, fees, testing costs, cancellation fees, and ancillary charges
assessed by licensed driver education providers.
(2) Regional differences in pricing throughout Louisiana.
(3) Factors contributing to cost increases.
(4) Comparative practices used by other states to regulate or support affordability in
driver education.
(5) Recommendations for legislation or administrative rule changes to increase
affordability and regulate pricing.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the office of motor vehicles may consult and
collaborate with the Louisiana Legislative Auditor in conducting this study.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the office of motor vehicles shall submit a
written report of findings and recommendations to the Senate Committee on Transportation,
Highways and Public Works and the Senate President Pro Tempore, and to the David R.
Poynter Legislative Research Library as required by R.S. 24:771 and 772, no later than
February 1, 2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
commissioner of the office of motor vehicles.
PRESIDENT OF THE SENATE
