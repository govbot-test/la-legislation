CRIMINAL PROCEDURE. Requires seventeen year olds and above be prosecuted in
district court for traffic offenses. (2/3 - CA5s19) (8/1/26)

2 To amend and reenact Children's Code Art. 804(3) and to enact Children's Code Art. 951.1,
3 relative to juvenile traffic violations; to provide for definitions; and to provide for
4 related matters.

6 Section 1. Children's Code Art. 804(3) is hereby amended and reenacted and
7 Children's Code Art. 951.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
8 Art. 804. Definitions
9 As used in this Title:

11 (3) "Delinquent act" means an act committed by a child of ten years of age
12 or older which if committed by an adult is designated an offense under the statutes
13 or ordinances of this state, or of another state if the offense occurred there, or under
14 federal law, except traffic violations. It includes an act constituting an offense under
15 R.S. 14:95.8, an act constituting an offense under R.S. 14:81.1.1(A)(2), and a direct
16 contempt of court committed by a child. "Delinquent act" shall not include a
17 violation of R.S. 14:82, 83.3, 83.4, 89, or 89.2 for a child who, during the time of the

DIGEST
SB 258 Original 2026 Regular Session Lambert
Present law defines a "delinquent act" as an act committed by a child of ten years of age or
older which if committed by an adult is designated an offense under the statutes or
ordinances of this state, or of another state if the offense occurred there, or under federal law,
except traffic violations.
Proposed law retains present law, except as it relates to traffic violations.
Proposed law defines a "child" as any person under the age of 21, including an emancipated
minor, who commits a delinquent act or traffic violation before attaining 17 years of age,
relative to traffic violations.
Effective August 1, 2026.
(Amends Ch. C. Art. 804(3); adds Ch. C. Art. 951.1)
