HB 779 Original 2026 Regular Session Freeman
Abstract: Authorizes the La. Dept. of Health (LDH) to provide for the sexually associated
infections that may be treated with expedited partner therapy.
Present law provides that a healthcare professional may, when treating a person for
gonorrhea or chlamydia, prescribe medication to treat the patient's sexual partners without
examining the patient's sexual partners, known as expedited partner therapy.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that trichomoniasis and any other sexually
associated infection determined by the LDH may be treated with expedited partner therapy.
Proposed law adds that a healthcare professional or pharmacist acting reasonably and in
good faith shall not be criminally or civilly liable for harm arising out of expedited partner
therapy.
Proposed law provides that LDH shall establish a list of sexually associated infections
eligible for expedited partner therapy.
(Amends R.S. 40:1121.6(A), (B), and (C)(intro. para.); Adds R.S. 40:1121.6(F) and (G))
