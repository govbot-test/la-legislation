SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 39
BY SENATORS WOMACK, CLOUD, LUNEAU, MORRIS, REESE AND SEABAUGH
AND REPRESENTATIVES BUTLER, CARRIER, DESHOTEL,

SCHAMERHORN
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and honors Dr. Paul Dean Coreil on the occasion of his
retirement as Chancellor of Louisiana State University of Alexandria and to recognize his
many accomplishments in higher education, public service, youth development, and the
Louisiana Cooperative Extension Service.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and honor Dr. Paul Dean Coreil on the occasion of his retirement as
3 Chancellor of Louisiana State University of Alexandria and to recognize his many
4 accomplishments in higher education, public service, youth development, and the
5 Louisiana Cooperative Extension Service.
6 WHEREAS, Dr. Paul Dean Coreil has devoted more than forty-three years of
7 distinguished service to the LSU System, including thirteen years on the campus of
8 Louisiana State University of Alexandria and eight years as Chancellor of LSUA, and his
9 career reflects an extraordinary commitment to the people and communities of Louisiana;
10 and
11 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil returned to LSUA in 2019 with a bold vision for growth,
12 access, innovation, and public service, and under his leadership the university experienced
13 one of the most transformative periods in its history; and
14 WHEREAS, during his tenure as chancellor, LSUA achieved record enrollment
15 growth, surpassing an ambitious goal of increasing enrollment by fifty percent within five
16 years in only three years, and growing to more than eight thousand students, a one hundred
17 forty-five percent increase during his tenure; and
18 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil's leadership helped establish LSUA as the fastest-growing

2 largest university in the state, while continuing to expand educational opportunity for
3 students across Louisiana and beyond; and
4 WHEREAS, under his leadership, LSUA reached another all-time enrollment record
5 of seven thousand seven hundred twenty-eight students in the Spring 2026 semester, further
6 demonstrating the sustained institutional momentum achieved during his administration; and
7 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil championed workforce-aligned academic innovation by
8 supporting the expansion of Professional Aviation, Accountants for the Future, Educators
9 Rising, the Interdisciplinary Pre-Med program, the LEAD V program, and a 3+3
10 Occupational Therapy pathway with LSU Health Shreveport; and
11 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil also advanced the development of a Health Education
12 Campus designed to expand nursing and allied-health training in central Louisiana, helping
13 address critical workforce shortages in the region; and
14 WHEREAS, under his watch, LSUA and the LSU System moved forward with
15 Louisiana's first three-year accelerated bachelor's degrees in STEM fields, including
16 Information Technology and Bioinformatics, demonstrating a forward-looking model of
17 workforce-aligned undergraduate education; and
18 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil's administration also oversaw the completion of key campus
19 projects, including the Martin Family Student Success Center, which stands as a lasting
20 investment in student access, support, and success; and
21 WHEREAS, during his tenure, LSUA gained increasing statewide and national
22 recognition, including ranking as the number one safest college campus in Louisiana and the
23 number seven safest college campus in America by Niche, and rising to number four in
24 Niche's 2025 Best Colleges in Louisiana rankings; and
25 WHEREAS, before his chancellorship, Dr. Coreil served with distinction as Vice
26 Chancellor of the LSU AgCenter and Director of the Louisiana Cooperative Extension
27 Service from 2001 until 2013, following an earlier career that began as a Sea Grant agent in
28 Cameron and Calcasieu parishes; and
29 WHEREAS, one of Dr. Coreil's most significant accomplishments in that role was
30 his work in wetland resources, where he helped private landowners, industry, and

2 liaison in matters of environmental stewardship and public policy; and
3 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil also made a profound and lasting contribution to youth
4 development through Louisiana 4-H, where over the course of thirty-four years he helped
5 strengthen the program, establish conservation initiatives such as Marsh Maneuvers, and
6 continue fundraising support for youth environmental programs even after retirement; and
7 WHEREAS, Dr. Coreil's lifetime of leadership and service has been recognized
8 through his induction into the LSU AgCenter Hall of Fame, the Louisiana 4-H Hall of Fame,
9 the National 4-H Hall of Fame, and the Louisiana Agriculture Hall of Distinction; and

11 access, workforce development, youth advancement, and faithful service to the state of
12 Louisiana.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 commend and honor Dr. Paul Dean Coreil on the occasion of his retirement as Chancellor
15 of Louisiana State University of Alexandria and does recognize his many accomplishments
16 and lasting contributions to higher education, public service, youth development, and the
17 people of Louisiana.
18 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express
19 its deepest appreciation to Dr. Paul Dean Coreil for his decades of dedicated leadership and
20 service and extends best wishes to him in his retirement.
21 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to Dr.
22 Paul Dean Coreil.
