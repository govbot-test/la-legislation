5 §1337. Appellate and supervisory jurisdiction; writs
6 A. The Criminal District Court Court of appeal with jurisdiction for the
7 Parish of Orleans shall have appellate jurisdiction of all cases tried before the
8 Municipal Court of New Orleans and the Traffic Court of New Orleans. Appeals
9 from the municipal and traffic courts shall be on the law and the facts and shall be
10 tried upon the records made and the evidence offered in said courts by the judge to
11 whom the appeal shall be allotted. In all cases tried before the judges of the criminal
12 district court in which an appeal does not lie to the supreme court, an appeal shall lie
13 on questions of law and fact to two or more of the judges of the criminal district
14 court, as prescribed by said court. The criminal district court shall adopt rules
15 regulating the manner of taking and hearing and deciding such appeals.
16 B. The Criminal District Court Court of appeal with jurisdiction for the
17 Parish of Orleans shall have general supervisory jurisdiction over the municipal and
18 traffic courts of New Orleans and shall have authority to issue writs of habeas corpus
19 in criminal cases, as well as such other writs and orders as are necessary in aid of the
20 jurisdiction of the court.
21 * * *
22 §1343. Allotment of cases among judges; holding of preliminary examination not
23 ground for recusation
24 A. All cases pending in the criminal district court shall be allotted equally
25 among Sections A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, and L the divisions of the court.
26 Except on Sundays, legal holidays, and legal half-holidays, the allotment of cases
27 shall be made public by classes daily at noon by the clerk or a deputy clerk selected
28 by him, in the presence of the district attorney. The fact the accused was committed
29 for trial at a preliminary examination shall not be grounds for the recusation of the
30 trial judge who held the preliminary examination.
