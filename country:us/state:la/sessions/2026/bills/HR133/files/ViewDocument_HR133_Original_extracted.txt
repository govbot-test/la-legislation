HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 133
BY REPRESENTATIVE MENA
COMMENDATIONS: Commends Colette Hirstius on being named president of Shell USA
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To commend Colette Hirstius on being named president of Shell USA.
3 WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize Colette Hirstius for her outstanding
4 professional achievements and to commend her on being named president of Shell USA; and
5 WHEREAS, Ms. Hirstius is a New Orleans native who grew up in Gentilly and
6 graduated from St. Mary's Dominican High School and Tulane University; and
7 WHEREAS, Ms. Hirstius's history is deeply rooted in the state of Louisiana; her
8 father, Broderick Bagert, was a lawyer and New Orleans City Council member in the 1970s
9 and served on the Louisiana Public Service Commission in the 1980s; and
10 WHEREAS, Ms. Hirstius's two-decade career at Shell has placed her at locations
11 around the world, including West Africa and the company's headquarters in The Hague,
12 Netherlands; and
13 WHEREAS, in her new role, Ms. Hirstius will oversee more than thirteen thousand
14 Shell employees and the European oil giant's portfolio of energy companies; and
15 WHEREAS, Ms. Hirstius will remain in New Orleans while serving as president of
16 Shell's U.S. operations, making her the first president of Shell USA to be based in Louisiana;
17 and
18 WHEREAS, Colette Hirstius has brought great pride to the state of Louisiana through
19 her service at Shell, and she is most deserving of the highest recognition for her outstanding
20 career achievements.
