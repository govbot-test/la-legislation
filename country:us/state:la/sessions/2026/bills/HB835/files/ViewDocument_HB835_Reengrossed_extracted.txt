HLS 26RS-1188 REENGROSSED
2026 Regular Session
BY REPRESENTATIVE SCHAMERHORN
WILDLIFE/MGT AREAS: Establishes a charter guide wildlife management area access
permit
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 56:302.9(H) through (J) and to enact R.S. 56:302.9(K), relative
3 to charter guide wildlife management area access permits; to create an annual charter
4 guide wildlife management area access permit; to establish a fee for the permit; to
5 provide for the privileges conveyed by the permit; to provide for an effective date;
6 and to provide for related matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 56:302.9(H) through (J) are hereby amended and reenacted and R.S.
9 56:302.9(K) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §302.9. Charter boat fishing guide license; nonresident fee
11 * * *
12 H. A charter boat fishing guide licensee performing for-hire fishing guide
13 activity within a wildlife management area may purchase a Charter Guide WMA
14 Access Permit. This permit satisfies the wildlife management area access permit
15 requirements for the charter fishing guide and his clients when engaged in a for-hire
16 fishing trip within a wildlife management area. The fee for the Charter Guide WMA
17 Access Permit shall be one hundred dollars. The permit is valid for one year,
18 beginning on January first of each calendar year and expiring on December
19 thirty-first of the same calendar year.

1 I. Each charter boat fishing guide licensee may complete a landing report and
2 return the report to the department at the time and manner designated by the
3 commission. The commission shall establish the information to be provided in the
4 landing report by the charter boat fishing guide licensee. The landing report may
5 include information on the number of trips taken, number of anglers fishing on a trip,
6 area fished, time fished, trip origin, any information determined by the commission
7 to be necessary to properly manage the fishery resource of the state, and any
8 information required by the commission to prepare stock assessments. However, the
9 commission shall not request any financial information. The information provided
10 by a licensee shall be maintained as confidential in accordance with R.S. 56:301.4.
11 The commission shall adopt rules and regulations in accordance with the
12 Administrative Procedure Act to implement the provisions of this Subsection.
13 I. Except for Subsection H, a violation of any provision of this Section shall
14 constitute a class four violation, R.S. 56:34.
15 J. Penalties.
16 (1) Except for Subsection I of this Section, a violation of any provision of
17 this Section shall constitute a class four violation subject to the penalties of R.S.
18 56:34.
19 (2) In addition to any other penalty provided for in this Section, for any
20 person convicted of a first offense of violating Subsection A or B of this Section, the
21 offender shall pay a fine of no less than one thousand dollars to the Department of
22 Wildlife and Fisheries. For any person convicted of a second offense of violating
23 Subsection A or B of this Section, the offender may only operate a vessel that
24 employs a vessel monitoring system for three years after the date of the conviction.
25 For any person convicted of a third or subsequent offense of violating Subsection A
26 or B of this Section, the offender may only operate a vessel that employs a vessel
27 monitoring system for ten years after the date of the conviction.
28 (2) (3) Whenever an offender is required to employ a vessel monitoring
29 system, access to the monitoring system shall be granted to the department, and the

1 offender shall notify the department on which vessel or vessels the offender will be
2 operating for commercial or recreational use. Any person required to be on board
3 a vessel with an approved vessel monitoring system shall comply with all rules and
4 regulations promulgated by the department or the commission to ensure compliance
5 with vessel monitoring system requirements. The cost of a vessel monitoring system
6 shall be the responsibility of the offender. The department shall approve the vessel
7 monitoring system for use pursuant to this Section.
8 (3) (4) Any charter guide or individual posing as a charter guide required to
9 operate a vessel with an approved vessel monitoring system who is found operating
10 any vessel that is not equipped with such a system, for commercial or recreational
11 purposes, may have their charter guide license suspended and may be prohibited
12 from obtaining such a license for the remainder of the year in which the person was
13 found not abiding by the vessel monitoring requirement. In addition, the violator
14 shall be sentenced to perform no less than forty hours of community service. If
15 available, the hours shall be served in a litter abatement community service program.
16 (4) K. The department shall adopt rules and regulations in accordance with
17 the Administrative Procedure Act to implement the provisions of this Section.
18 Section 2. This Act shall become effective upon signature by the governor or, if not
19 signed by the governor, upon expiration of the time for bills to become law without signature
20 by the governor, as provided by Article III, Section 18 of the Constitution of Louisiana. If
21 vetoed by the governor and subsequently approved by the legislature, this Act shall become
22 effective on the day following such approval.

HB 835 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Schamerhorn
Abstract: Establishes a charter guide wildlife management area (WMA) access permit for
$100 per year.
Present law authorizes the Wildlife and Fisheries Commission to regulate and manage all
wildlife management areas.
Page 3 of 4

are additions.

Present law prohibits commercial activity on WMAs.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law creates an annual charter guide WMA access permit to allow charter fishing
guides to operate within WMAs.
Proposed law establishes a fee of $100 for the charter guide WMA access permit.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Amends R.S. 56:302.9(H)-(J); Adds R.S. 56:302.9(K))
