HEALTH CARE: Requires disclosure of certain agreements between a hospital and a real

1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 40:2104.1, relative to certain agreement disclosures between hospitals and real
3 estate investment trusts; to provide for definitions; to provide for reporting
4 requirements; to provide for entering into a lease agreement with a real estate
5 investment trust; to provide for the publication of records; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. R.S. 40:2104.1 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
9 §2104.1. Healthcare real estate ownership transparency
10 A. For the purposes of this Section, the following terms have the meanings
11 ascribed to them:
12 (1) "Healthcare real estate investment trust" or "REIT" means a real estate
13 investment trust, as defined by 26 U.S.C. 856, whose assets consist of real property
14 held in connection with the use or operations of a hospital.
15 (2) "Main campus" means the physical premises where the primary hospital
16 building and its adjoining grounds are located, and is licensed as a hospital by the
17 department pursuant to R.S. 40:2100 et seq.
18 (3) "REIT lessee" means any hospital that is leasing its main campus from
19 a healthcare real estate investment trust.

1 B. Upon initial application for licensure or in an application for an annual
2 license renewal, every hospital that is a REIT lessee shall disclose that it is a REIT
3 lessee and shall submit to the department certified copies of all documents related
4 to the lease including but not limited to the lease, master lease, sublease, license or
5 any other such document related to the use, occupancy or utilization of the premises
6 occupied by the REIT lessee.
7 C. Any hospital that enters into a lease agreement with a real estate
8 investment trust regardless of whether the lease agreement constitutes acquisition as
9 defined in R.S. 40:2115.2, shall submit to the secretary of the department, within
10 fourteen calendar days of entering into the lease agreement, all documents related to
11 the lease, master lease, sublease, license or any other agreement with the REIT for
12 the use, occupancy or utilization of the premises occupied by the hospital.
13 D. The department shall maintain on the department's website a listing of
14 every licensed hospital that maintains a lease agreement with a real estate investment
15 trust.

HB 1118 Reengrossed 2026 Regular Session Fisher
Abstract: Provides relative to certain agreement disclosures between hospitals and real
estate investment trusts and the governing rules.
Proposed law provides for definitions.
Proposed law requires every hospital that is also a real estate investment trust lessee to report
to the secretary of the Louisiana Department of Health (LDH) all documents related to lease
agreements and occupancy of the hospital premises. Reporting must occur within 14
calendar days of entering into the agreement.

Proposed law provides that any documents submitted to the secretary of the LDH be
considered public record and available upon request.
Proposed law requires LDH to publish a list of every licensed hospital that is also a real
estate investment trust lessee, the annual total of complaints and their resolution, and
complaints and resolutions for each real estate investment trust lessee that has entered into
a new lease agreement in the last 12 months. This information is to be published on July 1,
2027, and every July 1 thereafter.
Effective upon signature of governor or lapse of time for gubernatorial action.
(Adds R.S. 40:2104.1)
