SLS 26RS-436 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE BILL NO. 390
BY SENATOR ALLAIN
MUNICIPALITIES. Creates a mandate for digital mapping of all utility systems in the state
of Louisiana. (gov sig)
1 AN ACT
2 To enact R.S. 45:1207, relative to publicly owned and privately owned utility systems; to
3 authorize the Louisiana Public Service Commission to identify the locations of all
4 utility systems in the state of Louisiana; to provide for the digital mapping of all
5 utility systems; to provide for the promulgation of rules and regulations; to provide
6 relative to a grant program; to provide for an effective date; and to provide for
7 related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 45:1207 is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 §1207. Digital mapping for public and private utility companies
11 A.(1) The Louisiana Public Service Commission shall secure information
12 from any entity, public or private, providing public utilities, including
13 electricity, water, natural gas, and wastewater, to at least one location in the
14 state to assist the office in compiling a statewide parish-by-parish map, in digital
15 form, identifying the locations of utility systems in the state of Louisiana.
16 B. The cost of mapping and locating utilities shall be assessed to the
17 owners of the utility systems, whether public or private.

DIGEST
SB 390 Original 2026 Regular Session Allain
Proposed law provides that the Louisiana Public Service Commission (LPSC) shall secure
information from any entity, public or private, providing public utilities, including
electricity, water, natural gas, and wastewater, to at least one location in the state to assist
the office in compiling a statewide parish-by-parish map, in digital form, identifying the
locations of utility systems in the state of La.
Proposed law provides that the cost of mapping and locating utilities shall be assessed to the
owners of the utilities, whether public or private.
Proposed law provides that the LPSC may promulgate rules and regulations necessary to
carry out the provisions of proposed law in accordance with the provisions of the
Administrative Procedure Act (APA).
