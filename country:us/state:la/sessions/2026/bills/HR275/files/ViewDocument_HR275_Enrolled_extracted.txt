HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 275
BY REPRESENTATIVE DICKERSON
A RESOLUTION
To urge and request the Department of Transportation and Development to study the
feasibility of incorporating an interstate highway signage maintenance and reporting
program.
WHEREAS, interstate highway signage serves a critical function in ensuring
transportation safety, facilitating effective navigation, and upholding the state's public
image; and
WHEREAS, when interstate highway signage is obscured by dirt or damaged by road
debris or other means it creates many challenges to their functionality; and
WHEREAS, it is currently federally mandated that interstate highway signs be
cleaned twice per year; and
WHEREAS, there are federal grants available for a variety of highway issues,
including addressing the maintenance of highway signs, such as the Safe Streets and Roads
for All Grant Program, the Surface Transportation Block Grant Program, and the Rural
Surface Transportation Grant Program; and
WHEREAS, the Department of Transportation and Development does not have a
procedure for maintaining and cleaning interstate highway signs; and
WHEREAS, instead of maintenance and cleaning of signs, the usual procedure of the
department is to replace the signs; and
WHEREAS, the federal government is in full support of a P.I.L.O.T. program to
establish a system for maintenance of interstate highway signs; and
WHEREAS, simply implementing a comprehensive statewide highway sign
maintenance and reporting program by routinely cleaning, applying protective treatments,
reporting damages, and documenting the status of signs serves as an effective solution to any
sign issues; and
WHEREAS, the maintenance cost per sign is between two hundred fifty to four
hundred seventy-five dollars; and

WHEREAS, the replacement cost per sign is between four thousand three hundred
fifty to six thousand six hundred fifty dollars; and
WHEREAS, routine maintenance of signs may double the expected lifespan of these
signs; and
WHEREAS, the implementation of an interstate highway maintenance program will
likely result in the creation of approximately twenty new positions; and
WHEREAS, the safety and overall well-being of the citizens of Louisiana would
benefit from interstate highway signage maintenance.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby urge and request the Department of Transportation and
Development to study the feasibility of incorporating an interstate highway signage
maintenance and reporting program to promote the safety and well-being of the state.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Department of Transportation and
Development shall submit a written report detailing its findings relative to the benefits and
feasibility of incorporating an interstate highway maintenance program within the state to
the House Committee on Transportation, Highways and Public Works prior to January 1,
2027.
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
secretary of the Department of Transportation and Development.
