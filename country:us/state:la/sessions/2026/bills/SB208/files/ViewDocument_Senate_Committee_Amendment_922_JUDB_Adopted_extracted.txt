SENATE COMMITTEE AMENDMENTS
2026 Regular Session
by Senator Cathey
1 AMENDMENT NO. 1
2 On page 1, line 2, after "To" delete the remainder of the line and add the following:
3 "amend and reenact R.S. 29:296(H) and to enact R.S. 29:296(B)(4), (F)(3), (I), and (J),
4 relative to services for veterans; to provide for restrictions on"
5 AMENDMENT NO. 2
6 On page 1, line 6, after "Section 1." delete the remainder of the line and insert the following:
7 "R.S. 29:296(H) is hereby amended and reenacted and R.S. 29:296(B)(4), (F)(3), (I),
8 and (J) are hereby enacted to read as follows:"
9 AMENDMENT NO. 3
10 On page 1, delete lines 9 through 11 and insert the following"
11 "B.(1) * * *
12 (4) No person shall receive compensation for any services rendered in
13 connection with any claim for a veteran that has never received a disability
14 rating.
15 * * *
16 F. Businesses engaging in the preparation of an initial claim or appeal of a
17 disability rating for a fee shall not do any of the following:
18 * * *
19 (3) Charge a fee for the termination of any contract or agreement, except
20 for those fees charged for actual work performed prior to the request for
21 termination.
22 * * *
23 H. A private right of action shall exist for a veteran against any person
24 who collected or intended to collect a fee from the veteran in connection with
25 any contract or agreement where the person's negligent or reckless conduct
26 caused a reduction of a disability rating, a denial of benefits, or a delay causing
27 economic loss.
28 (1) A successful right of action shall be entitled to actual damages, lost
29 retroactive benefits, diminished monthly compensation, and attorney fees.
30 (2) A successful action shall be entitled to treble damages for intentional
31 misconduct.
32 H. I. An entity assisting veterans with their initial disability claims as
33 prescribed within this Section shall, within one hundred twenty days of the request,
34 provide on an annualized basis of all of the following data to the Department of
35 Veterans Affairs:
36 (1) Aggregate number of serviced in the state.
37 (2) Number of claims approved, denied, and pending.
38 (3) Average claim return time.
39 (4) Number of clients who received a successful increase who have a
40 previously assigned "agent of record".
41 (5) Data provided shall exclude any items of personal financial, medical, or
42 other data deemed confidential, business privileged, or HIPAA protected
43 information.
44 J. Nothing in this Section shall be construed to regulate accredited
45 agents, attorneys, or representatives as defined in 38 CFR 14.627 or
46 representation before the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs."
