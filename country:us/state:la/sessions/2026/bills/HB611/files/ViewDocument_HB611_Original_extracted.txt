HEALTH/WOMEN'S: Provides relative to pregnancy-related service centers
1 AN ACT
2 To enact Part VI-H of Chapter 11 of Title 40 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to
3 be comprised of R.S. 40:2180.31 through R.S. 40:2180.38, relative to
4 pregnancy-related service centers; to require licensure by the Louisiana Department
5 of Health for pregnancy help centers providing healthcare services; to provide for
6 definitions, standards and oversight, and enforcement and penalties; and to provide
7 for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. Part VI-H of Chapter 11 of Title 40 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of
10 1950, comprised of R.S. 40: 2180.31 through R.S. 40:2180.38, is hereby enacted to read as
11 follows:
12 Part VI-H. LICENSING OF PREGNANCY HELP CENTERS
13 §2180.31. Short title
14 This Act shall be known and may be cited as the "Pregnancy Help Center
15 Healthcare Licensing Act".
16 §2180.32. Purpose
17 The purpose of this Part is to authorize the Louisiana Department of Health
18 to promulgate and publish rules and regulations for the licensing of pregnancy help
19 centers to increasingly provide services that involve medical screening, diagnostics,
20 or counseling presented as healthcare.

1 §2180.33. Definitions
2 For the purposes of this Part, the following terms have the following
3 meanings:
4 (1) "Client" means any individual who receives services from a pregnancy
5 help center.
6 (2) "Department" means the Louisiana Department of Health.
7 (3) "Healthcare services" means services that constitute or involve medical
8 care, including but not limited to any of the following:
9 (a) Pregnancy testing.
10 (b) Ultrasound imaging.
11 (c) Medical screening or diagnostic services.
12 (d) Pregnancy counseling or consultation presented as medical advice.
13 (4) "Licensed healthcare facility" means a facility licensed by the department
14 pursuant to Title 40 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950.
15 (5) "Pregnancy help center" means any organization, center, or facility,
16 whether for-profit or nonprofit, that has as its primary purpose the provision of
17 pregnancy-related services, counseling, testing, or information.
18 §2180.34. Licensure requirement
19 A. Any pregnancy help center that provides healthcare services shall be
20 licensed by the department as a healthcare facility.
21 B. No pregnancy help center shall provide healthcare services unless and
22 until it has obtained such licensure.
23 C. Pregnancy help centers that provide only non-medical services including
24 peer counseling, material assistance, education, or religious counseling and do not
25 provide healthcare services shall not be subject to licensure under this part.
26 §2180.35. Licensing Standards and Oversight
27 A. The Department shall promulgate rules and regulations in accordance
28 with the Administrative Procedure Act establishing minimum standards for licensure
29 which shall include but not be limited to:

2 (2) Clinical supervision by appropriately licensed medical professionals.
3 (3) Infection control and equipment safety standards.
4 (4) Client confidentiality and compliance with applicable privacy laws.
5 (5) Record keeping and reporting requirements.
6 (6) Complaint and inspection procedures.
7 B. Standards adopted pursuant to this Part shall be comparable to those

9 requirements unrelated to patient safety or quality of care.
10 §2180.36. Inspections and compliance
11 A. Licensed pregnancy help centers shall be subject to all of the following:
12 (1) Initial licensure inspections.
13 (2) Periodic inspections, including unannounced inspections, as determined
14 by the department.
15 B. The department may investigate complaints alleging violations of this Part
16 of applicable regulations.
17 §2180.37. Enforcement and penalties
18 A. The department may deny, suspend, or revoke licensure for violations of
19 this Part or rules adopted pursuant thereto.
20 B. Any pregnancy help center that knowingly provides healthcare services
21 without required licensure shall be subject to any of the following:
22 (1) Civil penalties not to exceed five thousand dollars per violation per day.
23 (2) Injunctive relief to prevent continued unlicensed operation.
24 C. The attorney general may bring an action to enforce the provisions of this
25 Part.
26 §2180.38. Construction
27 Nothing in this Part shall be construed to:
28 (1) Require any pregnancy help center to provide or refer for abortion
29 services.

HB 611 Original 2026 Regular Session Freeman
Abstract: Provides for the designation of pregnancy-related service centers, their licensure
by the Louisiana Department of Health, standards, and relevant definitions.
Proposed law names the proposed law the Pregnancy Help Center Healthcare Licensing Act.
Proposed law states the legislative purpose of the proposed law.
Proposed law lists relevant definitions.
Proposed law provides for any pregnancy help center to be licensed by the Louisiana
Department of Health (LDH); all rules regarding standards and oversight and inspections and
compliance shall be promulgated by LDH in compliance with the Administrative Procedure
Act.
Proposed law provides that enforcement and penalties for any pregnancy help centers that
violate licensure requirements are to be levied by the Louisiana Department of Health
(LDH).
Proposed law does not require pregnancy help centers to provide or refer for abortion
services; to regulate religious activity unrelated to healthcare services; and limits speech and
advocacy not connected to the provision of healthcare.
Proposed law provides for severability.
