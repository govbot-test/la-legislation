SLS 26RS-2358 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 92
BY SENATOR SELDERS
Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To recognize Wednesday, April 22, 2026, as Counseling Day at the Louisiana State Capitol
3 and to commend the Louisiana Counseling Association for its tireless dedication to
4 the advocacy for mental health services in the state of Louisiana.
5 WHEREAS, professional counselors provide an essential role in strengthening the
6 mental health and well-being of individuals, families, and communities throughout
7 Louisiana; and
8 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Counseling Association is a leading organization
9 dedicated to advancing the counseling profession by supporting licensed counselors and
10 promoting access to high-quality mental health services across the state; and
11 WHEREAS, professional counselors work diligently to address a wide range of
12 challenges, including anxiety, depression, trauma, substance abuse, and family-related
13 issues, helping individuals cope with and overcome life's most difficult circumstances; and
14 WHEREAS, through compassion, expertise, and a commitment to service, counselors
15 provide safe and supportive environments where individuals can find comfort, healing, and
16 peace of mind; and
17 WHEREAS, the Louisiana Counseling Association tirelessly advocates for policies
18 that expand access to mental healthcare, reduce stigma, and ensure that individuals in all
Page 1 of 2

1 communities receive the support they need.
2 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Senate of the Legislature of Louisiana
3 does hereby recognize Wednesday, April 22, 2026, as Counseling Day at the Louisiana State
4 Capitol and does hereby commend the Louisiana Counseling Association for its tireless
5 dedication to the advocacy for mental health services in the state of Louisiana.
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to
7 Louisiana Counseling Association.
