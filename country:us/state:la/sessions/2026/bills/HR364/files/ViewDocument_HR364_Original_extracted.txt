HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 364
BY REPRESENTATIVE FREEMAN
CONDOLENCES: Expresses condolences on the death of Sharon Courtney
1 A RESOLUTION
2 To express the condolences of the House of Representatives on the death of Sharon
3 Courtney and to honor her life, legacy, and contributions to the political landscape
4 of the city of New Orleans and the state of Louisiana.
5 WHEREAS, it is with profound sorrow that the Louisiana House of Representatives
6 has learned of the passing of Sharon Courtney on May 21, 2026, after a brief and courageous
7 battle with colon cancer; and
8 WHEREAS, Sharon was born in 1965 in Aberdeen, Mississippi, and graduated from
9 the University of Mississippi in 1987 with a degree in journalism; and
10 WHEREAS, Sharon moved to Washington, D.C., and began a thirty-eight-year
11 government relations career as a legislative assistant on Capitol Hill; she started in the office
12 of Congressman Larkin Smith and then served in the office of Congressman John Murtha,
13 where she spent five and a half years focusing on health care and education issues; and
14 WHEREAS, Sharon moved to New Orleans in 1994 and resided in the city for
15 thirty-two years; and
16 WHEREAS, Sharon brought her valuable experience on Capitol Hill to Tulane
17 University, where she spent over thirty years as a leader in community and government
18 relations; and
19 WHEREAS, Sharon was a key advisor to Tulane's president and played a vital role
20 aiding President Scott Cowen in reopening the university after Hurricane Katrina's
21 floodwaters devastated the city and scattered students to other campuses; and

1 WHEREAS, Sharon was a champion for the rebuilding of the city post-Katrina and
2 led efforts to secure two hundred seventy-five million dollars in crucial funding for all the
3 city's public and private universities; she was named a YLC Role Model by the Young
4 Leadership Council the spring of 2006; and
5 WHEREAS, during her tenure at Tulane University, Sharon successfully managed
6 community relations to secure city approval for a new on-campus football stadium, which
7 helped to energize the football program; her efforts were also instrumental in kickstarting
8 the New Orleans BioDistrict and creating a strategic plan for the one thousand two
9 hundred-acre economic development district aimed at growing the biosciences sector; and
10 WHEREAS, Sharon founded Neutral Ground Strategies in February of 2025, and in
11 her final days, she continued to serve higher education by helping the University of New
12 Orleans increase its funding through her work in hospitals; and
13 WHEREAS, Sharon also served in leadership roles for various boards, including
14 Market Umbrella, ReNEW Schools, and the Data Center; and
15 WHEREAS, Sharon was often referred to as one of the few honest brokers left in the
16 policy world; she was a steadfast advocate of the truth, constantly strived to make the world
17 a better place, and acted with respect, honesty, and integrity; and
18 WHEREAS, Sharon had a deep love of New Orleans and its cultural traditions; she
19 attended Jazz Fest every year that she lived in the city, became a member of the Krewe of
20 Muses and the Krewe of Dolly, and purchased Saints season tickets in 2006; and
21 WHEREAS, above all else, Sharon was a connector of people, a generous and loyal
22 friend, and the consummate host; she was happiest with a crowded table of family and
23 friends in her home, where she often hosted dinners large and small; her generosity was not
24 merely hospitality, but a generosity of the spirit; and
25 WHEREAS, Sharon leaves behind the legacy of her generosity of spirit that is felt
26 across the country and the world; left to cherish her memory are her husband, Chris Allen,
27 her parents, Jim and Diane Morgan, her siblings, Mark Courtney, Courtney Gregory and her
28 husband Clay, her beloved nephews, Eli, Fen, Jax, and Clay, and a host of friends and
29 colleagues who carry on her legacy.
