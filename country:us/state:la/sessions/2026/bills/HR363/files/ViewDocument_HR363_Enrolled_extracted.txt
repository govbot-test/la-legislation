HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 363
BY REPRESENTATIVE MURRAY
A RESOLUTION
James C. Carter, S.J., Ph.D., Jesuit priest, physicist, and the longest-serving president
of Loyola University New Orleans.
WHEREAS, it is with immense sadness and profound regret that the House of
Representatives of the Legislature of Louisiana has learned of the passing, on May 22, 2026,
of the Reverend James C. Carter, S.J., Ph.D.; and
WHEREAS, James C. Carter was born in 1927 in New York to Louisiana natives and
was the oldest of three boys; as a teenager, he attended St. Stanislaus High School, a
boarding school in Bay St. Louis, Mississippi; and
WHEREAS, he spent one year as a student at Loyola University New Orleans before
entering the Jesuit novitiate at Grand Coteau in 1945; he earned a bachelor's degree in
physics from Spring Hill College, a master's degree from Fordham University, and a
doctorate in physics from the Catholic University of America; his academic studies were
focused on nuclear physics, evolution, and religion; and
WHEREAS, after being ordained a Jesuit priest in 1958, Father Carter returned to
Loyola in 1960 to teach physics; he was named provost and academic vice president in 1970
and became the fourteenth president of the university in 1974; and
WHEREAS, Father Carter dedicated more than six decades of service to Loyola as
a student, professor, priest, administrator, and president; he served as president from 1974
to 1995 and guided the university through a period of significant academic growth and
institutional advancement while remaining steadfastly committed to its Jesuit and Catholic
identity; and

WHEREAS, after retiring from the presidency in 1995, Father Carter became
Loyola's first chancellor; he then served as pastor and superior of the Jesuit community from
2001 to 2004 and later returned as a pastoral minister and part-time professor to continue his
lifelong commitment to mentoring students; and
WHEREAS, Father Carter served the broader New Orleans community through
leadership roles with the Metropolitan Area Committee and National Conference for
Community and Justice; and
WHEREAS, Father Carter transitioned to a ministry of prayer in 2020; he first served
at the St. Alphonsus Rodriguez Pavilion in Grand Coteau, Louisiana, and later at St. Ignatius
Hall in Florissant, Missouri; and
WHEREAS, Father Carter received numerous honors in recognition of his
scholarship and service, including an honorary doctorate from Loyola University New
Orleans in 1995; and
WHEREAS, the Reverend James C. Carter, S.J., Ph.D., will be deeply missed, but
the legacy of his outstanding service to Loyola University New Orleans, the New Orleans
community, and the Jesuit Order shall live on in perpetuity in the hearts and minds of those
who were privileged enough to have known and loved him.
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby express heartfelt condolences on the death of the
Reverend James C. Carter, S.J., Ph.D., Jesuit priest, physicist, and the longest-serving
president of Loyola University New Orleans; does hereby express enduring appreciation for
his incredible dedication and service to Loyola and its students; and does hereby extend
sincere wishes that his family, friends, former colleagues, and loved ones find comfort in
their shared memories of his beautiful life.
SPEAKER OF THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
