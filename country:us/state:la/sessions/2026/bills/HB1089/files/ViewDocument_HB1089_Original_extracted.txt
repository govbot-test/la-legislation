1 AN ACT
2 To enact Chapter 25 of Title 6 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950, to be comprised
3 of R.S. 6:1431 through 1433, relative to CARE Accounts; to provide for the
4 establishment of CARE Accounts; to define certain terms; to provide for qualifying
5 medical expenses; to provide for the deposit of certain funds; to provide for the
6 disbursement of certain funds; to provide for applicability; and to provide for related
7 matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. Chapter 25 of Title 6 of the Louisiana Revised Statutes of 1950,
10 comprised of R.S. 6:1431 through 1433, is hereby enacted to read as follows:
11 CHAPTER 25. CARE ACCOUNTS
12 §1431. Short title
13 This Chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "CARE Account Act".
14 §1432. Definitions
15 For the purpose of this Chapter, the following definitions apply:
16 (1) "CARE Account" or "Compensation Allocated for Reimbursable
17 Expenses Account" means a money market account for which the court has ordered
18 the deposit of funds from a special damages award allocated for the purpose of future
19 medical payments in a delictual action.

5 §1433. Account deposits; disbursements; establishment and maintenance
6 A. In any delictual action for which the court awards special damages for
7 future medical expenses, the court shall order that the portion of the award allocated
8 for future medical expenses be placed into a CARE Account unless otherwise agreed
9 to by the parties and approved by the court.
10 B. A CARE Account shall be established in the name of and owned by the
11 judgment debtor for the use and benefit of the judgment creditor for the term of the
12 judgment creditor's life.
13 C. A CARE Account shall be established and maintained at a financial
14 institution as a money market deposit account.
15 D.(1) Funds deposited in a CARE Account shall only be used for the
16 purchase of qualifying medical expenses. However, the financial institution in which
17 the CARE Account is established has no duty to ensure transactions in the account
18 are made for qualifying medical expenses nor is the financial institution subject to
19 liability for transactions involving the account for the purchase of items and services
20 that are not qualifying medical expenses.
21 (2) The judgment debtor may contract with a third-party administrator for
22 services related to ensuring transactions involving a CARE Account are made only
23 for qualifying medical expenses.
24 E. If a judgment creditor attempts to use funds from a CARE Account to
25 purchase a qualifying medical expense and the transaction is declined, the judgment
26 creditor may utilize either of the following methods to effectuate the transaction:
27 (1) The judgment creditor may pay for the qualifying medical expense with
28 other funds and present an itemized receipt of the transaction to the financial

HB 1089 Original 2026 Regular Session Bamburg
Abstract: Establishes CARE Accounts for the payment of an award for future medical
payments resulting from a delictual action.
Proposed law provides a short title and defines "CARE Account" or "Compensation
Allocated for Reimbursable Expenses Account" as a money market account for which the
court has ordered the deposit of funds from a special damages award allocated for the
purpose of future medical payments in a delictual action.
Proposed law defines "qualifying medical expenses" as any product or service contained in
the most recently published list of Current Procedural Terminology and Healthcare Common
Procedure Coding System codes maintained by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid
Services.
Proposed law provides for the following:
(1) A CARE Account is for the use and benefit of the judgment creditor for the term of
the judgment creditor's life, and upon the death of the judgment creditor, any funds
remaining in the CARE Account reverts to the judgment debtor.
(2) A CARE Account is established by the judgment creditor and maintained at a
financial institution as a money market deposit account.
(3) Funds in a CARE Account are only to be used for the purchase of qualifying medical
expenses.
(4) Methods for a judgment creditor to use funds from a CARE Account to be
reimbursed for a qualifying medical expense if the transaction is declined.
