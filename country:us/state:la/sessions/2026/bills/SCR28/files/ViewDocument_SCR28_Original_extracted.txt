SLS 26RS-1846 ORIGINAL
2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 28
BY SENATOR WHEAT
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and celebrates Elmer Candy and recognizes Tuesday,
May 5, 2026, as Elmer Candy Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and celebrate Elmer Candy and to recognize Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as Elmer
3 Candy Day at the Louisiana State Capitol.
4 WHEREAS, Elmer Candy, located in Ponchatoula, Louisiana, is the second largest
5 Valentine heart box manufacturer in the country, manufacturing more than 46 million hearts
6 annually; and
7 WHEREAS, Elmer Candy is renowned for its traditional Easter treats, including
8 Gold Brick Eggs, Heavenly Hash, and Pecan Eggs, all cherished by locals; and
9 WHEREAS, the forerunner of the Elmer Candy was the Miller Candy Corporation,
10 which was founded in New Orleans in 1855, by former pastry chef Christopher Henry
11 Miller; and
12 WHEREAS, Miller's son-in-law, Augustus Elmer, eventually joined him in the
13 business as a partner; and
14 WHEREAS, in 1914, Elmer's sons joined the business and the corporation became
15 known as Elmer Candy; and
16 WHEREAS, the origin of Heavenly Hash Eggs can be traced back to 1923, with Gold
17 Brick Eggs and Pecan Eggs first being made in 1936; and these three iconic Easter season
18 favorites outsell national brands all along the Gulf Coast; and

1 WHEREAS, in the 1960s, Roy Nelson became a partner in the enterprise, eventually
2 purchasing the entire company from the Elmer family, and encouraged his son, Allan, to join
3 the endeavor; and
4 WHEREAS, the two men refocused the company's offerings to seasonal chocolates
5 rather than everyday candy and snacks; and
6 WHEREAS, in 2016, the company expanded its facility footprint by adding an
7 additional 70,000 square feet to its existing 330,000 square foot factory; and
8 WHEREAS, Elmer Candy leads the industry with the highest level of quality control,
9 food safety, and industry innovation; and
10 WHEREAS, today, the third generation of the Nelson family operates the Elmer
11 Candy company and continues to create sweet memories for all future generations of candy
12 lovers.
13 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
14 commend and celebrate Elmer Candy, the oldest family-owned chocolate company in the
15 country, and does hereby recognize Tuesday, May 5, 2026, as Elmer Candy Day at the
16 Louisiana State Capitol.
17 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to the
18 Nelson family of Elmer Candy.
