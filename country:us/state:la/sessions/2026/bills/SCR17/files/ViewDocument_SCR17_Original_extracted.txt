2026 Regular Session
SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 17
BY SENATORS BARROW, BASS, BOUDREAUX, CARTER, DUPLESSIS, HARRIS,
JACKSON-ANDREWS, JENKINS, LUNEAU, PRICE AND SELDERS
COMMENDATIONS. Commends and congratulates Louis S. Reine on the occasion of his
retirement as president of the Louisiana AFL-CIO.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate Louis S. Reine on the occasion of his retirement as president
3 of the Louisiana AFL-CIO.
4 WHEREAS, formed in 1955, the American Federation of Labor and Congress of
5 Industrial Organizations (AFL-CIO) is the largest federation of unions in the United States
6 with the Louisiana AFL-CIO supporting over 100,000 members across the state, including
7 local unions, building trade councils, and central labor councils advocating for better wages,
8 benefits, safe working conditions, and pro-worker policies; and
9 WHEREAS, Louis S. Reine has been a constant participant in each regular and
10 extraordinary legislative session over the past twenty-nine years as he has served as assistant
11 to the president and Director of Legislative and Political Activities for the Louisiana AFL-
12 CIO, including the position of Secretary-Treasurer from 2005-2006 prior to his election in
13 2026 as President of the Louisiana AFL-CIO; and
14 WHEREAS, while engaged in yearly battles with the Louisiana Association of
15 Business and Industry (LABI), Louis says, "[T]he majority of my personal dealings with
16 LABI have been with [lobbyist] Jim Patterson…[H]e has a constituency that pushes for
17 certain things. I have a constituency that pushes for other things. [H]e was always truthful
18 and a man of honor."; and

1 WHEREAS, Louis has been a member of the International Brotherhood of

3 Association Local 214 for over twenty-five years; and
4 WHEREAS, prior to joining the staff of the Louisiana AFL-CIO in 1997, Louis held
5 various positions in his local union ranging from outstanding apprentice to apprenticeship
6 director and administrator of the Health & Welfare and Pension Benefits Fund for
7 Carpenters Local 1098 while emphasizing economic security for workers through a fair
8 wage, the opportunity for their children to obtain an education, available and affordable
9 health care, and the ability to retire with a secure pension; and
10 WHEREAS, he has provided invaluable leadership and expertise on numerous state
11 boards and commissions, including serving on the Louisiana Technical College Advisory
12 Board, the Louisiana Workforce Investment Council, the Louisiana Workforce Commission,
13 the Louisiana Workers' Compensation Commission, and the Industry-Based Certification
14 Committee; and
15 WHEREAS, his public service further includes membership on many influential
16 bodies, including the Louisiana Racing Commission, the Louisiana Recovery Authority, the
17 Task Force on Structural Changes in Budget and Tax Policy, the Louisiana State School to
18 Work, the Louisiana Statewide Apprenticeship Administrators Association, the Louisiana
19 State Council of Carpenters, the Louisiana Health Care Commission, the Louisiana
20 Occupational Forecasting Conference, the Louisiana Economic Development Council, the
21 Louisiana High School Redesign Commission, the Louisiana Indigent Defense Board, and
22 the Louisiana Health Works Commission; Chair of the Louisiana Re-Entry Council, and
23 through the Louisiana Department of Education, Louis is recognized as a Baton Rouge
24 Vocational-Technical School Certified Technical Instructor; and
25 WHEREAS, Louis has also been deeply involved in civic and community
26 organizations, including serving as a National Board Member of the Council on
27 Occupational Education, participating in the East Baton Rouge Youth Council, the East
28 Baton Rouge Parish Vocational Advisory Council, the East Baton Rouge Parish School
29 Board Workforce Development Board, and the Southern States Apprenticeship Council
30 Steering Committee, Youth Apprenticeship, the Board of Directors for Young Emerging

1 Leaders of Louisiana, and volunteer service with the Greater Baton Rouge Food Bank; and
2 WHEREAS, although his professional accomplishments are numerous, Louis is
3 foremost a family man devoted to his wife, Sharon Flory Reine, and they are the proud
4 parents of five children and grandparents of six grandchildren; and
5 WHEREAS, through decades of tireless advocacy, leadership, and service, Louis S.
6 Reine has made a lasting and meaningful contribution to the workers of Louisiana and has
7 contributed to the betterment of this great state.
8 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
9 commend and congratulate Louis S. Reine upon the occasion of his retirement as president
10 of the Louisiana AFL-CIO and does further express appreciation for his many years of
11 service and dedication to the workers of Louisiana.
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to Louis
13 S. Reine with best wishes for continued success and happiness in a well-deserved retirement.
