9 §1275.3. Rural international physician; licensure; limitations
10 A. The board shall issue a restricted rural physician license to an applicant
11 who meets all of the following requirements:
12 (1) Holds a medical degree from a medical school listed in the World
13 Directory of Medical Schools.
14 (2) Has completed medical education substantially equivalent to a doctor of
15 medicine or doctor of osteopathic medicine.
16 (3) Has successfully passed Step 1, Step 2, and Step 3 of the United States
17 Medical Licensing Examination or an equivalent examination as determined and
18 approved by the board.
19 (4) Possesses certification from the Educational Commission for Foreign
20 Medical Graduates or equivalent credential verification approved by the board.

HB 1160 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Echols
Abstract: Creates a restricted rural physician license for international medical graduates
who meet certain requirements.
Proposed law authorizes the La. St. Board of Medical Examiners (board) to issue a restricted
rural physician license to certain international medical graduates.
Proposed law provides eligibility requirements, including medical education equivalency,
passage of licensing examinations, certification by the Educational Commission for Foreign

Medical Graduates or equivalent, clinical experience, background check, and English
proficiency.
Proposed law requires licensees to practice only in designated rural or medically underserved
areas or at approved facilities, and requires compliance with annual renewal requirements.
Proposed law requires a minimum two-year supervision period under a qualified supervising
physician and authorizes the board to permit unsupervised practice upon successful
completion of the supervision period.
Proposed law provides limitations on the restricted license and provides for disciplinary
action for violations.
Proposed law authorizes eligibility for a full and unrestricted license after five years of
continuous practice in a rural or medically underserved area, subject to specified conditions.
Proposed law requires the board to promulgate rules and regulations to implement proposed
law.
(Adds R.S. 37:1275.3)
