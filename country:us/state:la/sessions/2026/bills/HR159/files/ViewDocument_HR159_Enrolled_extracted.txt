2026 Regular Session
HOUSE RESOLUTION NO. 159
BY REPRESENTATIVE MILLER
A RESOLUTION
To designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as Louisiana Occupational Therapy Association
Day at the state capitol.
WHEREAS, Louisiana is home to over three thousand licensed occupational
therapists and occupational therapy assistants; and
WHEREAS, the month of April is recognized nationally as Occupational Therapy
Month; and
WHEREAS, the Louisiana Occupational Therapy Association advocates for progress
and innovation in the occupational therapy profession, promotes high standards of clinical
competency, and facilitates statewide relationships with the occupational therapy
community; and
WHEREAS, occupational therapists use a holistic approach to assist people and help
them participate in both necessary and leisurely activities; and
WHEREAS, occupational therapy provides numerous benefits to its patients,
including a reduction of fall risks and nursing home admissions, lowered readmission rates
to hospitals, lowered rates of depression, and improved quality of life for people with
chronic disease; and
WHEREAS, occupational therapists develop and implement fit for duty and injury
prevention programs in the industrial setting, which keep employees healthy and employers
financially competitive; and
WHEREAS, it is most fitting to recognize Louisiana's occupational therapists and
occupational therapy assistants for their contributions to the state.

HR NO. 159 ENROLLED
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the House of Representatives of the
Legislature of Louisiana does hereby designate Wednesday, April 15, 2026, as Louisiana
Occupational Therapy Association Day at the state capitol and does hereby express enduring
appreciation to occupational therapists and occupational therapy assistants for their
outstanding work to improve the quality of life for patients.
