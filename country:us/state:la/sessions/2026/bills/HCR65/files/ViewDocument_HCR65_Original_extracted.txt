2026 Regular Session
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 65
BY REPRESENTATIVE BUTLER
CONGRESS: Memorializes the United States Congress, the President of the United States,
the United States Department of Labor, the United States Department of Homeland
Security, and the Louisiana Congressional Delegation to take all necessary and
appropriate actions to reclassify all job duties of the crawfish industry as agricultural
labor services for purposes of the H-2A Temporary Agricultural Worker Program
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To memorialize the United States Congress, the President of the United States, the United
3 States Department of Labor, the United States Department of Homeland Security,
4 and the Louisiana Congressional Delegation to take all necessary and appropriate
5 actions to reclassify all job duties of the crawfish industry as agricultural labor
6 services for purposes of the H-2A Temporary Agricultural Worker Program.
7 WHEREAS, the Louisiana crawfish industry is a cornerstone of the state's
8 agricultural economy, generating hundreds of millions of dollars in annual economic impact,
9 and serving as an irreplaceable part of the state's cultural heritage; and
10 WHEREAS, crawfish production in Louisiana is highly seasonal and labor intensive,
11 requiring a reliable and timely workforce during peak harvest and processing periods; and
12 WHEREAS, handling, weighing, washing, grading, processing, peeling, packaging,
13 and freezing of crawfish for sale are fundamentally tied to aquacultural production, forming
14 a continuous chain of timely executed operations essential for moving the product from farm
15 to market; and
16 WHEREAS, due to the temporary and seasonal nature of crawfish harvest in
17 Louisiana, job postings are often left vacant due to a shortage of readily available, able,
18 willing, and qualified domestic workers; and

1 WHEREAS, the H-2A program could account for this domestic labor shortage while
2 not displacing or adversely affecting domestic workers or wages; and
3 WHEREAS, to employ workers under the H-2A program, an employer is required
4 to provide domestic workers, including H-2A workers, with adequate housing,
5 transportation, and meals, and pay wages in accordance with the current Adverse Effect
6 Wage Rate, as determined by the United State Department of Labor; and
7 WHEREAS, despite the seasonality and agricultural nature of Louisiana's crawfish
8 industry, current federal regulations classify job duties involving crawfish processing and
9 related work as nonagricultural, restricting employers to the oversubscribed and highly
10 competitive H-2B Temporary Non-agricultural Workers Program; and
11 WHEREAS, the H-2B program's limited visa cap, unpredictable supplemental visa
12 allotments, and lengthy processing delays have caused severe workforce shortages in
13 Louisiana's crawfish industry, resulting in reduced farm production, limited processing
14 capacity, financial losses, and significant disruptions across the supply chain, largely due to
15 administrative rulemaking regarding visa certifications, job duty classifications, and
16 supplemental visa distributions; and
17 WHEREAS, the H-2A program is specifically designed to meet seasonal agricultural
18 labor needs; and
19 WHEREAS, classifying all job duties performed by a crawfish worker, including but
20 not limited to fishing, baiting, handling, weighing, washing, grading, tagging, cleaning,
21 processing, peeling, packaging, freezing, and preparation of crawfish for sale as agricultural
22 labor would bring federal policy into alignment with the operational reality of crawfish
23 farming and would stabilize the workforce that Louisiana's crawfish industry depends on;
24 and
25 WHEREAS, ensuring an adequate, legal, and dependable labor supply is vital to the
26 survival of Louisiana's crawfish farmers, processors, and associated rural economies.
27 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
28 memorialize the United States Congress, the President of the United States, the United States
29 Department of Labor, the United States Department of Homeland Security, and the members
30 of the Louisiana Congressional Delegation to take all necessary and appropriate actions to

1 reclassify all job duties of the crawfish industry as agricultural labor services for purposes
2 of the H-2A Temporary Agricultural Workers Program.
3 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that reclassification should include revisions to
4 federal statutes, regulations, or administrative interpretations, as needed, to ensure that
5 Louisiana crawfish producers and processors will be able to utilize the H-2A program to
6 meet their essential seasonal workforce needs immediately.
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the action to reclassify the job duties of the
8 crawfish industry as agricultural labor services be taken expeditiously, with emergency
9 measures implemented to ensure the timely processing of the H-2A visas and to meet the
10 labor demands of the 2026 crawfish season.
11 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that copies of this Resolution be transmitted to the
12 President of the United States, the secretary of the United States Department of Labor, the
13 secretary of the United States Department of Homeland Security, the majority and minority
14 leaders of the United States Senate, the speaker and minority leader of the United States
15 House of Representatives, and each member of the Louisiana Congressional Delegation.
