DIGEST
SB 513 Original 2026 Regular Session Fesi
Proposed law provides a pilot program to authorize local governmental subdivisions or
political subdivisions to use the average bid method to determine the bidder for awarding
public contracts. Proposed law further provides the pilot program shall be limited to fifteen
projects per year and subject to the approval of the Senate and House committees on
transportation, highways and public works.
Proposed law provides for definitions.
Proposed law provides for requirements for a local governmental subdivision or a political
subdivision to specify in its bidding documents for a public work and how bids are awarded.

Proposed law provides no less than sixty days prior to advertising for bids a local
governmental subdivision or political subdivision deliver written notification of the proposed
project by name and description and the reason the method is in the public interest, to the
Senate and House committees on transportation, highways, and public works for review and
approval. If approved, and when the project is completed, the owner must submit a report
including final cost and evaluation of competition, cost and quality, the name of the bidder
awarded the contract, the amount of each responsive bid, the average mean of all responsive
bids, and the basis for award to both committees.
Proposed law provides proposed law will not apply to professional services.
Proposed law provides for DOTD to use the average bid method for road and highway
construction projects or other best project delivery method practices.
Proposed law provides if five or more responsive bids are received DOTD will average the
bids and the highest and lowest responsive bids. The department will award the contract to
the bidder whose responsive bid is closest to the average regardless of being over or under.
If less than three responsive bids are received, all bids will be rejected.
Proposed law provides proposed law will not apply to professional services.
Effective August 1, 2026
(Adds R.S. 38:2211.3 and R.S. 48:255.9)
