8 §202.1. Residential contractor Contractor fraud; penalties
9 A. Residential contractor Contractor fraud is the misappropriation or
10 intentional taking of anything of value which belongs to another, either without the
11 consent of the other to the misappropriation or taking, or by means of fraudulent
12 conduct, practices, or representations by a person who has contracted to perform any
13 home building improvement or residential construction, or who has subcontracted
14 for the performance of any home building improvement or residential construction.
15 A misappropriation or intentional taking may be inferred when a person does any of
16 the following:
17 * * *
18 (2) Uses, or causes an agent or employee to use, any deception, false
19 pretense, or false promise to cause any person to enter into a contract for home
20 building improvements or residential construction.

HB 70 Original 2026 Regular Session Walters
Abstract: Adds commercial property to the crime of contractor fraud.
Present law provides for the crime of residential contractor fraud and penalties.
Proposed law changes present law to also apply to commercial properties.
Present law provides an additional penalty when the victim is aged 65 or older.
Proposed law retains present law regarding 65-year-old victims and clarifies that this
provision only applies to residential properties.
(Amends R.S. 14:202.1(A)(intro. para.), (2), (3), and (6), (B), (C)(1), and (D))
