1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend and congratulate Grambling State University upon the celebration of the
3 centennial of the Grambling State University "World Famed" Tiger Marching Band
4 and to recognize its outstanding contributions to music, education, culture, and the
5 proud heritage of the state of Louisiana.
6 WHEREAS, Grambling State University is celebrating the centennial of its "World
7 Famed" Tiger Marching Band, marking one hundred years since the band's founding in
8 1926; and
9 WHEREAS, in 1926, founder and president of Grambling State University Charles
10 P. Adams requested Ralph Waldo Emerson Jones to establish and organize a marching band,
11 and with only seventeen instruments, Ralph Jones began laying the foundation for a proud
12 and enduring musical tradition; and
13 WHEREAS, from these humble beginnings, the Grambling State University Tiger
14 Marching Band has grown into one of the most distinguished collegiate marching bands in
15 the nation and has long served as a premier ambassador for Grambling State University and
16 the state of Louisiana; and
17 WHEREAS, beginning in 1952, under the leadership of renowned director Conrad
18 Hutchinson Jr., the band was elevated to new heights of precision, discipline, and artistry and
Page 1 of 3
SLS 26RS-4077 ORIGINAL
SCR NO. 79
1 became widely known as "The Best Band in the Land"; and
2 WHEREAS, the Grambling State University Tiger Marching Band has earned
3 national and international recognition through performances on some of the most prominent
4 stages in the world, including major collegiate and professional sporting events, such as its
5 historic appearance at Super Bowl I, United States Presidential Inaugural Parades and
6 inaugural events, international performances, and major motion pictures and national media;
7 and
8 WHEREAS, the band has continued for generations to showcase musical excellence,
9 cultural pride, and the distinctive tradition of historically Black college and university
10 marching bands, inspiring audiences across Louisiana, the nation, and the world; and
11 WHEREAS, the Grambling State University Tiger Marching Band has long been an
12 integral part of the tradition and pageantry of the Bayou Classic, contributing to one of
13 Louisiana's most celebrated annual cultural and collegiate events; and
14 WHEREAS, through its commitment to discipline, artistry, and excellence, the band
15 has provided generations of students with opportunities for musical achievement, leadership
16 development, and service to the university; and
17 WHEREAS, the band continues to serve as a source of pride and unity for students,
18 alumni, faculty, staff, and supporters and remains an enduring symbol of the heritage and
19 spirit of Grambling State University; and
20 WHEREAS, the celebration of the centennial of the Grambling State University
21 Tiger Marching Band is a fitting occasion to recognize and honor the directors, student
22 musicians, staff, alumni, and supporters whose dedication and talent have sustained this
23 proud legacy for one hundred years.
24 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
25 commend and congratulate Grambling State University upon the celebration of the
26 centennial of the Grambling State University "World Famed" Tiger Marching Band and does
27 hereby recognize its outstanding contributions to music, education, culture, and the proud
28 heritage of the state of Louisiana.
29 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that a copy of this Resolution be transmitted to Dr.
30 Martin Lemelle, Jr., President of Grambling State University.
