SENATE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 57
BY SENATOR SEABAUGH
of the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame.
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 To commend Jonathan Lucroy on being inducted into the 2026 class of the Louisiana Sports
3 Hall of Fame.
4 WHEREAS, each year, the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame in Natchitoches,
5 Louisiana, collaborates with the Louisiana Sports Writers Association in selecting a limited
6 number of sports figures who embody the character, skill, talent, and accomplishment
7 worthy of being named to the Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame; and
8 WHEREAS, the 2026 Louisiana Sports Hall of Fame has selected Jonathan Lucroy
9 as a member of its distinguished class of inductees; and
10 WHEREAS, Jonathan Lucroy was an early standout at Umatilla High School, where
11 he collected a number of honors throughout his career, including All-Area first team, All-
12 State first team, and All-Central Florida second team, as well as having his high school
13 jersey retired in 2011; and
14 WHEREAS, Jonathan Lucroy's collegiate career began at the University of Louisiana
15 at Lafayette from 2005 to 2007, where he established school records with 184 runs batted
16 in, 414 total bases, and 54 doubles, while ranking among the top five in hits, home runs,
17 batting average, and slugging percentage; and
18 WHEREAS, as a freshman in 2005, he earned Louisville Slugger Freshman All-

1 American and Second Team All-Sun Belt honors after batting a .379 batting average with
2 48 runs batted in; and
3 WHEREAS, he captured First Team All-Sun Belt Conference honors for the second
4 time and was named a National Collegiate Baseball Writers Association Third Team All-
5 American after batting a .360 batting average with eighteen home runs and a program-record
6 174 single-season total bases; and
7 WHEREAS, he led the Ragin' Cajuns to Sun Belt Conference titles and NCAA
8 Tournament appearances before being selected in the third round of the 2007 Major League
9 Baseball Draft by the Milwaukee Brewers; and
10 WHEREAS, Jonathan Lucroy enjoyed a distinguished twelve-year Major League
11 Baseball career from 2010 to 2021, playing for nine teams and compiling over 1,000 hits,
12 217 doubles, 108 home runs, and 548 runs batted in across 1,210 games; and
13 WHEREAS, he earned Major League Baseball All-Star selections in 2014 and 2016
14 and finished fourth in the National League Most Valuable Player voting in 2014, the same
15 season in which he set the Major League Baseball single-season doubles record for a catcher
16 with 53 doubles; and
17 WHEREAS, Jonathan Lucroy represented the United States in international
18 competitions, participating in the 2013 World Baseball Classic and helping Team USA
19 capture the gold medal in 2017; and
20 WHEREAS, following his retirement, he has remained committed to the
21 development of young athletes through coaching at the high school level and participating
22 in the Team USA Youth National Team Program, while prioritizing faith, family, and
23 community; and
24 WHEREAS, his career reflects excellence, leadership, sportsmanship, and
25 dedication, brings pride to the state of Louisiana, and serves as an inspiration to future
26 generations of athletes.
27 THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Legislature of Louisiana does hereby
28 commend Jonathan Lucroy on being inducted into the 2026 class of the Louisiana Sports
29 Hall of Fame.
