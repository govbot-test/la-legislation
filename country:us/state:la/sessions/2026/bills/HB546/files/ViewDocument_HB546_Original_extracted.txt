9 L.(1)(a) A peace officer or a peace officer accompanied by an emergency

16 in need of immediate hospitalization to protect such a person or others from physical
17 harm. The person may be transported only to a treatment facility as defined in R.S.
18 28:2.

HB 546 Original 2026 Regular Session Spell
Abstract: Authorizes a peace officer to take a person into protective custody in certain
circumstances.
Present law provides that a peace officer may take a person into protective custody if, as
result of his personal observation, there exists reasonable grounds to believe that the person
is a proper subject for an involuntary hold because the person is acting in manner dangerous
to himself, to others, or is gravely disabled and is in need of immediate hospitalization to
protect such person or others from physical harm.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that a peace officer may determine that reasonable
grounds exist to place a person in protective custody based on the peace officer's
observations or credible third-party information.
Present law provides that a peace officer shall transport a person in protective custody to a
treatment facility.
Proposed law retains present law and adds that a peace officer shall comply with existing
departmental practices and procedures.
(Amends R.S. 28:53(L)(1))
