9 CHAPTER 24-F. PRE-SPECTRUM ALERT ACTION PLAN
10 §2530.11. Pre-Spectrum Alert action plan; requirements; rules and regulations
11 A. The legislature finds that autism spectrum disorder (ASD)
12 significantly affects children in this country, creating unique challenges that can
13 lead to critical safety risks, such as elopement or wandering behaviors in
14 children. Children with ASD are prone to wander from safe environments at a
15 higher rate than their nonautistic peers. Elopement frequently results in severe
16 consequences, including fatalities caused by traffic accidents and drowning.
17 Notably, drowning is the leading cause of death of children with ASD in this

DIGEST
SB 227 Original 2026 Regular Session Harris
Proposed law requires the Dept. of Public Safety and Corrections (DPS&C), in cooperation
with the Dept. of Children and Family Services (DCFS), to institute a pre-Spectrum Alert
action plan to enhance the safety and well-being of children with autism spectrum disorder
(ASD) through immediate and effective community and emergency response.
Proposed law requires the plan to, at a minimum, consist of one or more programs to:
(1) Develop a training program and alert system for missing children with ASD which
is compatible with existing alert systems.
(2) Establish policies and procedures for responding to a reported missing child
emergency when the child has ASD, with the policies and procedures, at a minimum,
providing for all of the following:
(a) Immediate and widespread dissemination of critical information when a child
with ASD is reported missing.
(b) Enhancement of emergency response teams' competence by informing them
of the unique behaviors and needs of children with ASD.
(c) Measures to increase public awareness and understanding of the risks
associated with autism-related elopement, to foster community support for
children with ASD.
(3) Require a law enforcement agency, at a minimum, to do all of the following upon
receiving a missing child with ASD report:
