Section 1:
2026 Regular Session ACT No. 483 ENROLLED
BY SENATOR REESE
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact R.S. 15:587(A)(1)(b) and (F)(1), 587.2(B), and 587.7(B)(3), relative
3 to criminal history background checks; to provide for the Louisiana Bureau of
4 Criminal Identification and Information; to provide for the duty to provide certain
5 information to the Louisiana State Board of Private Security Examiners and
6 employers; to provide for the release of expunged criminal history record
7 information under certain circumstances; and to provide for related matters.
8 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
9 Section 1. R.S. 15:587(A)(1)(b) and (F)(1), 587.2(B), and 587.7(B)(3) are hereby
10 amended and reenacted to read as follows:
11 §587. Duty to provide information; processing fees; Louisiana Bureau of Criminal
12 Identification and Information
13 A.(1) * * *
14 (b) The Louisiana State Board of Private Security Examiners shall be entitled
15 to the criminal history record and identification files of the bureau on those persons
16 seeking to be licensed or registered by the board as a means of performing
17 background checks on those individuals. In addition, in order to determine an
18 applicant's eligibility or suitability for licensure or registration under the provisions
19 of the Private Security Regulatory and Licensing Law, each applicant shall be
20 fingerprinted, and the fingerprints shall be forwarded by the bureau to the Federal
21 Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history record check.
22 * * *
23 F.(1) An employer or his representative shall be entitled to obtain conviction
24 records of an applicant seeking employment, directly from the Bureau of Criminal
25 Identification and Information in order to further qualify the applicant for the
26 position being sought, if the applicant has signed a consent form authorizing the
27 employer to obtain such conviction records. Upon written request and upon

1 presentation of a consent form prepared by the bureau and executed by the applicant,
2 the bureau shall provide conviction records of the applicant to the employer or his
3 representative in a timely manner. In addition, each applicant shall be fingerprinted,
4 and the fingerprints shall be forwarded by the bureau to the Federal Bureau of
5 Investigation for a national criminal history check or shall submit fingerprints to
6 the bureau. Records obtained pursuant to the provisions of this Subsection shall not
7 include those records which have been ordered expunged pursuant to R.S. 44:9. The
8 provisions of Subsection B of this Section shall apply to any request made pursuant
9 to this Subsection. The bureau shall not be liable civilly or criminally for the release
10 of information released pursuant to the provisions of this Subsection.
11 * * *
12 §587.2. Institutions of postsecondary education; criminal history information
13 * * *
14 B. When a criminal history records check is requested pursuant to Subsection
15 A of this Section, the institution of postsecondary education shall be provided with
16 state or and national criminal history record information, or both, from the Louisiana
17 Bureau of Criminal Identification and Information and from the Federal Bureau of
18 Investigation relative to the applicant prospective employee whose fingerprints have
19 been obtained by the institution pursuant to this Section. The Bureau of Criminal
20 Identification and Information shall, upon request and after receipt of fingerprint
21 cards and other identifying information as required by the bureau from the
22 institution, make available to the institution all prior arrests for any sex offense,
23 aggravated offense, or any sexual offense against a victim who is a minor as defined
24 in R.S. 15:541 or any crime of violence as enumerated in R.S. 14:2(B) and
25 conviction information for any offense contained in the bureau's criminal history
26 record and identification files, which pertains to the applicant or prospective
27 employee, including convictions dismissed pursuant to Code of Criminal Procedure
28 Article 893 or 894. In addition, when the institution of postsecondary education
29 requests such criminal history record information, the bureau shall forward the
30 fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history

3 * * *
4 §587.7. Volunteer and employee criminal history system
5 * * *
6 B. * * *
7 (3)(a) When a criminal history records check is requested by a qualified
8 entity pursuant to Paragraph (1) of this Subsection, the bureau shall provide the
9 qualified entity with the state criminal history record information of the individual
10 subject to the inquiry. In addition, when the qualified entity requests national
11 criminal history records checks, the bureau shall forward the fingerprints to the
12 Federal Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history records check and
13 shall provide the qualified entity with the national criminal history record
14 information of the individual subject to the inquiry.
15 (b) The bureau may provide expunged criminal history record
16 information from the state's criminal history record repository to public
17 qualified entities at its discretion based upon strong evidence provided by the
18 public qualified entity that its possessing of the expunged information would be
19 in the best interest to protect the children, the elderly, or individuals with
20 disabilities, served by the public qualified entity. Private qualified entities shall
21 be prohibited from receiving expunged criminal history record information.
