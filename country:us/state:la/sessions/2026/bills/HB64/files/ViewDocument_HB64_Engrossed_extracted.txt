BY REPRESENTATIVE MUSCARELLO
CRIMINAL/PROCEDURE: Provides relative to the recall of arrest warrants
1 AN ACT
2 To amend and reenact Code of Criminal Procedure Article 205 and to enact Code of
3 Criminal Procedure Article 202(H), relative to arrest warrants; to provide for the
4 authority of a magistrate to recall an arrest warrant; to provide for notification; to
5 provide relative to the effective period of arrest warrants; and to provide for related
6 matters.
7 Be it enacted by the Legislature of Louisiana:
8 Section 1. Code of Criminal Procedure Article 205 is hereby amended and reenacted
9 and Code of Criminal Procedure Article 202(H) is hereby enacted to read as follows:
10 Art. 202. Warrant of arrest; issuance; recall
11 * * *
12 H. A magistrate may recall a warrant of arrest that he has previously issued
13 and shall immediately notify the law enforcement agency that is responsible for
14 execution of the warrant.
15 * * *
16 Art. 205. Effective period
17 A warrant of arrest remains in effect until executed or recalled.

HB 64 Engrossed 2026 Regular Session Muscarello
Abstract: Provides for the recall of arrest warrants.
Present law (C.Cr.P. Art. 202) provides for the issuance of arrest warrants.
Proposed law retains present law.
Proposed law permits a magistrate to recall an arrest warrant that he has previously issued.
Proposed law requires a magistrate who recalls a warrant of arrest pursuant to proposed law
to immediately notify the law enforcement agency that is responsible for execution of the
warrant.
Present law (C.Cr.P. Art. 205) provides that a warrant of arrest remains in effect until
executed.
Proposed law retains present law and provides alternatively that a warrant of arrest remains
in effect until recalled.
(Amends C.Cr.P. Art. 205; Adds C.Cr.P. Art. 202(H))
Summary of Amendments Adopted by House
The Committee Amendments Proposed by House Committee on Administration of
Criminal Justice to the original bill:

Section 2:
1. Make technical changes.

Section 3:
2. Require a magistrate who recalls a warrant of arrest to immediately notify the
law enforcement agency that is responsible for execution of the warrant.

Section 4:
3. Remove the proposed law prohibition relative to execution of recalled warrants.

Section 5:
4. Provide that a warrant of arrest remains in effect until recalled.
